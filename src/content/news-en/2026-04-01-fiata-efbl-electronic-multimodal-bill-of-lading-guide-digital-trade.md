<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>International Federation of Freight Forwarders Associations (FIATA)</strong> has published a practical guide to support the adoption of its <strong>Electronic FIATA Multimodal Bill of Lading (eFBL)</strong>. The publication is intended to help stakeholders across the supply chain understand how the eFBL can be implemented in a way that is <strong>legally robust</strong> and <strong>interoperable with existing systems</strong>. The guide is part of FIATA's broader effort to provide its members with structured guidance as the industry moves towards digital trade processes.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The eFBL is the electronic version of FIATA's <strong>Multimodal Transport Bill of Lading (FBL)</strong>, a negotiable document <strong>first introduced in 1968</strong>. The original FBL is aligned with rules developed by the <strong>United Nations Conference on Trade and Development (UNCTAD)</strong> and the <strong>International Chamber of Commerce (ICC)</strong>, as well as the legal framework for international multimodal transport contracts.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The guide explains in detail the legal frameworks with which the eFBL is compliant, digital signature standards, and counterparty data exchange protocols. The spread of the digital bill of lading means forwarders will <strong>eliminate paperwork errors</strong>, <strong>shorten processing times</strong>, and <strong>reduce environmental impact</strong>. FIATA stated that it will support its members in choosing eFBL-compliant platforms and is working with industry stakeholders to standardise systems.</p>
