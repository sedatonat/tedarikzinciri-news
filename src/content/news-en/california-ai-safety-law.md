<p style="text-align: justify">Comprehensive AI safety legislation enacted in California establishes standards for artificial intelligence deployment across public and private sectors. The law requires risk assessment, human-in-the-loop controls, model oversight, and incident reporting in high-risk domains including critical infrastructure, transportation, and healthcare.&nbsp;</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, transparency and traceability standards for AI-driven decision engines are expanding; audit logs and explainability requirements are being mandated for algorithms deployed across supplier selection through route planning.&nbsp;</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">On the corporate governance front, obligations to establish AI governance committees and maintain model inventories are being introduced, formalizing MLOps practices. Data privacy and security standards—including privacy-by-design and secure-by-design approaches—are being strengthened, with penalties for violations and mandatory remediation plans established. Small and medium enterprises will benefit from a phased implementation timeline and guidance mechanisms, while larger organizations face annual audits and certification processes.&nbsp;</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In essence, the legislation prioritizes assurance over speed in AI projects, promoting trustworthy artificial intelligence adoption across industries.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>Key Points:</strong></p>\n<p>1. The law introduces oversight in high-risk domains.</p>\n<p>2. Explainability and audit logs become mandatory.</p>\n<p>3. AI governance structures are being formalized.</p>\n<p>4. Privacy and security standards are being strengthened.</p>\n<p>5.
