<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Cargo vessel Istanbul Bridge</strong> dropped anchor at <strong>Rotterdam Port in the Netherlands on the morning of 23 October 2025</strong>, successfully completing the European leg of the <strong>China–Europe Arctic Express route</strong>. Thus the voyage conducted over the <strong>Arctic route spanning from China to Europe</strong> has formally concluded.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">With the discharge of the final container of the voyage, this transport line conducted over the <strong>Northern Sea Route</strong> has once again proven its <strong>time advantage and operational efficiency</strong>. However, the operator disclosed that the return voyage will be conducted <strong>not via the Arctic, but through the Suez Canal</strong>, a decision made taking into account <strong>regional loading demand and trade concentration</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The vessel</strong> will call at <strong>ports in Egypt and Turkey</strong> on its return journey to pick up new cargo and is expected to <strong>return to China in early December 2025</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Record-Time Arrival in Europe via the Arctic Route</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Istanbul Bridge</strong> departed from <strong>China's Ningbo-Zhoushan Port on 23 September 2025</strong>. Following the <strong>Northern Sea Route through the Arctic Ocean</strong>, the vessel reached <strong>Felixstowe Port in the United Kingdom within 20 days</strong>. It subsequently called at <strong>Hamburg Port in Germany on 15 October</strong> and <strong>Gdansk Port in Poland on 19 October</strong>, completing loading and discharge operations. At its final stop in <strong>Rotterdam</strong>, all operations were successfully completed.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This route provides <strong>significant time savings</strong> compared to conventional maritime transport, which takes approximately <strong>40 days via the Suez Canal</strong> and <strong>50 days via the Cape of Good Hope</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Haijie Shipping's New Target: Year-Round Arctic Voyages</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Li Xiaobin</strong>, <strong>Operations Director</strong> of <strong>Haijie Shipping (Hong Kong) Co., Ltd.</strong>, the vessel's operator, shared important plans regarding the future of the Arctic route in a statement:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Next year we will deploy more ice-capable vessels. Our goal is to establish the route on a permanent basis with regular sailings weekly or biweekly during the summer months."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Li further noted that the company will leverage data obtained from this experience to <strong>improve vessel design and fleet technical standards</strong>, thus working toward <strong>enabling year-round Arctic voyages</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Alternative Route in Winter: Eastern Europe Express</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">When the Arctic route closes during winter months due to ice formation, Haijie Shipping will launch a new hybrid transport service called <strong>"Eastern Europe Express"</strong> to maintain operational continuity. In this service, cargo will be transported to Europe <strong>via sea transport through the Suez Canal</strong> and then conveyed to final destinations <strong>via rail transport</strong>. Thus the China–Europe route can be completed in as little as <strong>25 days</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A New Milestone for the Ice Silk Road Strategy</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>China–Europe Arctic Express</strong> route operates over the <strong>Northern Sea Route</strong>, a <strong>new international maritime corridor connecting East Asia with Europe</strong>. This route stands out as a core component of the <strong>"Ice Silk Road"</strong> initiative, which aims to enhance China's logistics efficiency in global trade.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The strategic importance of the Arctic route extends beyond time savings to include significant benefits in terms of <strong>reduced fuel consumption</strong> and <strong>lowered carbon emissions</strong>. However, operations remain limited in seasonality due to <strong>the requirement for icebreaker support</strong> and <strong>environmental sensitivities</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Haijie Shipping (Hong Kong) Co., Ltd.</strong> is a Hong Kong-based shipping company operating in <strong>container transport between Asia and Europe</strong>. The company is active in the fields of <strong>intermodal transport</strong>, <strong>logistics services</strong>, and <strong>international route management</strong>, positioning itself as <strong>one of the pioneer operators of the China–Europe Arctic route</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
