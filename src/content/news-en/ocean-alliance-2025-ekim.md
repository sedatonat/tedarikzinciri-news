<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sea-Intelligence</strong>'s <strong>Sunday Spotlight</strong> report, in its 736th edition, reveals that following major alliance restructurings at the beginning of 2025, <strong>Ocean Alliance</strong> has risen to the position of <strong>clear market leader</strong> on both <strong>Transpacific</strong> and <strong>Asia–Europe</strong> routes.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">As the renewed route networks stabilize, analysis based on the <strong>eight-week moving average of "operated capacity market share"</strong> clearly demonstrates the global market impact of carrier alliances.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ocean Alliance Leads on Asia–Northern Europe Route</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The report shows that <strong>Ocean Alliance on the Asia–Northern Europe route</strong> is well ahead of competitors.<br>\nWhile the alliance's market share has long hovered in the <strong>36–38 percent</strong> range, it is expected to <strong>exceed 39 percent in the coming months and reach 41 percent by December 2025</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">This rate creates a strong gap ahead of both <strong>Gemini Cooperation</strong> and <strong>Premier Alliance</strong>.</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Gemini Cooperation</strong>'s share risks declining to <strong>24–25 percent</strong>,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Premier Alliance</strong> and <strong>MSC (Mediterranean Shipping Company)</strong> are positioned in close proximity in the same segment.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">According to Sea-Intelligence's analysis, this trend demonstrates that <strong>Ocean Alliance's long-term Asia–Europe strategy is bearing fruit</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strong Consolidation on Transpacific Route</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">A similar picture is emerging <strong>on Transpacific routes</strong>.</p>\n<p><br>\nOcean Alliance is expected to reach <strong>37 percent market share</strong> on the <strong>Asia–North America West Coast</strong> route by year-end.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">On the <strong>East Coast</strong> route, the share is expected to climb to the <strong>38–40 percent range</strong> — meaning it would be <strong>approximately 20 percentage points ahead of individual competitors</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">This result demonstrates that Ocean Alliance optimizes <strong>container capacity, service frequency, and port connectivity</strong> far more efficiently than competing alliances.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Competition Intensifying on Asia–Mediterranean Route</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The report notes that the <strong>Asia–Mediterranean route</strong> has a <strong>more fragmented and competitive market structure</strong>.<br>\nNevertheless, it is stated that <strong>Ocean Alliance has begun increasing its share here as well</strong> and <strong>is positioning itself to challenge MSC's traditional dominance</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In this region, strategic moves such as <strong>capacity reallocation among alliances and port rotation optimization</strong> are expected to intensify competition.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Gap Between Gemini and Premier Alliance Narrowing</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Gemini Cooperation</strong> (the Maersk &amp; Hapag-Lloyd partnership) has long held <strong>second place</strong>, but recent capacity losses have dragged the alliance's market share downward.</p>\n<p><br>\n <strong>Premier Alliance</strong> (a structure encompassing carriers such as Evergreen, ONE, and Yang Ming) is partially filling this gap and <strong>approaching the 20 percent range.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Sea-Intelligence analysts predict that <strong>as of 2026, the gap among the three major alliances may narrow</strong>, but <strong>Ocean Alliance will maintain its strategic advantage</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ocean Alliance: Strategic Power After Restructuring</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The alliance restructuring at the beginning of 2025 reshaped <strong>route sharing, vessel capacity allocation, and operational integration</strong> processes among carriers.</p>\n<p><br>\n<strong>Ocean Alliance</strong>, composed of the partnership of <strong>COSCO Shipping, CMA CGM, OOCL, and Evergreen</strong>, emerged as the strongest from this process.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The alliance's leadership is built particularly on the following factors:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>High utilization rate</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Comprehensive port coverage</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Rotation flexibility and low blank sailings rate</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Asia-based cost advantage</strong></p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">According to Sea-Intelligence, these factors have elevated Ocean Alliance to the position of "not only the largest, but also the most stable" alliance.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: Ocean Alliance Shapes New Balance in Global Shipping</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Following the 2025 restructuring, the global container shipping map is being redrawn.<br>\nOcean Alliance has shifted the balance of power among alliances in its favor by establishing <strong>clear leadership</strong> on both <strong>Asia–Europe</strong> and <strong>Transpacific</strong> routes.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Gemini Cooperation</strong> and <strong>Premier Alliance</strong> are attempting to remain competitive in this new environment <strong>with more limited capacity growth</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Though <strong>oversupply</strong> risk and <strong>price pressure</strong> persist in the container market, <strong>Ocean Alliance is expected to maintain its market leadership</strong> thanks to <strong>economies of scale and strategic alignment</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ocean Alliance</strong> holds the leading position on <strong>Transpacific and Asia–Europe routes</strong> following the 2025 restructuring.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Its share on the Asia–Northern Europe route could climb as high as <strong>41 percent</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Gemini Cooperation</strong> risks seeing its share decline to <strong>24–25 percent</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">On <strong>Transpacific East and West Coast routes</strong>, Ocean Alliance is <strong>approximately 20 percentage points ahead</strong> of its competitors.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">On the <strong>Asia–Mediterranean route</strong>, MSC's leadership is weakening, and the market is becoming more competitive.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Coordination among COSCO, CMA CGM, OOCL, and Evergreen</strong> is pivotal to Ocean Alliance's success.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Analysts expect the alliance to maintain its leadership through 2026 <strong>with high capacity management, low blank sailings rate, and global reach</strong>.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p>----------</p>\n<p><strong>News Link: </strong><a
