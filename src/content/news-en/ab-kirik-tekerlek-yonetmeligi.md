<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>"broken wheels" regulations</strong>, one of the most contentious issues in European rail freight over the past two years, will be completed by year-end. The <strong>JNS Broken Wheels Task Force</strong>, working under the coordination of the European Railway Agency (ERA), has been working extensively on new safety standards launched following the high-impact derailment accident that occurred in the Gotthard Base Tunnel in 2023.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>How did the Gotthard accident start this process?</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The derailment that occurred in Switzerland's Gotthard Base Tunnel in August 2023 served as a serious alarm bell regarding wheel safety in freight wagons across Europe. The accident's root cause was identified as <strong>metal fatigue and crack propagation</strong> in the type of wheel used; subsequently, European countries and operators recognized the need for coordinated reforms in wheel inspections, maintenance procedures, and component certification.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Following this incident, the ERA established a special technical working group within the <strong>Joint Network Secretariat (JNS)</strong>, comprising infrastructure managers, freight operators, and wagon owners: the <strong>Broken Wheels Task Force</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>What will the new regulations cover?</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Task Force members, the regulatory package being drafted focuses on four main areas:</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>1. Tightening of wheel inspection and maintenance requirements</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">More frequent ultrasonic and magnetic testing,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Mandatory "early detection" algorithms for critical wheel types,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Additional maintenance cycles for high-risk steel grades.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>2. Pan-European unified "wheel traceability" system</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new regulations will make <strong>individual traceability</strong> of wagon wheels mandatory from manufacture through the end of their operational life.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>3. Phased withdrawal of risky wheel types</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Speed restrictions or complete phase-out schedules are expected to be implemented for certain older wheel models.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>4. New responsibilities for railway operators</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Mandatory data sharing between wagon owners and infrastructure managers,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Periodic reporting to national safety authorities,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Re-certification of maintenance facilities.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Why is the sector divided?</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The cost of these regulations has split Europe's freight transport sector in two:</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Concerns:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">New wheel testing and maintenance cycles will create millions of euros in additional costs,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Some independent wagon owners will face investment pressure,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Capacity losses may occur during the transition period.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Supporters:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The safety risk is at an intolerable level,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Harmonized maintenance standards within a single market are essential,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Derailments can paralyze European logistics even for a single day.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts leading the effort, the regulations will be <strong>implemented on a phased basis between 2026 and 2027</strong> following completion, with compliance mandatory across all of Europe.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>High on the EU's safety agenda</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">These regulations will be aligned with the EU's <strong>EU Rail Safety Strategy</strong>, which aims to reduce accidents by 30% by 2030. Additionally, the wheel regulations will work in integration with other freight wagon reforms, such as noise reduction, brake modernization, and digital maintenance management.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Broken Wheels Task Force confirmed that the final version of the regulations will be published before year-end and noted that the draft text has been finalized in line with sector feedback.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">New EU "broken wheels" regulations will be <strong>completed by the end of 2025</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Foundation: The 2023 Gotthard Base Tunnel accident.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Scope: stricter maintenance, mandatory traceability, phasing out risky wheels.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The sector is divided due to costs.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Implementation will begin phased between 2026 and 2027.</p></li>
</ul>
