<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> reports that <strong>Robert J. Bowman</strong>, <strong>Editor-in-Chief</strong> of <strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong>, is releasing an annual <strong>ESG</strong> special issue. Analyst insights indicate that the <strong>Trump</strong> administration is pushing back against environmental regulations—evidenced by its withdrawal from the <strong>Paris Agreement</strong> on climate change (again). But do not let this lead you to believe that <strong>ESG</strong>—environmental, social, and governance—is no longer a concern of international business. On the contrary, companies are pressing forward with efforts to reduce the impact of their global supply chains on our air, water, land, and climate. Similarly, whether out of goodness of heart or growing pressure from consumers, there is increasing concern about human rights in factories, farms, mines, and warehouses. In response to this reality, we are returning with our annual special issue on <strong>ESG</strong> in supply chains. Here we have invited dozens of industry experts to update their latest thinking on a topic that affects every step of the product journey from design to disposal.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> is published by <strong>Keller International Publishing Corporation</strong>, with <strong>Robert J. Bowman</strong> as Editor-in-Chief. The annual <strong>ESG</strong> special issue is published as <strong>SCB Magazine Vol 29 No 2</strong>. Other leading industry publications include <strong>Supply Chain Management Review</strong>, <strong>Logistics Management</strong>, <strong>Modern Materials Handling</strong>, <strong>Inbound Logistics</strong>, <strong>DC Velocity</strong>, <strong>Journal of Commerce</strong> (<strong>S&amp;P Global</strong>), <strong>Lloyd's List</strong>, <strong>Reuters Sustainability</strong>, and <strong>Bloomberg Green</strong> as major ecosystem publications. The <strong>Trump</strong> administration's rollback steps on <strong>ESG</strong> and sustainability regulations include: (1) a second withdrawal from the <strong>Paris Agreement</strong> (January 2025); (2) suspension of implementation of the <strong>SEC Climate Disclosure Rule</strong>; (3) relaxation of <strong>EPA</strong> air quality standards; (4) narrowing of <strong>NEPA</strong> (<strong>National Environmental Policy Act</strong>) interpretations; (5) elimination of federal <strong>DEI</strong> (<strong>Diversity Equity Inclusion</strong>) programs, representing major anti-<strong>ESG</strong> federal policy moves.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the leading <strong>ESG</strong> reporting frameworks include: (1) <strong>GRI</strong> (<strong>Global Reporting Initiative</strong>; CEO <strong>Robin Hodess</strong>; <strong>Amsterdam</strong>); (2) <strong>SASB</strong> (<strong>Sustainability Accounting Standards Board</strong>; now under <strong>ISSB</strong>); (3) <strong>ISSB</strong> (<strong>International Sustainability Standards Board</strong>; <strong>IFRS Foundation</strong>); (4) <strong>TCFD</strong> (<strong>Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures</strong>); (5) <strong>CDP</strong> (formerly <strong>Carbon Disclosure Project</strong>; CEO <strong>Sherry Madera</strong>); (6) <strong>SBTi</strong> (<strong>Science Based Targets initiative</strong>); (7) <strong>UN Global Compact</strong>; (8) <strong>EU CSRD</strong> (<strong>Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive</strong>); (9) <strong>EU CSDDD</strong> (<strong>Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive</strong>); (10) <strong>California SB 253</strong> (<strong>Climate Corporate Data Accountability Act</strong>) as leading reporting and regulatory frameworks. Major <strong>ESG</strong> ratings firms include <strong>MSCI ESG</strong> (CEO <strong>Henry Fernandez</strong>), <strong>Sustainalytics</strong> (owned by <strong>Morningstar</strong>), <strong>S&amp;P Global Sustainable1</strong>, <strong>ISS ESG</strong>, <strong>Bloomberg ESG Data</strong>, <strong>FTSE Russell ESG Ratings</strong>, <strong>EcoVadis</strong> (CEO <strong>Pierre-François Thaler</strong>), <strong>Refinitiv ESG</strong>, and <strong>Moody's ESG Solutions</strong> as major ecosystem players.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the leading supply chain <strong>ESG</strong> technology platforms include <strong>EcoVadis</strong>, <strong>Sphera</strong> (CEO <strong>Paul Marushka</strong>), <strong>Sourcemap</strong> (CEO <strong>Leonardo Bonanni</strong>), <strong>Trase</strong>, <strong>Mighty Earth</strong>, <strong>Satelligence</strong>, <strong>Acclym</strong> (formerly <strong>Agritask</strong>), <strong>HowGood</strong>, <strong>Provenance</strong>, <strong>Worldly</strong> (formerly <strong>Higg</strong>), <strong>Watershed</strong>, <strong>Persefoni</strong>, <strong>Greenly</strong>, <strong>Sweep</strong>, <strong>Nzero</strong>, <strong>Plan A</strong>, <strong>SAP Sustainability Control Tower</strong>, <strong>Microsoft Cloud for Sustainability</strong>, <strong>IBM Envizi</strong>, and <strong>Salesforce Net Zero Cloud</strong> as major ecosystem players. Major <strong>ESG</strong> controversial issues include: (1) <strong>Scope 3</strong> emissions calculation; (2) greenwashing; (3) modern slavery (<strong>UK Modern Slavery Act</strong>; <strong>Australia Modern Slavery Act</strong>); (4) forced labor (<strong>Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act</strong>; <strong>Xinjiang</strong>); (5) child labor; (6) conflict minerals (<strong>Dodd-Frank Section 1502</strong>; <strong>EU Conflict Minerals Regulation</strong>) as leading industry discussions. The <strong>U.S. Department of Labor</strong> and <strong>Customs and Border Protection</strong> (enforcer of <strong>UFLPA</strong>) are major U.S. federal authorities. In conclusion, <strong>Bowman's</strong> argument that <strong>ESG</strong> remains relevant reflects the reality that global supply chain sustainability demands persist independent of geopolitical fluctuations—making <strong>EU CSRD</strong>/<strong>CSDDD</strong>/<strong>EUDR</strong> compliance preparation a major strategic priority for supply chain managers.</p>
