<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Port of Portland</strong> launched a new container terminal operation called the <strong>Oregon Container Terminal (OCT)</strong> at its Terminal 6 facility. The terminal will be operated by <strong>Harbor Industrial</strong> under a long-term lease arrangement, expanding the international trade capacity of the Pacific Northwest region. The terminal's reactivation is seen as a significant step toward reducing the region's dependence on alternative ports such as Long Beach and Tacoma.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The facility offers dual-rail access to <strong>BNSF Railway</strong> and <strong>Union Pacific</strong> lines, enabling rapid container delivery to inland markets. This dual-rail structure significantly expands intermodal transportation options from Oregon to the Midwest and East Coast. Officials announced that the terminal will offer more competitive freight rates for local exporters and play a critical role particularly in delivering agricultural products to Asian markets.</p><p style="text-align: justify">Port of Portland executives positioned the OCT launch within the <strong>"Ship Oregon"</strong> initiative. This initiative aims to strengthen the state's export infrastructure and create jobs. With the new terminal services, Portland's role in transpacific container trade will be revitalized and the region's supply chain resilience will increase. Harbor Industrial pledged to enhance operational efficiency by investing in modernization at the terminal.</p>
