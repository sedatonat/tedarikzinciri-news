<p>2024 is proving to be an exceptionally challenging year for microchip manufacturing. High costs, complex processes, and globally interconnected production networks are making worldwide supply chain crises increasingly complicated. Industries built on microchips must invest billions of dollars and dedicate years to production. This situation carries substantial risks for chip-dependent manufacturers, particularly given trade wars and other supply chain disruptions.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Manufacturers can take preventive measures against potential problems. To avoid production shutdowns in the event of chip shortages, diversification, reliable relationships, and effective inventory management strategies are recommended. With core production capacity concentrated at 92% in Taiwan, sourcing from multiple suppliers within Asia and beyond can reduce risk. Additionally, establishing long-term, trust-based supplier relationships can provide support during difficult times. Finally, effective inventory management is essential to protect against global supply chain fluctuations; it serves as a buffer against supplier-related disruptions.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5>Key Takeaways</h5>\n<ol>\n  <li><p>Microchip manufacturing requires high costs, complex processes, and globally interconnected structures.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Core microchip production capacity is dependent on Taiwan at 92%.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Diversification, reliable supplier relationships, and effective inventory management are critical to reducing supply chain risks.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Risk distribution should be achieved by sourcing from other Asian countries such as South Korea and China in addition to Taiwan.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Establishing long-term, trust-based supplier relationships provides support during challenging times.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Effective inventory management provides protection against supply chain fluctuations.</p></li>\n</ol>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><br></p>\n
