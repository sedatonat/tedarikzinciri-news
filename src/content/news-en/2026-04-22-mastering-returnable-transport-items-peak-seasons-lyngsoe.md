<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Returnable Transport Items</strong> (<strong>RTI</strong>) losses of 10-15% and the absence of real-time visibility represent critical bottlenecks in peak-season supply chains. <strong>RFID</strong> and <strong>BLE</strong> tracking deliver end-to-end asset visibility, reduce costs, improve efficiency, and strengthen operational agility. What creates bottlenecks in peak-season supply chains? Increasingly, the answer is not labour or transport capacity—it is the lack of visibility and control over <strong>RTI</strong>s. Pallets, roll cages, totes, and reusable containers form the backbone of high-volume logistics, and when they go missing or fall out of balance, operations slow or halt entirely. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>RTI</strong> represents critical infrastructure in <strong>FMCG</strong>, <strong>retail</strong>, <strong>postal &amp; parcel</strong>, <strong>automotive parts</strong>, and <strong>healthcare</strong> sectors. Pool pallet providers such as <strong>EUR-pallet</strong> (<strong>800x1200mm</strong>), <strong>UK pallet</strong> (<strong>1000x1200mm</strong>), <strong>CHEP</strong>, and <strong>LPR</strong> (<strong>La Palette Rouge</strong>) operate hundreds of millions of pallets at global scale.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Peak periods magnify these challenges. While many organizations experience annual <strong>RTI</strong> losses of 10-15%, manual tracking methods cannot keep pace with demand. This drives higher costs when they matter most, increased dwell times, and declining service performance. How can supply chains regain control? <strong>RFID</strong> and <strong>Bluetooth Low Energy</strong> (<strong>BLE</strong>) technologies—supported by platforms such as <strong>Lyngsoe Systems</strong>' real-time supply chain visibility solutions—deliver end-to-end tracking between warehouses, distribution centres, and customer locations. By assigning a digital identity to each asset, companies gain a single source of truth that improves planning, forecasting, and operational responsiveness. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>UHF RFID</strong> (<strong>EPCglobal Gen2</strong>) offers passive read capability at 5-10 metres, while <strong>BLE</strong> (<strong>Bluetooth 5.x</strong>) can track actively at ranges of 30-100 metres. <strong>Impinj</strong>, <strong>Zebra Technologies</strong>, <strong>Honeywell</strong>, and <strong>Alien Technology</strong> are leading players in the <strong>RFID</strong> reader and tag market. <strong>Tive</strong>, <strong>Roambee</strong>, <strong>Wiliot</strong>, <strong>Hanshow</strong>, and <strong>Sensitech</strong> position themselves in <strong>BLE</strong> and cellular tracking.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">At a large-scale logistics company managing more than 100,000 reusable assets across more than 100 locations, real-time tracking delivers measurable results: significantly reduced losses, lower manual workload through automated data capture, and better-balanced asset distribution during peak demand. From a supply chain perspective, reducing shrinkage rates improves asset turnover and generates working capital savings. <strong>EU PPWR</strong> (<strong>Packaging and Packaging Waste Regulation</strong>) and <strong>Extended Producer Responsibility</strong> (<strong>EPR</strong>) regulations accelerate the shift towards reusable packaging systems. <strong>Schoeller Allibert</strong>, <strong>ORBIS</strong> (<strong>Menasha Corporation</strong>), and <strong>SSI Schaefer</strong> position themselves as manufacturers in the <strong>plastic crate</strong>, <strong>foldable container</strong>, and <strong>bulk container</strong> categories.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, when raw data is transformed into action, <strong>RTI</strong>s shift from a source of friction to a strategic asset. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Lyngsoe Systems</strong> is expanding from <strong>RFID</strong>-based <strong>postal &amp; parcel</strong> sorting systems with references from <strong>Royal Mail</strong>, <strong>USPS</strong>, <strong>Deutsche Post DHL</strong>, and <strong>UPS</strong> into the <strong>RTI</strong> tracking domain. <strong>Geofencing</strong> and dwell time analysis automatically measure how long assets remain at each location and enable cycle time optimization. <strong>API</strong>-based integration with <strong>WMS</strong>, <strong>TMS</strong>, and <strong>ERP</strong> systems enables real-time synchronization of inventory accuracy. <strong>Edge gateway</strong>s filter and pre-process raw data from <strong>RFID</strong> readers at warehouses and distribution centres before sending it to the cloud. Ultimately, <strong>Lyngsoe</strong>'s case study clearly demonstrates that <strong>RTI</strong> visibility directly impacts peak-season performance and overall supply chain agility.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. <strong>RTI</strong> annual losses typically fall in the 10-15% range.<br>
2. Manual tracking cannot keep pace with demand in peak seasons.<br>
3. <strong>RFID</strong> and <strong>BLE</strong> provide end-to-end asset visibility.<br>
4. <strong>Lyngsoe Systems</strong> is expanding from <strong>postal/parcel</strong> origins into the <strong>RTI</strong> domain.<br>
5. The 100K+ asset case demonstrates reduced losses and balanced distribution.</p>
