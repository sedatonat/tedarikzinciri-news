<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>United Parcel Service (UPS)</strong> is expanding its <strong>RFID sensor</strong> infrastructure across all U.S. hubs, eliminating approximately <strong>20 million manual barcode scans per day</strong>. The company has so far invested about <strong>$100 million</strong> in <strong>smart label</strong> technology.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The sensors track parcel movement inside the hub in real time, reducing <strong>missort</strong> rates and providing faster status updates to both shipper and recipient. <strong>Ingram Micro</strong> is among the large-scale customers piloting the system across its shipping flow.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">UPS rival <strong>FedEx</strong> has also begun testing RFID on select customers' packages. Industry analysts assess that smart labels will become the standard for <strong>end-to-end visibility</strong> in parcel transportation in the coming years.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. UPS is rolling out the RFID sensor network across all U.S. hubs.<br>
2. Total investment stands at roughly $100 million.<br>
3. Twenty million manual scans per day are being eliminated.<br>
4. Ingram Micro is among the first large-scale customers to use the system.<br>
5. FedEx is running RFID trials in parallel.</p>
