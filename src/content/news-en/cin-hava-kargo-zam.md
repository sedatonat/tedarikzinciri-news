<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>U.S. President Donald Trump</strong>'s threat to impose <strong>an additional 100% tariff on Chinese goods</strong> effective November 1st is rapidly increasing demand in <strong>trans-Pacific air cargo traffic</strong>. According to information reported by <strong>The Journal of Commerce (JOC)</strong>, importers are accelerating their orders to avoid potential tariffs and shifting to <strong>air cargo channels</strong>, which offer faster delivery compared to ocean freight.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In a statement released by the White House on October 10th, the measure was framed as a response to <strong>China's export restrictions on rare earth minerals</strong>. These minerals are strategic raw materials for <strong>high-technology, renewable energy, and defense industries</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, even though no official tariff decision has yet taken effect, <strong>U.S. importers</strong> are unwilling to take risks. Due to the lengthy delivery times in <strong>ocean freight</strong>, they are rapidly shifting their orders to <strong>air freight</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, this situation is causing sudden capacity constraints, particularly on the <strong>trans-Pacific route</strong>. Congestion is occurring in shipments from major Chinese air cargo hubs such as <strong>Hong Kong</strong>, <strong>Shanghai</strong>, <strong>Shenzhen</strong>, and <strong>Guangzhou</strong> to U.S. airports in <strong>Los Angeles</strong>, <strong>Chicago</strong>, and <strong>Dallas</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Rising Demand Pushes Prices Higher, But Capacity Provides Balance</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The surge in air cargo demand is naturally driving up <strong>spot rates</strong>. However, according to analysts, prices are not spiking uncontrollably because <strong>air cargo capacity has increased in recent periods</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Major carriers such as <strong>Cathay Pacific</strong>, <strong>China Airlines</strong>, <strong>EVA Air</strong>, and <strong>Air China Cargo</strong> have <strong>increased flight frequencies</strong> on trans-Pacific routes during the post-pandemic recovery period. This is enabling supply to partially absorb the demand shock in the near term.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Nevertheless, exporters operating in sectors such as <strong>high-technology products, automotive components, and textiles</strong> are making aggressive bookings to ensure their goods reach the U.S. before tariffs take effect. <strong>Forwarder</strong> companies are reporting that air cargo capacity will be "nearly completely full" through the end of October.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Political Tensions Creating Economic Pressure</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">This development is being interpreted as a new phase of escalation in <strong>U.S.–China trade relations</strong>. Washington's new tariff plan is seen as retaliation against Beijing's policy of restricting strategic resources. However, these tit-for-tat moves are negatively impacting <strong>supply chain stability</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">While U.S. importers will bear <strong>elevated air cargo costs</strong> in the near term, these expenses are expected to be <strong>passed through to final consumer prices</strong> in the long term. Analysts warn that such policies could amplify <strong>global inflationary pressures</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>New Supply Chain Strategies Emerging</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Experts emphasize that <strong>"nearshoring"</strong> and <strong>"supply diversification"</strong> strategies are gaining importance in the future of supply chains. Excessive dependence on China makes companies more fragile in times of similar trade tensions. For this reason, many global manufacturers are shifting production bases to alternative countries such as <strong>Vietnam, Thailand, Mexico, and India</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Meanwhile, the increase in demand in the air transport sector is expected to <strong>continue through November and December</strong>. This is because U.S. retailers are attempting to rapidly replenish their <strong>holiday inventory</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, if the U.S. implements its new tariffs, there is a strong likelihood of <strong>further increases in air cargo prices for exports from China</strong>, <strong>rising supply chain costs</strong>, and <strong>disruption to international trade balance</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>U.S. President Donald Trump</strong> announced that an <strong>additional 100% tariff</strong> could be applied to Chinese goods effective November 1st.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Following this announcement, <strong>trans-Pacific air cargo demand</strong> increased sharply.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>China's export restrictions on rare earth minerals</strong> are viewed as the primary driver of this tension.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Spot rates</strong> are rising, but <strong>increased air cargo capacity</strong> is tempering prices.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Forwarders</strong> report that capacity will be nearly full through the end of October.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, this development is increasing the need for <strong>diversification in global supply chains</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">If new tariffs take effect, there is a risk of <strong>further increases in air transport costs and consumer prices</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
