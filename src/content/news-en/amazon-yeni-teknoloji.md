<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Amazon</strong> has unveiled three new technologies designed to transform its <strong>fulfillment and delivery operations</strong>: <strong>Blue Jay</strong>, <strong>Project Eluna</strong>, and <strong>smart delivery glasses</strong>. The company aims to use these innovations to <strong>boost efficiency</strong>, <strong>reduce repetitive tasks</strong>, and <strong>accelerate delivery speeds</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">These three systems represent a continuation of Amazon's technological initiatives throughout 2025. The company has also developed <strong>next-generation robotic systems</strong>, <strong>demand forecasting algorithms</strong>, and <strong>dynamic mapping solutions</strong> this year.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Blue Jay: Multi-Armed Robotic System</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Blue Jay</strong> is a <strong>multi-armed coordination system</strong> that enables multiple robot arms to work simultaneously. This technology significantly reduces warehouse workers' need for physical effort in tasks such as <strong>reaching, lifting, and stacking</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The system consolidates <strong>order picking</strong>, <strong>stowing</strong>, and <strong>consolidation</strong> tasks—previously performed at three separate robot stations—into a single workspace, delivering both <strong>time and space savings</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Amazon disclosed that it leveraged <strong>artificial intelligence</strong> and <strong>digital twin</strong> technologies in developing Blue Jay. According to the company, systems that previously required more than three years to complete (such as <strong>Robin</strong>, <strong>Cardinal</strong>, and <strong>Sparrow</strong>) were this time <strong>ready for production in as little as one year</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Blue Jay is currently being tested at an Amazon facility in <strong>South Carolina</strong> and can automatically manage approximately <strong>75% of warehouse inventory</strong>. Long-term, the system is expected to play a critical role, particularly in <strong>same-day delivery</strong> fulfillment centers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Project Eluna: Autonomous Decision-Support AI</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Project Eluna</strong> is an <strong>AI model that provides decision support to Amazon's operations managers</strong>. The company describes the system as a "<strong>digital team member capable of anticipating operational challenges and proposing solutions</strong>".</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Eluna analyzes <strong>historical and real-time data</strong> to provide warehouse managers with recommendations. For example, it answers questions like "<strong>Which department should personnel be shifted to?</strong>" and helps develop <strong>data-driven action plans</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Amazon, the system's core objective is to enable managers to <strong>act with foresight rather than react to crises</strong>. The project will initially be deployed at <strong>a distribution center in Tennessee</strong>, particularly during <strong>peak holiday shipping periods</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In subsequent phases, Eluna is planned to take an active role in:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ergonomic shift planning</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Maintenance schedule optimization</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Preventive safety measures</strong> and other areas.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Smart Delivery Glasses: Real-Time Support for Drivers</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Amazon's <strong>smart delivery glasses</strong> aim to provide delivery drivers with <strong>hands-free</strong> navigation and task information. The glasses make it easier for drivers to stay focused on their surroundings while <strong>reducing phone dependency</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The glasses perform functions such as <strong>identifying package locations</strong>, <strong>scanning barcodes</strong>, and <strong>providing navigation based on door numbers</strong>. They offer particular benefits in <strong>complex building or apartment deliveries</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Amazon stated that it has conducted initial tests with hundreds of drivers, and their feedback is shaping the design of the new version.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The company aims for the glasses to gain the following capabilities in the future:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Real-time defect detection</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Identification of packages left at the wrong address</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Automatic lens adjustment in low light</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Smart security alerts such as yard pet warnings</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">These three technologies are part of Amazon's broader transformation in <strong>automation, artificial intelligence, and ergonomics</strong> across its logistics ecosystem. The company's strategy is to operate human workers and machines in a <strong>complementary balance</strong>, achieving optimal results in both <strong>operational speed</strong> and <strong>worker safety</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Amazon unveiled three new systems: <strong>Blue Jay</strong>, <strong>Project Eluna</strong>, and <strong>smart delivery glasses</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Blue Jay</strong>, a multi-armed robotic system, <strong>reduces physical workload in warehouses</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Project Eluna</strong> is an autonomous AI model providing <strong>data-driven decision support</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Smart glasses</strong> offer drivers <strong>hands-free navigation</strong> and <strong>real-time defect detection</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The systems were developed through <strong>integration of AI, digital twin, and automation technologies</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Goal: <strong>Faster delivery, safer working environment, and optimized cost structure</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
