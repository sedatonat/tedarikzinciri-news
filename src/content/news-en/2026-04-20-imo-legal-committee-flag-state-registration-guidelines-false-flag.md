<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>International Maritime Organization (IMO) Legal Committee</strong> has approved new guidelines aimed at strengthening transparency and due diligence in ship registration. The move addresses a long-standing regulatory gap, as no binding international framework currently governs the registration of ships. The guidelines are intended to support both new and existing flag State registries by introducing practical measures to improve verification processes, strengthen ownership checks, and enhance oversight of registration procedures.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Closing the <strong>113th session of the Legal Committee</strong> in London (13-17 April), <strong>Arsenio Dominguez</strong>, Secretary-General of the IMO, described the development as a "welcome step" in reinforcing due diligence across ship registration systems. Dominguez stated: "This is a welcome step towards ensuring due diligence in ship registration systems for the benefit of safety, protection of the marine environment and the well-being of seafarers."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>False flag operations</strong> have emerged as the practice of "shadow fleet" vessels seeking to evade sanctions regimes by obtaining registration and insurance documents through fake or invalid flag states. This practice threatens global maritime safety and international sanctions compliance, particularly in the context of Russian-origin crude oil shipments. The IMO's new guidelines provide flag states with a consistent verification protocol in response to mounting fraud complaints involving registry service providers such as <strong>BMI Ship Registry</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. IMO Legal Committee approved new guidelines to enhance transparency in ship registration.<br>
2. Measures strengthen verification, ownership checks and oversight in flag state registries.<br>
3. IMO Secretary-General Arsenio Dominguez called the move a 'welcome step.'<br>
4. Decision was approved at the Legal Committee's 113th session in London (13-17 April).<br>
5. The new framework provides a global compliance standard against shadow fleet and false flag operations.</p>
