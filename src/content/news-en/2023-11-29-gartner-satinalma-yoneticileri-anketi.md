<p>According to a survey conducted by consulting firm Gartner, Inc. in June 2023, only 14% of Procurement Leaders express confidence that their organization has the talent to meet the function's future needs. A significant gap emerged between assessments of current and future talent requirements among the 111 Procurement Leaders surveyed.</p>\n<ul>\n  <li><p>While 46% of respondents feel confident about current talent needs, only 14% believe they have sufficient talent to meet future requirements.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The number expressing confidence in <strong>current</strong> capabilities was <strong>three times higher</strong> than those confident about <strong>future</strong> requirements.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Fareen Mehrzai, senior principal analyst in Gartner's Supply Chain Practice, noted that Procurement Leaders are generally confident about their current capabilities and ability to reach near-term goals, but are concerned about having sufficient talent for transformational objectives ahead, particularly in technology and providing strategic business consulting.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>The lack of confidence in future talent readiness stems from a shift in core competencies needed to support objectives associated with supply chain transformation. The survey found:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li><p>69% of respondents noted that business acumen has become more important in the last 12 months.</p></li>\n  <li><p>68% cited increased importance of technology and data skills.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Only 26% reported that "traditional procurement competencies" have become more important during the same period.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>These findings demonstrate <strong>significant gaps between current and future talent needs</strong> for the most critical capabilities. While 96% of respondents reported at least some gap in technology and data skills, 86% reported the same situation regarding business acumen.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n
