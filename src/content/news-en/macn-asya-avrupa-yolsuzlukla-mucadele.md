<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Maritime Anti-Corruption Network (MACN)</strong> has rolled out a new and comprehensive initiative to strengthen anti-corruption efforts across maritime supply chains. The program, titled <strong>"Transparent Trade Routes: Strengthening Integrity in Maritime Supply Chains,"</strong> is focused on <strong>Indonesia, Malaysia and Germany</strong> and spans a three-year timeframe. The initiative is financed by the <strong>Siemens Integrity Initiative</strong> and aims to scale up the <strong>Collective Action</strong> approach that MACN has developed in recent years.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to a statement by MACN, the program builds on <strong>more than six years of ongoing collaboration with Siemens.</strong> <strong>MACN CEO Cecilia Müller Torbrand</strong> emphasizes that the long-term support from Siemens Integrity Initiative demonstrates concretely that systemic change across the sector is possible. According to Torbrand, in multi-stakeholder and cross-border sectors like maritime, lasting transformation is only possible when <strong>the private sector, public authorities and civil society</strong> operate within the same framework.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Indonesia: Private Sector-Focused Structure and Operational Tools</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">In the Indonesia component of the program, MACN plans to establish <strong>a private sector-based alliance.</strong> This structure aims to promote the adoption of <strong>shared integrity standards</strong> in port operations and supply chain processes. Within this scope:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Integrity Training</strong> programs are being expanded,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>a structured platform for public–private sector dialogue</strong> is being created,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">MACN's critical <strong>HelpDesk</strong> system is being deployed.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>HelpDesk</strong> provides a mechanism through which ship agents and operators can <strong>escalate corruption requests in real time</strong> during port calls. This tool aims to enable companies to receive institutional and collective support rather than having to act alone.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Malaysia: Expansion of the Port Klang Pilot</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Malaysia</strong> represents not a fresh start for MACN, but rather <strong>an area where existing work is being expanded.</strong> The pilot initiative previously conducted at <strong>Port Klang</strong> is being <strong>extended to multiple ports and supply chain actors</strong> under this new program.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Planned activities include:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>training programs</strong> for industry players,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>deepening engagement with public authorities,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">support for translating <strong>regulations and rules into real-world field implementation</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">MACN notes that this approach aims to create <strong>practical behavioral change</strong> rather than merely theoretical alignment.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Germany: Compliance and Sanctions-Focused Approach</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">In the <strong>Germany</strong> component of the program, the focus is more on <strong>sector-based compliance</strong> and corporate governance structures. MACN is:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">developing <strong>sector-specific training modules,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">strengthening long-term support mechanisms under the <strong>Associate Membership</strong> model,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">providing guidance particularly on <strong>sanctions regimes, internal integrity systems and risk management.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This structure aims to make operators in Europe more resilient in the face of increasingly complex <strong>sanctions regimes</strong> and compliance obligations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Collective Action Model and Systemic Impact</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">At the heart of the program lies MACN's core working principle: the <strong>Collective Action framework.</strong> This model brings together:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">global maritime companies,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">SMEs,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">port authorities,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">public agencies and</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">civil society organizations</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">around the same table. Müller Torbrand notes that through <strong>data-driven dialogue</strong> and <strong>shared principles,</strong> <strong>lasting behavioral change</strong> can be achieved at a scale that individual companies cannot accomplish on their own.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Long-Term Goals and Global Context</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">MACN views this initiative as a critical step toward <strong>the organization's long-term goal</strong> of reducing corruption in maritime trade and <strong>creating a level playing field for compliant operators.</strong> Starting in 2011 with a small group of maritime companies, MACN has grown into <strong>a global network with over 225 members.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">While the new program concentrates on the Asia–Europe axis, the outputs generated and tools developed are intended to be <strong>applicable on a global scale.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Overall Assessment</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">MACN's new anti-corruption program covering Asia and Europe demonstrates that transparency in maritime supply chains is now no longer merely an ethical expectation but an <strong>operational necessity.</strong> The financial support from Siemens Integrity Initiative and the multi-stakeholder approach strengthen the program's potential to create not just awareness but <strong>measurable and lasting impact.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>MACN</strong> has launched a new three-year anti-corruption program in Asia and Europe.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The program is supported by <strong>Siemens Integrity Initiative.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Focus countries: <strong>Indonesia, Malaysia and Germany.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>HelpDesk</strong> provides real-time support for corruption incidents encountered in the field.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Operations are conducted around the <strong>Collective Action</strong> model.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">MACN's network has reached <strong>over 225 global members.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
