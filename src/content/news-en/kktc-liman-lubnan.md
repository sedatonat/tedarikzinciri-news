<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus (TRNC)</strong> has protested the re-signing of a maritime jurisdiction delimitation agreement between the <strong>Cyprus Administration</strong> and <strong>Lebanon</strong>, which was initialed in 2007, at a ceremony held on November 26 in <strong>Beirut</strong>. The agreement returned to the agenda following Lebanon's government approval of the document on October 23, 2025, after approximately <strong>18 years</strong> of waiting. The signing ceremony took place during a visit to Lebanon by Cyprus Administration leader <strong>Nikos Hristodulidis</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The TRNC characterizes the signing of the agreement as another link in a chain of unilateral actions taken by the Cyprus Administration in the Eastern Mediterranean since 2003. The statement emphasizes that these steps do not take into account the "<strong>equal rights and interests</strong>" framework of the Turkish Cypriot people. The TRNC maintains that the Cyprus Administration's unilateral maritime jurisdiction delimitation initiatives are "<strong>entirely null and void</strong>" and that the 2007 agreement falls within this category.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Referencing Cyprus's bicommunal structure, the TRNC argues that the management of the island's natural resources and maritime jurisdictions must be determined with the consent of both parties. The statement notes that all maritime jurisdiction arrangements made by the Cyprus Administration with "<strong>third parties, including Lebanon</strong>" are considered invalid by the TRNC. The TRNC emphasizes that this approach affects not only the rights of the Turkish Cypriot people, but also the balance and regional security in the Eastern Mediterranean.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The TRNC statement also makes an appeal to the international community. According to the statement, the TRNC calls for unilateral actions in the region not to be supported and demands that all states refrain from becoming parties to arrangements made without the approval of both sides of Cyprus. This demand carries significant weight against the backdrop of recent tensions in the Eastern Mediterranean caused by energy deposits, hydrocarbon exploration, and exclusive economic zone disputes.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Cyprus Administration, for its part, has long been conducting cooperation activities with neighboring countries in the Eastern Mediterranean on the grounds of <strong>maritime delimitation</strong>, <strong>EEZ agreements</strong>, and maritime security. Lebanon's approval of the agreement initialed with Lebanon in 2007 marks a new stage in terms of regional maritime jurisdiction arrangements. Lebanon's ratification of the agreement was delayed due to political instability in the country, economic crisis, and expectations regarding the future of energy projects.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The TRNC's protest is viewed as a reflection of longstanding debate on how Cyprus's bicommunal structure is perceived on international platforms. The statement reminds that the issue of maritime jurisdiction delimitation in the Eastern Mediterranean is not merely a technical maritime delineation exercise; it is directly related to political legitimacy, sovereignty areas, energy resources, and diplomatic balance.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">How the Cyprus Administration, Lebanon, and other actors in the region will position this agreement in the coming stages could affect the energy projects being pursued in the Eastern Mediterranean and regional maritime security policies. The TRNC's protest once again reminds the international community that the bicommunal structure of the Cyprus issue must not be overlooked.</p>
