<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Kazakhstan</strong> has formally reaffirmed its critical step in its transit strategy spanning Central Asia to the Middle East, keeping on the agenda its plan to establish <strong>a specialized transportation and logistics terminal</strong> at <strong>Shahid Rajaee Port</strong>, Iran's largest commercial port. During his address at the <strong>Kazakhstan–Iran Business Forum held in Astana on December 11</strong>, Kazakhstani President <strong>Kassym-Jomart Tokayev</strong> identified this terminal as one of the key projects of his nation's long-term logistics vision.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Tokayev, cargo volumes on the <strong>Kazakhstan–Turkmenistan–Iran railway line</strong> could <strong>double by 2030</strong>. To manage this increase sustainably, <strong>the Kazakh terminal planned at Shahid Rajaee</strong> is positioned as a foundational infrastructure element enabling rail-to-sea integration. President Tokayev emphasized that Kazakhstan sees itself as <strong>one of Eurasia's key transportation and logistics hubs</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Role in Eurasian Transit and the Iranian Dimension</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">In his remarks, Tokayev recalled that approximately <strong>85% of cargo shipments between China and Europe</strong> transit <strong>through Kazakhstan's territory</strong>. In this context, <strong>Iran</strong> is viewed by Kazakhstan not merely as a bilateral trade partner, but also as <strong>a strategic gateway providing access to open seas</strong>. Access via Iran to the <strong>Persian Gulf</strong> and broader global markets aligns with Kazakhstan's objectives to <strong>develop alternative transit routes bypassing Russia</strong>. </p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The planned terminal is expected to provide <strong>direct maritime access</strong> for Kazakhstan's exports while also supporting <strong>transit loads from other Central Asian countries</strong>. This structure will transform the Kazakhstan–Turkmenistan–Iran railway line into a <strong>multimodal (rail–sea)</strong> logistics chain.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Inter-Port Connections and Scope Expansion</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Kazakhstan aims to deepen cooperation with Iran not only through Shahid Rajaee but across a broader port network. Tokayev has prioritized strengthening connections among:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Kazakhstan's <strong>Aktau</strong> and <strong>Kuryk</strong> ports,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Iran's <strong>Amirabad</strong> and <strong>Anzali</strong> ports,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">as well as <strong>Bandar Abbas</strong> and <strong>Chabahar</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This approach aims to create alternative and flexible logistics routes along the <strong>Caspian Sea – Iran – Persian Gulf</strong> corridor.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Institutional Foundation: 2025 Agreement</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Shahid Rajaee terminal plan is built on an <strong>investment memorandum of understanding (MoU)</strong> signed in <strong>April 2025</strong> between Iran's <strong>Ports and Maritime Organization (PMO)</strong> and Kazakhstan's public institutions. Under this MoU:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Land allocation in the port's <strong>backshore area</strong> to the Kazakh side is envisaged,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Kazakh institutions are required to submit commercial and investment proposals <strong>within 30 days</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">An investment agreement is planned to be signed <strong>following PMO board approval</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>PMO interim chairman Saeed Rasouli</strong> describes Iran as <strong>"a gateway to open seas"</strong> for Kazakhstan and emphasizes that the country's <strong>1,800 km coastline</strong> offers strategic advantages for Central Asia.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Shahid Rajaee Port: Capacity and Signal of Confidence</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Shahid Rajaee Port</strong>, located near <strong>Bandar Abbas</strong> and adjacent to the <strong>Strait of Hormuz</strong>, stands out as Iran's most critical commercial port. The port handles <strong>75–83 million tons of cargo annually</strong>, accounting for <strong>85–90% of Iran's total container volume</strong> and <strong>more than half of the country's total trade volume</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The port experienced a major explosion and fire on <strong>April 26, 2025</strong>, in which <strong>at least 57 people lost their lives</strong> and <strong>more than 1,000 people were injured</strong>. However, Iranian authorities reported that days after the incident, cargo operations <strong>resumed gradually</strong>, with <strong>customs and railway volumes reaching or exceeding pre-incident levels by late April</strong>. Kazakhstan's decision to proceed with the terminal plan is seen as a clear signal of <strong>confidence in the port's operational recovery</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Broader Strategy: Non-Russian Routes</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Kazakhstan's Shahid Rajaee investment is part of a strategy of <strong>positioning logistics assets directly on key corridors</strong>. In <strong>June 2025</strong>, Kazakhstan opened a <strong>multimodal terminal</strong> at <strong>Poti Port</strong> in Georgia to support the <strong>Middle Corridor</strong>. Cargo transported via Poti reached approximately <strong>4.5 million tons</strong> last year, with mid-term targets of <strong>10 million tons</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Similarly, the Iran route creates a <strong>southbound alternative for Kazakhstan that bypasses Russia</strong>. Iran is also encouraging regional countries to invest in Shahid Rajaee and other Persian Gulf ports; <strong>Uzbekistan</strong> has already signed a separate memorandum for a logistics hub at Shahid Rajaee.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Overall Assessment</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Kazakhstan's reaffirmation of its plan to establish a special terminal at Shahid Rajaee Port demonstrates Iran's increasingly strengthened role in <strong>Central Asia's struggle for maritime access</strong>. If realized, the <strong>Kazakhstan–Turkmenistan–Iran</strong> rail-to-sea chain will strengthen, making Central Asia's access to Middle Eastern and global markets <strong>more diversified and resilient</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Kazakhstan has reaffirmed its plan for a <strong>special logistics terminal at Shahid Rajaee Port</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The terminal aims to strengthen the <strong>Kazakhstan–Turkmenistan–Iran</strong> corridor.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Cargo volumes are expected to <strong>double by 2030</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The project is based on an <strong>April 2025 MoU</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Shahid Rajaee handles <strong>85–90% of Iran's container traffic</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The investment aligns with a strategy to strengthen <strong>non-Russian transit routes</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
