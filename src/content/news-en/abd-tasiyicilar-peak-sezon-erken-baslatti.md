<p>Importers in the United States brought forward their heavy season shopping shipments earlier than expected this year ahead of the winter season. The primary driver of this early movement is new tariffs that the Biden administration plans to impose on certain goods imported from China. Increases were particularly notable in shipments to West Coast ports. This trend is occurring despite efforts to reduce reliance on China following the COVID-19 pandemic. The risk of a potential strike at East Coast ports also influenced importers' decisions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5>Key Points:</h5>
<ul>
  <li><p>U.S. shippers accelerated imports early to avoid new tariffs.</p></li>
  <li><p>Imports from China showed a 4.5% increase.</p></li>
  <li><p>Shipments were routed predominantly to West Coast ports.</p></li>
  <li><p>The risk of a potential strike at East Coast ports influenced import decisions.</p></li>
  <li><p>Peak season began in June.</p></li>
</ul>
