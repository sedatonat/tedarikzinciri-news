<p style="text-align: justify">HMM has recorded notable progress in its digitalization strategy by successfully completing three pilot projects aligned with the <strong>Digital Container Shipping Association (DCSA)</strong>, which sets digital standards in global container shipping. These pilots, conducted simultaneously with Genesis, Samsung SDS, and HelloContainer, demonstrated the speed, accuracy, and operational efficiency delivered by <strong>standard API-based data sharing</strong> in both Booking and Bill of Lading processes.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This initiative marked HMM's first effort to test DCSA standards with multiple ecosystem partners in a <strong>live operating environment</strong>. DCSA provided direct support in technical onboarding, integration facilitation, and stakeholder alignment, contributing to the seamless progress of the pilots.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>DCSA-compliant APIs accelerate standardization</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">HMM Digital Senior Manager <strong>John Kim</strong> noted that the DCSA Conformance system delivers significant value in testing the accuracy of APIs developed by carriers. According to Kim:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Conformance testing is just the beginning; real progress comes when parties implementing the same standard work together by running pilots."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">HMM believes that the completion of the three pilots established a solid foundation for transitioning to standard API usage in real operations and created important momentum in the company's digital transformation.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Pilot 1: DCSA Booking standard with Genesis</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The pilot conducted in collaboration with Genesis evaluated how <strong>Booking Confirmation</strong> processes could be shared between systems more quickly and completely.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Pilot achievements:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Supporting traditional <strong>EDIFACT</strong>-based processes with modern APIs,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Eliminating errors dependent on Excel, email, and manual entry,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Accelerating current information flow,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Reducing operational workload,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Providing important feedback to enhance DCSA guidance.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This work delivered notable improvement in existing systems where manual approval cycles frequently caused delays and costly downstream errors.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Pilot 2 and 3: Samsung SDS, HelloContainer, and FrieslandCampina collaboration</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The pilots conducted with <strong>Samsung SDS</strong> and shipper <strong>FrieslandCampina</strong> via HelloContainer tested how Booking and Bill of Lading standards perform across a broader supply chain.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Objectives:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Replacing fragmented EDI + email traffic with <strong>a standard API model</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Improving data quality between systems,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Eliminating inconsistencies in critical notifications such as ETA,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Reducing the need for repetitive custom integrations.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">HelloContainer currently uses the <strong>DCSA Track &amp; Trace</strong> standard and plans to bring Booking and Bill of Lading APIs live by the end of 2025.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>DCSA: "This is the beginning of scalable digital collaboration"</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">DCSA Chief Growth Officer <strong>Mariana Bock-Losada</strong> explained the significance of the pilots for the sector:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"When a carrier implements DCSA-compliant APIs, it can connect directly with any party using the same standards. No custom integration is required. True scalability becomes possible this way."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">This assessment underscores how API standardization provides a digital infrastructure <strong>compatible with the entire ecosystem at once rather than multiple custom integrations</strong> in the shipping industry.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">HMM successfully completed <strong>three DCSA pilots with Genesis, Samsung SDS, and HelloContainer</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Booking and Bill of Lading APIs were tested in live environments, increasing accuracy, visibility, and speed.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">EDIFACT + manual processes are being replaced by <strong>modern, standard APIs</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The pilots demonstrated that DCSA compliance is scalable in real operations.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">HelloContainer will go live with Booking and BL standards by end of 2025.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Standardization is making carrier–shipper–platform integrations <strong>faster, cheaper, and more reliable</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
