<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>FESCO Transport Group</strong> has launched a new container rail service from <strong>Chekhov</strong> in Moscow region to <strong>Blagoveshchensk and Khabarovsk</strong> in the Russian Far East. This is an important step strengthening the integration of Russian domestic trade on the east-west axis.</p><p style="text-align: justify">Trains run from the <strong>Chekhov Transport and Logistics Center</strong> to the stations of <strong>Blagoveshchensk</strong> and <strong>Krasnaya Rechka (Khabarovsk)</strong> <strong>twice a month</strong>. The <strong>transit time is about 13 days</strong>. The Group is also delivering containers from Chekhov to <strong>Chita</strong> — for the time being in a <strong>groupage format</strong> — later to be transformed into a full train service.</p><p style="text-align: justify">FESCO has also launched <strong>import block trains</strong> from its own <strong>Commercial Port of Vladivostok</strong> to Chekhov. These trains rapidly distribute imports from Asia into Russia's domestic market. The advantage of the two-way service is that it removes empty container repositioning difficulties and boosts bilateral trade efficiency. Chekhov is an important logistics hub in the southern part of the Moscow metropolitan area and a natural gateway for central Russian industry. FESCO's integrated service links Siberian and Far East rail networks seamlessly to the Moscow market.</p>

<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>

3. Groupage container deliveries also run from Chekhov to Chita.<br>
4. FESCO has started import block trains from Vladivostok to Chekhov.<br>
5. Two-way service removes empty container repositioning difficulties.</p>
