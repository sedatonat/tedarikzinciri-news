<p style="text-align: justify">The waves of blank sailings that defined much of 2025 on major transpacific shipping routes between China and the US are finally beginning to subside. <strong>According to project44 data</strong>, voyage cancellations that peaked in the spring months due to tariffs have retreated sharply in recent weeks, with carriers shifting to more balanced capacity management.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Throughout 2025, freight flows in both the China → US and US → China directions exhibited extreme volatility due to <strong>importers front-loading orders to avoid tariff increases</strong> and carriers making aggressive cuts to rebalance supply and demand.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>An 82–83 percent decline from spring peaks</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">China → US blank sailing rates have <strong>fallen 82 percent</strong> compared to peak levels in April.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Cancellations in the US → China direction have <strong>retreated 83 percent</strong> from their May highs.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This dramatic decline demonstrates that carriers have transitioned to <strong>more stable scheduling</strong> following the severe capacity constraints of the first half of the year.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Why was 2025 so volatile?</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Transpacific routes, which typically experienced monthly cancellations between 10 and 32 during 2024, effectively entered "swing mode" in 2025.<br>The reasons:</p>
<ol>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tariff shocks</strong> – New tariff announcements from the US administration</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Front-loading</strong> – Importers bringing cargo forward</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Carrier capacity miscalculation</strong> – Anticipation of demand weakness</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Expectations of new tariffs in September</strong> – Creating a second minor spike</p></li>
</ol>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, the new wave anticipated in October never materialized. Following the <strong>US–China trade ceasefire</strong>, carriers withdrew cancellation plans.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>From the project44 report:</strong></p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Planned blank sailing figures are tracking low as we move into November."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conditions remain more turbulent on other routes</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">While the China–US lane shows improvement, significant volatility continues on other global trade corridors:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>US East Coast → South Asia</strong>: Blank sailings <strong>increased 186 percent</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Asia → Canada</strong>: Blank sailings <strong>rose 80 percent</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>US West Coast → Europe</strong>: Blank sailings <strong>increased 60 percent</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">These increases suggest that <strong>demand for US exports remains weak</strong> and some countries are purchasing less cargo due to tariffs or economic slowdowns.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Q4 2025: More stable than expected</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">2025 was expected to be the most volatile year on record, but as we enter the final weeks of the year, the picture is more balanced:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Carriers have recalibrated capacity according to the new tariff regime.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The US–China ceasefire has eased market pressures by reducing cancellation plans.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Demand has stabilized as importers conducted heavy front-loading throughout the year.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>project44 assessment:</strong></p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Planned blank sailing rates are tracking low as we move into November. Carriers have adapted to the new environment."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Industry takeaway: "The storm has passed, but the waters remain choppy"</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The overall trend suggests stabilization as 2026 approaches. However:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Tariffs remain complex,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Carriers continue to revise capacity strategies,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Any new escalation in trade tensions could spark fresh volatility.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Nevertheless, blank sailing data confirms that <strong>the worst of 2025 has passed</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">China–US blank sailing rates have <strong>declined 82–83 percent</strong> from peaks.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">October proved calmer than expected; November shows low planned cancellations.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The US–China ceasefire was the primary driver in reducing voyage cancellations.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Significant volatility persists on other routes (particularly South Asia and Canada corridors).</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Carriers gradually adapted to the new tariff regime throughout 2025.</p></li>
</ul>
