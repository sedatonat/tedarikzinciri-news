<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Port of Rotterdam</strong>, Europe's largest port, has announced the launch of a comprehensive <strong>market consultation</strong> process to accelerate imports of hydrogen and hydrogen carriers (ammonia, methanol, LOHC – Liquid Organic Hydrogen Carriers). The initiative demonstrates the port's intention to take a central role in the energy transition and its readiness to assume an active position in resolving delays in hydrogen infrastructure development.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to port authorities, many companies are preparing plans to establish hydrogen import terminals; however, <strong>final investment decisions (FID)</strong> are being delayed significantly. This slowdown is hampering the integration of hydrogen into Europe's energy system. Hydrogen and its carriers are viewed as future <strong>alternatives to fossil fuels</strong>; however, uncertainties on both the supply and demand sides are complicating investment processes.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>The core challenge of energy transition: The chicken-and-egg conundrum</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Port of Rotterdam's statement, the largest delay in hydrogen projects stems from the market's classic <strong>"chicken-and-egg"</strong> problem:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Consumers (offtakers)</strong> demand price stability and guaranteed continuous supply.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Producers</strong> wait for sufficient demand and transportation capacity guarantees before investing.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This mutual hesitation makes it difficult both to secure financing and to commence infrastructure investments. In this consultation process, the port aims to identify <strong>all financial and non-financial bottlenecks</strong> perpetuating this cycle that delays investment decisions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Consultation process to run until January 16, 2026</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The market consultation process will continue until <strong>January 16, 2026</strong>. During this period, the port authority will:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">investors,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">terminal operators,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">hydrogen producers,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">energy suppliers,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">infrastructure investors<br>
gather feedback from all stakeholders.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Based on the information collected, Rotterdam Port Authority will determine <strong>how it can accelerate</strong> the development of hydrogen import terminals and <strong>through which instruments</strong> it can intervene. This role could take the form of financing support, infrastructure investments, risk-sharing mechanisms, or regulatory facilitation.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Other barriers complicating financing and implementation</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The statement also highlights other systemic challenges slowing hydrogen projects:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Electricity grid congestion</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Permit restrictions due to nitrogen emissions</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Uncertainty in public-private risk-sharing models</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Insufficient initial demand in volume scaling</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Long payback periods for investments</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">These barriers affect not only terminal construction, but more broadly Europe's energy transition strategy.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Rotterdam Port's role expanding further</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Port of Rotterdam is not merely managing infrastructure; it is also serving as <strong>a coordinator of industry and energy transition projects</strong>, functioning as a public company. The authority, jointly owned by the Dutch State and Rotterdam Municipality, is responsible for security, land allocation, infrastructure development, and the direction of energy projects in the port.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This new consultation process strengthens the port's goal of becoming Europe's <strong>gateway for the hydrogen value chain</strong>. Rotterdam aims to position itself as one of the primary hubs for green hydrogen production from the North Sea and hydrogen imports from the Middle East, Africa, and Scandinavia.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Port of Rotterdam has launched a <strong>market consultation</strong> to accelerate hydrogen import terminal development.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Objective: identification of financial and technical bottlenecks delaying investments.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The chicken-and-egg problem poses a critical barrier in hydrogen investments.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The consultation process will run until <strong>January 16, 2026</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Based on findings, the port may implement investment acceleration measures.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Rotterdam aims to become one of Europe's hydrogen hubs.</p></li>
</ul>
