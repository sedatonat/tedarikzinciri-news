<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> reports; analyst insight; as avian flu continues to spread through American chicken farms; the US is planning to import 420 million eggs from <strong>Turkey</strong> to fill supply gaps. According to <strong>CNN</strong> reports; the US expects to receive approximately 240 million eggs from <strong>Turkey</strong> by July — with the remainder due to arrive by year-end — totaling 37 million dollars in value. This marks a substantial increase from 2024; when <strong>Turkey</strong> — the world's fifth-largest egg exporter — exported 71 million eggs to the US. <strong>Turkey</strong> is the only country from which the US imports this refrigerated staple — while according to the <strong>American Egg Board</strong>; the US produces more than 7.5 billion eggs annually. The US <strong>Bureau of Labor Statistics</strong> (<strong>BLS</strong>) reported that the average retail price of a dozen eggs reached a record 8.05 dollars in February 2025.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective; the main egg producers and exporters in <strong>Turkey</strong> are the <strong>Central Union of Egg Producers</strong> (<strong>YUM-BİR</strong>; <strong>Ibrahim Afyon</strong> Chairman; <strong>Ankara</strong>); the <strong>Turkey Egg Producers Association</strong>; and key <strong>Turkey</strong> sector organizations. <strong>Turkey</strong> is among the leading global egg producers; with annual production of approximately 20 billion eggs. The main <strong>Turkey</strong> egg exporters include <strong>Lezita</strong> (<strong>Akdeniz Holding</strong>); <strong>Pak Chicken</strong>; <strong>Banvit</strong> (<strong>Banvit BRF Food</strong>; owned by <strong>BRF</strong>); <strong>Beypiliç</strong>; <strong>CP Bestpiliç</strong>; <strong>Şenpiliç</strong>; <strong>Erpiliç</strong>; <strong>Has Chicken</strong>; and are among the key <strong>Turkey</strong> poultry players — though for eggs; the regions of <strong>Karaman</strong>; <strong>Konya</strong>; <strong>Afyon</strong>; and <strong>Manisa</strong> are the main production centers. The <strong>Laying Hen Union</strong> is a key sector organization. <strong>Turkey</strong> holds a relatively resilient position against <strong>HPAI</strong> (<strong>highly pathogenic avian influenza</strong>) <strong>H5N1</strong> outbreaks — with high biosecurity standards serving as a major advantage. The <strong>Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry</strong> (<strong>Ibrahim Yumaklı</strong> Minister) is the primary <strong>Turkey</strong> agricultural authority.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective; the main egg producers and sector players in the US include <strong>Cal-Maine Foods</strong> (<strong>Sherman Miller</strong> CEO; based in <strong>Ridgeland Mississippi</strong>; US market share ~20%); <strong>Rose Acre Farms</strong> (<strong>Marcus Rust</strong>); <strong>Versova</strong> (formerly <strong>Centrum Valley</strong>); <strong>Hillandale Farms</strong>; <strong>Daybreak Foods</strong>; <strong>Michael Foods</strong> (<strong>Post Holdings</strong>); <strong>Vital Farms</strong> (<strong>Russell Diez-Canseco</strong> CEO; <strong>Austin Texas</strong>; pasture-raised premium); <strong>Pete and Gerry's Organic</strong>; <strong>Eggland's Best</strong> (<strong>Land O'Lakes</strong>); <strong>Happy Egg Co.</strong>; <strong>Nellie's Free Range</strong>; and <strong>NestFresh</strong>; among the key US egg players. The main federal authorities in the US include the <strong>USDA Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service</strong> (<strong>APHIS</strong>; managing <strong>HPAI</strong> surveillance and eradication); the <strong>USDA Food Safety and Inspection Service</strong> (<strong>FSIS</strong>); the <strong>FDA Center for Veterinary Medicine</strong> (<strong>CVM</strong>); and the <strong>CDC</strong>. The <strong>American Egg Board</strong> (<strong>Emily Metz</strong> President) and <strong>United Egg Producers</strong> (<strong>UEP</strong>; <strong>Chad Gregory</strong> CEO) are the key US egg sector organizations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective; the 2022-2025 US <strong>HPAI</strong> crisis — resulting in the loss of 168 million-plus poultry population due to <strong>H5N1</strong> avian influenza — represents the largest US animal health crisis in history. In addressing egg supplies to the US; alternative countries include <strong>Turkey</strong>; <strong>Brazil</strong>; <strong>South Korea</strong>; <strong>Poland</strong>; <strong>Lithuania</strong>; and <strong>India</strong>; serving as key potential export sources. The leading global egg exporters (by ranking) are: (1) <strong>Netherlands</strong>; (2) <strong>Poland</strong>; (3) <strong>Turkey</strong>; (4) <strong>Germany</strong>; (5) <strong>Spain</strong>; (6) <strong>Belgium</strong>; (7) <strong>Malaysia</strong>; (8) <strong>Brazil</strong>; (9) <strong>US</strong>; (10) <strong>China</strong>. The <strong>FAO</strong>; <strong>USDA Foreign Agricultural Service</strong>; and the <strong>International Egg Commission</strong> (<strong>IEC</strong>; <strong>UK</strong>) are key global egg data sources. Egg retail distribution in the US is dominated by <strong>Walmart</strong>; <strong>Kroger</strong>; <strong>Costco</strong>; <strong>Albertsons</strong>; <strong>Target</strong>; <strong>Whole Foods</strong> (<strong>Amazon</strong>); <strong>Trader Joe's</strong>; <strong>Aldi</strong>; <strong>H-E-B</strong>; and <strong>Publix</strong>. In conclusion; the US's egg imports from <strong>Turkey</strong> reflect how trade dependencies are being fundamentally redesigned in response to global food security and <strong>HPAI</strong> crisis — with biosecurity standards and <strong>HPAI</strong> resilience emerging as key strategic priorities for supply chain managers.</p>
