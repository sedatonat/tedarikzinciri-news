<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> video segment features <strong>Sean Stephens</strong>, Business Development Manager at <strong>Lyngsoe Systems</strong>, discussing with <strong>Wes Coleman</strong>, Industry Principal Warehouse at <strong>Zebra Technologies Corporation</strong>, why Radio Frequency Identification (<strong>RFID</strong>) has become so critical for today's distribution operations. <strong>RFID</strong> is not new — its roots extend back to World War II. So why discuss it now? "Mass adoption continues across many business segments," driven largely by customer demand, says <strong>Stephens</strong>. <strong>Stephens</strong> and <strong>Coleman</strong> detail five key benefits of modern <strong>RFID</strong> deployment in the warehouse: (1) enhanced tracking and visibility; (2) reduced labor costs and staff time; (3) improved accuracy and efficiency; (4) enhanced <strong>SKU</strong> visibility across the supply chain; (5) <strong>RFID</strong> tag prices have dropped from 30 cents in 2003 to just 3 cents today — while reliability has soared. "Real-time visibility across the supply chain is critical," says <strong>Coleman</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Lyngsoe Systems A/S</strong>, based in <strong>Aars, Denmark</strong>, is one of the world's largest providers of <strong>RFID</strong> and <strong>Auto-ID</strong> solutions for postal, baggage, healthcare, and retail logistics — <strong>Niels Aagaard Jensen</strong> serves as <strong>CEO</strong> of <strong>Lyngsoe Systems</strong>. <strong>Lyngsoe LIVE</strong>, <strong>Lyngsoe BAGGAGE</strong>, <strong>Lyngsoe POSTAL</strong>, <strong>Lyngsoe RETAIL</strong>, and <strong>Lyngsoe HEALTHCARE</strong> are the main product lines. <strong>Zebra Technologies Corporation</strong>, based in <strong>Lincolnshire, Illinois</strong>, with <strong>Bill Burns</strong> as <strong>CEO</strong>, is one of the world's largest providers of barcode printers, mobile computers, scanners, <strong>RFID</strong>, and location tracking solutions — growing through acquisitions including <strong>Symbol Technologies</strong>, <strong>Motorola Enterprise</strong>, <strong>Xplore Technologies</strong>, <strong>Reflexis Systems</strong>, <strong>Antuit.ai</strong>, and <strong>Matrox Imaging</strong>. <strong>Honeywell</strong> (<strong>Intermec</strong>, <strong>Datamax-O'Neil</strong>), <strong>Datalogic</strong>, <strong>SATO</strong>, <strong>Toshiba TEC</strong>, <strong>Avery Dennison</strong>, <strong>Impinj</strong>, <strong>Alien Technology</strong>, <strong>NXP Semiconductors</strong>, <strong>Identive</strong>, <strong>HID Global</strong> (<strong>ASSA ABLOY</strong>), and <strong>Smartrac</strong> (<strong>Avery Dennison</strong>) are major <strong>RFID</strong> and <strong>Auto-ID</strong> technology providers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>RFID</strong> technology standards include <strong>EPCglobal</strong> (<strong>GS1</strong>), <strong>EPC</strong> (<strong>Electronic Product Code</strong>), <strong>EPC Class-1 Generation-2</strong> (<strong>UHF Gen2</strong>), <strong>ISO/IEC 18000-6C</strong>, <strong>ISO/IEC 14443</strong> (<strong>HF</strong>), <strong>ISO/IEC 15693</strong>, <strong>NFC</strong> (<strong>Near Field Communication</strong>), <strong>Bluetooth Low Energy</strong> (<strong>BLE</strong>), <strong>UWB</strong> (<strong>Ultra-Wideband</strong>), and <strong>RTLS</strong> (<strong>Real-Time Location System</strong>) as primary standards. <strong>RFID</strong> frequency bands include <strong>LF</strong> (<strong>Low Frequency</strong>; 125-134 kHz), <strong>HF</strong> (<strong>High Frequency</strong>; 13.56 MHz), <strong>UHF</strong> (<strong>Ultra-High Frequency</strong>; 860-960 MHz), and <strong>Microwave</strong> (2.45 GHz, 5.8 GHz) as primary frequency ranges. <strong>Active</strong>, <strong>Passive</strong>, and <strong>Battery-Assisted Passive</strong> (<strong>BAP</strong>) are the main tag types. <strong>Walmart</strong> has enforced <strong>RFID</strong> tagging requirements from suppliers since 2022 — with <strong>Macy's</strong>, <strong>Lululemon</strong>, <strong>Decathlon</strong>, <strong>Inditex/Zara</strong>, <strong>Uniqlo</strong>, <strong>Nike</strong>, <strong>Adidas</strong>, <strong>Levi Strauss</strong>, <strong>Marks &amp; Spencer</strong>, <strong>Carrefour</strong>, and <strong>Tesco</strong> as major retail early adopters of <strong>RFID</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, Warehouse Management System (<strong>WMS</strong>) and <strong>RFID</strong> integration applications span real-time inventory accuracy (99%+ versus 63-78% manual), cycle counting efficiency (5-10K items per hour with <strong>RFID</strong> versus 100-200 items manually), and processes including receiving, put-away, picking, packing, shipping, cycle counting, physical inventory, and returns. <strong>Manhattan Active Warehouse Management</strong>, <strong>Blue Yonder Warehouse Management</strong>, <strong>SAP EWM</strong> (<strong>Extended Warehouse Management</strong>), <strong>Oracle WMS Cloud</strong>, <strong>HighJump/Körber WMS</strong>, <strong>Manhattan SCALE</strong>, <strong>Tecsys WMS</strong>, <strong>Synapse WMS</strong>, <strong>Logiwa WMS</strong>, <strong>Da Vinci Unified</strong>, <strong>Zebra Reflexis</strong>, <strong>Mecalux Easy WMS</strong>, <strong>Generix Solo</strong>, and <strong>Softeon</strong> are major <strong>WMS</strong> platforms. <strong>Locus Robotics</strong>, <strong>6 River Systems</strong>, <strong>Berkshire Grey</strong>, <strong>Symbotic</strong>, <strong>AutoStore</strong>, <strong>Exotec Skypod</strong>, <strong>Geek+</strong>, <strong>Hai Robotics</strong>, <strong>Quicktron</strong>, <strong>Fetch Robotics</strong> (<strong>Zebra</strong>), <strong>OTTO Motors</strong> (<strong>Rockwell</strong>), <strong>MiR</strong> (<strong>Teradyne</strong>), <strong>GreyOrange</strong>, and <strong>Vecna Robotics</strong> are major warehouse robotics providers. In sum, the insights shared by <strong>Lyngsoe</strong> and <strong>Zebra</strong> experts deliver a clear case study to industry professionals on the tangible business value of <strong>RFID</strong> deployment for warehouse operations.</p>
