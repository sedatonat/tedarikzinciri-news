<p style="text-align: justify">Leading global logistics companies including <strong>DHL</strong>, <strong>Scan Global Logistics</strong>, Austrian <strong>ÖBB</strong>, and Japanese <strong>Nippon Express</strong> continue to transport goods via Russian railways, according to <strong>Børsen</strong>. This development comes despite many companies reassessing their commercial relationships with Russia following the country's military operations against Ukraine.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DHL"><u><strong>DHL</strong></u></a>, one of the leaders in the global logistics sector, continues to transport goods through Russia. The company evaluates alternative routes to fulfill its commitment to providing customers with fast and reliable services, utilizing railway lines that pass through Russia. This strategy delivers both cost efficiency and reduced delivery times.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scan_Group"><u><strong>Scan Global Logistics</strong></u></a>, another major logistics company known for its flexible and customer-focused service approach, ships goods through Russia to meet customers' supply chain needs and gain competitive advantage. Russia's extensive railway network plays a critical role in the company's transportation operations between Asia and Europe.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Austria's national railway company <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austrian_Federal_Railways"><u><strong>ÖBB</strong></u></a>, one of Europe's largest railway operators, continues to transport goods through Russia. Owing to its strategic position in the Eurasian corridor, the company ensures seamless and efficient trade between Europe and Asia. By utilizing this route, <strong>ÖBB</strong> provides customers with fast and sustainable logistics solutions.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Japan-based <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nippon_Express"><u><strong>Nippon Express</strong></u></a>, one of Asia's largest logistics providers, conducts cargo shipments through Russia. The company develops alternative routes to counter disruptions in the global supply chain, supporting customers' business continuity. Russia's railway infrastructure strengthens <strong>Nippon Express</strong>'s connection between Asian and European markets.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Russia's military operations against Ukraine have triggered significant shifts in the global logistics sector. Many companies have reassessed their commercial relationships with Russia and sought alternative routes. However, major logistics firms such as <strong>DHL</strong>, <strong>Scan Global Logistics</strong>, <strong>ÖBB</strong>, and <strong>Nippon Express</strong> continue transporting goods through Russia, maintaining uninterrupted service to their customers.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">These companies' decisions to continue shipping through Russia are based on several factors. First, Russia's extensive and well-developed railway network plays an important role in trade between Asia and Europe. This route offers faster delivery times compared to maritime shipping and provides cost advantages. Additionally, railway transportation reduces carbon emissions and contributes to environmental sustainability.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Nevertheless, companies continuing operations through Russia also account for geopolitical risks and potential sanctions. As a result, companies update their risk management strategies, increase operational flexibility, and evaluate alternative routes. For example, routes such as the Trans-Caspian International Transport Route passing through Central Asia represent potential alternatives.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, the continued cargo shipments through Russia by major logistics companies such as <strong>DHL</strong>, <strong>Scan Global Logistics</strong>, <strong>ÖBB</strong>, and <strong>Nippon Express</strong> are important for maintaining global supply chain continuity and meeting customer demands.
