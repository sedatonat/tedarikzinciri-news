<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The United States</strong> signed a series of agreements <strong>strengthening trade relations in Southeast Asia</strong>, while simultaneously <strong>signaling a softening in trade tensions with China</strong>. The agreements concluded with <strong>Malaysia</strong>, <strong>Thailand</strong>, <strong>Cambodia</strong>, and <strong>Vietnam</strong> both <strong>establish reciprocal tariff rates</strong> and <strong>grant U.S. exporters access to new markets</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The agreements were announced during the <strong>ASEAN Summit in Malaysia</strong>. The White House announced that, following talks held over the weekend, it <strong>signed formal trade agreements with Malaysia and Cambodia</strong>, and <strong>framework deals with Thailand and Vietnam</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under these agreements, tariffs on goods imported from the U.S. have been established, while <strong>market access has been expanded</strong> for industrial, agricultural, and high-technology products from the U.S. by the respective countries. Additionally, pursuant to <strong>a Presidential Executive Order signed by Trump in September</strong>, countries signing agreements are subject to a <strong>0% tariff rate</strong> on certain products.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Trade Ceasefire with China on the Agenda</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>U.S. Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent</strong> and <strong>Chinese Vice Premier He Lifeng</strong> agreed on a new <strong>tariff agreement framework</strong> between the two countries during talks at the Malaysia summit. In a statement to <strong>CBS News</strong>, Bessent said, "We held discussions across a wide range of issues, from rare-earth magnets to agricultural products, and the fentanyl crisis."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under this framework, the <strong>100% additional tariffs scheduled to take effect on November 1 will be cancelled</strong>. China has also decided to <strong>postpone export restrictions on rare-earth elements for one year</strong>. Additionally, both parties agreed to <strong>extend the 90-day pause on retaliatory tariffs initiated in the spring</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Final approval was left to talks between <strong>Trump and Chinese President Xi Jinping</strong> on Thursday at the <strong>APEC Summit in South Korea</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Vietnam: Market Access and Digital Commerce-Focused Cooperation</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>U.S.–Vietnam</strong> framework agreement aims to provide U.S. exporters with broader <strong>market access</strong>. The U.S. will continue to apply a <strong>20% reciprocal tariff rate</strong> on goods imported from Vietnam. Vietnam committed to <strong>eliminating tariffs on industrial and agricultural products imported from the U.S.</strong> and to <strong>granting import permits for remanufactured goods</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The parties will cooperate on <strong>zero tariffs in digital commerce</strong>, <strong>acceptance of vehicles meeting U.S. security standards</strong>, <strong>reduction of non-tariff barriers</strong>, and <strong>strengthening supply chain resilience</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Thailand: Tariff Elimination and Agricultural Product Purchases</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under the <strong>U.S.–Thailand</strong> framework agreement, the U.S. will maintain a <strong>19% reciprocal tariff rate</strong>. Thailand agreed to <strong>eliminate tariffs on 99% of goods</strong>, <strong>reduce restrictions on industrial and agricultural products</strong>, and <strong>eliminate digital commerce barriers</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, Thailand committed to <strong>purchasing $2.6 billion annually in U.S. agricultural products (corn, soybean meal, etc.)</strong> and <strong>acquiring 80 U.S.-manufactured aircraft</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Malaysia: 220 Billion Dollar Trade and Investment Agreement</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The reciprocal trade agreement concluded with <strong>Malaysia</strong> encompasses both <strong>tariff rates</strong> and <strong>investment commitments</strong>. Malaysia will immediately eliminate tariffs on certain products and <strong>gradually reduce them over 5 to 9 years for others</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Malaysia also committed to <strong>$70 billion in capital investment in the U.S.</strong>, <strong>purchasing $150 billion in U.S. semiconductors, aerospace, and data center equipment</strong>. Additionally:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Annual purchase of 5 million tons of LNG (liquefied natural gas)</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Purchases of coal and telecommunications services valued at $204 million</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Purchase of 30 aircraft with 30 additional options</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">The agreement includes comprehensive provisions on digital commerce, intellectual property protection, worker rights, and supply chain security.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Cambodia: Broad Market Access and Tariff Transparency</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>U.S.–Cambodia trade agreement</strong> includes elements such as <strong>elimination of tariffs</strong>, <strong>termination of quotas</strong>, <strong>regulatory transparency</strong>, <strong>environmental and labor protections</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The U.S. will <strong>maintain reciprocal tariff rates for Cambodian goods below 19%</strong>, and apply <strong>0% tariffs on certain products</strong>.</p>
<p><br>Cambodia committed to <strong>providing non-discriminatory and qualified market access</strong> for U.S. goods, as well as <strong>eliminating import licenses and other trade barriers</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The agreement will take effect within several weeks, and either party may withdraw with six months' written notice.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Highlights:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>U.S.</strong> signed new trade agreements with <strong>Malaysia, Thailand, Vietnam, and Cambodia</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Progress was made toward a <strong>trade ceasefire with China</strong>; new tariffs were suspended.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Vietnam</strong> provided market access in digital commerce and agricultural products.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Thailand</strong> eliminated 99% of tariffs and plans to purchase <strong>80 aircraft</strong> from the U.S.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Malaysia</strong> made commitments in a <strong>220 billion dollar investment and purchase agreement</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Cambodia</strong> eliminated quotas and tariff barriers for U.S. products.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The new agreements also cover areas such as <strong>digital commerce, intellectual property, and supply chain resilience</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
