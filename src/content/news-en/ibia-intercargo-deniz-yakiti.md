<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The International Bunker Industry Association (IBIA)</strong> and the <strong>International Association of Dry Bulk Shipowners (INTERCARGO)</strong> have signed a <strong>reciprocal membership</strong> agreement to deepen collaboration in the marine fuels sector.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new agreement <strong>formalises a close working relationship between the two organisations</strong> on <strong>fuel quality, safety, efficiency and decarbonisation</strong> and <strong>aims to support the sustainable transformation of maritime shipping</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Objective: Safe, efficient and low-carbon operations</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Through this agreement, IBIA and INTERCARGO will gain <strong>broader access to each other's areas of expertise, activities and working groups</strong>. In doing so, both parties will <strong>strengthen dialogue between the fuel supply chain and shipowner communities</strong>, thereby <strong>supporting the development of safer and more sustainable bunker practices</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The agreement aims particularly to provide <strong>more coordinated representation</strong> on <strong>fuel policies, alternative energy sources and carbon reduction</strong> at global platforms <strong>such as the International Maritime Organization (IMO)</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Information sharing and best practices</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The reciprocal membership will offer the parties opportunities for <strong>information exchange, technical expertise sharing and dissemination of best practices</strong>. Additionally, the technical committees of IBIA and INTERCARGO will establish <strong>joint working groups</strong> on matters such as <strong>fuel quality and safety</strong>, <strong>standardisation of alternative fuels</strong>, <strong>fuel sampling</strong> and <strong>bunkering operations</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Industry bridge: Shipowners and fuel suppliers</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The collaboration between IBIA and INTERCARGO also aims to <strong>strengthen communication between shipowners and the fuel supply chain</strong>. Under this framework, the two organisations will work together on areas such as <strong>commercialisation of low and zero-carbon fuels, safe storage and development of bunkering infrastructure</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Moreover, this partnership is also regarded as an important step in <strong>reducing fuel-related safety incidents</strong> and <strong>harmonising international standards on fuel quality</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Statements from leaders</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">IBIA Executive Director <strong>Alexander Prokopakis</strong> commented on the collaboration as follows:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"We are delighted to welcome INTERCARGO to the IBIA family. As our sector undergoes transformation with new fuels and technologies, collaboration plays a critical role. This agreement will enable us to work hand in hand to develop safe, sustainable and pragmatic solutions."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">INTERCARGO Technical Committee Chairman <strong>Dimitris Monioudis</strong> said:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"INTERCARGO places great importance on dialogue and collaboration with all stakeholders for a sustainable dry bulk shipping sector. With IBIA as a member, we will gain access to deep technical knowledge on current and future fuel types.<br>
This collaboration will help our members develop safe and pragmatic transition strategies."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Shared vision in the decarbonisation process</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">In the maritime shipping sector, alternative fuels and new energy technologies are increasingly important as the industry works towards <strong>the IMO's 2050 zero-carbon targets</strong>. However, <strong>gaps in fuel quality, safety and supply chain infrastructure</strong> complicate the sector's transformation.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">By enabling <strong>fuel suppliers (bunker operators)</strong> and <strong>shipowner communities</strong> to work on the same platform, this agreement will <strong>contribute to making the transition to decarbonisation safer and more efficient</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, the IBIA–INTERCARGO partnership can accelerate maritime shipping's transformation by <strong>creating synergies</strong> in areas such as <strong>fuel standardisation, safety management and operational applicability of alternative fuels</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>IBIA</strong> and <strong>INTERCARGO</strong> have strengthened collaboration by signing a <strong>reciprocal membership agreement</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Objective: <strong>Joint work on fuel quality, safety, emission reduction and decarbonisation processes</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The agreement will provide <strong>more coordinated representation before the IMO and alignment of policies</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The partnership will <strong>strengthen communication between shipowners and bunker suppliers</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>IBIA:</strong> "We must develop safe and sustainable solutions for new fuels."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>INTERCARGO:</strong> "Technical knowledge sharing on fuel safety and sustainability is vital."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">This collaboration is viewed as a significant milestone in <strong>the transition to low and zero-carbon fuels in maritime shipping</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
