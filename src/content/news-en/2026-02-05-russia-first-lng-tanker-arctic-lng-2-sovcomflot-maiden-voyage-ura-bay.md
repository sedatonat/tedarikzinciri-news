<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>first LNG tanker built in Russia</strong> has completed its maiden voyage. According to <strong>Sovcomflot</strong>, the vessel loaded liquefied natural gas produced under the <strong>Arctic LNG 2 project</strong> in the <strong>Ob Bay</strong> and discharged it to the floating storage facility in the <strong>Ura Bay on February 3</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The gas carrier made a transit through the <strong>Northern Sea Route</strong> in the harsh Arctic conditions, moving at an <strong>average speed of 8.7 knots</strong> in the ice assisted by an icebreaker. During the NSR transit, <strong>preliminary tests of moving in the ice unassisted</strong> were made, which proved <strong>superior ice navigation capability compared to the previous generation Arc7 gas carriers of the "Christophe de Margerie" class</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The Arctic LNG 2 project is being built under Novatek's leadership as a natural gas liquefaction facility on the Yamal Peninsula. The project holds strategic importance for Russia's LNG exports to Asian markets. However, US sanctions have pressured the project's international financing and technology sourcing. Russian-built LNG tankers were developed to circumvent these sanctions and deepen the domestic supply chain. Domestic shipyard performance offers the potential for more voyages and direct deliveries to Asian customers in the 2026 season.</p>
