<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>FedEx</strong> announced it will extend its use of <strong>artificial intelligence (AI)</strong> to physical assets such as <strong>RFID sensors</strong> and <strong>robotic systems</strong> over the coming years. The company already optimizes <strong>100,000 first- and last-mile routes</strong> per day with AI and aims to embed AI in more than <strong>50%</strong> of its core operational workflows by 2028.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to data and AI lead Bala Vaidyanathan, RFID sensors are being tested on select customers' packages; the predictive maintenance platform <strong>MOBIUS</strong> has been deployed across 41 surface operations facilities and has prevented <strong>17,000 hours</strong> of potential downtime. The company says the platform delivers <strong>$10 million</strong> in annual savings.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">FedEx will also scale its <strong>Berkshire Grey</strong>-based autonomous <strong>trailer unloading system (Scoop)</strong> to thousands of dock doors across more than 20 U.S. hubs. As the <strong>Network 2.0</strong> transformation merges Ground and Express, agentic AI will increasingly generate weather-related rerouting recommendations automatically.</p>
