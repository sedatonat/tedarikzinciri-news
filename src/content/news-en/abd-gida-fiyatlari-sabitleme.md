<p style="text-align: justify">U.S. President <strong>Donald Trump</strong> has directed the launch of a comprehensive federal investigation into alleged <strong>price fixing</strong> and other <strong>anti-competitive practices</strong> within U.S. food supply chains, responding to mounting political and consumer pressure over rapidly rising grocery prices. According to <strong>Food Dive</strong>, the presidential executive order calls for the establishment of specialized <strong>task forces</strong> within the <strong>Department of Justice (DOJ)</strong> and <strong>Federal Trade Commission (FTC)</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The signed <strong>executive order</strong> extends beyond investigatory authority alone. The directive grants the DOJ and FTC broad powers to initiate <strong>enforcement actions</strong> against companies, propose new <strong>regulatory approaches</strong>, and even launch <strong>criminal proceedings</strong> where warranted. <strong>Grand jury investigations</strong> may be activated if evidence warrants. In this respect, the investigation is regarded as one of the most aggressive competitive actions directed at the U.S. food sector in recent years.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Background: Accelerating Food Inflation</strong></h3>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The Trump administration faces intense criticism over accelerating food inflation, particularly during 2024–2025. According to <strong>Urban Institute</strong> data, U.S. consumers are paying <strong>32% more per month for food compared to 2019</strong>. Price increases in meat, coffee, and essential food items have brought structural vulnerabilities in the food supply chain back into focus.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In the presidential executive order, Trump specifically identified <strong>meat processing</strong>, <strong>seed</strong>, <strong>fertilizer</strong>, and <strong>equipment</strong> sectors as carrying "<strong>vulnerabilities</strong>" to price fixing and competition violations. Trump further emphasized that anti-competitive conduct by <strong>foreign-controlled corporations</strong> poses "<strong>a particular threat to the stability and accessibility of America's food supply</strong>."</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Focus on Foreign-Controlled Companies</strong></h3>\n<p style="text-align: justify">One notable aspect of the investigation is its particular emphasis on foreign-controlled food companies. Two major players in the U.S. meat sector have long faced scrutiny over foreign ownership:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>JBS</strong>: Brazil-based, one of the world's largest beef producers.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Smithfield Foods</strong>: The U.S.'s largest pork producer, controlled by China-based <strong>WH Group</strong>.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">WH Group decided this year to spin off Smithfield's <strong>North American operations</strong>. This move is interpreted as a consequence of growing concerns about China's influence over the U.S. agriculture sector.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Meat Sector on Defense</strong></h3>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The Trump administration's focus on meat processing is directly linked to <strong>beef prices</strong> reaching record levels. The President recently rolled back some tariffs on products such as <strong>beef and coffee</strong> with the aim of reducing consumer prices.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">However, industry representatives contend that elevated prices stem not from <strong>cartels or price fixing</strong>, but from structural supply-side issues. <strong>Meat Institute</strong> CEO <strong>Julie Anna Potts</strong> summarizes the situation as follows:<br>\n <strong>"Despite high consumer prices for beef, beef packers have been losing money because the price of cattle is at record highs."</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">According to Potts, a tightening in cattle supply coupled with strong demand is eroding processors' margins. As a result of this pressure, <strong>Tyson Foods</strong> announced plans to lay off <strong>more than 4,000 workers</strong> and close one of its largest facilities. These developments indicate a <strong>profitability crisis</strong> is unfolding in the sector.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Legal and Supply Chain Implications</strong></h3>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The launched investigation is adding uncertainty to the U.S. food supply chain. Potential outcomes include:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>antitrust lawsuits</strong> against major food companies,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">tighter oversight of mergers and acquisitions,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">new regulations on pricing, supply, and contractual practices</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">These developments will directly impact not only producers but also <strong>distributors, retailers, and logistics providers</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic Assessment</strong></h3>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Trump's move reflects a view of food supply chains as no longer merely an economic domain but also a matter of <strong>politics and national security</strong>. Should the investigation uncover serious violations, a <strong>redefinition of the competitive regime</strong> in the U.S. food sector could come into play. Otherwise, the debate may shift toward the structural causes of food inflation rooted in supply, production, and agricultural policies.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h3>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Trump launched a federal investigation into <strong>price fixing</strong> allegations in food supply chains.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>DOJ</strong> and <strong>FTC</strong> will establish specialized task forces.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Criminal investigations and <strong>grand jury</strong> proceedings may be deployed if warranted.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">U.S. food prices are <strong>32% higher</strong> compared to 2019.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Focus sectors: meat processing, seed, fertilizer, and equipment.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Foreign-controlled companies face heightened scrutiny.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>----------</p>\n<p><strong>News Link: </strong><a href="https://www.supplychaindive.com/news/food-supply-chain-meat-price-fixing-investigation-trump-grocery/807301/"><u>https://www.supplychaindive.com/news/food-supply-chain-meat-price-fixing-investigation-trump-grocery/807301/</u></a></p>\n<p>--------------------</p>\n<p><strong>Author: </strong><a href="http://sedatonat.com/"><u>SedatOnat.com</u></a></p>\n<p>--------------------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
