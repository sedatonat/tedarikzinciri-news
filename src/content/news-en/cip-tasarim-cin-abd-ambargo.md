<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The U.S. Department of Commerce</strong> has imposed <strong>export license requirements</strong> on a broad range of products to China, including <strong>semiconductor design software, chemicals, machine tools, aircraft equipment, and fuel products</strong>, aimed at limiting China's access to strategic technologies.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br>
This move has the potential to <strong>further escalate trade and technology tensions between Washington and Beijing</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under the new restrictions, <strong>some previously issued export licenses have also been revoked.</strong><br>
The U.S. administration framed the move as intended to "block China's access to products critical to its key sectors."</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>EDA Software: A New Target in a Strategic Chokepoint</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">At the center of the bans are <strong>electronic design automation (EDA) software programs used for chip design</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br>
Firms such as <strong>Synopsys (SNPS.O)</strong>, <strong>Cadence (CDNS.O)</strong>, and <strong>Siemens EDA (Mentor Graphics)</strong> control <strong>more than 70% of the market</strong> in this sector.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to sources, <strong>official letters were sent to these companies last week by the U.S. Department of Commerce</strong>, requiring them to obtain <strong>a license</strong> before making sales to Chinese customers.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Although this is not a <strong>comprehensive ban</strong>, it was stated that <strong>each shipment would be subject to approval on a "case-by-case" basis</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br>
This will effectively slow China's access to advanced chip design tools.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Other Products Affected by the Restrictions</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new measures are not limited to EDA software.<br>
The restrictions also cover the following product groups:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Chemicals used in semiconductor manufacturing (butane, ethane, etc.)</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Machine tools and manufacturing equipment</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Aircraft equipment and components</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">In recent days, numerous U.S.-based suppliers have received <strong>notification letters from the Department of Commerce regarding the restrictions</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Corporate Reaction: Market Volatility</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Following news of the restrictions, <strong>Cadence shares fell 10.7%</strong>, and <strong>Synopsys shares dropped 9.6%</strong>.<br>
Both companies refrained from commenting.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Siemens</strong> announced it is "analyzing" the impact of the export restrictions.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br>
Synopsys CEO <strong>Sassine Ghazi</strong> stated in remarks to investors that <strong>the company has not yet received formal notification from U.S. authorities</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br>
Nevertheless, Synopsys maintained its 2025 revenue guidance, signaling <strong>an effort to preserve investor confidence</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Chinese market accounts for <strong>16% of Synopsys's total revenue</strong> and <strong>12% of Cadence's</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>China's Response: "The U.S. Is Weaponizing Technology"</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">China's Ministry of Foreign Affairs, in a statement to Reuters, said such measures <strong>disrupt global supply chain stability</strong> and amount to <strong>"turning technology and trade into weapons"</strong>:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"No sanctions or pressure can impede China's pace of development.<br>
No bullying can shake China's resolve for self-sufficiency."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Chinese state media outlet <strong>Xinhua</strong> noted in a report published last month that <strong>U.S. and European dominance in the EDA market represents a "critical chokepoint" for Chinese chip designers</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Domestic Alternatives and Market Response</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The U.S. export restrictions have triggered <strong>sharp gains in shares of Chinese EDA software firms</strong>:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Empyrean Technology (301269.SZ)</strong> shares rose <strong>17%</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Primarius Technologies (688206.SS)</strong> shares climbed <strong>20%</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">In 2023, <strong>Huawei</strong> announced it had developed its own EDA tools for 14 nm and above technologies.<br>
According to experts, such sanctions may accelerate China's <strong>drive toward technological self-sufficiency</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>White Oak Capital Partners</strong> investment director <strong>Nori Chiou</strong> offered this assessment:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"These kinds of bans are spurring China to rapidly develop its own software ecosystem.<br>
Much as occurred with semiconductors in the past, self-sufficiency in EDA will accelerate."</blockquote>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify"><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>The "Real Chokepoint": U.S. Software</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to a former Commerce Department official, EDA software is <strong>the genuine chokepoint in China's semiconductor design</strong>:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Without these tools, advanced chip design is not possible.<br>
The restrictions have been on the agenda for some time, but previous administrations shelved it as 'too aggressive.'"</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">These tools play a critical role in the design processes of chip giants such as <strong>Nvidia, Qualcomm, and Intel</strong>.<br>
Therefore, the U.S. move could <strong>trigger global restructuring in the EDA supply chain</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Geopolitical Impact and Possible Scenarios</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Experts believe this move will not only <strong>restrict technology access</strong>, but also serve as <strong>a bargaining chip in trade negotiations with China</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br>
The Trump administration appears likely to make <strong>export controls a diplomatic "tool"</strong> before moving to raise tariffs.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, in the short term, this move will mean <strong>revenue loss</strong> for U.S. companies and <strong>short-term operational delays</strong> for China.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br>
In the medium term, China is expected to <strong>rapidly expand its domestic EDA ecosystem</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: The Global Chip War Enters a New Phase</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The U.S.'s new export restrictions, targeting <strong>design software—a chokepoint in the semiconductor industry—</strong>elevate the technology war to the next level.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br>
This move could reshape both <strong>global supply chain dynamics</strong> and <strong>the revenue structure of U.S. software giants</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">As China puts the spotlight on firms such as <strong>Empyrean, Primarius, and Huawei</strong>, the U.S. trio of <strong>Cadence, Synopsys, and Siemens</strong> faces a more tightly regulated environment under global license controls.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The U.S. has imposed an export license requirement for <strong>EDA software, semiconductor chemicals, machine tools, and aircraft components</strong> to China.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Cadence (10.7% decline)</strong>, <strong>Synopsys (9.6% decline)</strong>, and <strong>Siemens EDA</strong> are directly affected.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">China's <strong>EDA dependence exceeds 70%</strong>; as alternatives, <strong>Empyrean</strong> and <strong>Primarius</strong> shares soared double digits.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Huawei</strong> disclosed it developed its own EDA tools for 14 nm chip manufacturing.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">China's Foreign Ministry: "This pressure cannot stop our development."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Analysts: The U.S. move has made EDA software a new <strong>"strategic weapon."</strong></p></li>
</ul>
