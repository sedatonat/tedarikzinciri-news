<p style="text-align: justify">China's major coastal ports are demonstrating strong performance as 2025 enters its final quarter. Despite persistent uncertainty in global logistics networks, <strong>Shanghai Port</strong> has surpassed the <strong>40 million TEU threshold</strong> over the first nine months of the year, reinforcing its world leadership position once again.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Shanghai: Strengthening its position as global leader</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Shanghai handled <strong>4.83 million TEU</strong> in September, recording <strong>13.6%</strong> growth compared with the previous month. This strong momentum has pushed the port's total volume since the start of the year to <strong>41.5 million TEU</strong>, representing a <strong>6.2%</strong> year-on-year increase.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This growth is noteworthy at a time when global shipping routes continue to operate around the Cape of Good Hope, tariffs remain on the agenda, and Red Sea uncertainty persists. Shanghai's performance is supported both by strengthened hinterland rail connections and a rebound in China's export demand.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ningbo-Zhoushan: Among the fastest-growing major ports</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ningbo-Zhoushan Port</strong>, ranking second to Shanghai, recorded <strong>3.83 million TEU</strong> in September with <strong>12.7%</strong> month-on-month growth. Year-to-date volumes reached <strong>32.5 million TEU</strong>, representing a <strong>9.9%</strong> year-on-year increase.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Ningbo-Zhoushan has captured one of the highest growth rates among Chinese ports, particularly thanks to strengthened regional feeder networks and enhanced ASEAN–East China trade corridors.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Broadly positive picture at other major Chinese ports</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The outlook at China's remaining major ports is broadly positive:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Qingdao:</strong> 2.8 million TEU (+6.5% month-on-month)</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tianjin:</strong> 2.22 million TEU (+3.3% month-on-month)</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Shenzhen:</strong> 2.63 million TEU (−12.4% month-on-month but +5.4% year-on-year)</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Guangzhou:</strong> 2.02 million TEU (−5.6% month-on-month; +5% year-on-year)</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">While Shenzhen and Guangzhou have seen monthly declines, year-on-year gains indicate that these ports continue on a strong trajectory overall.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Rising stars: Beibu Gulf, Yingkou, and Xiamen</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">China's mid-sized but rapidly ascending ports are also recording noteworthy growth:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Beibu Gulf:</strong> 840,000 TEU with <strong>12%</strong> month-on-month growth</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Yingkou:</strong> <strong>11.4%</strong> growth</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Xiamen:</strong> <strong>4.9%</strong> growth</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Dalian:</strong> <strong>2.2%</strong> growth</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Beibu Gulf in particular has become one of China's fastest-growing ports over the past three years, thanks to <strong>ASEAN trade corridors</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Overall outlook: Chinese ports remain robust despite global volatility</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">In 2025, global supply chains face:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">tariffs,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Red Sea/Suez uncertainty,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Cape of Good Hope rerouting,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Europe–US demand fluctuations<br>
yet Chinese ports are maintaining strong momentum despite these headwinds.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">The resilience of China's port system, the diversity of hinterland connections, and high levels of automation are supporting the country's export–import flows.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Shanghai crossed the 40 million threshold with <strong>41.5 million TEU</strong> in 2025.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Ningbo-Zhoushan recorded <strong>32.5 million TEU</strong> with <strong>9.9%</strong> year-on-year growth.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Ports such as Qingdao, Tianjin, Xiamen, and Beibu Gulf demonstrated double-digit or strong month-on-month gains.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Despite near-term declines at Shenzhen and Guangzhou, annual performance remains positive.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">China's overall port system is displaying <strong>high resilience</strong> in an environment of global uncertainty.</p></li>
</ul>
