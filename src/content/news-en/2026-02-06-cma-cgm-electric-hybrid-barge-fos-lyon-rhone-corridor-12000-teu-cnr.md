<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CMA CGM</strong> has announced the launch of an electric and hybrid inland <strong>barge project</strong> on the Fos–Lyon corridor, with an annual capacity potential of <strong>nearly 12,000 TEU</strong> and a targeted deployment within two years. The initiative marks a new step in the development of low-carbon logistics along the Mediterranean–Rhône–Saône (MeRS) axis.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The project was unveiled during a visit to Lyon by <strong>Rodolphe Saadé, Chairman and CEO of the CMA CGM Group</strong>, in the presence of French Minister for Transport Philippe Tabarot and Philippe Valentin, President of the Lyon Métropole Saint-Étienne Roanne Chamber of Commerce and Industry. It reflects CMA CGM's ambition to strengthen <strong>inland waterway transport in the Rhône Valley</strong> while improving the performance and sustainability of regional supply chains.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The project is supported by Compagnie Nationale du Rhône (CNR), which is developing dedicated <strong>charging infrastructure</strong> along the corridor. The combination of electric and hybrid propulsion enables barges to deliver zero local emissions on the Rhône. The solution both revives river transport and eases pressure on <strong>urban air quality</strong> by shifting freight off the road.</p>
