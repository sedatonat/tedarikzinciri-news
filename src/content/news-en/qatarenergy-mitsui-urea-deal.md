<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>QatarEnergy</strong> announced it has signed a <strong>20-year large-scale urea supply agreement</strong> with Japanese trading giant <strong>Mitsui &amp; Co., Ltd.</strong> Announced in Doha on November 10, 2025, the agreement will see QatarEnergy supply <strong>0.5 million tons annually starting January 2026</strong>. This long-term contract represents a key component of both Qatar's strategy to increase its influence in the global fertilizer market and Mitsui's objectives to secure food and agricultural supply chains.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Announcing the agreement, Qatar's State Minister for Energy Affairs and QatarEnergy CEO <strong>Saad Sherida Al-Kaabi</strong> noted that the partnership with Mitsui has been "further strengthened" through "decades-long strategic collaboration," stating:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"With growing global urea demand, we aim to increase our international production footprint to support food production and security."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Al-Kaabi also recalled QatarEnergy's plans for a new large-scale urea production complex announced in <strong>September 2024</strong>. Once operational, this facility will increase Qatar's urea production <strong>more than twofold</strong> and position the country as the world's largest urea exporter.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic Partnership: Long-Term Security for Agricultural Supply Chains</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to QatarEnergy's statement, this supply agreement reflects the company's <strong>strategy of establishing long-term relationships with leading global players in the fertilizer industry</strong>. Since urea is one of the most widely used nitrogen fertilizers in the global agricultural sector, long-term supply contracts are critical for food security.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Through its extensive trading network and strong position in the agricultural and chemical product supply chain, Mitsui will now have a <strong>stable long-term urea source</strong> for both Japanese and global customers with this agreement.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Qatar's Rapid Rise in the Fertilizer Sector</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">QatarEnergy is a central player not only in the country's oil and gas sector but also in <strong>petrochemicals and fertilizer production</strong>. Thanks to its cost advantage based on natural gas reserves, Qatar has a competitive edge in ammonia and urea production.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Once new investments are completed, Qatar will:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">become the world's largest urea exporter,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">emerge as a more dominant supplier in Asian and European markets,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">establish itself as a reliable source for long-term fertilizer contracts.<br></p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Mitsui: Multi-Sector Trading Giant</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Mitsui &amp; Co., Ltd.</strong> is one of Japan's leading general trading companies operating in numerous sectors including energy, chemicals, machinery, infrastructure, metal trading, food and logistics.<br>This agreement particularly strengthens Mitsui's position in:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">agricultural inputs,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">fertilizer supply chains,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">growing food demand in Asian and African markets.<br></p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Global Context: Urea Demand is Rising Rapidly</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">In recent years, worldwide urea demand has been steadily increasing due to:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">food production,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">growing populations,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">agricultural productivity requirements,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">geopolitical supply risks.<br></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">For this reason, the QatarEnergy–Mitsui agreement is not merely a supply contract between two companies; it is also viewed as <strong>a critical stabilizing step in the global food security chain</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">QatarEnergy and Mitsui have signed a <strong>20-year urea supply agreement</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Annual supply volume is <strong>0.5 million tons</strong> (commencing January 2026).</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The agreement aligns with Qatar's plans for a major facility that will more than double urea production.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">QatarEnergy is on track to become the world's largest urea exporter.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Mitsui secures long-term supply chains for agricultural and fertilizer operations.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Urea demand continues to grow on a global scale.</p></li>
</ul>
