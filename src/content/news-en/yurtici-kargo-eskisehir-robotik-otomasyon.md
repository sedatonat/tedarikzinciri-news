<p>Yurtiçi Kargo continues its technological investments in logistics. The Eskişehir Automated Transfer Center, the first of two major automation hub projects announced last September, has been officially opened.&nbsp;</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>The facility has the capacity to process 10,000 shipments per hour through automated systems and marks the first use of robots in the sector. Yurtiçi Kargo continues to take innovative steps in the logistics industry through existing and new projects. Following the Eskişehir facility, construction has begun on a larger capacity center in Ankara.</p>\n<h3>Definitions</h3>\n<ul>\n  <li><p><strong>Automation Hub:</strong> A facility where cargo acceptance, sorting, routing, and transfer operations are performed through automated systems.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Robot Deployment:</strong> The use of machines that perform operations through mechanical or digital automation in operational processes.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>IoT (Internet of Things):</strong> Technology that enables objects to communicate with each other and central systems via the internet.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<h3>Key Points</h3>\n<ul>\n  <li><p>The Eskişehir Automated Transfer Center has the capacity to process 10,000 shipments per hour.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Robot deployment in the sector was launched for the first time.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Yurtiçi Kargo is leveraging technology at the highest level to enhance operational speed and quality.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Transfer hubs are characterized as the heart of the logistics network.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The Eskişehir project enables sorting of mail shipments using robots.</p></li>\n  <li><p>A larger automation hub project has been initiated in Ankara.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n
