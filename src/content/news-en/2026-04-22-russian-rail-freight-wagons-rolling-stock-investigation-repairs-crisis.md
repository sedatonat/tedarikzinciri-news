<p style="text-align: justify">An official Russian investigation has highlighted that <strong>more than a tenth of all rail freight wagons are currently out of use</strong>. <strong>Wagon owners' incomes have collapsed</strong>, meaning there is no money to maintain the fleet. Analysts calculate that the <strong>repair rate would need to double</strong> to turn things around. Russia's rail sector under a war economy is under serious pressure both operationally and financially.</p><p style="text-align: justify">According to the report, freight volumes declining since 2022, the withdrawal of Western parts suppliers, and sanctions have accelerated the aging of the Russian rail fleet. <strong>Open-top wagons</strong> carrying coal, metal ore, and fertiliser, along with <strong>refrigerated wagons</strong>, lead the queue of units awaiting repair. <strong>Russian Railways (RZD)</strong> has stated that current repair capacity cannot meet demand and that operators have had to stockpile wagons at yards.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The investigation noted that this crisis, which threatens Russia's domestic logistics infrastructure, could add further pressure on rail freight tariffs. Wagon owners turning to <strong>Chinese and Belarusian</strong> manufactured parts as an alternative raises concerns about maintenance quality. The sector is calling for urgent measures to prevent disruption of coal shipments in the approaching winter. Rail freight, of strategic importance to the Russian economy, continues to be the backbone of energy and agricultural exports.</p>
