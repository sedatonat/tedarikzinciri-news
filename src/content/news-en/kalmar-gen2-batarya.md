<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Kalmar</strong>, a global player in port and terminal equipment, has rolled out its new-generation <strong>Gen 2 lithium-ion (Li-ion) battery</strong> system for electric <strong>straddle carrier</strong> solutions on a global scale. The new battery solution stands out for its increased energy capacity, improved <strong>thermal stability</strong>, and extended operating periods. With this move, Kalmar aims to accelerate its customers' transition toward safer, more efficient, and more sustainable <strong>cargo-handling</strong> operations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Gen 2 battery solution</strong> was developed specifically to meet the rising expectations of terminal and port operators seeking to transition to fully electric equipment. The <strong>advanced cell chemistry</strong> used in the battery offers a longer service life compared to earlier-generation battery technologies. This advancement directly benefits equipment owners by positively impacting <strong>total cost of ownership (TCO)</strong>, reducing battery replacement frequency and lowering long-term operational costs.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new-generation battery's <strong>nominal capacity</strong> has reached <strong>533 kilowatt-hour (kWh)</strong>. This represents approximately a <strong>25% increase</strong> compared to Kalmar's previous-generation battery technology. <strong>Usable capacity</strong> is rated at <strong>453 kWh</strong>. This capacity increase enables electric straddle carriers to operate uninterrupted for longer periods and provides operators with greater flexibility in shift planning.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The increased battery capacity extends the equipment's <strong>battery autonomy</strong> duration. Kalmar notes that this enables the concurrent application of different <strong>charging strategies</strong>. In particular, <strong>depot charging</strong> can be performed during meal breaks and supported by <strong>hands-free opportunity charging</strong> during shifts. This approach supports <strong>hot-seat operation</strong>, in which equipment can remain in continuous use across shift changes without downtime.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to information shared by Kalmar, electric straddle carriers equipped with the <strong>Gen 2 battery</strong> can provide <strong>up to 10 hours of net operating time</strong>, depending on energy consumption levels. This performance closely approximates a full shift operation for many terminal activities and reduces dependence on diesel equipment.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">With the introduction of the new battery solution, Kalmar has also integrated a <strong>megawatt charging system (MCS)</strong> into its testing processes. The MCS deployed at the <strong>test facility in Tampere, Finland</strong> is viewed as a significant step in terms of the role high-power charging infrastructure will play in future electric heavy-duty terminal equipment. This system aims to minimize operational interruptions by delivering higher energy transfer in shorter timeframes.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Marko Hopeaharju</strong>, who serves as <strong>Head of Horizontal Transportation Solutions</strong> at Kalmar, emphasizes that the Gen 2 battery represents a significant milestone for the company. According to Hopeaharju, the new solution represents a major advancement over previous generations in terms of energy capacity, safety, and operational lifespan. With longer operating periods and enhanced reliability, customers can transition to <strong>fully electric fleets</strong> without compromising performance.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From an industry perspective, this development demonstrates that the trends of <strong>electrification</strong>, <strong>decarbonization</strong>, and <strong>lifecycle sustainability</strong> are gaining momentum in port and terminal operations. Electric straddle carriers are emerging as a strategic solution, particularly for terminals with carbon emission reduction targets. Kalmar's Gen 2 battery investment demonstrates that this transformation is being addressed not only at the equipment level but also across energy infrastructure and operational models.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, the <strong>Kalmar Gen 2 battery solution</strong> expands the operational boundaries of electric straddle carriers and provides terminal operators with a longer-duration, flexible, and cost-optimized operating model. This move clearly demonstrates that the widespread adoption of electric solutions in port equipment will continue to accelerate in the coming period.</p>
