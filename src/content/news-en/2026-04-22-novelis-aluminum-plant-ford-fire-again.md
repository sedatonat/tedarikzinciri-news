<p style="text-align: justify">Aluminum supplier <strong>Novelis Inc.</strong> said employees were safely evacuated on November 20 after another fire broke out at its <strong>New York</strong> facility. The company, a critical supplier to <strong>Ford Motor Co.</strong>, said the fire is under control and teams are on site to ensure it is fully extinguished. Multiple local fire departments responded, <strong>Novelis</strong> said in a statement. This fire marks the latest incident at the facility that produces aluminum sheet metal for <strong>Ford</strong> vehicles, including <strong>Ford</strong>'s best-selling <strong>F-150</strong> pickup. A damaging fire broke out in September — halting production at the facility. <strong>Ford</strong> said production disruptions tied to the fire will reduce its adjusted profit this year by up to $2 billion. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Novelis Inc.</strong> is Atlanta, Georgia-based and was established in 2005 as a spinoff of <strong>Alcan</strong>'s rolling business. It was acquired in 2007 by <strong>Hindalco Industries</strong> (<strong>Aditya Birla Group</strong>, <strong>India</strong>) for $6 billion — making it the world's largest producer of flat-rolled aluminum products.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Local media reports indicate heavy black smoke clouds visible rising from the facility's roof, and say the fire originated in the facility's <strong>cold mill</strong>. <strong>Novelis</strong> declined to discuss whether the latest fire will impact plans to restart and accelerate production at the facility. <strong>Ford</strong> is still assessing the impact of the latest fire on November 20. "We are aware of the situation and are working with <strong>Novelis</strong> to learn more details," a spokesperson said. From a supply chain perspective, the <strong>Novelis Oswego</strong> facility, located in <strong>New York</strong>, is the largest aluminum sheet rolling mill in the northern <strong>U.S.</strong> — and is critical particularly for automotive-grade aluminum production. The <strong>Ford F-150</strong> has had an aluminum body since the 2015 model year — a global pioneer in the shift from steel to aluminum bodies. The <strong>F-150</strong> has been the best-selling truck in the <strong>U.S.</strong> for over 40 years, with annual sales of 700,000 to 900,000 units. <strong>Ford</strong>'s <strong>Dearborn Truck Plant</strong> (<strong>Michigan</strong>) and <strong>Kansas City Assembly Plant</strong> are the primary assembly facilities for the <strong>F-150</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the global aluminum industry's main players include <strong>Hindalco/Novelis</strong>, <strong>Hongqiao Group</strong> (<strong>China</strong>), <strong>Rusal</strong> (<strong>Russia</strong>), <strong>Rio Tinto Aluminium</strong>, <strong>Alcoa</strong>, <strong>Norsk Hydro</strong> (<strong>Norway</strong>), <strong>Emirates Global Aluminium</strong> (<strong>EGA</strong>, <strong>UAE</strong>), <strong>Aluminum Corporation of China</strong> (<strong>Chalco</strong>), <strong>Vedanta Aluminium</strong> (<strong>India</strong>), and <strong>Constellium</strong>. The primary segment distinction is <strong>upstream</strong> (bauxite/alumina/primary aluminum) versus <strong>downstream</strong> (rolled/extruded/cast/recycled). Key product categories include <strong>automotive grade aluminum</strong>, <strong>aerospace grade aluminum</strong>, <strong>can sheet</strong>, <strong>plate</strong>, <strong>foil</strong>, and <strong>extrusion</strong>. <strong>Novelis</strong> leads globally in <strong>recycled-content aluminum</strong> — recycling over 80 billion cans annually. <strong>Trump 2.0</strong> will raise steel/aluminum tariffs under <strong>Section 232</strong> to 25% (previously 50%) in February 2025 — intended to support <strong>U.S.</strong> aluminum production. <strong>Magna International</strong>, <strong>Constellium</strong>, and <strong>Aleris</strong> (sold to <strong>Novelis</strong>) are automotive aluminum component suppliers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the global automotive market's main OEMs include <strong>Toyota</strong>, <strong>Volkswagen Group</strong>, <strong>Stellantis</strong>, <strong>Hyundai-Kia</strong>, <strong>General Motors</strong>, <strong>Ford</strong>, <strong>Honda</strong>, <strong>Nissan</strong>, <strong>BMW Group</strong>, <strong>Mercedes-Benz Group</strong>, <strong>BYD</strong> (<strong>China</strong>), <strong>SAIC</strong> (<strong>China</strong>), <strong>Geely</strong> (<strong>China</strong>), <strong>Chery</strong>, <strong>Tata Motors</strong>, and <strong>Renault</strong>. <strong>Ford</strong>, with annual 2024 revenue of approximately $185 billion and based in <strong>Dearborn, Michigan</strong>, was founded in 1903 by <strong>Henry Ford</strong>. The <strong>F-Series</strong> pickups are a cornerstone of <strong>Ford</strong>'s profit engine — generating over $40 billion in annual revenue from a single vehicle family. <strong>Plant fire</strong>, <strong>supply chain disruption</strong>, <strong>single-source dependency</strong>, and <strong>concentration risk</strong> are fundamental elements of modern supply chain risk management. The successive fires at <strong>Novelis</strong>'s <strong>Oswego</strong> facility starkly highlight the <strong>resilience</strong> challenges in the automotive aluminum supply chain. <strong>BCP</strong> (<strong>Business Continuity Planning</strong>), <strong>dual-sourcing</strong>, <strong>geographic diversification</strong>, <strong>safety stock</strong>, <strong>scenario planning</strong>, and <strong>scenario planning</strong> are primary tools for OEMs' supply disruption response. In conclusion, <strong>Novelis</strong>'s second fire represents a repeated critical indicator of <strong>Ford</strong>'s aluminum supply vulnerability within two months.</p>
