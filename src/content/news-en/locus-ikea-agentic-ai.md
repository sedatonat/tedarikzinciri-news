<p style="text-align: justify">Ingka Group's (IKEA Retail's parent company) acquisition of Locus marks a new era in last-mile delivery technology. CEO <strong>Nishith Rastogi</strong> says the acquisition is "a strategic move that accelerates Locus's long-term vision," and the company will maintain operational independence while continuing to grow globally.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>IKEA's strategy: A single global logistics operating system</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">IKEA chose Locus because the platform combines:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Advanced route optimization</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Real-time visibility</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Intelligence in vehicle &amp; resource utilization</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Agentic AI-based decision automation</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">capabilities in one platform.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Locus is preparing to synchronize all of IKEA's delivery models — home delivery, buy online pickup in store, curbside pickup, in-store logistics — within <strong>a single global OS</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Rastogi, <strong>tandem deliveries</strong> (large product + assembly team arriving at the home simultaneously) are a critical innovation that enhances the IKEA customer experience.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, Locus analyzes vehicle capacity in real time to <strong>optimize load factors</strong>, delivering quick results in both cost reduction and carbon footprint reduction.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h2 style="text-align: justify"><strong>What is agentic AI? What makes Locus different?</strong></h2>
<p style="text-align: justify">Rastogi explains agentic AI through three components:</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>1) Input – Software learns the user</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">While traditional software expects users to learn the system language, agentic AI understands <strong>user intent</strong> in natural language.<br>
This makes technology much more accessible.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>2) Environment – Makes its own decisions in uncertain environments</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Classic systems work only within defined rules.<br>
Agentic AI, however:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">in uncertain scenarios,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">with incomplete data,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">under changing conditions</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">can <strong>make independent decisions</strong>. This is a major advantage in chaotic environments like logistics.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>3) Output – Produces action, not just recommendations</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">A traditional system "reports the problem," and humans solve it.<br>
Agentic AI "identifies the problem and solves it itself."</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Example:<br>
If there's an accident on a delivery route, the system instantly <strong>replans itself</strong>, without needing human intervention.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Real-world application: How agentic AI works in the field</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Rastogi, the role of AI agents is quite clear:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Reduces manual decision-making</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Optimizes routes on its own</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Responds <strong>in milliseconds</strong> to changes like traffic, closures, accidents, and weather</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Frees teams to focus on <strong>higher-level operations</strong> instead of planning</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">For example, even if Thursday's delivery plan is ready, if heavy traffic develops Thursday morning, the agentic system processes historical traffic patterns plus real-time data and executes <strong>automatic route updates across the fleet</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">These small changes at the micro level generate <strong>hours of planning savings</strong> at fleet scale.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Predictive analytics + Agentic AI: Adapting instantly to change</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Rastogi, companies still waste time relying solely on prediction-based systems.<br>
For example:<br>
New tariffs → new distribution network plan required.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Old way: forecast → analysis → decision → implementation<br>
Today: agentic AI → <strong>instant reconfiguration</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This prevents disruptions from having broad impact.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>The hardest disruptions to plan for</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Geopolitical shocks</strong> (tariffs, sanctions, wars)</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sudden shifts in consumer behavior</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Delivery workforce fluctuations</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The real impact of natural disasters</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Rastogi, the solution is not forecasting:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Build fundamentally flexible, self-adapting systems."</blockquote>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Will AI replace humans?</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The answer is clear: <strong>No.</strong><br>
Real value comes from "augmentative" AI that enables people to focus on creative and strategic work.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>The future of agentic AI: Living supply chains</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Rastogi, the biggest transformation ahead will be:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Supply chains will cease to be <strong>static networks</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">They will transform into <strong>continuously self-optimizing dynamic systems</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The rise of platforms like Etsy will increase pressure on <strong>localized 3PL networks</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Direct-to-consumer shipping will grow</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Agentic AI will become essential for local fulfillment</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The IKEA acquisition accelerates Locus's global AI logistics vision.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Goal: <strong>a single global logistics operating system</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Agentic AI: understands intent, works in uncertainty, produces its own actions.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Locus: route optimization + tandem delivery + capacity enhancement.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">AI doesn't replace people; it relieves them of heavy lifting to elevate their work.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The future: static supply chains → <strong>living, continuously optimized networks</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
