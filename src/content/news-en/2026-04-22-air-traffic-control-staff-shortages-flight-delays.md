<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>U.S. Federal Aviation Administration</strong> (<strong>FAA</strong>); is reporting staffing issues at airports in <strong>Nashville</strong>; <strong>Boston</strong>; <strong>Dallas</strong>; <strong>Chicago</strong> and <strong>Philadelphia</strong> and at air traffic control centers covering <strong>Atlanta</strong>; <strong>Houston</strong> and <strong>Dallas-Fort Worth</strong>. The staff shortages are a consequence of the <strong>U.S.</strong> government shutdown now in its seventh day — with effects spreading across the country. <strong>The Guardian</strong> reported on October 8 that staff shortages at <strong>U.S.</strong> airports are expected to cause further disruptions to air traffic, including cargo operations — and union leaders are issuing warnings for air traffic controllers and airport security screeners. On October 7, Transport Secretary <strong>Sean Duffy</strong> warned that the nation's air traffic control system is being harmed by the ongoing government shutdown. The <strong>FAA</strong> is slowing departures at several airports this week — as air traffic control facilities struggle to maintain staffing levels despite the federal government shutdown. Staff shortages include a high number of call-outs by workers at airports nationwide on October 6. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Federal Aviation Administration</strong> was established in 1958 — serving as the federal civil aviation regulatory authority under the <strong>U.S. Department of Transportation</strong> (<strong>DOT</strong>). <strong>Bryan Bedford</strong> is the <strong>FAA</strong> Administrator — appointed by <strong>Trump 2.0</strong>. The <strong>FAA Air Traffic Organization</strong> (<strong>ATO</strong>) manages the world's busiest airspace — handling 45,000+ daily flights and 16 million+ annual flights.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the <strong>National Air Traffic Controllers Association</strong> (<strong>NATCA</strong>) — the largest union representing air traffic controllers — stated in a statement on its website that it "does not approve, support, or condone" any federal employee participating in or supporting coordinated action that negatively affects the capacity of the <strong>National Airspace System</strong>. In <strong>Los Angeles</strong>, air traffic control at <strong>Burbank</strong> airport is closed and air traffic is being handled by another facility. <strong>Nashville</strong> airport is experiencing approximately two-hour delays due to staffing issues. Major <strong>U.S.</strong> airports include <strong>Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International</strong> (<strong>ATL</strong>; the world's busiest); <strong>Dallas-Fort Worth International</strong> (<strong>DFW</strong>); <strong>Denver International</strong> (<strong>DEN</strong>); <strong>Chicago O'Hare International</strong> (<strong>ORD</strong>); <strong>Los Angeles International</strong> (<strong>LAX</strong>); <strong>John F. Kennedy International</strong> (<strong>JFK</strong>); <strong>Las Vegas Harry Reid International</strong> (<strong>LAS</strong>); <strong>Orlando International</strong> (<strong>MCO</strong>); <strong>Charlotte Douglas International</strong> (<strong>CLT</strong>); <strong>Miami International</strong> (<strong>MIA</strong>); <strong>Newark Liberty International</strong> (<strong>EWR</strong>); <strong>Seattle-Tacoma International</strong> (<strong>SEA</strong>); <strong>San Francisco International</strong> (<strong>SFO</strong>); <strong>Phoenix Sky Harbor International</strong> (<strong>PHX</strong>); <strong>Boston Logan International</strong> (<strong>BOS</strong>); and <strong>Philadelphia International</strong> (<strong>PHL</strong>) — which are major passenger hubs.</p>
<p><br></p>

<strong>Chicago Rockford International Airport</strong> (<strong>RFD</strong>; <strong>UPS</strong>; <strong>Amazon Air</strong>); <strong>Oakland International Airport</strong> (<strong>OAK</strong>); and <strong>Ontario International Airport</strong> (<strong>ONT</strong>; <strong>UPS</strong>; <strong>FedEx</strong>) — which are major cargo hubs. <strong>FedEx Express</strong>; <strong>UPS Airlines</strong>; <strong>Cathay Pacific Cargo</strong>; <strong>Korean Air Cargo</strong>; <strong>China Airlines Cargo</strong>; <strong>Emirates SkyCargo</strong>; <strong>Qatar Airways Cargo</strong>; <strong>Lufthansa Cargo</strong>; <strong>Air France-KLM Martinair Cargo</strong>; <strong>IAG Cargo</strong>; <strong>Singapore Airlines Cargo</strong>; and <strong>Turkish Cargo</strong> are major global cargo carriers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the <strong>U.S.</strong> government shutdown occurs due to the failure to approve a <strong>continuing resolution</strong> (<strong>CR</strong>) or pass <strong>FY appropriations</strong> (<strong>fiscal year</strong>) budgets — forcing federal agencies to furlough "non-essential" personnel. <strong>Essential workers</strong> — including <strong>ATC</strong>; <strong>TSA</strong> (<strong>Transportation Security Administration</strong>); <strong>CBP</strong> (<strong>Customs and Border Protection</strong>); <strong>FEMA</strong> (<strong>Federal Emergency Management Agency</strong>); <strong>U.S. Border Patrol</strong>; and <strong>active military</strong> — are required to work without pay, though payment is made retroactively once the shutdown ends. <strong>NextGen</strong> is the <strong>FAA</strong>'s long-term modernization program to digitalize <strong>U.S.</strong> airspace. <strong>ADS-B</strong> (<strong>Automatic Dependent Surveillance-Broadcast</strong>); <strong>SWIM</strong> (<strong>System Wide Information Management</strong>); <strong>DataComm</strong>; and <strong>SBS</strong> (<strong>Surveillance and Broadcast Services</strong>) are major <strong>NextGen</strong> components. The controller staffing crisis is a result of historical hiring constraints at the <strong>FAA</strong> — 11,000 controllers in position against a target of 14,000. <strong>OAK</strong>; <strong>SFO</strong>; <strong>JFK Airport</strong>; <strong>Newark</strong>; and <strong>LaGuardia</strong> (<strong>LGA</strong>) are among towers chronically understaffed. As a result, the <strong>FAA</strong>'s <strong>ATC</strong> staffing shortages represent a concrete indicator of the direct impact of the <strong>U.S.</strong> government shutdown on the national airspace and air cargo flow.</p>
