<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>According to documents obtained by Bloomberg</strong>, the <strong>Trump administration</strong> is preparing a plan to <strong>open nearly all of the U.S. coastline to offshore oil exploration</strong>, despite objections from environmental groups and coastal states.<br>
The draft plan, being prepared by the <strong>Department of the Interior</strong>, envisions new oil and natural gas license sales <strong>spanning from the Atlantic to the Pacific, from Alaska to the Gulf of Mexico.</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The plan targets regions that Trump closed off during his 2020 election campaign with <strong>promises to protect tourism revenues in Republican states like Florida.</strong></p>
<p><br>
Environmental advocates characterize the initiative as "a step that exposes both ecosystems and coastal economies to the risk of oil spills."</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>All U.S. Coastlines Become Potential Exploration Areas</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>draft offshore oil leasing program</strong> prepared by the Trump administration is designed to cover 2026–2031.<br>
According to documents, the plan:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>U.S. east and west coasts</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Alaska regions</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Gulf of Mexico</strong> areas for <strong>oil and gas lease auctions.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Restrictions are still expected in some <strong>protected areas:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Northeast Canyons and Seamounts Marine National Monument (off Massachusetts)</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Papahānaumokuākea Marine National Monument (off Hawaii)</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, the inclusion of new areas near <strong>coral reefs and Arctic ecosystems</strong> has raised significant concerns among environmental organizations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Interior Department: "There Is an Energy Emergency"</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>A spokesman for the Interior Department</strong> did not confirm the plan in response to Bloomberg's questions but issued the following statement:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"The U.S. is facing a national energy emergency. All options are on the table to win the race with China in artificial intelligence and energy."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">This statement reveals that <strong>the Trump administration views energy production within the framework of strategic competition and security.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>New Opening 15 Years After Deepwater Horizon Disaster</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">This initiative comes at a time when concerns about offshore drilling safety remain fresh following <strong>the 2010 Deepwater Horizon explosion.</strong></p>
<p><br>
In the disaster, <strong>11 workers lost their lives, and 4.9 million barrels of oil spilled into the sea</strong> — the largest marine pollution event in U.S. history.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Now the Trump administration, citing <strong>"energy independence" and "economic growth,"</strong> wants to expand drilling areas <strong>beyond the Gulf of Mexico.</strong></p>
<p><br>
Trump has recently <strong>renamed the Gulf of Mexico the "Gulf of America"</strong> and described it as "America's energy field."</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Environmental Backlash: "Coastal Communities at Risk"</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Joseph Gordon of the environmental organization Oceana</strong> said the plan "strikes a blow to coastal protection efforts supported by both parties across the country:"</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Trump is ignoring the voices of communities, businesses, and local leaders opposed to offshore drilling.<br>
This action will put coastal economies and marine life at serious risk."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Environmental groups are preparing legal action against the Trump administration over issues including <strong>spill risk, climate impacts, and harm to marine ecosystems.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strong Response from States</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to documents, <strong>no state on the east coast</strong> indicated it wants to participate in offshore drilling.</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>States explicitly opposed:</strong> Delaware, Maine, Maryland, Massachusetts, New York, North Carolina, Rhode Island, and South Carolina.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Non-responsive:</strong> Connecticut, Georgia, Virginia, and New Hampshire.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Florida</strong> has not yet taken an official position.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">On the <strong>west coast</strong>, <strong>California, Oregon, and Washington</strong> have formed a unified front against offshore drilling.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Political and Industrial Realities</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Oil companies note that <strong>interest in exploration in new regions beyond the Gulf of Mexico is limited.</strong><br>
Data on potential reserves off the East Coast <strong>is confined to geological surveys conducted in the 1970s and 1980s.</strong><br>
Nevertheless, the industry states it "supports long-term opportunities for access to new fields."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This draft would replace the current leasing program, which included <strong>only three auctions planned for 2024–2029 under the Biden administration.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Political Tension: Contradicting 2020 Promises</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">During his 2020 election campaign, Trump <strong>banned new lease sales off Florida, Georgia, and the Carolinas through 2032.</strong></p>
<p><br>
As a result, the new draft plan represents <strong>a policy reversal that contradicts his earlier position.</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Biden administration, meanwhile, banned drilling in those same regions in early 2024, but Trump rescinded that ban <strong>with an executive order in January 2025.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: Drawing a New Line Between Energy Security and Environmental Risk</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Trump administration's offshore drilling initiative could represent <strong>a turning point in U.S. energy policy.</strong></p>
<p><br>
The plan prioritizes <strong>increasing energy supply and lowering prices</strong> while bringing <strong>environmental and political risks</strong> in its wake.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, both <strong>widespread state resistance</strong> and <strong>limited industry interest</strong> make the plan's feasibility uncertain.</p>
<p><br>
Nevertheless, the draft clearly demonstrates <strong>Trump's determination to make energy production a "national priority."</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Trump administration</strong> plans to open <strong>nearly all U.S. coastal waters</strong> to oil and gas exploration.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The plan is part of a new <strong>five-year offshore leasing program</strong> covering <strong>2026–2031.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Eight states, including Florida,</strong> are explicitly opposed to offshore drilling.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Interior Department</strong> defends the plan on the basis of an "energy emergency."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The plan could pose new environmental risks <strong>15 years after the Deepwater Horizon disaster.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The oil industry indicates <strong>interest in fields beyond the Gulf of Mexico is limited.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Environmental organizations are preparing <strong>to block the plan through legal means.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The draft represents <strong>a reversal of Trump's 2020 promise to ban coastal drilling,</strong> signaling a new energy strategy.</p></li>
</ul>
