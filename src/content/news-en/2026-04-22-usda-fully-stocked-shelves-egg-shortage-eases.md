<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> reports; analyst insight; U.S. Department of Agriculture (<strong>U.S. Department of Agriculture</strong>; <strong>USDA</strong>); states that supply chain challenges for eggs in grocery stores have &ldquo;substantially improved&rdquo; &mdash; as wholesale prices have declined ahead of the Easter holiday. In an egg market overview published on March 28, the <strong>USDA</strong> reports that consumers are &ldquo;once again seeing fully stocked shelves and enjoying a range of choices without purchase restrictions.&rdquo; The average wholesale price for a dozen eggs declined 8 percent between March 21–28 &mdash; and is down 63 percent from late-February record highs. However, the <strong>USDA</strong> notes that consumers are only beginning to see recently declining retail prices. The <strong>HPAI</strong> (<strong>Highly Pathogenic Avian Influenza</strong>) outbreak is receding.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the <strong>U.S. Department of Agriculture</strong> (<strong>USDA</strong>); <strong>Brooke Rollins</strong> Secretary (Trump 2.0); <strong>Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service</strong> (<strong>APHIS</strong>; the principal authority on <strong>HPAI</strong>); <strong>Agricultural Marketing Service</strong> (<strong>AMS</strong>; egg market reporting); <strong>Food Safety and Inspection Service</strong> (<strong>FSIS</strong>); <strong>Economic Research Service</strong> (<strong>ERS</strong>); are the principal U.S. federal agricultural authorities. Major egg producers in the U.S. include <strong>Cal-Maine Foods</strong> (<strong>Sherman Miller</strong> CEO; <strong>Ridgeland Mississippi</strong>; market leader &mdash; 20% of U.S. production); <strong>Rose Acre Farms</strong>; <strong>Daybreak Foods</strong>; <strong>Versova Holdings</strong>; <strong>Hillandale Farms</strong>; <strong>MPS Egg Farms</strong>; <strong>Hickman's Egg Ranch</strong>; <strong>Center Fresh Group</strong>; <strong>Sparboe Farms</strong>; <strong>Trillium Farm Holdings</strong>. <strong>United Egg Producers</strong> (<strong>UEP</strong>); <strong>American Egg Board</strong>; are the principal U.S. egg sector organizations. <strong>Vital Farms</strong> (<strong>Russell Diez-Canseco</strong> CEO; pasture-raised expertise); <strong>NestFresh</strong>; <strong>Eggland's Best</strong>; are the principal premium egg brands.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the <strong>HPAI H5N1</strong> outbreak in the U.S. during 2022–2025 is the worst avian influenza outbreak in history &mdash; with an estimated 168+ million poultry culled (according to <strong>USDA APHIS</strong> data) &mdash; with the egg-laying sector being the hardest hit segment. The <strong>Avian Influenza Indemnity Program</strong> is the U.S. federal compensation mechanism. At the beginning of 2025, the U.S. average retail price for a dozen eggs rose to $8.05 (February 2025 record; according to <strong>BLS CPI</strong> data). The <strong>USDA</strong> has signed import agreements with <strong>Turkey</strong>; <strong>Brazil</strong>; <strong>South Korea</strong> &mdash; to close the supply gap. <strong>Postobon</strong> (<strong>Colombia</strong>); <strong>Avipal</strong> (<strong>Brazil</strong>); are principal import suppliers. Major egg distributors in the U.S. include <strong>Sysco</strong>; <strong>US Foods</strong>; <strong>PFG</strong>; <strong>GFS</strong>; the principal foodservice channel players. Major U.S. retail egg buyers include <strong>Walmart</strong>; <strong>Kroger</strong>; <strong>Costco</strong>; <strong>Albertsons</strong>; <strong>Ahold Delhaize</strong> (<strong>Stop &amp; Shop</strong>; <strong>Food Lion</strong>); <strong>Publix</strong>; <strong>Wegmans</strong>; <strong>Whole Foods</strong> (<strong>Amazon</strong>); the principal chains.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the principal <strong>HPAI biosecurity</strong> measures include: (1) visitor control; (2) downtime (clothing change) requirements; (3) food/water contamination prevention; (4) isolation from wild birds; (5) cleaning and disinfection (C&amp;D) protocols; (6) quarantine zones; the principal measures. <strong>USDA APHIS</strong> National Veterinary Services Laboratories (<strong>NVSL</strong>); <strong>Iowa State University</strong>; <strong>University of Georgia</strong>; are the principal <strong>HPAI</strong> diagnostic laboratories. <strong>HPAI</strong> vaccine development is coordinated by the <strong>USDA Center for Veterinary Biologics</strong> &mdash; however, there is no approved <strong>HPAI</strong> vaccine for commercial use in the U.S. (due to export market impacts). The <strong>European Union</strong> (<strong>EFSA</strong>); <strong>OIE</strong> (<strong>World Organization for Animal Health</strong>; now <strong>WOAH</strong>); are the principal global animal health authorities. The <strong>FAO</strong> (<strong>Food and Agriculture Organization</strong>); is the principal UN agricultural authority. Principal egg consumption trends in the U.S. include: (1) cage-free; (2) pasture-raised; (3) organic; (4) liquid eggs; (5) egg whites (high protein); the principal segment trends. In conclusion, the <strong>USDA</strong>'s egg supply recovery report suggests that global agricultural supply chain fragility is being fundamentally redesigned &mdash; with biosecurity, alternative protein sources, and import diversification appearing as principal strategic priorities for supply chain managers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Points:</strong><br>
1. The <strong>USDA</strong> reports that egg supply chain challenges have &ldquo;substantially improved.&rdquo;<br>
2. Wholesale prices are down 63 percent from February records; retail price declines are only now beginning.<br>
3. <strong>HPAI H5N1</strong> has resulted in 168+ million poultry culled in the U.S. during 2022–2025.<br>
4. <strong>Cal-Maine Foods</strong> is the U.S. market leader (20% of production).<br>
5. The <strong>USDA</strong> is closing the supply gap through imports from <strong>Turkey</strong>; <strong>Brazil</strong>; <strong>South Korea</strong>.</p>
