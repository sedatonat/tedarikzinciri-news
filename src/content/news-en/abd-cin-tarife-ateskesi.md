<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The United States and China</strong>, following months of negotiations and mutual trade sanctions, have reached an agreement on <strong>tariffs, export controls, and logistics investigations</strong>. On the morning of <strong>October 30, 2025</strong>, at a summit held at <strong>Gimhae Air Base in Busan</strong>, <strong>U.S. President Donald Trump</strong> and <strong>Chinese State President Xi Jinping</strong> announced a <strong>trade ceasefire agreement between the two countries</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key provisions of the agreement</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The most significant element of the agreement is <strong>the U.S. reduction of certain tariffs on imports from China</strong>. In remarks to journalists, Trump stated that <strong>customs duties on import items linked to fentanyl trafficking have been reduced from 20% to 10%</strong> and that the decision <strong>takes effect immediately</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">A spokesman for China's Ministry of Commerce confirmed the reduction while disclosing that the U.S. will also <strong>suspend reciprocal tariffs applied to Chinese products for an additional year</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Nonetheless, U.S. Trade Representative <strong>Jamieson Greer</strong> emphasized that <strong>the total tax burden on Chinese goods will remain at approximately 47%</strong>:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Certain Section 301 and Section 232 tariffs remain in place. The general average is between 45-47%, and for some products, this rate can reach as high as 100%."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Easing of investigations and export restrictions</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The U.S. announced it will suspend its Section 301 investigation into China's maritime and logistics sectors for one year. This investigation had caused tensions between the two countries due to <strong>special fees and inspection measures applied by the U.S. to China-linked vessels at American ports</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Following this step, <strong>China</strong> announced it will lift its own retaliatory fees and port restrictions for the same period.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, China decided to <strong>defer the rare earth elements export restrictions it announced earlier this month for one year</strong>. In return, the U.S. will temporarily suspend <strong>export restrictions announced in September targeting companies with over 50% Chinese ownership, citing national security risks</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Agricultural and energy commitments</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">In a post on <strong>Truth Social</strong>, Trump stated that China has agreed to purchase <strong>"massive amounts of soybeans, sorghum, and other agricultural products"</strong> and <strong>begin a process to increase American energy imports</strong>. However, China's Ministry of Commerce stated that these commitments have <strong>not been formally confirmed</strong> and that negotiations are ongoing.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Both countries noted that the provisions of the agreement will <strong>be renegotiated annually</strong> and <strong>regular reviews will be conducted</strong> as needed.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Diplomatic messages from the summit</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The agreement was finalized during the <strong>Trump–Xi meeting</strong> held at the <strong>Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC)</strong> summit. This meeting represented the <strong>first face-to-face contact following escalating trade tensions throughout 2025</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">During his remarks at the meeting, Xi Jinping noted that it is normal for the two countries to <strong>"naturally have differences of opinion under different circumstances"</strong> and added:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"In a sea full of winds and waves, it is our responsibility to keep the China–U.S. relations ship on the right course. Cooperation will ensure this giant vessel moves forward safely."</blockquote>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Global impact and market expectations</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Economists note that this ceasefire will provide <strong>short-term relief on global supply chains</strong>, though <strong>full normalization</strong> will require a longer-term process. The suspension of restrictions, particularly on <strong>rare earth elements</strong> and <strong>high-technology components</strong>, is viewed as a positive development for <strong>U.S. manufacturing and the defense industry</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, given that <strong>tariffs have not been fully eliminated</strong> and <strong>trade disputes have not been fundamentally resolved</strong>, analysts characterize this agreement as a <strong>"limited ceasefire."</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Trump administration's move is also viewed as a strategy to <strong>relieve economic pressure from U.S. farmers and the industrial sector ahead of the 2026 elections</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The U.S.</strong> has reduced <strong>certain tariffs on imports from China from 20% to 10%.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Reciprocal tariffs and Section 301 investigation have been suspended for one year.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>China</strong> has deferred <strong>rare earth export restrictions for one year.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The U.S. has suspended the <strong>50% ownership rule restricting exports to China-linked companies.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">China has pledged to purchase <strong>large quantities of agricultural and energy products from the U.S.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The parties will <strong>renegotiate agreement terms annually.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The agreement was announced at the <strong>Trump–Xi summit in Busan</strong> and has created <strong>temporary relief in global supply chains.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
