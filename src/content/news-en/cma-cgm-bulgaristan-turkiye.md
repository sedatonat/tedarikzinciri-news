<p style="text-align: justify">French container shipping giant <strong>CMA CGM</strong> is launching a new feeder service between Bulgaria's <strong>Varna</strong> and <strong>Burgas</strong> ports and Turkey's <strong>Ambarlı</strong> and <strong>Gebze</strong> ports. The service aims to connect key ports in the Black Sea and Marmara Sea regions, boosting regional trade volumes.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The new feeder service will operate on a weekly schedule and be integrated into <strong>CMA CGM</strong>'s extensive global network. This will enable cargo from Bulgaria to reach various destinations in the Middle East, Asia and Africa via Turkey more quickly and efficiently. Similarly, cargo from Turkey can be transported to different regions of Europe through Bulgaria.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CMA CGM</strong> aims to provide customers with more flexible and faster logistics solutions through this new service. The company plans to contribute to regional trade development, delivering positive effects for both the Bulgarian and Turkish economies.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CMA CGM</strong> is launching a new feeder service between Bulgaria and Turkey.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The service connects Varna, Burgas, Ambarlı and Gebze ports.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The weekly service is integrated into <strong>CMA CGM</strong>'s global network.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The new service aims to increase regional trade volumes and contribute positively to both economies.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>---</p>\n<p><strong>News Link: </strong><a href="https://container-news.com/cma-cgm-feeder-service-bulgarian-ports-turkey/"><u>https://container-news.com/cma-cgm-feeder-service-bulgarian-ports-turkey/</u></a></p>\n<p>-------------------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
