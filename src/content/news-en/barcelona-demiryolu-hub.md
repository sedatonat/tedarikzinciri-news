<p style="text-align: justify">As security risks take centre stage in 2025 logistics trends, Europe's other critical axis continues to be <strong>rapid decarbonization backed by aggressive infrastructure investments</strong>. In this context, <strong>Port of Barcelona</strong> has secured final approval for the <strong>€265 million South Rail Hub</strong> project, set to launch one of the Mediterranean basin's most significant logistics transformations. Catalonia's administration approval of the <strong>Urban Master Plan</strong> formally initiates the port's long-prepared large-scale rail overhaul.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">At the heart of the project lies the repurposing of the city's former <strong>Llobregat riverbed</strong> area for heavy logistics operations. This zone, previously a flood channel, is being converted into a modern <strong>intermodal rail platform</strong>. The South Rail Hub creates a rail network with <strong>six terminals</strong> linking the port to strategic industrial zones such as <strong>Zona Franca</strong> and <strong>Polígon Pratenc</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The total <strong>€265 million</strong> budget breakdown clarifies the project's scope:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>€150 million</strong>: Construction of new terminals in the former riverbed</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>€70 million</strong>: Currently under construction <strong>Nou Llobregat Terminal</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>€45 million</strong>: Supporting infrastructure, connection roads, technical services</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>€26 million</strong>: Environmental-focused <strong>green areas</strong> investments</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This investment's strategic focus centres on the <strong>modal shift</strong> objective that European logistics circles have emphasised in recent years. Port of Barcelona aims to route cargo flows predominantly through <strong>rail freight</strong> rather than <strong>road freight</strong>. This approach offers a long-term solution both for reducing carbon emissions and overcoming mounting traffic congestion.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The rail shift performance in cargo handling through the port draws attention through the numbers.</p>
<p><br>
<strong>In 2024, rail operations</strong> removed <strong>155,000 truck journeys</strong> from the road system, delivering <strong>48,500 tonnes of CO₂</strong> savings. This impact has become even more pronounced over the past decade: the port has managed to eliminate <strong>1.7 million</strong> truck trips from the system. This transformation demonstrates that Barcelona operates one of Europe's most aggressive intermodal transition strategies.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The South Rail Hub plays a critical role not only in environmental impact but also in economic and operational resilience.</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Providing independent capacity against <strong>driver shortage</strong> risks</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Reducing pressure from rising <strong>diesel fuel costs</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Offering more predictable transit and lower CO₂ profiles to inland industries</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Expanding the port's door-to-door service capacity from 2026 onwards</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, the project strengthens Barcelona's position as one of the Mediterranean's <strong>"primary gateways"</strong> for ports. Particularly for cargo linked to China and India, it provides a more sustainable alternative to the cost risks of road haulage. Moreover, the expansion of the rail network accelerates the port's integration with Spain's inland market and Europe's northern corridors via France.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Considered alongside the security crises shaping the global logistics environment in 2025, Port of Barcelona's investment provides companies an inland solution that <strong>"reduces risk, lowers costs, and minimises carbon"</strong>. Thus, the port is establishing itself not only at the maritime interface but also in hinterland transport as one of Europe's most modern infrastructures.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The South Rail Hub project was approved with a <strong>€265 million</strong> budget.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The former Llobregat riverbed is being utilised as the new intermodal rail area.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The project comprises <strong>6 terminals</strong> connecting the port to Zona Franca and Polígon Pratenc.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">In 2024, <strong>155,000 truck journeys</strong> were replaced with rail transport, achieving <strong>48,500 tonnes of CO₂</strong> reduction.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Over 10 years, a total of <strong>1.7 million</strong> truck journeys were eliminated from the system.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">From 2026 onwards, Barcelona's position as Europe's primary Mediterranean gateway is strengthening.</p></li>
</ul>
