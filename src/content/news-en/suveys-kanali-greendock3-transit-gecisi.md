<p style="text-align: justify">The Suez Canal Authority (<strong>SCA</strong>) announced in December 2025 that <strong>GREEN DOCK 3</strong>, one of the world's largest towed floating docks, <strong>successfully transited through the Canal</strong>. SCA President <strong>Admiral Osama Rabiee</strong> emphasized that this operation proved "the Canal's ability to safely transit unconventional large marine units."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The transit of GREEN DOCK 3 required a complex <strong>special navigation operation</strong>. Because the floating dock does not move under its own power and is entirely maneuvered by tugboats. The operation was carried out with a total of <strong>five tugboats</strong>:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The tugboat <strong>PERSISTENCE</strong> towed the dock from the front,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Four SCA tugboats directed the dock from the sides and rear.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">The dock transited as part of the <strong>southern convoy</strong>. The journey, which began in Indonesia before the Canal transit, passed smoothly through the Red Sea and the <strong>Bab el-Mandab</strong> region.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Technical Specifications of the Massive Platform</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>GREEN DOCK 3</strong> is a colossal floating dock measuring 290 meters in length, 57 meters in width, and with an <strong>8-meter draft</strong> depth. Weighing <strong>42,000 tons</strong>, this structure ranks among the <strong>largest towed units</strong> ever to transit through the Suez Canal.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The transit lasted exactly <strong>24 hours</strong>, and throughout the operation, procedures including special traffic management, speed restrictions, tugboat coordination, and utilization of the Canal's newly widened sections were implemented.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Canal's Security Capability and Infrastructure Advantage</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Admiral Rabiee noted that this operation also conveyed an important message of confidence to maritime companies worldwide:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">That the Suez Canal is a <strong>safe and stable</strong> route,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">That the New Suez Canal is ideal for <strong>special operations</strong> thanks to its less winding, straighter, and widened canal structure.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">The widening of the Canal by 40 meters under the <strong>Southern Sector Development Project</strong> enables such large units to transit smoothly.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Economic Impact and Emission Savings</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Rabiee also explained the economic impacts of the operation.<br>
The Suez route is <strong>6,000 nautical miles</strong> shorter compared to the Cape of Good Hope. This difference:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Reduces distance by <strong>48%</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Significantly lowers fuel consumption and operational costs,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Prevents approximately <strong>996 tons of CO₂</strong> emissions.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">These savings enhance Suez's global strategic value for large-volume and low-speed-tolerance maritime operations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Performance of Operation Teams</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Rabiee praised the professionalism of SCA pilots and tugboat crews. <strong>Viktor Kishlian</strong>, captain of tugboat PERSISTENCE, stated in his remarks that the journey from Indonesia to Egypt passed safely and once again confirmed the Canal's "<strong>time and cost-saving</strong>" advantages.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Finally, Kishlian described the Suez Canal as "<strong>the best route between East and West</strong>," emphasizing its critical importance for global commerce.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">GREEN DOCK 3 is a massive <strong>42,000-ton</strong> floating dock that does not move under its own power.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">It transited through the Canal under escort of five tugboats in a <strong>24-hour special operation</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The Suez route is <strong>6,000 nautical miles</strong> shorter than the Cape route, with CO₂ savings of approximately 996 tons.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">With the widening of the New Suez Canal, large and unconventional units can now transit more safely.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The operation once again proved the Canal's security, capacity, and strategic importance for global commerce.</p></li>
</ul>
