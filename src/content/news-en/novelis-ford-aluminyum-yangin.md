<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Novelis Inc.</strong>, one of Ford Motor Company's most critical suppliers in the United States, faced renewed production disruption risk when fire broke out again at its aluminum facility in New York. The fire that occurred on November 20 was quickly brought under control, with the company confirming that all employees were safely evacuated. Firefighting crews responded with intense efforts, marking the facility's <strong>second major fire within months</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The facility produces aluminum sheet for a range of pickup trucks and commercial vehicles, including Ford's highest-volume model, the <strong>F-150</strong>. Consequently, any disruption at the plant directly impacts not only Novelis but also Ford's production and profitability plans. The first major fire in September led to factory closure, with warnings that Ford's annual adjusted profit could <strong>decline by as much as 2 billion dollars</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Local media reported <strong>dense black smoke</strong> rising from the facility's roof, with the fire originating in the "cold mill" section of the plant. While not yet officially confirmed, initial findings suggest the fire again started in the facility's metal processing line.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Novelis stated that fire suppression and cooling efforts were ongoing, but declined to comment on whether <strong>resumption of production and capacity expansion plans would be affected</strong>. This uncertainty is particularly concerning for Ford. In a statement, Ford said, "We are aware of the situation and are evaluating the impact with Novelis," indicating that the situation remains unresolved.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Timing is critically important. Ford's CFO <strong>Sherry House</strong> stated at a Barclays conference on November 19 that the plant was planned to <strong>reopen during Thanksgiving week or early December</strong>. This latest fire raises questions about whether the facility's restart will proceed as scheduled.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Novelis facility's importance to Ford cannot be overstated. The F-150 is among Ford's most critical models, both maintaining the company's dominance in the U.S. market and supporting corporate profitability. Any disruption in production could slow Ford's vehicle deliveries to dealers, affect inventory levels, and particularly pressure sales performance during year-end campaign periods. Moreover, recurring fires at such a central point in the supply chain may heighten investor questions about Ford's risk management capacity.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">How Novelis' production restart plans are affected will be a key focus in Ford's upcoming financial updates. A temporary shutdown carries the potential to create additional cost pressures on Ford's year-end balance sheet.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">For now, both companies have avoided providing details on the technical causes of the incident, but recurring fires make an in-depth examination of the facility's safety infrastructure inevitable.</p>
<p><br></p>

  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Ford's profit could <strong>decline by as much as 2 billion dollars</strong> due to the impact of the earlier fire.</p></li>
