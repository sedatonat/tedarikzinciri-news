<p style="text-align: justify">According to Libya's Ports and Maritime Transport Authority, a damaged Russian LNG carrier has broken free from its tow north of Benghazi, forcing Libyan authorities to issue an urgent navigational warning. The tow line attached to the Arctic Metagaz snapped on 22 April at about 15:00 local time during a towing operation, leaving the vessel drifting uncontrolled roughly 120 nautical miles off the Libyan coast.</p>

<p style="text-align: justify">The tug failed to re-establish the connection due to technical problems. Authorities warned that the vessel still carries quantities of liquefied natural gas and fuel materials, posing immediate risks to navigation and the marine environment.</p>

<p style="text-align: justify">Ships operating in the area were instructed to keep a minimum distance of five nautical miles and to report any changes in the vessel's condition, including leaks, emissions, smoke or structural deterioration. The incident marks a further setback in a salvage operation that began after the vessel sustained damage in early March.</p>
