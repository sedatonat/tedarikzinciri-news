<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Container traffic via Russian ports</strong> rose by 0.5 per cent in March 2026 compared with the same period last year. Data published by <strong>SeaNews</strong> showed that the Russian maritime sector delivered a relatively resilient performance despite Ukraine-war pressures and Western sanctions. Though modest, the increase was read as a positive signal after the sharp decline in 2024-2025.</p><p style="text-align: justify">Russia's container traffic is geographically distributed across four basins: the <strong>Far East</strong> (Vladivostok, Nakhodka, Vostochny), the <strong>Baltic</strong> (St. Petersburg, Ust-Luga, Kaliningrad), the <strong>Black Sea</strong> (Novorossiysk, Taman, Rostov-on-Don), and the <strong>Azov/Caspian</strong>. The Far East basin is the main engine of growth due to the rising trade volume with China. Baltic ports, on the other hand, remain under pressure due to EU sanctions and vessel inspection regimes.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The Black Sea basin remains stable thanks to trade with Türkiye, Iran, the UAE, and the Central Asian republics. Novorossiysk in particular leads in grain, fertiliser, and general container transport. According to <strong>Rosmorrechflot</strong> data, Russian ports maintained stability in total throughput while shippers increased the market share of local operators such as FESCO, Transcontainer, and RZD Logistics. The outlook for 2026 is for traffic to continue recovering through moderate growth.</p>
