<p style="text-align: justify">U.S. automotive parts giant <strong>O'Reilly Automotive</strong> is building its primary defense against the cost pressures created by trade tariffs in the sector around <strong>supplier health</strong> and <strong>supply chain diversification</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">CEO <strong>Brent Kirby</strong> said during an October 23 earnings call that the company is closely monitoring supplier performance across all dimensions to manage tariff-related risks. According to Kirby's remarks, supplier health management encompasses the following elements:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Shipping performance</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Product quality</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Catalog support</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Financial soundness</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Multisourcing</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This comprehensive approach enables the company to become more resilient against both tariffs and global supply chain shocks.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>China dependency declining rapidly</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Kirby noted that O'Reilly has been working on <strong>supplier diversification</strong> for years.<br>
While sourcing from China represented more than <strong>30 percent</strong> of total volume by the end of 2024, this figure has fallen to the <strong>mid-20s percent</strong> as of 2025.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Kirby:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"This rate is hundreds of basis points lower than last year and continues to decline."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, the real issue for the company is not the China percentage; it is <strong>the flexibility of the global supply mix</strong>.<br>
According to Kirby:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"What really matters is the ability to source from multiple countries and for suppliers to manage this dynamic structure effectively."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Thanks to this approach, the company can rapidly absorb the impact of tariff increases in any given country <strong>by shifting to another source</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Supplier partnerships: At the center of risk mitigation</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">O'Reilly's merchandising and supply teams work in "close contact" with suppliers. The objectives are:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Creating <strong>multiple sourcing points</strong> within product categories,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Moving away from risky geographies,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Ensuring product quality,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Detecting financially weak suppliers early.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This dynamic structure has become the company's greatest advantage, particularly as tariffs remain highly uncertain throughout 2025.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Kirby noted that in Q3, <strong>a significant portion of the tariff impact had been absorbed</strong>.</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Whatever comes, we are ready. Diversification across global supply makes us strong."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Pricing strategy: Agile but cautious</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">One component of tariff management is pricing.<br>
Kirby noted that the company <strong>closely monitors price movements</strong> and can adjust product prices when necessary to absorb tariff costs.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Nevertheless, O'Reilly continues to pursue a strategy of passing the lowest possible impact to customers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Supply chain in healthiest condition ever</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Kirby, the company has reached <strong>the strongest inventory availability levels</strong> since the pandemic.</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Inventory is healthy across the entire distribution network</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Product availability rates are high</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Supply performance is stable</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This situation is viewed as evidence that supply diversification and supplier health controls are working successfully.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">O'Reilly sees <strong>supplier health and diversification</strong> as its strongest tool against tariffs.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">China sourcing share has fallen to the <strong>mid-20s percent</strong>; the decline continues.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The company positions <strong>multisourcing</strong> capability as a strategic advantage.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Supply chain is in its healthiest period since 2020.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">As of Q3, "a large portion" of tariff impact has been overcome.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Pricing strategy is managed to support tariff absorption when needed.</p></li>
</ul>
