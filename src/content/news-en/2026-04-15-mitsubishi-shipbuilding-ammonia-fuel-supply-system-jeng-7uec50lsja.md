<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Mitsubishi Shipbuilding Co., Ltd.</strong>, part of <strong>Mitsubishi Heavy Industries (MHI) Group</strong>, has shipped an <strong>ammonia fuel supply system (AFSS)</strong> and an <strong>ammonia gas abatement system (AGAS)</strong> for <strong>Japan Engine Corporation (J-ENG)</strong>. Both systems are designed for J-ENG's first ammonia-fuelled marine engine model <strong>7UEC50LSJA-HPSCR</strong> and represent the first units produced by Mitsubishi Shipbuilding. Commissioning work will begin in due course.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The AFSS supplies ammonia fuel to the engine in a <strong>stable and safe</strong> manner, while the AGAS safely processes surplus ammonia generated during fuel switching from ammonia to conventional <strong>fuel oil</strong>. Both systems support <strong>remote operation</strong> and <strong>automatic control</strong> through an integrated control system, enhancing operational efficiency and safety.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>early market introduction</strong> of these systems will enable onboard verification combining J-ENG's 7UEC50LSJA-HPSCR ammonia-fuelled engine. The move is regarded as a pioneering step in <strong>next-generation advanced maritime propulsion</strong>. Ammonia fuel stands out as one of the most promising alternative fuel options for the transition to <strong>carbon-free maritime propulsion</strong>.</p>
