<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain Webinar</strong>; moderated by <strong>Bill Catania</strong>, founder and <strong>CEO</strong> of <strong>OneRail</strong>; <strong>Jeff Toewe</strong>, <strong>Chief Product Officer</strong>; <strong>David Rye</strong>, board member and operational advisor; and <strong>Helen Atkinson</strong>, Managing Editor of <strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong>. Five years after the peak of supply chain disruption, retailers and wholesalers face a new challenge: <strong>tech bloat</strong>. In their rush to adapt, companies embraced a patchwork of point solutions — many of which now sit idle, redundant, or worse, draining resources and slowing operations. In this candid, open webinar, speakers address the hidden costs of fragmented technology stacks and the unintended consequences of overdependence on siloed tools. Learn how leading brands are moving away from unnecessary platforms and adopting unified strategies that drive efficiency, flexibility, and performance across the supply chain. Join a conversation with no rules on how to escape technology overload and take back control.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>OneRail</strong>, headquartered in <strong>Orlando, Florida</strong>, is a last-mile delivery orchestration software provider founded by <strong>Bill Catania</strong> — delivering a final-mile delivery platform for retailers, wholesalers, and manufacturers. <strong>OmniPoint</strong>, <strong>OneRail</strong>'s core product platform, encompasses real-time visibility, route optimization, carrier management, returns management, and customer communication modules. <strong>Tech bloat</strong> is one of the most discussed post-pandemic SaaS economy topics — according to <strong>Gartner</strong>, enterprise <strong>SaaS</strong> spending exceeded $280 billion in 2024 — yet companies waste 30-40% of their <strong>SaaS</strong> spending on "shadow IT" or unused licenses. <strong>Productiv</strong>, <strong>Zylo</strong>, <strong>Torii</strong>, <strong>Vendr</strong>, <strong>Tropic</strong>, <strong>Cledara</strong>, and <strong>Spendflo</strong> are leading <strong>SaaS management platform</strong> (<strong>SMP</strong>) providers used by companies to optimize their <strong>SaaS</strong> portfolios.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the last-mile delivery sector has experienced dramatic growth over the past five years driven by the e-commerce explosion — representing a market exceeding $200 billion annually in the <strong>U.S.</strong> Leading last-mile and final-mile platforms include <strong>OneRail</strong>, <strong>Bringg</strong>, <strong>FarEye</strong>, <strong>Onfleet</strong>, <strong>DispatchTrack</strong>, <strong>Routific</strong>, <strong>Locus.sh</strong>, <strong>RouteSmart</strong>, <strong>Descartes Route Planner</strong>, <strong>Trimble TMS</strong>, <strong>Manhattan Active Last Mile</strong>, <strong>Blue Yonder Luminate Logistics</strong>, <strong>Körber Last Mile</strong>, <strong>Project44</strong>, and <strong>FourKites</strong>. Crowdsourced delivery platforms include <strong>DoorDash Drive</strong>, <strong>Uber Direct</strong>, <strong>Roadie</strong> (<strong>UPS</strong>), <strong>Postmates</strong> (<strong>Uber</strong>), and <strong>GrubHub</strong>. Major retailers operate their own last-mile networks: <strong>Amazon DSP</strong> (<strong>Delivery Service Partner</strong>), <strong>Amazon Flex</strong>, <strong>Walmart Spark</strong>, and <strong>Target Shipt</strong>. National carriers include <strong>UPS</strong>, <strong>FedEx</strong>, <strong>DHL</strong>, <strong>USPS</strong>, <strong>OnTrac</strong>, <strong>LSO</strong>, and <strong>LaserShip</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the <strong>tech rationalization</strong> process encompasses: (1) <strong>application portfolio management</strong> (<strong>APM</strong>), inventory of existing software; (2) <strong>usage analytics</strong>, actual usage metrics; (3) <strong>cost optimization</strong>, cancellation of unused licenses; (4) <strong>integration audit</strong>, inventory of <strong>iPaaS</strong> (<strong>Integration Platform as a Service</strong>), <strong>API</strong>, <strong>EDI</strong>, and <strong>webhook</strong> infrastructure; (5) <strong>vendor consolidation</strong>, transitioning from best-of-breed multi-vendor to unified single-vendor strategy; (6) <strong>build vs. buy</strong> decisions; (7) <strong>contract renegotiation</strong>; (8) <strong>change management</strong>; and (9) <strong>data architecture</strong> consolidation including data lake, data fabric, and <strong>master data management</strong> (<strong>MDM</strong>) initiatives. Leading <strong>iPaaS</strong> platforms include <strong>MuleSoft</strong> (<strong>Salesforce</strong>), <strong>Boomi</strong>, <strong>Workato</strong>, <strong>Informatica</strong>, <strong>SnapLogic</strong>, <strong>Tray.io</strong>, <strong>Celigo</strong>, <strong>Microsoft Power Automate</strong>, <strong>Zapier</strong>, and <strong>n8n</strong>. Ultimately, the webinar organized by <strong>OneRail</strong>'s <strong>Bill Catania</strong>, <strong>Jeff Toewe</strong>, and <strong>David Rye</strong> provides a roadmap for retail and wholesale supply chain leaders to rationalize their post-pandemic technology investments — a critical discussion ahead of the 2026 budget planning cycle.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. <strong>OneRail</strong>, led by <strong>Bill Catania</strong>, is a last-mile orchestration platform provider.<br>
2. Post-pandemic tech bloat represents a major challenge for retailers.<br>
3. Companies waste 30-40% of enterprise <strong>SaaS</strong> spending on shadow IT or redundant licenses.<br>
4. The trend is shifting from best-of-breed multi-vendor to unified single-vendor strategies.<br>
5. <strong>iPaaS</strong>, <strong>API</strong>, <strong>EDI</strong>, and <strong>master data management</strong> are core integration infrastructures.</p>
