<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Port of Rotterdam Authority</strong> reported a marginal 0.7 per cent decline in total throughput for the first quarter of 2026, with volumes slipping to <strong>103.0 million tonnes</strong> from 103.7 million tonnes in the same period last year. The reduction was primarily driven by lower flows of agribulk, coal, other liquid bulk and breakbulk. However, gains in iron ore and scrap metal, other dry bulk, crude oil, mineral oil products, liquefied natural gas (LNG), and container throughput measured in TEUs helped offset the overall decline.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Container throughput</strong> edged up by 0.3 per cent in TEUs, although tonnage fell by 3.2 per cent due to a sharp rise in empty container exports to Asia. Inland container flows increased by 11 per cent, driven by stronger services to Asia and North America. Liquid bulk throughput rose by 2.2 per cent overall, with crude oil volumes up 1.7 per cent to 25.2 million tonnes and mineral oil products climbing 10.3 per cent on stronger export flows. <strong>LNG throughput</strong> increased by 1.7 per cent, supported by colder temperatures and stock replenishment.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Geopolitical developments, including the closure of the <strong>Strait of Hormuz</strong>, have added volatility to global energy markets. Around 10 per cent of Rotterdam's crude oil throughput and 14 per cent of oil product volumes are typically linked to the Persian Gulf region. The authority noted that the impact on Q1 figures remained limited, with effects expected to become more visible in subsequent quarters. Breakbulk volumes declined by 1.5 per cent, while RoRo rose by 1.6 per cent on the back of a modest recovery in UK trade.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. Port of Rotterdam reported a 0.7 per cent decline in Q1 2026 throughput, slipping to 103.0 million tonnes.<br>
2. Container TEU volumes rose 0.3 per cent, while tonnage fell 3.2 per cent due to surging empty exports to Asia.<br>
3. Inland container flows grew 11 per cent on stronger Asia and North America services.<br>
4. Liquid bulk rose 2.2 per cent overall, with mineral oil products up 10.3 per cent and LNG up 1.7 per cent.<br>
5. Strait of Hormuz closure threatens 10-14 per cent of Rotterdam's oil throughput, with full impact expected in coming quarters.</p>
