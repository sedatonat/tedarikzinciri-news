<p style="text-align: justify">On Wednesday, 7 January 2025, a railway line in Birmingham city centre was closed due to a bridge strike that could potentially have resulted in a catastrophe. The incident marked the third bridge strike in the same week, following a serious accident that occurred four days earlier. <strong>Network Rail</strong> expressed grave concern over the rising frequency of such incidents despite ongoing national awareness campaigns.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Lorry drivers are being urged to be aware of their vehicles' height, as a bridge in Birmingham was struck three times within a week. In one of these incidents, a laden container lorry overturned and fell onto an active pedestrian walkway. The bridge over <strong>Summer Road</strong> near <strong>Erdington Station</strong> in the north of the city centre was forced to close repeatedly for safety inspections.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The railway bridge over <strong>Summer Road</strong> was struck just before 10:00 on Wednesday, 7 January. Two hours after reopening, it was struck again at 12:10 and remained closed until 13:45. This caused severe disruptions on the "Cross City" line, the suburban route between <strong>Birmingham New Street</strong>, <strong>Sutton Coldfield</strong>, and <strong>Lichfield</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In the incidents that occurred on Wednesday, one vehicle failed to remain at the scene, which could constitute a potential crime in the United Kingdom. In the more serious accident that occurred in the afternoon, a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_goods_vehicle"><u><strong>Heavy Goods Vehicle (HGV)</strong></u></a> with a tarpaulin canopy had the roof of its trailer torn open.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Martin Colmey</strong>, Operations Director for the Midlands Route at <strong>Network Rail</strong>, said: "There is no excuse for a lorry to strike a railway bridge. Our bridges are clearly marked and drivers should always know the height limitations of their vehicles. Striking a bridge while ignoring both is inexcusable and causes disruption to passengers and road users."</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The United Kingdom continues to suffer from incidents known as "bridge strikes". <strong>The Birmingham region experienced 65 bridge strikes last year</strong>, which resulted in a total of 6 days of delays to railway traffic. <strong>West Midlands</strong> police will question the drivers involved in two of the accidents and launch an investigation into the driver who left the scene.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Bridge strikes cause significant disruptions to the UK railway network. The severity of the strikes on <strong>Summer Road</strong> is evident; the road narrows under the bridge and has pedestrian walkways on both sides. The images show that these pedestrian walkways were put at risk; any pedestrian could have faced the danger of serious injury.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Birmingham is home to 3 intermodal railway terminals in the north of the city. It is unclear whether the overturned lorry was destined for one of these terminals. The fact that the tractor unit is registered in the United Kingdom suggests the driver may be local. Perhaps they should have paid more attention to <strong>Network Rail</strong>'s <a href="https://www.networkrail.co.uk/communities/safety-in-the-community/railway-safety-campaigns/wise-up-size-up/"><u><strong>Wise Up, Size Up</strong></u></a> campaign.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
