<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>icebreakers</strong> operated by <strong>Rosmorport</strong> federal port property management agency have completed their operations in the <strong>Sea of Azov</strong> and in the <strong>Caspian basin</strong> for the 2026 icebreaking season. According to Rosmorport, the icebreaking season ended in the <strong>Caspian on March 11</strong>. There, three Rosmorport-managed icebreakers — <strong>"Kapitan Bukaev", "Kapitan Metsaik", and "Kapitan Chechkin"</strong> — worked escorting some <strong>400 cargo vessels to Astrakhan and Olya</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">In the <strong>Sea of Azov</strong>, the icebreaking season <strong>closed in early March</strong>. There, more than <strong>1,190 ships</strong> were escorted to the ports of <strong>Azov, Yeisk, Taganrog, and Rostov-na-Donu</strong>. <strong>Six vessels</strong> managed by Rosmortport operated in Sea of Azov icebreaking operations.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The Russian icebreaker fleet provides critical operational capacity for Russian exports during the winter months. Icebreaking services in the Caspian and Sea of Azov particularly support commercial vessels continuing grain, coal, metal, and oil product shipments. Astrakhan is Russia's main port hub on the Caspian and a key node for trade with Iran, Kazakhstan, and Turkmenistan. Azov Sea ports form the backbone of logistics flows in the Crimea-Caucasus region. The 2026 season was conducted almost without interruption despite wartime conditions, highlighting Russia's economic reliance on inland waterway transport.</p>
