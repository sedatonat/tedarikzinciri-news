<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>America</strong>'s advanced manufacturers are adding uranium to their list of critical raw materials that must be sourced from foreign suppliers. <strong>Tech</strong> giants are increasingly turning to nuclear energy to power massive data centers being built across the country to support the next wave of artificial intelligence. And that means ensuring a stable uranium supply. <strong>Christo Liebenberg</strong>, co-founder and president of <strong>U.S.-based LIS Technologies Inc.</strong>, foresees "<strong>a collision course between America</strong>'s <strong>AI</strong> boom and the uranium supply chain</strong>." The problem: foreign suppliers account for 99% of the uranium concentrate needed for nuclear fuel. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>U.S. uranium production</strong> has experienced a 95%+ decline since the end of the <strong>Cold War</strong> and now operates limited facilities in <strong>Wyoming</strong>, <strong>Texas</strong>, <strong>Utah</strong>, and <strong>New Mexico</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Canada</strong>, <strong>Kazakhstan</strong>, and <strong>Australia</strong> currently make up the vast majority of <strong>America</strong>'s uranium imports. (<strong>Russia</strong>, another major producer, is barred from transferring low-enriched uranium to the <strong>U.S.</strong> under legislation signed by President <strong>Biden</strong> in August 2024, though exemptions are permitted under certain conditions.) <strong>Liebenberg</strong> says the uranium supply chain is a "real bottleneck" in efforts to produce sufficient nuclear energy to support <strong>AI</strong> development. The <strong>U.S.</strong> currently generates approximately 100 gigawatts of nuclear capacity, a level President <strong>Trump</strong> has committed to quadrupling by 2050. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Cameco</strong> (<strong>Canada</strong>), <strong>Kazatomprom</strong> (<strong>Kazakhstan</strong>), <strong>BHP</strong> (<strong>Australia Olympic Dam</strong>), <strong>Orano</strong> (<strong>France</strong>), <strong>Rosatom</strong> (<strong>Russia</strong>), <strong>CGN</strong> and <strong>CNNC</strong> (<strong>China</strong>) are the major players in global uranium production and conversion.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The uranium supply chain actually consists of five separate components: mining, milling, conversion, enrichment, and fuel production. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>uranium enrichment</strong> is performed using <strong>centrifuge cascade</strong> technology and has historically been dominated by <strong>Russia</strong> (<strong>Tenex</strong>), <strong>URENCO</strong> (<strong>UK</strong>, <strong>Germany</strong>, <strong>Netherlands</strong>), <strong>Orano</strong> (<strong>Tricastin France</strong>), and <strong>CNNC</strong>. The <strong>U.S.</strong> lacks enrichment capacity except for <strong>Centrus Energy</strong>'s <strong>HALEU</strong> (<strong>High-Assay Low-Enriched Uranium</strong>) facility in <strong>Piketon Ohio</strong> and <strong>Urenco USA</strong>'s <strong>Eunice</strong> facility in <strong>New Mexico</strong>. <strong>SMR</strong> (<strong>Small Modular Reactor</strong>) platform developers include <strong>NuScale</strong>, <strong>X-energy</strong>, <strong>TerraPower</strong> (<strong>Bill Gates</strong>), <strong>BWXT</strong>, <strong>GE Hitachi BWRX-300</strong>, <strong>Rolls-Royce SMR</strong>, and <strong>Holtec SMR-300</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>data center</strong> electricity demand could represent 9-12% of total consumption in the <strong>U.S.</strong> by 2030, according to <strong>EPRI</strong> and <strong>Goldman Sachs</strong> projections. <strong>Microsoft</strong> has signed a 20-year <strong>PPA</strong> with <strong>Constellation Energy</strong> to bring <strong>Three Mile Island Unit 1</strong> back online. <strong>Amazon</strong> is purchasing a data center adjacent to <strong>Talen Energy</strong>'s <strong>Susquehanna</strong> reactor. <strong>Google</strong> has signed an <strong>SMR</strong> agreement with <strong>Kairos Power</strong>. <strong>Meta</strong> has launched an <strong>RFP</strong>. The <strong>Inflation Reduction Act</strong> (<strong>IRA</strong>) offers <strong>PTC</strong>/<strong>ITC</strong> incentives for nuclear. <strong>NRC</strong> (<strong>Nuclear Regulatory Commission</strong>) licensing timelines remain one of the sector's major bottlenecks. <strong>Robert J. Bowman</strong>, senior editor at <strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong>, covers the topic. In conclusion, rebuilding the <strong>U.S.</strong> uranium supply chain is positioned among strategic priorities for the <strong>AI</strong> era.</p>
