<p style="text-align: justify">The U.S. and China have reached a significant compromise at a <strong>critical leaders' meeting in Busan</strong> following an intense trade war that continued through 2025.</p>
<p><br>
Following the meeting attended by Trump and Xi Jinping, the two sides agreed on a truce package that <strong>reduces tariffs</strong>, <strong>suspends investigations</strong>, <strong>halts reciprocal retaliation</strong>, and <strong>increases energy and agricultural purchases</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Fentanyl-linked tariffs reduced from 20% to 10%</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to an executive order issued by President Donald Trump, fentanyl-linked tariffs applied to certain products from China have been reduced to <strong>20% → 10%</strong> levels.</p>
<p><br>
The measure <strong>takes effect as of November 10</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">China's Ministry of Commerce confirmed the reduction and announced that the U.S. will <strong>defer for one year</strong> planned increases in elevated reciprocal tariffs on China.</p>
<p><br>
Period: <strong>through November 10, 2026</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Section 301 exemptions extended</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to a fact sheet released by the White House on November 1, the U.S. has also extended previously expiring <strong>Section 301 tariff exemptions</strong> <strong>through November 10, 2026</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to a statement by USTR Jamieson Greer, despite the tariff reductions:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The total tariff burden on goods from China remains at <strong>approximately 47%</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Some items can reach as high as <strong>100%</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Reciprocal investigations and shipping fees suspended for one year</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The U.S. will <strong>suspend for one year its Section 301 investigation</strong> into China's maritime and logistics sectors.</p>
<p><br>
As part of the same package, additional port fees applied by the U.S. to China-linked vessels will also be suspended for one year.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In return, China agreed to suspend its own retaliatory fees <strong>for the same period</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Rare earth controls deferred</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">China agreed to <strong>defer for one year the rare earth export controls</strong> it announced in October.<br>
Additionally:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">It will restructure rare earth export licenses</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">It will tighten chemical export controls to combat fentanyl supply chains</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">In return, the U.S. will suspend for one year <strong>national security-based export restrictions</strong> on certain firms with Chinese ownership of more than 50%.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Agricultural agreement: major purchase commitments</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Trump announced via Truth Social that China will begin "tremendous" agricultural product purchases from the U.S.<br>
White House details:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>12 million tons of U.S. soybeans</strong> within two months</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>25 million tons of soybeans</strong> annually between 2026–2029</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">U.S. <strong>sorghum</strong> purchases are resuming</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">U.S. <strong>hardwood logs</strong> imports are also returning</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">China's Ministry of Commerce has not yet confirmed all of these details.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Nexperia production reopening</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">In addition to agricultural goods, China agreed to "take appropriate steps" to restart operations of <strong>Nexperia</strong>, a Netherlands-based but China-owned company, in China.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Agreement to be renegotiated annually</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Both sides agreed that the truce package will be re-evaluated annually.</p>
<p><br>
This means the agreement is <strong>not permanent</strong>, but rather provides short-term tension relief.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Xi Jinping: "Despite storms, we must stay the correct course"</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">At the Busan meeting, Xi stated:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Our differences are normal. What matters is that the world's two largest economies manage their relationship in a stable manner."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">This statement shows that China is inclined, at least in the short term, to lower tensions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Fentanyl-linked tariffs reduced <strong>20% → 10%</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Reciprocal tariff increases <strong>deferred for one additional year</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Rare earth export controls <strong>suspended for one year by China</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">U.S. Section 301 maritime investigation <strong>suspended for one year</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">China committed to major purchases of <strong>soybeans, sorghum, and hardwood</strong> from the U.S.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Total tariff burden remains at <strong>45–47%</strong> levels.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The truce will be <strong>renegotiated annually</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
