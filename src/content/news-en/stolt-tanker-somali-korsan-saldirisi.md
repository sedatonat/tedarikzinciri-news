<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Stolt Tankers</strong>' <strong>Cayman Islands-flagged chemical tanker </strong><em><strong>Stolt Sagaland</strong></em><strong> came under attack by armed pirates off the Somali coast.</strong> The incident was confirmed by maritime security firm <strong>Vanguard Tech</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Attack occurred in early morning hours</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The incident took place on the morning of <strong>November 3 at approximately 04:45 local time</strong>, approximately <strong>332 nautical miles southeast of Mogadishu</strong>, within Somalia's exclusive economic zone (EEZ).</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">According to reports, the tanker was pursued by a <strong>small speedboat (skiff) launched from a "mothership."</strong> <strong>Four armed attackers in the boat approached the vessel from the port side and opened fire.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ship crew and security team responded swiftly</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The <em>Stolt Sagaland</em> crew immediately sounded the alarm, <strong>increased speed and initiated evasive maneuvers.</strong></p>\n<p><br>\nThe <strong>armed security team onboard</strong> <strong>returned fire and repelled the attack.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">After a gunfire exchange lasting several minutes, the pirates <strong>were forced to withdraw without approaching the vessel.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>No casualties or damage</strong> were reported. The tanker continued on its course following the attack.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Incident verification and security assessment</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The attack was confirmed by both the <strong>UK Maritime Trade Operations (UKMTO) centre</strong> and private security firm <strong>Ambrey</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>According to Ambrey's report</strong>, the pirate boat was able to approach the vessel by only <strong>0.05 nautical miles (approximately 90 metres).</strong> After the security team fired warning shots, <strong>the pirates returned to their mothership and fled the area.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>EU NAVFOR raises alert level</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Following the incident, <strong>the European Union Naval Force's (EU NAVFOR) Operation Atalanta</strong> <strong>elevated the threat level off the Somali coast.</strong></p>\n<p><br>\nThe organization assessed that an active <strong>Pirate Action Group (PAG)</strong> was operating in the area and that <strong>the group may be linked to an Iranian fishing vessel hijacked near Garacad last week.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Risk resurging in Somali waters</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Experts note that the incident bears signs of <strong>a potential resurgence of Somali piracy.</strong></p>\n<p><br>\n<strong>Ambrey</strong> reported that <strong>"suspicious dhow movements"</strong> had been recorded in the area prior to the attack and indicated a <strong>"realistic likelihood"</strong> of similar attempts recurring.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Recent months have seen a marked increase in pirate activity off the Somali coast, particularly in <strong>the western portions of the Indian Ocean.</strong></p>\n<p><br>\nAnalysts point to factors such as <strong>economic instability in the region, reduced naval patrols, and the reorganization of pirate networks</strong> as contributing to this uptick.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Vessel route and specifications</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The <em>Stolt Sagaland</em>, a super-segregator-type chemical tanker built in 2008 with a <strong>44,000 dwt capacity</strong>, had departed from <strong>Saudi Arabia's Al Jubail port</strong> at the time of the attack and was proceeding to its next port of call.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The tanker is equipped with <strong>specialized compartments for transporting chemicals and liquid cargo</strong> and is <strong>fitted with enhanced security measures.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Measures against Somali piracy</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Somali piracy, which peaked between 2010 and 2013, was largely suppressed thanks to <strong>international patrol forces (EU NAVFOR, NATO, U.S. Navy).</strong></p>\n<p><br>\nHowever, in recent years, <strong>the decline in international patrol operations</strong> and <strong>reduced fishing revenues in the region</strong> have created conditions for pirate groups to resume activities.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">EU NAVFOR officials have advised ship operators to <strong>maintain armed security teams in high-risk areas</strong>, <strong>increase radar surveillance during night transits</strong>, and <strong>strictly adhere to BMP5 (Best Management Practices) guidelines.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: pirate threat back on agenda</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The attack on <em>Stolt Sagaland</em> is regarded as <strong>one of the first concrete indicators of renewed pirate activity in Somali waters, which have been relatively quiet for an extended period.</strong></p>\n<p><br>\nAuthorities are warning commercial vessels to <strong>remain on high alert</strong> and <strong>immediately report any suspicious vessel movements.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Stolt Sagaland came under armed pirate attack off Somalia.</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The armed security team returned fire and prevented the attack from succeeding.</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>No casualties or damage reported.</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>EU NAVFOR Operation Atalanta elevated the threat level in the region.</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The pirates approached to approximately 90 metres before withdrawing.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The incident <strong>may be linked to an Iranian fishing vessel hijacked near Garacad last week.</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Experts warn: <strong>"Somali piracy is experiencing a resurgence, and regional risk is escalating."</strong></p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>----------</p>\n<p><strong>News Link: </strong><a
