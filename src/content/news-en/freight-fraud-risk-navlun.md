<p style="text-align: justify">Freight Fraud has emerged as a growing threat to global supply chains. According to Think Tank analysis, fraudulent carrier identities, falsified delivery records, and data manipulation are causing billions of dollars in losses. FBI IC3 data shows that such incidents increased by 63% between 2024 and 2025.</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Fraudsters typically steal credentials of legitimate carriers and redirect shipments through fake websites or email addresses. These attacks often occur when authentication processes are bypassed during urgent deliveries. AI-powered verification systems detect risky shipments by checking data such as historical performance, GPS tracking, and driver license matching.</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Industry organizations are promoting coordinated defense by establishing data sharing and joint blacklist mechanisms. National monitoring systems developed through collaboration between TIA, FBI, and CargoNet map recurring fraud patterns. Additionally, blockchain-based shipment identities provide verifiable trust throughout the delivery chain.</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, combating freight fraud requires not only technological solutions but also institutional collaboration. Transparent data sharing and disciplined verification chains reduce the impact of this threat.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>Key Points:</strong><br>\n- Freight fraud cases increased by 63% in 2024–2025.<br>\n- Fraudulent identities and data manipulation are the most common methods.<br>\n- AI-powered verification detects risky shipments.<br>\n- Joint TIA/FBI/CargoNet data systems map fraud patterns.<br>\n- Blockchain authentication strengthens the trust chain.</p>\n\n<p><strong>!!!
