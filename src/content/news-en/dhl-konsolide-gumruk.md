<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>DHL Global Forwarding (DGF)</strong> has launched a new <strong>consolidated customs clearance service</strong> designed to <strong>simplify U.S. retail import processes and reduce costs</strong>. The company explained that this new system is specifically designed for <strong>high-volume e-commerce and retail importers</strong> and streamlines operations by <strong>managing the customs process through a single consolidated declaration</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">DHL Global Forwarding is a logistics provider operating under the <strong>Deutsche Post DHL Group</strong> umbrella, <strong>focused on air and ocean transportation</strong>. According to the company's statement, <strong>rising tariffs, extended customs clearance times, and compliance risks</strong> are creating growing challenges, particularly for <strong>importers operating in consumer-focused sectors</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new service aims to address these issues with <strong>a faster, lower-cost customs clearance model that maintains high compliance standards</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Service Structure and Operations</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new system enables <strong>multiple shipments to be processed under a single customs entry</strong>. This allows companies to achieve both cost and time savings through <strong>consolidated tax and customs processing</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to DHL executives, this solution was developed to help companies importing to the U.S. rapidly adapt to new regulations, <strong>particularly following the removal of the "de minimis" threshold for goods originating in China</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Greg Nichols</strong>, <strong>Senior Vice President of Global Customs at DHL Global Forwarding</strong>, summed up the importance of the new system with these words:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Retailers now must deliver faster, more economically, and in full compliance with changing trade regulations. This service simplifies the process without requiring advanced technology infrastructure, reduces costs, and positions companies ahead of regulatory changes."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Nichols emphasized that this model would provide <strong>competitive advantage particularly during peak seasons such as Black Friday</strong> and <strong>holiday periods</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Technology Integration and Operational Process</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Jan Gora</strong>, Director of Product Development for DHL Global Forwarding Americas Region, noted that this service was developed as a <strong>direct response to market demand</strong>. According to Gora, the system's development was rapidly launched <strong>following preliminary discussions with China-based customers in March 2025</strong>.</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"This solution minimizes costs and creates speed advantages while ensuring full compliance with all customs regulations," said Gora, adding that the system <strong>manages both information and physical cargo flow end-to-end</strong>.</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Technically, the process includes the following steps:</p>
<ol>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Automatic integration of order data from e-commerce sellers,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Preparation of customs declaration by DHL and real-time status notification,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Consolidation of shipments at origin, air transportation to the U.S.,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Storage in bonded warehouses in the U.S.,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Handoff to final delivery partner after customs clearance is completed.</strong></p></li>
</ol>
<p style="text-align: justify">This model is designed for mid-sized retailers conducting <strong>high-volume imports (at least 1,000 packages per week)</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Competitive Advantage and Industry Impact</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">DHL says it is combining its <strong>global air transportation network</strong> and <strong>customs expertise</strong> through this service to provide customers with a <strong>fully integrated Door-to-Door solution</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Gora, this system will <strong>both drive growth with existing customers and facilitate access to new customer segments</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The company also noted in its statement that <strong>global tariff levels have reached their highest point since the 1930s</strong>, North American trade growth has fallen <strong>from 2.7% to 1.5%</strong>, and this is forcing businesses to <strong>restructure their supply chains</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">It was noted that retailers are increasingly turning to <strong>Delivered Duty Paid (DDP) models</strong> to <strong>simplify cross-border operations</strong>, while e-commerce continues to grow rapidly. <strong>Global cross-border e-commerce volume is expected to reach 4.81 trillion dollars by 2032</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to DHL, consumers are increasingly abandoning purchases due to <strong>"unexpected customs fees" and "complex return processes"</strong>. For this reason, <strong>transparent, predictable, and simplified customs solutions</strong> will create competitive advantage for retailers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>DHL Global Forwarding</strong> has launched the <strong>Consolidated Clearance Service</strong> for U.S. retailers.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The system consolidates <strong>multiple shipments under a single customs declaration</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Objective: <strong>Reduce costs, accelerate processes, and ensure full regulatory compliance.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Greg Nichols:</strong> "For our customers, cost, speed, and compliance are now the determining factors of competition."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The service was developed in response to demand created by the <strong>removal of the "de minimis" threshold</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Mid-sized retailers handling 1,000+ packages per week</strong> are the target segment.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>DHL</strong> now offers <strong>fully integrated Door-to-Door logistics solutions</strong> through this model.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Global e-commerce is expected to reach 4.81 trillion dollars by 2032</strong>; simplified customs clearance has become critical to this growth.</p></li>
</ul>
