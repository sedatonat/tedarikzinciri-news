<p style="text-align: justify">Roughly half of warehouse workers say automation is helping drive wage increases, and nearly two-thirds report higher job satisfaction working with technology compared to manual operations. According to a survey by robotics company <strong>Exotec</strong> covering more than 400 warehouse workers, 98% of respondents say automation makes them more productive. Another 48% describe themselves as optimistic about automation's role in warehouses, with 32% cautious and 20% neutral on the matter. "The data is clear—workers believe that working alongside automation offers new potential for long-term careers and immediate job satisfaction," <strong>Exotec</strong> CEO <strong>Romain Moulin</strong> said in a statement on November 17. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Exotec</strong> is France-based, founded in 2015 by <strong>Romain Moulin</strong> and <strong>Renaud Heitz</strong>, and is recognized for its <strong>Skypod goods-to-person</strong> 3D mobile robot system.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Warehouse workers also view automation as a stress reliever during holiday rush periods, with 70% noting that technology makes them less stressed during these peak times, and 32% reporting that the number of colleagues quitting during peak seasons has declined since automation was introduced. From a supply chain perspective, the global warehouse automation market is valued at approximately 25–30 billion dollars as of 2024 and is growing rapidly at a CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) of 10% or higher. <strong>Daifuku</strong> (<strong>Japan</strong>); <strong>Dematic</strong> (<strong>KION Group</strong>; <strong>Germany</strong>); <strong>SSI Schaefer</strong> (<strong>Germany</strong>); <strong>Murata Machinery</strong> (<strong>Japan</strong>); <strong>Honeywell Intelligrated</strong>; <strong>Vanderlande</strong> (<strong>Toyota Industries</strong>); <strong>Knapp</strong> (<strong>Austria</strong>); <strong>Swisslog</strong> (<strong>KUKA</strong>); <strong>Witron</strong>; <strong>TGW Logistics</strong> are major <strong>AS/RS</strong> (<strong>Automated Storage and Retrieval System</strong>) and full-stack integrator players. <strong>AutoStore</strong> (<strong>Norway</strong>); <strong>Exotec</strong> (<strong>France</strong>); <strong>Ocado Technology</strong> (<strong>UK</strong>); <strong>Attabotics</strong> (<strong>Canada</strong>); <strong>Hai Robotics</strong> (<strong>China</strong>); <strong>Geek+</strong> (<strong>China</strong>); <strong>Quicktron</strong> (<strong>China</strong>); <strong>Libiao Robotics</strong>; <strong>URBX</strong>; <strong>Attari</strong> are cube-based and 3D shelf players.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the <strong>AMR</strong> (<strong>Autonomous Mobile Robot</strong>) and <strong>AGV</strong> (<strong>Automated Guided Vehicle</strong>) market includes major players such as <strong>Locus Robotics</strong>; <strong>6 River Systems</strong> (<strong>Shopify</strong>); <strong>Geek+</strong>; <strong>Fetch Robotics</strong> (<strong>Zebra</strong>); <strong>inVia Robotics</strong>; <strong>MiR</strong> (<strong>Mobile Industrial Robots</strong>; <strong>Teradyne</strong>); <strong>OTTO Motors</strong> (<strong>Clearpath</strong>); <strong>Seegrid</strong>; <strong>Vecna Robotics</strong>; <strong>IAM Robotics</strong>. The <strong>piece-picking robot</strong> market includes major players such as <strong>Covariant</strong> (acquired by <strong>ABB</strong> in 2024); <strong>RightHand Robotics</strong> (<strong>Kardex</strong>); <strong>Berkshire Grey</strong> (<strong>SoftBank</strong>); <strong>Nimble Robotics</strong>; <strong>Dexterity</strong>; <strong>Plus One Robotics</strong>; <strong>Pickle Robot</strong>. <strong>WMS</strong> (<strong>Warehouse Management System</strong>); <strong>WES</strong> (<strong>Warehouse Execution System</strong>); <strong>WCS</strong> (<strong>Warehouse Control System</strong>) are the primary software layers. <strong>Manhattan Associates</strong>; <strong>Blue Yonder</strong>; <strong>SAP EWM</strong>; <strong>Oracle WMS</strong>; <strong>Körber</strong> (<strong>HighJump</strong>); <strong>Tecsys</strong>; <strong>Infor</strong> are leading <strong>WMS</strong> providers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, warehouse labor challenges are chronic in the <strong>U.S.</strong>, with annual <strong>turnover</strong> rates of 40–60% that have intensified with e-commerce growth. <strong>Ergonomics</strong>; <strong>safety</strong>; <strong>work satisfaction</strong>; <strong>wage compression</strong>; <strong>career progression</strong> are the primary pillars of modern warehouse workforce policy. <strong>Cobot</strong> (<strong>collaborative robot</strong>); <strong>human-in-the-loop</strong>; <strong>exoskeleton</strong> (<strong>Sarcos</strong>; <strong>Ekso Bionics</strong>; <strong>Levitate</strong>; <strong>German Bionic</strong>); <strong>computer vision QC</strong> are the technological underpinnings of automation's human-friendly face. The <strong>Exotec Skypod</strong> system can operate at heights up to 12 meters and is used by 100+ customers (<strong>Carrefour</strong>; <strong>Decathlon</strong>; <strong>Geodis</strong>; <strong>GAP</strong>; <strong>UNIQLO</strong>; <strong>Bergner</strong>; <strong>Ariat</strong>; <strong>Gap Inc.</strong>). <strong>Exotec</strong> achieved <strong>unicorn</strong> status in 2022 with a 2 billion dollar valuation. <strong>Labor retention</strong>; <strong>upskilling</strong>; <strong>reskilling</strong> are core warehouse automation human capital strategies. In conclusion, <strong>Exotec</strong>'s survey of 400 workers supports the view that warehouse automation delivers positive results not only for productivity, but also for worker satisfaction and retention, backed by concrete data.</p>
