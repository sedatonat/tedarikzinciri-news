<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Drewry</strong> survey findings indicate that the <strong>Suez Canal</strong> is expected to reopen toward the end of 2025. This development will have significant implications for global supply chains and the maritime shipping sector.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Suez Canal</strong> came into focus in 2021 following the grounding of the <strong>Ever Given</strong> vessel. That incident underscored the critical importance of the canal to global commerce. In its aftermath, work began on widening and modernizing the canal. The <strong>Egyptian government</strong> is undertaking various projects to increase the canal's capacity and prevent similar incidents.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Experts surveyed by <strong>Drewry</strong> note that the canal's reopening will bring significant improvements to maritime shipping. In particular, acceleration of trade between Asia and Europe and reduced costs are anticipated. Additionally, the ability to accommodate larger vessels through the canal is expected to increase cargo volumes.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Suez Canal</strong> handles approximately 12% of global maritime trade. Therefore, the canal's efficient operation is vital to the flow of international commerce. With the canal's reopening, supply chain disruptions are expected to decrease and logistics processes are anticipated to become more efficient.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Egyptian government</strong> is placing emphasis on international cooperation in the canal's modernization. Many countries and companies are investing in this project, contributing to making the canal safer and more efficient. These investments are expected to support economic growth in the region.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">With the canal's reopening, the need for alternative routes will also diminish. In particular, a reduction is expected in the number of long and costly voyages via the <strong>Cape of Good Hope</strong>. This will provide both time and fuel savings, contributing to the sustainability of the maritime shipping sector.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, the reopening of the <strong>Suez Canal</strong> by the end of 2025 is viewed as a positive development for global trade and maritime shipping. The canal's modernization and expansion will enable more efficient supply chain operations, reduce costs, and contribute to regional economic growth.</p>
<p><br></p>
