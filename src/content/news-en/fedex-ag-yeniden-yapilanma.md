<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>FedEx Corp.</strong> has shared the first concrete results of a comprehensive transformation program led by CEO <strong>Raj Subramaniam</strong> with investors. Financial results announced on December 18, 2025, revealed that the company's long-running <strong>extensive network restructuring</strong> and <strong>cost reduction initiatives</strong> are beginning to show tangible effects. The company raised the lower end of its full-year profit guidance while fourth-quarter earnings came in above Wall Street estimates.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the disclosed results, the performance improvement is being driven largely by <strong>rising volumes and pricing power in the U.S. domestic market</strong>. This picture demonstrates that FedEx's strategy to consolidate its ground and air transportation networks is beginning to generate operational efficiency. The strategy targets the integration of <strong>ground</strong> and <strong>air-freight</strong> networks, which the company has managed as separate operations for years, along with eliminating billions of dollars in costs from the system.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to assessments relayed by Bloomberg, <strong>weak demand</strong> throughout the year and uncertainty created by <strong>U.S. President Donald Trump's</strong> trade and tariff policies delayed a clear view of the transformation's effects. Nevertheless, fourth-quarter data pointed to the plan's progress. The company's adjusted earnings per share came in at <strong>$4.82</strong>, exceeding the <strong>$4.12</strong> market expectation compiled by <strong>Bloomberg</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">FedEx raised its full-year adjusted profit guidance to a range of <strong>$17.80 to $19.00 per share</strong>. While the midpoint of this range sits above analyst expectations, it represents a more cautious increase compared to the strong performance in the second quarter. This reflects management's awareness of risks despite near-term gains.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Investor perception of the transformation process has strengthened notably in recent months. FedEx is targeting <strong>$1 billion in permanent cost reductions</strong> by 2026. With this expectation, FedEx shares gained <strong>more than 28 percent</strong> over the six-month period through the December 18 close. Over the same period, major competitor <strong>United Parcel Service Inc.</strong> shares posted a gain of less than 3 percent.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, the transformation is proceeding amid new challenges. FedEx expects an unfavorable impact of <strong>approximately $600 million</strong> on adjusted earnings in the second half of the fiscal year. A significant portion of this impact stems from cost increases related to the grounding of <strong>MD-11 cargo aircraft</strong>. Those aircraft were taken out of service on safety grounds following a <strong>UPS</strong> crash near Louisville, Kentucky, in November that killed 14 people.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Company CFO <strong>John Dietrich</strong> stated that this situation could result in operating profit losses of up to <strong>$175 million</strong> in the second half of the fiscal year. <strong>MD-11</strong> aircraft make up approximately 4 percent of FedEx's fleet, and this situation raises the risk of service disruptions, particularly during peak seasons.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, FedEx is grappling with <strong>persistent global shipment demand weakness</strong> that has become permanent since the pandemic. Erratic tariff policies implemented during the Trump era have particularly hurt high-margin shipments on the <strong>China-U.S. corridor</strong>. The company has previously stated that this volatility generated a <strong>$1 billion</strong> negative impact on adjusted operating profit. Nevertheless, FedEx is painting a more optimistic picture by raising its annual revenue growth guidance to a range of <strong>5 to 6 percent</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Among analysts, cautious optimism prevails. <strong>Citi</strong> analyst <strong>Ariel Rosa</strong> emphasizes that progress signals toward long-term margin improvement goals are evident, but the process could advance unevenly due to macroeconomic conditions and cost pressures.</p>
