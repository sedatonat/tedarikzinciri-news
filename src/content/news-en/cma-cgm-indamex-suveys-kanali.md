<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CMA CGM</strong> announced a significant strategic shift at the end of 2025 on its <strong>INDAMEX</strong> service: the line will now operate a full-loop transit via the <strong>Suez Canal</strong> in both <strong>fronthaul</strong> (India/Pakistan → US East Coast) and <strong>backhaul</strong> (US East Coast → India/Pakistan) directions. The decision stands out as a notable "early return" step compared to the cautious stance major carriers have maintained following security issues in the Red Sea due to drone threats since late 2023.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new route's first full-loop voyage will be handled by <strong>CMA CGM VERDI</strong>. The vessel departs Karachi for New York on <strong>15 January</strong>. According to <strong>eeSea by Xeneta</strong> data, the Suez route reduces INDAMEX's total transit time by two weeks to <strong>77 days</strong>. This creates significant time savings for both carriers and shippers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Previous Situation: Piecemeal Suez Returns</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Throughout 2024–2025, CMA CGM conducted only <strong>case-by-case</strong> Suez transits, particularly limited test voyages on the backhaul leg. Xeneta Chief Analyst <strong>Peter Sand</strong> notes that this new decision marks a critical inflection point as the "<strong>first full east–west loop via Suez</strong>".</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Four additional vessels on the INDAMEX rotation (<strong>APL OREGON, CMA CGM PASSION, APL LE HAVRE, CMA CGM MAUPASSANT</strong>) will conduct eastbound Suez transits. Meanwhile, other CMA CGM fleet vessels (<strong>JULES VERNE, APL CHANGI, GALAPAGOS, GRACE BAY, APL MERLION, KIMBERLEY</strong>) are observed transiting eastbound; however, only <strong>MERLION</strong> and <strong>KIMBERLEY</strong> appear in the proforma schedule.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Industry Comparison: CMA CGM Ahead, Other Carriers on Hold</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Major carriers <strong>Hapag-Lloyd</strong> and <strong>Maersk</strong> have not announced comprehensive return schedules via the Red Sea. <strong>ZIM</strong> is awaiting completion of insurance approval processes. This picture shows CMA CGM making a faster return decision than its competitors.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, Sand emphasizes this does not constitute a <strong>broad-based industry return</strong>. In November 2025, <strong>only 120 container vessels transited Suez</strong>, compared to <strong>583 in October 2023</strong>. This gap demonstrates the security environment remains fragile and the majority of carriers have limited risk appetite.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Capacity Impact: Two Vessels Exiting Rotation</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Suez return does more than shorten transit times; it also makes the INDAMEX rotation more efficient. CMA CGM announced it can <strong>remove two vessels from the rotation</strong> with the Suez loop. Peter Sand interprets this development as a potential bellwether for <strong>sector-wide capacity impact</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Spot rates have already experienced sharp declines in 2025:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Far East → US East Coast fronthaul</strong> rates fell <strong>57%</strong> year-on-year,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Far East → North Europe</strong> rates declined <strong>53%</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">If CMA CGM's move is followed by more carriers, added capacity could push spot rates further down and expose some operators to <strong>loss-making territory</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic Assessment</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">CMA CGM's move carries weight on three levels:</p>
<ol>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Operational:</strong> Shorter transit times make INDAMEX more competitive on the Asia–US East Coast corridor.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Capacity:</strong> Removing two vessels from the rotation helps reduce costs.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Geopolitical:</strong> In an environment where Red Sea security remains fragile, CMA CGM is launching a controlled yet bold return strategy.</p></li>
</ol>
<p style="text-align: justify">The company's move signals the start of a "gradual risk acceptance" process in global shipping networks; however, Sand's warnings suggest this is not yet a <strong>full normalization</strong> step.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">INDAMEX now implements <strong>full-loop Suez routing</strong> in both directions.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">First voyage: <strong>CMA CGM VERDI</strong>, 15 January, Karachi → New York.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Transit time shortens by <strong>two weeks (77 days)</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Two vessels removed</strong> from rotation; capacity efficiency improves.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Suez return is not yet an industry-wide trend; only 120 transits occurred in November 2025.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Spot rates: FE–USEC <strong>–57%</strong>, FE–NEU <strong>–53%</strong>; further capacity additions could depress prices further.</p></li>
</ul>
