<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>According to data released by the Intermodal Association of North America (IANA)</strong>, <strong>September 2025 intermodal shipments showed a year-over-year growth of 2.4%</strong>. This growth exceeded <strong>the 1.6% increase in August</strong>, but remained below <strong>July's 4.4% expansion</strong>. The elevated growth seen in July had been driven by <strong>frontloading activities ahead of the White House's temporary pause on reciprocal tariffs</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In September, <strong>total units transported reached 1,550,599</strong>.</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Trailer</strong> shipments declined <strong>19.7% to 37,757 units</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Domestic container</strong> shipments increased <strong>5.8% to 745,507 units</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Total domestic equipment</strong> (trailers plus containers) volume grew <strong>4.2%, reaching 783,264 units</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>ISO (international) container shipments</strong> showed <strong>0.6% growth to 767,335 units</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">In the first nine months of the year, total volume reached <strong>13,891,972 units</strong>, representing <strong>a 3.8% increase</strong> compared to the same period last year. During this period, <strong>trailers declined 20.8%</strong>, while <strong>domestic container shipments increased 3.5%</strong>. ISO containers rose <strong>5.5% to 7,102,111 units</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to IANA's published <strong>"Intermodal Quarterly"</strong> report, <strong>second quarter volume grew 2.4% to 4,579,798 units</strong>, marking <strong>the seventh consecutive quarter of growth</strong>. This growth streak followed <strong>eight consecutive quarters of decline experienced during 2023–2024</strong>. The growth rate in the first quarter stood at <strong>6.3%</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The report noted that <strong>uncertainties in tariffs and trade policies</strong> persist, but positive indicators such as <strong>a 3.7% increase in retail sales</strong>, <strong>the manufacturing index reaching its highest level since 2018 (100.8)</strong>, and <strong>GDP growth estimates of 3%</strong> are providing support to the intermodal sector.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">IANA stated that the strong performance in the first half of the year was supported by the "<strong>pulling forward of shipments before tariffs take effect</strong>" strategy, which created opportunities for both <strong>domestic</strong> and <strong>international intermodal transportation</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, <strong>IANA President and CEO Anne Reinke</strong> emphasized that <strong>uncertainty has increased for the third quarter and beyond</strong> due to the White House's <strong>reciprocal tariffs that took effect today</strong>. Reinke noted that in recent years a "<strong>flatter</strong>" peak season has been experienced, this trend is expected to continue in 2025, and <strong>peak demand may shift toward year-end</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Reinke also stated that <strong>Surface Transportation Board (STB)</strong> data on the current state of intermodal service indicates that the "<strong>system is functioning properly</strong>".</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Gross Transportation Consulting President Larry Gross</strong> similarly noted that <strong>service levels are strong</strong> and <strong>the number of trains waiting at rail terminals is historically low</strong>. Gross stated, "This shows that the system is operating with adequate staffing and resources. Despite fluctuations caused by tariffs, the system is maintaining its resilience." He also added that <strong>the ports of Los Angeles and Long Beach reached record volumes in July</strong>, yet <strong>there was no congestion across the network</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Looking at the overall picture, <strong>as of September 2025, the intermodal transportation sector</strong> is <strong>continuing its steady growth despite high cost pressures and trade uncertainties</strong>. Analysts believe that growth will be <strong>moderate but sustainable</strong> in the coming period due to the <strong>U.S. tariff policies</strong> and <strong>global supply chain dynamics</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>September 2025 intermodal volume grew 2.4%</strong>, reaching a total of 1.55 million units.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Trailer shipments declined 19.7%</strong>, while <strong>domestic containers rose 5.8%</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>ISO container shipments showed 0.6% growth</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Growth for the first 9 months of the year reached 3.8%</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>IANA</strong> indicated that the increase stemmed from "<strong>shipment pulling ahead of tariffs</strong>".</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>STB data</strong> shows that the intermodal network is <strong>operating smoothly</strong> and <strong>there is no congestion at ports</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The 2025 peak season</strong> will trend <strong>later and flatter</strong> than in previous years.</p></li>
</ul>
