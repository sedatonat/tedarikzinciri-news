<p>Amazon's plan to build dozens of warehouses across rural America is expanding last-mile coverage. The initiative employs an ecosystem of micro-fulfillment centers, regional sortation hubs, and local delivery stations to enhance service efficiency. The investment shortens delivery times, extends reach, and enables Prime delivery promises across more regions. The network design adopts a hybrid model between hub-and-spoke and point-to-point architectures, with inventory placement managed through AI demand planning. Multiplier effects are expected on local employment and small-business supply chains.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Overall, the project both increases logistics capacity and democratizes e-commerce access for rural markets.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>\n1.
