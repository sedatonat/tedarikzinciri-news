<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Demand forecasting</strong> has entered a new phase with data enrichment and AI-powered analytics. Today's successful forecasting systems rely not only on historical sales data, but on multidimensional inputs such as <strong>POS (point-of-sale)</strong> information, <strong>promotion plans</strong>, <strong>price elasticity</strong>, <strong>weather data</strong>, and <strong>social media signals</strong>. This integration significantly improves the accuracy of <strong>demand sensing</strong> models in short-term forecasts.</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In the medium and long term, <strong>hierarchical forecasting</strong> and <strong>probabilistic models</strong> come into play. <strong>Quantile regression</strong> and <strong>Bayesian approaches</strong> move beyond classical deterministic models, representing <strong>uncertainty more realistically</strong>. As a result, planning processes shift from mere "point estimates" to <strong>risk-based decisions</strong> grounded in probability distributions.</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In technical architecture, solutions such as <strong>feature stores</strong>, <strong>RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)</strong>, <strong>vector databases</strong>, and <strong>AutoML (Automated Machine Learning)</strong> are becoming increasingly prevalent. These tools provide speed and consistency in model training, while <strong>causal inference</strong> and <strong>explainability</strong> methods facilitate acceptance of models in the business world.</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">From an operational rhythm perspective, strong <strong>S&OP (Sales & Operations Planning)</strong> or <strong>IBP (Integrated Business Planning)</strong> integration ensures that forecasts work in synchronization with planning processes. Forecast outputs feed directly into production planning, inventory management, and capacity allocation.</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In distribution and inventory strategies, <strong>MEIO (Multi-Echelon Inventory Optimization)</strong> solutions create confidence interval-based inventory policies. These policies are linked with <strong>allocation</strong> and <strong>substitution</strong> rules to minimize inventory costs while maintaining customer service levels. Demand uncertainty is thus managed within an optimized flexibility framework.</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">At the governance layer, <strong>Model Risk Management (MRM)</strong>, <strong>drift monitoring</strong>, and <strong>data ethics principles</strong> are critical. These structures continuously monitor model performance, detect deviations early, and ensure the long-term reliability of forecasting systems.</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, AI-powered demand forecasting is no longer merely an analytical tool; it has become an integral component of business strategy through the integration of <strong>data, algorithms, and processes</strong>. The supply chains of the future will be shaped by the <strong>speed, accuracy, and adaptive capability</strong> that these systems provide.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>Key Points:</strong></p>\n<ul>\n  <li><p>Demand sensing strengthens the short term.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Probabilistic approaches reflect uncertainty.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Feature stores and AutoML provide speed and quality.</p></li>\n  <li><p>MEIO optimizes inventory policy.</p></li>\n  <li><p>MRM and drift monitoring strengthen reliability.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p>----------</p>\n<p><strong>News Link:</strong> <a href="https://www.supplychainbrain.com/articles/41647-navigating-the-future-of-demand-forecasting"><u>https://www.supplychainbrain.com/articles/41647-navigating-the-future-of-demand-forecasting</u></a></p>\n<p>----------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
