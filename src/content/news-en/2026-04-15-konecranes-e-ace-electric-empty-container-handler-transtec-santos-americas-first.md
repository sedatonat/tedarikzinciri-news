<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Konecranes</strong> is deploying <strong>electric empty container handler</strong> technology in the Americas for the first time. Two <strong>E-ACE 7/8 ECC 90</strong> electric empty container handlers will be delivered to <strong>Transtec World</strong>'s container depot at the <strong>Port of Santos</strong> in Brazil. The order was placed in Q4 2025 with delivery scheduled for Q2 2026. The units will support stacking and repositioning operations across Transtec World's multi-yard network.</p><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Transtec World</strong>, one of the largest container depot operators at the Port of Santos, <strong>manages eight yards</strong> and handles <strong>more than 710,000 containers</strong> annually. The new electric units are intended to support high-frequency container movement and yard efficiency. Transtec World Operational Director <strong>Rogério Oliveira</strong> said they chose Konecranes for its brand quality and strong local support in Brazil.</p><p style="text-align: justify">Konecranes' electric ECC series offers zero emissions, lower noise, and operational cost advantages. The near-zero carbon footprint over the operating life of the units directly supports ports' sustainability targets. This first deployment in the Americas is expected to set a benchmark for other container depot operators in the region. Brazil is taking a leading role in electric port equipment adoption across Latin America.</p>
