<p style="text-align: justify">Following negotiations with the <strong>Teamsters</strong> union, <strong>United Parcel Service (UPS)</strong> agreed to cap its <strong>Driver Choice Program</strong> — a voluntary <strong>buyout</strong> — at <strong>7,500 drivers</strong>. The first 7,500 applicants among the company's roughly 105,000 drivers will be eligible for the program with a <strong>$150,000</strong> severance package.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The agreement is critical for UPS to execute its 2026 plan to reduce the workforce by <strong>30,000 positions</strong> in a disciplined manner. A significant share of the program is expected to be carried out in the <strong>first half of 2026</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Industry analysts assess that the decision, combined with <strong>parcel volume normalization</strong> and <strong>automation investments</strong>, will support UPS in tightening its cost structure. The Teamsters side stressed that the cap is an important gain for protecting frontline employment.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. The Driver Choice Program has been capped at 7,500 drivers.<br>
2. The severance package is $150,000 per driver.<br>
3. The total driver workforce is roughly 105,000.<br>
4. UPS targets a 30,000-position reduction in 2026.<br>
5. Most of the program will be executed in the first half of 2026.</p>
