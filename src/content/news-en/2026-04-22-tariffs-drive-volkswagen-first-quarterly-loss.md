<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Volkswagen</strong> reported a third-quarter operating loss of $1.5 billion &mdash; a result driven by ongoing financial pressure from Trump administration tariffs and a costly retreat from electric vehicles at its <strong>Porsche</strong> brand. <strong>Volkswagen</strong> expects tariffs to cost the company more than $5.7 billion by year-end and anticipates an additional $5.4 billion hit from its decision to reverse the all-electric strategy at <strong>Porsche</strong>. In a statement on October 30, <strong>Arno Antlitz</strong>, chief financial officer of the automotive manufacturer, addressed these results. This marks <strong>Volkswagen</strong>'s first quarterly loss in five years. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Volkswagen Group</strong> (<strong>Volkswagen AG</strong>), headquartered in <strong>Wolfsburg, Germany</strong> and founded in 1937, is the world's second-largest automotive manufacturer, after <strong>Toyota</strong>. Core brands include <strong>Volkswagen</strong>, <strong>Audi</strong>, <strong>Porsche</strong>, <strong>Lamborghini</strong>, <strong>Bentley</strong>, <strong>Bugatti</strong> (via <strong>Mate Rimac</strong> joint venture), <strong>SEAT</strong>, <strong>CUPRA</strong>, <strong>Škoda</strong>, <strong>Scania</strong>, <strong>MAN</strong>, and <strong>Ducati</strong>, along with <strong>VW Commercial Vehicles</strong>. The photograph, credited to <strong>iStock aquatarkus</strong>, was taken at the <strong>Volkswagen</strong> facility in <strong>Mosel, Germany</strong>. <strong>Mosel</strong> is located in <strong>Saxony</strong> near <strong>Zwickau</strong>, home to <strong>VW</strong>'s electric vehicle production plant for the <strong>ID. Series</strong> (ID.3, ID.4, ID.5).</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Porsche AG</strong> (<strong>Porsche Automobil Holding SE</strong> as majority shareholder) has major product lines including the <strong>Cayenne</strong>, <strong>Macan</strong>, <strong>Panamera</strong>, <strong>Taycan</strong>, and <strong>Boxster/Cayman</strong> alongside its iconic <strong>911</strong>. <strong>Porsche</strong> had aggressively targeted having 80% of new vehicle sales electric by 2030, but is reversing this goal in 2024–2025. The <strong>Macan EV</strong> reached production in 2024, though a fully electric <strong>911</strong> version is being shelved. Launches of the <strong>Cayenne EV</strong> and <strong>Panamera EV</strong> are being delayed as the company continues with internal combustion and hybrid variants. <strong>Oliver Blume</strong> serves as CEO of both <strong>Porsche</strong> and the <strong>Volkswagen Group</strong>. <strong>Audi</strong> is planning to close its <strong>Brussels</strong> facility due to the end of production for the <strong>e-tron GT</strong> and <strong>Q8 e-tron</strong>. <strong>VW</strong> plans to wind down <strong>ID.3</strong> production early at its <strong>Wolfsburg</strong> plant. <strong>Cariad</strong>, a <strong>VW Group</strong> subsidiary handling software and EV electronic architecture, has experienced delays and leadership changes. A $5.8 billion joint venture with <strong>Rivian</strong> was established to address <strong>VW</strong>'s software and EE architecture challenges.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the impact of <strong>Trump 2.0</strong> tariffs on global automotive is significant. <strong>Section 232 auto tariffs</strong> apply a 25% rate to all vehicles imported into the <strong>U.S.</strong>, including parts not in compliance with <strong>USMCA</strong>. The <strong>EU</strong> expects to negotiate a 15% reciprocal tariff rate with <strong>Trump 2.0</strong>, though for autos this represents a net increase. <strong>Volkswagen</strong>, <strong>Audi</strong>, <strong>Porsche</strong>, <strong>BMW</strong>, and <strong>Mercedes-Benz</strong> are major exporters to the <strong>U.S.</strong> market and operate local production facilities (<strong>VW Chattanooga, TN</strong>; <strong>BMW Spartanburg, SC</strong>; <strong>Mercedes Tuscaloosa, AL</strong>). <strong>Audi</strong>, as the <strong>VW Group</strong> brand without local U.S. production, faces the greatest tariff impact. <strong>Volkswagen</strong> is reviving the <strong>Scout Motors</strong> brand and building a new EV SUV and pickup facility in <strong>South Carolina</strong> with annual capacity for 200,000 vehicles, backed by a $2 billion investment.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, slowing global EV demand is forcing multiple automakers to revise their strategies. <strong>Ford</strong> is reducing <strong>F-150 Lightning</strong> production and discounting the <strong>Mustang Mach-E</strong>. <strong>General Motors</strong> (<strong>GM</strong>) is accelerating its <strong>Ultium</strong> platform with core EV lines including the <strong>Chevrolet Equinox EV</strong>, <strong>Blazer EV</strong>, <strong>Cadillac Lyriq</strong>, and <strong>GMC Hummer EV</strong>, though lowering annual production targets. <strong>Stellantis</strong> is similarly revising its EV strategy following <strong>Carlos Tavares</strong>'s departure as CEO at year-end 2024. <strong>Hyundai</strong>, <strong>Kia</strong>, and <strong>Genesis</strong> are maintaining relatively stronger EV momentum. <strong>Toyota</strong> is relatively sheltered from EV slowdown thanks to its aggressive hybrid strategy. Chinese EV manufacturers including <strong>BYD</strong>, <strong>SAIC</strong>, <strong>Geely</strong>, <strong>Chery</strong>, and <strong>Great Wall Motors</strong> are expanding exports globally, particularly into the <strong>European</strong> market. <strong>EU CBAM</strong> and potential tariffs on Chinese EVs aim to protect <strong>European</strong> manufacturers. Ultimately, <strong>Volkswagen</strong>'s first quarterly loss in five years signals that traditional automakers are undergoing serious structural transformation under the dual pressure of tariffs and the EV transition.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Points:</strong><br>
1. <strong>Volkswagen</strong> reported a Q3 operating loss of $1.5 billion.<br>
2. Estimated tariff costs by year-end exceed $5.7 billion.<br>
3. <strong>Porsche</strong>'s all-electric strategy reversal carries an additional $5.4 billion hit.<br>
4. <strong>CFO Arno Antlitz</strong> announced the results on October 30.<br>
5. This marks <strong>VW</strong>'s first quarterly loss in five years.</p>
