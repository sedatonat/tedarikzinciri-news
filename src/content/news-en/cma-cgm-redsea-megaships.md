<p style="text-align: justify">CMA CGM is conducting <strong>its first large-scale test transit</strong> following two years of ongoing security crisis in the Red Sea. The movement of two megamax-class vessels operating on Ocean Alliance's NEU4 and NEU5 services <strong>toward the Suez Canal</strong> indicates that line operators are evaluating cautiously reopening the route.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Analyst <strong>Linerlytica</strong> reported that <strong>the 17,859 TEU CMA CGM Benjamin Franklin</strong> operating on the NEU4 service is progressing toward the Suez on its <strong>Europe outbound eastbound backhaul return to the Mediterranean</strong>. Similarly, the <strong>23,112 TEU CMA CGM Sorbonne</strong> on the NEU5 service is heading toward the Mediterranean in a similar fashion.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">These two vessels mark <strong>the first entry of Ocean Alliance's megaships</strong> into the conflict zone in two years, and carriers are believed to be testing whether the Red Sea route can be <strong>carefully brought back into service in a controlled manner</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Cautious Return: "A Test, Not a Full Return"</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">While container lines generally continue to stay away from the Red Sea, CMA CGM's move is not yet being interpreted as a full-scale return.<br>
According to industry sources, test voyages are being used to:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">measure new risk levels on the route,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">evaluate insurance costs,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">test the effectiveness of naval corridors,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">assess the operational feasibility of Suez transits<br>
</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Other Ocean Alliance members — COSCO, OOCL and Evergreen — have yet to signal a return to the region.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Why Did CMA CGM Launch Tests?</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to industry analysts:</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>1) Transit Costs and Cape of Good Hope Route Pressure</strong></h5>
<p>The cost and duration burden of Cape of Good Hope circumnavigations has become significantly more pronounced, particularly on empty return legs (eastbound backhaul).</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>2) EU Naval Corridors More Effective</strong></h5>
<p>Patrol capacity from the European Union and certain international security forces has increased in the second half of 2025.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>3) Trade Ceasefire and Pause in Houthi Attacks</strong></h5>
<p>While the Houthis' announcement of a temporary pause in attacks does not render the region entirely safe, it has enabled some lines to conduct controlled test returns.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Risks Remain High</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Although the movement of two megaships toward Suez appears encouraging, analysts emphasize that risk remains substantial:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Marine insurance premiums remain very high,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Signs of Houthi rearming persist,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Naval corridors in the Bab el-Mandeb do not provide complete protection,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">A single incident could force routes back to Cape of Good Hope routing.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">For this reason, these transits are regarded as <strong>"exploratory in nature."</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Impact on Asia–Europe Trade</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">If the Red Sea route can be reopened safely:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Transit times could <strong>be reduced by 10–14 days</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Fuel and freight costs could decline,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The cargo balance at European transhipment hubs could shift,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Global schedule disruptions from Cape of Good Hope routing could diminish.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, a full return is also noted to potentially trigger <strong>sudden capacity congestion and increased congestion at European ports</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">CMA CGM routed two megaships <strong>on the Suez/Red Sea corridor</strong> as a test.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Benjamin Franklin (17,859 TEU) and Sorbonne (23,112 TEU) are returning to the Mediterranean and proceeding toward Suez.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">This movement represents <strong>the first megaship transit in two years</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Reasons for test voyages: insurance, security, naval corridors and cost optimization.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Risk remains high; this is a <strong>trial</strong>, not a full return.</p></li>
</ul>
