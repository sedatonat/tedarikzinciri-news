<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Beşiktaş Shipping</strong> has announced a significant strategic decision following a serious maritime incident off Senegal's coast on the night of November 27, 2025: the company has declared its immediate termination of <strong>all maritime operations linked to Russia</strong>. This decision comes after the Panama-flagged <strong>M/T Mersin</strong> tanker under the company's management suffered four external explosions off the coast of Dakar.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to statements from the company and Senegal authorities, explosions occurring around <strong>23:45 UTC</strong> resulted in seawater entering the vessel's <strong>engine room</strong> and the tanker experiencing <strong>propulsion loss</strong>. M/T Mersin was carrying <strong>diesel fuel</strong> at the time of the incident. Beşiktaş Shipping and local authorities confirmed that no <strong>casualty</strong> or <strong>injury</strong> occurred on the vessel. Additionally, no <strong>fuel spill</strong> was detected. Operations teams noted that the vessel remained in <strong>stable</strong> condition, with no situation threatening navigation safety and no environmental risk posed by the vessel.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Following the incident, <strong>tugs</strong>, specialized salvage teams, and naval assets were deployed, with <strong>containment boom</strong> positioned around the vessel. The company stated it is in full cooperation with insurers and Senegal authorities in technical and legal investigations. The cause of the explosions remains unclear; neither Beşiktaş Shipping nor Senegal authorities have provided any assessment regarding the attacking party or motivation.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Certain sections of the international media suggested that M/T Mersin may have been involved in "<strong>Russia-linked trade</strong>". This context is being associated with recent developments concerning <strong>shadow fleet</strong> vessels carrying Russian oil and tankers under sanctions pressure. However, no official institution, including Beşiktaş Shipping, has confirmed that the incident is part of a Russia-related conflict.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The most striking element in the company's statement is the decision to <strong>completely suspend Russia-linked voyages</strong>. Beşiktaş Shipping announced this decision while reiterating its full compliance with <strong>G7/EU price cap</strong> regimes. The statement emphasized that regional security has "deteriorated significantly" and that risks regarding vessel and crew safety have now reached <strong>unacceptable</strong> levels. The company stressed that <strong>crew safety</strong> and <strong>asset protection</strong> are the highest priorities.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Beşiktaş Shipping, part of Beşiktaş Group, is an established Turkish company that has been operating since the 1960s. The company is known to manage a fleet of approximately <strong>29 vessels</strong>, serving as both <strong>shipowner</strong> and <strong>technical manager</strong>. For this reason, the decision creates a notable shift in the Turkish maritime sector and particularly in Russia-related trade.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Beşiktaş Shipping's decision coincides with a period in the final quarter of 2025 marked by escalating drone threats, shadow fleet incidents, and operational pressures stemming from sanctions. Rising risk levels in the Black Sea and West African corridors are prompting many shipowners and technical management firms to reassess the region. The M/T Mersin incident stands out as one of the latest examples demonstrating how this risk landscape can directly impact commercial tanker operations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">A completed timeline for technical investigations and updates on M/T Mersin's status has not yet been announced. However, the vessel has been confirmed as stabilized and poses no environmental risk. The company's suspension of Russia-linked shipments may lead to new route preferences and risk distribution in the regional tanker market.</p>
