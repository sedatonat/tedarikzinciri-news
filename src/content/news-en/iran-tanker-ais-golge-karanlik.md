<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The National Iranian Tanker Company (NITC)</strong> fleet has returned to <strong>"dark operations"</strong> following <strong>three days of extraordinary AIS (Automatic Identification System) signal transparency.</strong></p>
<p><br>
 According to analysis by <strong>maritime AI firm Windward,</strong> between <strong>October 12–14, 2025,</strong> <strong>52 out of 88 Iranian-flagged tankers</strong> actively broadcast AIS signals — a situation unprecedented in the past seven years.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Analysts are struggling to make sense of the reason behind this brief window of transparency.</p>
<p><br>
The tankers that switched their AIS systems back on are now completely "dark" as of <strong>October 17, with only one vessel remaining visible.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Three Days of Visibility: A First in 7.5 Years</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to <strong>TankerTrackers.com</strong> data, Iranian-flagged tankers <strong>kept their AIS systems open without signal spoofing for the first time in seven and a half years.</strong></p>
<p><br>
Windward described the development in these terms:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Iranian tankers that have long been conducting 'dark' operations simultaneously opening their AIS signals is a situation never before observed."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">The locations of the tankers broadcasting AIS signals were spread across a wide region:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Iran's Kharg Island,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Indonesia,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Near Chinese ports,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Singapore Strait routes.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Notably, <strong>nine major NITC tankers were identified within Malaysia's exclusive economic zone (EEZ).</strong></p>
<p><br>
This region is a known hub for <strong>floating storage and ship-to-ship transfer (STS) operations</strong> of Iranian oil under U.S. sanctions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Brief Transparency Period: Signals Go Dark Again</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, this transparency was short-lived.<br>
 <strong>After October 14, AIS signals gradually disappeared,</strong> and <strong>by October 17, nearly the entire NITC fleet</strong> had stopped broadcasting signals.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Windward characterized this as <strong>"a simultaneous and coordinated withdrawal."</strong></p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"This represents a return to the Iranian fleet's typical operational model. Tankers become briefly visible only during Singapore Strait transits and then disappear again."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Timing Raises Questions</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to analysts, this sudden transparency period <strong>coincided with significant geopolitical developments:</strong></p>
<ol>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>United Nations sanctions against Iran were reinstated through the "snapback" mechanism.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Malaysian government</strong> launched <strong>a new wave of oversight against unauthorized STS operations in its waters.</strong></p></li>
</ol>
<p style="text-align: justify">Windward assesses that these two developments may have triggered the tankers' decision to temporarily broadcast signals:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Opening signals may have been a temporary legal compliance maneuver or a tactical move against inspection pressure."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, the motivation remains unclear. <strong>TankerTrackers.com</strong> commented, "It is unclear whether this change was a political decision by Iranian authorities or the result of another factor."</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Typical Behavioral Patterns of Iran's Tanker Fleet</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Iran's tanker fleet has long been part of a <strong>covert shipping network known as the "shadow fleet."</strong><br>
These vessels typically:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Turn off AIS systems to conceal their routes,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Transfer oil through ship-to-ship (STS) operations under different flags,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Broadcast AIS signals only temporarily during shipments to Asian markets (particularly China).</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Most vessels <strong>broadcast signals briefly while transiting the Singapore Strait</strong> and then <strong>disappear again near the Riau Islands.</strong></p>
<p><br>
This tactic is employed both to <strong>evade international sanctions</strong> and to <strong>ensure continuity of oil exports.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Windward: Early Detection via Artificial Intelligence</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Windward's <strong>Early Detection</strong> solution was the first system to detect this unusual signal traffic during <strong>October 12–14.</strong></p>
<p><br>
AI-based analysis confirmed the NITC fleet's pattern of <strong>simultaneous signal switching in different regions.</strong><br>
This simultaneity points less to coincidence and more to <strong>a coordinated decision-making mechanism.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: The Shadow Fleet's Veil Continues</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The brief AIS visibility of Iranian tankers created a notable anomaly <strong>in terms of international oil trade and sanctions monitoring systems.</strong></p>
<p><br>
But this "transparency period" has ended, and the Iranian fleet has returned to <strong>its invisibility strategy.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This incident reflects the delicate balance between <strong>geopolitical pressure, inspection risk, and sanctions compliance.</strong></p>
<p><br>
The brief AIS disclosure will likely be remembered as <strong>a temporary response to international pressure</strong> — but <strong>Iran's policy of using shadow fleets in oil shipping</strong> appears unchanged.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>October 12–14, 2025:</strong> <strong>52 out of 88 Iranian tankers broadcast AIS signals</strong> – the widest visibility achieved for the first time in seven years.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Windward:</strong> "This situation represents an unprecedented mass signal broadcast by the Iranian fleet."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>As of October 17,</strong> <strong>the entire NITC fleet has switched off AIS signals again.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Observations coincided with <strong>STS operations off Malaysian waters</strong> and <strong>the reinstatement of UN sanctions.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The Iranian fleet continues to <strong>use dark routes and AIS manipulation methods to transport oil to China.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, this incident provides a new example of how "shadow fleets" circumvent international energy monitoring systems.</p></li>
</ul>
