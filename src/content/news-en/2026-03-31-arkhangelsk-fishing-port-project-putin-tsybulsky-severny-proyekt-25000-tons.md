<p style="text-align: justify">Russian President <strong>Vladimir Putin</strong> held a working meeting with <strong>Governor of the Arkhangelsk Region Alexander Tsybulsky</strong>. According to the Kremlin press office, in response to the President's question about key investment projects, Tsybulsky outlined plans to <strong>build a major logistics complex as part of the Arkhangelsk seaport</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The facility will <strong>function as a fishing port</strong>. It will feature storage infrastructure and refrigerators capable of storing up to <strong>25,000 tons of frozen fish and 200 tons of live crab</strong>. Offering storage capacity for both export and domestic markets, the facility aims to boost the economic value-add of Russia's Arctic Ocean fisheries.</p><p style="text-align: justify">Two more <strong>logistics projects</strong> in Arkhangelsk are being implemented by <strong>Severny Proyekt, which operates a fleet of 25 vessels</strong>. Arkhangelsk is one of Russia's largest commercial ports on the Arctic Ocean and provides access to the Northern Sea Route. The region is also positioned as a logistics hub for fisheries, forest products, and Arctic oil and gas projects. The new investments aim to capture trade opportunities arising from the northern routes opened up by climate change.</p>
