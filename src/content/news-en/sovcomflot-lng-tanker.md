<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sovcomflot (SCF)</strong>, a Russian shipping company, has announced the delivery of a new-generation <strong>LNG-ready MR tanker</strong> "<strong>Ivan Aivazovsky</strong>" built by <strong>Zvezda Shipyard</strong> in Russia's Far Eastern region. The company positions this vessel, named after the renowned Russian maritime landscape painter <strong>Ivan Aivazovsky</strong>, as the first ship in a new tanker series specifically developed for product transportation in ice conditions. The tanker will operate under the Russian flag and will play a strategic role in Sovcomflot's modernization of its product tanker fleet.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new vessel has been designed to meet growing demand for Russia's product transportation along icy water routes. The ship holds <strong>Ice Class 1B</strong> certification, which enables safe operations on routes with moderate ice conditions during winter months. Sovcomflot emphasizes that vessels in this segment are increasingly needed, particularly in the Baltic, Black Sea, and along Russia's northern offshore routes.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">"<strong>Ivan Aivazovsky</strong>" also stands out as the first <strong>double-fuel R tanker</strong> built in Russia. This hybrid design demonstrates that the vessel has been constructed with both conventional fuel capabilities and infrastructure suitable for transition to <strong>LNG propulsion</strong> systems in the future. As demand for environmentally compliant, low-emission vessels increases within Russia's tanker fleet, Sovcomflot is aligning this vessel with medium-term energy transition goals through this delivery.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The vessel's technical specifications are as follows:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>LOA:</strong> 183 m</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Beam:</strong> 32.2 m</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Draft:</strong> 13.4 m</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Deadweight:</strong> 49,800 tons</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ice Class:</strong> 1B</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">These dimensions and capacity figures classify the vessel within the MR (Medium Range) segment of the product tanker market. The vessel is particularly well-suited for continuous transportation of petroleum products, jet fuel, diesel, and clean petroleum products along icy water corridors.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Zvezda Shipyard has assumed an increasingly important role in Russia's strategic energy transportation projects in recent years. The shipyard stands at the center of a long-term industrial development program designed by the Russian state to enhance maritime capacity and is expanding its capabilities in LNG-supported ship construction. The delivery of "Ivan Aivazovsky" represents a concrete example of this expansion process.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The fact that Sovcomflot's entire new tanker series has been designed for operations in icy waters reflects growing production and export volumes in Russia's northern energy corridors. Against the backdrop of international sanctions and changing global energy trade dynamics, the new tanker class is expected to provide safer and more energy-efficient voyages, particularly on routes serving Asian markets.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, the LNG-ready design is presented as an integral part of the company's long-term <strong>carbon footprint reduction</strong> strategy. As tanker operators worldwide accelerate their transition to zero or low-emission vessels, Sovcomflot is preparing its fleet for the future through this delivery in terms of environmental compliance.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">"<strong>Ivan Aivazovsky</strong>," as the first vessel in the new tanker series, establishes the technical design standard for the series. According to Sovcomflot's statement, the series raises company standards with respect to ice class, low-emission profile, fuel efficiency, and route safety criteria.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Overall, the delivery of the new LNG-ready MR tanker enhances Sovcomflot's product transportation capacity for icy waters and positions the company more strongly within the region's energy logistics network. At the same time, it represents a strategic turning point for the development of Russia's domestic shipbuilding industry.</p>
<p><br></p>
