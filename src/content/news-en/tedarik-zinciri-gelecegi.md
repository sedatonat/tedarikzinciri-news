<p><strong>RR Donnelley</strong> has released a survey showing that 97% of supply chain leaders are planning major restructuring initiatives over the next two years.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>These leaders are focused on strategic changes to boost operational efficiency and adapt to shifting market conditions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>73% of survey respondents report that they have begun restructuring efforts to make their supply chains more resilient and agile.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>This trend reflects companies' increased emphasis on flexibility and resilience following recent global supply chain disruptions.</p>
<p>Additionally, research conducted by <strong>Gartner</strong> reveals that 73% of companies are reshaping their supply chains for resilience and agility.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>This demonstrates that companies are taking significant steps to make their supply chains more flexible and robust.</p>
<p>Notably, supply chain and procurement leaders are adopting a cautious approach due to the impact of factors such as geopolitical conflicts, inflation, and changes in interest rates on business.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>This indicates that companies are inclined to use their supply chains to advance social responsibility and corporate values.</p>
<p>Furthermore, a guide prepared by <strong>GEODIS</strong> offers six steps for importers to prepare for 2025 tariffs.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>These steps include calculating tariff exposure, optimizing product classifications, and researching alternative suppliers.</p>
<p>These strategies aim to help companies manage costs and maintain competitive advantages.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>In conclusion</strong>, supply chain leaders are restructuring their operations, enhancing resilience, and taking strategic steps to prepare for future challenges and adapt to changing trade policies.</p>
