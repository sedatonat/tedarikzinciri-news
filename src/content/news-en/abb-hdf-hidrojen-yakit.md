<p style="text-align: justify">In line with decarbonization targets in the maritime sector, fuel cells are assuming an increasingly strategic role. In this context, <strong>ABB</strong> and <strong>HDF Energy</strong> have signed a <strong>Joint Development Agreement (JDA)</strong> to develop <strong>high-power fuel cell units</strong> for maritime applications. The agreement aims to pave the way for the use of hydrogen fuel cells at <strong>megawatt scale</strong> in large vessels.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The collaboration builds on the foundation established by a <strong>Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) signed between the parties in 2020</strong>. With the new agreement, fuel cells are intended to move beyond pilot projects and become a <strong>commercially viable</strong> technology. According to the plan, <strong>pilot installations are expected to take place in 2028–2029</strong>, with <strong>series production to commence from 2030 onwards</strong>. This timeline reflects a trajectory parallel to the maturation process of alternative fuel technologies in shipping.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The fuel cell unit to be developed is being designed with a capacity suitable for use in large seagoing vessels such as <strong>container feeder ships</strong> and <strong>liquefied hydrogen carriers</strong>. This represents a significant expansion of the application scope for fuel cells, which have so far been largely confined to small and medium-sized marine vessels. The project enables hydrogen-based power generation to become a power source that supports or complements main propulsion systems.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Under the collaboration, <strong>HDF Energy</strong> is responsible for the <strong>design and manufacture</strong> of large-scale fuel cell units. <strong>ABB</strong> brings its expertise in <strong>power converters</strong>, <strong>power management systems</strong>, and <strong>electrical and control integration</strong> to the project. The parties work together on technical specifications, conceptual design, and commercial opportunities. This structure delivers not only an equipment development process but also an end-to-end <strong>systems integration approach</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">One of the most significant advantages that fuel cells offer in shipping is their potential for <strong>emission reduction</strong>. Particularly when <strong>green hydrogen</strong> is used as fuel, the carbon footprint approaches nearly zero. The high-power unit to be developed enables replacement of <strong>diesel auxiliary genset</strong> systems used on existing vessels with hydrogen fuel cells. This transition presents a critical opportunity not only for newly built ships but also for <strong>retrofitting existing fleets</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">At the technical core of the project lies the <strong>ABB Onboard DC Grid™</strong> power system. This system enables the fuel cell unit to integrate seamlessly with other power sources such as <strong>battery energy storage systems (BESS)</strong>. As a result, the <strong>operational range and flexibility</strong> of hybrid power systems improve. Fuel cells play a role not only in meeting steady loads but also in balancing fluctuating energy demands.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Beyond that, the developed unit is not limited to ship applications alone. Its use as an auxiliary power source within <strong>shore power</strong> and <strong>charging infrastructure</strong> at ports is also under consideration. Particularly in ports with limited grid capacity, fuel cells position themselves as an important support tool for meeting <strong>peak power</strong> demands. This approach creates a more integrated <strong>electrification ecosystem</strong> between shipping and port infrastructure.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">HDF Energy CEO <strong>Hanane El Hamraoui</strong> emphasizes that this collaboration offers a practical and scalable solution for decarbonizing the maritime sector. ABB Marine & Ports President <strong>Rune Braastad</strong> notes that significant progress has been made for some time in developing applicable fuel cell solutions for large vessels, and this agreement advances the process one step further.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Overall, the ABB and HDF Energy collaboration demonstrates the potential for fuel cells to transition from being <strong>an auxiliary technology</strong> in shipping to becoming <strong>a key component of the main power architecture</strong>. This step shows that decarbonization targets in shipping are increasingly grounded on a more realistic technical and commercial foundation.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>\n<p><strong>Key Points</strong></p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">ABB and HDF Energy have signed an agreement to develop megawatt-scale fuel cells for maritime use.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Pilot implementations are planned for 2028–2029, with series production to commence in 2030.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Fuel cells are being designed with capacity suitable for use in large seagoing vessels.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The use of <strong>green hydrogen</strong> aims to significantly reduce emissions in shipping.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">ABB Onboard DC Grid™ provides integration and flexibility in hybrid power systems.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p>----------</p>\n<p><strong>News Link: </strong><a href="https://gcaptain.com/abb-and-hdf-energy-to-develop-high-power-fuel-cell-unit-for-large-seagoing-ships/"><u>https://gcaptain.com/abb-and-hdf-energy-to-develop-high-power-fuel-cell-unit-for-large-seagoing-ships/</u></a></p>\n<p>--------------------</p>\n<p><strong>Author: </strong><a href="http://sedatonat.com/"><u>SedatOnat.com</u></a></p>\n<p>--------------------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
