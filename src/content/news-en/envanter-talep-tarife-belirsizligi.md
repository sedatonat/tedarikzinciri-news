<p style="text-align: justify">Demand for industrial and logistics real estate in the United States gained renewed momentum in the third quarter of 2025, signaling <strong>robust growth</strong> despite tariff-driven economic uncertainty. According to a report published by Colliers, Q3 <strong>net absorption (occupied space growth)</strong> reached <strong>60 million sq ft</strong>, marking the highest level since the first quarter of 2023. This increase represents a surge of approximately <strong>20 million sq ft</strong> compared to the same period last year.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">While Colliers's analysis shows that the year-to-date total for the first three quarters still lags behind 2024, the "aggressive recovery" observed in the third quarter demonstrates that momentum has returned to the industrial real estate market. <strong>Phoenix</strong> and <strong>Indianapolis</strong> are leading demand growth, driven by strong big-box demand, build-to-suit projects, and large-scale user transactions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Demand Rising, Construction Pipeline Narrowing</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">While demand for industrial real estate is climbing, the opposite trend is emerging on the new construction side.</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">In Q3, the total industrial space under construction fell to <strong>270 million sq ft</strong>, the lowest level since 2018.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">This indicates that developers are focusing on controlling excessive supply risk and making projects <strong>demand-driven</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, construction activity remains robust in some high-demand markets such as <strong>Dallas–Fort Worth</strong> and <strong>Houston</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Colliers notes that this shift points toward a more balanced supply-demand environment:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Most new projects are concentrating in markets with sustainable tenant demand."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Companies Accelerate Warehouse Decisions Despite Tariff Uncertainty</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Prologis CEO <strong>Dan Letter</strong> stated during an October earnings call that companies are beginning to view tariff noise as "a short-term input":</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Customers have become less sensitive to short-term noise and continue with their long-term investments."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">This trend is particularly evident among:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">e-commerce companies,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">retail giants,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">manufacturers strengthening their supply chains to improve cost and service levels<br>
.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Recent companies announcing new warehouse investments include <strong>Dollar Tree</strong> and <strong>Ahold Delhaize</strong>, while <strong>New Balance</strong> opened a new distribution center in Salt Lake City last month.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Letter, the aggressive investment appetite of large, well-capitalized companies is setting a reference point for <strong>SMEs to follow the same path</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Vacancy Rates Signal Stabilization Ahead</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">As demand growth combines with gradual supply contraction, vacancy rates are expected to <strong>stabilize in coming quarters</strong>.<br>
In Q3, the industrial vacancy rate:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>7.4%</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">An increase of <strong>72 basis points</strong> year-over-year.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, despite this increase, the renewed acceleration in demand has the potential to balance vacancy levels.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sector Takeaway: "Warehouse Expansion Continues" Despite Tariff Uncertainty</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Throughout 2025, U.S. companies have faced:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">high tariffs,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">trade disputes,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">supply chain restructuring<br>
uncertainties.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Yet Q3 data shows that companies have not backed away from <strong>long-term warehouse and logistics investments</strong>.<br>
The key drivers include:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">e-commerce growth,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">nearshoring and friendshoring strategies,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">increased inventory holding trends (just-in-case inventory),</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">supply chain cost optimization needs.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Q3 2025 saw <strong>60 million sq ft</strong> of net absorption (+20M YoY).</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Phoenix and Indianapolis</strong> lead demand growth.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Industrial space under construction is at its <strong>lowest level since 2018 (270M sq ft)</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Companies continue <strong>long-term warehouse investments</strong> despite tariff uncertainty.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">E-commerce, retail, and supply chain transformations support storage demand.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Vacancy rate stands at 7.4%; supply-demand balance may stabilize in 2026.</p></li>
</ul>
