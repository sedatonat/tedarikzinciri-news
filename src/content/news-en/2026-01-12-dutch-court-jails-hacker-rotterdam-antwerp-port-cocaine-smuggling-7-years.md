<p style="text-align: justify">A Dutch appeals court has sentenced a 44-year-old man to <strong>seven years in prison</strong> for his role in a cyber-enabled drug smuggling operation that targeted major European ports, including Rotterdam and Antwerp. Judges found that the man provided the technical expertise that allowed an organised crime group to <strong>bypass port security systems</strong> and move cocaine through logistics hubs without detection. The scheme operated in 2020 and 2021 and relied on unauthorised access to internal port networks used to manage container movements and site access.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Prosecutors said the operation resulted in the import of approximately <strong>210 kilogrammes of cocaine</strong> through the <strong>Port of Rotterdam</strong>, one of Europe's busiest shipping centres. The drugs were concealed in a container of wine bottles aboard the cargo vessel <strong>Callao Express</strong>, which was intercepted by Dutch authorities in September 2020. Court findings demonstrated that the hacker penetrated port IT systems and <strong>manipulated container tracking records</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The incident underscores rising threats against the <strong>OT/IT cybersecurity</strong> of global ports. Container tracking systems, terminal operating systems (TOS) and <strong>handling PIN codes</strong> are among the primary targets of organised crime groups. Similar breaches have increased in recent years across Northern European hubs including Antwerp and Rotterdam. The ruling underscores the need for <strong>multi-layered access control and continuous monitoring</strong> in port IT/OT systems.</p>
