<p style="text-align: justify">Digital transformation is accelerating in the retail sector, but the success of this transformation largely depends on companies' <strong>data readiness</strong> levels. Research conducted by <strong>PYMTS</strong> and <strong>Caret from Fiserv</strong> between <strong>June 12 and August 7, 2024</strong> covered <strong>390 food and non-food retail executives</strong> at companies with annual revenues exceeding $500 million in 2023. The findings show that <strong>data-ready</strong> companies are achieving <strong>five times higher revenue growth</strong> compared to competitors lagging in data management.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Data-ready</strong> is defined as "<strong>the ability to access data that is correct, timely, secure, and formatted to support decision-making</strong>". Companies with this capability demonstrate superior performance not only financially, but also in operational areas such as <strong>agility, insight generation</strong>, and <strong>supply chain management</strong>. In particular, retailers with <strong>company-wide analytics teams</strong> have delivered <strong>nearly twice as much revenue growth</strong> compared to those without such teams.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the research, <strong>more than 50% of companies</strong> operate within <strong>silo structures</strong>. This situation complicates data sharing between departments, preventing <strong>holistic performance evaluation</strong>. Additionally, <strong>65% of companies lack access to real-time data</strong>, yet <strong>70% of participants</strong> state that this is "mission-critical" for their decision-making processes.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The fundamental causes of these data integrity gaps are identified as <strong>fragmented operations</strong>, <strong>lack of real-time data</strong>, and <strong>legacy systems</strong>. These three factors prevent companies from gaining momentum in <strong>innovation, customer acquisition, and supply chain efficiency</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Another important finding of the report is that data is actually the <strong>backbone of the business ecosystem</strong>. All operations depend on data flowing through software systems. If data is <strong>incorrect</strong>, <strong>incomplete</strong>, or comes from <strong>unreliable sources</strong>, this undermines the reliability of the software system and reduces the ability to generate <strong>actionable insight</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Therefore, businesses must first focus on <strong>data quality and access</strong>. Otherwise, analysis conducted with incorrect or fragmented data weakens <strong>strategic decision-making</strong> processes. In this context, the "data first, results later" approach becomes not a choice but a necessity for modern enterprises.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Data readiness is not merely a technological infrastructure issue; it is also a transformation that requires <strong>organizational culture</strong> and <strong>leadership vision</strong>. When companies free their data strategies from silo structures and consolidate them on a common platform, they strengthen not only insight generation, but also <strong>customer experience</strong> and <strong>supply chain visibility</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, the research demonstrates that companies with high data readiness grow faster, make more agile decisions, and adapt more easily to market changes. Businesses that fail to build a data-equipped structure fall behind in terms of innovation and competitive advantage.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li><p><strong>Data-ready</strong> companies increase their revenue by an average of <strong>5 times higher</strong>.</p></li>
  <li><p>Firms with <strong>company-wide analytics teams</strong> achieve <strong>nearly 100% additional growth</strong> compared to others.</p></li>
  <li><p><strong>50% of companies operate in silo structures</strong>, which limits data sharing and insight generation.</p></li>
  <li><p><strong>65% lack real-time data access</strong>; yet 70% of companies view this as critical.</p></li>
  <li><p><strong>Legacy systems</strong> are identified as one of the biggest obstacles to data integrity and operational agility.</p></li>
  <li><p>Companies with low data readiness suffer disadvantages in <strong>innovation, customer acquisition, and supply chain management</strong>.</p></li>
  <li><p>Reliable and timely data form the foundation of <strong>actionable insight</strong> and <strong>sustainable growth</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
