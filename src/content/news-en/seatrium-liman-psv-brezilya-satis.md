<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Singapore-based shipbuilding and marine engineering company Seatrium</strong> has decided to sell <strong>two platform supply vessels (PSVs)</strong> to <strong>Brazilian Posidonia Shipping and Trading.</strong> The total value of the transaction has been announced as <strong>$59.7 million.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The company stated that the transaction is part of its <strong>long-term strategy to divest non-core assets</strong> and <strong>increase capital efficiency and operational performance.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Details of the sale</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The transaction will be executed through <strong>the transfer of a 100% stake in Guanabara Navegação, Seatrium's Brazil-based wholly-owned subsidiary.</strong> This company was the owner of the two PSVs being sold.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In a statement from Seatrium, the company noted that the sale transaction is <strong>planned to be completed before the end of the financial year</strong> and will <strong>not have any adverse impact on the group's operations.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">It was emphasized that <strong>Posidonia Shipping and Trading is an independent third party with no direct connection to Seatrium.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic focus: return to core operations</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">In recent years, Seatrium has been undertaking a process of <strong>asset optimization and strategic simplification.</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br>
According to the company's statement, the sale of the PSV vessels will help direct capital resources <strong>toward core operational areas—engineering, energy and renewable projects—</strong> more effectively.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Proceeds from the sale will <strong>strengthen Seatrium's cash flow</strong> and <strong>increase the company's financial flexibility.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Company officials stated that this step is being taken in line with the <strong>"strategy to achieve a lighter balance sheet structure and profitable growth targets."</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Transfer of Guanabara Navegação</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under the transaction, all shares of <strong>Guanabara Navegação Ltda.,</strong> a Brazil-based ship-owning company, will be transferred to Posidonia.</p>
<p><br>
As a result, Posidonia will assume <strong>all operational assets and liabilities of Guanabara along with the two platform support vessels.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The sale is expected to contribute to <strong>the expansion of the marine services market in Brazil.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Seatrium's Brazil heritage</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Seatrium has been operating in Brazil for many years in the <strong>offshore energy and shipbuilding sectors.</strong></p>
<p><br>
Historically, the company's subsidiaries <strong>worked with Petrobras and other Latin American energy operators</strong> on significant platform, FPSO and maintenance projects.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, in recent times, Seatrium has <strong>diversified its global operations,</strong> focusing on renewable energy, LNG infrastructure and digital shipbuilding technologies.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Financial impact</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Seatrium disclosed that the aforementioned sale <strong>will not have a material impact on the company's income statement,</strong> but <strong>will strengthen cash flow.</strong></p>
<p><br>
According to the company's statement, the sale is <strong>"a decision that accelerates capital returns and supports long-term profitability."</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, while this sale reduces Seatrium's <strong>presence in the Brazil market,</strong> it serves the company's <strong>goal of making global operations more focused and efficient.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Posidonia's expansion move</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Posidonia Shipping and Trading</strong> is a private maritime company operating in offshore transport and support operations in Brazil.</p>
<p><br>
The purchase of two PSVs will <strong>increase the company's fleet capacity in the offshore energy sector</strong> and <strong>support oil and gas projects being conducted off the Brazilian coast.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: strategic portfolio simplification</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">This sale is being viewed as another step in Seatrium's strategy to create a <strong>leaner, capital-efficient and sustainable operating model.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The company is expected to increase investment in <strong>renewable energy projects, LNG infrastructure and digital shipbuilding solutions</strong> going forward.</p>
<p><br></p>

  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The sale will be executed through <strong>the transfer of a 100% stake in its Brazil-based subsidiary Guanabara Navegação.</strong></p></li>
