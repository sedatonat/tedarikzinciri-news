<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Maritime and Port Authority of Singapore (MPA)</strong> and <strong>PSA Singapore (PSA)</strong> have launched a joint <strong>Expression of Interest (EOI)</strong> to evaluate autonomous container feeder vessel operations. The EOI will scan technologies and operational models for future autonomous feeder operations in the <strong>Singapore Strait</strong> and port waters.</p><p style="text-align: justify">As vessel traffic increases, the authorities aim to ensure these movements are carried out efficiently, safely and sustainably. The EOI forms part of wider efforts by MPA and PSA to <strong>assess the readiness of autonomous technologies</strong> in maritime operations. The initiative will evaluate technological maturity, operational feasibility and safety performance under various scenarios. According to the authorities, such systems could also support new maritime roles, including <strong>remote vessel operators</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">A key element of the proposal is the development of a <strong>remote operations centre</strong>. This facility would enable shore-based management, monitoring and intervention for autonomous feeder vessels. MPA noted that applicants are expected to address a range of operational and technical considerations, including safety, system integration, cybersecurity and regulatory compliance. The EOI aligns with Singapore's broader goals for digital and automated transformation in shipping.</p>
