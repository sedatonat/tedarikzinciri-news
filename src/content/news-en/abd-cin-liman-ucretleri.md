<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The United States and China</strong> have agreed to <strong>suspend reciprocal port fees for one year</strong> as part of efforts to ease trade tensions. The decision was announced during a meeting between <strong>US President Donald Trump</strong> and <strong>Chinese State President Xi Jinping</strong> on the sidelines of the <strong>Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC) Summit held in South Korea on October 30, 2025.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">This development is being regarded as a key component of the <strong>comprehensive trade agreement</strong> being negotiated between the two countries. The agreement's overall objective is to ease escalating trade tensions that have accumulated over the past two years, including those related to <strong>tariffs, export controls, maritime investigations, and port charges.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Scope of the Agreement</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">China's <strong>Ministry of Commerce</strong> announced in an official statement that <strong>port fees applied to US-made, US-controlled, or US-flagged vessels in Chinese ports will be suspended for one year.</strong> Similarly, <strong>the US will remove port charges imposed on Chinese-made or Chinese-operated vessels.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">This decision represents a freeze on <strong>retaliatory port fees and control measures</strong> that have been applied in the maritime sectors of both countries over the past year. However, the ministry's statement did not provide clear information on <strong>when the suspension will take effect</strong> or <strong>a refund process for previously paid fees.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Easing Trade War Tensions</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The suspension of port fees is part of a <strong>"mini ceasefire" package announced at the end of October in the US-China trade war.</strong> This package also includes measures such as <strong>tariff reductions, deferral of export controls on rare earth elements,</strong> and <strong>the US halting its Section 301 investigation into China-linked companies.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Trump stated via <strong>Truth Social</strong> that the agreement is <strong>"fair, reciprocal, and economically win-win."</strong> A spokesperson for China's Ministry of Commerce said that both sides are advancing toward <strong>"restoring trade balance and building mutual trust."</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sectoral Impact</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">This decision represents <strong>a significant cost reduction</strong> for <strong>US and China-based shipping companies</strong> operating in <strong>international maritime transportation.</strong> According to experts, the suspension of port fees could reduce costs for <strong>container shipping companies operating on the Asia-Pacific route</strong> by 5–10 percent in the short term.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">However, uncertainties remain. The <strong>effective start date</strong> and <strong>refund mechanism</strong> have not yet been clarified. Additionally, <strong>whether the one-year suspension period will be extended</strong> will be decided at <strong>annual review meetings</strong> to be held between the two countries.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Significance of APEC Discussions</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The meeting between Trump and Xi in South Korea represents <strong>the most comprehensive in-person meeting</strong> in trade negotiations throughout 2025. During this meeting, the parties also made progress on issues such as <strong>agricultural purchases, energy cooperation, rare earth materials, and export restrictions.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">During the meeting, <strong>Xi Jinping</strong> stated:</p>\n<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"As two major economies, it is natural that we have different views. However, these differences can be resolved through cooperation and dialogue. Keeping the trade ship on the right course is a shared responsibility of both our nations."</blockquote>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Experts characterize the suspension of port fees as <strong>"politically symbolic but economically relieving."</strong> This development is expected to <strong>increase stability in maritime transportation</strong> and <strong>reduce uncertainty in the global freight market.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The US and China have suspended reciprocal port fees for one year.</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The decision is part of the <strong>trade ceasefire package</strong> announced during the <strong>APEC Summit in South Korea.</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Fees applied to US-flagged vessels in Chinese ports</strong> and <strong>to Chinese-flagged vessels in US ports</strong> have been removed.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The effective start date and refund mechanism</strong> have not yet been announced.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The removal of port fees will provide <strong>short-term relief in maritime transportation costs.</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The measure is part of the <strong>broader US-China trade ceasefire</strong> announced at the end of October.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, this decision is an important signal toward
