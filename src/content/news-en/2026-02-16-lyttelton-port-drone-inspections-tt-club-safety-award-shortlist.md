<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Lyttelton Port Company (LPC)</strong> has been shortlisted for the <strong>TT Club Innovation in Safety Award</strong> with its high-resolution drones to eliminate manual work-at-height inspections across its inland ports and depots. Handling around <strong>500,000 TEU annually</strong> in Christchurch, New Zealand, LPC previously relied on Elevated Work Platforms (EWPs) to conduct container roof checks, post-wind stack inspections and structural roof assessments.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Each empty container entering the yard required survey staff to ascend and descend an EWP to inspect the roof condition. Following high wind events exceeding <strong>32 knots</strong>, personnel were also required to enter operational areas to assess stack integrity, creating additional safety concerns and operational delays. The new approach replaces these manual processes with <strong>drones equipped with advanced zoom cameras</strong>, enabling remote visual inspections while keeping personnel out of high-risk zones.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Drone-based inspection both shortens container turnaround time and provides marked benefit in preventing falls and stack collapse incidents. <strong>UAV data</strong> is captured digitally and integrated into operational decision-support systems. LPC's approach offers a scalable <strong>digital safety standard</strong> template for container terminals exposed to environmental events.</p>
