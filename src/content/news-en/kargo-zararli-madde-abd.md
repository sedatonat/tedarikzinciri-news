<p style="text-align: justify">U.S. <strong>breakbulk</strong> and <strong>project cargo</strong> importers face substantial financial pressure throughout 2025 due to tariffs, economic uncertainty, shipping costs, and delays. To this challenging environment, <strong>CBP's intense inspections and steep penalties for pest detection in wooden packaging materials</strong> have now been added.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">U.S. Customs and Border Protection (<strong>CBP</strong>) is specifically targeting <strong>wooden packaging materials (WPM)</strong> that do not comply with <strong>ISPM 15 standards</strong> or contain pest organisms. Wooden crates, dunnage, pallets, and support beams used in project cargo are inherently highly susceptible to <strong>insect, larval, and fungal contamination</strong> due to their natural composition.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Due to CBP's tightened enforcement, importers face:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Seven-figure penalties</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Risk of cargo re-export</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Delays lasting days or weeks</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Increased port <strong>storage and labor costs</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Exposure to <strong>contract breach risks</strong> triggered by disrupted delivery schedules<br></p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Inspections Accelerated in 2025 Fiscal Year</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Throughout the 2025 fiscal year, CBP inspections conducted by the U.S. federal government show <strong>significant increases</strong>, particularly in the project cargo segment. CBP notes that non-fragile, large-scale project shipments frequently utilize wooden packaging and are therefore placed in a <strong>high-risk category</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Items commonly used in project cargo shipments include:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">generator casings,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">turbine packaging boxes,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">heavy steel equipment crates,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">maritime and offshore equipment boxes</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Since these are typically constructed from thick, unprocessed, and heavy timber components, they create an environment conducive to pest nesting.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Why Are Penalties So High?</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The U.S. emphasizes that the entry of pest organisms could cause <strong>billions of dollars in damage</strong> to agriculture, forestry, and ecosystems. Consequently, CBP enforces a <strong>zero-tolerance approach</strong> toward ISPM 15 violations.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under enforcement actions:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Cargo may be opened and repackaged at the facility,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Wooden materials are destroyed,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The company is classified as a "problematic importer",</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Customs processing becomes subject to heightened scrutiny.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">In some cases, penalties have reportedly exceeded <strong>1 million dollars</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Broad Impact on Supply Chains</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">These enforcement actions create not only cost pressures but also significant fragility in <strong>supply chain reliability</strong>. Large-scale components imported for U.S. energy, infrastructure, heavy industry, and renewable energy projects:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">delay projects when not delivered on time,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">impose additional costs,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">expose EPC contractors to contract penalties.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Many importers are <strong>restructuring their packaging supply chains</strong> due to increased CBP inspections and demanding stricter ISPM 15 certification from suppliers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Risk Mitigation Approaches</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Industry experts recommend the following methods to project cargo importers:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Verify ISPM 15 heat treatment</strong> certificates with every shipment,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Replace packaging materials with <strong>engineering composites</strong> or steel where possible,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Obtain a <strong>pest inspection report</strong> before loading,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Commission <strong>third-party surveys</strong> in supplier countries,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Provide <strong>advance notice</strong> to CBP at the arrival port.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">While these steps are costly, they remain far less expensive than the financial losses resulting from re-export.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">CBP's wooden packaging inspections have <strong>noticeably tightened in 2025</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">ISPM 15 non-compliance results in <strong>penalties approaching seven figures</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Heavy timber packaging used in project cargo carries <strong>high pest risk</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The combination of penalties and re-export creates serious <strong>delays and cost increases</strong> in supply chains.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Pre-shipment inspections and alternative packaging methods are becoming increasingly critical for risk management.</p></li>
</ul>
