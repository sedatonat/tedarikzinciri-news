<p style="text-align: justify">The Houthis' announcement that they are suspending attacks on Israel and commercial vessels has the potential to create a <strong>game-changing impact</strong> on global container shipping. However, market intelligence platform <strong>Xeneta's Chief Analyst Peter Sand</strong> emphasized that "the situation remains extremely uncertain," noting that carriers and insurers cannot return to normal operations based solely on verbal assurance.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Risk tolerance varies from carrier to carrier</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Sand noted that some lines are <strong>gradually testing the Red Sea</strong>. It is known that <strong>CMA CGM Zheng He</strong> and <strong>CMA CGM Benjamin Franklin</strong> from the CMA CGM fleet transited through Suez in November. Nevertheless, <strong>the number of container ships passing through the Suez Canal declined significantly throughout 2025</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the analyst, if there is a partial improvement in security perception, transit numbers could increase; however, <strong>expecting a rapid return to 2023 levels would not be realistic</strong>.</p>
<h4><br></h4>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Impact of Cape of Good Hope routings: 2 million TEU withdrawn from the market</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Currently, the majority of lines continue with Cape of Good Hope routings. This extended route has led to <strong>approximately 2 million TEU equivalent capacity being absorbed</strong> from the global fleet utilization. This situation has:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">tightened global supply,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">supported freight rates due to capacity shortage,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">increased vessel utilization for carriers.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Sand stated that a rapid and mass return to Suez would:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>reduce pressure on transport demand</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">bring global supply back to normal levels,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">and thereby <strong>sharply drive down freight rates</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Freight rates already declining</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Xeneta data, since the beginning of 2025:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Far East to Northern Europe,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Far East to Mediterranean,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Far East to US East Coast<br>
spot freight rates have declined by <strong>more than 50%</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">If the Red Sea reopens at full capacity, this could intensify further. Sand states that "a sudden influx of capacity into the market could trigger a new global freight rate collapse," with rates potentially declining by an <strong>additional 25%</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Risk for carriers: Profitability at the edge</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Many container lines are operating <strong>at the threshold of profitability</strong> as of late 2025.<br>
If freight rates decline further, companies may be forced to:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">idle vessels,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">implement slow-steaming,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">announce more blank sailings<br></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">These measures aim to artificially tighten supply and stabilize prices. However, at the same time, <strong>schedule reliability could deteriorate</strong> and supply chains could become vulnerable to new types of disruptions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Warning for shippers: Return to Suez could trigger a new wave of volatility</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Sand cautioned that as carriers return to Suez, the supply chain could experience short-term <strong>chaos and disorder</strong>:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"If Suez operations resume, we will initially see substantial uncertainty. Shippers should be prepared for this scenario."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">During this transition period, short-term pressures could emerge on ETAs, schedule stability, and port queues.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>About Xeneta</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Xeneta is a Norway-based <strong>freight market intelligence platform</strong> that provides carriers, forwarders, and global shippers with <strong>real-time rate and capacity analytics</strong>. The company maintains one of the largest data repositories for spot and contract freight rates.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Houthi suspension of attacks could trigger a return to Suez but <strong>uncertainty remains high</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Cape of Good Hope routings are absorbing <strong>2 million TEU</strong> from global fleet capacity.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Mass return to Suez could trigger <strong>sharp freight rate declines</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Major route spot rates have already fallen <strong>more than 50%</strong> in 2025.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">There is potential for rates to decline an <strong>additional 25%</strong> in 2026.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Shippers should be prepared for potential volatility.</p></li>
</ul>
