<p><strong>Samsung Heavy Industries (SHI)</strong> has signed a new contract valued at <strong>341.1 billion won (approximately $237 million)</strong> with <strong>a Liberia-based shipowner for the construction of three oil tankers</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The agreement was disclosed to the public through <strong>a regulatory filing made on October 24, 2025 in South Korea</strong>.</p>
<p>The contract was <strong>signed on October 23, 2025</strong> and is scheduled to be <strong>completed by March 31, 2029</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
In the filing, the project was defined as <strong>"oil tankers (3 vessels)"</strong>, while the counterparty was identified as <strong>"a shipowner in the Liberian region"</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>Scope and Financial Impact of the Agreement</strong></h5>
<p>The 341.1 billion won contract value represents approximately <strong>3.4% of SHI's total revenue from the previous year</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
Payment will be executed <strong>in phases</strong>, with the total amount calculated at <strong>an exchange rate of 1 dollar = 1,430.1 won on the contract date</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>This contract is viewed as part of Samsung Heavy Industries' <strong>strategy to expand its stable order portfolio</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The company has been strengthening its global competitiveness in recent times, particularly in <strong>high-value-added shipbuilding projects focused on energy transportation</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>Samsung Heavy Industries: Among Global Shipbuilding Leaders</strong></h5>
<p><strong>Samsung Heavy Industries Co., Ltd.</strong> is a publicly listed South Korean shipbuilder operating within the <strong>Samsung Group</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The company specializes in <strong>commercial vessels, offshore platforms</strong> and <strong>vessels designed for energy transportation</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>SHI operates at <strong>the Geoje Shipyard, one of the world's largest shipyards</strong>, and holds a leading industry position in the production of <strong>LNG carriers, VLCCs (Very Large Crude Carriers)</strong> and <strong>FPSO units (Floating Production Storage and Offloading)</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>Strategic Significance: Ongoing Demand for Energy Vessels</strong></h5>
<p>Oil tankers remain <strong>one of the critical links in the global supply chain for energy commerce</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
As of 2025, increases in <strong>fleet renewal in energy transportation</strong> and <strong>decarbonization investments</strong> are providing <strong>a strong order flow to South Korean shipbuilders</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Experts note that with this contract, SHI will further strengthen its position in <strong>the production of high-capacity tankers equipped with environmentally friendly propulsion systems</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>Conclusion: SHI Reinforces Its Position in the Global Shipbuilding Market</strong></h5>
<p>This new contract with the Liberian shipowner supports <strong>Samsung Heavy Industries' stable order book and global competitiveness</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The agreement also demonstrates the company's continued <strong>strategic focus on the energy transportation segment</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>The three new oil tankers to be completed by 2029 will once again demonstrate SHI's <strong>technological capacity and delivery reliability</strong>.</p>
