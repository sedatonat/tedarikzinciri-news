<p style="text-align: justify">Global logistics player <strong>CEVA Logistics</strong> has signed an agreement to acquire 100 percent of Italy-based <strong>Fagioli Group</strong>, aimed at expanding its capabilities in project logistics and heavy hauling. The transaction is subject to necessary <strong>regulatory approvals</strong>, and financial details have not been disclosed. The move demonstrates CEVA's progression toward a stronger position in complex, engineered logistics projects.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Fagioli Group</strong> is recognized globally as a project logistics specialist in <strong>engineered transportation</strong>, <strong>heavy hauling</strong>, and <strong>lifting operations</strong>. The company manages some of the world's most complex project cargo within large-scale <strong>infrastructure</strong>, <strong>energy</strong>, and <strong>industrial projects</strong>. In 2024, Fagioli generated revenue of <strong>251 million USD</strong>, demonstrating its strong position in a niche but high-value-added market segment.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The acquisition brings approximately <strong>450 Fagioli employees</strong> into the CEVA organization, including <strong>more than 40 engineers</strong> in technical and managerial roles. Through this merger, CEVA gains the capacity to manage the full spectrum of project logistics activities—from <strong>early planning</strong>, <strong>design</strong>, <strong>transportation</strong>, <strong>delivery</strong>, to <strong>final installation</strong>—under one roof. This integrated approach mitigates the coordination and accountability fragmentation risks commonly encountered in project-based freight transportation.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">CEVA Logistics currently operates <strong>global project logistics</strong> with support from <strong>more than 1,000 specialists</strong>. However, Fagioli's <strong>engineering know-how</strong> and <strong>owned equipment fleet</strong> bring operational execution strength to this structure. Particularly in <strong>heavy and oversized cargo</strong> transportation, equipment ownership provides a critical competitive advantage in operational flexibility and schedule control.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Fagioli's equipment portfolio encompasses <strong>crawler cranes</strong>, <strong>gantry cranes</strong>, <strong>strand jacks</strong>, <strong>self-propelled modular transporters (SPMTs)</strong>, specialized <strong>trailers</strong>, <strong>barges</strong>, and other advanced vehicles used in large-scale project transport. These assets enable CEVA to develop not only planning and coordination capabilities but also direct field execution competency in project logistics. Additionally, Fagioli maintains long-standing relationships with <strong>engineering, procurement and construction (EPC)</strong> firms and industrial customers, deepening CEVA's market access in <strong>Europe</strong>, <strong>Asia Pacific</strong>, and <strong>North America</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">CEVA Logistics CEO <strong>Mathieu Friedberg</strong> emphasizes that Fagioli's industry reputation and corporate values played a decisive role in the acquisition decision. According to Friedberg, integrating Fagioli's technical expertise into CEVA—part of <strong>CMA CGM Group</strong>—significantly strengthens the company's capacity to deliver <strong>state-of-the-art</strong>, <strong>end-to-end solutions</strong> to customers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This move represents a continuation of CEVA's strategic initiatives since joining <strong>CMA CGM Group</strong> in 2019. During this period, the company acquired <strong>Bolloré Logistics</strong> and developed a more integrated structure with CMA CGM in <strong>freight management</strong>. The Fagioli acquisition stands out as a step that deepens vertical integration, particularly in project logistics.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Fagioli Group CEO Fernando Bertoni</strong> notes that CEVA's global network will provide Fagioli with stronger <strong>access-to-market</strong>, local expertise, and financial support. This structure accelerates Fagioli's long-term growth objectives and enables the company to access larger-scale projects on a global basis.</p>
