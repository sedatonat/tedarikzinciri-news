<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Russia</strong> resumed its <strong>first wheat shipment to Indonesia in October</strong> following a brief export suspension.</p>
<p><br>
Approximately <strong>52,000 tons of wheat</strong> were loaded from <strong>Novorossiysk Port in the Black Sea</strong> and sent to Indonesia.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This development became possible following <strong>Indonesia's Quarantine Agency, under the Ministry of Agriculture,</strong> decision to <strong>extend accreditation for five grain testing laboratories in Russia.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Russian Laboratories Regain Approval</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Indonesia re-recognized the laboratories in Russia as <strong>"competent and reliable control institutions."</strong></p>
<p><br>
These laboratories will be <strong>responsible for quality control of grain exported to Indonesia.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This accreditation extension guarantees <strong>the sustainability of Russian grain exports</strong> and <strong>secure market access to Indonesia.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Indonesia: One of Southeast Asia's Largest Grain Importers</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Indonesia imports annually on average:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>11 million tons of wheat,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>2.7 million tons of soybeans,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>1.2 million tons of corn.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">These figures position the country as <strong>Southeast Asia's largest agricultural product importer.</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">During 2022–2025, Russia exported <strong>over 2.6 million tons of grain</strong> to Indonesia.<br>
The new accreditation decision opens the way for <strong>this volume to increase further in the coming years.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic Importance: The Southeast Asian Market</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Indonesia represents <strong>a strategic market for Russia in agricultural exports.</strong><br>
Through this accreditation extension, Russian agricultural producers will be able to:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Directly meet quality assurance requirements,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Accelerate export approval processes,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Gain market access in new grain and feed product categories.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, this development is viewed as part of Russia's <strong>export strategy focused on Asia.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: A New Balance in Exports</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Russia's resumption of wheat exports to Indonesia is viewed as <strong>a signal of normalization in agricultural trade relations.</strong></p>
<p><br>
The accreditation decision also strengthens <strong>cooperation between the two countries in agriculture and food security.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This step will <strong>deepen Russia's presence in the Asian market,</strong> while providing Indonesia with <strong>stable grain supply.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Russia</strong> resumed wheat exports to <strong>Indonesia as of October 2025.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">First shipment: <strong>52,000 tons of wheat loaded from Novorossiysk Port.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Indonesia</strong> extended accreditation for <strong>5 grain testing laboratories in Russia.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Indonesia annually imports: <strong>11 million tons of wheat, 2.7 million tons of soybeans, 1.2 million tons of corn.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Between 2022–2025, Russia exported 2.6 million tons of grain</strong> to Indonesia.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The new accreditation provides Russian producers with <strong>broader export opportunities in the Asian market.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
