<p style="text-align: justify">According to the analytic center of <strong>Rusagrotrans</strong>, <strong>Sudan</strong> has become the fastest-growing importer of Russian wheat. During the first half of the 2025/2026 agricultural season (July 2025 through March 2026), it acquired <strong>almost 3 times more</strong> wheat than during the same period of the previous season and entered the <strong>Top 5 of major importers with 1.66 mn tons</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">Most key importers also increased their acquisition volumes. <strong>Export of Russian wheat to Türkiye was up 2.6 times to 5.8 mn tons</strong>, <strong>to Iran up 2.5-fold to 1.86 mn tons</strong>, <strong>to Israel up 1.4-fold to 1.67 mn tons</strong>. <strong>Egypt maintained the previous year's level with 7.6 mn tons</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">Russian wheat exports are expected to set a historic record during this agricultural year (July 2025 through June 2026). Russian farm products are increasingly replacing Western alternatives in Middle Eastern and African markets. In the sanctions era, positioning the Russian agricultural sector as <strong>an alternative foreign currency source to oil revenues</strong> has become one of the Kremlin's strategic priorities. Grain shipments from Black Sea ports (Novorossiysk, Taman, Rostov-on-Don) continue regularly despite wartime conditions.</p>

<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. Sudan has become the fastest-growing importer of Russian wheat — almost tripled.<br>
2. In H1 of the 2025/2026 season, Sudan entered the Top 5 importers with 1.66 mn tons.<br>
3. Exports to Türkiye rose 2.6 times to 5.8 mn tons.<br>
4. To Iran rose 2.5-fold to 1.86 mn tons, to Israel 1.4-fold to 1.67 mn tons.<br>
5. Egypt maintained last year's level with 7.6 mn tons.</p>
