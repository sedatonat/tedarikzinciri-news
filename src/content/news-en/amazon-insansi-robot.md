<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Amazon</strong> is preparing to test <strong>humanoid robots</strong> in last-mile delivery processes. This initiative aims to measure the <strong>feasibility of package delivery</strong> in <strong>human-centric environments</strong>—such as stairs, sidewalks, doorsteps, and other complex areas—thereby developing a third automation layer to complement the company's existing <strong>AMR (Autonomous Mobile Robot)</strong> and <strong>drone delivery network</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a technical standpoint, the focus lies on advancing <strong>bipedal locomotion</strong>, <strong>grasping/manipulation</strong>, and <strong>safe human–robot interaction</strong>. In the systems Amazon is testing, combinations of <strong>computer vision</strong> and <strong>reinforcement learning</strong> stand out. This enables robots to perceive environmental factors, maintain balance, and dynamically navigate around obstacles.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From an operational perspective, the success of these tests will depend not only on technological capacity but also on <strong>risk assessment</strong>, <strong>public safety</strong>, <strong>insurance coverage</strong>, and <strong>municipal permitting processes</strong>. Given that robots will interact directly with people in public spaces, establishing safety standards and legal frameworks is of critical importance.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">These tests are viewed as part of Amazon's vision to <strong>extend last-mile automation to the doorstep</strong>. Humanoid robots will assume a <strong>complementary role</strong> to drone and AMR systems by providing human-like agility and decision-making capabilities in the "door-to-door" phase, the most challenging step in delivery.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In summary, these tests represent <strong>a new phase in autonomous logistics</strong>. By expanding the boundaries of robotic innovation in both physical flexibility and social adaptation, Amazon is moving one step closer to <strong>a fully automated delivery ecosystem</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Points:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li><p>Humanoid robots are tested in human-centric environments.</p></li>
  <li><p>Bipedal locomotion and grasping challenges are critical.</p></li>
  <li><p>Safety and permitting processes are decisive factors.</p></li>
  <li><p>They are complementary to drone and AMR networks.</p></li>
  <li><p>Doorstep automation represents a new phase.</p></li>
</ul>
