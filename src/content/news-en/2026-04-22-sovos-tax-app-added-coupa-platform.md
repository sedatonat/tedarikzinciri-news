<p style="text-align: justify">Tax compliance software provider <strong>Sovos</strong> is adding its <strong>Tax Determination Connector</strong> to the <strong>Coupa App Marketplace</strong>. <strong>Coupa</strong> is an <strong>AI</strong>-based platform for total spend management. The <strong>Connector</strong> automates real-time <strong>U.S. Sales &amp; Use Tax</strong> calculations and validations within the <strong>Invoicing</strong> platform. By embedding <strong>Sovos</strong>'s tax engine directly into <strong>Coupa</strong> workflows, it eliminates manual tax entry and ensures every invoice is processed correctly across all jurisdictions. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Sovos</strong> is a Boston-based tax automation provider acquired by <strong>HG Capital</strong> in 2018. Key competitors in the <strong>tax compliance</strong> space include <strong>Avalara</strong>, <strong>Vertex</strong>, <strong>Thomson Reuters ONESOURCE</strong>, <strong>Wolters Kluwer CCH SureTax</strong>, <strong>TaxJar</strong> (<strong>Stripe</strong>), <strong>Anrok</strong>, and <strong>Stripe Tax</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Connector</strong> enables the <strong>Coupa</strong> platform to make real-time calls to <strong>Sovos</strong> for each invoice, calculating tax rates by both jurisdiction and product, and validating results at both header and line levels. This real-time integration allows users to ensure tax compliance without leaving <strong>Coupa</strong>, reducing compliance risk and audit exposure for organizations of all sizes. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Coupa</strong> was founded by <strong>Rob Bernshteyn</strong> in 2006 and was taken private by <strong>Thoma Bravo</strong> for $8 billion in 2022. It is a pioneer in the <strong>BSM</strong> (<strong>Business Spend Management</strong>) category. Direct competitors to <strong>Coupa</strong> include <strong>SAP Ariba</strong>, <strong>Oracle Procurement Cloud</strong>, <strong>Jaggaer</strong>, <strong>Ivalua</strong>, <strong>GEP</strong>, and <strong>Workday Strategic Sourcing</strong>. The <strong>App Marketplace</strong> follows an ecosystem strategy similar to the <strong>Salesforce AppExchange</strong>, <strong>SAP Store</strong>, <strong>Oracle Cloud Marketplace</strong>, and <strong>ServiceNow Store</strong> models.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sovos</strong> CEO <strong>Kevin Akeroyd</strong> stated in a comment: "By connecting <strong>Sovos</strong>'s <strong>Tax Determination Engine</strong> to the <strong>Coupa</strong> platform, we are delivering our customers a simplified tax compliance experience, reducing manual tax entry, ensuring tax accuracy on invoices, and helping them stay audit-ready." From a supply chain perspective, the <strong>U.S. Sales &amp; Use Tax</strong> system encompasses approximately 13,000 jurisdictions (states, counties, cities, and special districts) and is therefore highly complex. The <strong>Wayfair v. South Dakota</strong> 2018 <strong>SCOTUS</strong> ruling created the concept of <strong>economic nexus</strong> and expanded jurisdiction obligations for remote sellers. <strong>VAT</strong> (<strong>Value Added Tax</strong>), <strong>GST</strong> (<strong>Goods and Services Tax</strong>), <strong>JCT</strong> (<strong>Japan Consumption Tax</strong>), <strong>HST</strong> (<strong>Harmonized Sales Tax</strong>), and <strong>IVA</strong> (Latin America) are global indirect tax regimes. E-invoicing mandates include systems such as <strong>Italy SDI</strong>, <strong>Spain SII</strong>, <strong>France Chorus Pro</strong>, <strong>India IRP</strong>, <strong>Brazil NFe</strong>, and <strong>Mexico CFDI</strong>, all within <strong>Sovos</strong>'s scope.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, tax automation in <strong>P2P</strong> (<strong>Procure-to-Pay</strong>) and <strong>O2C</strong> (<strong>Order-to-Cash</strong>) processes is positioned at the intersection of finance, accounting, tax, and procurement teams. Tax engines are integrated with <strong>ERP</strong> systems (<strong>SAP S/4HANA</strong>, <strong>Oracle ERP Cloud</strong>, <strong>Microsoft Dynamics 365</strong>, <strong>NetSuite</strong>, <strong>Workday Financial Management</strong>, <strong>Sage Intacct</strong>) and <strong>e-procurement</strong> platforms. <strong>Tax content</strong>, <strong>rate maintenance</strong>, <strong>product taxability mapping</strong>, <strong>exemption certificate management</strong>, <strong>nexus monitoring</strong>, and <strong>filing automation</strong> are components of modern tax compliance platforms. <strong>SOX 404</strong>, <strong>ICFR</strong> (<strong>Internal Control over Financial Reporting</strong>), and <strong>PCAOB</strong> audit standards require that tax processes be controlled. In conclusion, the <strong>Sovos</strong>-<strong>Coupa</strong> integration is a tangible example of modern <strong>BSM</strong> strategies that transcend the finance-procurement boundary.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>\n1. <strong>Sovos</strong> is adding its <strong>Tax Determination Connector</strong> to the <strong>Coupa App Marketplace</strong>.<br>\n2.
