<p><strong>Ericsson</strong> has invested $50 million in its 300,000 square-meter facility in the United States over the past two years, aiming to reduce the impact of supply chain disruptions. These investments are viewed as a safeguard against various supply chain disruptions, including potential new tariffs under the <strong>President Donald Trump</strong> administration. ​</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>Ericsson</strong>'s facility in <strong>Lewisville, Texas</strong> opened in 2020 in preparation for the changing geopolitical landscape. The facility supports a transition from a cost-focused supply chain to a structure that prioritizes resilience. <strong>President and CEO Börje Ekholm</strong> stated, "You need to account for supply chain resilience, and that's why we built the U.S. factory," noting that the company has invested to increase capacity across <strong>North America</strong>. ​</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>The company operates primary manufacturing facilities in the <strong>United States</strong>, <strong>Brazil</strong>, <strong>Mexico</strong>, <strong>India</strong>, <strong>China</strong>, <strong>Poland</strong>, <strong>Malaysia</strong>, and <strong>Estonia</strong>. This diversified global manufacturing network enables the company to maintain flexibility in the face of potentially higher import tariffs. <strong>CFO Lars Sandström</strong> remarked, "We have quite a broad-based manufacturing capacity and we utilize it. We have the opportunity to shift production among different sites, with both internal and external manufacturing facilities," adding that they have the opportunity to work with their supply chain depending on changes in <strong>United States</strong> trade policy under the <strong>Trump</strong> administration. ​</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>Trump</strong> announced last week that he plans to implement the tariffs he promised during his election campaign starting February 1. This announcement came one day after a call by federal agencies for a review of <strong>United States</strong> trade policy. <strong>Sandström</strong> stated, "We're all waiting to see what happens there," noting that there may be product exemptions to new import tariffs, and added, "But we're constantly trying to balance and utilize the system we have." </p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li><p><strong>Ericsson</strong> invested $50 million in its facility in <strong>Texas</strong>.​<a href="https://www.supplychaindive.com/news/ericsson-tariff-trump-supply-chain-resilience/738405/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">supplychaindive.com</a></p></li>\n  <li><p>This investment aims to enhance supply chain resilience.​</p></li>\n  <li><p>The company's global manufacturing network provides flexibility against tariffs.​</p></li>\n  <li><p>The <strong>Trump</strong> administration plans to implement new tariffs.​</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Ericsson</strong> is reviewing its strategies to adapt to these changes.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>---</p>\n<p><strong>News Link: </strong><a href="https://www.supplychaindive.com/news/ericsson-tariff-trump-supply-chain-resilience/738405/"><u>https://www.supplychaindive.com/news/ericsson-tariff-trump-supply-chain-resilience/738405/</u></a></p>\n<p>-------------------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
