<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> reports that the <strong>U.K. Civil Aviation Authority</strong> (<strong>CAA</strong>) has authorized drone consulting firm <strong>Drone Major</strong> to conduct <strong>U.K.</strong>'s first beyond visual line of sight (<strong>BVLOS</strong>) drone operations over critical national infrastructure—initially across certain railway extensions. In a statement dated June 13, <strong>Drone Major</strong> notes this marks the first time long-distance approved <strong>BVLOS</strong> drones have received the green light to operate over critical infrastructure. Drones will initially patrol <strong>U.K.</strong> rail networks, with potential to monitor energy, utilities, defense, border management, and other sensitive national infrastructure areas. Trials have now commenced on the 7.5-mile railway stretch between <strong>Wolverhampton</strong> and <strong>Sandwell &amp; Dudley Station</strong> in partnership with <strong>Network Rail</strong>. The company states the trials could significantly accelerate trespasser verification and interception—thus reducing train delays in a critical, heavily congested area—potentially saving lives while substantially improving speed and efficiency across <strong>U.K.</strong> rail services, with potential for millions in annual savings.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the <strong>U.K. Civil Aviation Authority</strong> (<strong>CAA</strong>), based in <strong>London UK</strong>, with <strong>Sir Stephen Hillier</strong> as Chair and <strong>Rob Bishton</strong> as CEO, is the <strong>U.K.</strong>'s independent expert aviation regulator. <strong>Drone Major Group</strong>, based in <strong>London UK</strong> with <strong>Robert Garbett</strong> as Founder &amp; CEO, is among global leaders in unmanned systems (<strong>UxS</strong>) consulting and integration. <strong>Network Rail</strong>, based in <strong>London UK</strong> with <strong>Andrew Haines</strong> as CEO, is the <strong>U.K.</strong>'s railway infrastructure operator, managing 20,000+ miles of track and 2,500+ stations. Key regulatory frameworks include <strong>U.S. FAA Part 108</strong> (<strong>BVLOS rule</strong>, in development), <strong>EASA Specific Category</strong> and <strong>SORA</strong> (<strong>Specific Operations Risk Assessment</strong>), and <strong>UK CAP 722</strong>. <strong>Wolverhampton</strong> and <strong>Sandwell &amp; Dudley</strong> are located in the <strong>West Midlands</strong>, within the <strong>West Midlands Combined Authority</strong> (former Mayor <strong>Andy Street</strong>; current Mayor <strong>Richard Parker</strong>). Other leading <strong>BVLOS</strong> pioneer countries include the <strong>U.S.</strong> (<strong>FAA Part 137 Section 333 BVLOS waivers</strong>), <strong>France</strong>, <strong>Germany</strong>, <strong>Spain</strong>, <strong>Israel</strong>, <strong>Singapore</strong>, <strong>UAE</strong>, <strong>Japan</strong>, <strong>South Korea</strong>, and <strong>Australia</strong>, and <strong>New Zealand</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the global commercial drone market includes major providers such as <strong>DJI</strong> (<strong>Da-Jiang Innovations</strong>, <strong>Shenzhen China</strong>, Founder <strong>Frank Wang</strong>), <strong>Skydio</strong> (<strong>Adam Bry</strong> CEO, <strong>U.S.</strong> defense pivot), <strong>Parrot</strong> (<strong>Henri Seydoux</strong> CEO, <strong>Paris France</strong>), <strong>AeroVironment</strong>, <strong>Teal Drones</strong> (<strong>Red Cat</strong>), <strong>Autel Robotics</strong>, <strong>Yuneec</strong> (<strong>Tianjin China</strong>), <strong>Anduril Industries</strong> (Founder <strong>Palmer Luckey</strong>), <strong>Shield AI</strong>, <strong>Brinc</strong>, <strong>Wingcopter</strong>, <strong>Quantum Systems</strong> (<strong>Munich Germany</strong>), <strong>Volocopter</strong>, <strong>Joby Aviation</strong>, <strong>Archer Aviation</strong>, <strong>EHang</strong>, and <strong>Lilium</strong>. Drone-based last-mile delivery programs include <strong>Amazon Prime Air</strong> (<strong>Andy Jassy</strong> CEO), <strong>Wing</strong> (<strong>Alphabet</strong>), <strong>Zipline</strong> (<strong>Keller Rinaudo</strong> CEO, medical delivery leader), <strong>UPS Flight Forward</strong>, <strong>FedEx</strong> with <strong>Elroy Air</strong>, <strong>Walmart</strong> with <strong>DroneUp</strong>, <strong>Manna</strong> (<strong>Ireland</strong>), and <strong>Matternet</strong>. The <strong>U.S. National Defense Authorization Act</strong> (<strong>NDAA</strong>) imposes restrictions on Chinese suppliers <strong>DJI</strong> and <strong>Autel</strong>, with the <strong>Blue UAS</strong> program, approved by the <strong>U.S. Department of Defense</strong>, listing vetted alternative drone suppliers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, critical infrastructure monitoring encompasses key drone application areas: (1) railways, (2) electric transmission lines, (3) natural gas pipelines, (4) oil pipelines, (5) ports and terminals, (6) bridges and tunnels, (7) dams and water infrastructure, (8) telecommunications towers, (9) data centers, and (10) border surveillance. The <strong>NIS2 Directive</strong> (<strong>Network and Information Security Directive 2</strong>) serves as the critical infrastructure protection framework in the <strong>EU</strong>, while <strong>CISA</strong> (<strong>Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency</strong>) leads in the <strong>U.S.</strong>, and the <strong>National Cyber Security Centre</strong> (<strong>NCSC</strong>) in the <strong>U.K.</strong> <strong>Network Rail</strong>'s pilot with <strong>Drone Major</strong> involves key stakeholders including the <strong>U.K. Department for Transport</strong> (<strong>Heidi Alexander</strong> Secretary of State) and the <strong>Office of Rail and Road</strong> (<strong>ORR</strong>). Trespass remains a major challenge in railway safety, with over 8,000 incidents annually in the <strong>U.K.</strong>, leading to delays, criminal liability, and potentially fatal accidents. Consequently, the <strong>CAA</strong>'s <strong>BVLOS</strong> authorization to <strong>Drone Major</strong> represents a structural inflection point in global unmanned systems integration into critical infrastructure protection operations, with infrastructure resilience emerging as a core strategic priority for supply chain leaders.</p>
