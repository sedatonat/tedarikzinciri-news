<p>Amazon has unveiled its newest Fulfillment Center (FC) design in Shreveport, Louisiana, featuring 10 times more robotic technology than previous designs. According to information published on the company's blog, this next-generation FC design employs eight different types of robotic systems, establishing a new standard for efficiency and safety across the industry.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Amazon states that it has built and scaled the world's largest industrial robotics fleet, designed to ease employees' work and enhance operational safety. The company emphasizes that the new design has transformed how work is performed and redefined productivity standards across the sector.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>The Shreveport FC, with its five-story structure and area exceeding 3 million square meters, is equivalent in size to 55 football fields and ranks among Amazon's largest facilities. When the center reaches full capacity, approximately 2,500 human workers will collaborate seamlessly with robotic systems.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>In the center utilizing eight different robotic systems, technological solutions have been integrated across all primary production areas and processes for the first time. This enables employees to collaborate with robotic systems in a seamless manner previously impossible.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Leading the robotic systems is a containerized multi-tiered inventory system called "Sequoia." Amazon notes that this system enables employees to store and pick products more quickly and safely. Additionally, a robotic arm called "Sparrow" can process over 200 million different product shapes, sizes, and weights using advanced computer vision and artificial intelligence systems.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Amazon states that this innovative approach not only increases operational efficiency but also creates hundreds of thousands of new job opportunities.
