<p style="text-align: justify">The U.S. has crossed a critical threshold in its long-running political and economic dispute with Venezuela. According to <strong>Bloomberg</strong> sources, U.S. forces seized a <strong>very large crude carrier (VLCC)</strong> oil tanker sailing off <strong>Venezuela's coast</strong> that appeared on sanctions lists. This move marks a pivotal turning point, as Washington has shifted its pressure campaign from primarily financial and commercial sanctions to the level of <strong>physical interdiction</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to U.S. officials, the operation was conducted under <strong>judicial enforcement action</strong> targeting a vessel classified as a <strong>stateless vessel</strong>. President <strong>Donald Trump</strong> confirmed the seizure operation in a statement from the White House on December 10, using the following words:<br>
 <strong>"We've just seized a tanker on the coast of Venezuela — large tanker, very large, largest one ever seized, actually."</strong></p>
<p><br>
Trump also hinted that "other steps are being taken."</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Seized Vessel: Skipper (Formerly: Adisa)</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to officials and sources close to the Guyana government, the seized vessel was identified as <strong>Skipper</strong>. The tanker, approximately <strong>20 years old</strong>, was previously known as <strong>Adisa</strong> and was placed on the U.S. sanctions list in <strong>2022</strong> on the grounds that it <strong>facilitated Iranian oil exports</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Although the vessel appears to carry a <strong>Guyana flag</strong> on paper, the country's <strong>Maritime Administration</strong> formally stated it had no connection to the tanker. This situation points to <strong>flag of convenience and unclear ownership</strong> practices commonly seen in <strong>shadow fleet</strong> operations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">A <strong>VLCC</strong> is a massive platform capable of carrying approximately <strong>2 million barrels of crude oil</strong>. U.S. officials assessed that Skipper was heading <strong>toward Cuba</strong>, though this route is noted as commercially unusual. Nevertheless, Washington has long harbored suspicions that Venezuela is marketing sanctioned petroleum through <strong>hard-to-trace routes via Cuba</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Market Reaction: Oil Prices Rise</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">International oil markets responded swiftly to the news. <strong>Brent crude</strong> rose <strong>as much as 0.8 percent</strong> in London trading. Analysts emphasize that such military–legal interventions create a <strong>geopolitical floor</strong> for oil supply expectations, and even relatively small volumes can influence market perception.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Rystad Energy</strong> Geopolitical Analysis Director <strong>Jorge Leon</strong> summarized the situation in these words:<br>
 <strong>"The U.S. seizing a Venezuelan tanker is a clear escalation from financial sanctions to physical interdiction."</strong></p>
<p><br>
According to Leon, such moves have a deterrent effect not only on Venezuela but on all actors facilitating its exports.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Military Operation Images and Institutional Involvement</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Hours after the operation, U.S. Attorney General <strong>Pam Bondi</strong> shared images on the X platform of heavily armed personnel boarding the vessel via <strong>Black Hawk helicopter fast-roping</strong>. Bondi disclosed that the <strong>FBI</strong>, <strong>Homeland Security Investigations</strong>, and <strong>U.S. Coast Guard</strong> participated in the operation, which was carried out with <strong>Department of Defense</strong> support.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">While it remains unclear whether the personnel involved were coast guard or special forces, the operation clearly carried the nature of <strong>military discipline and use of force</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Stern Response from Venezuela</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Venezuelan government characterized the seizure operation as <strong>"a blatant theft"</strong> and <strong>"act of piracy."</strong> In an official statement, the country emphasized it would defend its sovereignty and natural resources with <strong>"absolute determination."</strong> The statement included the following words:<br>
 <strong>"It was always about our natural resources, our oil."</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The seizure news coincided symbolically with opposition leader <strong>María Corina Machado</strong> receiving the <strong>Nobel Peace Prize</strong> on the same day. President <strong>Nicolás Maduro</strong> has in recent months called on the population to join armed militias against U.S. threats and increased military deployment across the country.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Chevron and Sanctions Exceptions</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Venezuelan state oil company <strong>PDVSA</strong> continues to work with a limited number of international partners. Among them is <strong>Chevron</strong>. Under a special license granted by the U.S. Treasury Department, Chevron is able to take a portion of crude oil obtained from joint fields with PDVSA. <strong>Chevron CEO Mike Wirth</strong> disclosed on December 10 that they are holding discussions with the Trump administration on sanctions compliance.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The U.S. seized the sanctioned VLCC oil tanker <strong>Skipper</strong> off Venezuela's coast.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The operation represents a shift from financial sanctions to <strong>physical naval interdiction</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The vessel was previously associated with carrying <strong>Iranian oil</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Oil prices rose following the news (<strong>Brent +0.8%</strong>).</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Venezuela characterized the incident as "piracy" and delivered a stern warning in response.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The operation was conducted under coordination between the FBI, DHS, and U.S. Coast Guard.</p></li>
</ul>
