<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The agreement on pharmaceutical trade that the United States and the European Union reached last summer</strong> represents a significant turning point for the global pharmaceutical sector in terms of both pricing and production strategies. The agreement will <strong>push up drug costs in the short term</strong>, but carries <strong>deep-reaching effects that could fundamentally alter the geographic distribution and production structures of the sector over the long term.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>The agreement's key elements: 15% pharma tariff + Section 232 exemption</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The transatlantic agreement imposes a <strong>15% customs duty</strong> on many pharmaceutical products exported from the EU to the US.<br>
In return, EU pharmaceuticals are <strong>exempted from additional Section 232 tariffs</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">These two elements together create a critical balance:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The tariff represents <strong>approximately $19 billion in additional annual costs</strong> for EU producers.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">However, the Section 232 exemption prevents the imposition of <strong>additional taxes reaching as high as 100%</strong> that the Trump administration had threatened.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">While the US continues to threaten high tariffs specifically targeting <strong>brand-name and patented pharmaceuticals</strong>, it is sending manufacturers the message that <strong>"if you build a facility in the US, you can be exempt from additional tariffs."</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>The impact initially falls on insurance companies, not patients</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tony Gulotta</strong> from Ryan Tax says that due to the high margins in the pharmaceutical market, price increases will be delayed in reaching end users:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Because <strong>insurance companies</strong> purchase most of the drugs in the US, the initial cost burden will fall on them.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">However, insurance companies can quickly pass this cost to consumers by <strong>raising their premiums</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">It should be noted that <strong>60% of US pharmaceutical imports</strong> come from Europe—indicating that the price impact will be substantial.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Legal uncertainty: IEEPA lawsuit and Section 232 risk</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Trump administration bases pharmaceutical tariffs on two different sources of authority:</p>
<ol>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>IEEPA (International Emergency Economic Powers Act)</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Section 232 (national security grounds)</strong></p></li>
</ol>
<p style="text-align: justify">The fact that tariffs imposed under IEEPA are <strong>under review by the Supreme Court</strong> is increasing uncertainty among companies.<br>
The court is expected to issue a decision in the <strong>December 2025–January 2026 period</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">If IEEPA is struck down and Trump turns to Section 232, a significant portion of EU pharmaceutical products could face new risks.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">For this reason, European manufacturers are pursuing a <strong>wait-and-see strategy</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Will domestic production return? The answer: "It's not that simple."</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Trump administration's goal is clear: <strong>to bring pharmaceutical production back to the US.</strong><br>
However, according to industry experts, achieving this goal involves <strong>high complexity and costs</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Gulotta's assessment, the main obstacles to reshoring are:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>State-by-state varying tax regimes in the US</strong> (sales, property, and income taxes)</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The necessity of importing expensive specialized equipment nonetheless</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The resulting <strong>cascading tax burden</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Clean rooms, sterilization, and environmental standards</strong> required in pharmaceutical manufacturing</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The absence in the US of <strong>economies of scale</strong> that overseas production has built over many years</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Building a pharmaceutical factory is not "as simple a plan as shifting garment production from China to Indonesia." It is a far more capital-intensive, far more heavily regulated, and much longer-term undertaking.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Short-term company strategies</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Gulotta, European manufacturers have turned to the following steps during this period of uncertainty:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Building inventory in the US</strong> to reduce price impact and supply disruptions,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Optimizing their product portfolios,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Developing supply chain plans tailored to the US market.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>What will happen in the medium to long term?</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The expectation is that instead of rapid reshoring, the sector will enter a process of <strong>gradual geographic restructuring</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Likely outcomes:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The most critical pharmaceutical categories could shift to partial US production,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">European companies will revise their five-year investment plans <strong>based on US's final tariff policy</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The global pharmaceutical supply chain will <strong>become increasingly regionalized</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">New facility investments in the US will be limited but strategic.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<h5><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The US–EU agreement imposes a <strong>15% tariff</strong> on pharmaceuticals; the Section 232 exemption provides additional protection.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">In the short term, <strong>prices and insurance premiums</strong> will come under pressure.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">European pharmaceutical producers could face <strong>$19 billion in additional annual costs</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The IEEPA decision and Section 232 risk are leaving the sector's investment decisions uncertain.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Reshoring is possible but represents a <strong>highly costly, complex, and long-term undertaking</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The sector's future: <strong>regionalization and gradual restructuring</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
