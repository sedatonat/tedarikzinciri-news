<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Adidas</strong> is pioneering next-generation warehouse architecture by integrating <strong>automation, data integration, and sustainability</strong> into modern <strong>distribution center (DC)</strong> design. The company's facilities are engineered to both enhance operational efficiency and reduce carbon footprint.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">At the warehouse automation layer, <strong>AMR (Autonomous Mobile Robots)</strong> and <strong>ASRS (Automated Storage and Retrieval Systems)</strong> work seamlessly integrated with <strong>goods-to-person</strong> and <strong>put-to-light</strong> technologies. This combination delivers <strong>speed and accuracy in order picking processes</strong> while reducing physical strain on workers. The system enables flexible and error-free operations even at high order volumes.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">On the energy side, Adidas is <strong>reducing operational carbon emissions</strong> through <strong>PV rooftop</strong> solutions, <strong>LED/IoT-based automation</strong>, and <strong>heat recovery</strong> systems. Intelligent energy management systems monitor consumption in real time, preventing unnecessary energy use. This approach keeps the brand's progress fully aligned with its ESG objectives.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>At the data layer</strong>, integration of <strong>WES (Warehouse Execution System)</strong> and <strong>WMS (Warehouse Management System)</strong> solutions with <strong>control tower</strong> infrastructure delivers <strong>real-time responsiveness</strong> to demand fluctuations. The system automatically allocates resources during peak periods, maintaining efficiency and minimizing delays.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Adidas also prioritizes <strong>human-machine collaboration</strong>. <strong>Ergonomic workstations</strong> and comprehensive <strong>training programs</strong> throughout the warehouse enhance worker safety while supporting productivity. This approach ensures automation works harmoniously with the human factor.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, Adidas's modern DC architecture combines <strong>speed, accuracy, energy efficiency, and low-carbon</strong> objectives to establish a new <strong>operational standard</strong> for the industry. Through this approach, the company is redefining the benchmarks for sustainable and intelligent warehouse concepts.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li><p>AMR/ASRS delivers speed and accuracy.</p></li>
  <li><p>PV/LED provides energy efficiency.</p></li>
  <li><p>Control tower enables rapid response to demand volatility.</p></li>
  <li><p>Ergonomics improves safety and quality.</p></li>
  <li><p>Low-carbon DC design is becoming standardized.</p></li>
</ul>
