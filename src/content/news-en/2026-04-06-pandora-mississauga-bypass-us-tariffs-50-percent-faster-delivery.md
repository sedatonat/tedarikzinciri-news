<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Pandora</strong> said the new distribution center in <strong>Mississauga, Ontario</strong> will cut Canadian online order delivery times by <strong>up to 50%</strong> and simplify the returns process. Previously, Canadian orders had to pass through <strong>U.S. distribution centers and U.S. customs</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The facility can process up to <strong>12,500 online orders per day</strong>; it is operated by <strong>GXO Logistics</strong> and is the first site to adopt Pandora's new logistics setup. The system runs on <strong>pick-to-light</strong> technology.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The investment is viewed as a major <strong>tariff exposure mitigation</strong> step ahead of the <strong>10% global tariff</strong> imposed by the Trump administration in February and the <strong>USMCA</strong> trilateral review scheduled for July 2026. Canada currently accounts for 14.3% of U.S. trade.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. Delivery times shorten by up to 50%.<br>
2. Canadian orders no longer pass through U.S. customs.<br>
3. The facility has a daily capacity of 12,500 orders.<br>
4. GXO Logistics operates the site.<br>
5. The move balances tariff exposure ahead of the July USMCA review.</p>
