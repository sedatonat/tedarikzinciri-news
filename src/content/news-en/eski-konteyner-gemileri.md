<p style="text-align: justify">Container shipping has long accepted an assumption: "<strong>new ships are more environmentally friendly</strong>." However, VesselBot's <em>Decoding Maritime Emissions Q3 2025</em> report seriously challenges this understanding.</p>
<p><br>
Analysis of <strong>more than 73,000 container vessel transits</strong> worldwide reveals that <strong>the cleanest voyages are being conducted by vessels aged 11–15 years old</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This finding demonstrates that despite billions of dollars in fleet renewal investments, operational parameters—speed, loading rate, route selection—are more critical than ship age.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Cleanest voyages: Vessels aged 11–15 years</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the analysis, vessels in this age group, compared to newer ships:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">demonstrate superior <strong>fuel efficiency</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">maintain more balanced <strong>operational speeds</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">employ more optimized <strong>route and loading strategies</strong><br>
resulting in lower CO₂ emissions.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This indicates that focus must be placed not only on technology, but also on operational management decisions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key trends: Operations, not age, are the deciding factor</strong></h4>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>1. Transit volumes increased, total emissions remained unchanged</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">While annual transit volumes have risen, there has been no significant increase in total emissions.<br>
This demonstrates the effectiveness of fleet optimization and speed reduction practices.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>2. Larger vessels are more eco-friendly</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Large, fully-loaded vessels,</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">with lower carbon intensity per unit</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">traveling continuously over long distances<br>
are far more efficient than smaller, frequently stopping-and-starting vessels.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Small feeder vessels, meanwhile, generate greater emissions due to short, irregular routes and low loading rates.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>3. South Korean-built vessels stand out</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Vessels constructed in South Korea are delivering better results than Chinese-built ships due to their design and deployment strategies.<br>
The reasons behind this difference include:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">hull design,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">engine efficiency,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">deployment on more appropriate routes.</p></li>
</ul>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Industry implication: The "new = clean" era is ending</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The report emphasizes that fleet renewal investments alone do not reduce emissions.<br>
What actually makes the difference:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>slow steaming</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>cargo optimization</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>route management</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>speed planning</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>operational efficiency</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">These findings raise important questions for investors and carriers:</p>
<ol>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Is investing only in new vessels sufficient?</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Where can operations be optimized for better emission outcomes?</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Should deployment strategies for small vessels be redesigned?</p></li>
</ol>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the report, the cumulative impact of operational improvements on emissions reduction may exceed that of new ship technologies.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic takeaways:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Reducing fleet age alone does not guarantee environmental performance.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Operational optimization—particularly the combination of speed and loading—represents the largest improvement opportunity.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Large vessels deliver the most eco-friendly per-unit results on long routes.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Short-distance transits using small feeder vessels represent the weakest link in carbon intensity.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">South Korean shipyards' efficiency advantage in vessel design stands out.</p></li>
</ul>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key points:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The cleanest voyages are conducted by <strong>vessels aged 11–15 years old</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Transit volumes increased but total emissions remained flat.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Large, fully-loaded vessels are far more efficient than small feeder ships.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">South Korean-built vessels demonstrate more eco-friendly performance than Chinese-built vessels.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Operational optimization is as critical as technology for emissions reduction.</p></li>
</ul>
