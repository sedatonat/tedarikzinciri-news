<p style="text-align: justify">In early December 2025, a significant communication crisis grabbed attention in the global maritime sector. The <strong>Suez Canal Authority (SCA)</strong> announced that Maersk would resume transits through the Red Sea beginning in early December. However, just hours after this announcement, <strong>Maersk</strong> and <strong>Hapag-Lloyd</strong> issued a public correction on behalf of their joint venture <strong>Gemini Cooperation</strong>:<br>
 <strong>"No timeline has been announced for Red Sea return."</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This situation clearly exposes the gap between the pressure from political authorities for an "immediate return" and the maritime companies' operational security priorities.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Gemini Cooperation Context: Why Is This So Critical?</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Gemini Cooperation</strong>, which became operational in February 2025, represents the new global alliance between Maersk and Hapag-Lloyd. During its launch, the two companies announced they would implement <strong>Cape of Good Hope routing</strong> on operational security grounds. This decision was among the largest fleet movements to suspend transits through the <strong>Suez Network</strong> due to attacks and insurance risks in the Red Sea.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Gemini system is designed in a <strong>hub-and-spoke</strong> structure. This model requires intense transshipment through fewer major ports. Therefore, a return to Suez is not merely a route change; it represents:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">the replanning of routes for hundreds of vessels,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">the rescaling of hub ports such as <strong>Port Said</strong> and <strong>Jeddah</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">the adjustment of feeder connections and storage capacity<br>
which constitute a comprehensive operational transformation.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">For this reason, the return decision for Gemini must be <strong>"all-in or all-out."</strong> Piecemeal transit would compromise the network structure and directly harm the promised <strong>90% schedule reliability</strong> target.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Maersk vs. CMA CGM: Two Different Risk Appetites</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Two distinct approaches are clearly emerging in the sector:</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>• CMA CGM – Aggressive Strategy</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Operating a limited number of vessels through the Red Sea.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Using <strong>naval escort</strong> when necessary.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Advancing with a focus on quickly recovering market share.</p></li>
</ul>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>• Maersk &amp; Hapag-Lloyd – Conservative Strategy</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Priority on <strong>schedule reliability</strong> and asset security.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Attempting to prevent the operational chaos that would result from having to return to Cape after a single attack.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">For this reason, unwilling to create the perception that they are returning.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This difference stems from variations in the companies' corporate cultures and operational models. Because Gemini's network structure is consolidated, its risk tolerance is lower.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Late 2025 – Early 2026 Supply Chain Impacts</strong></h5>
<h6 style="text-align: justify"><strong>1. Transit Times Will Remain Extended</strong><br>
Maersk/Hapag-Lloyd customers will likely continue operating with <strong>Cape routing</strong> through the first quarter of 2026. This route is 10-14 days longer than Suez.</h6>
<p><br></p>
<h6 style="text-align: justify"><strong>2. Phantom Capacity Risk</strong><br>
If vessels return to Suez, sudden congestion could occur in Middle Eastern and Red Sea ports.<br>
Terminals that have operated at low volumes for two years could create <strong>bottlenecks</strong> during a return to full capacity.</h6>
<p><br></p>
<h6 style="text-align: justify"><strong>3. Spot Rate Volatility</strong><br>
The SCA's early announcement had created a brief surge in freight rate declines in the market.<br>
Following Maersk's correction message, with capacity still "outside the shorter route," prices are expected to move upward again.</h6>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>What Does "Steps Toward" Mean?</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The term <strong>"steps toward return"</strong> appearing in Maersk's recent announcements is being misunderstood by the public. This term refers to <strong>operational preparation</strong>, namely:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">hub port inspections,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">updating security protocols,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">reassessing insurance requirements,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">rotation optimization<br>
and such measures.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>This does not mean a sailing schedule.</strong><br>
Maersk has not yet made a decision to "start sailing."</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The SCA said "they're returning in December"; Gemini companies immediately denied this.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Maersk and Hapag-Lloyd have not announced a <strong>fixed date for Red Sea return</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Gemini Cooperation's structure makes the return decision far more complex.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">While CMA CGM takes aggressive risks, Maersk/Hapag-Lloyd are proceeding cautiously.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Shippers should plan for <strong>Cape routing</strong> through Q1 2026.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">There is a risk of <strong>instant congestion</strong> at Red Sea ports following a return.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Spot freight rates may resume an upward trend following the announcement.</p></li>
</ul>
