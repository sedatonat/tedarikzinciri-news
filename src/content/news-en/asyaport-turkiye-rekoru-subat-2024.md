<p><a href="https://www.asyaport.com/en-US"><u>Asyaport</u></a>, Turkey's largest transit container port, set a new national record in February 2024 by handling over 206,000 TEU in just 29 days. This achievement built on the experience gained last year when the port welcomed 1,033 container ships and handled 24 million tonnes of cargo. Benefiting positively from the situation in the Red Sea, Asyaport played a critical role in transshipping loads from Northern Europe to Gulf countries. With <a href="https://www.msc.com/"><u>MSC</u></a>'s choice of <a href="https://www.asyaport.com/en-US"><u>Asyaport</u></a>, the port's handling volume increased further.&nbsp;</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><a href="https://www.asyaport.com/en-US"><u>Asyaport</u></a> aims to reach 4 million TEU as its 2024 target and plans to expand its capacity from 2.5 million TEU to 4 million TEU through a port expansion project. With these investments, <a href="https://www.asyaport.com/en-US"><u>Asyaport</u></a> seeks to contribute to Turkey's economy by handling more transit containers and generating greater foreign exchange revenue.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5>Key Points</h5>
<ol>
  <li><p><a href="https://www.asyaport.com/en-US"><u>Asyaport</u></a> broke a new Turkey record in February 2024 by handling over 206,000 TEU.</p></li>
  <li><p>The Red Sea situation led to a 103 percent increase in Asyaport's transit cargo handling volume.</p></li>
  <li><p><a href="https://www.msc.com/"><u>MSC</u></a> selected <a href="https://www.asyaport.com/en-US"><u>Asyaport</u></a> as a transshipment port for Far East and Middle East cargo.</p></li>
  <li><p>The 2024 target is to increase handling capacity to 4 million TEU through the port expansion project.</p></li>
  <li><p>Asyaport aims to improve operational efficiency and customer satisfaction through continuous investments.</p></li>
</ol>
