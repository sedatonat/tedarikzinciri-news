<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Hasbro Inc.</strong>, one of the largest U.S. toymakers, said the <strong>cyberattack</strong> detected on March 28, 2026 could lead to <strong>weeks of delays</strong> in product shipping. The company disclosed the incident in an <strong>8-K filing with the SEC</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Hasbro, whose portfolio includes <strong>Play-Doh</strong> and <strong>Transformers</strong>, activated <strong>business continuity plans</strong> to maintain its ability to accept orders, ship products and run other operations. After the attack, select systems were taken offline proactively and an investigation was launched with <strong>third-party forensics experts</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">A Hasbro spokesperson said business operations remain open while work continues to identify any potentially affected files, and that the proper authorities will be notified.</p>

<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. The attack was detected on March 28, 2026.<br>
2. Hasbro warned of weeks of shipping delays.<br>
3. An 8-K filing was submitted to the SEC.<br>
4. Select systems were proactively taken offline.<br>
5. A third-party forensics investigation is underway.</p>
