<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>LogiPharma 2026</strong> stands as the leading global life sciences supply chain event, bringing together 2,500+ senior leaders from pharma, biotech and medtech—shaping intelligent, resilient and sustainable networks that power the future of global health. This year's theme, "<strong>Intelligent Futures. Resilient Networks. Global Health Impact.</strong>", reflects a bold transition from digital transformation to digital autonomy and from resilience to global responsibility. With 150+ expert speakers, the 2026 agenda explores how <strong>AI</strong>, data and leadership are redefining end-to-end value chains across science, manufacturing, logistics and patient care. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>LogiPharma</strong> is an annual event organized by <strong>WBR</strong> (<strong>Worldwide Business Research</strong>)—held across major locations in <strong>Europe</strong> (<strong>Lyon France</strong>, <strong>Montreux Switzerland</strong>), the <strong>U.S.</strong> (<strong>Boston</strong> and <strong>San Diego</strong>), and <strong>Asia</strong> (<strong>Singapore</strong>). <strong>Worldwide Business Research</strong>, headquartered in <strong>London U.K.</strong>, organizes sector-leading events globally including <strong>LogiCon</strong>, <strong>Future Fashion</strong>, <strong>RetailWeek</strong> and <strong>FoodLogistics</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the global pharmaceutical industry stands at 1.5+ trillion dollars annually. Major players include <strong>Pfizer</strong>, <strong>Johnson &amp; Johnson</strong>, <strong>Roche</strong>, <strong>Novartis</strong>, <strong>Merck &amp; Co.</strong> (<strong>MSD</strong>), <strong>AbbVie</strong>, <strong>AstraZeneca</strong>, <strong>GSK</strong>, <strong>Sanofi</strong>, <strong>Eli Lilly</strong>, <strong>Bristol Myers Squibb</strong>, <strong>Bayer</strong>, <strong>Boehringer Ingelheim</strong>, <strong>Takeda</strong>, <strong>Novo Nordisk</strong>, <strong>Gilead Sciences</strong>, <strong>Amgen</strong>, <strong>Biogen</strong>, <strong>Regeneron</strong>, <strong>Vertex</strong>, <strong>Moderna</strong> and <strong>BioNTech</strong> among <strong>Big Pharma</strong> and leading biotech players. <strong>Eli Lilly</strong>'s <strong>Mounjaro</strong>/<strong>Zepbound</strong> (<strong>tirzepatide</strong>) and <strong>Novo Nordisk</strong>'s <strong>Ozempic</strong>/<strong>Wegovy</strong> (<strong>semaglutide</strong>)—<strong>GLP-1</strong> agonist obesity drugs—rank among the world's largest blockbusters. Modern drug modalities include <strong>biologic</strong>, <strong>biosimilar</strong>, <strong>mAb</strong> (<strong>monoclonal antibody</strong>), <strong>ADC</strong> (<strong>Antibody-Drug Conjugate</strong>), <strong>CAR-T</strong> cell therapy, <strong>gene therapy</strong>, <strong>mRNA</strong> and <strong>siRNA</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, pharmaceutical supply chains face unique challenges including <strong>cold chain</strong> management (2-8°C, -20°C, -70°C ultra-low for <strong>mRNA</strong>), <strong>GxP</strong> compliance (<strong>GMP</strong>, <strong>GLP</strong>, <strong>GDP</strong>, <strong>GCP</strong>, <strong>GVP</strong>), and regulatory frameworks from <strong>FDA</strong> (<strong>U.S. Food and Drug Administration</strong>), <strong>EMA</strong> (<strong>European Medicines Agency</strong>), <strong>PMDA</strong> (<strong>Japan</strong>), <strong>NMPA</strong> (<strong>China</strong>), <strong>MHRA</strong> (<strong>UK</strong>), <strong>Health Canada</strong>, <strong>TGA</strong> (<strong>Australia</strong>), <strong>ANVISA</strong> (<strong>Brazil</strong>), <strong>WHO PQ</strong>, with filing types including <strong>CEP</strong>, <strong>DMF</strong>, <strong>ANDA</strong>, <strong>NDA</strong>, <strong>BLA</strong> and <strong>IND</strong>. Key regulations combating counterfeit drugs include the <strong>DSCSA</strong> (<strong>Drug Supply Chain Security Act</strong>; <strong>U.S.</strong>) and <strong>FMD</strong> (<strong>Falsified Medicines Directive</strong>; <strong>EU</strong>), with <strong>track-and-trace</strong>, <strong>serialization</strong> and <strong>aggregation</strong> as core requirements. Leading cold chain 3PLs include <strong>DHL Life Sciences</strong>, <strong>FedEx Custom Critical</strong>, <strong>UPS Healthcare</strong> (<strong>Marken</strong>, <strong>Polar Speed</strong>), <strong>Kuehne+Nagel KN PharmaChain</strong>, <strong>DSV PharmaChain</strong>, <strong>CEVA Logistics</strong> and <strong>Körber Healthcare</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, modern pharmaceutical supply chain trends include <strong>direct-to-patient</strong> (<strong>DTP</strong>) shipments, <strong>specialty pharma</strong>, <strong>orphan drug</strong>, <strong>personalized medicine</strong>, <strong>precision oncology</strong>, <strong>theranostics</strong>, <strong>radioligand therapy</strong>, <strong>cell &amp; gene therapy</strong> (<strong>CGT</strong>)—both <strong>autologous</strong> (patient-specific) and <strong>allogeneic</strong> (off-the-shelf)—and <strong>vein-to-vein</strong> supply chains for <strong>CAR-T</strong>. Manufacturing innovations span <strong>continuous manufacturing</strong> versus <strong>batch manufacturing</strong>, <strong>3D printing</strong> for personalized dosing, <strong>digital twins</strong> and <strong>AI-driven drug discovery</strong> (including <strong>AlphaFold</strong>, <strong>Insilico Medicine</strong>, <strong>Recursion</strong> and <strong>Exscientia</strong>), along with <strong>generative AI</strong>. The <strong>BIOSECURE Act</strong> regulates U.S. relationships with Chinese CDMOs, with <strong>WuXi AppTec</strong>, <strong>WuXi Biologics</strong>, <strong>BGI</strong>, <strong>MGI</strong> and <strong>Complete Genomics</strong> among key targets. <strong>Trump 2.0</strong> also threatens drug tariffs. Ultimately, <strong>LogiPharma 2026</strong> serves as the industry's convergence point for the <strong>AI</strong>-autonomous transformation trajectory of global life sciences supply chains.</p>
