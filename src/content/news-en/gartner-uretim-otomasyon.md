<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Gartner</strong>'s latest report reveals that the global manufacturing sector requires <strong>restructuring in advanced automation</strong> implementation. While most companies acknowledge automation's role in creating competitive advantage, the report shows they are <strong>avoiding the necessary transformation steps required for implementation.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The research is based on a survey of <strong>128 senior supply chain executives (Chief Supply Chain Officers – CSCO)</strong>. Nearly <strong>half of respondents (48%)</strong> agreed that their current manufacturing strategies <strong>will not achieve expected business targets over the next three years.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The Gartner report emphasizes that <strong>two-thirds of companies (66%)</strong> are not implementing the <strong>aggressive redesigns</strong> required in their manufacturing operations. This contradicts rising expectations for <strong>advanced automation and digital manufacturing.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Gartner Vice President and Chief Analyst <strong>Simon Jacobson</strong> stated in a declaration dated October 28:</p>\n<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Executives want to embrace new capabilities that will boost competitiveness, but 66% of respondents say <strong>supply chain and manufacturing integration is the biggest challenge.</strong> This troubling reality should push leaders to reinvent their operations."</blockquote>\n<blockquote><br></blockquote>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Robotic automation's share is growing rapidly</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">According to Gartner data, <strong>21% of manufacturing operations</strong> are currently performed by <strong>advanced automation systems.</strong> This share is expected to reach <strong>32%</strong> within three years. Conversely, the share of human labor in manufacturing is projected to decline <strong>from 52% to 40%.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">These figures show that <strong>robotic systems, sensor technologies, and data-driven production planning</strong> are increasingly becoming central to manufacturing. However, according to Gartner, this transformation must be managed <strong>not only through technology investment but also alongside organizational culture and process changes.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Manufacturing strategies need a "reset"</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The overall conclusion of the report is that <strong>the manufacturing industry must enter a reset process in advanced automation.</strong> Gartner recommends the following steps to companies:</p>\n<ol>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Integrate new technologies incrementally:</strong> Technologies such as artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning, and autonomous production systems <strong>should be incorporated into production lines at an early stage.</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Empower facility managers:</strong> Factory managers should be given <strong>authority for data-driven decision-making;</strong> the use of real-time production analytics should be encouraged.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Accelerate supply chain–manufacturing integration:</strong> Production planning, procurement, maintenance, and logistics processes <strong>should be integrated under a single digital ecosystem.</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Optimize human–machine collaboration:</strong> The goal of automation should not be to eliminate humans entirely but to <strong>create hybrid models that support decision-making processes.</strong></p></li>\n</ol>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Management awareness growing but action lacking</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Gartner notes that manufacturing leaders' technological awareness is higher than in the past, but most are adopting a <strong>"wait-and-see" approach</strong> in strategic decisions. In particular, <strong>mid-sized manufacturers</strong> are slowing the process due to investment costs, transformation risks, and workforce resistance.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The report emphasizes that <strong>advanced automation</strong> can improve not only manufacturing speed but also <strong>supply chain flexibility, quality, and sustainability.</strong> However, according to Gartner, <strong>"technology investment alone without an implementation strategy will not deliver productivity gains."</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: A sector at the threshold of industrial transformation</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">According to Gartner's analysis, the manufacturing sector is currently <strong>struggling to balance technology investment with organizational change.</strong> Companies must <strong>accelerate their transition to a flexible, data-driven, autonomous systems-enabled manufacturing ecosystem</strong> in the coming period.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Successfully completing this transformation will be <strong>one of the key factors determining not only efficiency but also competitive advantage and supply chain resilience.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>48% of 128 CSCOs</strong> believe manufacturing strategies will be insufficient over the next 3 years.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>66% of companies</strong> face challenges in manufacturing-supply chain integration.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Automation's share will rise from 21% to 32%,</strong> while human labor share falls from 52% to 40%.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Gartner</strong> called for a manufacturing <strong>"reset" (restructuring).</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Recommendations: <strong>Integrate new technologies, empower facility managers, accelerate integration, adopt hybrid models.</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Automation supports not only manufacturing speed but also <strong>flexibility, quality, and sustainability</strong> goals.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p>----------</p>\n\n<p>--------------------</p>\n<p><strong>Author: </strong><a href="http://sedatonat.com/"><u>SedatOnat.com</u></a></p>\n<p>--------------------</p>\n\n<p>We
