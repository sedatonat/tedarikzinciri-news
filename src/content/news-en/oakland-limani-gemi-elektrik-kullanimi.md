<p>At Oakland Port, 94% of vessels now plug into the electrical grid while berthed rather than running their diesel engines. This represents a significant increase from 85% in 2023, climbing to 94% in the first half of 2024. The shift enables cleaner air as vessels shut down their diesel motors while docked. Port management commends the efforts of ship owners in making their vessels shore power-compatible and terminal operators in coordinating the necessary connections for berthed vessels. Additionally, the port plans to install portable shore power outlets in the future, which will increase flexibility for vessel berthing operations.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5>Key Points:</h5>\n<ol>\n  <li><p>94% of vessels at Oakland Port are plugging into electrical shore power.</p></li>\n  <li><p>This development helps improve air quality.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The port plans to install portable shore power outlets.</p></li>\n</ol>
