<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> Podcast explores how the order-management lifecycle is reaching maturity for automation—particularly in the form of artificial intelligence. Order management sits at the heart of global supply chains, encompassing every process needed to deliver products to the end customer. For this reason, it involves multiple business partners and serves as a mechanism to efficiently move data, documents, and physical products among them. Until recently, the order-management lifecycle has been conducted largely through manual processes—and in <strong>B2B</strong> transactions, this remains largely the case. It is time to fix all of this. In this episode, we speak with <strong>Michael Rabinowitz</strong>, Chief Executive Officer (<strong>CEO</strong>) of <strong>CoEnterprise</strong>, about automation—focusing on the rising role of agentic <strong>AI</strong>. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>CoEnterprise</strong>, based in New York City, New York, was founded in 2010 as a <strong>B2B</strong> integration, <strong>EDI</strong> (<strong>Electronic Data Interchange</strong>), analytics, and data consulting firm.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Agentic <strong>AI</strong> promises to transform the order management world—resulting in timely and synchronized data exchange, a culture of trust among partners, and the ability to make faster, smarter decisions ahead of potential disruptions. The host, <strong>Bob Bowman</strong>, is Editor-in-Chief (<strong>EIC</strong>) of <strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong>. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>order management</strong> is the beginning of the <strong>OTC</strong> (<strong>Order-to-Cash</strong>) process flow—with key steps including <strong>quote</strong>, <strong>order entry</strong>, <strong>credit check</strong>, <strong>availability check</strong>, <strong>order confirmation</strong>, <strong>pick/pack/ship</strong>, <strong>invoice</strong>, and <strong>collection</strong>. The primary integrated system layers are <strong>OMS</strong> (<strong>Order Management System</strong>), <strong>WMS</strong> (<strong>Warehouse Management System</strong>), <strong>TMS</strong> (<strong>Transportation Management System</strong>), <strong>ERP</strong> (<strong>Enterprise Resource Planning</strong>), and <strong>CRM</strong> (<strong>Customer Relationship Management</strong>). Leading <strong>OMS</strong> providers include <strong>Manhattan Active Omni</strong>, <strong>IBM Sterling Order Management</strong>, <strong>Oracle Order Management</strong>, <strong>SAP S/4HANA Sales</strong>, <strong>Salesforce Order Management</strong>, <strong>Kibo Commerce</strong>, and <strong>Aptos</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, major <strong>EDI</strong> standards include <strong>X12</strong> (U.S.), <strong>EDIFACT</strong> (international), and <strong>VICS EDI</strong> (retail). Key business transaction messages include <strong>EDI 850</strong> (<strong>Purchase Order</strong>), <strong>EDI 855</strong> (<strong>PO Acknowledgement</strong>), <strong>EDI 856</strong> (<strong>ASN</strong>, <strong>Advance Ship Notice</strong>), <strong>EDI 810</strong> (<strong>Invoice</strong>), <strong>EDI 820</strong> (<strong>Payment</strong>), and <strong>EDI 940</strong> (<strong>Warehouse Shipping Order</strong>). <strong>API</strong>, <strong>REST</strong>, and <strong>GraphQL</strong> are emerging as modern alternatives to <strong>EDI</strong>—though <strong>EDI</strong> remains dominant, particularly in large retail-supplier relationships. Leading <strong>B2B</strong> integration platforms include <strong>SPS Commerce</strong>, <strong>OpenText</strong> (<strong>GXS</strong>, <strong>Liaison</strong>, <strong>Covisint</strong>), <strong>TrueCommerce</strong>, <strong>DiCentral</strong>, <strong>Cleo</strong>, <strong>Babelway</strong>, <strong>Mulesoft</strong> (<strong>Salesforce</strong>), <strong>Boomi</strong>, <strong>Workato</strong>, and <strong>Tradeshift</strong>. Major enterprise integration platforms include <strong>Sterling B2B Integrator</strong> (<strong>IBM</strong>), <strong>SEEBURGER</strong>, <strong>Axway</strong>, <strong>Software AG</strong>, and <strong>TIBCO</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, core elements of modern <strong>AI</strong> agent architectures include <strong>autonomous agent</strong>, <strong>multi-agent system</strong>, <strong>tool-use</strong>, <strong>chain-of-thought</strong>, <strong>memory</strong>, and <strong>planning</strong> in agentic <strong>AI</strong>. Leading frontier LLM providers include <strong>OpenAI</strong> (<strong>GPT-5</strong>), <strong>Anthropic</strong> (<strong>Claude Opus/Sonnet/Haiku 4.x</strong>), <strong>Google DeepMind</strong> (<strong>Gemini Ultra</strong>), <strong>Meta AI</strong> (<strong>Llama 4</strong>), <strong>xAI</strong> (<strong>Grok</strong>), <strong>Mistral AI</strong>, <strong>Cohere</strong>, and <strong>Inflection AI</strong>. Major agent frameworks include <strong>LangChain</strong>, <strong>LlamaIndex</strong>, <strong>CrewAI</strong>, <strong>AutoGen</strong> (<strong>Microsoft</strong>), and <strong>Semantic Kernel</strong>. Leading sector-specific <strong>AI</strong> solutions include <strong>supply chain AI</strong>, <strong>ChatGPT for SCM</strong>, <strong>SAP Joule</strong>, <strong>Microsoft Copilot for Supply Chain</strong>, <strong>o9 Solutions</strong>, <strong>Project44 Movement GPT</strong>, and <strong>Blue Yonder Cognitive Solutions</strong>. Modern <strong>AI</strong>-enabled order management promises include <strong>predictive ETA</strong>, <strong>demand sensing</strong>, <strong>autonomous procurement</strong>, and <strong>self-healing supply chain</strong>. Prerequisites for <strong>AI</strong> success include <strong>data quality</strong>, <strong>master data management</strong> (<strong>MDM</strong>), <strong>data governance</strong>, and <strong>data lineage</strong>. Ultimately, Rabinowitz's vision of agentic <strong>AI</strong>-based <strong>B2B</strong> order management clearly illustrates the evolving frontiers of supply chain automation.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. The order-management lifecycle in <strong>B2B</strong> is still characterized by manual processes.<br>
2. Michael Rabinowitz, as <strong>CoEnterprise</strong> CEO, discusses agentic <strong>AI</strong> transformation.<br>
3. Bob Bowman, as <strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> EIC, hosts the discussion.<br>
4. Promise: timely/synchronized data, partner trust, faster/smarter decisions.<br>
5. Order management constitutes the fundamental process of global supply chains.</p>
