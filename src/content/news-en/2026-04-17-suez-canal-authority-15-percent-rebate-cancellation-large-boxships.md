<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Suez Canal Authority (SCA)</strong> has cancelled the <strong>15 per cent transit fee rebate</strong> previously granted to large container ships. The decision takes effect on <strong>7 April 2026</strong>. The suspension applies to laden and <strong>ballast container ships</strong> with Suez Canal Net Tonnage (SCNT) of <strong>130,000 tonnes or above</strong>, transiting either northbound or southbound.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In its latest periodical, the SCA said it had suspended <strong>Circular No. 3/2025</strong>, issued on 13 May 2025, as well as <strong>Periodical No. 29/2025</strong>, issued on 17 November 2025. Both measures related to the <strong>15 per cent rebate</strong> for qualifying container vessels. The original rebate was introduced for container ships of 130,000 tonnes or more and applied to vessels transiting in both directions, set to run from <strong>15 May 2025</strong> for an initial 90-day period. The new circular does not provide further detail beyond confirming the suspension and its effective date.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The change comes at a time when hopes of a broader recovery in <strong>Suez traffic</strong> remain limited. Many major carriers continue to route vessels via the Cape of Good Hope due to ongoing <strong>Red Sea risks</strong>. The rebate cancellation signals SCA's intent to support canal revenues and may directly impact <strong>freight costs</strong> and routing decisions for large container ships.</p>

<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. The Suez Canal Authority cancelled the 15 per cent rebate for large container ships.<br>
2. The decision took effect on 7 April 2026.<br>
3. The rebate applied to laden and ballast vessels of 130,000 SCNT and above.<br>
4. Two periodicals dated 13 May 2025 and 17 November 2025 were suspended.<br>
5. The move comes amid persistent Red Sea risks and weak Suez traffic.</p>
