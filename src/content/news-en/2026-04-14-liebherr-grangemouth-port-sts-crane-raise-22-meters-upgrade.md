<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Port of Grangemouth</strong> has completed a Liebherr crane upgrade raising two <strong>ship-to-shore (STS) cranes</strong> by <strong>2.2 metres</strong> to handle higher stacks and larger straddle carriers. The upgrade aims to extend asset life and increase capacity. The port operates three Liebherr STS cranes, and the two older units have now undergone the crane raise programme to <strong>futureproof</strong> container handling operations.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Handling <strong>more than 250,000 TEUs</strong> annually, the port continues to adapt to rising container volumes and evolving equipment requirements. <strong>Higher container stacks</strong> and the introduction of <strong>larger next-generation straddle carriers</strong> required increased clearance beneath the STS cranes to maintain safe and efficient operations.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Liebherr Transform</strong> delivered a <strong>crane raise upgrade</strong> increasing the lift height of the two STS cranes by 2.2 metres. <strong>Both cranes remained on the rail</strong> during the works, minimising disruption to terminal operations. Each crane was raised within <strong>six weeks</strong>, highlighting Liebherr's ability to deliver custom engineering solutions under tight timelines.</p>
