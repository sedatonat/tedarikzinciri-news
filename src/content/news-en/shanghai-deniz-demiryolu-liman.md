<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Shanghai sea-rail intermodal network</strong> reached a new milestone in 2025. According to <strong>China News</strong>, following the departure of a train carrying solar panel modules and automotive components from Shanghai <strong>Luchaogang Container Center Station</strong> on the <strong>morning of December 5</strong>, the year's cumulative sea-rail volume <strong>formally surpassed the 1 million TEU threshold</strong>. This figure represents the highest level on record, with <strong>year-on-year growth of approximately 20 percent</strong> according to official data.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">At the heart of this growth lie intensive operational improvements executed by <strong>China Railway Shanghai Group Co., Ltd.</strong> throughout 2025. The company:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">optimized <strong>locomotive crew scheduling</strong> processes,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">reduced <strong>train idle time</strong> at stations,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">increased the frequency of <strong>fully loaded by axle</strong> services,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">achieved a daily capacity of <strong>20 train pairs</strong> on the Nanxiang–Luchaogang line,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">indicated that a potential of <strong>22 train pairs</strong> is feasible when service scheduling is further refined.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Beyond these optimizations, the company's hybrid <strong>"block trains + single wagons"</strong> model generates approximately <strong>90,000 TEU</strong> in additional handling capacity annually. This model strengthens terminal–hinterland connectivity by providing single-wagon flexibility without compromising the efficiency of block trains.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Luchaogang's Critical Role and Infrastructure Expansions</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Luchaogang Container Center Station</strong>, constructed as the primary railway hub for <strong>Yangshan Deep-water Port</strong>, commenced operations on <strong>December 4, 2005</strong>. In recent years, the station has undergone a multi-phase capacity expansion program to alleviate the "last-mile" bottleneck:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">loading and unloading lines were extended,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">new and modernized <strong>gantry crane</strong> models were commissioned,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">dedicated container yards were expanded,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">an <strong>integrated dispatch center</strong> was established to consolidate railway–port–cargo operations under one roof.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Today the station operates across a total of <strong>32 full-length tracks</strong> arranged as <strong>4 groups × 8 lines</strong>. The average handling time for a container at the station is <strong>approximately 3 minutes</strong>. With a design capacity of <strong>1.2 million TEU/year</strong>, the station is approaching its capacity limits given the current growth trajectory; however, operational improvements are making the system increasingly efficient.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Regional and National Connectivity</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Shanghai's sea-rail network today provides connectivity with <strong>more than 40 cities across 10 or more provinces and municipalities</strong>:</p>
<p><br>
Major industrial zones such as <strong>Shanghai, Jiangsu, Zhejiang, Anhui, Sichuan</strong> occupy central positions in this network. Authorities characterize this traffic flow as <strong>"stable,"</strong> indicating the strengthening of China's domestic logistics integration.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>2024–2025 Performance Comparison</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">In 2024, Shanghai's sea-rail volume exceeded <strong>900,000 TEU</strong> and demonstrated <strong>growth exceeding 30 percent</strong> on an annual basis. The surpassing of <strong>1 million TEU in 2025</strong> on this foundation confirms the sustainable growth of the intermodal model.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Chen Songdi</strong>, Director of Multimodal Transportation at China Railway Shanghai Group, notes that total inbound–outbound volume could reach <strong>1.09 million TEU</strong> by year-end 2025, representing approximately <strong>15 percent growth</strong> compared to 2024.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic Assessment</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">This performance reflects three key trends:</p>
<ol>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Intermodal capacity is enhancing China's port competitiveness.</strong><br>
Bottlenecks in hinterland access for mega-ports such as Yangshan are being alleviated.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Rail transport is reducing the carbon intensity of short-to-medium-distance road freight.</strong><br>
The sea-rail model is gaining priority in line with China's environmental targets.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Equipment and process modernization are reducing the risk of terminal congestion.</strong></p></li>
</ol>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, Shanghai's sea-rail intermodal network not only set a volume record in 2025; it has simultaneously reinforced China's integrated logistics infrastructure's global competitive strength.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">2025 sea-rail volume <strong>surpassed 1 million TEU threshold</strong> (20 percent growth).</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Luchaogang Station's annual capacity stands at <strong>1.2 million TEU</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The Nanxiang–Luchaogang line achieved daily capacity of <strong>20–22 train pairs</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Hybrid <strong>block train + single wagon</strong> model generates <strong>+90,000 TEU</strong> additional capacity annually.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The network provides connectivity with <strong>40+ cities</strong> across 10 or more regions.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">2025 total is expected to exceed <strong>1.09 million TEU</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
