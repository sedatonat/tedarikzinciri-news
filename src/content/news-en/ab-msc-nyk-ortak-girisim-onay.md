<p style="text-align: justify">The European Commission has formally approved the establishment of a new joint venture between Switzerland-based <strong>MSC Mediterranean Shipping Company (MSC Group)</strong> and Japan-based <strong>Nippon Yusen Kabushiki Kaisha (NYK Group)</strong>. The decision was made following an examination conducted under the <strong>EU Merger Regulation</strong> (Case <strong>M.12166</strong>, and the Commission determined that the venture poses no competition concerns within the <strong>European Economic Area (EEA)</strong>, approving it through a <strong>simplified merger procedure</strong> for expedited clearance.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The joint venture's operational focus will be <strong>air and ocean-based freight forwarding services across Africa</strong>. Given rapid growth in Africa's logistics market, increasing competition, and infrastructure investments, this partnership aims to strategically strengthen MSC and NYK's presence in the region.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Joint Venture Structure</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The parties to the project are:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>MSC's side</strong> is represented by <strong>AGL Kenya Limited</strong>, the group's freight forwarding and logistics subsidiary in Kenya.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>NYK's side</strong> is represented by <strong>Yusen Logistics Global Management Co., Ltd.</strong>, the company's global forwarding and contract logistics division.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Both parties will hold <strong>joint control</strong> in the newly established company. The joint venture will be incorporated <strong>in Kenya</strong>, though its official name and brand positioning remain under discussion.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The legal structure of the partnership will be established through <strong>share purchase</strong>; meaning the parties will hold equal or shared control rights in the new company. This model is a preferred entry structure for global operators pursuing long-term expansion objectives in the African market.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Africa Market Attractiveness</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Over the past five years in Africa:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">rapid urbanization,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">port infrastructure investments,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">increased regional trade agreements (AfCFTA),</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">rising e-commerce penetration</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">have created high growth potential in freight forwarding and contract logistics segments. However, in many regions of the continent, logistics networks remain fragmented, reliable service providers are limited, and multimodal infrastructure is still developing. The MSC–NYK joint venture aims to fill this gap.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic Strengths of MSC and NYK</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>MSC Group</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">One of the world's largest container shipping operators.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Offers end-to-end services including <strong>port handling</strong>, <strong>logistics</strong>, and <strong>cruise services</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Operates a strong network of lines across Africa for years; particularly maintains robust presence in West and East African ports.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>NYK Group</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">An established Japanese company with 650+ offices across <strong>46 countries</strong> globally providing integrated logistics services.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Specialized in areas such as <strong>ocean &amp; air freight forwarding</strong>, <strong>warehousing</strong>, <strong>supply chain management</strong>, and <strong>temperature-controlled transport</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Although lacking substantial direct operations in Africa, the company's <strong>international logistics expertise</strong> will bring significant technical capabilities to the new venture.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Competition Impact and EU Assessment</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the Commission's analysis, while the joint venture combines the joint strength of two major global logistics players in the African market, it poses no competition risk within the EEA. This is because:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">numerous global and regional players operate in the Africa-bound forwarding market,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">the joint venture has limited impact on logistics activities within the EEA,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">MSC and NYK's core activities within the EU are concentrated in different segments.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Therefore, the transaction received outright approval rather than <strong>conditional approval</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic Conclusion</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new joint venture aims to combine MSC's strong African network with NYK's global logistics expertise to deliver <strong>comprehensive freight forwarding solutions</strong> to the continent. The Kenya-based structure will position East Africa as the entry point, with operations expected to expand to other African sub-regions over time.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The EU Commission approved the MSC–NYK joint venture through <strong>simplified merger procedure</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">JV focus: <strong>air &amp; ocean freight forwarding services in Africa</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Structure: AGL Kenya Limited + Yusen Logistics with <strong>joint control</strong> incorporated in Kenya.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">MSC: strong shipping network in Africa; NYK: extensive logistics expertise.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The Commission identified no competition risk for the EU.</p></li>
</ul>
