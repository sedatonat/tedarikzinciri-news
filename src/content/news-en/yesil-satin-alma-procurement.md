<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The 2025 U.S.–Canada automotive trade dispute</strong> is being assessed as one of the most serious supply chain disruptions in recent years.</p>
<p><br>
 <strong>As of April 2025</strong>, <strong>25% customs tariffs</strong> came into effect, particularly impacting <strong>small-scale Canadian automotive parts manufacturers (SSAP)</strong> severely.</p>
<p><br>
However, research conducted by <strong>Corrine Chen</strong> reveals that these manufacturers have proven far more resilient against tariffs than expected, thanks to their <strong>green procurement</strong> practices.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Green procurement stands out as a structure that strengthens not only <strong>environmental sustainability</strong> but also <strong>organizational agility, risk management, and strategic resilience</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
By integrating environmental criteria into procurement decisions, Canadian suppliers succeeded in both <strong>reducing their carbon footprint</strong> and <strong>gaining flexibility against cost pressures from tariffs</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Green Procurement: A Practice That Builds Resilience</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Green procurement</strong> refers to the consideration of environmental criteria such as <strong>energy use, emissions, material sourcing, and waste management</strong> in procurement processes.</p>
<p><br>
Canadian automotive suppliers implemented these practices on the basis of <strong>Kotter's Eight-Step Change Model (1996)</strong>, building organizational cultures that fostered both sustainability and resilience.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This study is based on field research conducted among <strong>small-scale manufacturers (SSAP) that make up 70% of Canada's automotive parts sector</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The findings show that <strong>firms adopting environmental criteria were able to adapt much more quickly to tariffs, supply disruptions, and cost increases</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>1. Sustained Urgency: Continuous Motivation for Change</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Traditional models view "urgency" as a driving force at the beginning of a change process.<br>
However, according to Chen's findings, <strong>sustained urgency</strong> transforms into a competitive advantage in prolonged uncertainty environments.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">As one supply chain leader stated:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"With 2035 emission targets approaching, we have no choice but to move fast."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Leaders positioned <strong>compliance</strong> and <strong>competitive advantage</strong> together, creating a dual-pressure model.<br>
This enabled companies to move beyond short-term crisis perception and adapt to <strong>a trade environment requiring continuous transformation</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Implementation Recommendation:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Make environmental targets visible by linking them to <strong>performance dashboards</strong> and <strong>customer expectations</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Use compliance timelines and cost projections as <strong>continuous reminders</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<h5><br></h5>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>2. Evolving Coalitions: Building Flexible Decision Networks</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Effective coalitions</strong> should consist of <strong>dynamic and cross-disciplinary networks</strong> rather than fixed committees.</p>
<p><br>
Canadian suppliers first established <strong>cross-functional teams</strong>, then integrated <strong>legal, logistics, and compliance consultants</strong> into this structure.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This approach provided a critical advantage during periods when tariffs suddenly changed supply chains.<br>
By rapidly incorporating diverse expertise into decision processes, firms enhanced their <strong>capacity to respond quickly</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Implementation Recommendation:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Design governance structures as <strong>adaptive networks</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Include external stakeholders (customs, legal, finance experts) in the process when necessary.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Rotate participants periodically to incorporate <strong>diverse perspectives</strong> into the system.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>3. Transparent Communication: Visualization and Feedback Loops</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Canadian manufacturers</strong> effectively leveraged <strong>transparent communication</strong> strategies in their green procurement processes.</p>
<p><br>
Leaders shared progress with both internal stakeholders and suppliers through <strong>data visualization dashboards</strong>, <strong>newsletters</strong>, and <strong>short video messages</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">A participant noted:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Internal dashboards allowed everyone to see their contribution. When tariff changes occurred, we could make decisions quickly."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">This method increased employee engagement and enabled rapid communication of <strong>cost impacts and alternative sourcing scenarios</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Implementation Recommendation:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Create <strong>digital communication dashboards</strong> for real-time data sharing.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Communicate technical information not only among specialists but across the entire organization in <strong>simple business-focused language</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Strengthen feedback loops to rapidly transmit <strong>field observations</strong> to senior management.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>4. Structural Empowerment: Integrating Authority with Process</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The research revealed that individual empowerment alone is insufficient; it must be <strong>integrated with procedures and templates</strong>.<br>
Firms restructured processes by embedding decision matrices and sustainability criteria into <strong>procurement templates</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">One manager described this method as follows:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"We divided tasks into phases. There was empowerment, but each step was clearly defined. This ensured coordination."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Implementation Recommendation:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Use <strong>decision matrices</strong> to include environmental risks in supplier evaluations.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Enable departments to act quickly yet cohesively through <strong>structured empowerment</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Visibly reward units that achieve green targets.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Resilience Dividend</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Green procurement practices not only delivered environmental benefits but also generated <strong>a return on investment in organizational resilience</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
According to the research, these four capabilities developed for environmental goals—<strong>sustained urgency, evolving coalitions, transparent communication, and structural empowerment</strong>—enabled firms to become <strong>more agile and resilient</strong> under tariffs, cost pressures, and regulatory changes.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Lessons for the North American Automotive Sector</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>U.S. automotive manufacturers</strong> can learn from Canadian suppliers to view <strong>sustainability as a competitive strength</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Incorporating <strong>green procurement</strong> practices into supplier evaluation criteria provides not only environmental compliance but also <strong>operational resilience against trade fluctuations</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Kotter's change model</strong> should be used as <strong>a flexible guide rather than a linear one</strong>; urgency and communication must be maintained continuously throughout the process.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: Sustainability = Resilience</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The main message of the research is clear:<br>
 <strong>Sustainability and operational excellence are no longer separate concepts.</strong><br>
Green procurement is not just an environmentally friendly approach; it is also <strong>a strategic tool that gives organizations resilience against tariffs, supply chain disruptions, and regulatory changes</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The experience of Canadian suppliers provides <strong>a roadmap for U.S. automotive manufacturers</strong>:<br>
Start with green procurement, develop agility, and transform these capabilities into organizational resilience capacity.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Canadian small-scale automotive parts manufacturers (SSAP)</strong> became <strong>more resilient to 25% U.S. tariffs</strong> thanks to green procurement practices.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">In the research, <strong>Kotter's change model</strong> was reinterpreted through four strategic capacities:<br>
<strong>Sustained urgency</strong>,<br>
<strong>Evolving coalitions</strong>,<br>
<strong>Transparent communication</strong>,<br>
<strong>Structural empowerment.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">These practices strengthened <strong>trade stability alongside environmental objectives</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>U.S. manufacturers</strong> can increase tariff resilience and competitiveness by collaborating with suppliers on sustainability-focused initiatives.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Bottom line: <strong>Green procurement is no longer just an environmental strategy—it is a commercial survival tool.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
