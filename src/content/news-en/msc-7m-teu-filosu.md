<p style="text-align: justify">The world's leading container shipping operator <strong>Mediterranean Shipping Co. (MSC)</strong> has reached a new industry milestone with its fleet <strong>formally surpassing the 7 million TEU threshold</strong>. According to the latest data from Alphaliner, MSC's total capacity now stands at <strong>7,002,757 TEU</strong>. This figure represents the outcome of an aggressive expansion strategy that MSC has pursued over the past decade through both organic growth and secondhand vessel investments.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">MSC's current fleet size outpaces all of the major shipping alliances competing against it. For instance, the <strong>Gemini Cooperation</strong> initiative formed by Maersk and Hapag-Lloyd has a combined capacity that lags behind MSC's existing fleet. Similarly, the combined fleet capacity of Asian carriers Yang Ming, HMM, and ONE, which make up the <strong>Premier Alliance</strong>, falls short of MSC's position.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This competitive lead has widened in particular due to MSC's <strong>purchase of nearly 400 secondhand vessels in the 2020s</strong>, coupled with the creation of a massive new order book. The company's order book still exceeds <strong>2 million TEU</strong>, meaning MSC is positioned to further widen the gap with its competitors in the coming years.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The final step that pushed MSC across the 7 million TEU threshold was the addition of the <strong>Newnew Star 2, a 3,534 TEU capacity vessel built in 2007</strong>, acquired from Chinese operator Hainan Yangpu Newnew Shipping. Alphaliner reports that the vessel will be deployed under the MSC Rabat IV designation.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">MSC's rapid growth has positioned <strong>Switzerland among the world's top 10 ship-owning nations</strong>. Despite being landlocked, Switzerland has climbed to <strong>9th place</strong> in VesselsValue's global ship ownership rankings thanks to MSC's container and cruise vessel fleets.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sources of Growth: Newbuild and Secondhand Strategy</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Between 2015 and 2025, MSC <strong>tripled its capacity</strong>. The key pillars of this growth can be summarized as follows:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Massive newbuild programs</strong> (numerous 24,000+ TEU megamax-class vessels)</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Aggressive purchases in the secondhand market</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Operational flexibility and expansion of global port presence</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Vertical integration strategy</strong> (terminal investments, logistics companies, intermodal operations)</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This rapid expansion enabled MSC to overtake Maersk in early 2022 and reclaim <strong>container shipping leadership after 25 years</strong>. At the current juncture, no other carrier appears positioned to surpass MSC.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sector Impact</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">MSC's massive fleet scale:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">is reshaping capacity competition,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">is influencing power dynamics in freight markets,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">is changing vessel deployment strategies on global routes,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">is forcing other carriers to reconsider their alliance arrangements.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">MSC's ability to operate outside alliances is made possible by the flexibility its scale provides—positioning the company in a unique position within the industry.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">MSC's fleet of <strong>7,002,757 TEU</strong> represents an industry first.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Its order book stands <strong>above 2 million TEU</strong>, meaning growth will continue.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">MSC's fleet is larger than the combined capacity of Gemini Cooperation and Premier Alliance.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The company acquired approximately <strong>400 secondhand vessels</strong> in the 2020s.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Switzerland ranks <strong>9th in global ship ownership rankings</strong> thanks to MSC.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">MSC's secondhand acquisitions and newbuild programs are reshaping power dynamics across the sector.</p></li>
</ul>
