<p style="text-align: justify">In a previous report, we noted that <strong>Hapag-Lloyd</strong>'s Management Board was in advanced negotiations for the potential acquisition; we can now confirm that Hapag-Lloyd has signed an agreement to acquire 100 per cent of <strong>ZIM Integrated Shipping Services Ltd.</strong> for $35 per share in cash. The total transaction value exceeds <strong>$4 billion</strong>. The acquisition is expected to strengthen Hapag-Lloyd's position as the fifth-largest container shipping company globally, with a fleet of over 400 vessels, standing capacity of more than 3 million TEU, and an annual transport volume exceeding 18 million TEU.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">As part of the transaction, Israel's leading private equity firm <strong>FIMI Opportunity Funds</strong> will assume ownership of a carved-out container liner business. The new company will operate 16 modern vessels, take responsibility for ZIM's <strong>Golden Share</strong>, retain the ZIM brand and maintain strategic connections with Israel. The structure is designed to address regulatory sensitivities around preserving Israel-flagged maritime shipping capacity.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The deal gives Gemini Cooperation member Hapag-Lloyd deep expansion across Mediterranean, Latin American and transpacific trades. Closing is subject to <strong>regulatory approvals</strong> and shareholder votes, pointing to completion in the second half of 2026. Industry analysts expect the merger to accelerate <strong>global container shipping consolidation</strong> and reshape alliance structures.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. Hapag-Lloyd has signed an agreement to acquire 100% of ZIM at $35 per share in cash, exceeding $4 billion in total value.<br>
2. The combined company will become the world's fifth-largest container carrier with 400+ vessels and 3M TEU capacity.<br>
3. FIMI Opportunity Funds will take over a carved-out 16-vessel container liner business retaining the ZIM brand and Golden Share.<br>
4. The structure addresses regulatory concerns about preserving Israel-flagged maritime shipping capacity.<br>
5. Closing is subject to regulatory approvals and shareholder votes, expected in the second half of 2026.</p>
