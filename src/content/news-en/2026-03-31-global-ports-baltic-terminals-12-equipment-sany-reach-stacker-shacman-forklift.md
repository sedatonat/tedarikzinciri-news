<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Global Ports</strong> has acquired <strong>12 units of port handling equipment</strong> for its Baltic terminals. The holding said in its statement that the investment aims to improve operational efficiency across its terminal network and contribute to sustainability targets.</p><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Petrolesport</strong> received a new <strong>superheavy 45-ton capacity reach stacker</strong> made by Sany and equipped with outriggers. The outriggers allow it to stack containers over a railway track. <strong>First Container Terminal</strong> received 5 <strong>Shacman port tractors</strong>, and <strong>Ust-Luga Container Terminal</strong> got 4 such units. These tractors can move trailers up to 90 tons heavy.</p><p style="text-align: justify">For <strong>Moby Dik in Kronshtadt</strong>, two <strong>electric-driven 3.5-ton capacity forklift trucks</strong> were acquired. These forklifts can operate for 8 hours without recharging and take 90 minutes to fully recharge. Electric equipment investment is part of the carbon footprint reduction strategy at Russia's Baltic ports. Global Ports pursues a strong localisation and cost-optimisation trend tilted toward Chinese equipment producers. The holding, part of Delo Group, is a leader in Russia's container handling market.</p>
