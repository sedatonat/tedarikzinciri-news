<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> reports; <strong>Jeff Clark</strong> (<strong>Executive Vice President</strong>; <strong>ODW Logistics</strong>); in an interview with <strong>Russell W. Goodman</strong> (<strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong>); analyst insight; the right framework and training are critical to reducing costs in labor management and freight consolidation operations. Customers are always looking for ways to lower costs — says <strong>Clark</strong>. Today, many hope that artificial intelligence can help them do this. For example, in the warehouse, artificial intelligence helps operators see where order picking can be done in a more timely and cost-efficient manner. "This is done in an encouraging way," says <strong>Clark</strong>. "It doesn't mean demeaning the pickers in any way. If you do this, you'll be more effective, he says." Inefficiency contributes to a number of situations — he says. For example, how much time is wasted at the start of a shift, during breaks, or at the restroom? <strong>Clark</strong> says that reasonable standards can determine that employees are not in a productive environment. Identifying this is not about putting people "in a bad light," but about increasing productivity. "You can manage this. You can make a decision."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>ODW Logistics</strong>, based in <strong>Columbus, Ohio, U.S.</strong>, with CEO <strong>John Anderson</strong>, founded in 1971, provides globally third-party logistics (<strong>3PL</strong>) — contract warehousing, fulfillment, transportation management, freight consolidation are primary service lines. <strong>ODW Logistics</strong> serves consumer packaged goods (<strong>CPG</strong>), food and beverage, retail, and e-commerce as primary segments. Other major mid-sized <strong>3PL</strong> providers in the U.S. include <strong>NFI Industries</strong> (<strong>Sid Brown</strong> CEO; <strong>New Jersey</strong>); <strong>Saddle Creek Logistics</strong> (<strong>Cliff Otto</strong> President; <strong>Lakeland, Florida</strong>); <strong>Ryder Supply Chain Solutions</strong> (<strong>Robert Sanchez</strong> CEO; <strong>Miami</strong>); <strong>Penske Logistics</strong> (<strong>Andy Moses</strong> President); <strong>Schneider Logistics</strong> (<strong>Mark Rourke</strong> CEO); <strong>JB Hunt Dedicated Contract Services</strong> (<strong>Shelley Simpson</strong> CEO; <strong>Arkansas</strong>); <strong>XPO Logistics</strong> (<strong>Mario Harik</strong> CEO; <strong>Greenwich, Connecticut</strong>); <strong>RXO</strong> (<strong>Drew Wilkerson</strong> CEO); <strong>GXO Logistics</strong> (<strong>Malcolm Wilson</strong> CEO; <strong>Greenwich</strong>); <strong>DHL Supply Chain</strong>; <strong>Kühne+Nagel</strong>; <strong>DSV Solutions</strong>; <strong>CEVA Logistics</strong> (<strong>CMA CGM</strong> owned); are key ecosystem players.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, major warehouse labor management system (<strong>LMS</strong>) providers include <strong>Manhattan Active Labor</strong> (<strong>Eddie Capel</strong> CEO; <strong>Atlanta</strong>); <strong>Blue Yonder Workforce Management</strong> (<strong>Duncan Angove</strong> CEO); <strong>Körber Supply Chain</strong>; <strong>SAP EWM Workforce</strong>; <strong>Easy Metrics</strong> (<strong>Dean Vogler</strong> CEO; <strong>Bellevue, Washington</strong>); <strong>TZA</strong> (<strong>ProTrack Warehouse</strong> Labor Management); <strong>Lucas Systems</strong> (<strong>Ken Ramoutar</strong> Chief Customer Officer); <strong>Honeywell Vocollect</strong>; <strong>Locus Robotics</strong> (<strong>Rick Faulk</strong> CEO; primary AMR integration with LMS); are key ecosystem players. <strong>Engineered labor standards</strong> (<strong>ELS</strong>); standard time measurement and methods analysis are primary methodological approaches. <strong>WERC</strong> (<strong>Warehousing Education and Research Council</strong>; <strong>Mike Mahoney</strong> CEO); is the leading industry organization. Freight consolidation methods include (1) <strong>multi-stop truckload</strong>; (2) <strong>pool distribution</strong>; (3) <strong>LTL consolidation</strong> (less-than-truckload consolidation); (4) <strong>cross-docking</strong>; (5) <strong>continuous moves</strong>; (6) <strong>backhaul optimization</strong>; are primary strategies.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, major U.S. LTL carriers include <strong>FedEx Freight</strong> (<strong>Lance Moll</strong> President); <strong>XPO LTL</strong> (<strong>Mario Harik</strong> CEO); <strong>Old Dominion Freight Line</strong> (<strong>Marty Freeman</strong> CEO; <strong>North Carolina</strong>); <strong>Estes Express Lines</strong>; <strong>Saia Inc</strong> (<strong>Fritz Holzgrefe</strong> CEO); <strong>ABF Freight</strong> (<strong>Seth Runser</strong> President); <strong>R+L Carriers</strong>; <strong>TForce Freight</strong> (<strong>TFI International</strong> owned); <strong>Averitt Express</strong>; <strong>Pitt Ohio</strong>; are among the top ten major LTL carriers. Freight consolidation platforms include <strong>FreightSnap</strong> (dimensional scanning); <strong>Convoy</strong> (shutting down — being acquired by <strong>Flexport</strong>); <strong>Uber Freight</strong> (<strong>Lior Ron</strong> CEO; owned by <strong>Transplace</strong>); <strong>C.H. Robinson</strong> (<strong>Dave Bozeman</strong> CEO; <strong>Eden Prairie, Minnesota</strong>); <strong>Echo Global Logistics</strong>; <strong>Coyote Logistics</strong> (<strong>RXO</strong> owned); <strong>Worldwide Express</strong>; are key ecosystem players. <strong>NMFTA</strong> (<strong>National Motor Freight Traffic Association</strong>; <strong>Joe Ohr</strong> COO); <strong>NMFC</strong> (<strong>National Motor Freight Classification</strong>); are primary LTL classification standards. In conclusion, <strong>Clark</strong>'s labor management advice suggests that globally warehouse workforce and freight consolidation cost optimization is being fundamentally redesigned — for supply chain managers, <strong>AI-powered LMS</strong> and <strong>multi-modal consolidation</strong> appear to be primary strategic priorities.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. <strong>Jeff Clark</strong> (<strong>ODW Logistics</strong>); the right framework and training are the foundation of cost reduction.<br>
2. <strong>AI</strong> provides the ability to see picking efficiency in the warehouse.<br>
3. Shift start, breaks, and restrooms are primary sources of inefficiency.<br>
4. Freight consolidation strategies include <strong>LTL</strong>, <strong>pool distribution</strong>, and <strong>cross-docking</strong>.<br>
5. <strong>ODW Logistics</strong> has been a regional <strong>3PL</strong> player in the <strong>Columbus, Ohio</strong> area since 1971.</p>
