<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>PMMI (The Association for Packaging and Processing Technologies)</strong> announced at its 2026 Executive Leadership Conference (Amelia Island, Florida) that it has approved the establishment of the <strong>"PACK gives BACK" Charitable Foundation</strong>. The new <strong>501(c)(3)</strong> organisation is designed to amplify the industry's philanthropic impact and strengthen member companies' contributions to community projects. The Board announced that the foundation has been adopted as a strategic priority.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The foundation received a <strong>$6 million initial investment from PMMI's reserve fund</strong>. This fund will be used both to expand existing charitable programmes and to support new initiatives. PACK gives BACK aims to create scalable impact by centralising the donations packaging and processing technology industry companies make to local communities.</p><p style="text-align: justify">PMMI officials stressed that the foundation will strengthen the sector's social responsibility profile and boost employee engagement. Board members, who discussed the topic at the Amelia Island conference, stated that the foundation will announce its first grants in the coming months. Major companies in the sector were invited to provide additional funding for the foundation. PACK gives BACK is taking shape as one of the largest coordinated charitable initiatives in the US packaging industry.</p>

<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. PMMI's Board approved the establishment of the PACK gives BACK Charitable Foundation.<br>
2. The foundation is formed as a 501(c)(3) with a $6 million initial investment from PMMI reserves.<br>
3. The goal is to amplify the philanthropic impact of the packaging and processing technology sector.<br>
4. The decision was announced at the 2026 Executive Leadership Conference (Amelia Island, Florida).<br>
5. First grants are expected to be announced in the coming months.</p>
