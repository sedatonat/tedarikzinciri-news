<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Liebherr mobile harbour cranes (MHCs)</strong> secured the majority of global new orders in 2025, achieving a <strong>record market share</strong>. The figures indicate <strong>sustained demand</strong> from terminal operators for established mobile harbour crane solutions, particularly as ports diversify <strong>cargo mixes</strong> without expanding fixed infrastructure.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Heavy-lift units</strong> remained central to procurement decisions, reflecting continued growth in steel, project cargo, and oversized shipments. <strong>Electric drive configurations</strong> gained further ground, cited for reducing <strong>local emissions</strong> while maintaining operational output. <strong>Portal slewing cranes</strong> also featured in bulk operations where berth efficiency and cycle consistency were priorities. Collectively, these trends shaped a <strong>solid forward order book</strong> extending into 2026.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Andreas Ritschel, General Manager Sales Mobile Harbour Cranes at Liebherr-Rostock GmbH, said that as the leading manufacturer of heavy-duty mobile harbour cranes, Liebherr designs cranes to deliver <strong>strength, precision, and longevity</strong>. Liebherr aims to track electrification, automation, and data-driven operation trends to provide solutions aligned with evolving port demands. The company's market position also signals <strong>supplier consolidation</strong> in the global port equipment market.</p>
