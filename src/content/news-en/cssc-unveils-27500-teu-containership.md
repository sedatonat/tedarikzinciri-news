<p>China State Shipbuilding Corporation (CSSC) unveiled the world's largest LNG-powered container vessel with a capacity of 27,500 TEU at the SMM Maritime Fair held in Hamburg. The massive vessel, named "Green Sealion 27500," stands out for both its capacity and environment-friendly technologies. The ship can operate on both conventional fuels and LNG, and has the capability to receive zero-emission energy while in port. Its optimized hull design has improved fuel efficiency.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>At the same event, CSSC also unveiled other ship designs powered by different fuel types and equipped with environmentally friendly technologies. These include the "Green Sealion 20000" with a capacity of 20,000 TEU powered by ammonia and the "Green Sealion 16000" with a capacity of 16,000 TEU powered by LNG. Additionally, modern liquefied gas carriers designed for ethane and ammonia transportation were also on display.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>These next-generation vessels are seen as a major step forward in environmental sustainability and energy efficiency in maritime transport. The ability to conduct zero-emission operations in ports signals a path toward leading environmentally friendly maritime transport in the future.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5>Key Points:</h5>\n<ol>\n  <li><p><strong>Green Sealion 27500</strong>: The world's largest container vessel, powered by LNG and with a capacity of 27,500 TEU.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Environment-Friendly Technology</strong>: Features power connection capability for zero-emission operation while in port.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Fuel Efficiency</strong>: Its optimized hull design results in lower fuel consumption.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>New Designs</strong>: Ammonia-fueled vessels with 20,000 TEU capacity and LNG-fueled vessels with 16,000 TEU capacity were also unveiled.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Investment in the Future</strong>: These vessels will play an important role in environmental sustainability in maritime transport.</p></li>\n</ol>
