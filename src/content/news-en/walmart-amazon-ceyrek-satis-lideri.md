<p style="text-align: justify">Amazon has seized the top spot in quarterly sales for the first time, surpassing <strong>Walmart</strong> in the fourth quarter of 2024. During this period, Amazon generated revenue of $187.8 billion, while Walmart reported revenue of $180.6 billion. ​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Amazon's achievement stems from the company's strong performance in e-commerce and cloud computing. In particular, <strong>Amazon Web Services (AWS)</strong> generated revenue of $25 billion with a 17% increase in the first quarter of 2024. Additionally, Amazon's North America sales rose 12% to $86.3 billion, while international revenue climbed 10% to $31.9 billion. The company's net income surged 225% compared to the same period of the previous year, reaching $10.4 billion. ​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Walmart, meanwhile, achieved revenue of $180.6 billion with a 4.1% increase during the same period. The company's comparable store sales in the United States grew 4.6%, while global e-commerce sales rose 16%. Walmart's advertising business also experienced a 29% increase. However, Walmart's earnings guidance for the first quarter of 2025 fell short of expectations, raising concerns among investors. ​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Both companies are making substantial investments in logistics and delivery services. Walmart utilizes its 4,700 stores as rapid delivery hubs, offering same-day delivery service through independent drivers via its <strong>Spark</strong> platform. In this way, in 2024 it delivered 5 billion items on the same day, reaching 93% of U.S. households. Amazon has expanded its delivery network, delivering 5.9 billion packages in 2023 and surpassing competitors such as <strong>UPS</strong> and <strong>FedEx</strong> in this area. ​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Both companies face criticism regarding employee working conditions. According to research conducted by <strong>Oxfam</strong>, half of Amazon and Walmart employees feel constantly monitored in the workplace, a situation that creates pressure for them to work faster. ​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In this competitive environment, Amazon and Walmart continue to make strategic moves to increase their market share. While Amazon maintains its strength in e-commerce and cloud computing, Walmart is expanding its physical store network and rapid delivery services. Both companies continue to invest in technological innovation and logistics infrastructure to provide better service to customers.​</p>
<p><br></p>

  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Walmart's revenue for the same period was recorded at $180.6 billion.​</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Amazon's cloud computing service <strong>AWS</strong> provided revenue of $25 billion with a 17% increase.​</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Walmart's comparable store sales in the United States increased by 4.6%.​</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Both companies are focusing on expanding their delivery networks to increase customer satisfaction.​</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Criticism regarding employee working conditions continues for both companies.</p></li>
</ul>
