<p>Profitability in the logistics sector remained weak throughout 2023. According to the American Transportation Research Institute's (ATRI) annual report, operating margins in the trucking sector fell to 6 percent or lower. This situation is directly linked to declining revenues and rising costs, particularly in truckload and specialized segments. Significant increases were recorded across expense categories, including insurance premiums, driver wages, and maintenance costs. Additionally, empty mile ratios and driver turnover rates both increased.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5>Key Points:</h5>
<ul>
  <li><p>Operating margins remained at 6 percent or below.</p></li>
  <li><p>Revenues declined while costs rose.</p></li>
  <li><p>Driver wages and maintenance expenses increased substantially.</p></li>
  <li><p>Empty mile ratios and driver turnover rates increased.</p></li>
</ul>
