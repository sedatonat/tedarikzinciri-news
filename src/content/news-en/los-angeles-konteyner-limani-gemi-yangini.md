<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Port of Los Angeles</strong>, the busiest maritime trade gateway in the United States, was forced to temporarily halt operations on the night of November 21 following a hazardous materials fire aboard the container ship <strong>One Henry Hudson</strong>. The incident was recorded as an emergency affecting both port security and public health in the region.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The Los Angeles Fire Department quickly declared the fire a "<strong>major emergency</strong>." As the ship was docked at the port and there was a risk of flames spreading to hazardous cargo, port management established a <strong>water security zone</strong> and <strong>completely suspended operations at four of the seven container terminals</strong> in the port.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">After the fire was brought under control, the vessel was pulled <strong>outside the port breakwater with the assistance of tugboats</strong> and anchored in a safer location. According to Bloomberg's vessel tracking data, the Panama-flagged One Henry Hudson had called at Asian ports including Tokyo, Nagoya, Kobe, and Singapore before arriving in Los Angeles on November 19. Port authorities confirmed that the vessel, with a capacity of approximately <strong>8,200 TEU</strong>, was carrying hazardous cargo.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In the initial hours of the fire, the Los Angeles Fire Department and emergency response teams in the area responded with <strong>more than 100 personnel</strong>. Due to heavy smoke, city officials issued warnings to residents to <strong>keep windows closed and remain indoors</strong> for several hours. This advisory was lifted once the fire was brought under control in the morning hours.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Los Angeles Mayor <strong>Karen Bass</strong> stated that the fire originated in a container carrying hazardous materials and that the response was conducted with care. Fire suppression crews used water cannons to cool the ship's hull and prevented the fire from spiraling out of control. No injuries were reported from the fire.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Maritime historian <strong>Sal Mercogliano</strong> provided live commentary on social media regarding aerial footage of the incident, drawing public attention to port security risks and the dangers of hazardous materials transport.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">This incident occurred three years after the Los Angeles–Long Beach port complex experienced severe congestion during the pandemic. Throughout 2025, U.S. President Donald Trump's tariff announcements prompted importers to front-load shipments, causing fluctuations in port volumes. Despite this, the Port of Los Angeles had not experienced significant delays or congestion in recent months.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Although the fire caused a brief operational pause, port officials stated that operations would <strong>gradually return to normal as the day progressed</strong>. However, the incident once again underscored the importance of emergency response capacity at major ports where hazardous materials transport is heavy.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h4>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">A <strong>hazardous materials fire</strong> broke out aboard the vessel One Henry Hudson.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Due to the fire, <strong>4 terminals at LA Port suspended operations</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">No injuries were reported on the vessel; residents in the area received a temporary "shelter in place" advisory.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">More than 100 firefighters brought the fire under control; the vessel was <strong>moved to open water</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The incident caused a temporary disruption to the port's stable operational flow in the post-pandemic period.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>----------</p>\n<p><strong>News Link: </strong><a href="https://www.supplychainbrain.com/articles/42891-container-ship-fire-halts-operations-at-port-of-los-angeles"><u>https://www.supplychainbrain.com/articles/42891-container-ship-fire-halts-operations-at-port-of-los-angeles</u></a></p>\n<p>--------------------</p>\n<p><strong>Author: </strong><a href="http://sedatonat.com/"><u>SedatOnat.com</u></a></p>\n<p>--------------------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
