<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Project44</strong> is adding the <strong>AI Freight Procurement Agent</strong> to its <strong>Intelligent TMS</strong> platform and automating carrier selection, rate comparison, and negotiations across modes. The new agent is replacing static, periodic bid cycles and spreadsheet-based negotiations with continuous, <strong>AI</strong>-based sourcing, mini-bids, and renewal negotiations within defined guardrails. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>project44</strong>, founded in 2014 by <strong>Jett McCandless</strong> in <strong>Chicago</strong>, competes against <strong>FourKites</strong>, <strong>Shippeo</strong>, <strong>Tive</strong>, <strong>Overhaul</strong>, and <strong>Wakeo</strong> in the <strong>real-time visibility platform</strong> space. The company introduced its <strong>Intelligent TMS</strong> product under the <strong>Movement</strong> brand in 2024, thereby expanding from <strong>visibility</strong> into the <strong>execution</strong> domain. <strong>Insight Partners</strong>, <strong>TPG</strong>, <strong>Goldman Sachs</strong>, <strong>Emergence Capital</strong>, and <strong>Sapphire Ventures</strong> are leading investors in <strong>project44</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Jett McCandless</strong>, founder and <strong>CEO</strong> of <strong>project44</strong>, stated: "<strong>Freight procurement</strong> is one of the largest controllable cost drivers in the supply chain. <strong>Intelligent TMS</strong> lays the foundation for continuous optimization. The <strong>AI Freight Procurement Agent</strong> transforms analytics into autonomous action within defined guardrails, delivering measurable savings while maintaining full control." From a supply chain perspective, the traditional flow of <strong>freight procurement</strong> consists of stages including <strong>RFP</strong> (Request for Proposal), <strong>RFQ</strong> (Request for Quotation), <strong>RFI</strong> (Request for Information), <strong>annual bid</strong>, <strong>mini-bid</strong>, <strong>spot market</strong>, <strong>routing guide</strong>, <strong>tendering</strong>, <strong>award allocation</strong>, and <strong>contract execution</strong>. Platforms such as <strong>Sleek Technologies</strong>, <strong>Emerge</strong>, <strong>Loadsmart</strong>, <strong>Convoy</strong> (shut down), <strong>Uber Freight</strong>, <strong>C.H. Robinson</strong>, and <strong>Transfix</strong> offer similar automation capabilities in the <strong>digital freight matching</strong> space.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>agentic AI</strong> is a next-generation <strong>AI</strong> architecture that not only analyzes data but gains the ability to take action without human intervention. <strong>Agent</strong>s developed by <strong>OpenAI</strong>, <strong>Anthropic</strong>, <strong>Google DeepMind</strong>, <strong>Microsoft</strong>, <strong>Salesforce</strong> (<strong>Agentforce</strong>), <strong>SAP</strong> (<strong>Joule</strong>), <strong>Oracle</strong>, and <strong>ServiceNow</strong> are being integrated into supply chain software. The concept of <strong>guardrails</strong> is based on <strong>NIST AI RMF</strong>, <strong>EU AI Act</strong>, and <strong>ISO/IEC 42001</strong> standards and limits the permitted action space for <strong>AI</strong> agents. <strong>Multi-agent system</strong> architectures are built with <strong>orchestrator</strong>, <strong>specialist agent</strong>, <strong>verifier agent</strong>, and <strong>human-in-the-loop</strong> components. <strong>Reinforcement learning from human feedback</strong> (<strong>RLHF</strong>), <strong>chain-of-thought reasoning</strong>, <strong>tool use</strong>, and <strong>retrieval-augmented generation</strong> (<strong>RAG</strong>) are core technical components of modern <strong>agentic AI</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the <strong>Intelligent TMS</strong> category combines <strong>real-time visibility</strong>, <strong>execution</strong>, <strong>procurement</strong>, and <strong>analytics</strong> on a single platform, unlike traditional <strong>TMS</strong> (<strong>SAP TM</strong>, <strong>Oracle OTM</strong>, <strong>Manhattan TMS</strong>, <strong>BluJay</strong>, <strong>MercuryGate</strong>, <strong>Descartes</strong>, <strong>Trimble TMS</strong>, <strong>3Gtms</strong>) providers. The shortcomings of the <strong>annual bid cycle</strong> become evident in market volatility — particularly during <strong>diesel</strong> price fluctuations, <strong>capacity crunch</strong>, <strong>port congestion</strong>, and <strong>peak season</strong> periods. Strategies such as <strong>continuous procurement</strong>, <strong>dynamic pricing</strong>, <strong>spot/contract blend</strong>, <strong>capacity reservation</strong>, and <strong>strategic carrier partnership</strong> are becoming new-era shields for <strong>shipper</strong>s. <strong>Lane analytics</strong>, <strong>carrier scorecard</strong>, <strong>OTIF</strong> (On Time In Full), <strong>tender acceptance rate</strong>, and <strong>cost per mile</strong> metrics are data inputs for <strong>AI</strong>-driven decision mechanisms. Ultimately, <strong>project44</strong>'s <strong>AI Freight Procurement Agent</strong> represents a tangible example of the transition from <strong>visibility</strong> to <strong>autonomous execution</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Points:</strong><br>
1. <strong>Project44</strong> is adding the <strong>AI Freight Procurement Agent</strong> to <strong>Intelligent TMS</strong>.<br>
2. Static bid cycles and spreadsheet negotiations are transforming into continuous <strong>AI</strong> sourcing.<br>
3. Mini-bids and renewal negotiations are automated within guardrails.<br>
4. <strong>Jett McCandless</strong> identifies <strong>freight procurement</strong> as the largest controllable cost driver.<br>
5. Autonomous action is balanced with maintained control.</p>
