<p style="text-align: justify">The U.S. trucking industry is facing <strong>a pronounced decline in demand</strong> despite expectations for a peak season in the second half of 2025. While <strong>41% of logistics professionals reported "high demand"</strong> in April, this figure fell to just <strong>26% by October 2025</strong>. These figures are based on <strong>a survey conducted by Tech.co with more than 270 logistics experts</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">According to the survey, the sector's traditional seasonal rhythm has been disrupted: instead of the typical increase in shipment volumes leading up to the Christmas and year-end period, <strong>shipments are below expectations and carriers are facing empty capacity</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tariffs intensifying financial pressure</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Tech.co editor <strong>Jack Turner</strong> stated in a November 19 statement that this decline is alarming for the industry.<br>\n20% of respondents answered that <strong>"our top priority is managing financial pressures"</strong>.<br>\nThese pressures include:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Widespread tariffs</strong> imposed by the U.S.,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Weakening in global trade,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Rising operating costs<br>\n.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Tariffs' particular impact on imports from China, India, Europe, and Mexico has led to <strong>declining freight volumes throughout the supply chain</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Industry research confirms the decline</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Consistent with Tech.co's findings:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>DAT Freight & Analytics</strong> reported that trucking freight volumes <strong>fell for the fourth consecutive month in October</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>ACT Research</strong> stated in its October 23 report that the sector is experiencing an <strong>"extended correction cycle"</strong>.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The drivers of this cycle include:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Soft demand,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Excess capacity (too many trucks, too little freight),</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Increasing cost pressures due to tariffs.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Many shippers built up their inventories in mid-year, immediately before tariff increases took effect. <strong>This pulled down expected shipment demand for the October–December period</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Port data also shows weakness</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Los Angeles Port</strong> reported volume losses of <strong>4% month-over-month</strong> in October and <strong>more than 6% year-over-year</strong>.<br>\nPort Executive Director <strong>Gene Seroka</strong> said in his November 18 briefing:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">U.S. retail and manufacturing sectors <strong>have full inventory levels</strong>,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Therefore, ports should expect more <strong>soft volume</strong> in the final two months of the year.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">According to Seroka, the <strong>"stocking rush"</strong> that occurred before the broad-based U.S. tariffs that took effect in early August <strong>triggered today's slowdown</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Fragile outlook across the sector</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">As demand falls, industry operators are struggling with:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Low freight rates,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Rising operating costs (fuel, labor, insurance),</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Tightening financing conditions.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Some carriers, particularly small fleets, <strong>report having to operate at a loss due to excess capacity</strong>.</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">On the <strong>manufacturing and consumption</strong> side:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The U.S. labor market is moving cautiously,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Some retailers and manufacturers are focused on <strong>drawing down existing inventory</strong> rather than placing new orders.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">This dynamic is keeping trucking weak even during the year's busiest period.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>What does the sector's future hold?</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Experts expect that in the first half of 2026:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tariffs</strong> will continue to weigh on trade,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Inventory destocking</strong> will continue,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Excess capacity</strong> will continue to pressure carriers.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">For sustainable recovery:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Trade flows must normalize,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Excess capacity must be removed from the market,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Tariff impacts must ease<br>\n.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key takeaways:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">U.S. trucking freight demand <strong>fell approximately 33% between April and October</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Demand remains weak even during peak season: only <strong>26%</strong> reported "high demand" in October.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Tariffs are weighing on trade and freight volumes; financial pressures are mounting.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Los Angeles Port lost <strong>4% month-over-month and 6% year-over-year</strong> volume in October.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The sector is being hit by a combination of <strong>excess capacity + weak demand + tariff shock</strong>.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>----------</p>\n<p><strong>News Link: </strong><a
