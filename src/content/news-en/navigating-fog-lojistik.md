<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Council of Supply Chain Management Professionals (CSCMP)</strong> released its annual <strong>"State of Logistics Report"</strong> for 2024 under the title <strong>"Navigating Through the Fog."</strong> The report reflects the ongoing atmosphere of <strong>uncertainty</strong> in the global supply chain and logistics sector. Four months later, speaking at the <strong>CSCMP Annual EDGE Conference,</strong> the report's lead author <strong>Korhan Acar</strong> emphasized that the sector is still moving through the same foggy environment.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Acar noted that the sector had been "searching for visibility" last year with expectations of recovery, but this year the fog has not cleared—instead, it has thickened. This picture is largely shaped by <strong>the Trump administration's "Liberation Day" tariffs</strong> and <strong>uncertainty surrounding logistics service demand recovery.</strong> Global trade dynamics, in particular, are creating imbalances across transportation modes.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The report's sectoral details support this overall picture.<br>
In the <strong>motor carrier</strong> segment, profitability has fallen to its lowest level in a decade. Average <strong>spot rates remain at $2 per mile,</strong> while <strong>contract rates</strong> hover around <strong>$2.40 per mile.</strong> Sluggish demand and increased competition are particularly straining smaller carriers.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Parcel</strong> volumes have stabilized in 2025 following declines in 2024. However, operators are now pursuing <strong>yield-focused</strong> strategies instead of chasing volume. Major players such as <strong>U.S. Postal Service, UPS,</strong> and <strong>FedEx</strong> face mounting pressure from <strong>Amazon,</strong> regional carriers, and <strong>gig economy</strong>-based services. This dynamic is reshaping pricing policies across the sector.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">On the <strong>ocean freight</strong> front, an <strong>overcapacity</strong> problem persists. This condition is driving freight rates lower. Companies are experiencing declines in import volumes following <strong>frontloading</strong> operations conducted during the summer months, particularly in preparation for tariffs.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Air freight</strong> rates have remained stable at approximately <strong>$3.10 per kg.</strong> However, growth momentum is weakening due to <strong>tariffs</strong> and <strong>de minimis exception regulations</strong> on small packages. Experts forecast that many companies will shift portions of air shipments to ocean transport due to cost pressures.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In <strong>rail</strong> transportation, <strong>flat or modest growth</strong> is expected for 2025. <strong>Intermodal transportation</strong> in particular is gaining strength, while weakness persists in <strong>bulk</strong> and <strong>energy cargo</strong> segments.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In the <strong>warehousing</strong> market, <strong>vacancy levels</strong> are rising and <strong>absorption</strong> is slowing. Nevertheless, <strong>resilient demand</strong> is holding. This trend reflects the continuing impact of <strong>e-commerce</strong>-driven dynamics.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Acar stresses that companies must focus on <strong>resilience</strong> in the current environment of uncertainty. According to him, both <strong>shippers</strong> and <strong>carriers</strong> must now view resilience not as a luxury, but as a <strong>strategic necessity.</strong> <strong>Artificial intelligence (AI)</strong> and <strong>automation</strong> technologies are reducing the cost of building resilient supply chains; therefore, the real risk is identified as <strong>standing still.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This emphasis indicates that the logistics sector must shift to a model driven not solely by cost concerns, but by <strong>adaptability and flexibility.</strong> Organizations accelerating technology investments are increasing their potential to gain competitive advantage over the long term.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, the CSCMP report demonstrates that the global logistics ecosystem has not yet emerged from uncertainty, but that firms advancing along the axes of <strong>strategic resilience, digitalization, and flexibility</strong> will gain a competitive edge in this environment.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li><p><strong>Motor carrier profitability</strong> is at its lowest level in a decade.</p></li>
  <li><p><strong>Parcel volumes</strong> are stabilizing in 2025; focus shifts to <strong>yield.</strong></p></li>
  <li><p><strong>Ocean freight</strong> is experiencing price declines due to overcapacity.</p></li>
  <li><p><strong>Air freight rates</strong> remain stable; growth momentum is weakening.</p></li>
  <li><p><strong>Rail transportation</strong> is showing modest growth.</p></li>
  <li><p><strong>In the warehousing market,</strong> vacancy levels are rising but demand remains resilient.</p></li>
  <li><p><strong>Resilience</strong> is now viewed as a <strong>strategic necessity</strong> in the sector.</p></li>
  <li><p><strong>AI and automation</strong> are reducing the cost of building resilient supply chains.</p></li>
</ul>
