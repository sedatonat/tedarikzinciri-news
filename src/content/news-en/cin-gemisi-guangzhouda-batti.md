<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>A Chinese-flagged vessel off Guangzhou</strong> <strong>sank following a collision with a Singapore-registered container ship.</strong></p>
<p><br>
The incident occurred <strong>Saturday night</strong> and was confirmed by the <strong>Singapore Maritime and Port Authority (MPA)</strong> in a statement released Sunday.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Two of the 15 crew members aboard the Chinese vessel Hai Li 5 remain missing.</strong></p>
<p><br>
<strong>No injuries were reported among the crew of the container ship Wan Hai A17.</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The MPA <strong>launched an investigation to determine the cause of the incident.</strong></p>
<p><br>
Search and rescue teams were reported to be <strong>continuing operations to locate the two missing seafarers.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Accident Details and Investigation</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Location of accident:</strong> Off Guangzhou, South China Sea</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Vessels involved:<br>
Hai Li 5</strong> (Chinese-flagged) – 15 crew members, 2 missing<br>
<strong>Wan Hai A17</strong> (Singapore-registered) – no injuries</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Time of incident:</strong> Saturday night, October 25, 2025</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Responsible authority:</strong> <strong>Singapore Maritime and Port Authority (MPA)</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">The MPA stated that the investigation into the collision is being conducted in terms of both <strong>technical navigation errors</strong> and <strong>maritime traffic management.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Shipping Traffic Congestion in the South China Sea</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The waters off Guangzhou are located immediately south of the <strong>Pearl River Delta</strong> and are known to be <strong>one of Asia's busiest shipping routes.</strong></p>
<p><br>
The region sits at the center of <strong>high-volume container shipping</strong> between <strong>China, Hong Kong, Singapore, and the Philippines.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Recent years have seen an increase in similar collision incidents in the region, attributed to <strong>heavy regional maritime traffic and poor weather conditions.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Chinese vessel Hai Li 5</strong> <strong>sank off Guangzhou.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Two of 15 crew members are missing; 13 were rescued.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Singapore-registered Wan Hai A17</strong> crew <strong>sustained no injuries.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The MPA</strong> <strong>launched a formal investigation into the collision.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Search and rescue operations</strong> are being conducted in coordination between China and Singapore.</p></li>
</ul>
