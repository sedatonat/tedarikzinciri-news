<p><strong>ABB</strong> has released its 2024 <strong>Generations</strong> articles under the theme "Expanding Horizons," designed to explore technological innovations, personalities, and collaborations that enable a smarter, safer, and more sustainable future in the maritime sector.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>This year's <strong>Generations</strong> publication emphasizes the maritime industry's need to transition to a new energy paradigm, increase operational efficiency, and adopt sustainable business models and best practices. It further highlights that accelerating operational excellence while promoting decarbonization means navigating new horizons, unlocking innovation, seizing opportunities, and achieving success. Throughout this process, the unique expertise of <strong>ABB Marine &amp; Ports</strong> in electrification, digitalization, and automation takes center stage.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>The publication features insights from <strong>Durand Naidoo</strong>, CEO of <strong>Linsen Nambi</strong>—South Africa's sole shipping company with 100% Black ownership—and <strong>Magda Kopczyńska</strong>, Director General of the European Commission's Directorate-General for Mobility and Transport, to underscore the value of diversity and inclusion in the maritime workforce.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>On the technical front, the <strong>ABB Dynafin™</strong> solution, developed under the leadership of <strong>Veli-Pekka Peljo</strong>, Senior Project Manager at ABB Solution Development, stands out. Peljo notes that success in developing the solution was made possible through "having the right mix of people, <strong>ABB</strong>'s strong innovation culture, and a commitment to always doing better."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Innovation emerges as one of the main themes of this year's <strong>Generations</strong> publication. With artificial intelligence (AI) among many developments shaping the maritime industry's digital transformation, readers are presented with how <strong>ABB</strong>'s AI-enabled smarter container handling solutions and the combination of AI with <strong>ABB</strong>'s domain-specific expertise facilitate more efficient maritime operations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>ABB</strong> is also driving various projects to enhance sustainability in the maritime sector. For example, <strong>ABB</strong> and <strong>Wallenius Marine</strong> are collaborating with <strong>Tärntank</strong> on <strong>OVERSEA</strong> fleet support services, aiming to support digitalization and sustainability goals across a fleet of six vessels.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Additionally, <strong>ABB</strong> was selected as the sole propulsion systems provider for five new hybrid ferries for <strong>Washington State Ferries</strong>. This partnership contributes to the adoption of environmentally friendly solutions in maritime transportation.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Among <strong>ABB</strong>'s innovative maritime solutions is the use of the <strong>ABB Azipod®</strong> propulsion system in the modernization of <strong>Juan Carlos I</strong>, the flagship of the <strong>Spanish Navy</strong>. This retrofit project aims to enhance energy efficiency in military maritime operations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>In conclusion, <strong>ABB</strong>'s 2024 <strong>Generations</strong> publication sheds light on the sector through articles emphasizing the importance of sustainability, innovation, and diversity in the maritime industry. Through its expertise in electrification, digitalization, and automation, <strong>ABB</strong> plays a critical role in shaping the future of the maritime sector.</p>
<p><br></p>
