<p>Supply chains sometimes experience problems before they even begin—not during end-to-end management, but at the outset. The core causes of failed supply chains are data silos, poor planning infrastructure, and a lack of process ownership. According to Gartner data, 62% of companies cannot access 40% of the data within their chain.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>This situation seriously weakens forecast accuracy and inventory management in supply and demand planning. AI-based integration layers consolidate data flow between disparate systems, enhancing visibility. However, technology alone is insufficient; unclear process ownership prevents data from being used meaningfully.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>In successful cases, companies redefine process accountability by linking each link in the chain's KPIs to one another.</strong> <strong>Digital twin-based modeling identifies bottlenecks at the beginning of the chain, optimizing resource allocation.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Ultimately, to prevent a supply chain from failing "before it starts," it requires corporate discipline and process ownership as much as data integration.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>Key Points:</strong><br>\n- Data silos cause the chain to become stuck before it begins.<br>\n- Gartner: Companies cannot access 40% of their data.<br>\n- AI-based integration enhances visibility.<br>\n- Lack of process ownership renders data ineffective.<br>\n- Digital twin identifies bottlenecks in advance.</p>\n<p><br>\n---<br>\n<strong>News Link: </strong><a href="https://www.supplychainbrain.com/blogs/1-think-tank/post/42324-why-your-supply-chain-is-stuck-before-it-starts"><u>https://www.supplychainbrain.com/blogs/1-think-tank/post/42324-why-your-supply-chain-is-stuck-before-it-starts</u></a><br>\n-------------------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
