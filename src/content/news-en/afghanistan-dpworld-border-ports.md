<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Afghanistan</strong> is taking a significant step with <strong>DP World</strong> to modernize its <strong>Hairatan</strong> and <strong>Torkham</strong> border gates with the goal of strengthening border trade and regional logistics integration. According to statements from the Afghan government, an <strong>investment term sheet</strong> was signed between the <strong>Afghanistan Ministry of Finance</strong> and DP World. The document was signed during a ceremony held on <strong>December 7 in Kabul</strong> at the office of the <strong>Deputy Prime Minister for Economic Affairs</strong>. The process was led by <strong>Mufti Abdullah Azam</strong>, Deputy Prime Minister for Economic Affairs.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Officials noted that the signed document serves as a <strong>framework leading to binding agreements</strong> and aims to implement <strong>foreign investment</strong> and <strong>operational modernization</strong> projects in subsequent phases. The framework's ultimate objective is defined as transforming Afghanistan's commercial border gates into logistics centers that comply with <strong>international standards</strong>, are <strong>technology-focused</strong>, and have <strong>high capacity</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Two-Phase Modernization Plan</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the government summary, the project is planned in <strong>two phases</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Phase One</strong> covers the <strong>Hairatan border port</strong> located in northern Afghanistan. Hairatan is considered Afghanistan's most critical trade gateway to Central Asia due to its direct connection to the <strong>Uzbek railway network</strong>. At this stage, Hairatan will be equipped with:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">modern <strong>cargo-handling equipment</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">upgraded <strong>yard infrastructure</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>digital customs and gate operation systems</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">advanced <strong>inspection and control technologies</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">are targeted for deployment.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Phase Two</strong> includes the <strong>Torkham border port</strong> located in <strong>Nangarhar province</strong>. Torkham serves as Afghanistan's <strong>busiest land border crossing with Pakistan</strong> and handles a significant portion of the country's import–export volume. The modernization at Torkham aims particularly to manage high-volume trade more rapidly and predictably.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Logistics Corridors and Economic Zones Connection</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Afghan officials are positioning the framework agreement with DP World not merely as an investment in two border gates, but as part of a <strong>broader logistics corridors</strong> and <strong>economic zones</strong> strategy. The modernization of Hairatan and Torkham is viewed as critical for Afghanistan to:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">strengthen its role as a <strong>transit country</strong> in Central Asia–South Asia trade,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">achieve <strong>time and cost reductions</strong> in customs procedures,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">become more integrated into regional supply chains</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">in these respects.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Result of Long-Term Negotiations</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The signed framework is actually the outcome of negotiations that have been ongoing <strong>since 2022</strong>. Afghanistan's <strong>Economic Commission</strong> formally authorized the negotiation of an <strong>MoU</strong> with DP World in 2022. Since then, the parties have maintained technical and institutional contacts. The term sheet signed on December 7 is regarded as the <strong>most concrete output</strong> of this process.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>DP World's Central Asia Strategy</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">For DP World, this framework in Afghanistan represents a new link in the company's investments in <strong>Central Asia</strong> and its surroundings. The company recently signed a joint venture agreement worth <strong>approximately 288 million USD</strong> with <strong>Tashkent Invest</strong> to build a <strong>multimodal logistics terminal</strong> near Tashkent. The Hairatan and Torkham projects in Afghanistan are viewed as the southern extension of this network.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Should the framework agreement in Afghanistan be transformed into binding agreements following <strong>technical studies</strong>, DP World is expected to play an active role in:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>operational expertise</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">terminal operations know-how,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">staff training and digital systems integration</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">areas. In turn, Afghan authorities are expected to carry out <strong>regulatory reform</strong> and <strong>customs modernisation</strong> processes.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Uncertainties and Open Issues</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">At this stage, information disclosed to the public does not include:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">investment amount,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">concession duration,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">construction and commissioning timeline</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">These elements are expected to be clarified in binding agreements to be signed in subsequent periods.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>General Assessment</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Afghanistan's investment framework signed with DP World for Hairatan and Torkham border gates stands out as an important step toward the country's <strong>regional logistics integration</strong> objectives. If implemented, the project could contribute to Afghanistan achieving <strong>efficiency, transparency, and capacity increases</strong> in border trade. At the same time, DP World is expected to further strengthen its growing logistics network along the Central Asia–South Asia axis.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Afghanistan's Ministry of Finance signed an investment framework with <strong>DP World</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The project covers <strong>Hairatan</strong> and <strong>Torkham</strong> border gates.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Hairatan is a strategic transit point connected to the <strong>Uzbek railway network</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Torkham is Afghanistan–Pakistan's <strong>busiest border gate</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The framework paves the way for binding agreements and foreign investment.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Investment amount and timeline have <strong>not yet been disclosed</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
