<p style="text-align: justify">Portugal's infrastructure manager <strong>Infraestruturas de Portugal (IP)</strong> has officially acknowledged years of delays in the rail modernisation works forming part of the <strong>TEN-T corridor</strong>. In February 2016, IP had unveiled an investment plan titled <strong>"Ferrovia 2020"</strong>, presenting a modernisation programme that positioned <strong>freight operators</strong>, rather than passenger services, as the main beneficiaries.</p><p style="text-align: justify">However, despite <strong>10 years</strong> passing since the plan was announced, most of the works remain incomplete. IP officials said the delays resulted from an overly ambitious scope, funding issues, and supply chain disruptions. Portugal's rail network plays a strategic role on the Atlantic-Mediterranean corridor, linking the Iberian Peninsula to central Europe.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The European Union's <strong>Trans-European Transport Network (TEN-T)</strong> policy mandates standardisation, electrification, and the ETCS signalling system for cross-border rail freight. Portugal's delays directly affect capacity on Spain-Portugal cross-border traffic and the EU's 2030/2050 TEN-T delivery targets. IP announced that a revised plan is being prepared and that priorities will be reset.</p>
