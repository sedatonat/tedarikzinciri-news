<p style="text-align: justify">French shipping giant <a href="https://www.cma-cgm.com/"><u><strong>CMA CGM Group</strong></u></a> has taken delivery of the first of four dual-fuel container vessels with a capacity of 24,000 TEU that it ordered. The next-generation vessel operates on liquefied natural gas (<strong>LNG</strong>) and is spearheading the adoption of environmentally friendly technologies in the maritime sector. ​</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ship Characteristics and Environmental Impact:</strong></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">With a capacity of 24,000 TEU (Twenty-foot Equivalent Unit), the vessel ranks among the world's largest container ships. Thanks to its <strong>LNG</strong>-powered engines, it significantly reduces carbon emissions compared to conventional fuels. This marks a critical step toward <strong>CMA CGM Group</strong>'s sustainability objectives.​</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sea Trials and Delivery Process:</strong></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The vessel was delivered to <strong>CMA CGM Group</strong> after successfully completing sea trials. During the trials, the ship's performance, maneuverability, and fuel efficiency were tested. Having passed all tests successfully, the vessel is now ready for operational service.​</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sectoral Impact and Future Plans:</strong></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">This delivery underscores the growing importance of <strong>LNG</strong>-fueled vessels in the maritime sector. <strong>CMA CGM Group</strong> plans to expand its fleet with more environmentally friendly and efficient vessels.
