<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>"View from the fence"</strong> approach underscores that the <strong>cost–speed–resilience</strong> balance in today's supply chains is no longer a temporary objective but rather a <strong>permanent strategic priority</strong>. Growing global uncertainties are driving companies away from the pursuit of a single "optimum model" and toward building a <strong>multi-scenario resilience architecture</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In global manufacturing strategies, firms are reshaping their <strong>global–regional production mix</strong>. <strong>Nearshoring</strong> and <strong>friend-shoring</strong> approaches aim to create <strong>more flexible, regionally balanced supply networks</strong> that counter tariffs, trade barriers, and geopolitical fluctuations. This structure delivers not only cost advantages but also <strong>rapid adaptation and supply security</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>On the operational side</strong>, businesses are dampening supply–demand fluctuations through practices such as <strong>multi-sourcing</strong>, <strong>safety stock</strong>, and <strong>decoupling point</strong> implementation. These buffer mechanisms act as "shock absorbers" at the vulnerable nodes in the chain, maintaining operational stability.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>On the technology front</strong>; solutions such as <strong>real-time visibility</strong>, <strong>event-driven orchestration</strong>, and <strong>AI forecasting</strong> are bringing <strong>foresight and agility</strong> to supply chains. Through this digital transformation, systems can anticipate disruptions, reposition resources, and shift from reactive to proactive modes.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>In financial discipline</strong>, tight control of <strong>working capital</strong> and <strong>cash-to-cash cycle</strong> metrics takes center stage. <strong>Supply chain finance</strong> tools strengthen the chain's financial resilience by supporting supplier liquidity. Thus "financial sustainability" becomes an extension of operational resilience.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>On the sustainability front</strong>, <strong>Scope 3 emissions accounting</strong>, <strong>right-sizing in packaging</strong>, and <strong>eco-design</strong> initiatives are gaining prominence. These steps support carbon reduction and resource efficiency while simultaneously reinforcing brand trust and reputation.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, <strong>uncertainty is no longer an exception but the new normal.</strong> Winning strategies rest not on a single correct model but on <strong>flexible, data-driven, multi-scenario resilience architectures</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5><strong>Key Takeaways</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li><p>Nearshoring and multi-sourcing provide flexibility.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Visibility/AI enhances agility.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Capital discipline encompasses suppliers too.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Right-sizing and Scope 3 take center stage.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Multi-scenario architecture is the new norm.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p>----------</p>\n<p><strong>News Link:</strong> <a href="https://www.supplychainbrain.com/articles/41573-view-from-the-fence" rel="noopener" target="_new"><u>https://www.supplychainbrain.com/articles/41573-view-from-the-fence</u></a></p>\n<p>----------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
