<p style="text-align: justify">Shipments transiting through the <strong>Strait of Hormuz</strong> remain stranded as escalating security concerns and recent attacks on commercial vessels continue to disrupt operations in the region. According to the latest update by <strong>Maersk</strong>, affected cargo includes exports from South Africa and imports destined for the country. These shipments are currently in transit on CMA CGM Antonio or at the <strong>Jebel Ali terminal</strong> in the UAE.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The disruption follows a sharp escalation in regional tensions, with reports indicating that multiple container ships have been targeted in recent days. As a result, safe passage through the Strait of Hormuz cannot be guaranteed, with shipping lines prioritising the safety of crew, vessels and cargo. Under the current conditions, shipments are expected to remain delayed, either onboard the <strong>CMA CGM Antonio or at Jebel Ali terminal</strong>, until the situation stabilises.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Alternative solutions, including <strong>land-bridge options via Oman and Saudi Arabia</strong>, have been explored. However, limited capacity and significantly higher costs have made these options difficult to implement on a larger scale. The situation remains highly dynamic, with ongoing assessments being carried out by regional teams. Shipping stakeholders continue to monitor developments closely as they evaluate possible solutions for the safe onward movement of cargo.</p>
<p><br></p>

1. Shipments through the Strait of Hormuz remain stranded due to security risks.<br>

3. Multiple container ships have reportedly been targeted in the region in recent days.<br>
4. Land-bridge alternatives via Oman and Saudi Arabia faced capacity limits and high costs.<br>
5. Shipowners prioritise safety while regional teams continue dynamic assessments.</p>
