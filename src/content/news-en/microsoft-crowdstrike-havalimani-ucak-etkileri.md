<p>A major computer outage affecting 8.5 million Microsoft devices caused significant disruptions across the supply chain, particularly in the air cargo and last-mile delivery sectors. A faulty software update from Texas-based CrowdStrike brought Microsoft Windows-based systems worldwide to a standstill. While ports, trucking companies, and railroads resolved the issue quickly, the airline industry experienced a longer recovery period. The incident, coinciding with peak e-commerce season, also led to delays in last-mile deliveries.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>\n<ol>\n  <li><p>A major global computer outage affected 8.5 million Microsoft devices worldwide.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The problem stemmed from a faulty software update from Texas-based CrowdStrike.</p></li>\n  <li><p>While ports, trucking companies, and railways resolved issues quickly, the airline industry recovered more slowly.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Occurring during peak e-commerce season, the outage caused delays in last-mile deliveries.</p></li>\n</ol>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>News Link:</strong> <a href="https://www.thescxchange.com/articles/10604-supply-chains-see-ripple-effects-of-microsoft-computer-crash"><u>https://www.thescxchange.com/articles/10604-supply-chains-see-ripple-effects-of-microsoft-computer-crash</u></a></p>
