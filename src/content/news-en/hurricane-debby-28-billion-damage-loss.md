<p>Hurricane Debby reached the southeastern United States on August 5, 2024, and caused a total of $28 billion in damage. This damage includes property destruction, business and wage losses, agricultural damage, infrastructure harm, and supply chain disruptions. Heavy rainfall triggered severe flooding, affecting a broad area from North Florida to the Northeast. The storm also negatively impacted tourism through travel disruptions and coastal erosion, which compounded economic losses.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5>Key Points:</h5>
<ol>
  <li><p>Total damage and economic loss from Hurricane Debby is estimated at $28 billion.</p></li>
  <li><p>Areas with the heaviest rainfall recorded more than 21 inches of precipitation.</p></li>
  <li><p>The storm caused coastal erosion and flooding, resulting in significant losses to the tourism sector.</p></li>
  <li><p>Airport closures and flight cancellations substantially increased economic damage.</p></li>
</ol>
