<p style="text-align: justify">Turkey <strong>temporarily halted two-way vessel traffic through the Dardanelles Strait on November 19</strong>. Both northbound and southbound passages were closed between <strong>07:00–18:00</strong> due to testing activities conducted under the <strong>"Straits Subsea Security Assurance Project"</strong> led by the Defense Industries Presidency.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The announcement was issued to the maritime community by the Dardanelles Traffic Information System, with the notice explicitly stating that traffic would be <strong>completely suspended in both directions</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A strategically vital waterway</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Dardanelles Strait forms the southern passage of the Turkish Straits system and constitutes a critical segment of a global trade corridor extending from the Aegean Sea through the Marmara, and onward to the Black Sea via the Istanbul Strait.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Approximately <strong>100 vessels</strong> transit this route daily. Primary cargo carried includes:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Crude oil</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Refined petroleum products</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Grain</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Fertilizers</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Mining products</strong><br>
among others.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Turkey's Minister of Transport and Infrastructure <strong>Abdulkadir Uroğlu</strong> previously disclosed that <strong>33,026 vessels</strong> transited the Dardanelles during the first nine months of 2025, while <strong>62,736 vessels</strong> passed through the Turkish Straits overall. These figures underscore how critical the temporary closure was from both regional and global logistics perspectives.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Subsea Security Project: Protecting critical infrastructure</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The project, administered by the Defense Industries Presidency, is developing a multi-layered security system designed to protect vessel traffic through the straits and coastal infrastructure against underwater threats.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Technical details regarding the scope of the tests were not shared publicly; however, subsea security projects typically encompass:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Subsea sensor networks,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Acoustic tracking systems,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Unmanned underwater vehicles (UUV),</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Diver detection systems,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Monitoring and analysis software at critical chokepoints<br>
among other components.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Such initiatives are widely deployed globally, particularly to protect strategically important waterways against sabotage, unauthorized passage, subsea explosives, and covert threats.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Short-term impact on global trade</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Because the closure lasted approximately 11 hours, no significant disruptions occurred; however, vessels waiting in the Strait formed temporary queues. Ship owners and agents had to account for minor scheduling delays in their voyage planning.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Given the Strait's geopolitical importance worldwide, such closures can affect:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Transit times,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Schedule reliability,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Fuel consumption and route planning<br>
even if only briefly.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Part of Turkey's security modernization</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">This testing activity is regarded as part of Turkey's broader modernization initiatives in coastal defense and strait security.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Once the system is fully operational:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Continuous monitoring of the straits,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Early warning of subsea threats,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Enhanced security levels for critical infrastructure<br>
are targeted.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The Dardanelles Strait was closed to two-way traffic on November 19 between <strong>07:00–18:00</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Reason: Testing under the Defense Industries Presidency's <strong>Subsea Security Project</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Annual transits through the Strait: <strong>33,000+ vessels</strong> (first 9 months), Turkish Straits total <strong>62,700+</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The route, which handles an average of 100 daily vessel transits, is critical to global commerce.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Tests are part of Turkey's strait security modernization program.</p></li>
</ul>
