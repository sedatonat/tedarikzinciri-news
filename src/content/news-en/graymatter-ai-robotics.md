<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>GrayMatter Robotics</strong> has officially opened its new 100,000-square-foot global headquarters and innovation center in <strong>Carson, California</strong>, unveiling what the company describes as <strong>the most advanced "interactive robotics experience center" in the United States</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
According to the company's announcement, this facility represents a major milestone in the field of <strong>AI-powered manufacturing automation</strong> at the national level.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new center houses <strong>more than 25 active robotic cells</strong> and provides both current and potential customers with the opportunity to experience how <strong>"physical AI" technology</strong> operates in a real production environment.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Building the Factories of Tomorrow in Carson</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ariyan Kabir</strong>, CEO and co-founder of the company, emphasized during the opening ceremony that the new facility is far more than just a headquarters:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"This facility represents our commitment to bringing advanced manufacturing back to American soil and specifically to the South Bay region."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Kabir explained the selection of Carson as driven by <strong>proximity to aerospace, maritime, and advanced manufacturing clusters</strong>, <strong>world-class port infrastructure</strong>, and <strong>highly skilled workforce availability</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The facility has already created <strong>over 100 highly skilled positions</strong> in areas including <strong>AI, robotics, multidisciplinary engineering, manufacturing operations, and technical support</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The company plans to expand this workforce with <strong>hundreds of new positions</strong> over the coming years.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Physical AI: The New Generation of Industrial Automation</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>GrayMatter Robotics</strong> is developing a novel technological approach that represents a transition from digital AI to physical AI.<br>
This approach enables <strong>artificial intelligence to interact directly with the physical world through robotic systems</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">CTO and co-founder <strong>Brual Shah</strong> described this transformation as follows:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Visitors can watch a robot that scans a part it has never seen before and programs itself in just one minute, then processes that part with a precision beyond human capability. This is manufacturing intelligence made tangible."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">The company's proprietary technology, <strong>GMR-AI</strong>, operates on a fundamentally different architecture from traditional industrial automation.<br>
 Based on <strong>physics-informed AI</strong> principles, this system requires no manual programming.<br>
The robots, by understanding <strong>material properties, geometries, forces, and tool behavior through physical models</strong>, can work <strong>safely and precisely</strong> even with parts they have never encountered before.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>"Physical AI" Beyond Digital AI</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Prof. Satyandra K. Gupta</strong>, Chief Scientist and co-founder of GrayMatter Robotics, defines physical AI as follows:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Physical AI is not an extension of digital AI; it is an entirely different category.<br>
Language models can generate text, but our AI can autonomously process an aircraft component it has never seen before.<br>
This is the era where artificial intelligence produces real-world results in the physical manufacturing environment."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">This vision represents <strong>the transition of artificial intelligence from data analysis to the production floor</strong>.<br>
GrayMatter Robotics defines the concept of "the factory of the future" as <strong>a hybrid manufacturing environment based on human–robot–AI collaboration</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Real-World Production Deployment with Industry Partners</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">GrayMatter Robotics' technology is already actively deployed in <strong>large-scale manufacturers</strong>.<br>
 <strong>Pierce Manufacturing (Oshkosh Corporation)</strong>, North America's leading <strong>fire truck manufacturer</strong>, has integrated the company's systems into its production lines.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Bob Schulz, President of Pierce Manufacturing</strong>, commented on the partnership:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"The early results we achieved with GrayMatter Robotics encouraged us to implement the systems at scale. When we visited the Carson facility, we saw they have a vision for how we can grow together."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Partnerships of this kind demonstrate the <strong>scalability and industry compatibility of GrayMatter Robotics' AI-enabled automation systems</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A New Era in U.S. Manufacturing</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The company has accelerated its growth by raising <strong>$85 million in venture capital funding</strong> in recent times.<br>
The new facility stands as the largest outcome of this investment.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">GrayMatter Robotics' objective is to reach <strong>global leadership status in the field of "physical AI-enabled manufacturing"</strong> in the United States.</p>
<p><br>
The center functions not only as a research, development, and manufacturing hub, but also as <strong>an interactive learning laboratory for customers, students, and industry professionals</strong>.</p>
