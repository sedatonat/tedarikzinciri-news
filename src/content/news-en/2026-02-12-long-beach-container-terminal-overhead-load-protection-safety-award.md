<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Long Beach Container Terminal (LBCT)</strong> has been shortlisted for the <strong>2026 TT Club Innovation in Safety Awards</strong> for its <strong>Overhead Load Protection System</strong>, designed to address significant risks in its extensive on-dock rail operations. LBCT operates more than 48,000 feet of rail track — about 14.6 km — making it <strong>one of the largest on-dock rail yards in North America</strong>. The site runs continuously, with train movements, container loading and discharge, inspections and maintenance often taking place simultaneously.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Containers are handled by six <strong>Intermodal Yard Cranes</strong> spanning eight working tracks, while ground personnel operate a wide range of vehicles and equipment. As outlined in the award submission: "There is significant risk to ground personnel when cranes are operating and passing over head." Risks include <strong>falling objects such as twist locks</strong> and debris, as well as components from the crane itself. LBCT engineered an innovative protective canopy and detection architecture to mitigate these hazards.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The system combines sensors that track crane motion with physical <strong>overhead load barriers</strong>, establishing a multi-layer defence between ground personnel and elevated loads. The solution both reinforces existing <strong>personal protective equipment</strong> protocols and preserves operational efficiency. LBCT's approach offers a benchmark model for intermodal rail safety at Pacific coast container terminals.</p>
