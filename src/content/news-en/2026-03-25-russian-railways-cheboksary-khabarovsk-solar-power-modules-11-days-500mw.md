<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Russian Railways</strong> has reported launching a new regular railway route from <strong>Cheboksary</strong> in Central Russia to <strong>Khabarovsk</strong> in the Russian Far East. The line is regarded as an important logistics corridor supporting sustainable energy in Russia's domestic market. <strong>Two block trains</strong> have already travelled the route carrying modules for building solar power stations. The <strong>transit time is 11 days</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">By the end of August 2026, <strong>twenty block trains</strong> are to run the new route delivering solar power production equipment with a total capacity <strong>exceeding 500 MW</strong>. Each block train carries roughly 25 MW of solar panel modules. This scale indicates a major investment aimed at strengthening Russia's energy grid in the Far East.</p><p style="text-align: justify">Cheboksary is the capital of the Chuvash Republic and one of Russia's energy equipment production centres. Khabarovsk is an important industrial hub in Russia's Far East and an area where solar power investment is concentrated. Using the <strong>Trans-Siberian</strong> mainline, the route covers over 6,000 km in 11 days. This reinforces Russian rail's competitive advantage in long-distance transport. Transporting solar panel modules by road or sea would be disadvantageous in breakage risk and cost.</p>
