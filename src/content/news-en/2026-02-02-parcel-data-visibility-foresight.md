<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Parcel shipper</strong>s are simultaneously working to absorb the impact of general rate increases (<strong>GRIs</strong>) while contending with expanding accessorial charges and shifts in carrier performance. Yet many organizations continue to make critical decisions based on incomplete data. In an environment where pricing structures are becoming exponentially more complex year after year, this approach is increasingly indefensible. Organizations that invest in visibility and smarter data have an easier time understanding cost drivers, evaluating carrier proposals, and foreseeing problems before they impact budgets. Within this context, parcel costs continue to climb; <strong>GRI</strong>s typically average between 5% and 7%, and <strong>accessorial charges</strong>, depending on shipping profile, can consume a significant portion of total spending.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This mounting complexity forces organizations to navigate without complete cost visibility amid changing carrier performance and new service options. The need that many organizations are now defining as a "smarter parcel data approach" is precisely where this gap widens. <strong>Shipper</strong>s are moving from forecasting to <strong>scenario modeling</strong>, testing minimum charges, <strong>dimensional (DIM) factors</strong>, discounts, and service level options against actual shipping history to clearly see how contract changes affect costs. This process uncovers significant differences between the initial proposal and the structure best suited to their operations, and provides meaningful leverage in contract negotiations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Shipping records provide a fundamental starting point for holistic understanding; however, the most accurate picture emerges when this data is analyzed together with contracts, invoice details, accessorial charge documentation, and carrier correspondence. When these elements come together, organizations can identify discrepancies with contract terms, unexpected accessorial charge trends, variations in service performance, and line items silently eroding budgets. This kind of integrated view helps teams move from surface-level reporting to actionable insights. But most teams lack the time or capacity to manually reconcile all this data. Manually prepared reports are slow, quickly outdated, and insufficient at capturing the nuance of shifting carrier behavior.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Modern <strong>parcel intelligence</strong> platforms bring these data sources together and analyze them rapidly, enabling organizations to detect changes early and respond with greater confidence. Today's tools deliver unified visibility, timely alerts, and rapid modeling, enabling teams to act on opportunities rather than react to surprises. On the outlook side, the parcel market continues to fragment and pricing structures are growing more complex at an accelerating pace; consequently, <strong>decision intelligence</strong> is becoming a core competency for <strong>shipper</strong>s. Organizations that consolidate their data, proactively model contract options, and continuously monitor performance gain measurable advantages in cost control and service quality. Firms whose current auditing or analytics provider falls short are increasingly moving to platforms that offer deeper visibility, predictive insights, and stronger negotiation support.</p>
<p><br></p>

2. <strong>Accessorial charges</strong> can consume a large portion of total spending depending on shipping profile.<br>
3. <strong>Scenario modeling</strong> reveals differences between the initial proposal and the optimal structure.<br>
4. Shipping data is analyzed together with contracts, invoices, and accessorial charge documentation.<br>
5. <strong>Parcel intelligence</strong> platforms leave manual reporting behind.</p>
