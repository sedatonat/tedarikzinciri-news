<p>Maersk has announced plans to reroute multiple container vessels through the Suez Canal and Red Sea in the near term, following a period of course changes prompted by Houthi attacks on ships in the Red Sea.&nbsp;</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>The move signals a return to the region by major global shipping firms such as Maersk and Hapag-Lloyd. Maersk's share price declined, as the shorter nature of these routes could pressure shipping rates downward.&nbsp;</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Shares of other shipping companies also fell in value. As U.S.-led military operations plan to provide protection to vessels, Maersk and other firms continue to assess security conditions and adjust their routes accordingly.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>News Link:</strong> <a href="https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/maersk-schedules-dozens-vessels-travel-via-suez-canal-2023-12-27/"><u>https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/maersk-schedules-dozens-vessels-travel-via-suez-canal-2023-12-27/</u></a></p>
