<p><strong>The Russian Federation Government</strong>, at a meeting chaired by <strong>Deputy Prime Minister Vitaly Savelyev</strong>, approved a new <strong>road map</strong> for the deployment of <strong>fully autonomous (Level 5) driverless trucks</strong> on public roads.</p>
<p><br>
The meeting was announced by the <strong>Russian Government Press Office</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>The plan aims to expand the limited number of autonomous vehicle programs currently operating in the country to establish <strong>a nationwide driverless transport network</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>90 Level 3 Trucks in Active Use</strong></h5>
<p>Russia currently has <strong>90 trucks equipped with Level 3 autonomous driving technology</strong> in operation.</p>
<p><br>
The new plan envisions expanding the routes where these pilot vehicles operate and opening <strong>new routes dedicated to driverless transport</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Additionally, the deployment of a digital infrastructure system called <strong>"digital road twins"</strong> is planned.</p>
<p><br>
This system will create a virtual digital copy of roads to support <strong>traffic management, sensor data integration, and security monitoring</strong> processes.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>Legal Regulations for Level 5 Autonomous Vehicles Coming</strong></h5>
<p>Under the plan, the <strong>Russian Federation Ministry of Transport</strong> has been tasked with preparing a <strong>draft law concerning vehicles with high automation levels</strong>.</p>
<h5><br>
The draft is expected to be submitted to the government in <strong>the first quarter of 2026</strong>.</h5>
<p><br></p>
<p>This regulation will define the <strong>legal status of fully autonomous (Level 5) vehicles</strong> and <strong>traffic safety standards</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The goal is to enable the safe deployment of driverless vehicles in commercial transport.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>Strategic Goal: Autonomous Logistics Infrastructure</strong></h5>
<p>Russia's initiative is part of its goal to <strong>digitalize the logistics sector and reduce dependence on the human factor</strong>.<br>
The government's long-term plans include:</p>
<ul>
  <li><p><strong>Creation of driverless transport corridors</strong>,</p></li>
  <li><p><strong>Development of intelligent transportation systems</strong>,</p></li>
  <li><p><strong>Strengthening of data sharing and cybersecurity standards</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p>In a statement following the meeting, <strong>Vitaly Savelyev</strong> noted that the project will provide Russia with <strong>technological independence and efficiency in its transport system</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>Global Context: Competition in Autonomous Transport Intensifying</strong></h5>
<p>Russia's step is a response to the research and development work undertaken by the <strong>United States, China, and the European Union</strong> in driverless transport.</p>
<p><br>
As <strong>Level 5 autonomous vehicle testing</strong> accelerates worldwide, countries are also working to establish <strong>legal and ethical frameworks</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>With this plan, Russia aims to become one of a few countries integrating <strong>legal, digital, and operational infrastructure for autonomous transport</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li><p>Russia approved an <strong>official road map for fully autonomous (Level 5)</strong> trucks.</p></li>
  <li><p>The country currently has <strong>90 Level 3 trucks</strong> actively in use.</p></li>
  <li><p>The plan encompasses opening <strong>new driverless routes</strong> and implementing the <strong>"digital road twins" system</strong>.</p></li>
  <li><p>The <strong>Ministry of Transport</strong> will submit <strong>draft legislation on highly automated vehicles</strong> in the first quarter of 2026.</p></li>
  <li><p>Strategic objective: <strong>build a digital logistics infrastructure and expand driverless transport</strong>.</p></li>
  <li><p>This step aims to position Russia ahead in the <strong>global autonomous transport race</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
