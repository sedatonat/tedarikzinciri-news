<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>WD-40</strong> reported rising costs for certain petroleum-based specialty chemicals, a trend <strong>CFO Sara Hyzer</strong> told investors was partly due to recent geopolitical developments in the <strong>Middle East</strong>. The impact of higher raw material costs typically takes 90 to 120 days to appear in the company's <strong>COGS</strong> (<strong>Cost of Goods Sold</strong>), <strong>Hyzer</strong> said, citing <strong>WD-40</strong>'s production cadence and inventory life cycles as factors for the lag. "We do not expect that our gross margin will be significantly impacted until the fourth quarter of <strong>fiscal year 2026</strong> based on current inventory levels," <strong>Hyzer</strong> said.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">For <strong>WD-40</strong>, direct exposure to the conflict is limited to about 3% of the company's global sales for fiscal year 2025, which ended last August, <strong>CEO and President Steven Brass</strong> said. The company's <strong>Middle East</strong> presence includes one manufacturing partner and third-party distributors that also handle sales. Despite added cost pressures, <strong>WD-40</strong> expects to achieve its <strong>FY2026</strong> guidance for net sales growth of 5% to 9% year over year, based in part on crude oil prices ranging between $95 and $115 per barrel. Earlier guidance assumed a $65 to $85-per-barrel range.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Iran war</strong>'s ripple effects have begun to spread across several spokes in supply chains. French fry maker <strong>Lamb Weston</strong> reported it expected international volumes to decline in the second half of the fiscal year, in part due to the evolving <strong>Middle East</strong> conflict. The war has also upended packaging supply chains and raised costs across the <strong>CPG</strong> industry, which is still contending with uncertainty from tariffs imposed by the <strong>Trump</strong> administration. In addition, delivery costs are rising as escalating fuel costs prompt higher surcharges and rates from <strong>FedEx</strong>, <strong>UPS</strong> and other parcel carriers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>

3. Gross margin impact not expected before Q4 FY2026.<br>
4. Crude oil assumption raised from $65-85 to $95-115 per barrel range.<br>
5. Iran war also affects CPG packaging supply chain and delivery costs.</p>
