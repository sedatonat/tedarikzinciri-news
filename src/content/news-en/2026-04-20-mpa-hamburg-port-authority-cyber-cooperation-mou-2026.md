<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Maritime and Port Authority of Singapore (MPA)</strong> has signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with the <strong>Hamburg Port Authority (HPA)</strong> and leading academic institutions from Singapore and Germany to strengthen cooperation on maritime cybersecurity across port operations and digital systems. The agreement also includes the <strong>Singapore Institute of Technology (SIT)</strong>, the <strong>Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD)</strong>, the <strong>University of Hamburg (UHH)</strong> and the <strong>Hamburg University of Technology (TUHH)</strong>, building on a 2024 letter of intent covering digitalisation, decarbonisation and cyber resilience.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under the MoU, the partners will collaborate on developing technologies to enhance cyber resilience across port and maritime environments. This includes cybersecurity systems for ports, <strong>unmanned surface vessels</strong>, remote ship operations, shipboard operational technology, and <strong>ship-shore connectivity</strong>. The agreement was signed by representatives from both port authorities and academic institutions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Port cyber threats have emerged as a systemic risk to global supply chains in recent years, from Maersk's 2017 NotPetya attack to the <strong>2023 ransomware disruption at the Port of Nagoya</strong>. The MPA-HPA partnership aims to develop a standard defence framework against the growing attack surface targeting automated terminal operations, smart port platforms and remotely operated ships. The inclusion of academic institutions facilitates threat intelligence sharing, joint research projects and cyber professional training programmes.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. MPA Singapore and Hamburg Port Authority signed an MoU on maritime cybersecurity.<br>

3. Cooperation covers cybersecurity for unmanned surface vessels and remote ship operations.<br>
4. MoU builds on a 2024 letter of intent covering digitalisation and decarbonisation.<br>
5. The agreement aims to develop a joint defence standard for ship-shore connectivity and port platforms.</p>
