<p>Norwegian shipping company <strong>Bulkship Management AS</strong> has rejected allegations by Dmitry Medvedev, Deputy Chairman of Russia's Security Council, that it refused to assist the crew of the Russian cargo vessel <strong>Ursa Major</strong>, which sank in the Mediterranean.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Medvedev claimed that the Norwegian-flagged vessel <strong>Oslo Carrier 3</strong> did not provide assistance to the crew of <strong>Ursa Major</strong>, a ship serving as the military construction unit of Russia's Defense Ministry. <strong>Ursa Major</strong> experienced problems on Monday between Spain and Algeria, and 14 of its 16 crew members abandoned the vessel in lifeboats. The ship's owner, <strong>Oboronlogistika</strong>, stated that three explosions occurred on the vessel's hull and characterized the incident as an act of \"terrorism.\"</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Spain's Maritime Rescue Service announced that they received a distress signal from <strong>Ursa Major</strong> on Monday and dispatched two ships and a helicopter to the area. Medvedev claimed that <strong>Oslo Carrier 3</strong> refused to render assistance, stating, \"This is unforgivable!\" Kremlin spokesman Dmitry Peskov also remarked that the failure to assist those in distress at sea violates all maritime laws and represents a situation worthy of complete condemnation.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>In response, Oslo-based <strong>Bulkship Management AS</strong> denied the allegations, stating that Spain's Maritime Rescue Coordination Center instructed the captain not to take the crew aboard because a rescue vessel was already on its way. The company emphasized that the lifeboat approached their vessels during this period, weather conditions were favorable, and the crew was not in danger. It also released photographs showing the lifeboat approaching <strong>Oslo Carrier 3</strong> and subsequently being towed by a Spanish vessel.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Medvedev characterized the alleged conduct of the Norwegian vessel as part of anti-Russia actions in Europe, stating that Moscow must respond using all available means, including hybrid methods. Meanwhile, Finnish authorities have detained a Russian vessel suspected of damaging an underwater power cable connecting Finland and Estonia in the Baltic Sea. Robin Lardot, Director of Finland's National Investigation Bureau, stated they are investigating a serious act of sabotage.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li><p><strong>Bulkship Management AS</strong> has denied allegations that <strong>Oslo Carrier 3</strong> refused to assist the <strong>Ursa Major</strong> crew.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Spain's Maritime Rescue Coordination Center instructed the Norwegian vessel not to take the crew aboard because a rescue vessel was already en route.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Ursa Major</strong> sank following three explosions between Spain and Algeria; the incident has been characterized as an act of \"terrorism.\"</p></li>\n  <li><p>Medvedev viewed the incident as part of anti-Russia actions in Europe and said retaliation was necessary.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Finland has detained a Russian vessel suspected of damaging an underwater cable in the Baltic Sea; a serious sabotage investigation is underway.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>---</p>\n<p><strong>News Link: </strong><a href="https://gcaptain.com/five-decades-at-sea-argo-merchant-oil-spill-drift-card-turns-up-on-scottish-isle/"><u>https://gcaptain.com/five-decades-at-sea-argo-merchant-oil-spill-drift-card-turns-up-on-scottish-isle/</u></a></p>\n<p>-------------------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
