<p style="text-align: justify">Maritime transport risks in the Black Sea have escalated dramatically as of December 2025. <strong>Bloomberg</strong>'s latest analysis reveals that <strong>war-risk insurance</strong> prices in the region have surged sharply following Ukraine's attacks on Moscow-linked vessels. According to data from <strong>Marsh</strong>, the world's largest insurance broker, war insurance rates for ships calling at Russian ports have climbed from the previous <strong>0.25–0.30%</strong> band to <strong>1%</strong> — representing a <strong>surge of more than 250%</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Ukraine announced last week that it had attacked two vessels belonging to Russia's <strong>shadow fleet</strong>, the clandestine tanker fleet used to circumvent sanctions. Two additional attacks on Moscow-linked vessels followed. These incidents further complicate an already fragile security environment in the Black Sea.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In response to these developments, insurers are repricing the region. <strong>Munro Anderson</strong>, Operations Chief at <strong>Vessel Protect (Pen Underwriting)</strong>, notes that ships calling at Russian ports now face evaluation of a "<strong>broader range of possible strike locations</strong>," with higher probability of repeated attacks. The concentration of attacks has also raised the likelihood of Russian retaliation. Indeed, Russian President <strong>Vladimir Putin</strong> elevated risk perception on December 2 by stating that Russia could "strike back."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The threat in the Black Sea is not limited to Ukraine and Russia-linked vessels. According to Romania's Defence Ministry, on December 3, naval commando units destroyed a <strong>Sea Baby drone</strong> <strong>36 nautical miles east of Constanta</strong>. This incident demonstrates that commercial vessels from outside the region are also at risk from drone attacks. A <strong>multi-actor threat landscape</strong> is emerging for all Black Sea nations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Dylan Mortimer</strong>, Marine Hull U.K. War Leader at Marsh, points out that premium increases are directly linked to rising attack numbers, with targets now extending beyond ships to include <strong>port and terminal infrastructure</strong>. Attacks on terminal infrastructure have the potential to disrupt not only vessel security but all logistics flows in the region.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">These developments become even more critical as <strong>shadow fleet</strong> activities amplify risk levels in the region. Shadow tankers consist of poorly maintained, uninsured, or underinsured vessels. Attacks on these ships — or technical failures — create significant environmental pollution risk and the possibility of <strong>shipping lane blockage</strong> in the Black Sea. This automatically raises the risk category for compliant commercial vessels operating in the area.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, premium increases in the Black Sea produce three primary consequences:</p>
<ol>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Cost Escalation:</strong> All shipments through the region (grain, oil, chemicals) face higher insurance costs.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Route Changes:</strong> Some carriers are shifting to longer routes or alternative transshipment points to bypass the Black Sea leg.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Policy Restrictions:</strong> Insurers now offer narrower coverage policies and may refuse insurance entirely for certain vessels.</p></li>
</ol>
<p style="text-align: justify">In sum, the Black Sea has become one of the world's highest-risk maritime regions in the final weeks of 2025. As the maritime dimension of the Ukraine-Russia conflict expands, the insurance market reflects this risk, rendering the region an expensive, unpredictable, and security-intensive logistics area.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Black Sea war-risk insurance premiums have surged more than 250% to reach 1%.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Ukraine carried out two attacks on Russia's <strong>shadow fleet</strong> tankers; two additional incidents occurred.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Russia signaled retaliation; risk perception elevated.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Romania destroyed a <strong>Sea Baby drone</strong> off Constanta.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Insurers now categorize both vessels and terminal infrastructure within the same risk framework.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">These premium increases directly impact supply chain costs and route planning.</p></li>
</ul>
