<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Federal Maritime Commission (FMC)</strong> Chairman <strong>Louis E. Sola</strong> and Commissioner <strong>Daniel B. Maffei</strong> made significant statements before the <strong>U.S. Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation</strong> on <strong>January 28, 2025</strong>, regarding the operation, control, and usage costs of the <strong>Panama Canal</strong>.&nbsp;</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Sola emphasized that the <strong>Panama Canal</strong> has been a vital waterway for maritime operations for over a century, underscoring its importance to the United States. More than 40% of U.S. container traffic, valued at approximately <strong>270 billion dollars</strong> annually, passes through this waterway. Various types of vessels—including container ships, passenger ships, bulk carriers, general cargo ships, and product tankers—use this waterway to transport cargo to and from the United States. ​</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The reduction in capacity at the <strong>Panama Canal</strong>, coupled with the effective closure of the <strong>Suez Canal</strong> due to <strong>Houthi</strong> attacks in the <strong>Red Sea</strong>, has had significant impacts on maritime trade. This situation has resulted in increased freight rates, tariffs, and/or transit delays. If no solution is found, projections indicate that by 2050 the canal's capacity could decline by 50%, which would have serious implications for global trade and the U.S. economy. ​</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Commissioner <strong>Daniel B. Maffei</strong> disclosed that in <strong>July 2024</strong>, he and other commission members held a meeting with newly elected <strong>Panama President Jose Mulino</strong> and senior officials in his administration regarding maritime policy. Maffei stated that as the commission gains more information on how Panama and the <strong>Panama Canal Authority</strong> will address another drought crisis, and receives further feedback from U.S. importers and exporters, the commission is prepared to take appropriate measures when necessary. ​</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Louis E. Sola</strong> emphasized that more than one-third of the U.S. economy is connected to goods, commodities, and inputs transported by sea, and that the <strong>Panama Canal</strong> is an indispensable part of these maritime-dependent supply chains. Sola stated, "Preserving the viability of the <strong>Panama Canal</strong> for the coming century and beyond is a priority if we want to maintain our economic competitiveness." ​</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In <strong>November 2024</strong>, <strong>Bloomberg</strong> reported that then-president-elect <strong>Donald Trump</strong>'s policy mix, which includes rising tariffs, could reduce demand for container transportation services in 2025. This situation could negatively affect trade volumes passing through the <strong>Panama Canal</strong> and consequently the U.S. economy. ​</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Panama Canal</strong> is a vital waterway for U.S. maritime trade, with more than 40% of U.S. container traffic passing through this canal.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Reductions in the canal's capacity can have serious impacts on global trade and the U.S. economy.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>FMC</strong> maintains close contact with <strong>Panama</strong> on maritime policies and is prepared to take appropriate measures when necessary.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>U.S. economy</strong> is heavily dependent on goods transported by sea, and the <strong>Panama Canal</strong> is a critical component of this supply chain.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Donald Trump</strong>'s plans to increase tariffs could negatively affect demand for container shipping services and, consequently, trade passing through the <strong>Panama Canal</strong>.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>---</p>\n<p><strong>News Link: </strong><a href="https://www.porttechnology.org/news/fmc-highlights-panama-canals-importance-to-us-economy/"><u>https://www.porttechnology.org/news/fmc-highlights-panama-canals-importance-to-us-economy/</u></a></p>\n<p>-------------------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
