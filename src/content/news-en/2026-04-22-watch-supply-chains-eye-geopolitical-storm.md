<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> reports; <strong>Robert J. Bowman</strong> (<strong>Editor-in-Chief</strong>); how should supply chains navigate the challenges posed by mounting geopolitical tensions? <strong>Nick Banich</strong>, Partner at <strong>Miebach Consulting</strong> and Chief Revenue Officer for <strong>USA</strong> and <strong>Canada</strong>, offers guidance. As the <strong>U.S.</strong> emerges from the Cold War era when the nation served as "the world's sole central pole of power," economic supremacy is ending, <strong>Banich</strong> contends. "The world is becoming a blue marble again—and companies need to start coming to terms with this," he says. Tariffs are here to stay in some form, and companies must begin rethinking their supply chains accordingly. Volatility, <strong>Banich</strong> argues, is the "new norm"—and companies responding by redesigning networks and mapping upstream risks are "the ones less frozen compared to others." The lesson for today: Businesses must "be ready to pivot quickly—and they need resilience and redundancy in their supply chains," <strong>Banich</strong> states. "The next four years will resemble the last four years much more than the previous 40 years." Decisions about where to source products must be made in a way that minimizes risk. <strong>Banich</strong> suggests that companies must engage in "what-if" scenario planning with the understanding—as corporate financial statements frequently state—that "past performance is not indicative of future results."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Miebach Consulting</strong>, founded in <strong>Frankfurt, Germany</strong> by <strong>Joachim Miebach</strong> (1973) with <strong>Klaus Geier</strong> as CEO, is a global supply chain and logistics strategy consulting firm with 25+ offices and 350+ consultants serving major clients including <strong>Henkel</strong>, <strong>BASF</strong>, <strong>Bayer</strong>, <strong>Coca-Cola</strong>, <strong>Pepsi</strong>, <strong>Nestlé</strong>, <strong>Unilever</strong>, and <strong>Procter &amp; Gamble</strong>. <strong>Nick Banich</strong> serves as the regional leader for <strong>USA</strong> and <strong>Canada</strong> at <strong>Miebach</strong> and brings prior experience from <strong>Honeywell Intelligrated</strong> and <strong>Dematic</strong>. Other major supply chain consulting firms include <strong>McKinsey &amp; Company</strong> (<strong>Bob Sternfels</strong> Global Managing Partner), <strong>Bain &amp; Company</strong>, <strong>Boston Consulting Group</strong> (<strong>BCG</strong>), <strong>Accenture</strong> (<strong>Julie Sweet</strong> CEO), <strong>Deloitte</strong>, <strong>EY</strong>, <strong>KPMG</strong>, <strong>PwC</strong>, <strong>Kearney</strong>, <strong>Oliver Wyman</strong>, <strong>Capgemini Invent</strong>, <strong>Roland Berger</strong>, <strong>Strategy&amp;</strong>, <strong>L.E.K. Consulting</strong>, <strong>Maine Pointe</strong>, <strong>Establish Inc.</strong>, <strong>St. Onge Company</strong>, <strong>enVista</strong>, <strong>Crimson &amp; Co.</strong>, and <strong>Argon &amp; Co.</strong>, which are key ecosystem players.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, resilience and redundancy strategies include: (1) multi-sourcing and supplier diversification; (2) nearshoring and friendshoring; (3) safety stock and strategic inventory; (4) dual sourcing and backup suppliers; (5) supply chain mapping and multi-tier visibility; (6) scenario planning and stress testing; (7) flexible manufacturing and postponement; (8) logistics flexibility and multimodal options; (9) regional supply hubs; and (10) circular economy and reverse logistics, which are key strategic tools. Major professional organizations include the <strong>Council of Supply Chain Management Professionals</strong> (<strong>CSCMP</strong>; <strong>Mark Baxa</strong> CEO), the <strong>Association for Supply Chain Management</strong> (<strong>ASCM</strong>; <strong>Abe Eshkenazi</strong> CEO), and the <strong>Supply Chain Council</strong>. Leading risk assessment providers include the <strong>Geopolitical Risk Index</strong> (<strong>GPR</strong>; <strong>Dario Caldara</strong> and <strong>Matteo Iacoviello</strong> at the <strong>Federal Reserve</strong>), the <strong>Economic Policy Uncertainty Index</strong> (<strong>EPU</strong>), <strong>Eurasia Group</strong> (<strong>Ian Bremmer</strong> Founder), <strong>Stratfor</strong>, and <strong>RAND Corporation</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, AI and machine learning-based scenario planning tools are provided by <strong>o9 Solutions</strong>, <strong>Kinaxis Maestro</strong>, <strong>Anaplan</strong>, <strong>Blue Yonder</strong>, <strong>SAP IBP</strong>, <strong>Oracle SCP</strong>, <strong>Aera Technology</strong>, and <strong>Coupa Supply Chain Planning</strong>—and as <strong>Banich</strong> notes, wholesale replacement is not necessary; modular capabilities added to existing <strong>ERP</strong> and <strong>APS</strong> systems are also utilized. Key platforms for digital twin and simulation include <strong>AnyLogistix</strong>, <strong>Llamasoft</strong> (owned by <strong>Coupa</strong>), <strong>Logility</strong>, <strong>JDA</strong>, and <strong>Optilogic</strong>. Leading providers for stress testing and red teaming include <strong>Resilinc EventWatchAI</strong>, <strong>Everstream Discover</strong>, <strong>Interos i-Score</strong>, and <strong>S&amp;P Global Panjiva</strong>. In conclusion, <strong>Banich</strong>'s advice on geopolitical storms and flexibility suggests that the global supply chain design paradigm is being redesigned from efficiency-focused to resilience-focused—with what-if scenario planning and multi-tier visibility emerging as core strategic priorities for supply chain leaders.</p>
<p><br></p>

1. <strong>Nick Banich</strong> (<strong>Miebach Consulting</strong>) characterizes volatility as the "new norm."<br>
2. Tariffs are here to stay—companies must redesign their supply chains.<br>
3. The next four years will resemble the last four years much more than the previous 40.<br>
4. What-if scenario planning is a primary decision-making tool.<br>
5. AI/ML is added without wholesale replacement—it is a modular capability.</p>
