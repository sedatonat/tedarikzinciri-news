<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Russian Railways</strong> has reported sending the <strong>first long block train consisting of 142 standard railcar units</strong> from <strong>Tobolsk in the Tyumen region</strong>. After arrival at <strong>Vladivostok</strong> in the Russian Far East, the containers will be loaded on board a ship to be <strong>carried to China</strong>. This trip sets a record for long block train capacity in Russian rail history.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The service deploying long block trains was launched in 2024 to serve a major petrochemical holding company. The service delivers <strong>polypropylene</strong> from Tyumen to the Russian Far East. The first <strong>100-railcar block train left Tobolsk on September 16, 2024</strong>. In 2025, the train length was increased to <strong>125 standard railcars</strong>, with service frequency reaching <strong>13-15 trains a month</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">In 2026, 17 125-railcar long block trains have departed under the new expansion. The current 142-railcar train has crossed a new threshold in operational efficiency. The long block train model provides advantages in fuel economy, locomotive efficiency, and cost per freight unit. The Tyumen-Vladivostok route covers over 5,500 km via the Trans-Siberian mainline. Polypropylene exported to China is used as a raw material in plastic manufacturing — a signal of the Russian petrochemical sector pivoting to China as an alternative to Western markets.</p>
