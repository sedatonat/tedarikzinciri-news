<p style="text-align: justify">French container shipping and logistics group <strong>CMA CGM</strong> has reached another significant milestone in its low-carbon fleet transformation. On <strong>December 11</strong>, the company formally named and took delivery of the <strong>methanol dual-fuel container ship CMA CGM ANTIGONE</strong> at a ceremony held at <strong>Jiangnan Shipyard</strong> in Shanghai. The vessel marks <strong>CMA CGM's first methanol dual-fuel container ship delivered from a Chinese shipyard</strong> and represents the <strong>eighteenth vessel</strong> produced by the yard for the group.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Representatives from <strong>CMA CGM</strong>, <strong>China Shipbuilding Trading</strong>, <strong>Jiangnan Shipyard</strong>, financial institutions, and classification society <strong>Bureau Veritas (BV)</strong> attended the ceremony. <strong>Harriet Wu</strong>, senior advisor at <strong>CMA CGM China</strong>, served as the ship's godmother. According to company statements, the vessel is under the command of <strong>Captain Zhang Zequan</strong> and will enter service on the <strong>Asia–Middle East–Mediterranean</strong> route under the <strong>Phoenician Express (BEX2)</strong> service.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Kun Series and Jiangnan's Technical Expertise</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CMA CGM ANTIGONE</strong> is designated as the <strong>lead ship</strong> of Jiangnan Shipyard's newly developed <strong>"Kun" series</strong> of methanol dual-fuel ultra-large container vessels. According to Chinese maritime industry sources, this series was developed as <strong>an entirely proprietary yard design</strong>—an ultra-large, highly efficient platform compatible with next-generation fuel systems.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Jiangnan Shipyard</strong> is <strong>the only shipyard in China</strong> capable of simultaneously designing and building ultra-large container ships with <strong>three different fuel options</strong> (conventional, LNG, and methanol). This capability strengthens the technical foundation of CMA CGM's long-term strategic partnership with Chinese shipyards.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Novel Construction Processes Applied</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">For the ANTIGONE project, Jiangnan implemented numerous new construction and testing procedures for the first time, including:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Structural strength tests for methanol tanks in floating dock conditions</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Transfer of a ring section of the methanol tank between yard and vessel</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Pre-lift integrity acceptance of accommodation and deckhouse units</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Elevator systems activation prior to delivery</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Full ship simulation container stowing and lashing tests</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Engine-room power-generation motor activation before floating out</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The yard's <strong>first safe methanol bunkering operation</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">The vessel's time in the floating dock was approximately <strong>three and a half months</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Technical Specifications and Safety</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CMA CGM ANTIGONE</strong> features</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>a length of 366 metres</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>a beam of 51 metres</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>a summer deadweight capacity of approximately 156,100 tonnes</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">The vessel operates under <strong>Bureau Veritas</strong> classification.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The methanol fuel system was developed under an approved <strong>alternative design</strong> incorporating <strong>rupture discs</strong> and <strong>spectacle flanges</strong> upstream of pressure-relief valves. This configuration enhances <strong>operational safety</strong> when handling volatile fuels such as methanol.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>CMA CGM's Methanol and LNG Strategy</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">ANTIGONE joins CMA CGM's rapidly expanding <strong>methanol-capable fleet</strong>. The group operates a <strong>12-ship methanol program</strong>, including the <strong>13,000 TEU CMA CGM IRON</strong> and sister vessels delivered from South Korean yards in 2025.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">CMA CGM has announced investments of <strong>approximately $20 billion USD</strong> in <strong>LNG and methanol-fuelled vessels</strong>. The company aims to have <strong>more than 150 low-carbon fuel-capable ships</strong> in its fleet <strong>by 2029</strong>. This approach aligns with the group's <strong>IMO decarbonization targets</strong>, regulatory compliance, and fuel flexibility strategy.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CMA CGM Vice President Xavier Leclercq</strong> emphasized that the vessel's construction and delivery resulted from <strong>close collaboration between Jiangnan Shipyard, China Shipbuilding Trading, BV, and financial institutions</strong>. <strong>Hu Hongyu</strong>, General Manager Deputy of Jiangnan Shipyard, highlighted that <strong>18 vessels have been delivered to CMA CGM since 2013</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Overall Assessment</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The commissioning of CMA CGM ANTIGONE reaffirms that methanol has become <strong>one of the main alternative fuels</strong> in global container shipping. This investment, combined with Chinese shipyards' technical capabilities, represents a critical step for CMA CGM in both fleet modernization and <strong>multiple fuel strategy</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CMA CGM ANTIGONE</strong> is CMA CGM's <strong>first methanol dual-fuel</strong> vessel delivered from China.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Shipyard: <strong>Jiangnan Shipyard</strong>, Series: <strong>Kun</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Dimensions: <strong>366 m length</strong>, <strong>51 m beam</strong>, <strong>156,100 DWT</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The vessel will serve on the <strong>BEX2 (Phoenician Express)</strong> route.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Jiangnan is the <strong>only shipyard in China</strong> capable of building ULCV with three different fuel types.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">CMA CGM is targeting <strong>150+ low-carbon vessels by 2029</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
