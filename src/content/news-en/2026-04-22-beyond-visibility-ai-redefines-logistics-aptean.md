<p style="text-align: justify">For years, logistics leaders have made significant investments in visibility — dashboards, alerts, and reports showing what is happening. Yet despite all this data, many organizations still face the same challenges: disconnected systems, slow decision-making, rising costs, and continuous pressure to do more with less. Visibility alone has not delivered the agility, flexibility, or performance that today's supply chains demand — and leaders know it. From a supply chain perspective, the evolution from <strong>visibility</strong> to <strong>orchestration</strong> is highlighted in <strong>Gartner</strong>'s <strong>Supply Chain Top 5 Strategic Trends 2026</strong> report. The <strong>real-time visibility platform</strong> category (<strong>FourKites</strong>, <strong>project44</strong>, <strong>Tive</strong>, <strong>Shippeo</strong>, <strong>Overhaul</strong>, <strong>Wakeo</strong>, <strong>Roambee</strong>) is expanding toward the <strong>execution layer</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>GenAI</strong> is changing this. With the introduction of intelligent agents and <strong>agentic workflow</strong>s, logistics is moving from passive insights to proactive orchestration. These agents do not just analyze — they take action. They continuously monitor, predict, and recommend. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Aptean</strong> was formed in 2012 through the merger of <strong>CDC Software</strong> with <strong>Consona</strong> and is backed by <strong>Vista Equity Partners</strong>, <strong>TA Associates</strong>, and <strong>Charlesbank Capital Partners</strong>. It offers a modular <strong>ERP</strong>/<strong>SCM</strong> portfolio across <strong>discrete manufacturing</strong>, <strong>process manufacturing</strong>, <strong>distribution</strong>, <strong>retail</strong>, <strong>fashion</strong>, <strong>food &amp; beverage</strong>, <strong>compliance</strong>, <strong>routing &amp; scheduling</strong>, <strong>EAM</strong> (<strong>Enterprise Asset Management</strong>), and <strong>TMS</strong> categories. <strong>Paragon Routing</strong> and <strong>Routeware</strong> acquisitions support <strong>Aptean</strong>'s logistics expansion.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the speakers are <strong>Jim Endres</strong> (<strong>Director of TMS</strong>) and <strong>Chris Peel</strong> (<strong>Director of TMS Product Management</strong>) from <strong>Aptean</strong>, and moderator <strong>Robert Bowman</strong>, <strong>Editor-in-Chief</strong> of <strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong>. Yonder TMS</strong>, <strong>Descartes</strong>, <strong>MercuryGate</strong>, <strong>Trimble TMS</strong>, <strong>3Gtms</strong>, <strong>Aptean</strong>, <strong>Alpega</strong>, <strong>BluJay</strong> (<strong>E2open</strong>), <strong>Coyote</strong> (<strong>RXO</strong>), and <strong>Kuebix</strong> (<strong>Trimble</strong>). <strong>load building</strong>, <strong>mode optimization</strong>, <strong>routing</strong>, <strong>tendering</strong>, <strong>tracking</strong>, <strong>freight audit</strong>, <strong>settlement</strong>, <strong>parcel rating</strong>, and <strong>multi-stop optimization</strong> are core modules of modern <strong>TMS</strong> systems.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, practical
