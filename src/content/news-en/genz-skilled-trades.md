<p><br>
Gen Z's move into skilled trades is creating significant opportunities for the logistics and manufacturing sectors. Apprenticeships, micro-credentials, and on-the-job learning models are gaining prominence in educational programs.&nbsp;</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Companies are offering scholarships, internships, and clear career pathway guidance to attract qualified labor in areas such as warehouse automation and maintenance.&nbsp;</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Partnerships with community colleges and industry associations are expanding the talent pool. The trend has the potential to close capacity gaps amid an aging workforce and mounting digitalization pressures.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. Apprenticeships and short-term certifications take center stage.<br>
2. Companies attract talent through scholarships and internships.<br>
3. Community college partnerships increase.<br>
4. Digitalization drives demand for maintenance and automation roles.<br>
5. The aging workforce gap can be bridged through this pathway.</p>
