<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SCB Contributor</strong> <strong>Nicole Rycroft</strong> notes that progress on the EUDR (<strong>EU Deforestation Regulation</strong>) represents an important political signal of intent after months of uncertainty. However, pushing back the law's implementation by an additional year to December 2026 and exempting key commodities such as imported printed products risks weakening what needs to be a flagship regulation for forests, climate, and reduced supply chain risk. Exempting specific products like books and newspapers sets a concerning precedent for potential sector-by-sector carve-outs—continuing business uncertainty at a time when the <strong>EU</strong> needs consistent, economy-wide rules to keep forests standing and build supply chain resilience. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>EUDR</strong>, formalized as <strong>Regulation (EU) 2023/1115</strong>, mandates proof of deforestation-free status for entry into the <strong>EU</strong> market for beef, cocoa, coffee, palm oil, rubber, soya, and wood (and their derivative products).</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Full traceability and deforestation-free supply chains must become the norm—many leading companies have already invested to meet and exceed these standards. What business and investment need now is clear rules and a level playing field. Brands must seize the opportunity to restructure their supply chains for transparency and deforestation-free sourcing. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>EUDR</strong>'s impact on the fashion industry is particularly evident in the viscose, rayon, modal, lyocell, and cellulosic-based synthetic fibers supply chain. <strong>Lenzing</strong> (<strong>Austria</strong>), <strong>Aditya Birla Group</strong> (<strong>India</strong>, <strong>Birla Cellulose</strong>), <strong>Sateri</strong> (<strong>RGE</strong> group), <strong>Asia Pacific Rayon</strong>, <strong>Tangshan Sanyou</strong>, and <strong>Yibin Grace</strong> are leading producers in the global <strong>MMCF</strong> (<strong>Man-Made Cellulosic Fibres</strong>) market. <strong>FSC</strong> (<strong>Forest Stewardship Council</strong>) and <strong>PEFC</strong> (<strong>Programme for the Endorsement of Forest Certification</strong>) serve as certification frameworks for forest products.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Nicole Rycroft</strong> is founder and <strong>executive director</strong> of <strong>Canopy</strong>—an environmental organization based in <strong>Vancouver, Canada</strong>—focused on protecting the supply chains of the fashion, packaging, and publishing industries from old-growth and endangered forests. The <strong>Hot Button Report</strong> is <strong>Canopy</strong>'s annual <strong>MMCF</strong> producer scorecard. Key operational requirements for <strong>EUDR</strong> compliance include <strong>geolocation</strong> (plot-level GPS coordinates), <strong>traceability</strong>, <strong>due diligence statement</strong> (<strong>DDS</strong>), and <strong>EU Information System</strong> (<strong>TRACES NT</strong>) integration. <strong>Satellite monitoring</strong>, <strong>blockchain</strong>, <strong>DNA testing</strong>, and <strong>isotope analysis</strong> are among the leading supply chain verification technologies. <strong>Global Forest Watch</strong> (<strong>WRI</strong>), <strong>Trase</strong>, <strong>MapHubs</strong>, <strong>Satelligence</strong>, <strong>Descartes Labs</strong>, and <strong>Planet Labs</strong> are leading satellite-based forest monitoring platforms. <strong>RSPO</strong> (<strong>Roundtable on Sustainable Palm Oil</strong>), <strong>RTRS</strong> (<strong>Round Table on Responsible Soy</strong>), <strong>Bonsucro</strong> (sugar), <strong>Rainforest Alliance</strong>, and <strong>UTZ</strong> are commodity-specific sustainability standards.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the <strong>EUDR</strong> compliance deadline was originally December 30, 2024—then delayed by one year (December 30, 2025) by the <strong>EU Commission</strong> and <strong>Council</strong>, and now further postponed to December 30, 2026. For <strong>SMEs</strong> (<strong>Small and Medium-sized Enterprises</strong>), the deadline is June 30, 2026. <strong>EUDR</strong> supersedes and expands on <strong>EUTR</strong> (<strong>EU Timber Regulation</strong>, 2013) and <strong>FLEGT</strong> (<strong>Forest Law Enforcement Governance and Trade</strong>) frameworks. <strong>Brazil</strong>, <strong>Indonesia</strong>, <strong>Malaysia</strong>, <strong>Vietnam</strong>, <strong>Cambodia</strong>, <strong>Côte d'Ivoire</strong>, <strong>Ghana</strong>, <strong>Cameroon</strong>, <strong>Peru</strong>, and <strong>Colombia</strong> are among the countries most affected by <strong>EUDR</strong> implementation. The <strong>fashion industry</strong> brands <strong>H&M</strong>, <strong>Inditex</strong> (<strong>Zara</strong>), <strong>Shein</strong>, <strong>Uniqlo</strong> (<strong>Fast Retailing</strong>), <strong>Nike</strong>, <strong>Adidas</strong>, <strong>VF Corporation</strong>, <strong>PVH</strong>, <strong>Levi Strauss</strong>, <strong>Patagonia</strong>, <strong>Kering</strong>, <strong>LVMH</strong>, and <strong>Burberry</strong> must restructure their supply chains under <strong>EUDR</strong>. The <strong>EU Strategy for Sustainable and Circular Textiles</strong>, <strong>ESPR</strong> (<strong>Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation</strong>), and <strong>DPP</strong> (<strong>Digital Product Passport</strong>) are complementary textile-specific regulations. In conclusion, <strong>Rycroft</strong>'s call for <strong>EUDR</strong> compliance clearly demonstrates fashion's opportunity to lead in forest risk management.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. <strong>EUDR</strong> compliance deadline pushed back one additional year to December 2026.<br>
2. Printed product exemptions like books and newspapers create a concerning sector carve-out precedent.<br>
3. <strong>Nicole Rycroft</strong>, <strong>Canopy</strong> founder, writes as an <strong>SCB Contributor</strong>.<br>
4. Full traceability and deforestation-free supply chains must become the norm.<br>
5. Brands must seize the opportunity for transparency and responsible sourcing.</p>
