<p style="text-align: justify">Date: April 30, 2026; Time: 11:00 a.m. ET; Live Webinar: 1 hour. What if your routes could learn from every single delivery? In this <strong>AI Exchange</strong> session, we examine how moving beyond static service time assumptions unlocks a new level of fleet performance. Traditional routing treats every stop as predictable, but in reality, each one is shaped by order size, product mix, field conditions, dwell requirements, and team readiness. <strong>AI</strong> and machine learning transform the model by learning from actual delivery behavior and continuously applying that intelligence to future routes. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>last-mile delivery</strong> accounts for 40–53% of total logistics costs and, with e-commerce growth, has become the most expensive segment of the supply chain. <strong>Descartes Systems Group</strong> competes against <strong>Manhattan Associates</strong>, <strong>Blue Yonder</strong>, <strong>Oracle</strong>, and <strong>SAP</strong> in <strong>route planning</strong> and <strong>real-time visibility</strong>, offering the <strong>Descartes Routing &amp; Mobile</strong>, <strong>Descartes MacroPoint</strong>, and <strong>Descartes ShipRush</strong> product suite.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">At the same time, performance planning does not stop there. Real-time communication, accurate <strong>ETA</strong>s, proactive updates, and shared visibility keep shipments, drivers, and customers in sync, helping teams stay coordinated and reducing unnecessary follow-up calls. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>OTIF</strong> (<strong>On-Time In-Full</strong>) is the primary performance metric that retailers enforce on suppliers, and a threshold of 95%+ is the industry standard. <strong>Walmart</strong>, <strong>Target</strong>, <strong>Costco</strong>, <strong>Kroger</strong>, and <strong>Home Depot</strong> impose 1–3% penalties for <strong>OTIF</strong> violations in their <strong>vendor agreement</strong>s. <strong>Real-time ETA</strong> is calculated by combining <strong>traffic data</strong>, <strong>weather</strong>, <strong>service time history</strong>, <strong>driver experience</strong>, and <strong>vehicle telemetry</strong> data.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The speakers are <strong>Cyndi Brandt</strong>, Vice President of Fleet Solutions at <strong>Descartes</strong>, and <strong>Sergio Torres</strong>, Senior Vice President of Product Management at <strong>Descartes</strong>. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>VRP</strong> (<strong>Vehicle Routing Problem</strong>) and <strong>VRPTW</strong> (<strong>Vehicle Routing Problem with Time Windows</strong>) algorithms are transitioning from classical <strong>OR</strong> (<strong>Operations Research</strong>) techniques to machine learning–based approaches. <strong>Reinforcement learning</strong> is being used to learn from driver behavior, traffic patterns, and <strong>service time</strong> deviations. <strong>NVIDIA cuOpt</strong>, a <strong>GPU</strong>-accelerated <strong>VRP</strong> solver, can deliver results 100x faster than traditional <strong>CPU</strong>-based solvers. <strong>Descartes Routing</strong> serves major customers such as <strong>Cisco</strong>, <strong>Sysco</strong>, <strong>Pepsi</strong>, <strong>Coca-Cola</strong>, <strong>Republic Services</strong>, and <strong>Waste Management</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, how <strong>last-mile</strong> visibility translates into customer engagement directly affects <strong>NPS</strong> (<strong>Net Promoter Score</strong>) and <strong>CSAT</strong> (<strong>Customer Satisfaction</strong>) scores. <strong>Delivery window narrowing</strong> through <strong>SMS</strong>, <strong>email</strong>, <strong>push notification</strong>, and <strong>in-app messaging</strong> channels reduces customer complaints stemming from delivery time violations. <strong>FedEx</strong>, <strong>UPS</strong>, <strong>DHL</strong>, and <strong>Amazon Logistics</strong> are the players raising the standards of the <strong>last-mile</strong> experience. Research from <strong>Boston Consulting Group</strong> (<strong>BCG</strong>) and <strong>McKinsey</strong> shows that more than 60% of consumers prioritize <strong>delivery experience</strong> in their purchase decisions. Ultimately, <strong>Descartes</strong>' <strong>AI Exchange</strong> webinar clearly outlines the <strong>AI</strong>-powered new standards of <strong>last-mile</strong> routing and customer engagement.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>\n1. <strong>Descartes</strong> webinar will be held on April 30, 2026.<br>\n2.
