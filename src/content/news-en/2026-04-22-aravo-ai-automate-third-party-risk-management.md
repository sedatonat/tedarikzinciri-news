<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Aravo</strong>, a provider of third-party risk and resilience technology, has introduced <strong>Aravo AI</strong>, an artificial intelligence capability embedded within its <strong>Intelligence First Platform</strong>. <strong>Aravo AI</strong> features artificial intelligence agents built directly into third-party risk assessments and management, enabling automation of manual processes, real-time data access, and enhanced transparency and auditability. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>third-party risk management (TPRM)</strong> encompasses a broad range of processes including <strong>vendor management</strong>, <strong>supplier qualification</strong>, <strong>tier-N visibility</strong>, and <strong>ESG due diligence</strong>. The <strong>NIST 800-161</strong>, <strong>ISO 27036</strong>, and <strong>EU CSDDD</strong> (<strong>Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive</strong>) frameworks form the regulatory foundation for these processes.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In a statement dated April 21, the company said: "<strong>Third-party risk management (TPRM) is one of the clearest use cases for AI. The work is data-heavy, time-sensitive, and tied directly to business outcomes</strong>." <strong>Aravo</strong> notes that teams can now use <strong>Aravo AI</strong> to run more continuous processes that adapt as new risks and requirements emerge, rather than relying on point-in-time reviews and manual coordination. The core idea is to make assessments more consistent and improve the quality of data underlying decisions. From a supply chain perspective, the concept of <strong>continuous monitoring</strong> is replacing traditional <strong>vendor risk</strong> programs based on <strong>annual review</strong> or <strong>quarterly assessment</strong> cycles. Data from <strong>vendor risk feed</strong> providers such as <strong>Bitsight</strong>, <strong>SecurityScorecard</strong>, <strong>RapidRatings</strong>, and <strong>Black Kite</strong> is processed in real-time on platforms like <strong>Aravo AI</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Teams can also review, challenge, and override AI outputs at every point. This is enabled by transparent visibility into the data and sources behind each recommendation. As a result, manual work is reduced and teams can focus on judgment and decision-making. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>human-in-the-loop</strong> architecture is critical for compliance with <strong>NIST AI RMF</strong> and the <strong>EU AI Act</strong>. <strong>Audit trail</strong>, <strong>model card</strong>, and <strong>data lineage</strong> are mandatory components for independent <strong>SOC 2 Type II</strong> audits. Automatic analysis by AI of standard questionnaires such as <strong>SIG</strong> (<strong>Standardized Information Gathering</strong>) and <strong>CAIQ</strong> (<strong>Consensus Assessments Initiative Questionnaire</strong>) in the <strong>vendor onboarding</strong> process can reduce supplier approval time from days to hours.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Aravo</strong>'s move is a typical example of the <strong>agentic AI</strong> wave in the <strong>GRC</strong> (<strong>Governance, Risk and Compliance</strong>) software market. Competitors such as <strong>OneTrust</strong>, <strong>ServiceNow GRC</strong>, <strong>Archer</strong>, <strong>MetricStream</strong>, and <strong>Diligent</strong> are integrating similar capabilities into their platforms. Highly regulated sectors such as <strong>banking</strong>, <strong>insurance</strong>, <strong>pharma</strong>, and <strong>defense</strong> can better manage complex issues like <strong>vendor concentration risk</strong>, <strong>fourth-party risk</strong>, and <strong>concentration of subprocessors</strong> through AI-powered continuous monitoring. Following supply chain attacks like <strong>SolarWinds</strong> and <strong>MOVEit</strong>, new standards such as <strong>SBOM</strong> (<strong>Software Bill of Materials</strong>) and <strong>VEX</strong> (<strong>Vulnerability Exploitability eXchange</strong>) are being added to the <strong>TPRM</strong> scope. Ultimately, the introduction of <strong>Aravo AI</strong> is a new sign that artificial intelligence in risk management is moving from the experimental phase to the operational phase.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Points:</strong><br>
1. <strong>Aravo AI</strong> delivers artificial intelligence agents embedded within the <strong>Intelligence First Platform</strong>.<br>
2. AI enables manual process automation, real-time data access, and auditability.<br>
3. <strong>TPRM</strong> stands out as a use case for AI because it is data-intensive and time-critical.<br>
4. Continuous processes are replacing point-in-time reviews.<br>
5. Teams can review and override AI outputs at every stage.</p>
