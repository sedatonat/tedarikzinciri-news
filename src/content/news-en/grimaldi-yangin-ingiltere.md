<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Grande Roma</strong>, a car carrier operated by <strong>Grimaldi Euromed</strong>, lost <strong>propulsion power</strong> following an <strong>engine room fire that broke out at the exit of the English Channel on the night of 25 October 2025</strong>. The incident occurred around <strong>22:00 local time</strong>. According to a statement from the company, the fire started in the <strong>engine room of the vessel</strong>, which was <strong>built in 2003, flying the Italian flag, with a capacity of 5,379 vehicle units (CEU)</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Upon detection of the fire, the crew promptly <strong>activated the carbon dioxide (CO₂) suppression system</strong> and initiated <strong>bulkhead cooling</strong> operations. The captain confirmed that <strong>no crew members were injured</strong> and that <strong>there was no spillage or pollution at sea</strong>. However, as the vessel's <strong>propulsion system was completely disabled</strong>, Grimaldi activated its <strong>emergency response team</strong> and requested the <strong>chartering of an appropriate tugboat</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Following the incident, the company deployed <strong>Abeille Liberté</strong>, a high-capacity tugboat from the <strong>Les Abeilles</strong> fleet. The tugboat reached the vessel at <strong>09:36 on the morning of 26 October</strong> and <strong>successfully commenced the towing operation</strong>. The vessel was <strong>scheduled to arrive at Le Havre Port in France on 27 October</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the <strong>latest AIS (Automatic Identification System)</strong> data, <strong>Grande Roma</strong> is in "<strong>not under command</strong>" status and proceeding in the <strong>Baie de la Seine</strong> region, <strong>north of Caen</strong>. The vessel had departed from <strong>Antwerp Port in Belgium on 24 October</strong> bound for <strong>Jebel Ali Port in the United Arab Emirates</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In a statement following the incident, the company confirmed that full support and coordination were provided to both the crew and cargo owners. Grimaldi also stated: "<strong>We extend our thanks to the French and English maritime authorities</strong> and to <strong>Les Abeilles</strong> for their <strong>swift and professional response</strong>."</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Technical and Operational Assessment</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, the fire starting in the <strong>engine room</strong> caused a serious malfunction in the vessel's power system, leading to <strong>complete power loss (blackout)</strong>. In such incidents, the rapid deployment of <strong>CO₂ suppression systems</strong> plays a critical role in preventing the fire from spreading to the vessel's fuel and power systems. Additionally, the <strong>bulkhead cooling</strong> method prevents the ship's structure from overheating during the fire, reducing the <strong>risk of metal deformation</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Grande Roma</strong> is an important member of Grimaldi's fleet of <strong>Ro-Ro (roll-on/roll-off)</strong> vessels that transport automobiles and heavy vehicles. The company operates regular routes <strong>between Europe, Africa, the Mediterranean, and the Middle East</strong>. Although the incident was brought under control without damaging the company's operational reputation, it is expected to prompt a review of <strong>power security and maintenance protocols</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Similar incidents have occurred in the past, particularly on <strong>older Ro-Ro vessels</strong>, and have generally been caused by <strong>fuel line issues or generator-related technical failures</strong>. However, in this case, the crew's <strong>rapid response in compliance with protocols</strong> prevented both loss of life and environmental damage.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Current Status</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Grande Roma</strong> is currently being <strong>towed to Le Havre Port by the Abeille Liberté</strong> tugboat and will undergo technical inspection upon arrival. While the incident is one of several minor operational disruptions the Grimaldi fleet has experienced recently, it is being viewed as a case that will provide the company with important lessons in terms of <strong>vessel safety and maintenance procedures</strong>.</p>
