<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Todd Kolber</strong>, co-managing director of <strong>Logistics Reply</strong>, notes that agentic artificial intelligence is improving the operations of warehouse management systems (<strong>WMS</strong>). According to <strong>Kolber</strong>'s perspective, current <strong>WMS</strong> architecture is insufficient. Planners look months or even years ahead, operations are tasked with meeting these forecasts, and management must serve as the "<strong>traffic cop</strong>" keeping everything running. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>WMS</strong>, along with <strong>WCS</strong> (<strong>Warehouse Control System</strong>) and <strong>WES</strong> (<strong>Warehouse Execution System</strong>), form three main layers of the <strong>warehouse stack</strong>. <strong>Manhattan Associates</strong>, <strong>Blue Yonder</strong>, <strong>SAP EWM</strong>, <strong>Oracle WMS Cloud</strong>, <strong>Körber</strong>, <strong>Softeon</strong>, and <strong>Logistics Reply</strong> are the market's major players, and all are moving toward <strong>agentic AI</strong> integration.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Kolber</strong> states: "<strong>The problem that a warehouse management system has is that it can't see the whole</strong>." <strong>Kolber</strong> adds: "<strong>It's answering a need for a given task and fulfilling orders, but it's not really providing what is the total optimal outcome for a process or facility. There's still a lot to be desired today</strong>." Either management or a solution must be far more aware at the "<strong>umbrella level</strong>" about all resources, functions, and technologies across the entire facility. <strong>Kolber</strong> emphasizes: "<strong>The key word is orchestrate – orchestrate what happens in that facility</strong>." From a supply chain perspective, <strong>warehouse orchestration</strong> encompasses the synchronized coordination of <strong>AGV</strong> (<strong>Automated Guided Vehicle</strong>), <strong>AMR</strong> (<strong>Autonomous Mobile Robot</strong>), <strong>AS/RS</strong> (<strong>Automated Storage and Retrieval System</strong>), <strong>conveyor</strong>, <strong>sorter</strong>, and human labor. Automation providers such as <strong>Locus Robotics</strong>, <strong>6 River Systems</strong>, <strong>Geek+</strong>, <strong>AutoStore</strong>, and <strong>Symbotic</strong> offer an <strong>API-first</strong> approach for <strong>WMS</strong> integration points.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">So much data and activity happens in real time that it becomes impossible for any individual or team to make the right decision about what action should be taken. <strong>Kolber</strong> states: "<strong>It's going to be critical that AI and agentic AI systems play a major role in bringing all that data together, analyzing it, looking at historical trends and patterns, then making decisions based on all that information in real time and providing the optimal answer</strong>." More importantly, when unexpected things happen—when a large order comes in, when a machine begins performing at historically lower speeds, or when an area of the warehouse goes down—people cannot react fast enough to recover. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>real-time event processing</strong> runs on <strong>streaming architecture</strong> based on <strong>Apache Kafka</strong>, <strong>Apache Flink</strong>, and <strong>Confluent</strong>. <strong>Digital twin</strong> technology, via platforms such as <strong>Siemens Tecnomatix Plant Simulation</strong>, <strong>FlexSim</strong>, and <strong>AnyLogic</strong>, simulates <strong>what-if</strong> scenarios and provides the foundation for agentic AI action decisions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Kolber</strong> states: "<strong>AI can analyze all that data in real time, solve things in such a way that you can still meet the goals of that day, and do it at optimal cost</strong>." From a supply chain perspective, <strong>cost-to-serve</strong> optimization requires real-time recalculation of the balance between <strong>labor cost</strong>, <strong>equipment utilization</strong>, <strong>energy consumption</strong>, and <strong>SLA</strong> (<strong>Service Level Agreement</strong>) penalty costs. For <strong>3PL</strong> (<strong>Third-Party Logistics</strong>) operators, this approach enables simultaneous optimization of different customer requirements in <strong>multi-tenant</strong> facilities. <strong>Logistics Reply</strong>, as part of the <strong>Reply Group</strong> and headquartered in <strong>Italy</strong>, serves a global customer base across <strong>EMEA</strong>, <strong>Americas</strong>, and <strong>APAC</strong>. In conclusion, <strong>Kolber</strong>'s vision signals that the <strong>WMS</strong> market will evolve over the next 3–5 years from order management alone to integrated real-time facility orchestration.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. <strong>Kolber</strong> notes that current <strong>WMS</strong> architecture is insufficient.<br>
2. <strong>WMS</strong> cannot see the whole picture; it operates only on a task basis.<br>
3. The concept of "<strong>orchestrate</strong>" is key to coordinating all resources and technologies.<br>
4. Real-time data exceeds human decision-making capacity; <strong>AI</strong> and <strong>agentic AI</strong> play a critical role.<br>
5. <strong>AI</strong> enables achievement of daily goals at optimal cost.</p>
