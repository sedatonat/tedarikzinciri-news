<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>"Vassily Golovnin" diesel-electric icebreaker</strong> operated by <strong>FESCO Transport Group</strong> set sail to the Antarctic with cargo for India's polar stations. According to the Group, this is the <strong>eighth Antarctic mission</strong> of the vessel under the contract with <strong>India's National Centre for Polar and Ocean Research</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The icebreaker set sail from <strong>Cape Town in late December</strong>. The <strong>expedition will continue until April 2026</strong>. "Vassily Golovnin" will visit two Indian polar stations — <strong>Bharrati and Maitri</strong> — delivering <strong>fuel and supplies</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The icebreaker will also deliver <strong>a new shift of Indian polar researchers</strong>. This team will replace their current colleagues and continue scientific work in Antarctica. Personnel rotation is a critical logistical operation that keeps Antarctic stations continuously operational. FESCO's cooperation with India sustains a long-term, reliable international scientific partnership. It highlights that Russian maritime capabilities remain valuable to global science even in the sanctions era.</p>

1. FESCO's "Vassily Golovnin" icebreaker sailed from Cape Town for its 8th Antarctic mission.<br>

3. The expedition will continue until April 2026.<br>
4. Fuel and supplies will be delivered to Bharrati and Maitri stations.<br>
