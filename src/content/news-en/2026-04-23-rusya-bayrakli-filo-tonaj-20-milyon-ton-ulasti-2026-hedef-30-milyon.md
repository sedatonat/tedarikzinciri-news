<p style="text-align: justify">Vitaly Klyuev, Director of the Ministry of Transport's Department of State Policy in Maritime and Inland Waterway Transport, said the combined tonnage of cargo vessels registered under the Russian flag has more than doubled over the past two years, reaching almost 20 million tonnes. According to Klyuev, by the end of 2026 the figure is expected to reach 30 million tonnes.</p>

<p style="text-align: justify">Speaking at the 6th International Conference "Risks in Marine Insurance: Best Practices, Russian and International Experience," Klyuev said up to 1,000 vessels — most of them large tankers — may be waiting to transfer to the national flag. Russia is prioritising the build-out of its own flag, insurance and classification infrastructure in the face of Western sanctions.</p>

<p style="text-align: justify">Klyuev believes a rift in understanding international maritime law is one of the reasons behind this need. Russian P&I insurer capacity is growing; some countries such as India have expanded their list of approved Russian insurers to preserve port access for Russian tankers.</p>
