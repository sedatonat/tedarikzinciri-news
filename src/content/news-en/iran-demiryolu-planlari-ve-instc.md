<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Iran's Railway Initiatives and the Future of INSTC</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Iran has recently announced plans to construct nine national railway corridors. These projects aim to transform the country into a major railway transit hub and increase regional connectivity, particularly within the framework of the <strong>International North-South Transport Corridor</strong> (<strong>INSTC</strong>). INSTC is a multimodal transport network designed to facilitate goods transportation between India, Iran, Azerbaijan, Russia, and Europe. This corridor has the potential to significantly reduce transportation time and costs compared to conventional routes.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Iran's railway projects include strategic infrastructure investments such as the development of <strong>Chabahar</strong> Port and the completion of the <strong>Rasht-Astara</strong> railway line. Chabahar Port is viewed as a critical hub for trade with India and Afghanistan due to its direct access to the Gulf of Oman. The Rasht-Astara line strengthens INSTC's land connectivity by linking Iran to Russia through Azerbaijan.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Trump's Presidency and Potential Impacts</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">On January 20, 2025, <strong>Donald Trump</strong> was sworn in as the 47th President of the United States. During his previous presidency, Trump pursued a hardline policy toward Iran and implemented economic sanctions that negatively affected Iran's economy. Trump's return to the presidency could impose new pressures on Iran's current and planned infrastructure projects.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Within Trump's "America First" policy framework, the emphasis on energy independence could reshape energy dynamics in the Middle East. This could negatively impact Iran's energy exports and, consequently, the financing of railway projects. Furthermore, Trump's close relationships with Israel and Saudi Arabia could further strain Iran's geopolitical position in the region.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Russia and Iran Cooperation</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Days before Trump took office, <strong>Vladimir Putin</strong> and Iran's President <strong>Masoud Pezeshkian</strong> signed a partnership agreement in Moscow that includes closer military cooperation. This agreement aims to increase the two countries' resilience against Western sanctions and strengthen cooperation on regional projects. Russia's interest in INSTC and potential support for Iran's railway projects could be critical for the continuity of these infrastructure initiatives.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>INSTC's Strategic Importance</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">INSTC was designed as a trade corridor extending from India through Iran to Azerbaijan and Russia, and from there to Europe. This corridor offers an alternative route to the Suez Canal, with the potential to reduce transportation time and costs. Holding strategic significance for India and Russia in particular, INSTC aims to increase trade volumes in the region.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The completion of Iran's railway projects will directly impact INSTC's effectiveness. Completion of the Rasht-Astara line in particular will provide seamless land connectivity, enhancing the corridor's appeal. However, possible new sanctions imposed by the Trump administration on Iran could negatively affect the financing and completion timeline of these projects.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>In Summary...</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Iran's railway projects and INSTC's future could be significantly affected by Trump's policies during his presidency. New sanctions that the United States might impose on Iran could complicate the financing and international cooperation processes of these projects. However, the strategic partnership between Russia and Iran, as well as other countries' interest in INSTC, offer hope for the continuation of these projects.
