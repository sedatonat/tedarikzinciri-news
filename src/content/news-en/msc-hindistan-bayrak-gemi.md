<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Mediterranean Shipping Co. (MSC), the world's largest container shipping company,</strong> has decided to <strong>reflag 12 container ships under the Indian registry.</strong> This move is part of the company's strategy to diversify its investments in India's <strong>maritime, port, and logistics sectors,</strong> <strong>moving beyond the port-to-port shipping model.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>New fleet under Indian flag</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">MSC CEO <strong>Soren Toft</strong> stated on Monday that the <strong>transition of these vessels to the Indian flag</strong> (reflag) represents the company's "<strong>long-term support for India's maritime goals.</strong>"</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"India is a key country for MSC. We are experiencing strong growth in maritime shipping, port operations, and logistics investments," Toft said.</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">This move follows similar flag changes previously undertaken by <strong>CMA CGM</strong> and <strong>Maersk.</strong> This trend indicates that major liner operators are accelerating their efforts to position <strong>India as a regional maritime hub.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>MSC's strategic presence in India</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">MSC is currently <strong>the largest maritime shipping operator with Indian origin.</strong> Through partnerships with <strong>Adani Ports and Special Economic Zone (APSEZ),</strong> the company is conducting <strong>terminal operations and development investments at the Mundra (Gujarat)</strong> and <strong>Ennore (Chennai) ports.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Thanks to these investments, MSC extends beyond maritime transportation alone in India, also channeling capital into <strong>container terminals, hinterland transportation, and logistics storage infrastructure.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to analysts, the reflagging of 12 vessels under the Indian flag will provide MSC with <strong>advantages under local regulations and bilateral trade agreements.</strong> In particular, <strong>India's cabotage (coastal shipping)</strong> policies offer locally flagged vessels <strong>preferential sailing rights and tax incentives.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A step aligned with India's maritime ambitions</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">MSC's decision aligns with <strong>the Indian government's Vision Maritime India 2030 strategy.</strong> This strategy aims to position the country as one of the leading centers in <strong>global maritime trade, shipbuilding, and maritime services.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Indian maritime authorities are preparing new policies that include <strong>customs facilitation, ship maintenance support, and financial incentives</strong> to encourage more international operators to <strong>register vessels with the local maritime registry.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">MSC's move also demonstrates that these efforts are <strong>building confidence among international investors.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>MSC's diversification strategy</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">This development is an extension of MSC's <strong>global diversification</strong> strategy pursued in recent years. The company is rapidly expanding not only in transportation but also in <strong>port terminal management, land logistics, storage, and digital supply chain solutions.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">India holds significant potential in this regard, given its <strong>regional manufacturing and export centers.</strong> India's <strong>"Make in India"</strong> and <strong>"Sagarmala"</strong> programs, which focus on developing manufacturing and port infrastructure, are creating <strong>new investment opportunities for operators like MSC.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Regional and global implications</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">India's <strong>geostrategic location</strong> offers MSC a significant advantage in <strong>Asia–Middle East–Africa shipping corridors.</strong> This newly reflagged fleet will provide MSC with <strong>flexibility in regional operations,</strong> <strong>customs facilitation,</strong> and <strong>competitive advantage in coastal trade.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, this decision will also contribute to <strong>India's goal of increasing "national fleet capacity"</strong> in the maritime industry.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>MSC</strong> has decided to <strong>reflag 12 container ships under the Indian flag.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The decision is part of the company's strategy to support <strong>its logistics and port investments in India.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Investments continue at Mundra and Ennore terminals</strong> through partnerships with <strong>Adani Ports.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Reflagging will provide <strong>cabotage privileges and tax advantages.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The move is aligned with <strong>Vision Maritime India 2030</strong> and <strong>Sagarmala Program</strong> objectives.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">MSC is transitioning from <strong>global shipping alone to an integrated logistics network.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">This development provides strategic support to <strong>India's vision of becoming a global maritime center.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
