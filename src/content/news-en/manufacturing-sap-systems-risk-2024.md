<p>Failure to update SAP systems has emerged as a major risk in the manufacturing sector. Many companies advancing rapidly in their digital transformation processes continue to rely on outdated SAP systems. This situation leaves them vulnerable to cyberattacks. The lack of support for legacy SAP versions leads to an increase in security vulnerabilities within these systems, creating potential dangers that could result in production halts, significant financial losses, and reputational damage.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>The digital transformation process, which accelerated particularly in the post-COVID-19 period, prompted many manufacturers to rapidly integrate ERP systems without implementing adequate security measures. However, this speed has led to a proliferation of security gaps. Additionally, the manufacturing sector faces increasingly complex ERP systems. These systems are not only required to perform traditional functions, but must also be integrated with sustainable practices and service-oriented models. This growing complexity creates challenges in maintaining system security.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Cybercriminals, targeting SAP systems in particular, can inflict serious damage. Research shows that ransomware attacks targeting SAP systems increased by 400 percent between 2021 and 2023. This demonstrates how significant a threat the manufacturing sector faces.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Today, manufacturers must implement modern security measures to combat these threats. Automated security processes play a critical role in minimizing human error and enabling rapid response. Additionally, it is important to use continuous threat intelligence and research findings to identify security vulnerabilities in ERP systems and quickly remediate these gaps.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>In summary, failure by manufacturers to update their SAP systems leaves them defenseless against cyberattacks. This situation creates a major risk that could halt production processes, result in severe financial losses, and cause reputational damage. Manufacturers must minimize this risk by updating their SAP systems and implementing modern security measures.</p>
<p><br></p>

  <li><p>Lack of support for legacy SAP systems creates serious security vulnerabilities in the manufacturing sector.</p></li>

  <li><p>The accelerated pace of digital transformation integrations has led to the proliferation of security gaps.</p></li>
