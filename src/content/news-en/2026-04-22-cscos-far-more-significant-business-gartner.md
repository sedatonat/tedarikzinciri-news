<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> reports: <strong>Helen Atkinson</strong>, <strong>Managing Editor</strong>, is focusing on an intriguing new role in the supply chain management industry: robot manager. No joke. Within the next few years there will be so many robots that approximately 5% of workforce managers will be managing robots like people. Of course, not quite like people—since robots don't get sick, take vacation, or take cigarette breaks—<strong>Gartner's</strong> <strong>Tom Enright</strong>, Vice President Analyst, noted in his presentation at <strong>Gartner's Supply Chain Symposium/XPO</strong> on May 5 in <strong>Orlando, Florida</strong>, during the session "<strong>Gartner's Top Strategic Supply Chain Predictions for 2025 and Beyond</strong>." "Going forward there will be robot fleets," <strong>Enright</strong> said. "And they won't just be in distribution centers. They'll be in stores, call centers, factories—everywhere. They will support people and human decision-making—not replace it." This rise of robots will be just one of the sharp, technology-driven changes coming to the supply chain world, making the role of any organization's chief supply chain officer (<strong>CSCO</strong>) far more central, <strong>Enright</strong> said. They will be shifting from "trucks and sheds" toward a much more customer-oriented role. Additionally, <strong>CSCOs</strong> will need to embrace AI and machine learning. "By 2028, 15% of day-to-day supply chain decisions will be made autonomously by AI agents—leaving humans to focus on more critical decisions." Major changes will come from three main areas, <strong>Enright</strong> said: people, AI, and customers. "Even if only 50% of our predictions prove correct, the <strong>CSCO</strong> role will become unrecognizable."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Gartner Inc.</strong> (<strong>NYSE: IT</strong>), based in <strong>Stamford, Connecticut, U.S.</strong>, with <strong>Gene Hall</strong> as CEO, founded in 1979, is the global leader among technology research and consulting firms—with 2024 annual revenue of $6.3 billion USD and 21,000+ employees. <strong>Gartner Supply Chain</strong> (formerly <strong>Gartner Logistics Strategies</strong>; formerly <strong>AMR Research</strong>, acquired in 2009) includes principal analysts <strong>Mike Burkett</strong>, Distinguished VP Analyst; <strong>Tom Enright</strong>; <strong>Simon Bailey</strong>; <strong>Pierfrancesco Manenti</strong>; and <strong>Dwight Klappich</strong>. The <strong>Gartner Magic Quadrant</strong>; <strong>Hype Cycle</strong>; and <strong>Top 25 Supply Chains</strong> ranking are key outputs—with 2024 <strong>Top 25</strong> leaders including <strong>Schneider Electric</strong>, <strong>Cisco</strong>, <strong>Colgate-Palmolive</strong>, <strong>Microsoft</strong>, and <strong>Johnson &amp; Johnson</strong> as principal leading firms. Other major supply chain research firms include <strong>Forrester Research</strong> (CEO <strong>George Colony</strong>), <strong>IDC</strong>, <strong>Nucleus Research</strong>, <strong>ARC Advisory Group</strong>, and <strong>Lora Cecere</strong> (<strong>Supply Chain Insights</strong>) as principal ecosystem players.</p>

<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, principal supply chain robotics providers include <strong>Symbotic</strong> (Founder <strong>Rick Cohen</strong>; <strong>Walmart</strong> as principal customer); <strong>Locus Robotics</strong> (CEO <strong>Rick Faulk</strong>); <strong>6 River Systems</strong> (<strong>Ocado</strong>-owned); <strong>Geek+</strong>; <strong>AutoStore</strong> (CEO <strong>Mats Hovland Vikse</strong>; <strong>Norway</strong>); <strong>Exotec</strong> (<strong>France</strong>); <strong>GreyOrange</strong>; <strong>Berkshire Grey</strong> (<strong>SoftBank</strong>-owned); <strong>Fetch Robotics</strong> (<strong>Zebra Technologies</strong>-owned); <strong>Boston Dynamics</strong> (<strong>Hyundai</strong>-owned); <strong>Agility Robotics</strong> (CEO <strong>Damion Shelton</strong>; humanoid "Digit"); <strong>Figure AI</strong> (CEO <strong>Brett Adcock</strong>); <strong>Apptronik</strong>; and <strong>1X Technologies</strong> (<strong>OpenAI</strong> investment) as principal ecosystem players. <strong>AI agent</strong> and <strong>autonomous decision-making</strong> providers include <strong>o9 Solutions</strong>, <strong>Aera Technology</strong>, <strong>Project Atlas</strong> (<strong>Microsoft</strong>), <strong>SAP Joule</strong>, <strong>Salesforce Agentforce</strong>, <strong>Google Vertex AI</strong>, <strong>Anthropic Claude</strong>, and <strong>OpenAI</strong> as principal platforms. <p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the evolution of the <strong>CSCO</strong> role includes: (1) 1980-2000, <strong>VP of Logistics</strong>; operational role; (2) 2000-2010, <strong>VP of Supply Chain</strong>; process optimization; (3) 2010-2020, <strong>Chief Supply Chain Officer</strong>; strategic role; (4) 2020-2024, post-pandemic resilience; ESG; supply chain visibility; (5) 2025+, AI; autonomous decision-making; customer-focused; as principal role evolution. <strong>Gartner's</strong> other 2025 strategic predictions include: (1) By 2027, 50% of supply chains will increase operational efficiency by 10%+ through <strong>generative AI</strong> usage; (2) By 2028, 25% of large 1,000-person companies will have an additional <strong>chief sustainability officer</strong> role; (3) By 2030, advanced robotics adoption will augment 30% of the global workforce; (4) <strong>Quantum computing</strong> will create real-world impact in supply chain optimization post-2030; (5) <strong>Circular supply chain</strong> will become mandatory due to regulatory pressure; as principal trend axes. <strong>Gartner Symposium/Xpo</strong>; <strong>Gartner CIO Conference</strong>; and <strong>Gartner Tech Growth &amp; Innovation Conference</strong> are principal annual conferences. In conclusion, <strong>Enright's</strong> predictions regarding <strong>CSCO</strong> evolution are fundamentally redefining the global supply chain leadership role and the AI/robotics adoption paradigm—with customer-oriented transformation and autonomous AI agent adoption appearing to be principal strategic priorities for supply chain managers.</p>
