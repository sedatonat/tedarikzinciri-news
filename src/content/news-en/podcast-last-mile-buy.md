<p>The build versus buy dilemma in last-mile logistics is being discussed in terms of balancing capital investment and speed of execution.&nbsp;</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>The podcast compares the control and differentiation advantages of building in-house fleet &amp; tech with the scale and speed benefits of using 3PL/4PL partnerships and delivery platforms. In the short term, the buy option offers rapid market entry and low CAPEX; in the long term, a build strategy reduces per-unit costs and customizes customer experience.&nbsp;</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Hybrid approaches combine regional insourcing and private fleets in dense areas with outsourced models in low-density zones. The decision depends on parameters such as order volume, basket composition, SLA requirements, and geographic coverage.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Key Takeaways:<br>\n1. Build delivers control and differentiation.<br>\n2. Buy offers rapid scaling and low CAPEX.<br>\n3. Hybrid models are prevalent.<br>\n4. The decision depends on demand and SLA parameters.<br>\n5.
