<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain Webinar</strong> addresses the relentless complexity of supply chain management. Managing with disconnected tools is like flying a plane with three separate cockpits. <strong>Hexcel</strong> is moving to a single integrated process — allowing the entire network to function as one entity. In this webinar, <strong>Hexcel</strong>'s <strong>Supply Chain Manager</strong> <strong>Dan Carrick</strong>, together with <strong>Dassault Systèmes</strong>, shares how leading organizations are transforming supply chain planning by integrating <strong>AI</strong>, virtual twins and real-time execution systems. Attendees will hear how <strong>Hexcel</strong> is unifying fragmented tools, synchronizing multi-site operations, and accelerating decision-making across a complex global network. The session provides practical insights into how to connect internal silos across any industry, increase resilience, foster collaboration, and achieve measurable improvements in service and cost performance — featuring speakers <strong>Daniel Carrick</strong> (<strong>Hexcel Supply Chain Manager</strong>), <strong>Adrian Wood</strong> (<strong>DELMIA Dassault Systèmes Director Strategy &amp; Marketing</strong>), and <strong>Sarah Barnes-Humphrey</strong> (<strong>Let's Talk Supply Chain Founder</strong>).</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Hexcel Corporation</strong>, based in <strong>Stamford, Connecticut</strong>, is one of the world's largest advanced composite materials manufacturers — offering carbon fiber, resin, honeycomb structure, prepreg materials, with applications in aerospace, defense, industrial, wind, automotive, and marine sectors. <strong>Tom Gentile</strong> is <strong>Hexcel Chairman, CEO &amp; President</strong>. <strong>Hexcel</strong>'s key customers include <strong>Boeing</strong>, <strong>Airbus</strong>, <strong>Lockheed Martin</strong>, <strong>Northrop Grumman</strong>, <strong>Raytheon Technologies/RTX</strong>, <strong>Bombardier</strong>, <strong>Embraer</strong>, <strong>Dassault Aviation</strong>, <strong>Leonardo</strong>, <strong>Vestas</strong>, <strong>Siemens Gamesa</strong>, and <strong>GE Vernova</strong>. Primary aircraft and helicopter platforms using <strong>Hexcel</strong> composite materials include the <strong>Boeing 787 Dreamliner</strong>, <strong>Airbus A350 XWB</strong>, <strong>F-35 Lightning II</strong>, <strong>F/A-18 Super Hornet</strong>, <strong>V-22 Osprey</strong>, <strong>Eurofighter Typhoon</strong>, <strong>Rafale</strong>, and <strong>Gripen</strong>. Major <strong>Hexcel</strong> production facilities are located in <strong>Decatur, Alabama</strong>; <strong>Salt Lake City, Utah</strong>; <strong>Roussillon, France</strong>; <strong>Les Avenieres, France</strong>; <strong>Burnley, England</strong>; <strong>Stade, Germany</strong>; <strong>Linz, Austria</strong>; and <strong>Casablanca, Morocco</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Dassault Systèmes SE</strong>, based in <strong>Vélizy-Villacoublay, France</strong>, is one of the world's largest providers of 3D design, <strong>PLM</strong> (<strong>Product Lifecycle Management</strong>), and simulation software — <strong>Pascal Daloz</strong> is <strong>CEO</strong>. The <strong>3DEXPERIENCE Platform</strong> is its flagship platform, encompassing <strong>CATIA</strong> (3D design), <strong>SOLIDWORKS</strong>, <strong>SIMULIA</strong> (simulation), <strong>DELMIA</strong> (manufacturing and operations), <strong>ENOVIA</strong> (<strong>PLM</strong>), <strong>NETVIBES</strong>, <strong>GEOVIA</strong>, <strong>BIOVIA</strong>, <strong>EXALEAD</strong>, <strong>3DVIA</strong>, <strong>MEDIDATA</strong>, and <strong>Centric PLM</strong> in its core product portfolio. <strong>DELMIA</strong> provides virtual twin solutions for manufacturing engineering, supply chain planning, production operations, workforce planning, and logistics — strengthened by acquisitions of <strong>Quintiq</strong> and <strong>Ortems</strong>. Key competitors include <strong>Siemens Digital Industries Software</strong>, <strong>PTC</strong>, <strong>Autodesk</strong>, <strong>Hexagon AB</strong>, <strong>Bentley Systems</strong>, <strong>ANSYS</strong>, <strong>Altair</strong>, <strong>Synopsys</strong>, <strong>Aspen Technology</strong>, and <strong>AVEVA</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, integrated planning methodologies include <strong>S&amp;OP</strong> (<strong>Sales &amp; Operations Planning</strong>), <strong>S&amp;OE</strong> (<strong>Sales &amp; Operations Execution</strong>), <strong>IBP</strong> (<strong>Integrated Business Planning</strong>), and <strong>DDMRP</strong> (<strong>Demand Driven Material Requirements Planning</strong>). A virtual twin (digital twin) represents the real-time digital equivalent of a physical asset — a foundational concept of <strong>Industry 4.0</strong> and <strong>Industry 5.0</strong> — used throughout the design, manufacturing, maintenance, and operational lifecycle. Leading virtual twin platforms include <strong>NVIDIA Omniverse</strong>, <strong>Microsoft Azure Digital Twins</strong>, <strong>AWS IoT TwinMaker</strong>, <strong>Siemens Xcelerator</strong>, <strong>PTC ThingWorx</strong>, and <strong>GE Digital Predix</strong>. Major integrated planning software providers include <strong>Kinaxis Maestro</strong>, <strong>o9 Solutions</strong>, <strong>Blue Yonder Cognitive Solutions</strong>, <strong>SAP IBP</strong>, <strong>Oracle SCM</strong>, <strong>Anaplan</strong>, <strong>OneStream</strong>, <strong>Workday Adaptive</strong>, <strong>JDA</strong>, <strong>Manhattan Active</strong>, <strong>RELEX Solutions</strong>, <strong>ToolsGroup</strong>, <strong>John Galt Solutions</strong>, <strong>Logility</strong>, and <strong>Demand Solutions</strong>. In summary, the <strong>Hexcel</strong> and <strong>Dassault Systèmes</strong> webinar presents industry professionals with a clear case study demonstrating the practical application of integrated intelligence and virtual twin technologies for the global composite materials supply chain.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. <strong>Hexcel</strong> is consolidating disconnected supply chain tools into a single integrated process.<br>
2. <strong>Dassault Systèmes DELMIA</strong> delivers <strong>AI</strong>, virtual twin, and real-time execution integration.<br>
3. Speakers include <strong>Daniel Carrick</strong> (<strong>Hexcel</strong>), <strong>Adrian Wood</strong> (<strong>DELMIA</strong>), and <strong>Sarah Barnes-Humphrey</strong>.<br>
4. <strong>Hexcel</strong> is a key composite materials supplier to <strong>Boeing</strong>, <strong>Airbus</strong>, <strong>Lockheed</strong>, and <strong>Vestas</strong>.<br>
5. Virtual twins and <strong>S&amp;OP/IBP</strong> are core integrated planning methodologies.</p>
