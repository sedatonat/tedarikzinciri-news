<p>On December 19, 2024, the <strong>International Brotherhood of Teamsters</strong> union launched the largest strike against Amazon in U.S. history. Thousands of workers at seven facilities in cities including New York, Atlanta, San Francisco, and Los Angeles walked off the job demanding better wages, benefits, and working conditions. The strike took place during the busiest period of the holiday season.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Teamsters General President <strong>Sean M. O'Brien</strong> stated, "If your package is delayed during the holidays, blame Amazon's insatiable greed," highlighting the company's lack of respect for workers. The union announced that Amazon had ignored a negotiation date set for December 15, prompting workers to decide to strike.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Amazon spokeswoman <strong>Kelly Nantel</strong> rejected the Teamsters' claims, asserting that the union does not represent Amazon workers and that operations have continued smoothly. Nantel said, "The Teamsters have threatened Amazon workers and third-party drivers to force them to join the union; this is illegal and the subject of numerous unfair labor practice cases against the union."</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>The strike could affect Amazon's holiday season deliveries. Customers in the New York region in particular may experience delivery delays of one to three days. Experts indicate that the strike will have limited impact on Amazon's overall operations, but may cause some disruptions due to its timing.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Amazon avoids direct negotiation responsibility by employing drivers through third-party companies. However, because drivers wear Amazon uniforms and operate Amazon trucks, they view themselves as Amazon employees. The Teamsters argue that these drivers hold joint employer status under a National Labor Relations Board decision.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>The strike is viewed as part of a growing backlash against Amazon's stance on workers' rights. Previously, Amazon workers have organized various protests over working conditions and unionization rights. This strike is regarded as an important step in workers more forcefully expressing their demands.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li><p>The Teamsters union launched the largest strike against Amazon in U.S. history.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The strike is taking place at seven facilities in New York, Atlanta, San Francisco, and Los Angeles.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Workers are demanding better wages, benefits, and working conditions.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Amazon rejected the union's claims, stating that operations are unaffected.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The strike could cause delays in holiday season deliveries.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Although drivers are employed through third-party companies, they view themselves as Amazon employees.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The Teamsters argue that drivers hold joint employer status.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The strike is viewed as part of growing backlash against Amazon's stance on workers' rights.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>---</p>\n<p><strong>News Link: </strong><a href="https://www.supplychain247.com/article/amazon-labor-teamsters-strike-seven-domestic-facilities"><u>https://www.supplychain247.com/article/amazon-labor-teamsters-strike-seven-domestic-facilities</u></a></p>\n<p>-------------------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
