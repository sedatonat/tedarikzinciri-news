<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Levi Strauss &amp; Co.</strong> is nearing completion of its transition from operating owned distribution centers to a new warehouse network based on <strong>leased facilities and third-party logistics (3PL)</strong> models. The company plans to end the <strong>simultaneous operation of both owned and leased facilities</strong> by early 2026. This transition is viewed as a critical component of Levi's <strong>strategic transformation toward direct-to-consumer (DTC)</strong> channels.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Harmit Singh, Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer (EVP &amp; CFO)</strong> of Levi's, announced during the company's third-quarter financial results presentation that <strong>distribution costs increased 19.5 percent year-over-year</strong>. Singh noted that the primary drivers of this increase were the <strong>parallel operation of warehouse networks</strong> and a <strong>reclassification of certain e-commerce expenses</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">A company spokesperson stated that operational costs are expected to decline upon completion of the transition, and that the new network will provide "<strong>greater efficiency and flexibility</strong>." Through this new structure, Levi's aims to deliver more balanced service to both direct-to-consumer and wholesale channels.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This transformation process initially began with a <strong>strategic shift announced in 2024</strong>. As part of this effort, Levi's decided to <strong>close its owned facility in Kentucky</strong> and entered into agreements with <strong>3PL partners</strong> to operate new distribution centers in the <strong>Groveport, Ohio</strong> region.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The company also previously announced that a <strong>facility in Mississippi</strong> would transition to the 3PL model; however, as of October 2025, <strong>Levi's confirmed it no longer operates any distribution operations in Mississippi</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">While Levi's operates a distribution center in <strong>Henderson, Nevada</strong> within the United States, it also maintains operations in leased facilities in the <strong>San Francisco</strong> and <strong>Erlanger, Kentucky</strong> regions. The company's objective is to <strong>reduce the number of owned warehouses and shift toward a flexible leasing model</strong>, thereby making its <strong>supply chain more agile</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Harmit Singh's remarks, the <strong>direct-to-consumer sales channel (DTC)</strong> accounts for <strong>more than 40 percent of total sales</strong> in the U.S. market. The new warehouse model is being designed to support this segment. Singh stated that the company expects <strong>a reduction in distribution costs per unit</strong> once the transformation is complete.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This restructuring process is proceeding in parallel with a significant change in Levi's supply chain leadership. <strong>Chris Callieri</strong> assumed the role of <strong>Senior Vice President and Chief Supply Chain Officer (SVP &amp; Chief Supply Chain Officer)</strong> in September. Callieri previously held a similar position at <strong>Victoria's Secret</strong> and has taken over the responsibilities of retiring former COO <strong>Liz O'Neill</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This transformation of Levi's warehouse network is directly linked not only to operational efficiency but also to <strong>adapting to digitalized consumer behavior patterns</strong>. By transitioning to a leased and 3PL-based structure, Levi's aims to achieve <strong>scalability</strong>, <strong>cost flexibility</strong>, and <strong>channel-agnostic performance optimization</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In short, Levi's is transitioning from a <strong>traditional warehouse ownership model</strong> to a <strong>logistics-as-a-service</strong> approach. This move directly aligns with the brand's global objectives for faster delivery, reduced operational burden, and increased customer satisfaction.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Levi's will complete its transition from owned warehouses to leased facilities by early 2026</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Distribution costs rose 19.5 percent</strong>, though declines are expected once the transition is complete.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Kentucky facility closed</strong>, <strong>Mississippi operations terminated</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>New leader Chris Callieri is guiding the transformation</strong> with his supply chain expertise.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>DTC sales exceed 40 percent</strong>, with the new network aimed at strengthening this segment.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Strategic objectives: <strong>flexibility, efficiency, and cost optimization</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
