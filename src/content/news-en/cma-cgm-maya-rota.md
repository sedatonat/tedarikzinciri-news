<p style="text-align: justify">CMA CGM has announced the launch of <strong>a new weekly dedicated service for Central America's West Coast</strong> as part of its strategy to strengthen its global liner network and respond more quickly to regional market dynamics. The new route, which the company has named the <strong>Maya Service</strong>, will directly connect Mexico's <strong>Lázaro Cárdenas</strong> port with critical Central American ports.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The new line is regarded as a stable connection addressing the rising trade traffic along Central America's west coast, regional supply chain pressures, and operational complexities at ports. In its official announcement, CMA CGM noted that the establishment of the Maya Service represents "a response to regional market fundamentals and recent operational challenges."</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>New Maya Route Cycle</strong></h4>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The service route is planned as follows:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Lázaro Cárdenas (Mexico)</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Puerto Caldera (Costa Rica)</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Corinto (Nicaragua)</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Acajutla (El Salvador)</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Puerto Quetzal (Guatemala)</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Lázaro Cárdenas (Mexico)</strong></p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">This cycle will strengthen trade flows between Central America and northern Mexico while facilitating connections to South America and Asia. Notably, Lázaro Cárdenas' extensive transit capabilities to Asia will serve as a gateway to Central America via the new route.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Vessel Allocation and Operational Structure</strong></h4>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The Maya Service will operate with a fixed fleet of two vessels:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>m/v CMA CGM SURABAYA</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>m/v MAGDALENA SCHULTE</strong></p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">These two vessels will maintain the weekly cycle uninterrupted. The carrying capacity of both vessels has been determined to suit the relatively smaller ports on Central America's west coast. Through this structure, CMA CGM aims to deliver service continuity, regular connectivity, and weekly frequency advantages.</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The service's inaugural sailing will begin on <strong>January 2, 2025</strong> with <strong>CMA CGM SURABAYA</strong> (Voy. 0UC01S1MA).</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic Importance</strong></h4>\n<p style="text-align: justify">CMA CGM's new service addresses three critical objectives:</p>\n<ol>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Meeting growing demand in Central America</strong><br>\n– The region is experiencing significant growth, particularly in the transportation of agricultural products, cold chain cargo, and retail merchandise.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strengthening Asia connectivity through Lázaro Cárdenas</strong><br>\n– Enabling integration with CMA CGM's Asia–West America services to optimize transit times.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Logistic stability and regular frequency</strong><br>\n– Carriers needed stable services due to port congestion and irregular scheduling in Central America.</p></li>\n</ol>\n<p style="text-align: justify">As CMA CGM continues its growth with 16 billion dollars in revenue according to latest quarterly data, the new Maya Service is viewed as a significant step in further strengthening the company's presence across the Americas.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h4>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">CMA CGM announced the new <strong>Maya line for Central America's West Coast</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Route cycle: Lázaro Cárdenas – Puerto Caldera – Corinto – Acajutla – Puerto Quetzal – Lázaro Cárdenas.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Service will be operated with 2 vessels: <strong>SURABAYA</strong> and <strong>MAGDALENA SCHULTE</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">First sailing begins January 2, 2025.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The new route is viewed as a strategic move addressing regional demand, operational challenges, and Asia connectivity.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>----------</p>\n<p><strong>News Link:
