<p style="text-align: justify"><a href="https://www.usps.com/"><u><strong>U.S. Postal Service</strong></u><u> (</u><u><strong>USPS</strong></u><u>)</u></a> has decided to collaborate with <a href="https://doge.gov/"><u><strong>Department of Government Efficiency</strong></u><u> (</u><u><strong>DOGE</strong></u><u>)</u></a>, led by <strong>Elon Musk</strong>, to reduce costs and improve operational efficiency. As part of this initiative, approximately 10,000 employees are expected to depart through an early retirement program within the next 30 days.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Postmaster General Louis DeJoy</strong> stated in a letter to Congress that <strong>USPS</strong>, with an annual budget of 78 billion dollars, faces significant challenges, and the collaboration with <strong>DOGE</strong> will help address these issues. Key challenges facing <strong>USPS</strong> include mismanagement of retirement assets and restrictive regulatory requirements.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This collaboration is viewed as part of <strong>USPS</strong>'s "Delivering for America" 10-year modernization plan. However, implementation of this plan has resulted in significant mail delivery delays, particularly in Houston, drawing criticism.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>DOGE</strong> team is known for aggressive cost-reduction approaches in previous government reforms and aims to find efficiency-enhancing solutions for <strong>USPS</strong> operations. However, some critics view this collaboration as a step toward <strong>USPS</strong> privatization, raising concerns for rural Americans who depend on postal services.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>USPS</strong> reported a loss of 9.5 billion dollars in fiscal year 2024, with much of these losses attributed to factors beyond management control. This situation has raised serious concerns about <strong>USPS</strong>'s financial sustainability.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>USPS</strong>'s current structure aims to provide reliable postal service to all Americans regardless of geographic location. However, proposals such as privatization or merger with the <strong>Commerce Department</strong> create uncertainty about the future of this universal service obligation. Concerns are mounting regarding the accessibility and affordability of postal services, particularly for rural residents.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>National Association of Letter Carriers</strong> President <strong>Brian Renfroe</strong> supports retirement fund reforms but opposes privatization efforts, noting that such moves would threaten workers' jobs and the agency's universal service obligation.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">These measures to improve <strong>USPS</strong>'s financial condition spark important discussions about the future of postal services and American access to them. Concerns persist regarding the continuity and quality of postal services, especially for residents in rural and remote areas.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>USPS</strong> is collaborating with <strong>DOGE</strong> to reduce costs and improve operational efficiency.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Approximately 10,000 employees are expected to depart through early retirement within the next 30 days.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>USPS</strong> reported losses of 9.5 billion dollars in fiscal year 2024.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>DOGE</strong> team is known for aggressive cost-reduction approaches in government reform.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Proposals for <strong>USPS</strong> privatization or merger with the <strong>Commerce Department</strong> create uncertainty about the future of postal services.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>National Association of Letter Carriers</strong> President <strong>Brian Renfroe</strong> opposes privatization efforts.</p></li>
</ul>
