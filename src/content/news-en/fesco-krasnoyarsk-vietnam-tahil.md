<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>FESCO Transportation Group</strong> has launched <strong>containerized grain exports</strong> from <strong>Krasnoyarsk</strong>, located in Russia's Siberian region, to Vietnam, expanding the company's geographic reach while establishing a new logistics channel for the regional agricultural economy. In a statement, the company emphasized that this new route represents a <strong>strategic gateway for West Siberian agricultural producers to Asian markets</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Krasnoyarsk Region's Agricultural Role</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Krasnoyarsk Krai</strong> is positioned as one of West Siberia's leading agricultural centers. The region is particularly known for the production of <strong>high-quality wheat and barley</strong>. Until now, grain exports from Krasnoyarsk have primarily been conducted via <strong>bulk carriers</strong> with limited flexibility. This method, requiring large shipments, has made it difficult for small and medium-sized producers to access export markets.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>containerized shipping model</strong> deployed by FESCO is changing this structure. Through containerization, grain can now be exported in <strong>smaller batches</strong>, with more flexible contracts, and in forms suited to different buyer profiles. This situation is <strong>diversifying export capacity</strong> for producers in the Krasnoyarsk region and strengthening their negotiating power.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Fully Integrated Intermodal Solution</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">FESCO is providing an end-to-end integrated logistics solution by utilizing <strong>all elements of intermodal transportation</strong> in this shipment. The operation's main steps are structured as follows:</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Rail Transportation:</strong><br>
Grain-laden containers are transported from <strong>Bazaikha Station</strong> in Krasnoyarsk to Vladivostok via regular and rapid <strong>rail services</strong>. This stage provides a reliable land link from the Siberian interior to Far Eastern ports.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Port Operations:</strong><br>
Containers are handled at the <strong>Commercial Port of Vladivostok (CPV)</strong>, a FESCO group asset. Having the port within the group enhances operational coordination and timing control.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Maritime Transportation:</strong><br>
The sea leg is conducted via <strong>FESCO Vietnam Direct Line (FVDL)</strong>, FESCO's regular service. This line provides direct connectivity between Russia's Far East and Southeast Asia.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>FVDL Service and the Vietnam Market</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>FESCO Vietnam Direct Line (FVDL)</strong> plays a critical role in trade between Russia and Southeast Asia. Wheat departing from Krasnoyarsk reaches <strong>Haiphong Port in Vietnam in approximately 21 days</strong>. This timeframe is considered competitive transit time for exports from the Siberian interior.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Vietnam, with its growing population and expanding food processing industry, holds the position of <strong>steady grain importer</strong>. The containerized solution offered by FESCO provides Vietnamese buyers with more <strong>predictable deliveries</strong>, lower inventory risk, and more flexible supply models.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Regional Development and Strategic Impact</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">FESCO emphasizes that this new route is not merely a commercial transportation activity but also contributes to <strong>Siberian regional development</strong>. Direct access to Asian markets for agricultural products manufactured in interior regions like Krasnoyarsk encourages efficient utilization of regional logistics infrastructure and helps ensure that <strong>added value remains in the region</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From the company's perspective, this service expands FESCO's <strong>agricultural logistics portfolio</strong> and enables Russia to assume a more active role in <strong>agricultural export chains</strong> beyond its traditional energy and raw materials exports.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Future Plans</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Following the success of pilot shipments, FESCO plans to transform this containerized grain export service from Krasnoyarsk to Vietnam into a <strong>regular service</strong>. The company also aims to expand this model beyond Vietnam to <strong>other Southeast Asian countries</strong> for wheat, barley, and various grain products.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This approach offers Russian agricultural producers seeking to access Asian markets <strong>lower entry barriers</strong>, <strong>reliable logistics</strong>, and <strong>single-window managed intermodal solutions</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Overall Assessment</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">FESCO's launch of containerized grain transportation on the Krasnoyarsk–Vietnam route represents a <strong>new-generation agricultural logistics model</strong> linking Russia's interior regions to Southeast Asia. Intermodal integration, containerization, and the use of regular services enable a more flexible, reliable, and scalable trade structure for both producers and buyers. As this model becomes more widespread, Siberian agriculture's visibility and competitiveness in Asian markets could increase.</p>
