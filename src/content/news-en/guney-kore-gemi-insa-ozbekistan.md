<p style="text-align: justify">South Korea is turning to <strong>Uzbekistan</strong>, a landlocked nation, to recruit workers and address severe labor shortages in its shipbuilding sector. Under this initiative, approximately 280 Uzbek workers will begin employment this summer at various shipyards around the city of <strong>Ulsan</strong>. These workers will be paid South Korea's minimum wage of 10,030 won per hour, approximately $7.60.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">While foreign workers have previously worked in South Korea's shipbuilding industry, this marks the first time a local government is directly involved in the recruitment process. The city of <strong>Ulsan</strong> is supporting this initiative by establishing shipbuilding training facilities in <strong>Uzbekistan</strong>. The success of this pilot program will be evaluated for potential expansion into larger-scale recruitment efforts in the future.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Rising shipbuilding orders in recent years have created a significant labor shortage in South Korea. The national government has responded by increasing annual caps on skilled worker visas and signing training and recruitment agreements with Asian countries. Within this framework, South Korea has also recruited workers from landlocked <strong>Nepal</strong>, with plans previously announced for up to 3,000 Nepalese workers to work in the country. Additionally, many workers from Southeast Asian nations including the <strong>Philippines</strong>, <strong>Indonesia</strong>, and <strong>Thailand</strong> are coming to work in South Korea.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">South Korea will recruit 280 workers from <strong>Uzbekistan</strong> to fill labor gaps in its shipbuilding sector.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Workers will be employed at shipyards around <strong>Ulsan</strong> and will earn the hourly minimum wage of 10,030 won, approximately $7.60.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">For the first time, a local government is directly participating in the recruitment process and establishing training facilities in <strong>Uzbekistan</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">South Korea has previously recruited workers from countries including <strong>Nepal</strong>, the <strong>Philippines</strong>, <strong>Indonesia</strong>, and <strong>Thailand</strong>.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>---</p>\n\n<p>-------------------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
