<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>TT Club</strong> and <strong>BSI Consulting</strong> have released their 2025 Cargo Theft Report, highlighting a sharp escalation in cargo crime worldwide. The report points to a more complex and adaptive threat landscape, with criminal networks increasingly leveraging technology and adjusting tactics in response to changing economic conditions. <strong>Brazil, Mexico, India, the United States, Indonesia, Chile, China, Germany and South Africa</strong> were identified as having the highest cargo theft incident volumes.</p><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Food and beverage</strong> cargo remained the most targeted commodity category, followed by agriculture, electronics and building materials. <strong>Trucks</strong> continued to account for around <strong>70 per cent</strong> of global incidents, while <strong>22 per cent</strong> involved insider theft. In North America, rail-related theft rose from <strong>4 per cent</strong> in 2024 to <strong>10 per cent</strong> in 2025, with organised groups increasingly targeting containers in transit. In Europe, Germany, Italy, the United Kingdom, France and Spain saw the highest volumes.</p><p style="text-align: justify">In the UK, losses reached <strong>$149 million</strong> in 2024, with a <strong>$9 million</strong> smartphone theft at Heathrow Airport among the year's headline incidents. Maritime risks also intensified, with <strong>sea piracy</strong> increasing <strong>85 per cent</strong> in the first half of 2025. <strong>Mike Yarwood</strong>, Managing Director of Loss Prevention at TT Club, emphasised that as criminal groups evolve their tactics the industry must shift toward proactive risk management. <strong>Jim Yarbrough</strong>, Global Supply Chain Solutions Director for BSI Consulting, added that the data reinforces the need for commodity-specific risk assessment, enhanced <strong>GPS tracking</strong> and cyber resilience.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. The TT Club / BSI Consulting 2025 Cargo Theft Report documents a sharp surge in global cargo crime.<br>
2. Food and beverage is the most-targeted category, followed by agriculture, electronics and building materials.<br>
3. In North America, rail-related theft rose from 4% in 2024 to 10% in 2025.<br>
4. UK losses hit $149M in 2024, including a $9M smartphone theft at Heathrow Airport.<br>
5. Sea piracy jumped 85% in the first half of 2025, adding a fresh risk vector for shippers.</p>
