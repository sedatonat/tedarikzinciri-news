<p style="text-align: justify">Container shipping operator <strong>CMA CGM</strong> and infrastructure investment firm <strong>Stonepeak</strong> have announced a joint terminal agreement covering a portfolio of ports operated by <strong>CMA CGM</strong> globally. According to a statement from the two companies dated January 28, under the joint venture branded <strong>United Ports</strong>, <strong>Stonepeak</strong> will invest 2.4 billion dollars for a 25% minority stake in 10 port terminals operated by <strong>CMA CGM</strong>. The deal coincides with a period in which carrier-investor partnerships are re-emerging as a defining trend in global port value chains, underscoring <strong>Stonepeak</strong>'s appetite for large-scale port portfolios. From a supply chain perspective, this structure emerges as a hybrid model in which the line operator retains full operational control while unlocking capital.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Terminals included in the agreement comprise <strong>Fenix Marine Services</strong> in <strong>Los Angeles</strong>; <strong>Port Liberty</strong> terminals in <strong>New York</strong> and <strong>New Jersey</strong>; <strong>Santos</strong> terminals in <strong>Brazil</strong>; and <strong>Nhava Sheva Freeport Terminal</strong> in <strong>India</strong>. The portfolio also encompasses additional terminals in <strong>Taiwan</strong>, <strong>Vietnam</strong>, and <strong>Spain</strong>. This geographic distribution constitutes a strategic clustering that consolidates hub nodes across <strong>Trans-Pacific</strong>, <strong>Trans-Atlantic</strong>, <strong>Asia-Europe</strong>, and <strong>Latin America</strong> routes within a single investment basket. <strong>Nhava Sheva</strong>'s position in the Indian subcontinent and <strong>Santos</strong>'s role in <strong>Brazil</strong>'s agricultural export chain demonstrate that the portfolio is balanced across both north-south and east-west corridors.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>James Wyper</strong>, a senior executive at <strong>Stonepeak</strong>, characterizes the joint venture as <strong>"a truly differentiated opportunity to invest in a high-quality portfolio of strategically located terminals"</strong> and underscores the significance of investing alongside one of the world's largest and most respected shipping and logistics groups. <strong>CMA CGM</strong> retains full operational control of its terminals under the agreement and plans to redeploy the 2.4 billion dollars received from <strong>Stonepeak</strong> to expand supply chain capacity and broaden core business operations. In this context, the investment functions as a leverage instrument that expands the line operator's financial base while preserving terminal autonomy.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The deal structure grants <strong>Stonepeak</strong> the option to allocate an additional 3.6 billion dollars toward future joint terminal projects. This option channel signals the transformation of the <strong>United Ports</strong> brand into a platform through which new acquisitions or greenfield terminal investments can be pursued in the future. The transaction is expected to be finalized in the second half of 2026, once regulatory approvals are completed. From a supply chain perspective, the <strong>CMA CGM</strong>-<strong>Stonepeak</strong> structure becomes a critical link in a growing trend whereby line operators share port assets with infrastructure funds to enhance capital efficiency, offering an alternative approach to the <strong>Maersk</strong>-<strong>APMT</strong> and <strong>MSC</strong>-<strong>TIL</strong> models.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Highlights:</strong><br>

5. The transaction is expected to be finalized in the second half of 2026.</p>
