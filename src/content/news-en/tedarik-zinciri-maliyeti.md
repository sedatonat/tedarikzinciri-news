<p style="text-align: justify">Today's supply chains face significant challenges due to increasing complexity and fragmented systems. A 2025 study conducted by <strong>FourKites</strong> reveals that 75% of supply chain leaders require 3 to 10 different systems to make critical decisions, and 58% of billion-dollar companies suffer from critical blind spots.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Control Tower Effectiveness and Visibility Gaps</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the research, only 22% of control towers are able to take meaningful action. This situation indicates that the majority of supply chain managers lack sufficient visibility into operational processes. Visibility deficiencies lead to serious disruptions in supply chain management, reducing efficiency and increasing costs.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Challenges Caused by System Fragmentation</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Fragmented systems disrupt information flow in supply chain processes. This slows down decision-making and makes it prone to errors. In particular, the lack of integration between different systems results in inconsistent data and operational inefficiencies.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Importance of Integration and Its Barriers</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the <strong>Global supply chain governance</strong> article, both internal and external integration enhance the effectiveness of supply chain management. Internal integration refers to coordination between departments within a firm, while external integration encompasses collaboration with external partners. However, barriers such as information gaps, communication breakdowns, and insufficient top management guidance complicate integration efforts.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Leaders' Approaches to System Fragmentation</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The research indicates that leaders adopt different strategies to address system fragmentation. Some invest in new technologies to integrate their existing systems, while others restructure their processes to adopt a more unified approach. The common goal of these strategies is to increase overall supply chain visibility and ensure operational efficiency.<a href="https://www.supplychainbrain.com/articles/41145-supply-chain-breaking-point-the-true-cost-of-fragmented-systems" rel="noopener" target="_blank">supplychainbrain.com</a></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Cost of Visibility Deficiencies</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Critical visibility gaps cause unexpected disruptions in supply chain operations. These disruptions negatively impact customer satisfaction and can lead to revenue loss. Additionally, they can increase operational costs and result in the loss of competitive advantage.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Advantages Provided by Integrated Systems</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Integrated systems provide real-time data flow in supply chain processes, accelerating decision-making and increasing its accuracy. Through this, inventory management is optimized, transportation costs are reduced, and customer demands can be met more quickly. Furthermore, integrated systems enhance transparency throughout the supply chain, enabling the early identification of potential risks.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Role of Technology Investments</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Leading companies invest in integrated software solutions such as <strong>ERP</strong> and <strong>SCM</strong> to improve supply chain processes. These technologies enable data to be collected and analyzed on a centralized platform, increasing operational efficiency. Additionally, advanced technologies such as <strong>IoT</strong> and <strong>artificial intelligence</strong> enhance automation and forecasting capabilities in supply chain processes, contributing to proactive decision-making.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Importance of the Human Factor</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In addition to technology investments, the human factor plays a critical role in supply chain management. Trained and experienced personnel ensure effective use of technology, contributing to the smooth operation of processes. Furthermore, continuous training and development of employees facilitate rapid adaptation to changing market conditions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Fragmented systems create serious challenges and costs in supply chain management. To overcome these challenges, companies must invest in integrated systems, restructure their processes, and continuously train their employees. These steps will increase the overall efficiency of the supply chain and help provide a competitive advantage.</p>
