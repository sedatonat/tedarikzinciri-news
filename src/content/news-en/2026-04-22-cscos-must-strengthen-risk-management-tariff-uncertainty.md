<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> reports; <strong>SCB Think Tank</strong>; analyst insight; chief supply chain officers (<strong>CSCOs</strong>); are becoming accustomed to uncertainty — and 2025 is expected to be no different — especially as companies brace for tariff unpredictability and potential market fluctuations. In this climate; <strong>CSCOs</strong> must find effective ways to enhance organizational responsiveness and, where possible, gain competitive advantage in the shifting trade environment. A strong risk management strategy is critical to have in place. Yet confusion over the meaning of "resilience" is creating a challenge for many organizations. In a recent <strong>Gartner</strong> survey; 82% of supply chain leaders indicated that resources allocated for proactive risk mitigation — including resilience — are being directed to the wrong risks. This misallocation is associated with an 18% decline in profitability according to survey respondents.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective; <strong>Gartner Inc.</strong> is a leading global IT and supply chain research and consulting firm based in <strong>Stamford, Connecticut</strong>, founded in 1979, with <strong>Gene Hall</strong> as CEO. <strong>Gartner Supply Chain</strong> is led by <strong>VP Analyst Simon Bailey</strong> along with key analysts <strong>Tom Enright</strong>; <strong>Mike Burkett</strong>; and <strong>Suzie Petrusic</strong>. Key annual supply chain reports include <strong>Gartner Top 25 Supply Chains</strong>; <strong>Gartner Magic Quadrant</strong>; and <strong>Gartner CSCO Survey</strong>. Main competitors to <strong>Gartner</strong> include <strong>Forrester Research</strong> (CEO <strong>George Colony</strong>); <strong>IDC</strong> (<strong>Crawford Del Prete</strong> President); <strong>451 Research</strong> (owned by <strong>S&P Global</strong>); <strong>Frost & Sullivan</strong>; <strong>Everest Group</strong>; <strong>HfS Research</strong>; and <strong>Constellation Research</strong>. Leading academic resources for CSCOs include the <strong>MIT Center for Transportation & Logistics</strong> (directed by <strong>Yossi Sheffi</strong>); <strong>Stanford Global Supply Chain Management Forum</strong>; <strong>Michigan State Eli Broad</strong> School; <strong>Penn State Smeal</strong> College; and <strong>University of Tennessee Haslam</strong> Business School.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective; leading <strong>supply chain risk management</strong> (<strong>SCRM</strong>) software providers include <strong>Resilinc</strong> (CEO <strong>Bindiya Vakil</strong>; based in <strong>Milpitas, California</strong>); <strong>Everstream Analytics</strong> (CEO <strong>Julie Gerdeman</strong>; based in <strong>San Marcos, California</strong>); <strong>riskmethods</strong> (owned by <strong>Sphera</strong> as of 2023); <strong>Interos</strong> (CEO <strong>Jennifer Bisceglie</strong>; based in <strong>Arlington, Virginia</strong>); <strong>Riskonnect</strong>; <strong>Achilles</strong>; <strong>SAP Ariba Risk Management</strong>; <strong>Coupa Risk Aware</strong>; <strong>Jaggaer ESM</strong>; <strong>Ivalua Supplier Risk</strong>; <strong>Project44</strong>; and <strong>FourKites</strong>. Leading <strong>tariff management</strong> and <strong>trade compliance</strong> software providers include <strong>Thomson Reuters ONESOURCE</strong>; <strong>Descartes Systems</strong> (CEO <strong>Edward Ryan</strong>; based in <strong>Toronto</strong>); <strong>QAD Precision</strong> (owned by <strong>QAD</strong>; formerly <strong>Precision Software</strong>); <strong>Avalara</strong>; <strong>Amber Road</strong> (owned by <strong>E2open</strong>); <strong>Integration Point</strong>; <strong>Livingston International</strong>; <strong>Expeditors</strong>; and <strong>Flexport</strong>. Key U.S. trade authorities include <strong>U.S. Customs and Border Protection</strong> (<strong>CBP</strong>); and <strong>U.S. Trade Representative</strong> (<strong>USTR</strong>; <strong>Jamieson Greer</strong> for Trump 2.0).</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective; the primary <strong>CSCO</strong> risk management priorities for 2025 include: (1) <strong>Trump</strong> tariffs (China; Mexico; Canada; EU; sector-specific); (2) <strong>USMCA</strong> 2026 review uncertainty; (3) <strong>Red Sea</strong> and <strong>Suez Canal</strong> disruptions (<strong>Houthi</strong> attacks); (4) <strong>Panama Canal</strong> drought and ownership questions (<strong>BlackRock</strong> involvement); (5) <strong>Taiwan Strait</strong> geopolitical tensions; (6) protracted <strong>Russia-Ukraine</strong> war; (7) climate and natural disasters; (8) cybersecurity (<strong>NotPetya</strong>; <strong>SolarWinds</strong>; <strong>Colonial Pipeline</strong>); (9) <strong>HPAI</strong> and food safety; and (10) talent and workforce challenges. Leading <strong>SCRM</strong> frameworks include <strong>ISO 31000</strong> (Risk Management); <strong>NIST SP 800-161</strong> (Supply Chain Risk Management); <strong>SCOR Model</strong> (<strong>Supply Chain Operations Reference</strong>; governed by <strong>ASCM</strong>); and the <strong>Supply Chain Risk Management Consortium</strong> (<strong>SCRMC Council</strong>). Key resilience design strategies include: (1) <strong>multi-sourcing</strong>; (2) <strong>nearshoring</strong>/<strong>friend-shoring</strong>; (3) <strong>strategic stockpiling</strong>; (4) <strong>visibility</strong> (<strong>SCCT</strong>; <strong>n-tier</strong>); (5) <strong>scenario planning</strong> (conducted by firms such as <strong>FTI Consulting</strong>; <strong>Kearney</strong>; and <strong>BCG</strong>); (6) <strong>digital twins</strong>; and (7) <strong>AI risk monitoring</strong>. In conclusion; <strong>SCB Think Tank</strong>'s advocacy for <strong>CSCO</strong> risk management is focused on the accelerating global uncertainty stemming from <strong>Trump</strong> 2.0 tariff uncertainty — with <strong>multi-sourcing</strong>; <strong>nearshoring</strong>; <strong>visibility</strong>; and <strong>scenario planning</strong> emerging as key strategic priorities for supply chain managers.</p>
