<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CMA CGM</strong> has announced the launch of a new <strong>barge transportation corridor</strong> between <strong>Que Vo and Haiphong</strong> in Northern Vietnam in response to growing production volumes and export demand to the U.S. market. This new service strengthens the company's barge network in the region while aiming to connect strategic industrial zones in northern Vietnam more directly to critical U.S. ports and <strong>Inland Point Intermodal (IPI)</strong> facilities.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new corridor integrates major industrial clusters such as <strong>Bac Ninh, Hanoi and Phu Tho</strong> with Haiphong Port, offering manufacturers a <strong>faster, lower-cost and lower-emission</strong> logistics option. Northern Vietnam has become one of the critical links in global supply chains for electronics, textiles, automotive parts and consumer goods in recent years, making this connection strategically vital for exports to the U.S. market.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This barge operation works in full integration with three major CMA CGM U.S. services departing from Haiphong:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>EXX (Expedited Express)</strong> – the company's premium express transit service,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CBX</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Pearl</strong> services.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Through this integration, the barge line accelerates inland-to-port connectivity, optimizing total transit time to the U.S. The <strong>two-day barge transit</strong> creates significant advantages in terms of both timing and cost within the supply chain. Additionally, the corridor's <strong>bi-directional (export–import) and bi-weekly</strong> regular operation model provides stable capacity and predictability.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This new service responds to both global carbon reduction trends and customer sustainability demands. Barge transportation delivers <strong>lower fuel consumption</strong>, <strong>reduced air pollution</strong> and <strong>lower CO₂ emissions</strong> compared to road transportation. By expanding its global barge network, CMA CGM aims to strengthen not only cost advantages but also investments in <strong>green logistics</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a U.S. market access perspective, the Que Vo barge line brings production regions in northern Vietnam closer to critical gateways on the U.S. West and East Coasts. This enables manufacturers to ship with more competitive delivery times than ever before. Improved capacity utilization is expected, particularly on shipments to <strong>California, Pacific Northwest, Gulf Coast and East Coast</strong> ports in the U.S.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In the global trade environment, as geopolitical pressures, freight rate volatility and cost increases persist, major carriers' integration of deep-sea and coastal shipping (barge plus deep-sea shipping) is becoming increasingly strategic. CMA CGM's move in Northern Vietnam creates <strong>more predictable, cost-effective and flexible</strong> options for manufacturers, exporters and U.S. importers alike.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new Que Vo–Haiphong corridor is poised to balance regional cargo flows while strengthening Vietnam's position in the U.S. market. When the two-day fast barge transit is combined with premium services like EXX, products shipped from Vietnam are expected to see marked improvements in delivery times.</p>
<p><br></p>
