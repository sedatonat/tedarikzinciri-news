<p style="text-align: justify">A quota for <strong>paddy rice export</strong> will be in force in the <strong>Russian Federation</strong> from <strong>January 1 to December 31, 2026</strong>. The governmental decree was signed on <strong>December 19, 2025</strong>. The quota reflects an important shift in the Russian government's agricultural export policy.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The quota amounts to <strong>200 thousand tons a year</strong>. <strong>Exports within the quota limits will be subject to a zero tax</strong>, while those <strong>exceeding it will be subject to a tax equivalent to 50 per cent of the customs value</strong>. The decision is expected to stimulate rice growing in Russia and exports to new markets.</p><p style="text-align: justify">At present, a temporary ban on paddy rice export is in force in Russia. The <strong>ban has been in force since July 2022</strong> and will remain <strong>until December 31, 2025</strong>. The new quota that will replace the ban provides a controlled transition. The 2022 ban was introduced to stabilise rice prices in the Russian domestic market and secure domestic consumption needs. The new quota offers the sector limited access to overseas markets while preserving domestic stock security. Krasnodar Krai, Russia's largest rice-producing region, anticipates increasing export opportunities. Türkiye and the Central Asian republics are among the main buyers of Russian rice.</p>

<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>

4. The quota replaces a ban in force since July 2022.<br>
5. Krasnodar Krai — Russia's largest rice-producing region — will benefit from the opportunity.</p>
