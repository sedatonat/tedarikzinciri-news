<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Hapag-Lloyd</strong> won the second zero-emission container shipping tender organised by <strong>ZEMBA (Zero Emission Maritime Buyers Alliance)</strong>. Under the agreement, Hapag-Lloyd will provide service on a transoceanic trade lane with large dual-fuel container vessels using hydrogen-derived <strong>e-methanol</strong> from <strong>2027 onwards</strong>. The contract will run for at least 3 years and will deliver a total of <strong>120,000 tonnes of CO2e</strong> emissions abatement.</p><p style="text-align: justify">ZEMBA is a buyer alliance established to catalyse zero-emission maritime transport, bringing together <strong>more than 45 global cargo owners</strong> including <strong>Amazon</strong>, <strong>IKEA</strong>, and <strong>Nike</strong>. The alliance aims to direct shipowners toward green fuel investments through a demand-based approach. The e-methanol that Hapag-Lloyd will use will be produced from renewable-energy-based hydrogen and captured carbon, offering <strong>a 90 per cent reduction in lifecycle emissions compared to fossil fuels</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">Hapag-Lloyd executives emphasised that winning the ZEMBA tender is a critical step on the company's path to its <strong>2045 net-zero</strong> target. The company has already ordered 24 methanol dual-fuel 23,500 TEU vessels, and these vessels will be deployed in the ZEMBA service. The agreement is regarded as a significant example showing how direct demand from cargo owners can mobilise green maritime fuel supply, beyond regulatory incentives.</p>
