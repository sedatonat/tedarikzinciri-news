<p style="text-align: justify">According to the Panama Canal Authority, the canal posted higher traffic and cargo volumes in the first half of fiscal 2026, with stronger container, LPG and energy-product flows driving demand for transit reservations. The canal handled 6,288 transits from October 2025 through March 2026, up 224 year on year, while cargo volumes rose to 254m PC/UMS tons from 243m tons a year earlier.</p>

<p style="text-align: justify">Daily traffic averaged 34 vessels in January and 37 in March, with peak days exceeding 40 transits. The waterway said it remains open and fully operational, with water levels at optimal levels. Reservation demand has tightened, with some ships paying more than $1 million in recent auctions.</p>

<p style="text-align: justify">The authority said these spikes are temporary and demand-driven rather than a sign of sustained congestion. Average auction prices climbed to about $385,000 between March and April, from roughly $135,000 to $140,000 before the Middle East conflict. Most vessels continue to book transits in advance.</p>

2. Cargo volume rose from 243m to 254m PC/UMS tons.<br>
3. Daily traffic exceeded 40 vessels on peak days.<br>
4. Some auctions saw transit fees exceed $1 million.<br>
5. Average auction price climbed to about $385k, up from $135-140k before the war.</p>
