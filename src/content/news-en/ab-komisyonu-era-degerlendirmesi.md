<p style="text-align: justify">The European Commission has published its institutional assessment of the <strong>European Union Agency for Railways (ERA)</strong>. According to the report, ERA is viewed as a <strong>relevant, necessary, and strategic institution</strong> in the European railway sector. The agency's contributions to EU-wide <strong>interoperability</strong>, <strong>single railway market</strong>, and <strong>safety improvements</strong> are explicitly confirmed.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">However, the Commission emphasizes that ERA's current performance <strong>does not fully meet the EU's long-term expectations</strong>, particularly noting that <strong>the pace of harmonization has proven insufficient</strong>, with reforms progressing slowly due to certain national-level barriers.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>ERA's Strengths</strong></h4>\n<p style="text-align: justify">According to the Commission, ERA's most important contributions to the sector are:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Advancing common rules</strong> toward the Single European Railway Area (SERA) objective,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Creating a central framework for common safety methods (CSM) and safety certification processes,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Developing <strong>interoperability</strong> within ERTMS and technical specifications (TSI),</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Facilitating information sharing between national authorities,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Serving as a reference institution for accident investigations, safety culture, and risk-based regulation.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The Commission report notes that ERA's technical capacity and expert pool are strong. It also states that the expansion of the agency's authority with the Fourth Railway Package has provided <strong>a more consistent safety and certification regime across the EU</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Areas Where Expectations Were Not Met</strong></h4>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The Commission notes that ERA's results <strong>have not fully reached the expected impact level</strong>, with three key issues standing out:</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>1) National Implementation Barriers</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Although EU countries have adopted ERA standards, in the implementation process:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">national exemptions,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">bureaucratic differences,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">additional requirements in certification processes<br>\nare causing <strong>harmonization to slow down</strong>.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">ERA should assume a more proactive "effective governance" role in reducing these barriers.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>2) Slower-than-Expected Progress on Interoperability</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Notably:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">ERTMS field deployment,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">removal of national systems,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">TSI compliance rate<br>\nare not progressing at the desired pace.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">According to the Commission, "EU railways remain fragmented," and ERA needs stronger coordination mechanisms.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>3) Limited Impact on Market Dynamism</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The single European railway market has failed to reach the intended level of competition. The Commission states that ERA should more aggressively support:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">data transparency,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">process simplification,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">barrier reduction to entry<br></p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: 'Positive but Needs to Accelerate'</strong></h4>\n<p style="text-align: justify">While the EU Commission assessment confirms ERA's importance, it notes that the institution is "<strong>progressing in the right direction but moving slowly</strong>." For ERA to achieve its 2030 targets:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">stronger binding cooperation with national authorities,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">tighter monitoring mechanisms,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">data-driven policy development,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">accelerated programs in ERTMS and TSI implementation<br>\nare needed.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">ERA's future performance is stated to directly affect the speed of European railway market integration.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h4>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The EU Commission rated ERA as a "relevant and necessary" institution.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">However, progress on <strong>interoperability and single market</strong> objectives lags expectations.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">National implementation differences are slowing the process.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">A stronger coordination role from ERA in ERTMS and TSI work is expected.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Accelerating the agency's performance is critical for the EU's 2030 railway targets.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>\n<p>----------</p>\n\n<p>--------------------</p>\n<p><strong>Author: </strong><a href="http://sedatonat.com/"><u>SedatOnat.com</u></a></p>\n<p>--------------------</p>\n\n<p>We would be pleased to receive your feedback.</p>\n<p>Wishing you happy reading.</p>\n
