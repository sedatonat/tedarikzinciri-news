<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Louisiana</strong>, the historic center of American shipbuilding, is reviving its maritime legacy while opening a new chapter in global competition through <strong>artificial intelligence-powered autonomous vessels</strong> and <strong>advanced manufacturing</strong>. <strong>Greater New Orleans, Inc.</strong> President <strong>Michael Hecht</strong> describes this transformation as "<strong>where Louisiana's history and future converge</strong>".</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Hecht emphasizes that projects across the state represent not merely economic recovery but <strong>an integrated transformation spanning defense, aerospace, and energy sectors</strong>. For instance, the partnership between <strong>Metal Shark</strong> (Jeanerette, Louisiana) and <strong>HavocAI</strong> (Rhode Island) manufactures <strong>AI-integrated unmanned surface vehicles for the U.S. Navy</strong>. According to Hecht, these projects demonstrate that "Louisiana is reasserting itself as a center for defense technology."</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Next-generation icebreakers and port investments</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">One of the most notable developments is <strong>Houma-based Bollinger Shipyards'</strong> contract to <strong>construct four new Arctic icebreakers for the U.S. Coast Guard</strong>. Announced on October 10, this <strong>6 billion-dollar project</strong> is part of the <strong>Arctic Security Cutter program involving 11 vessels under U.S.–Finland partnership</strong>. Hecht notes the project will create "<strong>up to 600 local jobs</strong>" and "<strong>strengthen NATO's Arctic defense capacity</strong>".</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In parallel, <strong>St. Bernard Parish</strong> is constructing a <strong>1.8 billion-dollar new container terminal</strong>. This terminal will allow <strong>neo-post-Panamax</strong> vessels that cannot fit under bridges to pass directly into the New Orleans River sub-region.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>National maritime strategy: Maritime Action Plan (MAP)</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Louisiana's developments are being evaluated within the framework of <strong>the "Maritime Action Plan (MAP)" initiated by executive order on April 9, 2025 by the Trump administration</strong>. <strong>The plan, expected to be completed by November 5, 2025</strong>, aims to revive the U.S. <strong>maritime share from 5% of global ship production in the 1970s to today's 0.2%</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">White House objectives include:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Making U.S. flag vessels and U.S.-built ships competitive in international markets</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Rebuilding maritime industry production infrastructure</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Developing and preserving skilled maritime workforce</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Establishing permanent federal funding mechanisms</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Within this strategy, <strong>new port fees are being applied to Chinese-built and foreign-operated vessels entering U.S. ports</strong>. China has countered with similar tariffs. <strong>Atlantic Container Line</strong> will be affected to the tune of <strong>34 million dollars annually</strong> from these fees.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Transformation focused on advanced technology</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Hecht, the U.S. maritime industry can regain competitiveness not through massive container vessels but through <strong>producing mid-sized, high-technology vessels</strong>:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"America's advantage lies in small but technologically advanced vessels. Competing with China, South Korea, and Japan in giant ships is not realistic," Hecht states.</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Thus <strong>Louisiana's future</strong> is being shaped around <strong>autonomous systems, military vessels, and platforms serving the aerospace sector</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>New investments: Saronic and Port Alpha</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">In April, <strong>Texas-based Saronic</strong> announced a <strong>250 million-dollar investment plan</strong> after acquiring the historic shipyard in <strong>Franklin, Louisiana</strong>. The company plans to manufacture <strong>autonomous surface vehicles ("water drones") for military and commercial clients</strong>. This investment will create <strong>500 new jobs</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Saronic's long-term goal is to establish <strong>Port Alpha</strong>, a <strong>"next-generation autonomous vessel manufacturing hub"</strong> to begin construction in 2026. This facility, with multi-billion dollar investment and <strong>serial production capacity</strong>, is expected to give the U.S. competitive advantage in the global autonomous surface vehicle market.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Shipyards serving the aerospace industry</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Louisiana shipyards are now operating not only in maritime but also in <strong>space logistics</strong>. <strong>Bollinger Shipyards</strong> is producing a <strong>rocket landing platform for Rocket Lab</strong> and collaborating on joint projects with firms like <strong>United Launch Alliance</strong> and <strong>Space Perspective</strong>. Hecht describes this transformation as "<strong>industrial evolution from ship to spacecraft</strong>".</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Historical continuity and innovation</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">During World War II, Louisiana left its mark on maritime history through <strong>Higgins Industries' landing craft</strong>, followed later by <strong>Textron's hovercraft projects</strong>. According to Hecht, today's technology investments represent a continuation of this legacy:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"The Louisiana shipbuilding industry has always been defined by innovation. Today, this innovation is occurring faster and more comprehensively than before."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Louisiana</strong> is experiencing <strong>technology-driven rebirth in shipbuilding</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Metal Shark</strong> and <strong>HavocAI</strong> are developing <strong>autonomous AI-powered vessels for the U.S. Navy</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Bollinger Shipyards</strong> will create 600 jobs through its <strong>6 billion-dollar icebreaker</strong> project.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Saronic</strong> is targeting <strong>autonomous vessel production</strong> with a <strong>250 million-dollar investment</strong> and the <strong>Port Alpha</strong> project.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Maritime Action Plan</strong> aims to <strong>rebuild U.S. maritime production capacity</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Louisiana shipyards now serve <strong>defense, energy, and aerospace sectors</strong>.</p></li>
