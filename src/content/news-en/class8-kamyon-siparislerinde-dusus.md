<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Class 8 truck orders</strong>, a leading indicator of the U.S. heavy commercial vehicle market, continued their downward trajectory in November 2025. According to preliminary data released by <strong>FTR</strong> and <strong>ACT Research</strong>, November net orders showed pronounced contraction on both a monthly and year-over-year basis, signaling a weak start to 2026 for the sector.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>According to FTR data, November Class 8 net orders totaled 20,200 units</strong>. This figure represents:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>a 17% monthly decline</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>a 44% year-over-year decline</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, November's order level fell well short of the <strong>10-year average of 28,910 units</strong>. Based on FTR's 12-month rolling order calculation, the industry posted <strong>214,797 Class 8 orders</strong> over the year—a figure representing one of the weakest trends in recent years.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This decline follows a brief positive movement in October. While October orders of <strong>24,300 units</strong> showed an 18% month-over-month increase, they fell <strong>22% year-over-year</strong>—marking the <strong>tenth consecutive year-over-year decline</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">FTR's analysis points to several key drivers of November's weakness:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Weak freight demand</strong> and depressed spot rates,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Fleets postponing <strong>replacement</strong> and <strong>expansion</strong> plans,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Persistent excess capacity</strong> pressures,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Elevated <strong>financing</strong> and <strong>equipment costs</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Tariff volatility,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>EPA'27 NOx rule</strong> uncertainty,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Weak <strong>carrier profitability</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Both <strong>vocational</strong> and <strong>on-highway</strong> segments declined on monthly and year-over-year bases, though the vocational segment showed relatively better performance. This underscores that a "cautious demand" outlook remains in effect as 2026 begins.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">FTR notes that risks are mounting, particularly for the <strong>2026 order cycle</strong>. Cumulative net orders for September through November are down <strong>36% year-over-year</strong>. Nevertheless, FTR identifies two positive factors that could lend modest support:</p>
<ol>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Greater clarity on tariff frameworks:</strong><br>
New tariff structures have increased costs but in a "measured and targeted" manner, creating room for supply chain diversification and reshoring.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Removal of EPA'27 extended warranty requirements delivering meaningful cost relief:</strong><br>
Where NOx rules were previously expected to lift total equipment costs by nearly 100%, elimination of extended warranty requirements could cut this cost increase in half.</p></li>
</ol>
<p style="text-align: justify">Despite these two positive developments, FTR Senior Analyst <strong>Dan Moyer</strong> emphasizes that fleet investment decisions remain under pressure:<br>
"<strong>Improved clarity has not been enough to offset weak freight fundamentals, limited profitability, elevated capital costs…</strong>"<br>
According to Moyer, fleets are redirecting focus toward <strong>cost control</strong>, <strong>maintenance discipline</strong>, and <strong>asset utilization</strong> priorities, which is delaying meaningful equipment demand recovery.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>ACT Research data</strong> presents a similar picture. According to ACT, November Class 8 net orders came in at <strong>19,700 units</strong>, representing a year-over-year decline of <strong>4.7%</strong>. November is typically considered the third-strongest order month of the year; thus, this decline reflects performance below expectations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">ACT Research Analyst <strong>Carter Vieth</strong> identifies the core issue as fleets' lack of <strong>profitability</strong>:</p>
<p><br>
"<strong>Spot rates continue to tread along the bottom… demand in key freight sectors is lagging.</strong>"</p>
<p><br>
According to Vieth, while reduced EPA'27 uncertainty is a positive step, strong order momentum should not be expected until underlying economic fundamentals improve.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In summary, November 2025 data reveals that the Class 8 truck market remains under pressure, and the 2026 order cycle faces a challenging outlook. Despite tariff and regulatory clarity, the market is unlikely to see strong order recovery without improvements in <strong>freight demand</strong>, <strong>pricing power</strong>, and <strong>fleet profitability</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">November 2025 Class 8 orders fell <strong>44% year-over-year</strong> and <strong>17% monthly</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">12-month rolling total: <strong>214,797 units</strong> (weak trend).</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Fleets are prioritizing <strong>cost control</strong> and <strong>asset utilization</strong> over investment.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">2026 order cycle at risk; September–November cumulative orders down <strong>36% year-over-year</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">EPA'27 uncertainty declining while freight demand remains severely weak.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">ACT data similarly points to decline (<strong>19,700 units</strong>, year-over-year –4.7%).</p></li>
</ul>
