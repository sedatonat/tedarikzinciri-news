<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CentrePort</strong>, the New Zealand port operator, has delivered the first major operational application of its private <strong>5G</strong> network rolled out late last year. The port's digital and engineering teams installed <strong>5G-enabled Peplink modems</strong> on <strong>reach stackers</strong>, allowing operators to connect directly to terminal systems. The deployment is one of the most ambitious private 5G integration steps in the country.</p><p style="text-align: justify">CentrePort CEO <strong>Anthony Delaney</strong> said that the team previously had to rely heavily on radio traffic to coordinate container movements, which complicated safety-critical communications. With the new setup, operators can access CentrePort systems through on-board tablets, freeing radios for essential safety-critical communications. Early performance testing has indicated stable connectivity with no reported dropouts and clear gains in operational efficiency.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The initial deployment included high-power 5G radio transceivers and antennas mounted on existing port masts. The next phase will expand the network across additional port assets. CentrePort is also preparing a trial integrating <strong>5G connectivity with its CCTV infrastructure</strong>, aimed at giving operators broader situational awareness. CentrePort expects the private 5G network to serve as a scalable backbone for future RTG telemetry, smart sensors and advanced telematics.</p>
