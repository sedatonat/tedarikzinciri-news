<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Canada's economy</strong> experienced significant slowdown in the third quarter due to <strong>weakening consumer spending</strong> and the effects of <strong>ongoing trade tensions with the U.S.</strong> According to <strong>Statistics Canada</strong> data, retail sales <strong>rose just 0.2% in the July–September period</strong>. This rate fell below the 0.3% increase in the second quarter, marking the <strong>weakest retail performance in the past year</strong>.</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sharp Monthly Sales Decline</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">September sales <strong>fell 0.7%</strong>, matching economist expectations. <strong>Preliminary estimates for October</strong> show sales were <strong>flat</strong>. This picture reveals that consumers are <strong>exercising caution due to high prices, trade uncertainty, and the impact of tariffs</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tariff Effects Growing in Automotive Sector</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">U.S. tariffs have particularly triggered <strong>sharp contraction in the automotive sector</strong>:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Motor vehicle sales <strong>fell 2.9% in September</strong> — the first decline in three months.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Sales at new vehicle dealerships declined noticeably.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">By contrast, automotive sales rose <strong>7.4% in the first nine months of the year</strong> — economists attribute this gain to <strong>consumers rushing to purchase vehicles before tariffs worsen further</strong>.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Bank surveys show that Canadians <strong>firmly believe tariffs will drive up vehicle prices</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Mixed Picture in Core Retail Sales</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Outside of automotive, the picture is more balanced:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">In September, <strong>non-automotive sales rose 0.2%</strong> — better than economist expectations of a 0.5% decline.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">However, <strong>core retail sales</strong> (excluding vehicles and gasoline) show notable weakness.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Largest declines:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Building materials and garden equipment stores: –2%</strong> (third consecutive decline)</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">General merchandise stores experienced mild contraction</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Largest gains:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Food and beverage retailers: +0.8%<br>Beer, wine, and liquor stores showed strong growth<br>Supermarkets and discount stores also rose</strong></p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Provincial Sales Declined</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In September, six of ten provinces saw sales decline. The sharpest drops:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ontario: –1.2%</strong> (Toronto –2.3%)</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>British Columbia: –0.9%</strong> (Vancouver –1.0%)</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">With Ontario serving as Canada's <strong>manufacturing hub</strong>, this decline could have broader economic implications.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Slowing Population Growth Dampening Consumption</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In recent years, <strong>rapid population growth</strong> was the key driver behind Canada's robust economic expansion. However, the federal government's new <strong>immigration restrictions</strong> have dramatically slowed population growth.</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">This is accelerating <strong>weakness in household spending</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Central Bank: No Further Rate Cuts Expected</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Bank of Canada (BoC)</strong> is now in a <strong>wait-and-see</strong> mode after reducing its policy rate to <strong>2.25%</strong>:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The bank says the current rate level is in the "<strong>appropriate range</strong>".</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Economic slowdown and weak consumption reduce the need for more aggressive rate cuts.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The BoC expects third-quarter growth to materialize at only around <strong>0.4% on an annualized basis</strong>.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Economic Outlook Remains Uncertain</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Charles St-Arnaud, chief economist at Alberta Central</strong>, noted that weak retail performance will <strong>contribute only modestly to GDP</strong>, stating:</p>\n<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"In terms of the economic outlook, the critical factor will be the resilience of the labour market."</blockquote>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Shelly Kaushik</strong> from Bank of Montreal sums up the situation as follows:</p>\n<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"While low interest rate support persists, trade uncertainty is weighing on the retail sector. The economy is moving along a path of modest growth."</blockquote>\n<blockquote><br></blockquote>\n<h5><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li><p>Canadian retail sales at <strong>Q3 +0.2%</strong> mark the slowest pace in a year.</p></li>\n  <li><p>U.S. tariffs hit <strong>automotive sales hard</strong> (September –2.9%).</p></li>\n  <li><p>Core retail showed limited growth, buoyed by food &amp; beverage categories.</p></li>\n  <li><p>A dramatic slowdown in population growth is creating a drag on consumption.</p></li>\n  <li><p>BoC cut rates to <strong>2.25%</strong> and is now in holding mode.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Economists expect <strong>low growth for the remainder of 2025</strong> and <strong>elevated uncertainty</strong>.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<h5><strong>Sources</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li><p>Bloomberg News — <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/" rel="noopener" target="_new">https://www.bloomberg.com/news/</a> (accessed November 21, 2025)</p></li>\n  <li><p>Statistics Canada — https://www150.statcan.gc.ca</p></li>\n  <li><p>Bank of Canada — <a href="https://www.bankofcanada.ca/" rel="noopener" target="_new">https://www.bankofcanada.ca</a></p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>----------</p>\n<p><strong>News Link: </strong><a href="https://www.supplychainbrain.com/articles/42887-canada-retail-sales-slow-in-third-quarter-as-tariffs-persist"><u>https://www.supplychainbrain.com/articles/42887-canada-retail-sales-slow-in-third-quarter-as-tariffs-persist</u></a></p>\n<p>--------------------</p>\n<p><strong>Author: </strong><a href="http://sedatonat.com/"><u>SedatOnat.com</u></a></p>\n<p>--------------------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
