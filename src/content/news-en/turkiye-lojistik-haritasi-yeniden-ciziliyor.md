<p>According to statements from Uraloğlu, who conducted inspections at the Kemalpaşa Logistics Center as part of the Izmir programme:</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Work is continuing on existing projects such as the Kemalpaşa Logistics Center, while studies, design, and planning phases are ongoing for new logistics centers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>The Kemalpaşa Logistics Center is expected to make significant contributions to Turkey's logistics infrastructure through its expansion potential, strategic location, and the advantages it offers. Additionally, this center and other projects are forecast to strengthen Turkey's competitive position in the logistics sector and enable it to play a more strategic role in international trade.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3>Key Points:</h3>
<ol>
  <li><p>Target to increase the number of logistics centers from 13 to 26 by 2053.</p></li>
  <li><p>Plan to raise rail transport's share from 5 percent to 22 percent.</p></li>
  <li><p>Intention to reduce road freight transport's share from 72 percent to 57 percent.</p></li>
</ol>
