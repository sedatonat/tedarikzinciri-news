<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Hasbro</strong> disclosed that it detected <strong>unauthorized access</strong> in its corporate IT environment on March 28, 2026, and reported the incident to the <strong>U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)</strong> via an <strong>8-K filing</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The company said it is working with <strong>third-party forensics firms</strong> to determine the scope of the incident and has isolated affected systems. Initial assessments indicate that manufacturing and distribution operations continue, though some back-office processes have experienced temporary disruption.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The incident has reignited debate over <strong>cyber resilience</strong> in supply chains across the <strong>toy and entertainment sector</strong>. Hasbro said it would share further findings on whether customer or partner data was impacted as the investigation progresses.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. Hasbro reported the March 28, 2026 attack to the SEC via an 8-K filing.<br>
2. Investigation continues with third-party forensics firms.<br>
3. Affected systems were isolated; manufacturing continues.<br>
4. Back-office processes experienced temporary disruption.<br>
5. A final assessment of customer data impact is pending.</p>
