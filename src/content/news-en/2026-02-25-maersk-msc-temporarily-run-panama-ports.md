<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>APM Terminals</strong> and <strong>Terminal Investment</strong> have been assigned to temporarily operate two contested ports situated at either end of the <strong>Panama Canal</strong> following the <strong>Panamanian</strong> government's cancellation of contracts held by Hong Kong-based <strong>CK Hutchison</strong>. The decision was announced shortly after the government ordered the <strong>Panama Maritime Authority</strong> to seize the ports on February 23. From a supply chain perspective, the temporary placement of two major container operators in terminal operations reduces near-term uncertainty in the call rotation schedules of <strong>Asia-Americas</strong> and <strong>transpacific</strong> service providers and supports rotation stability in service contract renewals. Simultaneously, <strong>Maersk</strong> and <strong>MSC</strong>'s terminal presence in the same geography creates a headline subject to scrutiny by competition authorities during a period when alliance structures have undergone significant transformation following the end of the <strong>2M</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">As reported by <strong>CNBC</strong>, <strong>Panama</strong> has selected <strong>Maersk</strong>'s <strong>APM Terminals</strong> unit to operate the <strong>Balboa</strong> port on the canal's <strong>Pacific</strong> side for the next 18 months. <strong>Mediterranean Shipping Co.</strong>'s <strong>Terminal Investment</strong> unit will operate the <strong>Cristobal</strong> port on the opposite side of the canal during the same period. This arrangement is designed to maintain uninterrupted vessel traffic while <strong>Panama</strong> conducts a tender process for new permanent contracts. From a supply chain perspective, the 18-month transition window represents the period of highest risk vulnerability in the process of transferring terminal operating systems (<strong>TOS</strong>), crane operation crews, and yard operations. Maintaining uninterrupted <strong>VLCS</strong> and <strong>Neo-Panamax</strong> vessel traffic emerges as a critical performance metric for line operators.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The step was taken following the <strong>Supreme Court of Panama</strong>'s decision to invalidate <strong>CK Hutchison</strong>'s long-term concession agreements. In the months leading to the decision, <strong>U.S. President Donald Trump</strong> frequently expressed concerns about <strong>CK Hutchison</strong>'s ties to <strong>China</strong> and argued that <strong>Beijing</strong>-linked influence at the <strong>Panama Canal</strong> posed a potential national security risk to <strong>U.S.</strong> commerce. <strong>Panamanian</strong> authorities, by temporarily assigning <strong>APM Terminals</strong> and <strong>Terminal Investment</strong>, aim to provide carriers and shippers with assurance that operations will remain stable while a longer-term concession process is conducted. From a supply chain perspective, this temporary structure serves as a critical operational insurance function for maintaining exports and <strong>reefer</strong> flows linked to the <strong>Colon Free Zone</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Meanwhile, <strong>CK Hutchison</strong> has characterized the seizure as "<strong>unlawful</strong>" and announced plans to initiate legal proceedings against the <strong>Supreme Court</strong> decision and <strong>Panama</strong>'s takeover of the ports. <strong>China</strong> has also ordered state-owned firms to cease negotiations on new projects in <strong>Panama</strong> and is encouraging carriers to reroute cargo to alternative ports. From a supply chain perspective, potential route changes by <strong>Chinese</strong> line operators and state-owned shippers represent a development that could increase volume performance at regional alternative transshipment hubs such as <strong>Buenaventura</strong>, <strong>Manzanillo</strong>, <strong>Cartagena</strong>, and <strong>Caucedo</strong>. Ultimately, the <strong>Panama Canal</strong> port structure is experiencing a multi-layered transformation dynamic in which geopolitical, regulatory, and operational layers are being simultaneously reshaped.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Points:</strong><br>
1. <strong>APM Terminals</strong> operating <strong>Balboa</strong> for 18 months.<br>
2. <strong>Terminal Investment</strong> operating <strong>Cristobal</strong> during the same period.<br>
3. <strong>Supreme Court</strong> invalidating <strong>CK Hutchison</strong>'s concessions.<br>
4. <strong>CK Hutchison</strong> planning to pursue legal remedies against the seizure.<br>
5. <strong>China</strong> ordering its own firms to halt <strong>Panama</strong> projects.</p>
