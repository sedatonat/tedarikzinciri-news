<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Russian Federation Government</strong> is extending a temporary ban on gasoline exports through <strong>December 31, 2025</strong>, to protect domestic supply security. This decision has been formalized through <strong>Government Decree No. 1503 dated September 30, 2025.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to a statement from the Government Press Office, the decision applies to all <strong>exporters and producers.</strong> As a result, restrictions on the sale of <strong>crude oil derivatives and automotive fuels</strong> abroad will continue.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The government has cited the fundamental rationale for the ban as maintaining <strong>stability in Russia's domestic fuel supply, preventing price fluctuations,</strong> and <strong>ensuring energy security.</strong> In particular, increased domestic demand in recent months and supply shortages in certain regions have prompted the government to extend this measure.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, through <strong>a separate government decree numbered 1502,</strong> restrictions have been placed on exports of <strong>diesel fuel,</strong> <strong>gas oil,</strong> and <strong>ship fuel.</strong> However, this decision <strong>excludes fuel producers</strong> from its scope. This regulation aims to provide producers with a certain amount of export flexibility while keeping the supply chain under control.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">These decisions represent a significant shift in Russia's <strong>energy policy.</strong> While the country previously relied heavily on energy exports in past years, it has recently adopted <strong>a strategy that prioritizes the domestic market.</strong> According to experts, this move aims to both <strong>ease domestic price pressures</strong> and <strong>increase stock levels in preparation for the winter season.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Energy analysts point out that a similar ban <strong>was also implemented in 2024</strong> and contributed to price stability in the domestic market. However, they also note that short-term declines in export revenues may occur.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Although the ban will expire at the end of 2025, the government retains the authority to <strong>review this timeframe again based on economic and geopolitical developments.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Meanwhile, this decision is also drawing attention in international fuel markets. Since Russia is one of the world's leading exporters of oil and refined products, such restrictions can <strong>cause short-term fluctuations in the global supply-demand balance.</strong> Particularly, intermediary firms exporting to European and Asian markets are working to manage the effects of this decision by <strong>revising their supply plans.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, <strong>Russia's extension of the gasoline export ban</strong> stands out as a measure that will affect both domestic economic balance and international energy markets. Through this decision, the government's priority is shaped towards <strong>ensuring the continuity of domestic consumption and preserving price stability.</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Under Government Decree No. 1503,</strong> <strong>gasoline exports are banned through December 31, 2025.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The scope of the ban includes <strong>all exporters and producers.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Decree No. 1502</strong> also restricts exports of <strong>diesel, gas oil, and ship fuel,</strong> though producers are exempted from this decision.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The government's aim is to protect <strong>domestic market supply security</strong> and <strong>fuel price stability.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The ban is expected to result in <strong>temporary reductions in energy revenues.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The decision is expected to create <strong>short-term effects on global fuel supply and prices.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
