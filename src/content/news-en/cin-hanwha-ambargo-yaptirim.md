<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>China</strong> has escalated trade tensions with <strong>Washington</strong> by imposing sanctions on <strong>five U.S.-linked subsidiaries</strong> of South Korean shipbuilding giant <strong>Hanwha Ocean</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
 According to <strong>Reuters</strong>, the decision was announced on <strong>October 14, 2025</strong>, and following the announcement, <strong>Hanwha Ocean shares fell 5.8%</strong>, while rival <strong>HD Hyundai Heavy Industries</strong> declined <strong>4.1%</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The sanctions were announced on the same day that <strong>both China and the United States introduced new port fees against each other's vessels</strong>. However, <strong>China exempted ships built by itself from this measure.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Beijing: "U.S. Cooperation Threatens China's Sovereignty"</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>China Ministry of Commerce</strong> explained the rationale for the sanctions as follows:</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">"Hanwha Ocean's U.S.-linked subsidiaries have supported U.S. government investigative activities, thereby endangering China's sovereignty, security, and development interests."</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p>The ministry announced that Chinese entities and individuals must now cease <strong>all commercial activities and cooperation with these five Hanwha subsidiaries.</strong></p>
<p><br>
This move is viewed as part of Beijing's <strong>new sanctions policy targeting companies engaged in cooperation with the West in defense and strategic manufacturing sectors.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Hanwha's Response and U.S. Investments</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Hanwha Ocean</strong> stated to Reuters that it is closely monitoring the potential impacts of the sanctions and remains <strong>committed to maintaining customer support.</strong></p>
<p><br>
The company emphasized that <strong>its investments in the U.S. maritime sector</strong> will continue.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">One of Hanwha's major initiatives is the <strong>investment in Philadelphia-based "Hanwha Philly Shipyard."</strong></p>
<p><br>
The company announced a <strong>5 billion dollar investment plan</strong> for this shipyard, which it <strong>acquired in 2024 for 100 million dollars.</strong></p>
<p><br>
This investment is viewed as part of South Korea's <strong>commitment to invest up to 150 billion dollars to revitalize U.S. shipbuilding industry.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Hanwha's rival <strong>HD Hyundai Heavy Industries</strong> is similarly <strong>conducting negotiations for shipyard acquisitions in the United States.</strong></p>
<p><br>
According to Reuters, these developments are part of <strong>the U.S. strategy to close its weakness against China in ship production.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Seoul's Response and Quest for Diplomatic Balance</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>South Korean Ministry of Foreign Affairs</strong> announced that it is evaluating China's sanctions and will work in cooperation with Beijing, other ministries, and industry stakeholders <strong>to mitigate economic impacts.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Experts note that this situation has placed <strong>Seoul in a difficult diplomatic position in the U.S.-China competition.</strong></p>
<p><br>
On one hand, South Korea is <strong>supporting U.S. efforts to rebuild defense industry supply chains;</strong> on the other hand, it is trying to preserve economic relations due to <strong>its high trade volume with China.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Trade Tensions and Geopolitical Context</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">China's sanctions against Hanwha should be understood in the context of <strong>protectionist policies</strong> and <strong>strategic manufacturing competition</strong> that gained momentum <strong>during former U.S. President Donald Trump's administration.</strong></p>
<p><br>
The Trump administration had requested support from <strong>allies such as Japan and South Korea</strong> to help revitalize <strong>the U.S. shipbuilding industry (particularly the production of warships).</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">During this process, tensions between China and the United States escalated over <strong>port fees, customs tariffs, and technology transfer restrictions.</strong></p>
<p><br>
Both countries introduced <strong>new port fees against each other's vessels in October,</strong> but decided to maintain <strong>the tariff suspension agreement valid until November 2025.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This situation increases uncertainty in global supply chains, while directly affecting <strong>maritime shipping and defense industry companies.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: Shipbuilding Sector Becomes New Trade Battleground</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">China's sanctions against Hanwha demonstrate that <strong>competition in Asia-based defense and shipbuilding industries</strong> is entering a new phase.</p>
<p><br>
The U.S. <strong>efforts to strengthen domestic production</strong> are converging with China's <strong>strategy to preserve regional influence.</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This development is a clear indicator that <strong>the U.S.-China trade war has expanded to the defense industry dimension.</strong></p>
<p><br>
For multinational actors like Hanwha, this situation requires <strong>new strategic decisions in geopolitical risk management, supply chain diversification,</strong> and <strong>market balancing.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>China</strong> imposed sanctions on <strong>five U.S.-linked subsidiaries</strong> of <strong>Hanwha Ocean.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Rationale: The companies <strong>endangered China's security by supporting U.S. investigative activities.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Following the decision, <strong>Hanwha shares fell 5.8%</strong> and <strong>HD Hyundai Heavy fell 4.1%.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Hanwha</strong> is executing a <strong>5 billion dollar investment plan</strong> for <strong>Philly Shipyard</strong> in the U.S.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>South Korean Ministry of Foreign Affairs</strong> aims to limit the impact of sanctions through increased diplomatic engagement with China.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The incident creates a new area of tension between <strong>the U.S. policy to strengthen shipbuilding industry</strong> and <strong>China's economic countermeasures.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>U.S.-China trade competition</strong> has now extended to <strong>maritime and defense industries.</strong></p></li>
