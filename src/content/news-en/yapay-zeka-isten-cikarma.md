<p style="text-align: justify">The United States has taken an important step forward in workforce policy. Republican Senator <strong>Josh Hawley (Missouri)</strong> and Democratic Senator <strong>Mark Warner (Virginia)</strong> introduced the <strong>AI-Related Job Impacts Clarity Act</strong>, a bipartisan bill, on November 5. The legislation aims to ensure that job losses resulting from artificial intelligence and automation are tracked and reported with greater transparency.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under the proposed bill, <strong>large companies and federal agencies</strong> would be required to report all layoffs resulting from AI technologies to the <strong>U.S. Department of Labor (DOL)</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The Department would then compile these reports into <strong>a public report accessible to both the public and Congress</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Hawley's statement, the goal is to make AI technology's impact on the workforce <strong>measurable</strong>, <strong>transparent</strong>, and <strong>capable of informing policy</strong>:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"The American people deserve to understand how our workforce is changing based on accurate information. We should be working toward a future where AI serves people, not one where people are at the mercy of AI."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Hawley cites research suggesting that AI could increase unemployment by <strong>as much as 20% over the next five years</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Washington takes a series of steps as AI's impact on the workforce grows</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">AI's transformation of the workforce is no longer theoretical; it has become <strong>a measurable reality</strong>.<br>
For this reason, lawmakers are attempting to manage both risks and opportunities through various political mechanisms.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">For example:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Senator Bernie Sanders</strong> stated in a report released last month that artificial intelligence could <strong>eliminate 97 million jobs within a decade</strong>.<br>
Among the policies Sanders has proposed are:<br>
A <strong>32-hour work week</strong> without loss of pay,<br>
A <strong>robot tax</strong> (automation tax),<br>
Elimination of tax incentives applied to AI investments,<br>
Protections for family and health leave.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Representative Nancy Mace</strong> introduced bipartisan legislation to increase federal employees' access to AI training and modernize the public sector.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Senator Mark Kelly</strong> released an <strong>"AI for America"</strong> roadmap and proposed an <strong>AI Horizon Fund</strong> financed by major AI companies.<br>
The objective: worker retraining, investment in social safety nets, responsible technology adoption.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Although these initiatives are still in the early stages of the legislative process, there is visible cross-party momentum in Congress regarding AI policy.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>States are implementing strong and swift regulations</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">While the federal process moves more slowly, many states are taking aggressive steps to regulate AI use:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>New York</strong>: <strong>Mandatory bias audits</strong> for AI-powered hiring systems.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>California</strong>: <strong>AI risk management policy</strong> requirement for employers.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Colorado</strong>: <strong>Mandatory disclosure</strong> to candidates that AI was used in hiring.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This trend demonstrates that AI's rapid expansion into areas such as human resources, manufacturing, customer service, and office automation is generating <strong>stricter transparency and accountability requirements</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Why does this bill matter?</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new legislation would:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Make it difficult for companies to conceal AI-related layoffs,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Help the public better understand AI's economic impacts,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Facilitate institutions and lawmakers in developing <strong>sound economic policies</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Create a database for workforce protection strategies.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Furthermore, it aims to fill the gap of <strong>official and comprehensive data</strong> that has been lacking in discussions about AI-related job losses to date.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Hawley and Warner introduced legislation <strong>requiring mandatory reporting of AI-related layoffs</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The DOL would compile this data and <strong>make it publicly available</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">AI is expected to impact the workforce by as much as 20%.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Sanders: "97 million jobs" at risk; a 32-hour week and robot tax were proposed.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">States are already implementing bias audits and AI disclosure requirements.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The bill is critical for <strong>transparency</strong>, <strong>economic planning</strong>, and <strong>workforce protection</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
