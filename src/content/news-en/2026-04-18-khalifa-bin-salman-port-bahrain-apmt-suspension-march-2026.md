<p style="text-align: justify">Port operations at <strong>Bahrain's Khalifa Bin Salman Port</strong> have been <strong>suspended with immediate effect</strong> following guidance from government and regulatory authorities. In an operational advisory, terminal operator <strong>APM Terminals (APMT) Bahrain</strong> confirmed that the suspension covers both <strong>berth</strong> and <strong>gate operations</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The measure took effect on <strong>12 March</strong> and will remain in place until <strong>13 March 2026</strong>. All gate appointments scheduled during this period have been <strong>cancelled</strong>, and customers have been asked not to dispatch trucks or personnel to the terminal. APMT Bahrain said further updates on the resumption of operations will be communicated as the situation develops.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Khalifa Bin Salman Port</strong> serves as <strong>Bahrain's main commercial gateway</strong>, handling containerised cargo and other goods moving through the country's maritime trade routes. The temporary stoppage could cause short-term disruptions to regional supply chains and transit cargo flows passing through Bahrain. The operator stated that additional information will be shared depending on evolving conditions.</p>
