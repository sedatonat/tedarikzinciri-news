<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CMA CGM</strong> has reported that severe weather conditions are impacting navigation in the Atlantic along the French, Spanish and Moroccan coastlines. The company said the situation involves an event of <strong>exceptional intensity and duration</strong>, particularly in the <strong>Bay of Biscay</strong>. As a precautionary measure, CMA CGM has decided to place vessels transiting the area in <strong>safe positions</strong> to ensure the safety of crews and cargo. The measure applies across the entire area from southern Morocco to the entrance of the English Channel, with holding positions notably in the <strong>Seine Bay and Gibraltar</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">"The situation is being closely monitored, and our teams remain fully mobilised," CMA CGM said in a statement. The carrier listed the impacted services as <strong>FAL, EPIC, US, EURAF, LATAM and SSL</strong>. Navigation in the Bay of Biscay is expected to be disrupted for approximately <strong>72 hours</strong>. Vessels held during this period will continue rotation when conditions allow, leading to <strong>temporary delays</strong> in container planning along European routes.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Shippers can expect adjustments in equipment turn and port ETAs. The event underlines that extreme weather driven by <strong>climate change</strong> has become a persistent supply chain risk factor across global container chains. CMA CGM will continue updates on the situation through customer notification platforms; marine insurance and <strong>general average</strong> implications will also be closely monitored.</p>
