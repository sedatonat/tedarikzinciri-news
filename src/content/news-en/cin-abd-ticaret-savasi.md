<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>As the trade war launched by US President Donald Trump enters its sixth month,</strong> <strong>China's export resilience</strong> clearly demonstrates Beijing's critical role in global supply chains.</p>
<p><br>
Despite <strong>US tariffs reaching 55 percent,</strong> China continues to export <strong>approximately $1 billion worth of goods to the United States daily.</strong></p>
<p><br>
This data shows that Beijing <strong>maintains its bargaining power</strong> against Washington's aggressive trade policies.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Export Flows Continue Despite Tariffs</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">In the third quarter of 2025, over <strong>$100 billion in products</strong> were shipped from China to the United States.<br>
This volume helps Beijing sustain economic growth in line with its annual targets, while <strong>raising its trade surplus with the United States to $67 billion.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to a Bloomberg report, although China's total foreign trade has seen <strong>double-digit declines over the past six months,</strong> some product categories have grown compared to 2024.</p>
<p><br>
In particular, exports of <strong>e-cigarettes, electric bicycles, refined copper cathode,</strong> and <strong>electrical cables</strong> have risen in value.</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>E-bicycle exports:</strong> In the third quarter, surpassed <strong>$500 million,</strong> outpacing the same period last year.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Refined copper cathode exports:</strong> Increased from zero to <strong>$270 million.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Electrical cables:</strong> Reached <strong>$405 million with an 87 percent increase</strong> in value.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Conversely, sectors such as <strong>LCD televisions</strong> and <strong>gaming consoles</strong> experienced sharp declines in exports.<br>
</p>
<p><strong>Nintendo</strong> and <strong>Microsoft</strong> have been shifting their production to countries like <strong>Vietnam</strong> to avoid higher tariffs.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>China's Dominance in Strategic Products Continues</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Despite US tariff pressure, China remains an indispensable supplier in strategic sectors including <strong>rare earths,</strong> <strong>electronic components,</strong> <strong>pharmaceutical raw materials,</strong> and <strong>battery chemicals.</strong><br>
<strong>Bloomberg economists Chang Shu and David Qu</strong> explain the situation as follows:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"China's strong position in global supply chains provides significant short-term bargaining power against US importers. Repositioning production will take time."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">This dependency grants Beijing <strong>a significant diplomatic advantage</strong> ahead of negotiations on <strong>extending the 90-day tariff ceasefire set to expire in November.</strong></p>
<p><br>
The <strong>Xi Jinping</strong> administration, by maintaining export flows to the United States, <strong>keeps economic growth on track</strong> and <strong>strengthens its negotiating position against Washington.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Cracks in the US Tariff Wall</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>ANZ Bank China strategist Zhaopeng Xing</strong> points out that there are <strong>numerous loopholes</strong> in the US tariff implementation.</p>
<p><br>
Some American importers route goods <strong>through third countries (such as Mexico or Vietnam)</strong> and declare lower customs values through trans-shipment.</p>
<p><br>
This allows them to <strong>avoid paying the full tariff rate when goods reach the United States,</strong> according to reports.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Xing notes that "US Customs lacks sufficient personnel to detect such methods," highlighting the system's weaknesses.</p>
<p><br>
This emerges as a factor that <strong>limits the effectiveness of tariffs implemented by the Trump administration.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>E-commerce and Micro-Export Growth</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Despite tariff pressures, Chinese e-commerce giants <strong>Shein</strong> and <strong>Temu from PDD Holdings</strong> continue selling <strong>billions of dollars worth of small packages to US consumers</strong> through their platforms.</p>
<p><br>
Despite the elimination of the <strong>"de minimis"</strong> rule in May 2025, the total value of these products subject to <strong>54 percent tariffs</strong> reached <strong>$5.4 billion.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, <strong>business-to-business (B2B)</strong> e-commerce exports soared from <strong>$31 million in August to $201 million in September.</strong></p>
<p><br>
This indicates that Chinese suppliers are now transitioning to a model of <strong>warehousing and redistribution within the United States</strong> rather than sending products directly to US consumers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Power Balance Before Trump–Xi Talks</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">US President <strong>Donald Trump</strong> expressed optimism that a "good deal" would emerge from talks with Xi expected to take place at a <strong>summit in South Korea next week.</strong></p>
<p><br>
However, he also noted that the talks could still be cancelled.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Trump listed <strong>rare earths,</strong> <strong>fentanyl,</strong> and <strong>soybeans</strong> as priority agenda items for the negotiations.</p>
<p><br>
Nonetheless, based on the current situation, China's <strong>strategic production advantage</strong> in these areas grants Beijing <strong>significant bargaining power</strong> at the negotiating table.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: Balance of Trade War Resilience Favors China</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Despite high US tariffs, China <strong>maintains its commercial resilience in the short term.</strong></p>
<p><br>
Since global supply chain reconfiguration takes time, <strong>China's production network and cost advantages</strong> grant Beijing <strong>economic and diplomatic power.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, in the long term, <strong>the Trump administration's onshoring policy</strong> and <strong>US efforts to support domestic manufacturing</strong> could gradually reduce bilateral trade volume.</p>
<p><br>
Nevertheless, today's figures clearly demonstrate that <strong>China's role in the global manufacturing hub remains unchanged.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">China exports <strong>approximately $1 billion worth of goods to the United States daily.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Although <strong>US tariffs have reached 55 percent, export volume resumed growth in September.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Over $100 billion in Chinese goods reached the United States in the third quarter,</strong> with a trade surplus of <strong>$67 billion.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Notable growth was seen in e-cigarettes, e-bicycles, refined copper cathode, and electrical cables</strong> exports.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>US customs loopholes and trans-shipment routes</strong> reduce the impact of tariffs.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Chinese e-commerce platforms achieved <strong>$5.4 billion in micro-exports despite tariffs.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Beijing holds <strong>a strong negotiating position ahead of November talks on extending the tariff ceasefire.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">While <strong>global supply chain restructuring continues long term,</strong> <strong>China's production and supply power will not change in the short term.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
