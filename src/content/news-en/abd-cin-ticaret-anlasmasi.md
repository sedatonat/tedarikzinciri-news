<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The US Trade Representative (USTR)</strong> has launched a new investigation under <strong>Section 301</strong> to examine whether China is fulfilling its obligations under the <strong>"Phase One Agreement" signed in 2020</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The investigation will assess whether China is breaching its commitments on <strong>intellectual property (IP), forced technology transfer, agriculture, and financial services</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This move comes at a time when <strong>US–China trade relations are entering a period of renewed tension</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Trump Administration Revisits 2020 Agreement</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the <strong>Federal Register notice issued by USTR on October 24, 2025</strong>, the investigation aims to determine whether <strong>US rights under the agreement have been violated</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
In response to China's conduct, <strong>retaliatory actions</strong> such as <strong>new tariffs or import restrictions</strong> may also be considered.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This process is intended to review the implementation of the <strong>trade agreement signed in January 2020 during President Donald Trump's first term</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The agreement was implemented following the <strong>trade war of 2018–2019</strong> with the goal of partially reducing tariffs and increasing bilateral trade.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Provisions of the Phase One Agreement</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under the <strong>2020 agreement</strong>:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The US agreed to <strong>reduce tariffs on $120 billion worth of products imported from China</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">China, based on 2017 data, committed to <strong>additional imports of $217 billion from the US</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">In return, the US maintained <strong>Section 301 tariffs</strong> on other products.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">China also pledged to <strong>reduce tariffs on 850 different products</strong> and increase <strong>global imports</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, according to USTR's new assessment, China <strong>fell short of its import target from the US at $200 billion</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
This figure shows that <strong>the import volume China committed to was largely not realized</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>USTR: "China Failed to Meet Commitments"</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">USTR's statement included the following remarks:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Despite all diplomatic contacts with China over the past five years,<br>
China has failed to fulfill its commitments in the areas of Intellectual Property, forced technology transfer, agriculture, and financial services."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">In this context, USTR will determine whether <strong>US trade interests have been harmed</strong> by <strong>China's practices that contravene the agreement's provisions</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Investigation Process: Public Participation Call</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">USTR will hold <strong>a public hearing on December 16, 2025</strong> as part of the investigation.</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The public comment period begins on October 31</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Comments and hearing participation requests will be accepted through December 1</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Participants will be able to provide information on how China has implemented its commitments in the agreement and <strong>offer recommendations to the Trump administration on measures that should be taken</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>New Wave of Tension: Pressure Escalates Following Tariff Ceasefire</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">This investigation creates new tension in US–China trade relations <strong>following a temporary tariff ceasefire signed in the spring</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
Both sides had <strong>agreed to halt tariff increases</strong>, but disagreements persist over <strong>implementation and import commitments</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Trump administration's move is also seen as a harbinger of <strong>a new round of trade negotiations with China</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The White House confirmed that <strong>President Trump and Chinese President Xi Jinping will meet in South Korea next week</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Analysis: US Enters New "Section 301" Pressure Cycle</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Section 301</strong> investigations are one of the mechanisms that give the US <strong>the authority to impose unilateral sanctions against unfair trade practices</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
This mechanism was also the legal basis for the 2018 trade war.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The new investigation is part of the Trump administration's <strong>strategy to increase economic pressure on China</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">China's <strong>failure to meet import commitments</strong> could pave the way for <strong>renewed tariff increases</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">For US companies, this situation signals <strong>a new period of uncertainty in supply chain planning</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>US Trade Representative (USTR)</strong> is reviewing China's compliance with the <strong>2020 Phase One Trade Agreement</strong> under a <strong>Section 301 investigation</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">China fell short of its import target from the US at <strong>$200 billion</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The investigation may result in retaliatory steps including <strong>new tariffs and trade restrictions</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The public hearing is scheduled for December 16, 2025</strong>, with the comment period running <strong>October 31–December 1</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">This move could trigger <strong>a new wave of tension in US–China relations following the tariff ceasefire</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Experts point out that this investigation signals the Trump administration's <strong>renewed pressure campaign against China</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
