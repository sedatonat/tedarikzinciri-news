<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ocean Network Express (ONE)</strong> has signed a <strong>share purchase agreement</strong> to acquire a 30 per cent stake in <strong>Hutchison Laemchabang Terminal Limited (HLT)</strong>, located at <strong>Laem Chabang Port</strong> in Thailand. HLT plays a central role in Thailand's <strong>Eastern Economic Corridor (EEC)</strong> and acts as a key gateway for regional and global trade flows.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The operator manages multiple facilities at the port, including the automated <strong>Terminal D</strong>, alongside <strong>Terminals C1, C2, and A3</strong>, positioning it as a <strong>leading deep-water terminal operator</strong> in the country. ONE's move is set to strengthen its foothold in Southeast Asia, securing long-term <strong>terminal capacity access</strong> in one of its core markets.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The investment also aligns with broader efforts by ocean carriers to gain greater control over <strong>landside infrastructure</strong> amid ongoing supply chain volatility. <strong>Hiroki Tsujii</strong>, Global Chief Officer, Product & Network Division at ONE, said the investment in HLT marks a significant milestone for the company. The deal cements ONE's position in Thailand while aiming to deliver a more reliable <strong>terminal service chain</strong> to customers worldwide.</p>
