<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Portchain</strong> announced a significant sustainability milestone in the global container shipping industry. <strong>Portchain Network</strong>, the platform developed by the company, has contributed to preventing <strong>800,000 metric tonnes of CO₂ emissions</strong> since its launch in 2022.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This savings is equivalent to <strong>654,922 petrol vehicles used for one year</strong>, <strong>239 wind turbines' annual production</strong>, or <strong>13 million trees growing over 10 years</strong> in terms of environmental impact.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Portchain Network: Digital collaboration ecosystem</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Portchain Network</strong> is <strong>a global digital platform</strong> that provides <strong>standardized data sharing</strong> among <strong>mainline carriers</strong>, <strong>feeder operators</strong>, and <strong>port terminals</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The <strong>Portchain Connect</strong> system, which forms the core of the platform:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Shares real-time vessel schedules</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Provides berth alignment in port planning</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Enables vessels to arrive at ports at the right time</strong> through <strong>Just-In-Time (JIT)</strong> port calls<br>
driving operational efficiency.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">As challenges like <strong>global congestion, climate-related disruptions, and port congestion</strong> grow in maritime shipping, <strong>Portchain's digitalization model</strong> simplifies this complex planning process.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Fuel savings and emission reduction</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Portchain data, thanks to <strong>JIT port calls</strong>:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>5.9% reduction in fuel and CO₂ emissions</strong> when planning between vessel and port is done <strong>24 hours before arrival</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Up to 14% reduction</strong> when planning is done <strong>before departure from the previous port</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Carriers and terminals in the network have achieved <strong>2–4% average fuel savings</strong>, while some cases have attained <strong>efficiency gains of up to 9%</strong>. These results demonstrate <strong>the concrete environmental benefits of digital coordination in the maritime sector</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Portchain CEO: "We aim to reduce 0.5% of global emissions"</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Portchain CEO and Co-founder Niels Kristiansen</strong> summarized the company's long-term goals as follows:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Our goal is to reduce 0.5% of global emissions. This path goes through the widespread adoption of Just-In-Time calls across the entire maritime industry. This goal requires scale, collaboration, and digitalization."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Kristiansen emphasized that Portchain Network still has significant growth potential, noting that <strong>as more carriers and terminals join the network, efficiency and sustainability will increase exponentially</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>The JIT approach: A new standard in maritime shipping</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The JIT model enables vessels to arrive at ports precisely on time by <strong>optimizing voyage speeds</strong>. This way, vessels avoid unnecessary acceleration or waiting, reducing <strong>fuel consumption and carbon emissions</strong>. Additionally, <strong>berth utilization efficiency</strong> at ports increases and <strong>coordination times shorten</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Through <strong>digitalization and secure data sharing</strong>, <strong>real-time information flow</strong> is enabled between vessels and port authorities. This situation <strong>reduces port congestion</strong>, <strong>lowers delays</strong>, and <strong>improves energy efficiency</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sectoral impact</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The maritime industry is undergoing significant transformation in the 2020s due to <strong>fuel costs, carbon regulations (IMO 2023)</strong>, and <strong>supply chain disruptions</strong>. Portchain Network is at the center of this transformation, making <strong>"digital port integration"</strong> a standard concept.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, as digital networks become more widespread, <strong>carbon reduction targets in maritime shipping</strong> will become far more achievable.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Kristiansen characterizes this achievement as a "beginning":</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"This is only the first step. True transformation will happen when efficiency, financial sustainability, and environmental impact complement each other."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Portchain Network</strong> has delivered <strong>800,000 tonnes of CO₂ savings</strong> since 2022.</p></li>

  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Average fuel savings 2–4%</strong>, reaching <strong>up to 9%</strong> in some cases.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CEO Niels Kristiansen:</strong> "We aim to reduce 0.5% of global emissions."</p></li>

</ul>
