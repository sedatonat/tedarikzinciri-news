<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CMA CGM</strong> has decided to increase freight rates for shipments to the United States effective March 15, 2025. The increase applies to container transportation from <strong>Asia</strong>, the <strong>Middle East</strong>, the <strong>Indian Subcontinent</strong>, and <strong>Latin America</strong> to various U.S. ports.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new pricing will be applied at different rates for 20-foot and 40-foot containers. For example, for shipments from <strong>Asia</strong> to <strong>West Coast</strong> ports, an additional charge of $200 USD will apply for 20-foot containers and $400 USD for 40-foot containers. For shipments to <strong>East Coast</strong> and <strong>Gulf</strong> ports, an additional charge of $300 USD will apply for 20-foot containers and $600 USD for 40-foot containers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">A similar increase applies to shipments from the <strong>Middle East</strong> and <strong>Indian Subcontinent</strong> to the U.S. An additional charge of $250 USD will be required for 20-foot containers and $500 USD for 40-foot containers. For shipments from <strong>Latin America</strong> to the U.S., an additional charge of $150 USD will apply for 20-foot containers and $300 USD for 40-foot containers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The price increase is attributed to rising operational costs and fluctuations in global supply chains. <strong>CMA CGM</strong> states that such adjustments are necessary to continue delivering high-quality service to its customers.</p>
<p><br></p>
