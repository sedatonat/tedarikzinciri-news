<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Port of Oakland</strong> handled <strong>198,667 TEUs</strong> in March 2026, a significant recovery from the 163,254 TEUs recorded in February, as vessel call activity normalised following the <strong>Lunar New Year</strong> slowdown. The port received 86 vessel calls during the month, up from 72 in February, driving the improvement in overall container throughput. March trade flows were notably balanced, with imports reaching 99,091 TEUs and exports 99,576 TEUs.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The result reinforces Oakland's established profile as a port with structural strength in both import and export trade. On a year-on-year basis, March volume was down 8.6 per cent compared to March 2025. <strong>Year-to-date (YTD)</strong> throughput through the first quarter totalled 557,859 TEUs, a 7.3 per cent decrease from the same period in 2025, consistent with the broader softness in West Coast import volumes observed across the quarter.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Bryan Brandes</strong>, Maritime Director at the Port of Oakland, described March as a month reflecting operational stability and balanced trade flows. Oakland is positioned as a <strong>dominant US West Coast gateway for agricultural exports</strong>, playing a critical role in dairy, almond, dried fruit and wine exports. The near-even split between exports and imports provides a structural buffer against the pressure from US-China tariff dynamics that could otherwise turn empty container repositioning to Asia against the port.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. Port of Oakland handled 198,667 TEUs in March 2026, a strong rebound from February.<br>
2. Operations regained pace post Lunar New Year with 86 vessel calls.<br>
3. Imports reached 99,091 TEUs and exports 99,576 TEUs, nearly evenly split.<br>
4. March volume fell 8.6 per cent YoY; YTD throughput dropped 7.3 per cent.<br>
5. Maritime Director Bryan Brandes characterised the month as evidence of operational stability.</p>
