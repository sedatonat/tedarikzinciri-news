<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Port of Portland</strong> has appointed Krishna Walker as <strong>Chief Shared Prosperity and Legal Officer (CSPLO)</strong>, with her tenure set to begin on <strong>4 May 2026</strong>. Walker joins from <strong>CareVet</strong>, where she served as Chief Legal and Corporate Affairs Officer. In that role, she led the legal execution of more than 200 business acquisitions, supporting a tenfold expansion of the company over six years.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Her prior experience includes senior leadership at <strong>Rabobank North America</strong> and over a decade advising growing companies and large non-profit organisations as outside general counsel. Her background in public governance dates back to 1995, when she was appointed by Evan Bayh to the <strong>Ball State University Board of Trustees</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Curtis Robinhold</strong>, Executive Director of the Port of Portland, said: "Krishna's experience and success leading complex and broad portfolios of work stood out as we looked for the right leader." The Port of Portland was recently in the news with an <strong>SM Line and Westwood Shipping agreement</strong> that restored vessel calls at the T6 container terminal. Walker's CSPLO role encompasses, alongside legal affairs, the integration of community equity and shared prosperity programmes into port authority decision processes.</p>
