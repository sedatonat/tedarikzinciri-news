<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Mediterranean Shipping Company (MSC)</strong> has launched <strong>a new intermodal rail link that will connect Serbia directly to global trade networks</strong>. Starting in October 2025, the line operates between <strong>the Batajnica terminal near Belgrade</strong> and <strong>the Port of Trieste on Italy's Adriatic coast</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The new line stands out as a critical connection that integrates <strong>landlocked countries such as Serbia, North Macedonia, and Montenegro</strong> into <strong>MSC's global maritime and logistics networks</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Faster access to global markets</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The new intermodal service delivers <strong>shorter transit times</strong> and <strong>more predictable supply chains</strong> for customers shipping <strong>automotive parts, ore, machinery, packaging materials, and consumer goods</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Operating with regular weekly departures, the line can carry <strong>more than 70 TEU container capacity</strong>. Through this connection, customers gain access to a solution that is <strong>more sustainable and cost-effective than road transportation</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Stevica Carapic, MSC's Regional Director for Serbia, North Macedonia, and Montenegro</strong>, underscored the service's strategic importance for regional logistics integration, stating:</p>\n<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"This new rail line represents a step that strengthens Serbia and the Balkans' position within MSC's global logistics network.<br>\nBy combining rail and maritime transportation, we can offer our customers more reliable and sustainable supply chain solutions."</blockquote>\n<blockquote><br></blockquote>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Trieste: The Adriatic's gateway to global logistics</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The new connection centers on <strong>Trieste, one of Europe's most strategic ports</strong>. As <strong>an Adriatic gateway where MSC operates its own terminal</strong>, Trieste represents <strong>a vital hub in its maritime network reaching Asia, Africa, and the Americas</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Through this connection, exporters in Serbia gain <strong>direct access to MSC's global intermodal network</strong>. Via Trieste, <strong>faster and eco-friendly transport is provided to the Mediterranean, Far East, Northern Europe, and North American markets</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sustainability and efficiency benefits</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">According to MSC, the new rail line will:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Provide a more efficient solution as an alternative to road transportation</strong>,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Deliver eco-friendly logistics by reducing CO₂ emissions</strong>,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Enhance operational flexibility by shortening transit times</strong>.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">This line strengthens <strong>MSC's intermodal infrastructure across Europe</strong> while representing a step aligned with the <strong>European Green Deal</strong> objectives.</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">MSC stated in its announcement, "This connection will not only accelerate shipping but will also help our customers <strong>reduce their carbon footprint</strong>."</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Serbia's regional logistics role strengthens</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In recent years, Serbia has made significant investments toward <strong>becoming a regional logistics hub in the Balkans</strong>. The country is positioned as <strong>a strategic transit point for reaching Central European, Mediterranean, and Black Sea markets</strong> thanks to its location on <strong>Pan-European Transport Corridor X</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">MSC's new line, integrated with <strong>dry port infrastructure in the Balkans</strong>, makes the <strong>door-to-port model more accessible for landlocked countries</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A new link in the intermodal network</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The new line is part of MSC's <strong>intermodal expansion strategy</strong> across Europe. The company has launched new rail connections between <strong>Hungary, Poland, Slovakia, and the Czech Republic</strong> over the past two years.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Through these efforts, MSC <strong>shifts high-volume cargo from roads to rail</strong>, supporting both <strong>sustainable transportation objectives</strong> and <strong>reducing logistics costs</strong>.</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Carapic summed up the significance of the new line:</p>\n<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"This connection represents a transformative step not only for MSC but also for Serbia's regional trade potential.<br>\nThrough the Trieste link, customers can now reach global markets directly and securely through a single gateway."</blockquote>\n<blockquote><br></blockquote>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>MSC</strong> has launched a new intermodal rail line <strong>from the Batajnica terminal near Belgrade to the Port of Trieste</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The line carries <strong>automotive, machinery, ore, and consumer goods</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Weekly departures with 70+ TEU capacity</strong> provide <strong>shorter transit times and lower emissions</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Trieste is <strong>MSC's primary sea gateway in the Adriatic</strong> and exit point to global markets.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The line promotes <strong>eco-friendly and sustainable transportation as an alternative to roads</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>MSC's European intermodal
