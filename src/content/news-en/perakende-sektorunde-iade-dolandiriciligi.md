<p>In response to rising return fraud, companies are offering guidance on how to protect themselves against these threats, which coincide with increased sales during major events such as the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl"><u>Super Bowl</u></a>. Electronics retailers in particular face situations known as "wardrobing," where consumers purchase new televisions for a major game and then return them after the event. According to data from <a href="https://www.gotrg.com/"><u>goTRG</u></a>, TV return rates spike to 11% in March following the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl"><u>Super Bowl</u></a>, representing a 36% increase. Although return rates declined overall to 8.95% in 2023, return fraud reached a record high of 101 billion dollars. To combat this issue, experts recommend strategies including the use of return management software, the establishment of effective return policies, partnerships with certified refurbishment vendors, and strengthened employee training programs. These measures are critical in reducing fraud and maintaining retail integrity.</p>

<h5><strong>Key Takeaways</strong></h5>
<ol>
  <li><p>Increased consumer spending during the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl"><u>Super Bowl</u></a> elevates the risk of return fraud.</p></li>
  <li><p>Electronics retailers face significant spikes in TV sales followed by substantial returns.</p></li>
  <li><p>Return fraud has become a serious issue, reaching 101 billion dollars in 2023.</p></li>
  <li><p>Companies can counter this problem by deploying return management software and implementing strict return policies.</p></li>
  <li><p>Employee training and appropriate partnerships are among the effective methods in combating fraud.</p></li>
</ol>
