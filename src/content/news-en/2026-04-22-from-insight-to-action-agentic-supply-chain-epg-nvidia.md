<p style="text-align: justify">Supply chain execution is undergoing fundamental transformation. As complexity accelerates and disruptions become the norm, reactive rule-based systems can no longer keep pace. In this <strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> webinar, leaders from <strong>EPG</strong> and <strong>NVIDIA</strong> examine how agentic artificial intelligence is transforming logistics from insight-driven visibility to autonomous, intelligent execution. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>EPG</strong> (<strong>Ehrhardt Partner Group</strong>) competes in the European <strong>WMS</strong> market against <strong>SAP EWM</strong>, <strong>Manhattan</strong>, <strong>Blue Yonder</strong>, and <strong>Körber</strong> with its <strong>LFS WMS</strong>, <strong>Greenstone WCS</strong> (<strong>Warehouse Control System</strong>), and <strong>Lydia Voice</strong> voice-based picking solutions. <strong>NVIDIA</strong>'s <strong>Omniverse</strong>, <strong>Isaac Sim</strong>, <strong>cuOpt</strong>, and <strong>Metropolis</strong> platforms form the infrastructure layer for supply chain and logistics <strong>AI</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, we can understand how the transition from reactive logistics to autonomous supply chains is developing and what this means for supply chain leaders planning the next era of performance and resilience. <strong>Agentic AI</strong> represents the next evolution of <strong>generative AI</strong>, incorporating capabilities for <strong>tool use</strong>, <strong>multi-step reasoning</strong>, <strong>autonomous decision-making</strong>, and <strong>self-correction</strong>. <strong>Anthropic</strong>'s <strong>Claude</strong>, <strong>OpenAI</strong>'s <strong>o1</strong> and <strong>o3</strong>, <strong>Google DeepMind</strong>'s <strong>Gemini</strong>, and <strong>Meta</strong>'s <strong>Llama</strong> series form the foundation model layer. Orchestration frameworks such as <strong>LangChain</strong>, <strong>CrewAI</strong>, <strong>AutoGen</strong>, and <strong>LangGraph</strong> enable multiple agents to work collaboratively across logistics workflows.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, specific logistics applications of agentic <strong>AI</strong> focus on areas such as dynamic routing for <strong>last-mile delivery</strong> via <strong>route optimization</strong>; automated exception resolution for <strong>warehouse exception management</strong>; multi-DC inventory balancing for <strong>inventory rebalancing</strong>; spot market negotiations for <strong>carrier procurement</strong>; and alternative supplier discovery for <strong>supply risk monitoring</strong>. <strong>EPG</strong>'s <strong>Greenstone</strong> platform is positioned to deliver agentic decision mechanisms between the automation hardware layer and the <strong>WMS</strong>. The orchestration layer for <strong>conveyor</strong>, <strong>sorter</strong>, <strong>AS/RS</strong> (<strong>Automated Storage and Retrieval System</strong>), <strong>AMR</strong> (<strong>Autonomous Mobile Robot</strong>), and <strong>AGV</strong> (<strong>Automated Guided Vehicle</strong>) systems becomes the natural deployment ground for agentic <strong>AI</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>NVIDIA</strong>'s role in this segment extends beyond <strong>GPU</strong> hardware to encompassing capabilities delivered to logistics leaders through its <strong>CUDA</strong> software ecosystem: <strong>cuOpt</strong> for <strong>VRP</strong> (<strong>Vehicle Routing Problem</strong>), <strong>Isaac Sim</strong> for robot training simulation, and <strong>Omniverse</strong> for <strong>digital twin</strong> creation. The <strong>NVIDIA H100</strong>, <strong>H200</strong>, <strong>B200</strong>, and <strong>GH200</strong> Grace Hopper superchips serve as foundational hardware for <strong>foundation model</strong> training and inference. <strong>Edge AI</strong> through the <strong>NVIDIA Jetson Orin</strong> platform provides local decision mechanisms for warehouse robots, autonomous forklifts, and <strong>AMR</strong>s. In essence, the vision reflected in the joint webinar between <strong>EPG</strong> and <strong>NVIDIA</strong> is concrete evidence that supply chain execution will be fundamentally reshaped by agentic <strong>AI</strong> in the years ahead.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. The webinar brings together leaders from <strong>EPG</strong> and <strong>NVIDIA</strong>.<br>
2. Reactive rule-based systems fall short in the face of complexity.<br>
3. Agentic <strong>AI</strong> transforms logistics from visibility to autonomous execution.<br>
4. <strong>NVIDIA cuOpt</strong> accelerates routing optimization with <strong>VRP</strong>.<br>
5. <strong>EPG Greenstone</strong> serves as the automation hardware orchestration layer.</p>
