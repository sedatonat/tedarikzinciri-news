<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Rising regulations in global trade</strong> are putting customs authorities under unprecedented pressure. In particular, <strong>transparency demands regarding product components, supplier networks, and manufacturing processes</strong> have reached levels that exceed the <strong>inspection capacity of many countries' customs authorities.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP)</strong> is at the forefront of this challenge. <strong>The Trump administration's escalating inspection policies on import supply chains</strong> are requiring CBP to <strong>analyze complex supply chains at the component level.</strong> However, current infrastructure and resources are proving insufficient to handle inspections at this depth.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Trade policies and capacity imbalance</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to reporting by <strong>Eric Johnson,</strong> recent increases in <strong>international trade regulations</strong> — particularly on issues such as human rights, environmental impact, and strategic technology transfer — are imposing new obligations on importers and exporters.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">For example, <strong>the U.S. Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act (UFPLA)</strong> requires companies to <strong>ensure ethical compliance at every link in their supply chains.</strong> However, such regulations create <strong>significant challenges for both companies and customs authorities in terms of data tracking and verification.</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to a CBP official:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Policies are forcing border agencies to conduct more inspections, but our current systems and personnel are insufficient for this level of scrutiny."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Altana and CBP partnership: Digital inspection pilot</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">In response to these challenges, <strong>CBP</strong> is running a joint pilot project with technology company <strong>Altana.</strong> The project was developed to <strong>increase data sharing and supplier visibility in complex supply chains.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Global companies such as <strong>Maersk,</strong> <strong>LL Bean,</strong> and <strong>BASF</strong> have also joined the pilot program.</p>
<p><br>
The digital platform called <strong>Altana Atlas</strong> uses <strong>artificial intelligence and big data analytics</strong> to map millions of supply relationships, enabling <strong>real-time tracking of product origins and supply chain connections.</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The goal is to enable CBP to <strong>faster identify risky suppliers, illegal activities, or connections to sanctioned regions</strong> in supply chains.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Altana CEO Evan Smith</strong> commented on the project as follows:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"The problem customs authorities face is not a lack of data, but a lack of data interpretation. We help them identify risky relationships by connecting this data."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A global problem</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The United States is not alone. Countries such as the <strong>European Union,</strong> the <strong>United Kingdom,</strong> <strong>Canada,</strong> and <strong>Australia</strong> are also increasing <strong>supply chain transparency requirements</strong> for importers and exporters. However, in these countries too, customs agencies' data collection and analysis capacity <strong>is falling far behind the pace of increase.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In particular, <strong>the complexity of global production networks</strong> makes it nearly impossible to properly map a product's components, subcontractors, and raw material sources.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, the <strong>"political pressure–institutional capacity" imbalance</strong> could lead to <strong>delays and increased costs in global trade flows</strong> in the coming years.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Regulations rising, digitalization essential</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">For customs authorities to withstand this pressure, technologies such as <strong>digital supply chain intelligence,</strong> <strong>data-sharing networks,</strong> and <strong>automated verification systems</strong> need to be widely implemented.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Solutions like Altana <strong>represent an early stage of this transformation.</strong> However, many countries' customs administrations still rely on <strong>manual processes, fragmented databases, and limited risk analysis infrastructure.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Johnson's reporting, while the project CBP is conducting with Altana demonstrates the potential of this transformation, it also reveals <strong>customs authorities' digital capacity shortfalls.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, <strong>rising political inspection demands</strong> can only be met through <strong>technological modernization</strong> and <strong>international collaboration.</strong> Otherwise, <strong>the complexity of trade regulations will continue to threaten the speed of global supply chain operations.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Altana</strong> and <strong>CBP</strong> are running a pilot project for supply chain transparency.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Participants: <strong>Maersk, LL Bean, BASF.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Objective: <strong>Digitalize supplier connections to identify risky sources.</strong></p></li>

  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The U.S., EU, and other countries</strong> are expanding human rights and environment-focused trade laws.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Experts: "Without data analytics, political demands are operationally unsustainable."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Solution: <strong>AI-powered supply chain intelligence and digital collaboration platforms.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
