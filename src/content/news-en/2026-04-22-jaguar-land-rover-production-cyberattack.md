<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Jaguar Land Rover</strong> (<strong>JLR</strong>) has told suppliers that production at its U.K. factories will not resume for at least until September 24 following a major cyberattack — with industry sources warning the disruption could extend through November, according to <strong>BBC News</strong>. A criminal investigation is underway into the attack, which occurred more than two weeks ago, forcing <strong>JLR</strong> to shut down its IT networks — paralyzing production at vehicle factories in <strong>Solihull</strong> and <strong>Halewood</strong> and the engine plant in <strong>Wolverhampton</strong>. Production has also halted at major factories in <strong>Slovakia</strong> and <strong>China</strong>, as well as a smaller facility in <strong>India</strong>. <strong>JLR</strong>, owned by India's <strong>Tata Motors</strong>, has dismissed reports that the disruption could last weeks or months as "speculation." However, <strong>BBC News</strong> reports that suppliers — mostly small and medium-sized enterprises — lack the financial resources to cope with an extended outage.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Jaguar Land Rover Automotive plc</strong> (<strong>JLR</strong>) is based in <strong>Whitley Coventry England</strong> and is the United Kingdom's largest automaker, having been acquired from <strong>Ford</strong> by <strong>Tata Motors</strong> in 2008 for $2.3 billion. <strong>Adrian Mardell</strong> is JLR's CEO. <strong>Tata Motors Limited</strong>, based in <strong>Mumbai India</strong>, is the automotive division of the <strong>Tata Group</strong> — <strong>P.B. Balaji</strong> is CEO of <strong>Tata Motors</strong>. The <strong>JLR Solihull Plant</strong> (<strong>West Midlands England</strong>) manufactures <strong>Range Rover</strong>, <strong>Range Rover Sport</strong>, <strong>Range Rover Velar</strong>, <strong>Discovery</strong>, and <strong>Defender</strong>. The <strong>JLR Halewood Plant</strong> (<strong>Merseyside England</strong>) produces <strong>Range Rover Evoque</strong>, <strong>Discovery Sport</strong>, and is being prepared for new EV models. The <strong>JLR Wolverhampton Engine Manufacturing Centre</strong> (<strong>West Midlands England</strong>) manufactures <strong>Ingenium</strong> petrol and diesel engines. The <strong>JLR Nitra Plant</strong> (<strong>Nitra Slovakia</strong>) produces <strong>Discovery</strong> and <strong>Defender</strong> — with annual capacity of 150,000 vehicles. The <strong>JLR Changshu Plant</strong> (<strong>Jiangsu China</strong>) is a joint venture with <strong>Chery Jaguar Land Rover</strong>, producing <strong>Evoque</strong>, <strong>XE</strong>, <strong>XF</strong>, <strong>E-Pace</strong>, and <strong>F-Pace</strong>. The <strong>JLR Pune Plant</strong> (<strong>Maharashtra India</strong>) is a joint venture with <strong>Tata Motors</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, cyberattacks on the automotive sector have increased dramatically in recent years. <strong>Honda</strong> (2020 <strong>Ekans/Snake</strong> ransomware), <strong>Toyota</strong> (2022 <strong>Kojima Industries</strong> supplier attack), <strong>Volkswagen</strong> (2021 data breach), <strong>Kia</strong> (2021 <strong>DoppelPaymer</strong> ransomware), <strong>Mercedes-Benz</strong> (2022 <strong>Hatch</strong> ransomware), <strong>Stellantis</strong> (2024 <strong>IVR</strong> breach), <strong>Nissan</strong> (2023 data breach), <strong>Hyundai Europe</strong> (2024 <strong>Black Basta</strong> ransomware), <strong>BMW</strong> (2023 supplier attack), <strong>Ferrari</strong> (2023 <strong>RansomEXX</strong>), <strong>Continental</strong> (2022 <strong>LockBit</strong>), <strong>Denso</strong> (2022 <strong>Pandora</strong> ransomware), <strong>Bridgestone</strong> (2022 <strong>LockBit</strong>) are major automotive sector cyberattack incidents. <strong>Auto-ISAC</strong> (<strong>Automotive Information Sharing and Analysis Center</strong>) was established in the U.S. in 2015 for automotive sector cyber threat intelligence sharing. <strong>ISO/SAE 21434</strong> is the automotive cybersecurity engineering standard. <strong>UNECE WP.29 R155</strong> and <strong>R156</strong> require cybersecurity and software update management for vehicle type approval. <strong>TISAX</strong> (<strong>Trusted Information Security Assessment Exchange</strong>) is an information security assessment framework for the European automotive supply chain.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>JLR</strong> employs over 33,000 people in the U.K. and over 104,000 globally — making it a major employment source for the U.K. automotive sector. The U.K. automotive supply chain includes major suppliers such as <strong>GKN</strong>, <strong>Magna International</strong>, <strong>ZF Friedrichshafen</strong>, <strong>Bosch</strong>, <strong>Continental</strong>, <strong>Dana Incorporated</strong>, <strong>BorgWarner</strong>, <strong>Cooper Standard</strong>, <strong>Lear Corporation</strong>, <strong>Adient</strong>, <strong>Aptiv</strong>, and <strong>Autoliv</strong>. <strong>SMMT</strong> (<strong>Society of Motor Manufacturers and Traders</strong>) is the U.K. automotive sector representative body — <strong>Mike Hawes</strong> is Chief Executive. U.K. automotive production stood at 905,000 vehicles in 2024 — declining from a 1999 peak of 1.98 million — with <strong>JLR</strong> representing over 40% of production. The post-Brexit <strong>U.K.-EU Trade and Cooperation Agreement</strong> (<strong>TCA</strong>) is critical for the automotive sector — requiring compliance with <strong>rules of origin</strong>. The <strong>NCSC</strong> (<strong>National Cyber Security Centre</strong>) is the U.K. cybersecurity agency — playing a role in investigations related to the <strong>JLR</strong> attack. In conclusion, the <strong>JLR</strong> cyberattack underscores once again the importance of cyber resilience for the global automotive industry — serving as a critical warning about vulnerabilities in the <strong>just-in-time</strong> supply chain model.</p>

3. Industry sources warn disruption could extend through November.<br>
4. <strong>Tata Motors</strong> is the parent company of <strong>JLR</strong>.<br>
5. Small and medium-sized suppliers face financial difficulties.</p>
