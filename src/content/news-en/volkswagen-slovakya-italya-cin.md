<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Volkswagen</strong> has launched a new <strong>intermodal transport link</strong> between Europe and Asia. The new service runs from the brand's <strong>production facility in Bratislava, Slovakia</strong> to <strong>Venice Port (Marghera) in Italy</strong>. From there, automobiles are transported to <strong>China by maritime shipping</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This new transport model aims to reduce <strong>carbon emissions</strong> and <strong>shorten shipping times</strong> through an <strong>integrated rail and maritime combined transport system</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Rail link: From Bratislava to Marghera Port</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The rail component of the service directly connects Volkswagen's <strong>Bratislava production facility</strong> with <strong>Venice's Marghera Port</strong>. Automobiles transported by rail are loaded onto specialized Ro-Ro (roll-on/roll-off) vessels for shipment to China.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">As Marghera Port becomes <strong>a strategic automotive logistics hub in the Northern Adriatic region</strong>, Volkswagen's operations will further strengthen the port's <strong>Asian connections</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to port authorities, this new service will contribute to the regional economy in terms of both <strong>high-volume automotive transport</strong> and <strong>environmentally friendly intermodal solutions</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic importance of the route</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The creation of this new link is part of <strong>Volkswagen Group's strategy to diversify its Europe–Asia supply chain network</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
Bratislava is home to <strong>one of Volkswagen's key factories for SUV and electric vehicle production</strong>, and a significant portion of vehicles produced there are exported to Asian markets.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This new route through Marghera to China provides <strong>a direct maritime connection from Central European production centers to Asia</strong>, reducing both <strong>shipping times and road traffic</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, this route offers a <strong>shorter distance to Asia compared to traditional Northern European ports (such as Hamburg or Bremerhaven)</strong> and <strong>significantly reduces fuel consumption and carbon emissions</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sustainable transport objectives</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Volkswagen developed this project as part of its <strong>decarbonization strategy in logistics</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The company aims to <strong>reduce logistics-related CO₂ emissions by 30% by 2030</strong> by <strong>increasing the share of rail transport in its European operations</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Through the Bratislava–Marghera connection:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Using rail transport instead of road shipping</strong> will significantly reduce carbon emissions,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Renewable energy sources</strong> used at the port will further lower environmental impact,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>combined transport model</strong> will set an example for sustainable supply chains.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Marghera Port's transformation</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new service strengthens <strong>Marghera Port's importance in international automotive and Ro-Ro transport</strong>.<br>
Infrastructure investments at the port in recent years have improved operational efficiency through <strong>high-capacity rail lines</strong> and <strong>automotive loading terminals</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Port management notes that Volkswagen's selection of this route demonstrates that <strong>Italy's northern ports have become strategic hubs for trade with China</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Volkswagen's global logistics vision</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Volkswagen aims to build an <strong>integrated intermodal logistics network</strong> between its production facilities in Europe and Asian markets.</p>
<p><br>
The new Bratislava–Venice–China link is seen as one of the first concrete steps toward this vision.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This initiative was developed to <strong>respond more flexibly to growing automotive demand in China</strong>, <strong>reduce geopolitical risks in the supply chain</strong>, and <strong>make European production more integrated into global logistics networks</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: A sustainable connection between Europe and Asia</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new intermodal service accelerates <strong>Volkswagen's logistics transformation</strong> while strengthening <strong>sustainable trade between Europe and China</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Sending vehicles from Bratislava to China via Marghera stands out as <strong>a supply chain model with high energy efficiency and low emissions</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Volkswagen</strong> has launched <strong>a new intermodal transport service</strong> on the <strong>Slovakia (Bratislava) – Italy (Marghera) – China</strong> route.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The rail and maritime combination provides <strong>faster and more environmentally friendly logistics solutions</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Marghera Port</strong> is becoming <strong>a new Ro-Ro hub for Europe–Asia trade</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Target: <strong>Reduce logistics emissions by 30% by 2030.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The route is a <strong>strategic link directly connecting Central European production centers to Asia</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The new connection is viewed as <strong>an important part of Volkswagen's sustainable supply chain vision</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
