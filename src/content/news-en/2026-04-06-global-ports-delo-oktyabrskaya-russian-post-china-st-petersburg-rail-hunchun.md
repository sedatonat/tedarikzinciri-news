<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Global Ports</strong>, a stevedoring holding incorporated into <strong>Delo Group</strong>, <strong>Oktyabrskaya Railway</strong> (a subsidiary of Russian Railways), and <strong>Postal Logistics Company</strong> (a subsidiary of Russian Post) have agreed to develop a regular railway container service from China to Northwest Russia. A tripartite agreement was signed on <strong>2 April</strong> in <strong>St. Petersburg</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The new service is planned to launch by the end of the second quarter of 2026. It is aimed at delivering <strong>mail and parcels, consumer goods, clothing, and footwear</strong> to Russia. The <strong>estimated transit time from China to St. Petersburg is 15 days</strong>. This saves nearly half compared with ocean shipping via the Cape of Good Hope.</p><p style="text-align: justify">Block trains will be routed from <strong>Hunchun in China</strong> via the Kamyshovaya border crossing into the Russian Federation. The line will advance northwest along the Trans-Siberian mainline, using the Vladivostok-Moscow route. St. Petersburg is positioned as a hub for distribution to Baltic markets and Northern Europe. Delo Group's terminal infrastructure, Russian Railways' rail network, and Russian Post's distribution capacity together provide an integrated end-to-end service. Rising e-commerce volumes and Russian consumer demand for Chinese goods form the basis for the service's long-term success.</p>
