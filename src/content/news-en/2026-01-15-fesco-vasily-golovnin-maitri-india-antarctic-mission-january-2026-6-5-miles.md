<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>"Vassily Golovnin" diesel-electric icebreaker</strong> operated by <strong>FESCO Transport Group</strong> has reached the <strong>Indian Maitri station</strong> in the Antarctic. According to the Group, Maitri is the first stop in "Vassily Golovnin"'s this year's Antarctic mission. The mission is being performed under FESCO's contract with <strong>India's National Centre for Polar and Ocean Research</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The icebreaker set sail from <strong>Cape Town</strong> in late December, and <strong>the expedition will continue until April 2026</strong>. "Vassily Golovnin" will visit two Indian polar stations — <strong>Bharrati and Maitri</strong> — <strong>delivering fuel and supplies</strong>. This is a continuation of FESCO's long-running Antarctic cooperation with India.</p><p style="text-align: justify">Due to the harsh ice conditions, the vessel cannot approach the shore closer than 6.5 miles. The remaining distance is completed using small auxiliary vessels and helicopters. The Maitri station is India's permanent research base on Queen Maud Land, conducting work in climate, geology, and biology. FESCO's Antarctic operations serve as a showcase of Russia's polar maritime capability and stand out as one of the rare channels of international scientific cooperation during the sanctions era.</p>
