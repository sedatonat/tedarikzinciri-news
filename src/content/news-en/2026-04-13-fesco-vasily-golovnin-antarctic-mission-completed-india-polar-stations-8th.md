<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>FESCO Transport Group</strong> has reported successfully completing the Antarctic mission performed by its <strong>diesel-electric icebreaking vessel "Vasily Golovnin"</strong>. The vessel returned to the starting point in <strong>Cape Town, South Africa</strong> after a <strong>102-day</strong> voyage of more than <strong>10,000 miles</strong>, during which it visited the Indian polar stations <strong>Baharati</strong> and <strong>Maitri</strong> (twice).</p><p style="text-align: justify">The vessel also spent several days off <strong>Queen Maud Land</strong> where the crew assisted Indian researchers in their experiments and data collection. This was the <strong>eighth Antarctic mission</strong> accomplished by the "Vasily Golovnin" within the framework of FESCO's cooperation with India's <strong>National Centre for Polar and Ocean Research</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">During the voyage, more than 1,000 tons of scientific equipment, food, fuel, and medical supplies were delivered to India's polar stations. Antarctica's harsh climate requires specially reinforced icebreaking-capable vessels. Although the Vasily Golovnin was built in 1988, it maintains its performance through regular modernisation investments. FESCO emphasised that the long-running India cooperation is evidence of the Russian maritime sector continuing to play a key role in global scientific research missions.</p>
