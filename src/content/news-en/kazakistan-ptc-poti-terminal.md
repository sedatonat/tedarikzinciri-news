<p style="text-align: justify"><a href="https://tr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kazakistan_Demiryollar%C4%B1"><u><strong>Kazakhstan Railways</strong></u><u> (</u><u><strong>KTZ</strong></u><u>)</u></a> and transport and logistics services provider <a href="https://ptc-holding.kz/"><u><strong>PTC Holding</strong></u></a> have signed a memorandum of understanding to build a multimodal container terminal with annual capacity of 80,000 TEU in the Georgian city of Poti. The project, which will attract a total investment of 30 million dollars, will form part of the <a href="https://www.utikad.org.tr/Detay/Sektor-Haberleri/30964/trans-hazar-uluslararasi-tasima-guzerg%C3%A2hi-titr-uyeleri-kazakistan-da-bir-araya-geldi"><u><strong>Trans-Caspian International Transport Route</strong></u><u> (</u><u><strong>TITR</strong></u><u>)</u></a> and will enhance the route's efficiency while strengthening its position in international cargo transportation.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The terminal construction is planned to handle increasing cargo volumes along the TITR and optimize logistics processes. This initiative is seen as part of broader efforts to improve infrastructure in the region and eliminate bottlenecks. The governments of Kazakhstan and Georgia aim through such projects to increase the route's capacity and boost their competitive strength in the international cargo transportation market.&nbsp;</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The terminal to be built at Poti Port will cover 9 hectares and have annual capacity to process 80,000 containers. This terminal will facilitate cargo flow along the TITR by enabling transshipment between the universal park and containers. In 2024, transportation volume between Kazakhstan and Georgia increased by 62 percent to reach 4.5 million tonnes. This growth is viewed as an indicator of efforts to increase the route's capacity and optimize logistics processes.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The terminal's construction is regarded as part of Kazakhstan's strategy to expand its network of overseas terminals. This project aims to strengthen Kazakhstan's role as a transit hub in Central Asia and increase trade flows in the region.
