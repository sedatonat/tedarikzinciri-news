<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Greece-based shipowner Metrostar Management</strong> announced its complete exit from the <strong>crude oil shipping market</strong> with the sale of its final two <strong>Suezmax tankers</strong>. The company's decision is viewed as a result of ongoing fleet restructuring strategy pursued in recent years.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under operations led by <strong>Theodore Angelopoulos</strong>, Metrostar sold the two vessels <strong>Crude Levante</strong> and <strong>Crude Zephyrus</strong>, both built in 2021, to <strong>Delta Tankers</strong>. Each tanker has a capacity of <strong>156,800 DWT</strong> and was constructed at <strong>New Times Shipbuilding</strong> in China.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to market sources, the sale price reached <strong>approximately 78 million dollars per vessel</strong>, totaling <strong>156 million dollars</strong>. This figure is <strong>significantly above</strong> the <strong>56–57 million dollar price range</strong> for similar <strong>Suezmax newbuilding orders</strong> placed in China during 2018–2019. According to experts, this indicates that <strong>strong price levels in the secondhand tanker market</strong> remain intact.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Both vessels are equipped with engines compliant with <strong>Tier III</strong> emission standards and have <strong>Ballast Water Treatment System (BWTS)</strong> installations. The vessels are scheduled to undergo <strong>special survey in mid-2026</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">With this sale, Metrostar has disposed of its remaining crude oil assets and decided to focus exclusively on <strong>product tankers</strong>. The company's fleet currently includes <strong>LR1 and LR2</strong> type product tankers. Over the past several years, Metrostar has been systematically selling vessels in the <strong>Aframax and Suezmax segments</strong>, capitalizing on strong secondhand values and gradually reducing its exposure in this space.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The company's strategic restructuring process is viewed as a precaution against uncertainties in the energy shipping segment, driven by <strong>rising operating costs</strong>, <strong>decarbonization pressures</strong>, and <strong>market volatility</strong>. According to analysts, Metrostar's exit from crude oil is an indicator of <strong>environmental and financial sustainability-focused transformation in the global tanker fleet</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Meanwhile, buyer <strong>Delta Tankers</strong> has further strengthened its <strong>robust Suezmax fleet of approximately 30 vessels</strong> through this transaction. Under the management of <strong>Diamantis Diamantidis</strong>, the company ranks among Europe's largest Suezmax operators. Delta's fleet also includes <strong>numerous VLCCs (Very Large Crude Carriers)</strong> and one <strong>Aframax tanker</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Recent price increases in the tanker market have continued due to <strong>trade route changes stemming from Russian sources</strong>, <strong>rising freight earnings</strong>, and <strong>limited newbuilding supply</strong>. These price fluctuations in the Suezmax segment have driven <strong>acceleration in secondhand tanker transactions</strong> among both investors and shipowners.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Following this sale, Metrostar will continue to concentrate on the <strong>product shipping market</strong> with a more agile and lower-carbon fleet. The company's focus centers on <strong>transportation of refinery products, clean fuels, and petrochemicals</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Experts characterize Metrostar's move as a "<strong>strategic refocus</strong>", while noting that <strong>Delta Tankers'</strong> aggressive fleet expansion policy signals the continuation of <strong>strong demand in the Suezmax segment</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>

  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Vessels sold: <strong>Crude Levante</strong> and <strong>Crude Zephyrus</strong>, each <strong>156,800 DWT</strong>.</p></li>
