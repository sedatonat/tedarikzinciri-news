<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Freightos</strong>, a leading player in digital freight logistics and shipping technology, is undergoing a significant management restructuring. The company's founder and CEO, <strong>Zvi Schreiber</strong>, has announced his intention to step down from the CEO role effective January 31, 2026. According to Freightos's announcement, Schreiber will not sever ties with the company entirely; following the transition, he will remain with Freightos as a <strong>non-executive board member</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The decision is viewed as part of Freightos's transition into the "<strong>next phase</strong>" of its development. As the company shifts from founder-led management to professionally scaled operations, operational focus is expected to sharpen around growth, profitability, and global expansion. During this transition, the current <strong>Chief Financial Officer (CFO)</strong>, <strong>Pablo Pinillos</strong>, will assume the role of <strong>interim CEO</strong> effective <strong>February 1, 2026</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In his statement, Zvi Schreiber underscored the scale and maturity that Freightos has achieved. Schreiber indicated that the company now requires a different leadership profile, emphasizing that the incoming CEO's focus on the <strong>scale-up</strong> phase will be critical. This approach reflects the founder-to-professional-manager transition model commonly observed in technology startups, now being implemented at Freightos as well.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Freightos has established itself as a significant player in the global logistics ecosystem in recent years through its digital freight marketplaces and pricing transparency solutions. Particularly during the post-pandemic period marked by freight volatility, supply chain disruptions, and price fluctuations, Freightos's offerings in <strong>real-time pricing</strong>, <strong>digital booking</strong>, and <strong>marketplace</strong> solutions gained prominence. Through this process, the company has evolved beyond being merely a technology provider into becoming a data producer and market benchmark.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Schreiber's departure from the CEO role does not signal the end of this growth story. Rather, it marks the company's transition into a new corporate phase. The founder's continued presence on the board ensures strategic continuity while opening the way for more operationally and scale-focused leadership execution. For investors and business partners, this transition conveys a message of <strong>managed continuity</strong> rather than uncertainty.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The fact that Pablo Pinillos, who assumes the interim CEO role, has a finance background is a noteworthy element. Freightos is expected to place greater emphasis on <strong>profitability</strong>, <strong>cash management</strong>, and <strong>operational efficiency</strong> in the coming period. Sustainable financial performance—one of the most pressing challenges digital platforms face post-growth—gains priority with this leadership choice.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The company has launched its search process for a permanent CEO. The direction Freightos takes during this period will be decisive in guiding management selections. Among possible scenarios, a leader profile that would accelerate integration with global logistics players or a manager with expertise in SaaS and marketplace scaling stands out. In either case, Freightos appears to be targeting an organizational structure governed by <strong>corporate discipline rather than founder-driven reflexes</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Zvi Schreiber's use of the phrase "moving toward new ventures" in his statements also signals a personal turning point. However, in the Freightos case, this departure is positioned as a planned and strategic transition rather than an abrupt or crisis-driven change. In this respect, the decision reflects a typical evolution followed by maturing ventures in the logistics technology sector.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In broad terms, the CEO transition at Freightos demonstrates that the company has moved beyond the <strong>foundation and product validation</strong> phase and is now entering an era of <strong>scaling, consolidation, and institutionalization</strong>. The steps taken in the coming period will serve to further clarify Freightos's role within the global logistics ecosystem.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Zvi Schreiber will step down from his role as Freightos CEO effective January 31, 2026.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Schreiber will remain with the company as a non-executive board member.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">CFO Pablo Pinillos will assume the interim CEO role beginning February 1, 2026.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The transition is associated with Freightos's entry into a new growth phase and scaling efforts.</p></li>

</ul>
