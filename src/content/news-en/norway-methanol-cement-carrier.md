<p style="text-align: justify">Germany-based <strong>Hartmann Group</strong> and <strong>Heidelberg Materials Northern Europe</strong> have decided to deploy a <strong>new methanol-fueled cement carrier</strong> for Norwegian coastal shipping operations. According to the company statement, the project aims to move <strong>away from fossil fuels</strong> in Norwegian domestic maritime transport and significantly reduce carbon footprint. The new vessel is being designed in compliance with Norway's strict environmental regulations and green shipping policies.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The project is supported by <strong>60 million NOK</strong> in grants through Norway's <strong>NOx Fund</strong> mechanism and falls within the country's <strong>Green Shipping Programme</strong>, which is based on public-private partnership cooperation. Authorities note that these subsidies not only alleviate the financial burden on the project, but also accelerate the commercialization of alternative-fuel vessels.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ownership and Operational Structure</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under the agreement, the <strong>design, ownership, and operation of the vessel will be undertaken by Hartmann Group</strong>. The operational structure is configured as follows:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Hartmann Reederei</strong>: Ship design and general ownership,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>United Bulk Carriers USA</strong>: Operational management,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>InterMaritime Shipmanagement</strong>: Technical management.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This structure represents a typical example of <strong>Hartmann Group's multi-layered ship management model</strong>. The new vessel will operate under a <strong>10-year time charter</strong> agreement with <strong>Heidelberg Materials Norge</strong>. This long-term contract ensures the economic sustainability of the investment for both the ship owner and the cargo owner.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Technical Specifications and Cargo Capacity</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The newly built vessel will be designed with a <strong>dual-fuel</strong> configuration, using <strong>green methanol</strong> as the primary fuel. This approach enables the ship to align with <strong>carbon-neutral fuel supply chains</strong> in the long term. The vessel will have a cargo capacity of approximately <strong>9,000 tons of cement</strong>, representing <strong>approximately 1,000 tons greater cargo capacity</strong> compared to the current fossil-fueled vessel in operation.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The vessel will conduct regular <strong>high-frequency shuttle services</strong> from <strong>Heidelberg Materials' Brevik facility</strong> to Norway's major ports. The ports to be served include:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Oslo</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Bergen</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Kristiansand</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Stavanger</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This model reduces dependence on road transport and delivers a <strong>lower-emission, more predictable</strong> logistics solution for coastal shipping.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Emission and Efficiency Impact</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The ship hull and propulsion systems are being designed according to <strong>energy-optimised hull</strong> principles. According to the company statement, this will enable the vessel to deliver <strong>annual CO₂ savings of approximately 6,000 tons</strong> compared to current fossil-fueled tonnage. Overall, <strong>approximately 80 percent emission reduction</strong> is targeted on the route.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Knut Omreng</strong>, Logistics Director at <strong>Heidelberg Materials Norge</strong>, states that the project delivers benefits not only environmentally but also operationally. According to Omreng, the new vessel provides <strong>transport efficiency</strong> by carrying more cargo while fully aligning with Norway's climate goals.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic Context: Green Shipping and Cement Logistics</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Cement transport is recognized as an <strong>energy-intensive</strong> logistics activity due to its high volume and low unit value. For this reason, Heidelberg Materials views carbon reduction in maritime transport as a <strong>critical lever</strong>. The company ships over <strong>3.5 million tons of cement</strong> annually in the Nordic and Baltic regions. Given volumes of this scale, the emission reduction achieved by a single vessel makes a meaningful difference to total carbon footprint.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Hartmann Group</strong> is positioned as a shipping company specialized in this sector through its fleet of <strong>pneumatic self-discharging cement carriers</strong>. The new methanol-fueled vessel represents an investment aligned with the <strong>group's strategic direction</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Overall Assessment</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The methanol-fueled cement vessel to be deployed by Hartmann Group and Heidelberg Materials for Norway demonstrates that <strong>alternative fuels are not limited to container or passenger ships alone</strong>, but are rapidly expanding into bulk and industrial cargo transport. The long-term charter model, public support, and clear emission targets demonstrate that such projects are becoming commercially viable.</p>
<p><br></p>
