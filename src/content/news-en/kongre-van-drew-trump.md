<p style="text-align: justify">Republican Congressman <strong>Jeff Van Drew</strong> is working with President-elect <strong>Donald Trump</strong> to draft an executive order aimed at halting offshore wind turbine operations along the East Coast.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Van Drew has criticized the approval process for these projects, characterizing them as "an economic and environmental disaster."</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This initiative comes at a critical moment not only for New Jersey's ambitious offshore wind energy plans, but also for the nation's emerging offshore wind sector.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The state aims to generate 11 gigawatts of offshore wind electricity by 2040, representing 30 percent of East Coast wind energy production.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In October 2024, the <strong>Bureau of Ocean Energy Management (BOEM)</strong> approved the <strong>Atlantic Shores South</strong> project, which will be located off New Jersey's coast.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The project comprises two facilities that will generate 2,800 megawatts of power, enough to supply approximately one million homes.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Van Drew has consistently opposed offshore wind energy development and has voiced growing concerns about the projects' impacts.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Previously, he has highlighted specific incidents, including a turbine malfunction in Nantucket, and warned of potential environmental risks, noise violations, and rising costs to taxpayers.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In 2023, he introduced legislation proposing a complete moratorium on all current and future offshore wind projects.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">An executive order draft obtained by <strong>Heatmap News</strong> calls for "suspending offshore wind development."</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Beyond halting new permits, the draft would impose a moratorium on all offshore wind projects currently under construction.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The proposed executive order directly conflicts with the <strong>Biden-Harris</strong> administration's goal of installing 30 gigawatts of offshore wind capacity by 2030.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">President-elect Trump has been an outspoken opponent of offshore wind energy development and pledged to sign an executive order targeting the sector on his first day in office.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Previously, he has repeatedly criticized offshore wind projects as fatal to birds and whales.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">To date, the Biden administration has approved 11 commercial-scale offshore wind projects, which collectively generate 19 gigawatts (GW) of clean energy—enough to supply over 6 million homes.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>BOEM</strong> has held six offshore wind lease auctions since 2022, including a record-breaking sale off New York and the first-ever sales off the coasts of the Pacific Ocean, the Gulf of Mexico, and the Gulf of Maine.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Atlantic Shores</strong> project, a joint venture between <strong>Shell New Energies US</strong> and <strong>EDF Renewables</strong>, along with many other projects in the development stage, faces an uncertain future as the incoming administration prepares to adopt an entirely different approach to offshore wind energy development.</p>
