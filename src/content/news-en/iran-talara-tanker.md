<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Talara</strong>, a Marshall Islands-flagged tanker, <strong>had its course altered by Iranian forces</strong> while transiting through the Gulf of Oman. The tanker was carrying <strong>high-sulfur fuel oil from the United Arab Emirates to Singapore</strong>. The incident has been corroborated by maritime security firms, the <strong>UK Maritime Trade Operations (UKMTO)</strong> unit, and the vessel's technical manager.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The vessel's course abruptly changed <strong>20–22 nautical miles east of Khor Fakkan</strong>. Vessel tracking data showed the Talara heading toward Iran's Cask terminal and the AIS signal cutting out in that area <strong>following the sudden course change</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>IRGC intervention alleged</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Security firm <strong>Ambrey</strong> reported that three small vessels approached the Talara, and the ship immediately altered its course thereafter.</p>
<p><br>
Maritime security firm <strong>Vanguard Tech</strong> stated that the incident was carried out by the <strong>Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps Navy (IRGC Navy)</strong> and that the vessel had been ordered to head toward Iranian shores.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">UKMTO confirmed a verified incident in the region and assessed it as potentially <strong>"state-sponsored activity."</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ship operator: Contact with crew lost</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Technical manager <strong>Columbia Shipmanagement</strong> reported that contact with the vessel was lost at <strong>08:22 local time</strong>. The ship was transiting off Khor Fakkan at that moment. The company stated it was coordinating with relevant authorities and the vessel owner <strong>Pasha Finance Inc.</strong>, with <strong>crew safety</strong> being the primary concern.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">No additional reports have been made regarding any <strong>injuries, damage,</strong> or security risks in the incident. Iranian authorities and UAE officials did not respond to requests for comment on the matter.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A critical incident in the context of regional tensions</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The waters surrounding the Gulf of Oman and the Strait of Hormuz have witnessed numerous ship seizures, interventions, and state-sponsored operations in recent years. Iran is a known actor that has at times applied <strong>forced diversion</strong> to foreign-flagged vessels on grounds of various sanctions or legal disputes.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Talara incident is assessed to potentially:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">impact commercial vessel security in the region,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">disrupt energy transport flows,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">increase insurance premiums<br>
again under pressure.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">In similar incidents in the past, the Lloyd's Market Association categorized the Gulf of Oman as a "high-risk zone." Such incidents, particularly on routes carrying high-sulfur products and oil derivatives, increase the need for <strong>risk management and escort requirements.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>What does the AIS data show?</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to maritime monitoring sources, the Talara's <strong>last AIS signal</strong> was recorded near Iran's oil terminal at <strong>Jask</strong>. This indicates that the vessel entered Iranian territorial waters and was likely brought under official control.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The vessel's current status, the health and security conditions of the crew, and whether the ship has been formally seized have not yet been verified.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The Marshall Islands-flagged <strong>Talara</strong> tanker was diverted to Iranian shores by Iranian forces.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The incident occurred <strong>20–22 nm</strong> east of Khor Fakkan.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Ambrey and Vanguard Tech report <strong>IRGC Navy intervention.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The ship's operator reported contact with crew was lost at <strong>08:22.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The last AIS signal was received near <strong>Jask terminal.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Iranian and UAE authorities have not issued any statement on the matter.</p></li>
</ul>
