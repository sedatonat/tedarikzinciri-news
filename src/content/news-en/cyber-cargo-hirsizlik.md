<p style="text-align: justify">A new wave of threats has emerged in the global logistics sector. According to a report published by <strong>Proofpoint Inc.</strong>, <strong>cyber criminals are collaborating with organized crime organizations to steal shipments from trucking companies.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Proofpoint, based in California</strong>, reported that attackers are <strong>specifically targeting trucking companies and freight brokers.</strong> Attacks are being carried out by deploying <strong>malware that provides remote access to company networks.</strong> The ultimate goal is to <strong>seize shipments and sell the stolen goods in online marketplaces or overseas.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>"A full-scale supply chain threat"</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Selena Larson, senior threat intelligence analyst at Proofpoint, emphasized that <strong>these attacks affect not just individual companies but the entire supply chain ecosystem:</strong></p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"The impact of these attacks extends from ships at ports to truck drivers to retailers. Ultimately, this chain reaches consumers. This is a full-scale supply chain threat."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Attacks are being carried out primarily through <strong>load boards.</strong> Cyber criminals infiltrate <strong>online systems where load bookings are arranged between carriers and brokers.</strong> They then send <strong>"online setup packet" documents containing malicious links via fake emails.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The software used by attackers typically appears to be <strong>legitimate remote access tools,</strong> which makes detection difficult.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>How do the attacks work?</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Proofpoint's research, criminals first <strong>infiltrate carrier company systems to steal credentials.</strong> They then use these credentials to send <strong>fake load postings</strong> to brokers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">When a carrier responds to one of these postings, hackers send <strong>load details containing a malicious link</strong> via email. Because carriers must act quickly due to high demand, they typically click on these links, allowing hackers to compromise their networks.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Proofpoint researcher <strong>Ole Villadsen</strong> explained the process:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"There is enormous pressure to grab loads. Companies move quickly to avoid missing a new load opportunity. This haste makes the attackers' job easier."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Attacks could cost billions</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to <strong>National Insurance Crime Bureau (NICB)</strong> data, <strong>cargo thefts increased by 27% in 2024</strong> with <strong>a further 22% increase expected in 2025.</strong> Currently, it is estimated that <strong>approximately 35 billion dollars</strong> in cargo is stolen annually.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Stolen products typically include <strong>energy drinks, electronics, and food products.</strong> Larson notes that <strong>energy drinks in particular have become targets because they are banned or restricted from sale in many countries overseas.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A global problem</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Proofpoint emphasizes that attacks are primarily directed at <strong>cargo companies in North America but are becoming a global threat.</strong> Researchers have findings suggesting that hacker groups <strong>may originate from Russia or Eastern Europe.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Villadsen described these attacks as <strong>"the marriage of cybercrime and organized crime":</strong></p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"This represents the point where the digital world intersects with underground criminal networks. Cyber attacks not only steal data, but also target physical goods."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Urgent call for industry-wide action</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Experts note that companies must <strong>increase cybersecurity awareness against such attacks,</strong> <strong>better protect load boards,</strong> and <strong>invest in employee training.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Proofpoint has issued warnings to <strong>freight brokers and carrier firms</strong> to <strong>not open links in incoming emails without verifying their source</strong> and to <strong>enable load verification systems with two-factor authentication.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Larson noted that countering this threat requires <strong>coordinated efforts among law enforcement, the private sector, and users,</strong> stating:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"This is no longer just a cyber attack—it's a physical supply chain threat. We can only stop it by working together."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Proofpoint Inc.</strong> found evidence that <strong>hackers are collaborating with organized crime networks to commit cargo theft.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Attacks are being conducted through <strong>load boards</strong> and <strong>fraudulent email campaigns.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Cargo thefts increased by 27% in 2024,</strong> with <strong>a further 22% increase expected in 2025.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Annual losses: approximately 35 billion dollars.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Most targeted products: <strong>energy drinks, food, and electronics.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Proofpoint: "This is <strong>the convergence of cybercrime and organized crime</strong> – a new threat model for the global supply chain."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Call to companies: <strong>Two-factor authentication, load verification systems, and staff awareness training</strong> are critical measures.</p></li>
</ul>
