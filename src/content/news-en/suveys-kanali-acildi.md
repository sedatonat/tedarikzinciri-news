<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sea-Intelligence</strong> released a new analysis showing that should <strong>transits through the Suez Canal resume following an Israel–Hamas ceasefire</strong>, the global container shipping sector could experience significant capacity relief.</p>\n<p><br>\nAccording to the analysis, reactivating the Suez route could <strong>free up approximately 2.1 million TEU of shipping capacity (equivalent to 6.5% of the global fleet).</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">This development has the potential to <strong>fundamentally reshape trade balances in Asia–Europe and Asia–US East Coast shipping</strong> by recovering vessel capacity currently locked into <strong>longer routes circumnavigating Africa.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Post-Ceasefire Scenario: Two Major Impacts on Global Shipping</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">According to Sea-Intelligence's modeling, reopening the Suez route will generate two primary effects:</p>\n<ol>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Significant relief in global fleet capacity:</strong><br>\nVessels returning from the longer African circumnavigation routes will redeploy to the Asia–Europe lane, <strong>reducing transit times</strong> and <strong>lowering shipping costs.</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Near-term congestion surge at European ports:</strong><br>\nWith cargo from Asia arriving in Europe simultaneously, <strong>record increases in handling volumes</strong> could occur.</p></li>\n</ol>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Suez Closure: Long Routes and Capacity Squeeze in 2025</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Since late 2024, escalating Middle East tensions and <strong>Houthi attacks have severely curtailed transits through the Suez Canal.</strong></p>\n<p><br>\nShippers have been forced to divert to longer routes via the <strong>Cape of Good Hope</strong> for Asia–Europe and Asia–US East Coast services, <strong>requiring approximately four additional vessels per trade route.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">These longer routes, while driving up fuel costs, have effectively <strong>"locked in" a substantial portion of global vessel supply.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Various Reopening Scenarios: Immediate or Phased Transition</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">While <strong>Sea-Intelligence has not forecasted when Suez transits will resume</strong>, it modeled several scenarios:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Immediate switch</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Phased returns over 2, 4, and 6 weeks</strong></p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Under an immediate switch scenario:</strong></p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Asia–Europe import flows would <strong>double for a two-week period</strong>,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">European ports' handling volumes would spike <strong>to 39% above previous record levels.</strong></p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Even in an eight-week phased transition</strong>, port handling volumes would still run <strong>10% above historical peaks.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Challenge for European Ports: A New Wave of Congestion</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Sea-Intelligence cites the capacity crunch experienced in March 2025, emphasizing that <strong>Suez Canal reopening could strain European port infrastructure once again.</strong></p>\n<p><br>\nA sudden surge in cargo flow would increase the risk of <strong>berth congestion and container backlogs</strong>, particularly at major hubs like <strong>Rotterdam, Antwerp-Bruges, and Hamburg.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In the near term, <strong>port operations would need to scale up</strong>, while longer-term adjustments would restore <strong>more balanced vessel routes and delivery cycles.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Global Impact: A Rebalancing of Logistics</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Reopening the Suez route would bring relief not only to <strong>Asia–Europe services</strong> but also to <strong>Asia–US East Coast trade.</strong></p>\n<p><br>\nShorter transit times, fuel savings, and fleet optimization could contribute to <strong>lower freight rates.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, <strong>delivery times from Asian production centers to Europe and the US could shorten by 10 to 14 days</strong>, potentially <strong>accelerating inventory turns and rebalancing global supply chains.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: 2.1 Million TEU of Capacity Returns</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">According to Sea-Intelligence's estimate, as Suez transits normalize:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Approximately 2.1 million TEU of capacity</strong>, or <strong>6.5% of the active global fleet</strong>,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">will become available for redeployment <strong>after being released from extended African circumnavigation routes.</strong></p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">This represents the largest capacity recovery seen since the post-pandemic period.<br>\nHowever, experts caution that resuming transits could trigger a complex transition: <strong>near-term port congestion</strong> followed by <strong>mid-term freight rate declines.</strong></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sea-Intelligence</strong> disclosed that Suez Canal reopening could <strong>release 2.1 million TEU of capacity.</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">This equates to <strong>6.5% of the global active container fleet.</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The need for <strong>four additional vessels per route</strong> on <strong>Asia–Europe and Asia–US East Coast services</strong> would be eliminated.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Under an <strong>immediate return scenario</strong>, European ports could see <strong>39% surge in volumes.</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Even with <strong>phased transitions, a 10% increase</strong> is anticipated.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Analysts forecast <strong>near-term port congestion</strong>, followed by <strong>longer-term freight stabilization.</strong></p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Suez reopening</strong> could initiate <strong>a strategic period of normalization in global
