<p style="text-align: justify">Russia-based <strong>Ametist Line</strong> has announced the launch of a new <strong>single-vessel shuttle service</strong> between <strong>Novorossiysk and Izmir</strong> in the first months of 2026. The new service is viewed as a strategic expansion to strengthen the company's presence on the Black Sea–East Mediterranean corridor. The shuttle line will operate with a single 500 TEU-capacity vessel, deployed from the existing <strong>Novorossiysk–Mersin–Beirut</strong> route that currently serves this vessel. The company indicates that the aforementioned legacy route may be entirely <strong>discontinued</strong> as a result of this development.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Ametist Line currently operates three additional Novorossiysk-based <strong>single-vessel loops</strong>. These routes operate vessels in the <strong>700 TEU</strong> and <strong>900 TEU</strong> capacity range and connect the Black Sea with major East Mediterranean ports. The company's current operational network comprises the following connections:</p>
<ol>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Novorossiysk ↔ Ambarlı &amp; Gebze</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Novorossiysk ↔ Mersin &amp; Alexandria</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Novorossiysk ↔ Haifa &amp; Ashdod</strong></p></li>
</ol>
<p style="text-align: justify">This structure positions Ametist Line as one of the region's most active single-vessel feeder operators. The new Izmir route will provide the company with a more comprehensive network across both the Turkish market and the broader East Mediterranean hinterland.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Regional Logistics Context and Strategic Impact</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Novorossiysk–Izmir</strong> shuttle service offers a new alternative, particularly for trade flows extending from the Black Sea to the Aegean Sea. Turkey's Aegean region is a critical hub both in terms of export volume and logistics gateways to Europe. When combined with Izmir's port infrastructure (Aliağa, Alsancak) and Novorossiysk's oil, grain, and container capacity, the route is assessed as potentially creating a new <strong>east–west micro-corridor</strong> for regional trade.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Given the infrastructure constraints experienced in regions served by Ametist Line during 2024–2025, security threats from drone activity, and increasing <strong>Black Sea war-risk premiums</strong>, stable short-distance shuttle services are becoming more attractive to carriers. The fleet's small capacity and single-vessel configuration provide both flexible operational planning and cost-efficient connections. For <strong>regional shippers</strong> in particular, this represents an advantage in making transit times predictable.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The company's deployment of the Izmir route also affects capacity balance between <strong>Turkish ports</strong> and the <strong>Russian Black Sea gateway</strong>. To date, the majority of cargo arriving in Izmir ports from Russia has been directed toward distribution hubs centered around Istanbul and the Marmara region. This new shuttle service can create <strong>direct call</strong> capacity, allowing cargo to bypass the Marmara region and reach the Aegean hinterland directly.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Implications for East Mediterranean–Black Sea Supply Chains</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Ametist Line's move will provide customers with more <strong>routing options</strong>, higher frequency, and competitive advantages in specific markets. In this context:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Acceleration of short-distance trade between Turkey and Russia,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Lower feeder costs for Izmir-based exporters,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Expansion of Novorossiysk's role as a <strong>regional transshipment node</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Furthermore, given rising risks in the Black Sea throughout 2025 (such as <strong>Sea Baby drone</strong> destruction and increasing war insurance premiums), regular shuttle services can help carriers manage their risk profiles.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, the <strong>Novorossiysk–Izmir shuttle</strong> line not only strengthens Ametist Line's network in the East Mediterranean; it also provides smaller and mid-sized shippers in the region with a more predictable, connected, and cost-effective option. From 2026 onwards, the route is expected to create <strong>network coherence</strong> alongside the company's three other shuttle routes.</p>
<h2 style="text-align: justify"><br></h2>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The new shuttle line launches in early 2026 with a single 500 TEU vessel.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The existing <strong>Novorossiysk–Mersin–Beirut</strong> route may close.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The company currently operates three single-vessel loops (700–900 TEU).</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The Izmir route will strengthen Ametist Line's East Mediterranean presence.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The shuttle service may create a new micro-corridor for Black Sea–Aegean trade.</p></li>
</ul>
