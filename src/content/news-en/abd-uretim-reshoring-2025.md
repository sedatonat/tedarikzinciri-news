<p style="text-align: justify">The long-debated "reshoring" agenda in the United States has gained renewed importance as of 2025. However, <strong>Rosemary Coates</strong>, Director of the Reshoring Institute, says this transformation lies somewhere between "<strong>yes and no</strong>" — it is <strong>partially possible</strong>, but <strong>a large-scale and rapid return is not realistic</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Coates recalls that in the 1990s and early 2000s, American companies operated with the goal of "getting to China as quickly as possible," which became a reflex across the sector. Few firms at that time gave thought to long-term strategy, risk, or infrastructure. While the trend began to reverse after the 2012 elections and China discussions, the real turning point emerged with the US-China trade wars.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>C-suite white paper: "We want to manufacture in the US, but only if the economics allow"</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">In 2025, the Reshoring Institute conducted interviews with 18 major US company executives and identified these common trends:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">CEOs have <strong>halted investment and hiring</strong>; they do not want to move without clarity on the trade environment.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">All of them would <strong>prefer</strong> to manufacture in the US, but say it is not possible without <strong>cost advantages</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Tariff costs will inevitably be <strong>passed on to consumers</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">All executives are evaluating <strong>Mexico</strong> as an alternative to China.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The "China +1/+2" model is now the <strong>new standard</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Coates, one of the fastest growing trends is <strong>movement toward Mexico</strong>. Both Chinese and American companies are setting up manufacturing facilities in Mexico to benefit from USMCA advantages. The wage differential is striking: hourly wages in San Diego stand at $17.25, while in Tijuana they are <strong>$2.59</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>The type of manufacturing that can return to the US: not low-skill labor, but sophisticated production</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Coates, the manufacturing that can feasibly return to the US is not labor-intensive sectors like <strong>footwear, textiles, or low-budget electronics assembly</strong> — sectors that traditionally employ workers for long shifts at low wages.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Manufacturing in the US must be based on a modern model:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Robotics and automation</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>3D printing</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>AI-based production scheduling systems</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>MES systems</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>High-precision machinery</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">In Coates' words:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Manufacturing in the US is clean, automation-supported, based on shorter working hours, and generally requires higher skill levels."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">For this reason, footwear or small electronics manufacturing cannot realistically return to the US; however, advanced machine manufacturing, chip assembly, precision metal processing, and automation-heavy production can make a comeback.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Critical infrastructure the US lacks: factories, workforce, transportation, energy</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Coates lists the most critical obstacles facing the US as follows:</p>
<p><br></p>
<h6 style="text-align: justify"><strong>1. Skill shortage</strong></h6>
<p style="text-align: justify">The US does not face a labor shortage so much as a <strong>skills gap</strong>.<br>
Particularly:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">welders</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">robot operators</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">electrical technicians</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">machinery specialists</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">are in insufficient supply.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h6 style="text-align: justify"><strong>2. Factory shortage</strong></h6>
<p style="text-align: justify">The US lacks "the land and infrastructure suitable for building thousands of new factories."<br>
Building a new factory requires:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">roads, bridges, ports, logistics capacity</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">high energy demand<br>
and the US electrical grid cannot handle the current load.</p></li>
</ul>
<h6 style="text-align: justify"><strong>3. Energy infrastructure</strong></h6>
<p style="text-align: justify">Next-generation production lines require high energy intensity; the US grid needs <strong>10–15 years</strong> of investment to support this.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h6 style="text-align: justify"><strong>4. Logistics weaknesses</strong></h6>
<p style="text-align: justify">In some US regions, transportation to a port can take <strong>3 days</strong> — which is not competitive for global supply chains.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Which products can be manufactured in the US, and which cannot?</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Reshoring Institute evaluates whether a product can be manufactured in the US based on <strong>BOM analysis</strong>.<br>
If <strong>more than 50% of a product's cost</strong> is labor, US manufacturing is <strong>not economically viable</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Therefore:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>high labor-intensity products</strong> → China, Vietnam, Mexico</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>machine-intensive manufacturing</strong> → US or Mexico</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Coates' examples:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Footwear, small electronics</strong>: will not return</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Textile weaving facilities</strong>: feasible through automation</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>High-technology manufacturing</strong>: ideal target for the nation</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">A complete transformation of US manufacturing is <strong>not possible</strong>, but a <strong>strong resurgence in sophisticated manufacturing</strong> can occur.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">CEOs want US manufacturing, but cost calculations are decisive.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Mexico is the strongest alternative with low labor costs and USMCA advantages.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The US's greatest obstacles are: <strong>skills gap</strong>, <strong>factory infrastructure</strong>, <strong>energy capacity</strong>, <strong>logistics weakness</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Labor-intensive manufacturing will not return; automation-focused production can.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">For reshoring, <strong>robotics + 3D printing + AI + automation</strong> are critical.</p></li>
</ul>
