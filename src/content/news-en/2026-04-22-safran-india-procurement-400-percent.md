<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>French</strong> aircraft engine manufacturer <strong>Safran SA</strong> plans to increase component sourcing from <strong>India</strong> fivefold to 500 million euros (579 million dollars) by 2030 — underscoring the country's emerging position as a significant aviation market. <strong>Safran</strong> currently procures approximately 100 million euros' worth of components from the <strong>South Asian</strong> nation but plans to substantially expand its supplier base — according to CEO <strong>Olivier Andriès</strong> in an interview. The company plans to work with more <strong>Indian</strong> partners in line with its long-term growth strategy, he added. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Safran SA</strong>, based in <strong>Paris, France</strong>, was established in 2005 through the merger of <strong>Snecma</strong> and <strong>Sagem</strong> — making it a global aerospace and defense company. <strong>Safran Aircraft Engines</strong> (formerly <strong>Snecma</strong>); <strong>Safran Helicopter Engines</strong> (formerly <strong>Turbomeca</strong>); <strong>Safran Landing Systems</strong>; <strong>Safran Electronics & Defense</strong> (formerly <strong>Sagem</strong>); <strong>Safran Cabin</strong>; <strong>Safran Seats</strong> — are its main business units.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Paris</strong>-based company will open a <strong>LEAP</strong> engine maintenance, repair and overhaul (MRO) center in <strong>Hyderabad</strong>, a <strong>South Indian</strong> city, on November 26. This will help <strong>Indian</strong> airlines access these aircraft services locally instead of traveling abroad — which is more expensive and time-consuming. <strong>Safran Electronics & Defense</strong> signed an agreement with state-owned enterprise <strong>Bharat Electronics Ltd.</strong> earlier this week to manufacture an air-ground weapon system. The <strong>Hammer</strong> system is designed for multiple platforms including <strong>Rafale</strong> fighter aircraft and the <strong>Indian</strong>-built light combat aircraft <strong>Tejas</strong>. Approximately half of the 30 million euro investment comes from <strong>Safran</strong> — according to <strong>Andriès</strong>. From a supply chain perspective, the <strong>LEAP</strong> engine is produced by <strong>CFM International</strong> — a 50:50 joint venture between <strong>Safran Aircraft Engines</strong> and <strong>GE Aerospace</strong>. The <strong>LEAP-1A</strong> is used in the <strong>Airbus A320neo family</strong>; the <strong>LEAP-1B</strong> in the <strong>Boeing 737 MAX</strong>; and the <strong>LEAP-1C</strong> in the <strong>COMAC C919</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>India</strong>'s <strong>Defence Acquisition Procedure 2020</strong> prioritizes domestic manufacturing — requiring foreign manufacturers to reinvest a portion of contract value into the local defense ecosystem. The <strong>Indian Air Force</strong> is purchasing 36 <strong>French Rafale</strong> fighter aircraft — and <strong>Dassault Aviation SA</strong> subsequently confirmed the sale of 26 <strong>Rafale Marine</strong> aircraft to the <strong>Indian Navy</strong>. <strong>Bharat Electronics Limited</strong> (<strong>BEL</strong>), based in <strong>Bangalore</strong>, is <strong>India</strong>'s defense electronics state enterprise. <strong>Hindustan Aeronautics Limited</strong> (<strong>HAL</strong>); <strong>Bharat Dynamics Limited</strong> (<strong>BDL</strong>); <strong>Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited</strong> (<strong>BHEL</strong>); <strong>Mazagon Dock</strong>; <strong>Garden Reach Shipbuilders</strong> — are core players in <strong>India</strong>'s defense industry. <strong>Tata Advanced Systems</strong>; <strong>Mahindra Defence</strong>; <strong>Larsen & Toubro Defence</strong>; <strong>Adani Defence</strong>; <strong>Reliance Defence</strong> — are private sector defense players. <strong>Tejas Mk1A</strong>; <strong>Tejas Mk2</strong>; <strong>AMCA</strong> (<strong>Advanced Medium Combat Aircraft</strong>) — are domestic fighter aircraft programs.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the global aircraft engine market features <strong>Safran/CFM</strong>; <strong>GE Aerospace</strong>; <strong>Pratt & Whitney</strong> (<strong>RTX</strong>); <strong>Rolls-Royce</strong>; <strong>IAE</strong> (<strong>International Aero Engines</strong>); <strong>Engine Alliance</strong> — as major players. <strong>Safran</strong>'s 2024 annual revenue is approximately 27 billion euros. <strong>India</strong> is the world's fastest-growing aviation market — with <strong>IndiGo</strong>; <strong>Air India</strong> (<strong>Tata Group</strong>); <strong>Air India Express</strong>; <strong>Akasa Air</strong>; <strong>SpiceJet</strong>; <strong>Vistara</strong> (merging into <strong>Air India</strong>) — as leading carriers. <strong>IndiGo</strong> alone has placed orders for over 500 <strong>Airbus A320 family</strong> aircraft — the world's largest single airline order book. <strong>Air India</strong> has ordered 470 new aircraft (a mix of <strong>Airbus</strong> and <strong>Boeing</strong>). <strong>Tata Group</strong> is locally assembling <strong>Airbus C295</strong> tactical transport aircraft in <strong>Vadodara</strong>. <strong>Make in India</strong> and <strong>Atmanirbhar Bharat</strong> (Self-Reliant India) policies are accelerating the localization of the aerospace supply chain. <strong>Bangalore</strong>; <strong>Hyderabad</strong>; <strong>Chennai</strong>; <strong>Pune</strong>; <strong>Nagpur</strong>; <strong>Vadodara</strong> — are <strong>India</strong>'s aerospace manufacturing and MRO clusters. In conclusion, <strong>Safran</strong>'s plan to increase <strong>India</strong> sourcing fivefold is a concrete indicator of the era in which global <strong>OEM</strong>s are positioning <strong>India</strong> as a strategic production and MRO hub.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Points:</strong><br>
1. <strong>Safran</strong> plans to increase sourcing from <strong>India</strong> from 100M to 500M euros.<br>
2. <strong>LEAP</strong> engine MRO center opens in <strong>Hyderabad</strong> on November 26.<br>
3. <strong>Hammer</strong> air-ground weapon joint production agreement signed with <strong>Bharat Electronics</strong>.<br>
4. <strong>Indian Air Force</strong> acquiring 36 <strong>Rafale</strong>; <strong>Indian Navy</strong> acquiring 26 <strong>Rafale Marine</strong>.<br>
5. <strong>India Defence Acquisition Procedure 2020</strong> mandates local reinvestment.</p>
