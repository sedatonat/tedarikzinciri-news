<p style="text-align: justify">The Suez Canal is witnessing <strong>the strongest vessel traffic recovery in two years</strong> as tensions in the Red Sea and Bab el-Mandeb region gradually ease. The Suez Canal Authority (SCA) reported that October achieved <strong>the highest monthly transit rate since the end of 2023</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In October, <strong>229 vessels</strong> transited the canal. When evaluating July through October together, total tonnage showed <strong>a 10% annual increase</strong>. While vessel count growth was only <strong>2%</strong>, the data indicates that <strong>larger, more heavily-laden vessels are returning to the canal</strong>. Over this four-month period, transiting vessel numbers exceeded <strong>4,400</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Major shipping lines return to the route</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">SCA Chairman <strong>Admiral Ossama Rabiee</strong> convened a meeting with 20 major shipping lines and agents to discuss the recovery at Suez. The objectives:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Recoordinate transit schedules and tariffs</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Invite lines to conduct "trial runs"</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Rebuild confidence in the Suez route</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">One of the most notable returns came from <strong>CMA CGM</strong>. The line confirmed that two of its <strong>17,000 TEU</strong>-capacity vessels transited the canal under escort by European Union naval forces. CMA CGM plans to increase transit frequency in the coming months.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>MSC, Hapag-Lloyd, COSCO, and Evergreen</strong> and other major operators reported closely monitoring the region, noting that transits will accelerate as security conditions stabilize. However, some lines remain cautious about deploying their largest vessels—particularly <strong>23,000 TEU+ ultra-large</strong> class ships—to the region.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>SCA's strategy: Rebuilding confidence</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Rabiee emphasized that the Suez Canal carries <strong>12% of global trade</strong> and is a vital artery for world commerce. Following the disruptions of the past two years, the primary goal is to convey to shipping lines that "Suez is secure and predictable again."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">During the crisis period, many carriers rerouted their vessels around <strong>the Cape of Good Hope</strong>, a shift that significantly increased both costs and transit times. Since canal revenues are among Egypt's most critical foreign exchange sources, recovery is of paramount importance.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>The major hurdle: insurance costs remain high</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Abdel Aziz Nabil</strong>, a representative of Inchcape Shipping Agency attending the meeting, stated that <strong>maritime insurance costs</strong> remain the region's biggest obstacle.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Insurance premiums, particularly due to Red Sea security risks, continue to be elevated:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">This situation makes the return decision difficult for many lines</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Full normalization is not expected until insurance costs decline</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Some lines continue to transit under <strong>naval escort</strong> on security grounds.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Overall outlook: Gradual improvement</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">As the fourth quarter of 2025 approaches, the SCA's message is clear:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Recovery has begun</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tonnage is growing</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Larger vessels are returning to Suez</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>However, full recovery will take time due to insurance and geopolitical risks</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">The SCA is supporting carriers with regular consultative meetings to help them revise routing plans, while sharing transit data with the industry through a "monthly rapid feedback" model.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">For now, the trend is positive; however, the Suez Canal's complete return to pre-crisis traffic levels will depend on <strong>insurance pricing, regional stability, and carrier risk appetite</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>229 vessels</strong> transited in October; July–October combined tonnage increased <strong>10%</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Major lines like CMA CGM and MSC are conducting "trial returns."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The SCA is working to rebuild confidence through regular meetings.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The biggest obstacle: elevated <strong>Red Sea insurance costs</strong> remain.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Recovery has started, but full normalization will take time.</p></li>
</ul>
