<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>As of Saturday, October 25, 2025</strong>, Italy is officially permitting <strong>freight trains to operate with a single driver</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The new regulation is being regarded as an <strong>"epochal shift"</strong> in the railway sector and is viewed as <strong>a significant step in aligning the country's railway operations with European standards</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This change has been welcomed by <strong>Fermerci, the Italian railway industry association</strong>. Fermerci stated that the measure represents <strong>"finally achieving compliance with European norms"</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A Historic Alignment Step for the Sector</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Until now, freight trains in Italy have typically been <strong>operated by two drivers</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The new regulation, enabled by advances in <strong>technological safety systems (such as ETCS, GSM-R, and remote monitoring systems)</strong>, makes it possible to <strong>safely conduct single-driver operations</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In many European countries — particularly <strong>Germany, France, the Netherlands, and Spain</strong> — <strong>single driver operations (SDO)</strong> have long been in practice.</p>
<p><br>
Therefore, Italy's decision is viewed as an alignment step that will <strong>enhance its competitive strength in terms of operational efficiency and cost optimization</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Expected Impact: Efficiency, Cost, and Competitiveness</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Fermerci and other sector stakeholders, this reform will:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Simplify personnel planning</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Reduce operational costs</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Make driving times and scheduling more flexible</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Enhance the competitive strength of Italian train operators</strong> on <strong>international freight corridors</strong> (such as TEN-T networks).</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, some <strong>unions and safety experts</strong> argue that the measure <strong>could increase workload</strong> and that <strong>additional safety measures to mitigate human error risk</strong> will become mandatory.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A New Era in Line with European Standards</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new regulation also contributes to Italy's <strong>full compliance with standards established by European railway agencies (ERA)</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
This will enable Italian operators, particularly on <strong>Europe's north-south freight corridors</strong> (such as the Scandinavia–Mediterranean Corridor), to <strong>participate in a more integrated transport chain</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, this step is not merely a technical regulation but also a reflection of <strong>digitalization and automation trends in railway logistics</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>As of October 25, 2025</strong>, <strong>freight trains in Italy can be operated by a single driver</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The decision was characterized by <strong>Fermerci</strong> as a <strong>"historic step in terms of compliance with European standards"</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The measure will provide <strong>efficiency and cost advantages</strong>, though <strong>rigorous implementation of safety protocols</strong> will be essential.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Most European countries</strong> are already using the single-driver system.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">This change aims to strengthen <strong>international integration and competitiveness of Italian rail freight transport</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
