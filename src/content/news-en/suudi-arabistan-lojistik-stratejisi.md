<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Saudi Arabia</strong> is reassessing its investment priorities in response to shifting global economic conditions and <strong>geopolitical tensions in the Red Sea</strong>. The Riyadh administration is moving away from <strong>real estate megaprojects</strong> toward <strong>logistics and maritime infrastructure</strong>, aiming to strengthen the <strong>logistics component of its Vision 2030 strategy</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, this shift represents a <strong>pragmatic transformation in the kingdom's investment strategy</strong>. Seeking to increase non-oil revenue sources, Saudi Arabia is now allocating more resources to <strong>ports, intermodal transport networks, maritime trade, and warehouse infrastructure</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Red Sea risks drive strategic shift</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Recent <strong>logistics disruptions, maritime security concerns</strong>, and <strong>restructuring in international shipping routes</strong> in the Red Sea have prompted Riyadh to alter its strategic focus.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Red Sea</strong> is home to Saudi Arabia's main trade gateways, including <strong>Jeddah Islamic Port</strong> and <strong>King Abdullah Port</strong>. However, escalating <strong>geopolitical tensions</strong> and <strong>disruptions in vessel transits</strong> in the region have driven the kingdom toward <strong>building more resilient and diversified logistics infrastructure</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>New investment direction: Logistics-centered growth</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Saudi authorities are repositioning their investment portfolio to focus on <strong>maritime infrastructure</strong> and <strong>supply chain logistics</strong>.<br>
This includes:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Expansion of the <strong>King Salman Logistics Center</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Development of <strong>multimodal transport corridors in the Eastern Region</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Strengthening of <strong>NEOM and Oxagon projects</strong> with logistics connectivity,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Enhanced <strong>integration of Gulf ports with road and rail networks</strong><br>
are being planned.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">The goal is to position Saudi Arabia as <strong>a logistics hub between the Gulf, Africa, and Asia</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Financial rationalization of investments</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Experts note that Riyadh views this strategic shift as necessary not only for security but also for <strong>economic sustainability</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
<strong>High costs</strong> and <strong>lengthy return periods</strong> in real estate projects have prompted the government to focus on <strong>infrastructure projects capable of generating faster returns</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new investment wave will be driven primarily through <strong>Saudi Ports Authority (Mawani)</strong> and <strong>Saudi General Authority of Logistics (SAL)</strong>. These institutions are launching new incentive programs through private sector partnerships to <strong>increase container terminal capacity</strong> and <strong>accelerate cargo flow</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Vision 2030's logistics pillar accelerates</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under the Vision 2030 framework, Riyadh aims to <strong>increase the logistics sector's contribution to GDP to above 10 percent by 2030</strong>. To this end, <strong>59 logistics zones across the country</strong> are planned.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, <strong>port modernization projects</strong> have accelerated through collaboration among companies including <strong>Saudi Global Ports, Bahri, Aramco Logistics</strong>, and <strong>DP World Saudi Arabia</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Furthermore, investments in <strong>green port technologies</strong>, <strong>smart customs solutions</strong>, and <strong>carbon-reducing transport systems</strong> are being prioritized. These initiatives support Saudi Arabia's goal of becoming a <strong>sustainable logistics center</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Investor interest growing</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to investment analysts, this transformation could make Saudi Arabia <strong>more attractive to international logistics investors</strong>. The logistics sector's <strong>short-term cash flow</strong> generation is viewed as <strong>a lower-risk option compared to long-term real estate-focused projects</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, the global restructuring in maritime transport—particularly congestion at the Suez Canal—has made the idea of <strong>developing alternative logistics corridors through the Red Sea</strong> more appealing.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Experts note that Riyadh's move <strong>aligns with Vision 2030's financial sustainability and inclusive growth objectives</strong> and reflects the kingdom's <strong>rapid progress toward becoming the Middle East's logistics hub</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Saudi Arabia</strong> is redirecting investments from <strong>real estate projects toward logistics infrastructure</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Geopolitical risks in the Red Sea</strong> and <strong>global supply chain disruptions</strong> have accelerated the strategic transformation.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>New investments:</strong> Port expansions, multimodal networks, green transport systems.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Vision 2030 target:</strong> Increase logistics sector's contribution to GDP to <strong>above 10 percent</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Riyadh</strong> aims to become a <strong>logistics hub</strong> along the Gulf–Africa–Asia axis.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Focus: <strong>Short-term returns, sustainable growth, and investment diversification</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
