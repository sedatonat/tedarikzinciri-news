<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Mitsubishi Heavy Industries (MHI)</strong> has signed an <strong>EPC (engineering, procurement and construction)</strong> contract with Turkmenistan's state chemical company <strong>SC Turkmenhimiya</strong> to build the country's largest ammonia and urea production complex. The project is being executed jointly with Turkey-based <strong>GAP İnşaat</strong>, and the groundbreaking ceremony was held on November 2, 2025, with the participation of Turkmenistan's President <strong>Serdar Berdimuhamedov</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new facility will be located on the Caspian Sea shore in the <strong>Kiyanly</strong> region of Balkan state and is planned to commence operations in 2030.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Turkmenistan's Largest Fertilizer Plant</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Upon completion, the project will represent the largest capacity ever achieved in the country's chemical and fertilizer industry. The complex's daily production capacity comprises:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>2,000 tons of ammonia</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>3,500 tons of urea</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This volume is designed to support both domestic consumption and export. Turkmenistan gains a strategic advantage in regional fertilizer production thanks to its substantial natural gas reserves.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Project Scope</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The EPC work conducted by MHI and GAP encompasses:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">basic and detailed engineering of the facility,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">equipment procurement,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">construction,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">commissioning,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">auxiliary facilities,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">offsite utilities,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">temporary scaffolding, transportation and product loading systems<br/>and related works.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">MHI assumes primary responsibility for engineering and equipment procurement, while Mitsubishi Corporation will provide support to the project.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>CO₂ Capture Unit for Low-Carbon Production</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The facility will incorporate <strong>Advanced KM CDR Process™</strong> carbon capture technology developed jointly by MHI and Kansai Electric Power. This technology enables the capture and reuse of a significant portion of CO₂ from the production process or its secure management, thereby reducing the facility's environmental impact.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This approach is critical for fertilizer exports that comply with <strong>carbon footprint criteria</strong> gaining prominence in both European and Asian markets.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>MHI's Track Record in Turkmenistan</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Mitsubishi Heavy Industries previously completed a similar fertilizer facility in <strong>Garabogaz</strong> in 2018 in collaboration with Mitsubishi Corporation and GAP. The Garabogaz plant has operated stably since then, demonstrating MHI's credentials as a reliable engineering partner in Turkmenistan's chemical industry projects.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new Kiyanly project represents a new chapter in MHI's collaboration with Turkmenistan in the chemical and fertilizer sectors. It also reinforces GAP İnşaat's capacity for infrastructure and industrial facility construction in the region.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Company Profiles</strong></h4>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Mitsubishi Heavy Industries (MHI)</strong></h5>
<p>A Tokyo-based conglomerate and one of Japan's largest engineering groups, operating across a broad portfolio including energy systems, infrastructure, industrial machinery, aerospace, and defense.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>SC Turkmenhimiya</strong></h5>
<p>The state agency governing Turkmenistan's chemical sector. Development of fertilizers, industrial chemicals, and related production facilities is coordinated at the ministerial level.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>GAP İnşaat (Çalık Holding)</strong></h5>
<p>A major Turkey-based EPC contractor that has long executed infrastructure, industrial, energy, and transportation projects in Turkmenistan.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">MHI signed an EPC contract with Turkmenhimiya for <strong>the country's largest ammonia–urea complex</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Daily capacity: <strong>2,000 tons of ammonia</strong>, <strong>3,500 tons of urea</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The project is being executed in partnership with GAP İnşaat.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The facility will commence operations in 2030.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">CO₂ capture unit <strong>Advanced KM CDR Process™</strong> will be integrated.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">MHI is building Turkmenistan's second major fertilizer facility following the 2018 Garabogaz project.</p></li>
</ul>
