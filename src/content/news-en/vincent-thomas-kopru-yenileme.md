<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Vincent Thomas Bridge at the entrance to Los Angeles Harbor</strong> will undergo comprehensive <strong>redecking</strong> work beginning in early 2026. The 63-year-old bridge plays a critical role both in the harbor's highway connections and in heavy vehicle traffic across the region.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The California State Transportation Agency (CalSTA)</strong> announced that the project timeline will proceed as planned, and that launching the renewal work <strong>with no delays</strong> is the <strong>"highest priority"</strong> for ensuring the bridge's safety.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Bridge serves as one of the harbor's main arteries</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Vincent Thomas Bridge is one of the most critical infrastructure connections <strong>linking Los Angeles Harbor with Terminal Island</strong>. Daily, an average of <strong>53,000 vehicles</strong>, approximately <strong>3,400 of them heavy vehicles</strong>, use the bridge. This traffic is significant both for <strong>in-harbor container transportation and the ground connections for Trans-Pacific trade</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Redecking project will take priority</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Pacific Maritime Association (PMSA)</strong> President <strong>Mike Jacob</strong> said that in meetings CalSTA has held with harbor stakeholders over the past two weeks, <strong>the redecking work will take precedence over plans to increase the bridge's clearance height</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Jacob stated, "The Agency emphasized that priority must be given to the redecking process to prevent long-term worsening of structural safety risks on the bridge."</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Height increase plans delayed</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Los Angeles Harbor and terminal operators had aimed to <strong>increase the clearance height of the Vincent Thomas Bridge</strong> to facilitate the passage of mega container ships in the Super Post-Panamax class. However, CalSTA indicated that this project is not feasible in the near term.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to officials, raising the bridge's height would be an <strong>extremely complex process both in engineering and financing terms</strong>, so the current priority will be <strong>safety and maintenance-focused renewal</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CalSTA</strong> clarified that the current plan covers only <strong>the renovation of the bridge deck, repair of steel surfaces, corrosion removal, and increased load-bearing capacity</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Renewal process and traffic impact</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under the project scope, the bridge's <strong>concrete decks and steel surfaces</strong> will be completely renewed. During these works, <strong>temporary traffic restrictions</strong> are anticipated. However, CalSTA announced that to avoid disrupting port operations, the work will be conducted <strong>in phases, during night shifts, and with weekend scheduling</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The project is expected to take <strong>approximately two years</strong>. Upon completion of the renewal, the bridge will be <strong>modernized to maintain its structural integrity for the next 40 years</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic importance growing</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, Los Angeles Harbor and the neighboring <strong>Long Beach Harbor</strong> rank as <strong>the nation's busiest maritime gateway</strong>. Together, these two ports manage approximately <strong>40 percent of the nation's Asia-sourced container traffic</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">For this reason, safe operation of infrastructure elements such as the Vincent Thomas Bridge directly affects not just <strong>regional logistics flow</strong>, but also <strong>national supply chain stability</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>PMSA President Mike Jacob</strong> said, "The bridge is not just critical for Los Angeles but serves as a vital artery for the entire U.S. West Coast transportation network. When the renewal process is complete, this bridge will once again be a symbol of the nation's logistics resilience."</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Long-term planning on the agenda</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">CalSTA and Los Angeles Harbor will <strong>reassess height increase and marine traffic clearance expansion projects</strong> after the redecking is completed. These projects are expected to be revisited for the <strong>post-2030 period</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Redecking work on the Vincent Thomas Bridge</strong> will <strong>begin in early 2026.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The project aims to <strong>enhance the bridge's structural safety and durability</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Plans to increase bridge height</strong> have been postponed for now.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CalSTA</strong> has designated safety maintenance as the "highest priority step."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The bridge, with daily passage of <strong>53,000 vehicles</strong> and <strong>3,400 trucks</strong>, serves as <strong>Los Angeles Harbor's main logistics corridor.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Project duration is <strong>approximately two years</strong>, to be conducted in phases and with traffic-friendly methods.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Following redecking, the bridge will <strong>safely serve for the next 40 years.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
