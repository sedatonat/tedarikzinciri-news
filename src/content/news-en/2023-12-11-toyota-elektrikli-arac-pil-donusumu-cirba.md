<p>Toyota Motor North America announced it will partner with battery materials services and recycling company <a href="https://www.cirbasolutions.com/"><u>Cirba Solutions</u></a> to expand its battery recycling network. The partnership will focus on battery collection efforts for hybrid, plug-in hybrid, and electric vehicles.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>The initiative will focus on collecting, transporting, disassembling, and processing end-of-life lithium-ion electric vehicle batteries across the Midwest and Eastern United States, with operations taking place at Cirba's Lancaster facility in Ohio. Toyota noted that the facility has a 95 percent recovery rate.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Christopher Yang, Vice President of Business Development at Toyota Motor North America, said, "Cirba Solutions' extensive and well-established transportation and recycling network enables Toyota to have nationwide battery collection and recycling operations to reduce our costs and operational carbon footprint."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Toyota stated that the agreement with Cirba Solutions will reduce overall logistics and transportation costs by at least 70 percent, based on 2022 data.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Toyota currently collects approximately 25,000 used vehicle batteries annually from its dealer network and expects this number to increase as the company sells more electric vehicles.</p>
