<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Krenar Komoni</strong>, CEO and founder of <strong>Tive</strong>, notes that agentic artificial intelligence is having a major impact on software developers, triggering supply chain's transition from recording systems to "action" systems. <strong>Komoni</strong> says that <strong>AI</strong> is beginning to "commoditize" software development. <strong>ChatGPT</strong> models like <strong>Claude</strong> from <strong>Anthropic</strong> are demonstrating the ability to write code from scratch by creating video games that would have taken people years to build. <strong>Komoni</strong> uses the phrase "It's just opened up the eyes of everybody in software development." From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Tive</strong> competes with rivals like <strong>project44</strong>, <strong>FourKites</strong>, <strong>Shippeo</strong>, <strong>Overhaul</strong>, and <strong>Roambee</strong> in the real-time visibility category, offering a multi-modal tracker hardware plus <strong>SaaS</strong> platform model. <strong>Cellular IoT</strong>, <strong>LoRaWAN</strong>, and <strong>BLE</strong>-based condition monitoring are core technologies in the cold chain and high-value goods segments.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, <strong>Komoni</strong> suggests that it may be time for some software companies to be repriced, as their value is being disrupted by <strong>AI</strong> models. For logistics and transportation companies, it is critical to begin adopting <strong>AI</strong> today. In contrast to their positions a year ago, many in that sector are now convinced that the technology works. To win with <strong>AI</strong>, however, companies will need one of three things: proprietary data; physical products or goods that cannot be replaced by <strong>AI</strong>; or direct customer relationships. From a supply chain perspective, the concept of data moat demonstrates that the defensibility of <strong>SaaS</strong> companies no longer comes from software quality or user interface, but from data ownership and network effect. As foundation models emerge, proprietary data and workflow integration depth are the two core sources of competitive advantage for vertical <strong>SaaS</strong> companies.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Generative and agentic <strong>AI</strong> models are rapidly moving from recording systems to action systems. In recent months, tools like agentic voice agents have made it possible for <strong>AI</strong> to take action and be trusted by users to do so. <strong>Komoni</strong> uses the phrase "I can have a conversation with ChatGPT and feel like I'm talking to another human." This has major implications for logistics companies, which can now rely on technology to perform tasks such as issuing alarms when temperatures inside refrigerated containers exceed acceptable limits. From a supply chain perspective, cold chain monitoring is critical for compliance with <strong>USP &lt;1079&gt;</strong>, <strong>GDP</strong> (<strong>Good Distribution Practice</strong>), <strong>ICH Q7</strong>, and <strong>21 CFR Part 11</strong>. Temperature excursions in the 2-8°C pharmaceutical cold chain, -25°C frozen food, and -80°C ultra-cold mRNA vaccine chain trigger product loss and recalls. Excursion management protocols are prime candidates for being delegated to the automated decision-making mechanisms of agentic <strong>AI</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">For now, humans remain in the loop. But ultimately, <strong>Komoni</strong> uses the phrase "they're going to start trust AI to do the right thing. Then they'll become more involved in taking care of the worst-case exceptions." From a supply chain perspective, the human-on-the-loop model, unlike human-in-the-loop, envisions humans stepping in not in every decision, but only in exceptional situations. The <strong>NIST AI Risk Management Framework</strong> and <strong>Article 14</strong> of the <strong>EU AI Act</strong>'s human oversight requirements draw the legal boundaries of this model. In operations that are not safety-critical, such as logistics, but are high-value, the pace of agentic automation adoption is higher. The <strong>API-first</strong> approach and <strong>tool use</strong> capability provide the technical infrastructure for integrating <strong>Anthropic</strong>, <strong>OpenAI</strong>, <strong>Google DeepMind</strong>, and <strong>Meta</strong>'s foundation models with logistics platforms. Ultimately, <strong>Komoni</strong>'s views herald a fundamental reshaping of the supply chain software market over the next 2-3 years.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>\n1. <strong>Komoni</strong> notes that supply chain software is transitioning from recording to action.<br>\n2. <strong>Anthropic</strong>'s <strong>Claude</strong> can write code from scratch.<br>\n3. Three winning paths: proprietary data; physical product; direct customer relationship.<br>\n4. Refrigerated container temperature alarm is a concrete logistics application of agentic <strong>AI</strong>.<br>\n5. Humans will remain in exceptions; routine decisions will be delegated to <strong>AI</strong>.</p>\n\n\n<p><br></p>\n<p>[43859]</p>
