<p style="text-align: justify">UK-based technology firm <strong>Sedna</strong> has acquired <strong>Flytta</strong>, an artificial intelligence-powered workflow automation platform for global commerce and supply chains. The acquisition price was not disclosed.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Flytta</strong> was initially developed at <strong>Casper Shipping</strong> to streamline internal operations and was later commercialized through further development by <strong>Seer</strong>. The platform's import and export automation workflow is now part of <strong>Sedna AI</strong> and serves customers including <strong>Casper Customs</strong>, <strong>Pentagon Freight Services</strong>, and <strong>Mobius Technology</strong>.</p>
<p>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sedna</strong> Chairman <strong>Leigh Steed-Middleton</strong> noted that the acquisition accelerated the company's artificial intelligence strategy and strengthened its position in delivering value-added workflows tailored to industry-specific needs.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">As part of the acquisition, <strong>Flytta</strong> co-founders <strong>Alan Appleyard</strong> and <strong>Owain Brennan</strong> will join the <strong>Sedna</strong> team. With this expansion, <strong>Sedna</strong> will open a new office in <strong>Middlesbrough</strong>, in addition to its existing offices in <strong>Athens</strong>, <strong>Cape Town</strong>, <strong>Copenhagen</strong>, <strong>London</strong>, and <strong>Singapore</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sedna</strong> CEO <strong>Bill Dobie</strong> stated that the new <strong>Middlesbrough</strong> office will drive the next wave of artificial intelligence innovation in global commerce by leveraging talent in the North of England. This investment strengthens <strong>Sedna</strong>'s artificial intelligence capabilities while reinforcing the UK's leading position in digital commerce transformation.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Founded in 2017, <strong>Sedna</strong> was launched to address current email challenges such as email overload, data loss, and siloed operations. Backed by <strong>Insight Partners</strong>, <strong>GK Goh Ventures</strong>, <strong>Stride.VC</strong>, <strong>Chalfen Ventures</strong>, and <strong>SAP</strong>, the company counts many leading shipping and logistics firms among its customers, including <strong>Bunge</strong>, <strong>FedNav</strong>, <strong>Glencore</strong>, <strong>Oldendorff</strong>, <strong>Norden</strong>, <strong>Stena Bulk</strong>, and <strong>MOL Chemical Tankers</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sedna</strong> recently also acquired <strong>Nordic IT</strong>, a Copenhagen-based email management specialist. This move strengthened email's position as the primary protocol in shipping while expanding the company's capacity to deliver artificial intelligence-powered, connected solutions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sedna</strong> plans to launch other artificial intelligence-powered workflows and automations in the near term, such as <strong>Noon Report Analysis Automation</strong>. These developments further advance the company's mission to connect the maritime ecosystem and transform data into actionable insights.</p>
