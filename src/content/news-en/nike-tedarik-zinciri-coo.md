<p style="text-align: justify">Global sports apparel and footwear giant <strong>Nike</strong> has undertaken a comprehensive restructuring of its top management to streamline operational structure and accelerate technology integration. The company announced the appointment of <strong>Venkatesh Alagirisamy</strong>, serving as <strong>Chief Supply Chain Officer (CSCO)</strong>, to the newly created position of <strong>Executive Vice President (EVP) and Chief Operating Officer (COO)</strong>. The appointment was announced through a letter to employees from CEO <strong>Elliott Hill</strong>, with the decision taking effect as of <strong>December 8, 2025</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under the new structure, Alagirisamy will retain his existing supply chain responsibilities while simultaneously assuming direct management of <strong>Nike's technology organization</strong>. This marks the first time Nike has unified supply chain and technology under a single <strong>operational umbrella</strong>. The move demonstrates the company's commitment to achieving end-to-end digital integration throughout the <strong>create–plan–make–deliver–sell</strong> cycle.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In his letter, CEO Elliott Hill characterized Alagirisamy's expanded role with the following statement:<br>
<strong>"[Alagirisamy] and his team will now be able to look end-to-end to ensure technology is fully integrated across the company and into how we create, plan, make, deliver and sell our world-class innovations."</strong></p>
<p><br>
This statement reflects Nike's positioning of technology investments not as a separate function, but as a core element of <strong>operational excellence</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Streamlining Leadership Structure</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">With the creation of the COO position, Nike eliminated two critical C-level roles. The company's former <strong>Chief Technology Officer (CTO)</strong> <strong>Muge Dogan</strong> and <strong>Chief Commercial Officer (CCO)</strong> <strong>Craig Williams</strong> departed the company under the new structure. This decision signals Nike's shift away from function-based silos toward an <strong>integrated operations model</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to industry observers, this streamlining aims to accelerate decision-making processes and embed technology investments directly into supply, manufacturing, logistics, and sales operations. At a time when factors such as <strong>demand volatility</strong>, <strong>nearshoring</strong>, <strong>sustainability pressures</strong>, and <strong>omnichannel complexity</strong> are increasing, Nike has chosen to manage operational alignment from a single command center.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Alagirisamy's Profile: From Supply Chain to Technology</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Venkatesh Alagirisamy</strong>, with approximately <strong>20 years of experience at Nike</strong>, has served as the company's <strong>chief supply chain officer</strong> since 2020. In this role, he has managed global strategy for:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>raw material sourcing</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>manufacturing</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>logistics</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>integrated planning</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>operations</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>sustainability</strong>, and</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Express Lane</strong> organizations.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Alagirisamy's background aligns with Nike's current transformation objectives. Prior to his CSCO appointment, he held roles such as <strong>Express Lane Global Operations VP</strong> and <strong>Converse COO</strong>. These experiences have provided him with deep expertise in both speed-to-market and brand-based operations management.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic Significance: Supply Chain and Technology Convergence</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Nike's move confirms that the supply chain is no longer viewed merely as a <strong>cost and delivery</strong> function, but equally as a <strong>technology-enabled source of competitive advantage</strong>. Digital planning, advanced demand forecasting, automation, data analytics, and sustainability solutions are assuming more central roles in Nike's operational architecture.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Alagirisamy's appointment as COO serves the company's objectives for:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>end-to-end visibility</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>faster decision-making</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>improving ROI on technology investments</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>shortening product lifecycle</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">and similar strategic goals.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Industry Context</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">In recent years, Nike has faced significant fluctuations in its global retail and manufacturing network. Post-pandemic supply chain disruptions, reduced dependence on China, <strong>nearshoring</strong>, and <strong>sustainable manufacturing</strong> pressures have driven the company toward a more agile operating model. For this reason, the integration of supply chain leadership with technology is being read not merely as an organizational change, but as a <strong>strategic pivot</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Overall Assessment</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Nike's entrusting the COO role to a supply chain-origin executive reflects the trend of <strong>supply chain centralization</strong> in the global retail sector. The consolidation of technology, planning, and operations under unified leadership demonstrates Nike's intent to enhance competitive strength in speed, agility, and integration.</p>
<p><br></p>

<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Venkatesh Alagirisamy</strong> was appointed <strong>EVP and COO</strong> at Nike.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Alagirisamy continues in his <strong>CSCO</strong> role.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">With the COO role, the <strong>technology organization</strong> now reports directly to supply chain leadership.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Nike streamlined its structure by eliminating the <strong>CTO and CCO</strong> positions.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Objective: to integrate technology and operations end-to-end.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The appointment supports Nike's transformation focused on speed, agility, and digitalization.</p></li>
</ul>
