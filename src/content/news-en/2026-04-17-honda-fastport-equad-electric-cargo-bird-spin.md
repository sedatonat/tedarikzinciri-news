<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Honda</strong> announced the commercial launch of <strong>eQuad</strong>, a four-wheeled electric cargo vehicle developed under its <strong>Fastport</strong> brand for the last-mile delivery market. The company unveiled its first major partnership with scooter-share operators <strong>Bird</strong> and <strong>Spin</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">eQuad will be used in dense urban areas for <strong>battery swapping</strong>, maintenance and scooter repositioning. Honda Fastport General Manager Jose Wyszogrod said the vehicle was designed to address rising urban congestion, emissions and operating costs.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The vehicle is built in a modular way for use across parcel delivery, grocery distribution, food service and direct-to-consumer fulfillment. Honda offers it together with a <strong>fleet-as-a-service</strong> model that bundles the hardware with the software needed to run operations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. Honda Fastport eQuad is now commercially available.<br>
2. Bird and Spin are among the first major customers.<br>
3. It will be used for battery swapping and scooter repositioning.<br>
4. Suitable for parcel, grocery and food-service distribution.<br>
5. Offered as hardware plus a fleet-as-a-service software bundle.</p>
