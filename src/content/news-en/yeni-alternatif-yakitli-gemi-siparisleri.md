<p style="text-align: justify">In recent years, interest in environmentally friendly fuel alternatives has been growing in the maritime sector. In this context, according to <strong>DNV</strong> data, ship orders powered by liquefied natural gas (<strong>LNG</strong>) demonstrate a distinct advantage over other alternative fuels. <strong>DNV</strong>'s report reveals that nearly all new alternative fuel ship orders fall within the <strong>LNG</strong> category.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>LNG</strong> is regarded as a cleaner option among fossil fuels and has the potential to reduce carbon emissions. For this reason, the maritime industry aims to reduce its environmental impact by transitioning to <strong>LNG</strong>. However, <strong>LNG</strong> also has environmental drawbacks such as methane leakage, and this situation is criticized by some environmental organizations.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Among other alternative fuels are options such as <strong>ammonia</strong>, <strong>methanol</strong>, and <strong>hydrogen</strong>. These fuels stand out in projects targeting zero carbon emissions. However, technological and economic challenges related to the use of these fuels are greater compared to <strong>LNG</strong>. In particular, the storage and transportation of fuels such as <strong>ammonia</strong> and <strong>hydrogen</strong> are not compatible with existing infrastructure, and this situation makes the adoption of these fuels difficult.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>DNV</strong>'s data clearly demonstrates the maritime sector's preference for <strong>LNG</strong>. This situation shows that the sector views <strong>LNG</strong> as the most suitable alternative fuel in the short term. However, in the long term, other alternative fuels must also be developed and adopted to achieve zero carbon emission targets.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, the vast majority of alternative fuel ship orders in the maritime sector are focused on <strong>LNG</strong>. This trend reflects the sector's efforts to fulfill its environmental responsibilities. However, for true sustainability, other alternative fuels such as <strong>ammonia</strong>, <strong>methanol</strong>, and <strong>hydrogen</strong> must also be considered, and investments in this area must be increased.​</p>
<p><br></p>
