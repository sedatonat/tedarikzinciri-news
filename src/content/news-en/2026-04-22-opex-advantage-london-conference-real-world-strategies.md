<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> reports that in a business landscape characterized by relentless change, delivering genuine <strong>Operational Excellence</strong> has become critical — requiring organizations not merely to react but to anticipate and adapt. Operations and continuous improvement leaders must rapidly integrate new technologies and build resilient, human-centric cultures. Addressing this critical need, <strong>London</strong> is hosting the <strong>Operational Excellence &amp; Business Improvement Conference</strong>. On 10 September 2025 at the <strong>Copthorne Tara Hotel Kensington</strong>, this focused, single-day event is designed for senior professionals seeking strategies that translate from theory into action. Participants will gain direct insights from cross-sector leaders including <strong>Jaguar Land Rover</strong>, <strong>Shell</strong>, <strong>Tesco</strong>, <strong>Network Rail</strong>, and <strong>Philips</strong>. Extracting measurable value from <strong>artificial intelligence</strong> and automation has become critical. <strong>Damian Hall</strong>, <strong>Head of Programme &amp; Change</strong> at <strong>Ralph Lauren</strong>, will explore <strong>AI</strong>'s evolving role and the balance between technology and human expertise. <strong>Ravi Jay</strong> from <strong>Jaguar Land Rover</strong> will examine global <strong>AI</strong> transformations. A panel featuring leaders from <strong>Shell</strong> and <strong>AXA Partners</strong> will present real-world case studies demonstrating cost savings and efficiency gains.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Operational Excellence</strong> (<strong>OpEx</strong>) is a management philosophy that organizations use to continuously improve their value streams. The main methodology frameworks include: (1) <strong>Lean Manufacturing</strong>, the <strong>Toyota Production System</strong> (<strong>TPS</strong>), pioneered by <strong>Taiichi Ohno</strong> and <strong>Shigeo Shingo</strong>; (2) <strong>Six Sigma</strong>, developed by <strong>Motorola</strong> and <strong>Bill Smith</strong>, employing <strong>DMAIC</strong> (<strong>Define-Measure-Analyze-Improve-Control</strong>); (3) <strong>Lean Six Sigma</strong>, a combination of both; (4) <strong>Theory of Constraints</strong> (<strong>TOC</strong>), by <strong>Eliyahu Goldratt</strong>; (5) <strong>Total Quality Management</strong> (<strong>TQM</strong>), advanced by <strong>W. Edwards Deming</strong> and <strong>Joseph Juran</strong>; (6) <strong>Kaizen</strong>, continuous improvement; (7) <strong>Hoshin Kanri</strong>, policy deployment; (8) <strong>5S</strong> (<strong>Sort-Set in order-Shine-Standardize-Sustain</strong>); (9) <strong>Value Stream Mapping</strong> (<strong>VSM</strong>); (10) <strong>Poka-Yoke</strong>, error prevention; (11) <strong>Agile</strong> and <strong>SAFe</strong> (<strong>Scaled Agile Framework</strong>); (12) <strong>Business Process Management</strong> (<strong>BPM</strong>); and (13) <strong>Robotic Process Automation</strong> (<strong>RPA</strong>). Key certifying organizations include <strong>ASQ</strong> (<strong>American Society for Quality</strong>), <strong>IASSC</strong> (<strong>International Association for Six Sigma Certification</strong>), <strong>Lean Six Sigma Academy</strong>, <strong>EFQM</strong> (<strong>European Foundation for Quality Management</strong>), <strong>BPMI</strong>, and <strong>OMG BPMN</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the <strong>OpEx</strong> technology ecosystem encompasses: (1) <strong>process mining</strong> — <strong>Celonis</strong> (<strong>Munich Germany</strong>), <strong>UiPath Process Mining</strong>, <strong>Apromore</strong>, <strong>QPR</strong>, <strong>Software AG ARIS Process Mining</strong>, <strong>SAP Signavio</strong>, <strong>Microsoft Power Automate Process Mining</strong>; (2) <strong>RPA</strong> — <strong>UiPath</strong>, <strong>Automation Anywhere</strong>, <strong>Microsoft Power Automate</strong>, <strong>Blue Prism</strong> (<strong>SS&amp;C Technologies</strong>), <strong>NICE</strong>, <strong>Pegasystems</strong>, <strong>WorkFusion</strong>; (3) <strong>BPM</strong> — <strong>Appian</strong>, <strong>Pega</strong>, <strong>Bonita</strong>, <strong>Camunda</strong>, <strong>Mendix</strong>, <strong>OutSystems</strong>; (4) <strong>quality management</strong> — <strong>MasterControl</strong>, <strong>Sparta Systems TrackWise</strong> (<strong>Honeywell</strong>), <strong>ETQ Reliance</strong>, <strong>Veeva Vault QMS</strong>, <strong>Intellect Compliance</strong>; and (5) <strong>continuous improvement</strong> — <strong>KaiNexus</strong>, <strong>Sii</strong>, <strong>Wevo</strong>, <strong>Tervene</strong>, <strong>Improvability</strong>. <strong>Generative AI</strong> and <strong>agentic AI</strong> are creating a new transformation wave in the <strong>OpEx</strong> field — with natural language-based process analysis, automated anomaly detection, and recommendation-driven process improvement as key capabilities.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the institutions represented at the conference are global pioneers in operational transformation. <strong>Jaguar Land Rover</strong> (<strong>JLR</strong>; <strong>Coventry UK</strong>; owned by <strong>Tata Motors</strong>; CEO <strong>Adrian Mardell</strong>); <strong>Shell plc</strong> (<strong>London UK</strong>; CEO <strong>Wael Sawan</strong>); <strong>Tesco PLC</strong> (<strong>Welwyn Garden City UK</strong>; CEO <strong>Ken Murphy</strong>; the <strong>UK</strong>'s largest retailer); <strong>Network Rail</strong> (<strong>London UK</strong>; CEO <strong>Andrew Haines</strong>; the <strong>UK</strong>'s railway infrastructure operator); <strong>Philips</strong> (<strong>Royal Philips</strong>; <strong>Amsterdam Netherlands</strong>; CEO <strong>Roy Jakobs</strong>); <strong>Ralph Lauren Corporation</strong> (<strong>New York</strong>; CEO <strong>Patrice Louvet</strong>); <strong>AXA Partners</strong> (<strong>AXA Group</strong>; CEO <strong>Thomas Buberl</strong>); <strong>Anglian Water Services</strong> (CEO <strong>Mark Thurston</strong>); and <strong>Thames Water</strong> (CEO <strong>Chris Weston</strong>) are the main participating institutions. The <strong>UK</strong>'s <strong>OpEx</strong> and continuous improvement community platforms include <strong>Manufacturing Excellence</strong>, <strong>EEF</strong> (<strong>Make UK</strong>), <strong>BSI</strong> (<strong>British Standards Institution</strong>), and <strong>The Manufacturer</strong>. In conclusion, the <strong>OPEX London Conference</strong> represents an important gathering point for the global operational excellence community to share knowledge — with <strong>AI</strong>, automation, and the integration of human-centric culture emerging as key strategic themes.</p>
