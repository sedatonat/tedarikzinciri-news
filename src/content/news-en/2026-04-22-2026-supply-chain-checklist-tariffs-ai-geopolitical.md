<p style="text-align: justify">The global supply chain is entering a new era—one defined by continuous disruption. From shifting tariffs and geopolitical conflict to regulatory volatility, cyber risks and climate shocks, volatility is no longer the exception; it is the rule. Legacy systems and disconnected tools cannot keep pace, leaving you vulnerable to costly errors, delayed responses and missed opportunities. From a supply chain perspective, the concept of <strong>polycrisis</strong> is defined by the <strong>WEF</strong> (<strong>World Economic Forum</strong>) <strong>Global Risks Report 2026</strong> and explains how multiple crisis streams are occurring simultaneously. The <strong>Trump 2.0</strong> tariff regime, <strong>Section 301</strong> China tariffs, <strong>Section 232</strong> national security tariffs, <strong>reciprocal tariffs</strong> under the <strong>IEEPA</strong> (<strong>International Emergency Economic Powers Act</strong>) are becoming the dominant agenda item for supply chain managers in 2026.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>2026 Supply Chain Checklist</strong> is your roadmap for building resilience, agility and profitability in this unpredictable landscape. Within it, practical strategies that supply chain leaders are implementing are discovered. From a supply chain perspective, the concepts of <strong>resilience</strong>, <strong>agility</strong> and <strong>antifragility</strong> have been popularized by <strong>Nassim Taleb</strong>, <strong>Yossi Sheffi</strong> (<strong>MIT CTL</strong>) and <strong>Manuel Hensmans</strong>. <strong>Gartner</strong>'s <strong>Top 25 Supply Chains</strong> highlights companies such as <strong>Cisco</strong>, <strong>Schneider Electric</strong>, <strong>Colgate-Palmolive</strong>, <strong>Microsoft</strong> and <strong>Johnson &amp; Johnson</strong>. The <strong>SCRM</strong> (<strong>Supply Chain Risk Management</strong>), <strong>BCM</strong> (<strong>Business Continuity Management</strong>) and <strong>ERM</strong> (<strong>Enterprise Risk Management</strong>) frameworks are based on <strong>ISO 22301</strong> and <strong>ISO 31000</strong> standards.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This checklist is not theory—it is a practical tool for benchmarking your current operations, identifying critical gaps and prioritizing next steps. Whether you are focused on reducing costs, improving efficiency or preparing your operations for the future, these strategies help you adapt faster than competitors and capture growth opportunities amid uncertainty. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>maturity model</strong> approaches and frameworks within <strong>SCOR</strong> (<strong>Supply Chain Operations Reference</strong>) and <strong>ASCM</strong> (<strong>Association for Supply Chain Management</strong>) enable the evaluation of <strong>Plan, Source, Make, Deliver, Return, Enable</strong> processes. <strong>Digital twin</strong>, <strong>control tower</strong>, <strong>scenario planning</strong> and <strong>stress testing</strong> applications serve as decision support tools in volatile environments.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the critical focus areas for 2026 are use of <strong>FTZ</strong> (<strong>Foreign Trade Zone</strong>) and <strong>bonded warehouse</strong> for <strong>tariff engineering</strong>, <strong>generative</strong> and <strong>agentic AI</strong> pilot projects for <strong>AI adoption</strong>, <strong>SBOM</strong> (<strong>Software Bill of Materials</strong>) and <strong>NIS2</strong> compliance for <strong>cybersecurity</strong>, <strong>scope 3 emissions</strong> and <strong>physical climate risk</strong> modeling for <strong>climate resilience</strong>, and <strong>multi-tier supplier visibility</strong> and <strong>UFLPA</strong> (<strong>Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act</strong>) compliance for <strong>geopolitical mapping</strong>. <strong>SAP IBP</strong>, <strong>Kinaxis</strong>, <strong>Blue Yonder</strong>, <strong>o9 Solutions</strong>, <strong>Anaplan</strong>, <strong>Coupa</strong>, <strong>OMP</strong> and <strong>RELEX</strong> are leading platforms in the <strong>integrated business planning</strong> space. In conclusion, the <strong>2026 Supply Chain Checklist</strong> is a concrete guide that supply chain leaders must use to establish strategic priorities in a volatile period.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. The global supply chain is entering an era of continuous disruption.<br>
2. Tariffs, geopolitics, regulation, cyber and climate are simultaneous sources of pressure.<br>
3. Legacy systems and disconnected tools cannot keep pace.<br>
4. The checklist is positioned as a practical benchmarking tool.<br>
5. Resilience, agility and profitability are key capabilities for 2026.</p>
