<p style="text-align: justify">On January 26, 2025, an undersea fiber optic cable between <strong>Latvia</strong> and <strong>Sweden</strong> in the Baltic Sea was damaged due to external interference, according to reports. <strong>Latvian Prime Minister Evika Silina</strong> stated following an extraordinary government meeting that the damage was likely caused by external factors and was of considerable significance.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In response to the incident, the <strong>Latvian Navy</strong> dispatched a patrol boat to examine a vessel suspected of being connected to the incident. Additionally, two other vessels in the area have been brought into the investigation. <strong>Latvia</strong> is working in coordination with <strong>NATO</strong> and other countries in the Baltic Sea region to clarify the details of the incident. <strong>Prime Minister Silina</strong> stated on the <strong>X</strong> platform that the incident is being investigated together with Swedish allies and <strong>NATO</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Swedish Navy</strong> spokesperson <strong>Jimmie Adamsson</strong> stated that the cause of the damage and whether it was intentional have not yet been determined. <strong>NATO</strong> announced that it will deploy frigates, patrol aircraft, and marine drones to protect critical infrastructure in the Baltic Sea and reserved the right to take action against vessels that pose a security threat.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">These developments are linked to mounting concerns about energy and communications infrastructure following <strong>Russia</strong>'s invasion of <strong>Ukraine</strong>. Last month, <strong>Finnish</strong> police reported that the <strong>Estlink 2</strong> power line between <strong>Estonia</strong> and <strong>Finland</strong> and four telecommunications cables were damaged by a tanker whose anchor was dragged along the seabed.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The damaged cable between Latvia and Sweden connects the town of <strong>Ventspils</strong> to the island of <strong>Gotland</strong> and is damaged in Sweden's exclusive economic zone. The <strong>Latvian Radio and Television Centre (LVRTC)</strong> stated that communications providers have switched to alternative transmission routes and are attempting to charter a vessel for repairs. It was noted that the exact manner in which the cable was damaged will become clear when repair work begins.</p>
<p><br></p>
