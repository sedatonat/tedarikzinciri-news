<p>Driving suppliers to develop quality capabilities beyond mere compliance with regulations remains a common challenge for supplier quality leaders. According to respondents to <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gartner"><u>Gartner</u></a>'s Supplier Quality Management Survey, while suppliers may possess the necessary information and capabilities, approximately one-third of organizations struggle to motivate suppliers in this direction.&nbsp;</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>While financial incentives are a frequently employed method, this approach falls short of sustainability and does not prove sufficiently attractive to suppliers. This approach fails to address the underlying causes of suppliers' hesitation toward improvement.&nbsp;</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Top concerns include the magnitude of investment required to develop supplier quality capabilities and the potential negative impacts such initiatives may have on their own operations.&nbsp;</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Beyond financial incentives, organizations can evaluate <strong>four</strong> approaches to address supplier concerns:&nbsp;</p>\n<ol>\n  <li><p>Providing guidance in improvement implementation,&nbsp;</p></li>\n  <li><p>Reducing audit and reporting requirements,&nbsp;</p></li>\n  <li><p>Proactively minimizing potential negative impacts on suppliers' business operations&nbsp;</p></li>\n  <li><p>Emphasizing value for suppliers' other business areas.&nbsp;</p></li>\n</ol>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>These approaches, in contrast to financial incentives, remain within the control of supplier quality leaders and their implementation may sometimes require cross-functional collaboration.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h3><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h3>\n<ul>\n  <li><p>Suppliers need to develop quality capabilities broadly, not just for regulatory compliance.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Financial incentives may not be sufficiently attractive and do not address root causes.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Suppliers' concerns regarding investment requirements and potential negative business impacts must be addressed.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Providing guidance in improvement implementation, reducing audit and reporting requirements, minimizing negative impacts on business operations, and emphasizing value for other business areas can serve as incentives.<br>\n</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>News Link:</strong> <a href="https://www.scmr.com/article/4-nonmonetary-motivators-drive-supplier-quality-improvements"><u>https://www.scmr.com/article/4-nonmonetary-motivators-drive-supplier-quality-improvements</u></a></p>
