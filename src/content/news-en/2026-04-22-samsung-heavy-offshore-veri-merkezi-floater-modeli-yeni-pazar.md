<p style="text-align: justify">According to Splash247, Korean shipbuilding giant Samsung Heavy Industries has announced plans to target the AI compute boom through an offshore data centre floater model. The company is working on a new platform concept that promises lower cooling costs and wide access to renewable energy compared with onshore data centres.</p>

<p style="text-align: justify">The offshore data centre floater concept offers deep-sea cooling, integration with wind and solar generation and flexible location selection. Samsung Heavy plans to create a new revenue line by transferring its offshore yard experience to the rapidly growing AI infrastructure sector.</p>

<p style="text-align: justify">The model aims to serve hyperscaler players like NVIDIA, Amazon and Microsoft whose GPU-cluster capacity needs are expanding rapidly. In terms of energy efficiency and emissions goals, offshore data centres could relieve the water and power pressure on onshore facilities. Samsung Heavy is targeting a first prototype in 2027-2028.</p>
