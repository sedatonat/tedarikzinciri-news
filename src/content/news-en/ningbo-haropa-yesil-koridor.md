<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>A new international green shipping corridor</strong> has been established between <strong>China's Ningbo-Zhoushan Port</strong> and <strong>France's HAROPA Port Complex (Le Havre–Rouen–Paris)</strong>. The corridor was developed under the <strong>International Green Shipping Corridor Cooperation Initiative</strong> and was announced during the <strong>2025 North Bund Forum</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The objective is to accelerate <strong>decarbonisation in maritime transport</strong>, create a sustainable cooperation model for <strong>clean-energy vessels</strong> and <strong>low-carbon port operations</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>China–France cooperation in green shipping</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">This initiative is based on a <strong>Memorandum of Understanding (MoU)</strong> signed under the <strong>Maritime Silk Road Forum</strong> and brings together key stakeholders including <strong>Bureau Veritas Marine &amp; Offshore</strong>, <strong>Zhejiang Provincial Seaport Investment &amp; Operation Group</strong>, <strong>HAROPA Ports</strong>, <strong>MSC Group</strong>, <strong>MSC Terminal Investment Limited</strong>, <strong>CMA CGM</strong> and <strong>China Waterborne Transport Research Institute</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This multi-stakeholder collaboration will establish coordination among <strong>port authorities, shipping lines, classification societies and research institutions</strong>, setting an exemplary framework for <strong>zero-carbon fuel infrastructure</strong>, <strong>fuel certification</strong> and <strong>emission reduction standards</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A seven-pillar green framework</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The forum unveiled a <strong>seven-pillar framework for the Green Shipping Corridor Initiative</strong>. This framework supports the development of an <strong>international green corridor network</strong> and focuses on the following areas:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Next-generation clean-energy vessels,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Near-zero carbon port operations,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sustainable fuel production and supply chains,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Green fuel certification and verification systems,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>International standard alignment and information sharing.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This framework aims to contribute to the <strong>IMO 2050 net-zero vision</strong> in alignment with <strong>Paris Climate Agreement</strong> objectives.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Technical leadership: Bureau Veritas (BV)</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Bureau Veritas (BV)</strong> has assumed the role of <strong>technical lead partner</strong>, tasked with developing <strong>certification, verification and emission reduction technologies</strong> for the project.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Working with other stakeholders, BV will:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Develop the corridor's certification system,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Establish fuel quality and safety protocols,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Develop new ship energy efficiency solutions.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Matthieu de Tugny</strong>, Senior Vice President of Bureau Veritas's Industrial and Raw Materials unit, commented on the collaboration:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"This corridor will exemplify international cooperation in green maritime transport. While strengthening China–Europe maritime ties, it will directly contribute to global climate objectives. Bureau Veritas is committed to turning this vision into reality through innovation and certification support across the entire value chain."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Contributing to the global decarbonisation agenda</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new green corridor is viewed as a concrete step toward <strong>transitioning maritime transport to sustainable energy</strong> in line with <strong>the IMO's net-zero carbon emission target by 2050</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Participating companies—particularly <strong>MSC</strong> and <strong>CMA CGM</strong>—are already investing in alternative fuels such as <strong>biomethane, methanol and LNG</strong>. The corridor will serve as a testing platform for <strong>integrating such technologies into real operations</strong> and <strong>standardizing fuel supply infrastructure</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Zhejiang Seaport Group</strong> and <strong>HAROPA Ports</strong> will carry out joint projects to develop <strong>electrification, shore power</strong> and <strong>carbon tracking systems</strong> at ports. This will accelerate the <strong>green transition pace at both Asian and European ports</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>One of the first links in an international network</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, the Ningbo–HAROPA connection will be among the first links in a future <strong>global green shipping corridor network</strong>. This network will promote <strong>low-carbon transport on Asia–Europe trade routes</strong>, setting an example for <strong>emission management, fuel certification and sustainable supply chains</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The project also aligns with <strong>China's objective of increasing green technology exports in the maritime sector</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: The green corridor as a symbol of strategic cooperation</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">This initiative transcends a simple environmental project between two countries, instead serving as <strong>a diplomatic and economic model for green transformation in maritime transport</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">With Bureau Veritas's technical leadership and support from global operators such as CMA CGM and MSC, <strong>the implementation of international standards to accelerate emission reduction in shipping</strong> is expected.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">A <strong>green shipping corridor</strong> has been established between <strong>Ningbo-Zhoushan (China)</strong> and <strong>HAROPA (France)</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The initiative was developed under the <strong>International Green Shipping Corridor Cooperation Initiative</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Partners:</strong> Bureau Veritas, MSC, CMA CGM, HAROPA Ports, Zhejiang Seaport Group and China Waterborne Transport Research Institute.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Objectives: <strong>Clean-energy vessels, low-carbon ports, sustainable fuel chains and certification systems.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Bureau Veritas</strong> will provide technical support for certification and emission reduction.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The initiative is aligned with <strong>IMO 2050 net-zero targets</strong> and the <strong>Paris Agreement</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The project represents a new milestone in <strong>China–Europe green maritime cooperation</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
