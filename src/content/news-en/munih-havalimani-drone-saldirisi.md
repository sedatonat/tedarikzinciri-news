<p style="text-align: justify">Munich Airport temporarily halted operations after multiple drones were spotted in the vicinity of the airport.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Operations were then restarted in a controlled manner. Airport security protocols emphasize geofencing, integrated radar and imaging detection, and rapid response teams. The disruption caused short-term delays in European air cargo and passenger flows; slot coordination and re-accommodation plans were activated.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Airlines cancelled or rescheduled certain flights due to crew duty time limits and aircraft rotation constraints. The incident has renewed attention to the importance of U-space regulations and counter-UAS technologies.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Ultimately, the new risk category posed by unmanned aircraft systems in airspace management is increasing the flexibility requirements of airport operations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></p>
<p>1. Drone alerts result in short-term operational disruptions.</p>
<p>2. Geofencing and integrated detection protocols are being implemented.</p>
<p>3. Slot coordination is critical in delay management.</p>
<p>4. Counter-UAS solutions are increasing in importance.</p>
<p>5. Operational flexibility mitigates risks.</p>
