<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Reliance Industries, India's largest oil importer and the country's most powerful conglomerate</strong>, announced that it has completely halted oil purchases from Russia. This decision stands out as a strategic move driven by <strong>EU bans taking effect in 2026</strong> on one hand, and <strong>US sanctions coming into force on November 21</strong> on the other.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Reliance's statement, <strong>the export-oriented refinery complex at Jamnagar</strong> will no longer process Russian crude oil. This facility is known as the world's largest oil export refinery and represents one of India's key nodes in global energy trade.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The company stated that the transition process was <strong>completed ahead of schedule</strong>:</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">"The transition process has been completed ahead of plan to ensure full compliance with EU restrictions coming into effect on January 21, 2026," the company said.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h2 style="text-align: justify"><strong>EU and US pressure proved decisive</strong></h2>
<p style="text-align: justify">This move is directly linked to <strong>the EU's ban on products derived from Russian oil sourced through third countries starting in 2026</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
In other words, even if a refinery purchases Russian oil and converts it to diesel, gasoline, or jet fuel — and the product is exported from a country like India to the EU — it falls under the ban.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">At the same time, <strong>new US sanctions targeting Rosneft and Lukoil</strong> accelerated Reliance's decision. Washington has tightened the global sanctions regime by designating these two companies as entities <strong>"providing financing for war."</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The White House</strong> welcomed Reliance's decision:</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">"We welcome this change and look forward to progress in US–India trade discussions."</p>
<p><br></p>
<h2 style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Trump administration's pressure on India</strong></h2>
<p style="text-align: justify">US President Donald Trump has long pressured India to <strong>reduce purchases of Russian oil and defense systems</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
Within the <strong>50% total tariff</strong> imposed on India in August 2025,</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>25% was imposed due to Russian oil and weapons purchases</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Trump administration frames this policy as part of its strategy to <strong>"drain Moscow's war economy."</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h2 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Is India's energy strategy changing?</strong></h2>
<p style="text-align: justify">Since 2022, India has increased purchases of <strong>discounted oil from Russia</strong>, at times reaching import levels close to 1.5 million barrels per day.</p>
<p><br>
However, as sanctions have expanded:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Insurance</strong> became harder to find,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tanker access</strong> was restricted,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Financing</strong> channels narrowed,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Export products bound for the EU market</strong> came under risk.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">For this reason, Reliance's move is viewed as a <strong>return to India's strategy of diversifying its energy supply chain</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h2 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Market impacts</strong></h2>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, Reliance's decision could:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Reduce Russian oil demand</strong> in the medium term,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Increase purchases</strong> from Saudi Arabia, Iraq, and the US,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Trigger realignments</strong> in global refinery trade,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Reduce sanction risk</strong> for fuel exports to the EU.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Furthermore, Jamnagar refinery's discontinuation of Russian oil processing could also <strong>weaken Russia's price advantage in the Asian market</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h2 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Political and economic consequences</strong></h2>
<p style="text-align: justify">The decision is critical not only in terms of energy trade but also for <strong>US–India relations</strong> and <strong>global geopolitical balance</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The US views India's move as <strong>a strategic gain</strong>.<br>
India, in turn, may seek:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Lower tariffs in trade</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Technology transfer facilitation</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Cooperation in the energy sector</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This development signals that when the EU ban takes effect in 2026, <strong>Asian refineries will reshape their export strategies to Europe</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h2 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h2>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Reliance has <strong>completely halted Russian oil imports.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Reason: <strong>EU's 2026 ban</strong> and <strong>US sanctions</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The White House welcomed the decision.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">India was a major Russian oil buyer between 2022–2024; now it must diversify its supplies.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Countries like Turkey, China, and the UAE may see their role in Russian oil flows increase.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, the decision will create <strong>a significant shift in direction in global oil trade</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
