<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Bloomberg</strong> reported that the <strong>Trump administration</strong> has backed away from its plan to <strong>auction offshore oil and natural gas drilling rights along the Atlantic coast of the United States</strong>. The decision came following <strong>strong opposition from Republican states in the Southeast region</strong> and <strong>environmental organizations</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Reasons for the reversal: Political and environmental pressure</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The White House had previously considered <strong>conducting extensive offshore drilling auctions along the U.S. East Coast</strong>. However, the plan sparked serious concerns in <strong>Republican states with tourism-dependent economies, such as Florida, South Carolina, and Georgia</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to sources, the cancellation of drilling plans in regions outside the <strong>"Gulf of America,"</strong> as the <strong>Gulf of Mexico</strong> has been renamed, was intended to <strong>reduce both economic and political risks</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">During Trump's first term, the <strong>Atlantic drilling plan</strong> was rejected by <strong>more than 150 municipalities</strong> and <strong>numerous state leaders, including Republicans</strong>. Governors of <strong>Florida and South Carolina</strong> in particular openly opposed the project, citing threats to coastal tourism.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Interior Department reviewing the plan</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>draft five-year offshore oil and gas exploration program</strong> being prepared by the <strong>U.S. Department of the Interior</strong> is currently being revised. This program is updated at regular intervals by federal law and is subject to <strong>public comment and congressional review</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to sources, while the draft program has not yet been released publicly, <strong>sales plans for the Atlantic coast have been removed</strong>, though <strong>the Gulf of Mexico</strong>, <strong>the West Coast</strong>, and <strong>certain areas off Alaska</strong> remain on the sales list.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">A <strong>White House official</strong> declined to comment on the leaked documents related to the matter, offering only the following statement:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"President Trump has kept his promise to revitalize the American energy sector. By removing the restrictions imposed by the Biden administration, we have unleashed energy production."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Reactions from industry and environmental groups</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">While the decision was welcomed by <strong>environmental organizations</strong>, the <strong>energy industry</strong> took a more cautious stance.</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Environmentalists</strong> argue that opening new oil and gas fields at a time of accelerating global warming is <strong>unacceptable for marine ecosystems and climate targets</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Oil industry representatives</strong>, while supporting new area licensing in the <strong>Gulf of Mexico</strong>, emphasize that <strong>exploration activities in the Atlantic are economically uncertain</strong>, with existing geological data dating back to the <strong>1970s and 1980s</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Limited energy potential</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">There is a <strong>lack of modern geological data</strong> regarding hydrocarbon potential along the Atlantic coast. Current information about the region is limited to <strong>drilling data from half a century ago</strong> and <strong>approximately 40 legacy exploratory wells</strong>. This is a key factor in reducing energy companies' interest.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Nevertheless, the <strong>Gulf of Mexico</strong> remains the primary region for U.S. offshore energy production. The Trump administration intends to continue licensing new sales in the Gulf and make this region the <strong>"center of national energy security."</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Geopolitical and economic context</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">This decision reflects the tension between <strong>energy independence rhetoric</strong> and <strong>regional political balance</strong>. While the Trump administration continues <strong>policies to increase fossil fuel production</strong>, it also had to account for <strong>public opposition in Republican states</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, this reversal can also be viewed as <strong>a strategic move to reduce political risks in coastal states ahead of the 2026 presidential election</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, while the Trump administration <strong>has shelved the Atlantic drilling plan</strong>, it continues <strong>policies to increase energy production capacity in the Gulf of Mexico, Alaska, and the West Coast</strong> regions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Trump administration</strong> has backed away from <strong>oil and gas exploration plans off the Atlantic coast</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The withdrawal occurred due to <strong>tourism concerns from Southeast Republican states</strong> and <strong>environmental opposition</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Gulf of Mexico</strong>, <strong>West Coast</strong>, and <strong>Alaska</strong> remain among planned sales areas.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>More than 150 coastal municipalities</strong> opposed the earlier draft.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>oil industry</strong> characterizes Atlantic discoveries as <strong>economically risky</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The decision is being viewed as <strong>a political risk-reduction strategy ahead of the 2026 election</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Trump administration</strong> has renamed the <strong>Gulf of Mexico as the "Gulf of America"</strong> and is directing energy investments accordingly.</p></li>
</ul>
