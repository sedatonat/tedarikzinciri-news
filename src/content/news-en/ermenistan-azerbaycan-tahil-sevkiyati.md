<p style="text-align: justify">Russian Railways (<strong>RZD</strong>) has announced the successful completion of <strong>the first grain shipment</strong> following the resumption of transit transportation to Armenia via Azerbaijan, a route that has remained closed since the 1990s. This development signals a reshaping of transport corridors in the South Caucasus and the revival of commercial ties among the three countries after years of disruption.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the announcement, <strong>1,050 tons of wheat</strong> was loaded into 15 hopper cars at the <strong>Dimitrovgrad</strong> station in Russia's Ulyanovsk region, then transported via Azerbaijan and subsequently through the Georgian route to <strong>reach Armenia</strong>. This shipment represents the de facto reopening of a trade corridor that has remained closed for years on the Russia–Azerbaijan–Georgia–Armenia line.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Route closed in the 1990s reopened</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Transit transportation between Russia and Armenia was suspended <strong>in the early 1990s</strong> due to regional conflicts following the dissolution of the Soviet Union. Political tensions between Azerbaijan and Armenia, in particular, led to the closure of transport corridors.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Today's first shipment is viewed as a tangible result of efforts toward diplomatic normalization in the region. RZD stated that the shipment was made possible <strong>within the framework of official decisions</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>New shipments on the horizon</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Russian Railways announced plans to send <strong>132 cars of wheat by the end of January 2026</strong> along the same route to Armenia. Additionally, the corridor is being evaluated for transporting not only grain but various types of cargo.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This development represents a critical step for Armenia's food security. The country is heavily dependent on wheat and grain products from Russia. The reopening of the Azerbaijan route is expected to:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">reduce logistics costs,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">shorten shipping times,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">increase supply chain stability.<br></p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Regional logistics reshaping</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The resumption of this transit marks a strategic transformation for the South Caucasus. The opening of the long-closed corridor:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">facilitates Russia's access to Armenia,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">strengthens Azerbaijan's central position in the regional logistics network,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">reinforces Georgia's role as a transit country.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This corridor could eventually become part of a larger logistics ecosystem at the intersection of <strong>North–South and East–West transport networks</strong>, encompassing Turkey, Iran, and Central Asia.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Geopolitical implications</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">While the new transit route in the region suggests increased diplomatic momentum in Azerbaijan–Armenia relations, the process remains complex. The transit decision's background includes:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">normalization talks recently conducted between the two countries,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">regional trade pressures,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Russia's efforts to diversify Caucasus logistics.<br></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Nevertheless, the corridor's sustainability will depend largely on regional stability.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>first wheat shipment from Russia to Armenia via Azerbaijan</strong> has been completed.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Shipment: <strong>1,050 tons of wheat in 15 hopper cars</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Transit has reopened for the <strong>first time since the 1990s</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">An additional <strong>132 cars</strong> will be sent by the end of January 2026.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The corridor's permanence depends on regional stability.</p></li>
</ul>
