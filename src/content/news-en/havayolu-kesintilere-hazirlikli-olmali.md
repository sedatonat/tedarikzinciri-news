<p>The recent global software outage caused major flight delays and cargo shipment disruptions. Following these events, the U.S. Congress introduced new legislation requiring airlines to strengthen their operational resilience plans. The bill aims to ensure that airlines are better prepared for anticipated disruptions such as severe weather conditions and information technology (IT) system failures. Under this legislation, companies will need to develop strategies to minimize the impact of such incidents. Additionally, measures against cybersecurity risks are being emphasized. The law's objective is to ensure passengers receive a safe and seamless travel experience.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5>Key Points:</h5>\n<ol>\n  <li><p><strong>Legislative Proposal:</strong> Makes operational resilience plans mandatory for airlines.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Objective:</strong> Being prepared for anticipated disruptions such as severe weather and IT failures.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Cybersecurity:</strong> The proposal includes IT system security and protection against cyber threats.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Airlines:</strong> Companies must develop strategies to minimize the impact of such events.</p></li>\n</ol>\n<p><br></p>\n
