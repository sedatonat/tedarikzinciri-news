<p><strong>Hyster-Yale Materials Handling, Inc.</strong>, a manufacturer of forklifts and container handling equipment under the <strong>Hyster</strong> and <strong>Yale</strong> brands, has announced its plan to meet domestic manufacturing requirements under the <strong>Build America, Buy America (BABA) Act</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>The company aims to expand its existing American manufacturing facilities to provide high-capacity models tailored to the needs of infrastructure projects subject to BABA requirements, with a particular focus on electric options.</p>
<p>Among these plans are the identification of the most suitable manufacturing location in the United States, the expansion of strategic supply agreements to meet local material requirements, and further development of electric power options for high-capacity equipment.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>The BABA Act is legislation designed to increase the use of American-manufactured materials in federally financed infrastructure projects across the United States.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>The law requires federal funding for infrastructure projects such as roads, bridges, ports, and public transit systems to prioritize materials manufactured in the United States, including steel, iron, and various construction materials.</p>
<p><strong>Tony Salgado</strong>, Director of Operations at <strong>Hyster-Yale</strong>, said, "In addition to collaborating with our key domestic suppliers, we are working to determine how best to leverage our own American manufacturing infrastructure to provide a competitive solution for our customers and stakeholders."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>"We are committed to maintaining industry-leading standards in design, durability, and performance—qualities our brands are known for worldwide and that our customers trust and expect."</p>
<p>Earlier this year, <strong>Hyster Company</strong> announced the availability of <strong>Power Cellect</strong>, an optional package for 27 Hyster electric forklift models.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Power Cellect allows customers to transition between traditional lead-acid and low-maintenance thin-plate pure lead (TPPL) and lithium-ion alternative power sources in electric vehicles.</p>
<p>This solution is particularly beneficial for fleets transitioning to low-maintenance battery solutions and for equipment that will be used across different applications throughout its service life.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Additionally, <strong>Hyster</strong>'s empty container handling capabilities help ports save time and costs by allowing them to transport two empty containers simultaneously.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>This dual-container handling feature offers significant advantages for operations seeking to manage increasing demand while maintaining efficiency.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Hyster-Yale</strong>'s commitment to complying with BABA requirements reflects the company's determination to support American manufacturing and collaborate with domestic suppliers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>This initiative aligns with the objectives of the BABA Act, which aims to increase the use of domestically manufactured materials in infrastructure projects across the United States.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>By developing electric power options for high-capacity equipment, the company aims to both support environmental sustainability and provide innovative solutions to its customers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>These initiatives reinforce <strong>Hyster-Yale</strong>'s commitment to maintaining industry-leading standards and meeting customer expectations.</p>
<p><br></p>
