<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>FedEx</strong>, <strong>UPS</strong> and <strong>DHL Express</strong> say they are working to secure tariff refunds on eligible shipments and issue the refunds back to the customers who originally paid them, according to notices posted on their websites. <strong>U.S. Customs and Border Protection</strong> (<strong>CBP</strong>) launched its dedicated refund system for now-defunct <strong>International Emergency Economic Powers Act</strong> (<strong>IEEPA</strong>) tariffs on Monday, with the first phase of the system's rollout focused on handling entries liquidated in the previous 80 days. According to <strong>CBP</strong>, once an entry is submitted and accepted, it will take roughly 60-90 days to issue the refund.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>FedEx</strong> said it will submit refund declarations to <strong>CBP</strong>, adding that it has "committed to do that on behalf of all customers for whom we served as <strong>customs broker</strong>." <strong>FedEx</strong> will issue refunds for <strong>IEEPA</strong> tariffs paid by customers once the company begins to receive refunds. For shipments where <strong>UPS</strong> was the <strong>importer of record</strong>, the company said it "will work to request and retrieve <strong>IEEPA</strong> tariff refunds from <strong>CBP</strong> on our customers' behalf." <strong>UPS</strong> added that it cannot issue the refunds until it receives the funds from <strong>CBP</strong>, but has established a process to issue refunds to the payors after that.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>DHL Express</strong> said it will automatically file refund claims for eligible entries covered by the first phase of <strong>CBP</strong>'s refund system rollout. Like <strong>FedEx</strong> and <strong>UPS</strong>, <strong>DHL</strong> will return the refunds <strong>CBP</strong> issues to the party that originally paid the duties. For shipments where a <strong>DHL</strong> customer served as the <strong>importer of record</strong>, the customer can initiate refund claims directly through <strong>CBP</strong>'s processes or through an authorized representative. On the first day of the refund system's launch, over 55,000 parties submitted claims for more than 4 million imports, <strong>CBP</strong> Executive Assistant Commissioner <strong>Susan S. Thomas</strong> said.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. FedEx, UPS and DHL will receive IEEPA tariff refunds from CBP and pass them to customers.<br>
2. CBP refund system operates with a 60-90 day processing window.<br>
3. FedEx will file on behalf of all customers for whom it served as customs broker.<br>
4. DHL will automatically file claims for eligible entries in the first rollout phase.<br>
5. On the launch day, over 55,000 parties submitted claims for 4M+ imports.</p>
