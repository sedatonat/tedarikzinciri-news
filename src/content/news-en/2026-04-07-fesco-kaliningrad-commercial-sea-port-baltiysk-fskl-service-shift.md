<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>FESCO Transport Group</strong> has shifted its <strong>FESCO Saint-Petersburg Kaliningrad Line (FSKL)</strong> to <strong>Kaliningrad Commercial Sea Port</strong> from <strong>Baltiysk</strong>. According to the Group, the terminal change has allowed FESCO to consolidate all cargo it handles in the region at one terminal, reduce handling time, and intensify operations.</p><p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, <strong>road delivery to and from Kaliningrad Commercial Sea Port is shorter</strong>, which optimises last-mile delivery in the city of Kaliningrad and the entire Kaliningrad region. <strong>FSKL</strong> was the first cabotage service FESCO launched in the Baltic. The line links St. Petersburg and Kaliningrad deploying two sisterships — <strong>FESCO Navarin</strong> and <strong>FESCO Novik</strong> — with a total capacity of over <strong>1,400 TEU</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The service frequency continues on a regular basis. Kaliningrad holds strategic logistical importance as Russia's exclave surrounded by EU territory. While overland traffic via Lithuania and Poland is occasionally disrupted by sanctions and inspections, FSKL's direct sea connection guarantees trade between the Russian mainland and this region. The terminal change contributes significantly to continuity of supply for the region's industry.</p>

<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. FESCO shifted the FSKL service from Baltiysk to Kaliningrad Commercial Sea Port.<br>
2. The shift consolidates all regional cargo at one terminal.<br>
3. Road delivery distances to Kaliningrad Commercial Sea Port are shorter; last-mile delivery is optimised.<br>
4. FSKL remains FESCO's first cabotage service in the Baltic.<br>
5. The line operates with FESCO Navarin and FESCO Novik totalling over 1,400 TEU capacity.</p>
