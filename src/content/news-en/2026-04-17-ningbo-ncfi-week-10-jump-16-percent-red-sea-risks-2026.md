<p style="text-align: justify">The composite <strong>Ningbo Containerized Freight Index (NCFI)</strong> jumped 16.1 per cent from the previous week to <strong>1,147.5 points</strong> in Week 10, reflecting <strong>heightened Red Sea risks</strong>. Of the 21 trade lanes tracked by the index, 10 recorded higher freight rates, nine declined, and two remained unchanged. Among major ports along the <strong>Maritime Silk Road</strong>, nine reported rising indices while seven saw decreases.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Freight rates to <strong>Europe</strong> and the <strong>Mediterranean</strong> declined as early March rate hikes by some carriers were offset by continued capacity oversupply. The NCFI Europe route fell 3.1 per cent to <strong>956 points</strong>. The Eastern Mediterranean index dropped 8.1 per cent to 1,115.7 points, while the Western Mediterranean route slipped 0.6 per cent to 1,334 points. On <strong>transpacific</strong> routes, demand lagged while capacity returned to the market, weighing on spot rates.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Ningbo–<strong>US East Coast</strong> index fell 3.9 per cent to 908.4 points, and the Ningbo–<strong>US West Coast</strong> index also moved lower. The overall picture shows that while Red Sea security concerns drove longer-haul rate increases, classic Asia–Europe and Asia–US corridors continued to face <strong>rate weakness</strong>. The mixed outlook suggests freight markets remain caught between regional risks and capacity dynamics.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. The composite NCFI rose 16.1 per cent to 1,147.5 points in Week 10.<br>
2. Of 21 lanes, 10 saw rate increases, nine declined, and two were unchanged.<br>
3. The Europe route fell 3.1 per cent to 956 points.<br>
4. Eastern Mediterranean dropped 8.1 per cent and Western Mediterranean slipped 0.6 per cent.<br>
5. Weak demand on transpacific routes pushed spot rates lower.</p>
