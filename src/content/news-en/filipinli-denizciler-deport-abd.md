<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Diplomatic tensions escalate along the Washington-Manila corridor.</strong></p>
<p><br>
In recent months, <strong>a rising number of Filipino seafarers have been denied entry and deported from U.S. ports</strong>, prompting the Philippine government to launch formal diplomatic intervention.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Philippine Embassy</strong> announced on Tuesday that it has engaged with <strong>the U.S. State Department</strong> and <strong>the Department of Homeland Security (DHS)</strong>, among other agencies, regarding the issue.</p>
<p><br>
The Embassy noted that recent cases have created a "troubling trend."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Philippine government data, <strong>approximately 100 Filipino seafarers have been deported from the U.S. through mid-2025.</strong></p>
<p><br>
According to sources, most of these cases involve crew members serving aboard <strong>cruise ships</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Manila: "The Welfare of Our Seafarers is Our Priority"</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Embassy statement included the following:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"The welfare of our seafarers is our primary priority.<br>
We are actively engaged with our counterparts in Washington to ensure that Filipino seafarers arriving in the U.S. receive fair and transparent treatment."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">The statement also noted that the Manila administration is <strong>working in collaboration with unions, shipowners, and port agents to compile a verified list of incidents</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Possible Causes: New Visa Procedures and Security Reviews</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Seafarer entry denials are typically linked to <strong>visa irregularities, document discrepancies, or security concerns identified during Port State Control inspections</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
However, the scale of the recent increase has raised suspicions that <strong>the U.S. may have implemented changes to its screening protocols or security practices</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The U.S. State Department and Department of Homeland Security had not issued an official statement on the matter as of the publication of this report.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Philippine Maritime Authority Examining Data</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Maritime Administration</strong> in Manila is requesting data from local <strong>manning agencies</strong> to determine whether the incidents are indicative of <strong>a systematic change or isolated cases</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Local media reported that the incident has <strong>resonated strongly with the Philippine public</strong> and that the government expects the U.S. to demonstrate "clarity and cooperation."</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Changes in U.S. Visa Policy: Pressuring the Industry</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to information obtained by <strong>Splash</strong>, numerous <strong>shipowners and crew management companies</strong> have been experiencing significant difficulties with U.S. visas in recent months.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">These issues stem from a series of <strong>executive orders</strong> signed by the <strong>Trump administration</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The new regulations have eliminated the long-standing <strong>multiple-entry</strong> seafarer visas, replacing them with <strong>single-entry</strong> visas.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under this system:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Seafarers are <strong>sent back to their home countries</strong> after their first U.S. port visit,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>They must reapply for a visa</strong> to continue working aboard the same vessel.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, <strong>visa validity periods have been shortened</strong>, which contradicts the standard contract durations under the <strong>Maritime Labour Convention (MLC)</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
As a result, seafarers cannot maintain continuous work flows, and shipowners are facing <strong>serious operational difficulties with crew rotation schedules</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Analysis: Labor Policies Affecting Maritime Flows</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Experts caution that this narrowing of U.S. visa policy may affect not only individual seafarers but also <strong>international maritime flows</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
Filipino seafarers comprise <strong>approximately 25 percent of the global crew workforce</strong> — in other words, one in four ship workers is Filipino.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Consequently, a lasting barrier to this group's access to the U.S. market could also impact <strong>maritime shipping operations</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: A New Tension Point Between Diplomacy and Seafaring</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The matter of deported Filipino seafarers is creating <strong>a new area of debate at the intersection of international maritime labor mobility and national security policy</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The Manila government is <strong>pursuing solutions through diplomatic channels</strong>, but the U.S.'s <strong>new security-focused visa policies</strong> show no signs of backing down in the near term.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This development indicates that issues surrounding <strong>workforce access and travel freedom in the global maritime sector</strong> will become increasingly contentious in the coming period.</p>
<p><br></p>
