<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> reports that <strong>Federal Ammunition</strong>, a manufacturer of hunting rifle cartridges, shotshells, centerfire and rimfire ammunition and components, is based in <strong>Anoka, Minnesota</strong>, with more than a century of history and currently employs 1,500 workers. In November 2024, the company was sold to <strong>Czechoslovak Group</strong>, based in <strong>Prague</strong>, which also acquired ammunition brands <strong>Remington</strong>, <strong>Hevi Shot</strong>, <strong>Speer</strong>, and <strong>CCI</strong>. <strong>Federal Ammunition</strong> takes pride in its expertise in ammunition production and manufacturing knowledge. However, when it comes to order fulfillment and inventory management, the company has found that manual warehouse processes are preventing it from ensuring timely and accurate shipments. The company faces several operational challenges: (1) the <strong>ERP</strong> version it uses is no longer supported, creating troubleshooting issues and increasing operational risk; (2) the <strong>WMS</strong> is used only for outbound shipping tasks such as carrier selection, load building, label printing, and shipping documentation, while most major warehouse functions remain untouched; (3) there are significant errors in the <strong>ERP</strong> and <strong>WMS</strong> interfaces, with much of the warehouse still operating on a paper-based system. Manual order picking is slowing operations and leaving room for errors.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Federal Ammunition</strong> has separated from <strong>Vista Outdoor Inc.</strong>'s <strong>Sporting Products</strong> division, acquired by <strong>Czechoslovak Group</strong> (<strong>CSG</strong>, <strong>Prague, Czech Republic</strong>) in November 2024 for $2.15 billion. <strong>Czechoslovak Group</strong> is a defense industry conglomerate owned by <strong>Michal Strnad</strong>. Following the acquisition, the new company was rebranded under the name <strong>The Kinetic Group</strong>, with <strong>Federal Ammunition</strong>, <strong>Remington Ammunition</strong> (<strong>Lonoke, Arkansas</strong>), <strong>CCI</strong> (<strong>Lewiston, Idaho</strong>), <strong>Speer Ammunition</strong> (<strong>Lewiston, Idaho</strong>), and <strong>HEVI-Shot</strong> as its primary brands. Key U.S. firearms and ammunition sector competitors include <strong>Olin Corporation</strong>'s <strong>Winchester</strong> division (<strong>East Alton, Illinois</strong>); <strong>Vista Outdoor</strong>'s <strong>Outdoor Products</strong> segment (<strong>Revelyst</strong>, <strong>Camp Chef</strong>, <strong>Bell</strong>, <strong>Giro</strong>, <strong>Bushnell</strong>); <strong>Sig Sauer</strong> (<strong>Newington, New Hampshire</strong>); <strong>Hornady Manufacturing</strong> (<strong>Grand Island, Nebraska</strong>); <strong>Smith &amp; Wesson</strong> (<strong>Maryville, Tennessee</strong>); <strong>Sturm Ruger &amp; Co.</strong> (<strong>Southport, Connecticut</strong>); <strong>O.F. Mossberg &amp; Sons</strong>; and <strong>Beretta USA</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, modern warehouse modernization includes: (1) <strong>Tier 1 WMS</strong> solutions such as <strong>Manhattan Active Warehouse Management</strong>, <strong>Blue Yonder Warehouse Management</strong>, <strong>SAP Extended Warehouse Management</strong> (<strong>EWM</strong>), <strong>Oracle Warehouse Management Cloud</strong> (<strong>WMS Cloud</strong>, formerly <strong>LogFire</strong>), <strong>Körber Supply Chain</strong> (<strong>HighJump</strong>), <strong>Infor WMS</strong>, <strong>Microsoft Dynamics 365 SCM</strong>, <strong>Made4net</strong>, <strong>Softeon</strong>, <strong>Tecsys</strong>, <strong>3PL Central</strong> (<strong>Extensiv</strong>), and <strong>Mantis WMS</strong>; (2) <strong>RF</strong> scanners from <strong>Zebra Technologies</strong>, <strong>Honeywell</strong>, and <strong>Datalogic</strong>; (3) voice-directed picking solutions such as <strong>Honeywell Voice</strong>, <strong>Lucas Systems</strong>, and <strong>Vocollect</strong>; (4) pick-to-light; (5) put-to-light; (6) <strong>AS/RS</strong> (<strong>Automated Storage and Retrieval Systems</strong>); (7) <strong>AGV</strong> (<strong>Automated Guided Vehicles</strong>); (8) <strong>AMR</strong> (<strong>Autonomous Mobile Robots</strong> from providers such as <strong>Locus Robotics</strong>, <strong>6 River Systems</strong>, <strong>Geek+</strong>, <strong>Fetch Robotics</strong>); (9) goods-to-person solutions from <strong>Kardex</strong>, <strong>Symbotic</strong>, <strong>AutoStore</strong>, and <strong>Exotec</strong>); and (10) conveyor and sortation systems from <strong>Honeywell Intelligrated</strong>, <strong>Dematic</strong>, <strong>Daifuku</strong>, <strong>Murata</strong>, <strong>Beumer</strong>, <strong>Körber/Sicon</strong>, and <strong>Bastian Solutions</strong>. Additional supply chain software categories include <strong>YMS</strong> (<strong>Yard Management System</strong>), <strong>TMS</strong> (<strong>Transportation Management System</strong>), <strong>OMS</strong> (<strong>Order Management System</strong>), and <strong>DOM</strong> (<strong>Distributed Order Management</strong>).</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the U.S. ammunition industry operates under a regulatory framework overseen by <strong>NSSF</strong> (<strong>National Shooting Sports Foundation</strong>) and <strong>SAAMI</strong> (<strong>Sporting Arms and Ammunition Manufacturers Institute</strong>). Since 2020–2021, U.S. civilian ammunition demand has reached historic levels, with <strong>NICS</strong> (<strong>National Instant Criminal Background Check System</strong>) figures setting records, creating supply chain constraints in raw materials such as copper, brass, lead, nickel, nitrocellulose, nitroglycerin, tungsten, steel, and plastic. <strong>Federal Ammunition</strong>'s warehouse modernization project includes the following key steps: (1) expanding <strong>SKU</strong> numbers through multi-brand integration; (2) reslotting; (3) additional bulk locations; (4) <strong>RF</strong> scanning; (5) voice-directed picking; (6) end-to-end <strong>WMS</strong> functional coverage; and (7) modernizing <strong>ERP</strong>-<strong>WMS</strong> integration. In conclusion, <strong>Federal Ammunition</strong>'s warehouse modernization case exemplifies the challenges of post-acquisition operational integration and technological transformation in the U.S. manufacturing sector, and appears to serve as a reference point for future <strong>M&amp;A</strong> integration projects managing the transition to high <strong>SKU</strong> variety with minimal disruption.</p>
