<p style="text-align: justify">Walmart is transforming its supply chain operations from a system that merely reacts to visible disruptions into <strong>a proactive structure capable of responding in advance to upcoming change signals</strong>. The company is using <strong>artificial intelligence-powered analytics</strong> to combine multiple sources—including <strong>POS (Point of Sale) data</strong>, <strong>weather</strong>, <strong>promotional calendars</strong>, <strong>social media trends</strong>, and <strong>supplier performance</strong>—from in-store and online demand to build <strong>demand sensing</strong> models. This enables demand fluctuations to be predicted and managed before they occur.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Walmart's <strong>predictive ETA (Estimated Time of Arrival)</strong> and <strong>inventory positioning</strong> engines dynamically optimize inventory placement. Which distribution center or store a product should be located in is continuously updated based on demand projections. Additionally, <strong>substitution recommendations</strong> offered by the system minimize shelf gaps in the event of supply delays or stock shortages. This keeps the customer experience uninterrupted.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">On the warehouse side, collaboration between <strong>computer vision</strong> and <strong>AMR (Autonomous Mobile Robots)</strong> reduces operational errors. Image processing technologies monitor whether products are picked correctly and positioned accurately. AMRs adjust routes based on these validations to optimize workflow. During the shipping phase, <strong>dynamic routing</strong> and <strong>yard management</strong> systems reduce wait times, improving truck and loading dock efficiency.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In supplier relationships, Walmart increases performance visibility through <strong>scorecard</strong> and <strong>control tower</strong> implementations. This allows exceptions to be identified and resolved early. Alerts for delays, quality issues, or logistics deviations are managed in real time.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In summary, Walmart's <strong>proactive artificial intelligence-based supply chain</strong> approach enhances not only the balance of speed and cost, but also <strong>service levels</strong> and <strong>customer satisfaction</strong>. This model, powered by data-driven forecasting engines, is establishing a new standard in the retail sector.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Points:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li><p>Demand sensing is fed by multiple data sources.</p></li>
  <li><p>Predictive ETA optimizes inventory positioning.</p></li>
  <li><p>Computer vision + AMR reduces errors.</p></li>
  <li><p>Dynamic routing lowers wait times.</p></li>
  <li><p>Control tower accelerates exception management.</p></li>
</ul>
