<p style="text-align: justify">Greek tanker owner <strong>Performance Shipping</strong> has confirmed the end of a sales process for <strong>P Sophia, a 2009-built aframax</strong>, the oldest vessel in its fleet. The <strong>forward sale + exclusivity agreement</strong> announced by the company in April was contingent on the vessel being selected as a candidate for an <strong>FPSO (Floating Production Storage and Offloading)</strong> conversion project. However, the project in question did not favor P Sophia, and the agreement was thus automatically terminated.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Performance Shipping's announcement, the unnamed third party acting as the buyer informed the company that the vessel was eliminated at the <strong>final cut</strong> stage for the relevant offshore project. This project is aimed at an offshore production field expected to be awarded in 2026 and involved the selection of a tanker suitable for FPSO conversion. Since P Sophia did not pass this selection, the exclusivity arrangement that took effect in April 2025 has automatically terminated.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Financial Dimensions of the Deal</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under the canceled agreement:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The sale price was fixed at $36 million USD</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The project would be awarded in 2026, and if the tanker were delivered <strong>within 120 days</strong>, the sale would be completed.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, a bonus of <strong>an additional $1 million USD</strong> would have been applied if delivery could be achieved <strong>by the end of September 2025</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This structure was a good example of the "conditional forward sale model" common in FPSO conversion projects. Because the tanker was deemed unsuitable for conversion, the financial agreement has been entirely eliminated.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>FPSO Conversion Market Context</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">While VLCCs in the 530,000–700,000 dwt range are typically preferred for FPSO conversion, some projects also consider <strong>aframax class</strong> vessels. However, FPSO suitability depends on numerous technical criteria including hull integrity, structural fatigue indicators, tank arrangement, pump capacity, and life-cycle costs.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">P Sophia's status as the oldest vessel in the <strong>fleet age profile</strong> (2009 built) may have been a factor in its non-selection. Because FPSO conversions will encompass a 20–25 year operating period, younger tonnage may be preferred.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Impact on Company Fleet</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">With the conversion tender excluding P Sophia, the tanker will continue to remain in <strong>active commercial operations</strong> within Performance Shipping's fleet. The company operates a <strong>12-vessel</strong> aframax fleet on a fully delivered basis. P Sophia's retention in the fleet:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>capacity retention</strong> in spot rotations,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>additional income potential</strong> from rising Black Sea and Med region freight rates,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">opportunity for <strong>asset value appreciation</strong> due to rising second-hand tanker prices</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">can provide such advantages.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">On the other hand, P Sophia's presence may create a burden in terms of <strong>maintenance cost</strong> and technical investment planning with respect to the average age of the fleet. Particularly during 2024–2026, increasing <strong>CII (Carbon Intensity Indicator)</strong> and <strong>EEXI compliance</strong> requirements in the tanker market are driving higher operational costs for older vessels.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic Assessment</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">As competition intensifies in the FPSO conversion market, finding suitable tankers for conversion is becoming increasingly difficult. Performance Shipping's decision to retain P Sophia reflects a mid-term approach to capitalize on favorable freight rate trends in the spot market. Furthermore, the company could evaluate the vessel as an alternative candidate if another conversion project emerges in the future; however, this possibility is low for a 2009-built aframax.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In broad terms, P Sophia's failure to be sold does not create a major problem in the company's financial planning. Tonnage tightness continues in the tanker market, and the company's active fleet utilization remains strong.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">With P Sophia's elimination from the FPSO conversion tender, the <strong>$36 million USD forward sale</strong> agreement was canceled.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The vessel's age may have been one of the reasons for its non-selection for conversion.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">P Sophia will continue commercial operations in Performance Shipping's <strong>12-vessel</strong> aframax fleet.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Technical criteria in the FPSO conversion market are very stringent; older tonnage is at a disadvantage.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The company may focus on generating revenues from the spot market in the near term.</p></li>
</ul>
