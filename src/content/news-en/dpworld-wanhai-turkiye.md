<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>DP World</strong>, through a new partnership with <strong>Wan Hai Lines</strong>, has launched a new shipping line that will <strong>position Turkey as a significant hub in Far East–East Mediterranean container traffic</strong>. The service, named <strong>FM1</strong>, will <strong>directly connect ports in China and Southeast Asia</strong> to <strong>Turkey's Gulf Terminal</strong> via the Suez Canal.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the agreement, <strong>the FM1 service will call at Gulf Terminal on a weekly basis</strong> and will operate with vessels each with <strong>4,300–6,500 TEU</strong> capacity. The first vessel on the route, <strong>Wan Hai 513</strong>, <strong>made its inaugural call to Turkey on October 23, 2025</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic significance of the new FM1 service</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new service will <strong>directly connect Asian ports such as China, Vietnam, Thailand, Malaysia, and Singapore</strong> with <strong>trade centres in the East Mediterranean</strong>, thereby <strong>reducing transit times</strong>. This route will provide <strong>an alternative and faster shipping connection</strong> for cargo transiting through the Suez Canal.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">With this move, <strong>Turkey's Gulf Terminal</strong> becomes a <strong>key logistics hub in the trade network between the Far East and Europe</strong>. The terminal holds a central position in DP World's <strong>East European and Mediterranean strategy</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>DP World East Europe Vice President Kris Adams</strong> commented following the agreement:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"We are delighted to bring Wan Hai Lines into our East European network. Our Gulf Terminal plays a strategic role in connecting Turkey to global trade routes. The FM1 service strengthens this connection and further solidifies DP World's position in the region," he said.</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Wan Hai Lines: A brand expanding from Asia to Europe</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Wan Hai Lines</strong>, headquartered in Taiwan, is a container shipping company and a <strong>leader in intra-Asia transport</strong>. In recent years, the company has rapidly expanded its container capacity and risen to become the <strong>world's 11th largest ocean shipping company</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The FM1 service is strategically significant as it represents <strong>Wan Hai's second major route with direct connections to European ports</strong>. Through this, the company aims to take on a stronger role in the <strong>Asia–Europe transport chain</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to DP World officials, this new service line in Turkey will <strong>further increase DP World Gulf Terminal's capacity to handle growing regional cargo traffic</strong>. The terminal stands out both as <strong>one of the new calling points for Asia–Europe trade</strong> and as <strong>a logistics hub connecting the Gulf, Black Sea, and Middle East routes</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Turkey's role in the global logistics network strengthens</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, the commencement of FM1 operations through Turkey will <strong>strengthen the country's strategic logistics position in the East Mediterranean</strong>. The proximity of Gulf Terminal to the Suez Canal provides <strong>advantages in reducing transit times and lowering costs in Asia–Europe trade</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This move by DP World is part of the company's strategy to <strong>optimize shipping connections between Europe, the Middle East, Africa, and Asia</strong>. Most recently, the company announced a <strong>5 billion dollar investment plan for Indian ports</strong> and accelerated its regional expansion plans.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new FM1 service demonstrates that DP World's investments in Turkey will <strong>directly contribute to regional container shipping</strong> and position Turkey as a <strong>priority transshipment hub</strong> in <strong>Asia–Mediterranean supply chains</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li><p><strong>DP World</strong>, in partnership with <strong>Wan Hai Lines</strong>, has launched the <strong>FM1 Far East–East Mediterranean service</strong> in Turkey.</p></li>
  <li><p><strong>The FM1 service</strong> connects <strong>China and Southeast Asia to Turkey's Gulf Terminal via the Suez Canal</strong>.</p></li>
  <li><p>The service will operate on <strong>weekly schedules</strong> with vessel capacity in the <strong>4,300–6,500 TEU</strong> range.</p></li>
  <li><p>The first vessel, <strong>Wan Hai 513</strong>, <strong>made its inaugural call to Gulf Terminal on October 23, 2025.</strong></p></li>
  <li><p><strong>Wan Hai</strong> is the <strong>world's 11th largest container line</strong>, and FM1 is its <strong>second service with European connections.</strong></p></li>
  <li><p><strong>DP World</strong> is positioning Turkey at <strong>the centre of the East Mediterranean logistics chain</strong>.</p></li>
  <li><p>Objectives: <strong>Shorter transit times, increased capacity, and strengthening Turkey's role in Asia–Europe trade.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
