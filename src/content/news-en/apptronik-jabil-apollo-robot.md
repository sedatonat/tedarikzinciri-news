<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Apptronik</strong>, an Austin-based robot manufacturer, has reached an agreement with supply chain giant <strong>Jabil</strong> to collaborate on the manufacturing and testing of <strong>Apollo</strong> humanoid robots. The partnership aims to accelerate the integration of humanoid robots into manufacturing processes and create a more efficient work environment in factories.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Apollo</strong> robots will be tested in real-world conditions at <strong>Jabil</strong>'s facilities, performing simple and repetitive tasks such as inspection, sorting, line-side delivery, and fixture placement. This is expected to free up human workers to focus on more creative and cognitively demanding projects. If the tests prove successful, <strong>Jabil</strong> will begin manufacturing <strong>Apollo</strong> robots, enabling them to contribute to their own production processes.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Apptronik</strong> was founded in 2016 as a laboratory at the University of Texas at Austin and signed an agreement with <strong>NASA</strong> in 2022 to develop humanoid robots. The company's first humanoid robot, <strong>Apollo</strong>, launched in August 2023. <strong>Apptronik</strong> CEO <strong>Jeff Cardenas</strong> has stated that a humanoid robot must be able to fit into any space where a human can fit and operate all the tools that humans use. This approach facilitates the integration of robots into a world designed for people.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This partnership marks <strong>Apptronik</strong>'s second initiative to integrate humanoid robots into factory environments. Following an agreement with <strong>Mercedes-Benz</strong> in March 2024, <strong>Apollo</strong> robots are planned to be tested on simple tasks at the company's production lines. Additionally, <strong>BMW</strong> successfully tested humanoid robots from <strong>Figure</strong>, a California-based robot manufacturer, at its Spartanburg facility in South Carolina in June 2024. In that trial, <strong>Figure 02</strong> robots successfully placed sheet metal components mounted as parts of a chassis or a vehicle's base frame during a multi-week pilot program.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The partnership between <strong>Apptronik</strong> and <strong>Jabil</strong> is seen as a significant step in integrating humanoid robots into manufacturing processes. These robots are expected to take on repetitive and simple tasks, allowing human workers to focus on more complex and creative work. If the tests succeed, <strong>Jabil</strong> will undertake production of <strong>Apollo</strong> robots, enabling them to contribute to their own manufacturing processes.</p>
