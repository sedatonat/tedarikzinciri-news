<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Mediterranean Shipping Co. (MSC)</strong> is pursuing a capacity reduction strategy on the Asia-North Europe route by replacing its megaships with smaller-tonnage vessels to ease pressure on freight rates, which have been trending downward since the start of the year, according to <strong>Alphaliner</strong> analysis.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This shift coincides with a period in which <strong>MSC</strong> is restructuring its operations as an independent carrier. Following the termination of its <strong>2M</strong> partnership with <strong>Maersk</strong>, <strong>MSC</strong> has entered into a new slot-sharing agreement with <strong>Ocean Network Express (ONE)</strong>, <strong>HMM</strong>, and <strong>Yang Ming Marine Transportation</strong> for Asia-Europe trade, covering nine services.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">These strategic moves are seen as part of <strong>MSC</strong>'s efforts in capacity management and adapting to market conditions. Fluctuations in the global container shipping market and excess supply are driving carriers to make more flexible and efficient operational decisions.</p>
<p><br></p>

<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>MSC</strong> is reducing capacity on the Asia-North Europe route by replacing megaships with smaller vessels.</p></li>
