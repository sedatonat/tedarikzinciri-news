<p style="text-align: justify">With consumer expectations rising and volatility persisting, retail and <strong>e-commerce</strong> brands are being forced to rethink speed, cost, data, and reliability when selecting last-mile partners. Several key considerations warrant attention: When reliability is factored into carrier selection, it has become the primary metric, displacing speed. Delivery leaders acknowledge that shoppers prefer consistency, even if it means a later delivery window. Missed <strong>SLA</strong>s disrupt forecasting, inflate costs, and silently erode brand credibility with customers. Brands are best served by using carriers with consistent pickup quality, tight line-haul monitoring, and reliable last-mile performance. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>UniUni</strong> operates a last-mile delivery network focused on <strong>Asian e-commerce</strong> (notably <strong>Shein</strong>, <strong>Temu</strong>, and <strong>TikTok Shop</strong>) in the <strong>U.S.</strong> and <strong>Canada</strong> markets, competing against <strong>FedEx</strong>, <strong>UPS</strong>, <strong>USPS</strong>, <strong>OnTrac</strong>, and <strong>LaserShip</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Tariff clarity matters more to brands than lower pricing. Because <strong>e-commerce</strong> brands often operate on thin margins, transparency and predictability in pricing are critical. Through automation, agile carriers can offer clear, consistent rates that enable brands to forecast accurately and protect margins. From a supply chain perspective, transparent presentation of <strong>parcel pricing</strong>, <strong>dimensional weight</strong> (<strong>DIM</strong>) calculations, <strong>fuel surcharge</strong>, <strong>residential delivery surcharge</strong>, <strong>peak season surcharge</strong>, <strong>oversize fee</strong>, <strong>address correction fee</strong>, and <strong>declared value</strong> is the problem that <strong>parcel auditing</strong> firms (<strong>71lbs</strong>, <strong>ShipMatrix</strong>, <strong>Reveel</strong>, <strong>Refund Retriever</strong>) are solving. <strong>GRI</strong> (<strong>General Rate Increase</strong>) is applied six to eight times annually by major carriers and typically ranges from 5 to 7 percent.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Scalable networks are the way to address demand spikes. <strong>E-commerce</strong> is subject to sudden demand surges—rapid sales jumps driven by viral social media posts or influencer promotions, for example. Traditional carriers are not equipped to handle demand spikes, which can result in capacity constraints, line hauls embargoes, or "no guarantee" service just as shopper expectations peak, leading to limited delivery service and additional charges. Diverse, hybrid carrier networks designed to flex in response to demand are gaining traction. From a supply chain perspective, a <strong>multi-carrier strategy</strong> requires routing intelligence across <strong>regional carrier</strong>s (<strong>UniUni</strong>, <strong>OnTrac</strong>, <strong>Pitney Bowes</strong>, <strong>X Delivery</strong>), <strong>USPS Priority Mail</strong>, <strong>UPS SurePost</strong>, <strong>FedEx SmartPost</strong>, and gig-economy-based last-mile providers (<strong>Roadie</strong>, <strong>DoorDash Drive</strong>, <strong>Veho</strong>).</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Delivery reliability depends on data integrity. As carrier networks are diversified, data quality and consistency become critical because each additional handoff creates a potential source of error. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>EDI</strong> (<strong>Electronic Data Interchange</strong>) standards—<strong>EDIFACT</strong> and <strong>ANSI X12</strong>—and modern API-based integrations are essential for tracking event consistency (<strong>EDI 214</strong>, <strong>EDI 990</strong>). <strong>Address validation</strong>, <strong>geocoding</strong>, <strong>delivery photo</strong>, <strong>POD</strong> (<strong>Proof of Delivery</strong>), and <strong>signature capture</strong> are core components of delivery documentation. Integration of <strong>OMS</strong> (<strong>Order Management System</strong>), <strong>WMS</strong>, <strong>TMS</strong>, and <strong>parcel platform</strong>s (<strong>ShipBob</strong>, <strong>ShipMonk</strong>, <strong>ShipStation</strong>, <strong>Shippo</strong>, <strong>EasyPost</strong>) ensures end-to-end data consistency. In summary, <strong>UniUni</strong>'s <strong>Top 5</strong> checklist clearly captures modern last-mile carrier selection criteria.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. Reliability outpaces speed in last-mile carrier selection.<br>
2. Tariff transparency is critical for thin-margin <strong>e-commerce</strong>.<br>
3. Scalable networks address viral demand spikes.<br>
4. Data integrity is mandatory across multiple handoffs.<br>
5. <strong>UniUni</strong> operates an <strong>Asian e-commerce</strong>-focused last-mile network.</p>
