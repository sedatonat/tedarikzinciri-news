<p>The Panama Maritime Authority (PMA) has accelerated the deregistration of six Panama-flagged vessels newly added to the United Kingdom's sanctions list. The PMA's decision is regarded as a significant step taken to shield itself from international sanctions and preserve the integrity of the Panama registry.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>The United Kingdom stands as the country applying the most extensive sanctions against shadow vessels linked to Russia. The Barbados Maritime Ship Registry, based in London, has emerged as the registry with the highest proportion of vessels subject to UK sanctions. The PMA highlighted that this registry has had more than 73 tankers targeted following the latest UK sanctions package. This development has occurred since UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer took office in early July.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Ramón Franco, Director General of Panama's Maritime Commerce Directorate, stated in a declaration that protecting the Panama registry from international sanctions and maintaining distance from vessels included on such lists is a fundamental responsibility of the state. Franco said, "Preserving the integrity of the Panama registry and cleansing it of international sanctions is one of our state's fundamental responsibilities."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>According to data obtained from Kpler, in November, Gabon, Liberia, and the Cook Islands flags removed the highest numbers of grey and sanctioned vessels from their registries. These countries, in contrast to Panama, complied with international regulations by removing sanctioned vessels from their records.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>As part of the United Kingdom's sanctions regime against Russia, the high number of vessels listed by the Barbados Maritime Ship Registry has intensified pressure on global maritime transportation, while Panama's firm stance against these vessels is being closely monitored by the international community. The PMA's swift action could serve as an example for other flag authorities and contribute to the effective enforcement of international sanctions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Panama's move is viewed as part of efforts to increase the Panama registry's compliance with international standards and transparency. Furthermore, it is regarded as an important step in strengthening Panama's role in global maritime trade while securing the confidence of the international community.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>In conclusion, the swift and resolute action taken by the Panama Maritime Authority against sanctioned vessels aims to protect the country's reputation in the maritime sector and contribute to the effective enforcement of international sanctions. This development reinforces Panama's strategic position in the international maritime and trade arena, while establishing an important reference point for other flag authorities facing similar situations.</p>
