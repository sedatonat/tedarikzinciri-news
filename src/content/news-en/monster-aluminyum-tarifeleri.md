<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Monster Beverage</strong> disclosed that it is experiencing increased production costs from elevated tariffs on imported aluminum but expects the impact to remain <strong>modest</strong>. CEO and President Hilton Schlosberg stated during an earnings call on November 6 that the company's largest cost pressure stems from <strong>aluminum used in can production</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The tariffs have driven a sharp increase, particularly in what is known as the <strong>Midwest premium</strong> — a regional markup added to aluminum's base price. The Midwest premium, which reflects factors such as energy, transportation, and trade disputes, is a critical pricing component in the U.S. domestic aluminum supply chain. In 2025, this premium has reached record levels.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Blake Hurtik of Argus Media, the primary driver of this increase is President Donald Trump's <strong>50% Section 232 aluminum import tariff</strong>, implemented over the course of the year. This tariff affects a large portion of aluminum entering the United States, triggering significant price increases in the domestic market.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Schlosberg said Monster is working to offset these cost increases through <strong>mitigation strategies</strong>:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Incorporating higher premiums into operational planning,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Diversifying suppliers,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Optimizing can costs,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Making price adjustments where necessary.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Monster implemented a price increase on November 1, but according to the CEO, this increase is <strong>not directly related to the tariffs</strong>, rather part of the company's broader revenue growth strategy.<br>
Schlosberg:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Our pricing strategy takes into account consumer behavior, brand momentum, channel and package mix."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Impacts Expected to Continue Through 2026</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Monster does not anticipate that tariffs will create <strong>"material" headwinds</strong> to operating results. However, impacts are expected to persist in a <strong>modest manner</strong> through the fourth quarter of 2025 and throughout 2026.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">While this suggests the company has a resilient business model, it indicates that the cost pressure created by tariffs still requires careful management.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tariffs Straining Other Food Producers</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Aluminum tariffs are not affecting Monster alone; most packaged food manufacturers are being pressured.<br>
For example:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Campbell's Company</strong> disclosed <strong>insufficient domestic supply</strong> of steel derivatives used in can production.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Campbell's indicated it can offset only <strong>60%</strong> of the tariff impact by fiscal 2026.<br>
CEO Mick Beekhuizen's comment captures the situation:</p></li>
</ul>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"There is not sufficient capacity in the United States. If there were, we would already be sourcing domestically."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">This example demonstrates that packaging metals supply is both <strong>insufficient</strong> and <strong>more sensitive to tariffs</strong>; consequently, prices are moving higher at an accelerated pace.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sector Takeaway: Indirect Costs of Tariffs Expanding</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Because aluminum cans are critical to the cost structure in the energy drink sector, tariffs are creating <strong>broader-than-expected impacts</strong> across the supply chain:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Price increases at can manufacturers</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Higher cost pressure on beverage brands</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Medium-term upward risk to end-consumer prices</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Need for more aggressive profit margin management strategies</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">While Monster's "modest impact" language points to the company's resilience, each increase in aluminum prices could affect competition in the global energy drink market over the long term.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Monster characterizes the impact of aluminum tariffs as <strong>"modest."</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The largest pressure stems from <strong>Midwest premium increases</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Tariff: <strong>50% Section 232 aluminum duty</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Monster is implementing mitigation measures, but impacts are expected to <strong>continue through 2026</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Other producers like Campbell's are also facing supply constraints and cost pressure.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Aluminum tariffs represent a risk that is <strong>rapidly expanding indirect costs</strong> in the beverage sector.</p></li>
</ul>
