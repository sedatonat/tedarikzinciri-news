<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>British International Investment</strong>, the U.K. government-owned development finance institution with a portfolio of 9.9 billion pounds ($13.6 billion), has suspended its investments with port operator <strong>DP World</strong> following allegations of connections between the company's <strong>chief executive officer</strong> and disgraced financier <strong>Jeffrey Epstein</strong>. The decision underscores how critical <strong>ESG</strong> and governance considerations have become as investment filters in the global shipping ecosystem. In a statement to <strong>Bloomberg News</strong>, a <strong>BII</strong> spokesperson said, "We are shocked by the allegations emerging in the Epstein files regarding Sultan Ahmed bin Sulayem," and added that no new investments would proceed until the company takes necessary action.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>BII</strong>, fully reporting to the U.K.'s <strong>Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office</strong>, has become the second partner to publicly suspend investments with <strong>DP World</strong> over <strong>bin Sulayem</strong>'s alleged connections to <strong>Epstein</strong> in less than 24 hours. On February 10, <strong>Canadian</strong> pension fund <strong>Caisse de Depot et Placement du Quebec (CDPQ)</strong> announced it was suspending future investment plans with <strong>DP World</strong> while serving as a major financial partner in multiple projects for the company. These two moves have significantly hardened the pressure that global development finance and pension capital pools are exerting on the port operator in a single-day cycle. From a supply chain perspective, these withdrawals signal that <strong>DP World</strong> will have to revisit its expansion planning across Africa and Asia in the coming years.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The moves follow the publication of emails by the <strong>U.S. Department of Justice</strong> and other sources, which <strong>Bloomberg News</strong> obtained last summer. The emails show that <strong>DP World</strong>'s <strong>CEO</strong> corresponded with <strong>Epstein</strong> for over a decade following the financier's 2008 conviction on various charges, including trafficking of minors for sexual exploitation. These records make it far more difficult for financial partners subject to corporate governance standards to maintain their relationships. <strong>BII</strong>, as an impact investing institution, assesses investment opportunities based on partners' governance practices and lists gender equality and business integrity among its priorities on its website.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In 2021, <strong>BII</strong>—then known as <strong>CDC Group</strong>—announced its intention to invest alongside <strong>DP World</strong> in an <strong>African platform</strong> beginning with ports in <strong>Senegal</strong>, <strong>Egypt</strong>, and <strong>Somaliland</strong>. The fund initially committed $320 million and planned to deploy an additional $400 million in investments over several years. <strong>DP World</strong> was to transfer its stakes in the three ports to this structure and was expected to invest a further $1 billion. The current suspension decision rapidly halts <strong>BII</strong> from channeling additional capital into an African port platform of this scale. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>DP World</strong>'s growth plans for its portfolio of ports in East and West Africa—including <strong>Doraleh</strong>, <strong>Berbera</strong>, and <strong>Dakar</strong>—will be redesigned over time due to the withdrawal of corporate capital partners. Ultimately, the <strong>Epstein</strong> files stand as a significant case demonstrating the power of <strong>ESG</strong> and governance filtration in the global port value chain.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>Key Points:</strong><br>
1. <strong>BII</strong> suspends new investments with <strong>DP World</strong>.<br>
2. <strong>CDPQ</strong> announced one day earlier that it was halting future investments.<br>
3. The allegations are based on correspondence between <strong>DP World CEO Sultan Ahmed bin Sulayem</strong> and <strong>Epstein</strong>.<br>
4. The <strong>African platform</strong> jointly created by <strong>BII</strong> and <strong>DP World</strong> covers ports in <strong>Senegal</strong>, <strong>Egypt</strong>, and <strong>Somaliland</strong>.<br>
5. <strong>BII</strong> identifies business integrity and gender equality as impact investment priorities.</p>
