<p style="text-align: justify">APM Terminals has secured an eight-year extension of its Valencia concession to 2049 after committing more than €10m in upgrades. The approval takes the concession to the 50-year statutory maximum under Spanish port law, completing a tenure that began in 1999 and has been extended sequentially.</p>

<p style="text-align: justify">The latest extension is tied to additional investments exceeding 20% of the original concession commitment, meeting the legal threshold for a substantial modification. The main project is the electrification of the Levante quay through installation of the terminal's first Onshore Power Supply system, part of a four-unit rollout planned in Valencia.</p>

<p style="text-align: justify">The first phase includes a power delivery centre and the transformation and conversion substation required for shore power operations, in line with European port decarbonisation targets for 2030 and APM Terminals' target to reduce emissions from its operations by 70% by 2030 and reach net zero by 2040.</p>

<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. APM Terminals' Valencia concession is extended to 2049.<br>
2. The extension is conditioned on over €10m in additional investment.<br>
3. The Levante quay will be electrified with a shore power system.<br>
4. A total of four shore power units are planned in Valencia.<br>
5. APM Terminals targets 70% emissions cut by 2030 and net zero by 2040.</p>
