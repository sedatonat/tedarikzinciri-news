<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain Podcast</strong>'s bonus episode features <strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> Editor-in-Chief <strong>Bob Bowman</strong> in conversation with <strong>Bloodhound Tracking Device, Inc.</strong> CEO <strong>Curtis Spencer</strong> about cargo theft tracking and security trends. The conversation was recorded at the 2025 <strong>Intermodal Expo</strong> organized by <strong>IANA</strong> (<strong>Intermodal Association of North America</strong>) in <strong>Long Beach</strong>. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Bloodhound Tracking Device, Inc.</strong> is a U.S.-based provider of covert cargo tracking devices and security solutions &mdash; among its core products are battery-powered <strong>GPS</strong>-based trackers, covert devices installed at the container, trailer, and pallet level, and real-time alert systems. <strong>Curtis Spencer</strong> is CEO of <strong>Bloodhound</strong>. <strong>Robert J. Bowman</strong> (<strong>Bob Bowman</strong>) is Editor-in-Chief of <strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> &mdash; one of the industry's most experienced editors. <strong>Intermodal Association of North America</strong> (<strong>IANA</strong>) is the leading U.S. rail intermodal industry organization &mdash; it provides a platform among intermodal transportation companies, Class I railroad players, intermodal marketing companies (<strong>IMC</strong>), chassis providers, and driver organizations. <strong>Joni Casey</strong> is President &amp; CEO of <strong>IANA</strong> &mdash; serving in a long-standing capacity. <strong>Intermodal Expo</strong> is <strong>IANA</strong>'s flagship annual conference &mdash; bringing together the U.S. rail/truck intermodal sector.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the U.S. cargo theft crisis has reached record levels in the 2024-2025 period. <strong>CargoNet</strong> (<strong>Verisk</strong>) reported 3,798 cargo theft incidents in the U.S. and Canada in 2024 &mdash; a 26% year-over-year increase, with stolen goods valued at over $455 million. <strong>Strategic theft</strong> (<strong>fictitious pickup</strong>, <strong>identity theft</strong>, <strong>double-brokering</strong>) has become the leading category of cargo theft cases in 2024. <strong>Real-time GPS tracking</strong>, <strong>geofencing</strong>, <strong>tamper detection</strong>, <strong>covert tracking devices</strong>, <strong>seal integrity monitoring</strong>, and <strong>door sensor</strong> are primary security technologies. Key tracking and visibility platforms include <strong>BloodHound Tracking</strong>, <strong>Spireon</strong> (<strong>Solera</strong>), <strong>Skybitz</strong> (<strong>Telular</strong>), <strong>Samsara</strong>, <strong>Geotab</strong>, <strong>Verizon Connect</strong>, <strong>Trimble</strong>, <strong>ORBCOMM</strong>, <strong>Sensata Technologies</strong>, <strong>Phillips Connect</strong>, <strong>Tive</strong>, <strong>Roambee</strong>, <strong>Project44</strong>, <strong>FourKites</strong>, <strong>Overhaul</strong>, <strong>Sensitech</strong>, <strong>Controlant</strong>, and <strong>OnAsset Intelligence</strong>. <strong>RFID</strong> (<strong>Radio Frequency Identification</strong>), <strong>BLE</strong> (<strong>Bluetooth Low Energy</strong>), <strong>LoRaWAN</strong>, <strong>NB-IoT</strong>, <strong>LTE-M</strong>, <strong>5G</strong>, and satellite (<strong>Iridium</strong>, <strong>Globalstar</strong>, <strong>Inmarsat</strong>, <strong>Starlink</strong>) are primary <strong>IoT</strong> connectivity protocols.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the U.S. intermodal transportation ecosystem's leading Class I rail carriers include <strong>BNSF Railway</strong> (<strong>Berkshire Hathaway</strong>), <strong>Union Pacific</strong>, <strong>CSX Transportation</strong>, <strong>Norfolk Southern</strong>, <strong>Canadian National</strong> (<strong>CN</strong>), <strong>Canadian Pacific Kansas City</strong> (<strong>CPKC</strong>), and <strong>Kansas City Southern de Mexico</strong> (<strong>CPKC</strong> subsidiary). Key <strong>IMC</strong> (<strong>Intermodal Marketing Company</strong>) players include <strong>Hub Group</strong>, <strong>J.B. Hunt Intermodal</strong>, <strong>Schneider Intermodal</strong>, <strong>STG Logistics</strong>, <strong>NFI Intermodal</strong>, <strong>RoadOne IntermodaLogistics</strong>, <strong>BNSF Logistics</strong>, <strong>UP Distribution Services</strong>, <strong>Knichel Logistics</strong>, <strong>Mode Global</strong>, and <strong>Pacer International</strong> (merging with <strong>XPO</strong>). Leading chassis pool providers include <strong>TRAC Intermodal</strong>, <strong>DCLI</strong> (<strong>Direct ChassisLink Inc</strong>), <strong>FlexiVan</strong>, <strong>Milestone Equipment Holdings</strong>, and <strong>SeaCastle</strong>. <strong>UIIA</strong> (<strong>Uniform Intermodal Interchange Agreement</strong>) is the standard contract for intermodal equipment interchange. Major U.S. intermodal hubs include <strong>Chicago</strong> (<strong>BNSF Logistics Park Chicago Joliet</strong>, <strong>UP Global IV Joliet</strong>, <strong>CSX Bedford Park</strong>, <strong>Norfolk Southern Landers</strong>), <strong>Memphis</strong>, <strong>Dallas-Fort Worth</strong>, <strong>Kansas City</strong>, <strong>Atlanta</strong>, <strong>Charleston</strong>, <strong>Savannah</strong>, <strong>Columbus Ohio</strong> (<strong>Rickenbacker</strong>), <strong>Los Angeles-Long Beach</strong>, <strong>Oakland</strong>, and <strong>Seattle-Tacoma</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the global <strong>cargo theft</strong> ecosystem includes key industry organizations such as <strong>BSI Supply Chain</strong>, <strong>TT Club</strong> (<strong>through transport mutual</strong>), <strong>FreightWatch International</strong> (<strong>SensiGuard</strong>), <strong>National Insurance Crime Bureau</strong> (<strong>NICB</strong>), <strong>Transported Asset Protection Association</strong> (<strong>TAPA</strong>), <strong>National Cargo Theft Task Force</strong> (<strong>NCTTF</strong>), and <strong>FBI Cargo Theft Task Force</strong>. <strong>TAPA FSR</strong> (<strong>Facility Security Requirements</strong>), <strong>TSR</strong> (<strong>Trucking Security Requirements</strong>), and <strong>PSR</strong> (<strong>Parking Security Requirements</strong>) are the most widely adopted global security standards. <strong>C-TPAT</strong> (<strong>Customs-Trade Partnership Against Terrorism</strong>), <strong>AEO</strong> (<strong>Authorized Economic Operator</strong>), <strong>FAST</strong> (<strong>Free and Secure Trade</strong>), and <strong>SAFE Framework of Standards</strong> (<strong>WCO</strong>; <strong>World Customs Organization</strong>) are primary security and compliance programs. <strong>Cargo theft hot spots</strong> in the U.S. include <strong>California</strong> (particularly <strong>San Bernardino</strong> and <strong>Los Angeles County</strong>), <strong>Texas</strong>, <strong>Florida</strong>, <strong>Illinois</strong>, <strong>Georgia</strong>, and <strong>New Jersey</strong> as primary risk areas &mdash; globally, <strong>Mexico</strong>, <strong>Brazil</strong>, <strong>South Africa</strong>, <strong>India</strong>, <strong>China</strong>, and <strong>Russia</strong> rank among the highest-risk countries. In conclusion, the conversation between <strong>Curtis Spencer</strong> and <strong>Bowman</strong> reflects a concrete manifestation of cargo security rising as an industry priority in intermodal operations.</p>
