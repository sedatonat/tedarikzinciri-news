<p style="text-align: justify">Bulgaria's Transport and Communications Ministry announced that the tanker <strong>Kairos</strong> has been stabilized off the coast of Ahtopol, though comprehensive rescue operations remain underway due to poor weather conditions. The tanker drifted for <strong>several days</strong> after departing Turkish territorial waters before being detected by Bulgarian national surveillance systems, triggering emergency response teams to the area.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the ministry's statement, after an unidentified vessel that was not responding to radio calls was detected, contact was established with the coordination center in Ankara, and the vessel was confirmed to be <strong>Kairos</strong>. A Bulgarian <strong>Border Police</strong> boat and <strong>Navy helicopter</strong> were dispatched to the vessel, and surveillance operations were initiated with maritime, air, and ground assets.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ten crew members remain aboard</strong>, with good health status and adequate food and water supplies. The fact that Kairos is <strong>not carrying cargo</strong> significantly reduces the environmental risk level. Bulgarian authorities reported that no <strong>oil spill</strong> or environmental threat has been observed.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, the most significant factor hampering rescue operations is <strong>winds exceeding 15 m/s</strong> and <strong>sea conditions at Beaufort scale levels 4–5</strong> in the area. For this reason, crew evacuation and vessel towing are currently not feasible. Bulgarian authorities stated that <strong>crew evacuation</strong> and <strong>towing preparation</strong> will proceed as soon as weather conditions improve.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Turkey's Role in the Incident: Explosion and Earlier Intervention</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The initial critical incident involving Kairos occurred <strong>on November 28 in the northern Istanbul Strait</strong>. Turkey's Transport and Infrastructure Ministry announced that an <strong>explosion</strong> occurred on the vessel and that <strong>Coast Guard</strong> teams intervened and extinguished the fire. At that time, <strong>25 crew members were evacuated</strong>, and the vessel's status was recorded as "external impact," triggering technical and legal investigations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">While the cause of the explosion has yet to be determined, it is understood that the vessel subsequently drifted northward in an uncontrolled manner following the incident. The vessel's movement from Turkish territorial waters toward Bulgarian waters has once again underscored the importance of maritime safety coordination between the two nations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Regional Security and Risk Assessment</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">In the final quarter of 2025, increasing security incidents in the Black Sea and Marmara region, particularly <strong>shadow fleet</strong> activities, tanker accidents, and the emergence of unmanned systems (such as <strong>Sea Baby drones</strong>), have become increasingly complex. The Kairos incident beginning with claims of "external impact explosion" signals that the risk matrix in the region has expanded.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Bulgarian authorities note that because Kairos is not carrying oil, the likelihood of an environmental disaster is low; however, they warn of the risk that the vessel could drift uncontrollably due to poor weather conditions. For this reason, the <strong>Maritime Administration</strong>, <strong>Border Police</strong>, <strong>Bulgarian Navy</strong>, and <strong>Burgas Province</strong> units have established a joint <strong>crisis-management</strong> structure.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Current Status</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Kairos remains <strong>at anchor</strong> off Ahtopol and is in a stabilized condition. The next phase of the operation is the safe evacuation of crew members and towing of the vessel. Both Bulgarian and Turkish authorities are continuing technical and legal investigations and have confirmed that the vessel does not pose an environmental risk.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The Kairos tanker was detected off Ahtopol after drifting for several days.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The vessel did not respond to radio calls; identification was confirmed through coordination with Turkey.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ten crew members</strong> remain aboard; their health status is good.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">As it is not carrying cargo, environmental risk is low; no <strong>oil spill</strong> has been observed.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Weather conditions (15+ m/s, Beaufort 4–5) are delaying evacuation and towing.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Following the November 28 explosion, Turkey evacuated 25 people; "external impact" is under investigation.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Bulgarian authorities are monitoring the operation with maritime, air, and ground assets.</p></li>
</ul>
