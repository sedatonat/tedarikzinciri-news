<p style="text-align: justify">DNV and Menon Economics have introduced a new index alongside their long-running <em>Leading Maritime Cities</em> index, this time ranking the world's <strong>leading container ports</strong>: the <strong>Leading Container Ports of the World (LCP)</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the initial results:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>1. Singapore</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>2. Shanghai</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>3. Ningbo-Zhoushan</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>4. Rotterdam</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>5. Busan</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">The LCP evaluates a total of <strong>160 ports</strong> across <strong>35 objective indicators</strong> including efficiency, connectivity, sustainability, alternative fuel infrastructure, capacity expansion, and customer value.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Dr. Erik Jakobsen from Menon Economics noted that "the most successful ports both expand capacity and integrate automation and digitalization at every point of operation," emphasizing that investments in <strong>clean energy and integrated hinterland connections</strong> notably enhance performance.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The report also identified regional leaders:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>North America:</strong> New York &amp; New Jersey</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Europe:</strong> Hamburg</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Africa:</strong> Tanger Med</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Middle East:</strong> Jebel Ali</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Oceania:</strong> Sydney</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new LCP index will compete with the <strong>Container Port Performance Index (CPPI)</strong>, currently published by the World Bank and S&amp;P Global. Shanghai ranked first in the CPPI's latest report, while Singapore failed to crack the top 10. The new LCP methodology places greater emphasis on ports' <strong>broader impact and transformational capacity</strong>.</p>
