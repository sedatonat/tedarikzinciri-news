<p>Gartner has identified eight critical trends to watch in supply chain technology. These technological trends are rapidly evolving, with most expected to mature within the next two to five years.&nbsp;</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Gartner warns that organizations failing to keep these trends on their radar risk falling behind. Here are some of these trends:</p>\n<ol>\n  <li><p><strong>Machine customers</strong>: Defined by automated purchases made by internet-connected devices or algorithms. For example, a printer that orders its own ink when ink levels run low.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>End-to-end sustainable supply chain solutions</strong>: Many supply chain solutions such as transportation, planning, and procurement are integrating sustainability data into decision-making processes.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Augmented connected workforce</strong>: Wearable or mobile devices that support information transfer and training.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Next-generation humanoid robots</strong>: Robots that look and move like humans are being used for tasks such as order picking in warehouses.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>AI-powered vision systems</strong>: Cameras, computer vision, and artificial intelligence use pattern recognition to detect issues and security problems in real time.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Composite AI</strong>: Companies must consider the human element in their AI strategies.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Supply chain data management</strong>: As advanced analytics and AI become more prevalent, having strict policies and processes in place to maintain high data quality is critical.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Cyber ransom</strong>: The impact of ransomware attacks can be paralyzing. According to Gartner research, two-thirds of enterprises worldwide have been affected by ransomware attacks, with 84% of those affected reporting significant revenue losses.</p></li>\n</ol>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Gartner notes there is unprecedented consensus among business leaders on the strategic importance of implementing digital technologies. According to research, 78% of CEOs plan to increase investment in digital capabilities in 2024, while 82% of supply chain leaders indicate they will invest more in supply chain technology in 2024–2025.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5>Key Takeaways</h5>\n<ul>\n  <li><p>Eight major supply chain technology trends identified; expected to mature within 2-5 years ahead.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The strategic importance of investment in digital technologies is widely accepted among business leaders.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Cyber ransom attacks cause significant financial losses for organizations.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>News Link:</strong> <a href="https://www.thescxchange.com/articles/10247-gartner-spotlights-eight-supply-chain-technology-trends"><u>https://www.thescxchange.com/articles/10247-gartner-spotlights-eight-supply-chain-technology-trends</u></a></p>
