<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Pacific International Lines (PIL)</strong> has put into service its <strong>fourth LNG dual-fuel "O" class container vessel</strong> as part of its environmentally conscious fleet modernization program. The <strong>Kota Orkid</strong>, which has a capacity of 8,200 TEU, was named after <strong>Singapore's national flower, the orchid</strong>, and symbolizes <strong>resilience, elegance, and progress</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Naming ceremony</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">At a ceremony held at <strong>Yangzijiang Shipbuilding Group's shipyard in Jiangsu, China</strong>, the vessel was named by <strong>Annie Yap, wife of PIL Board Member Yap Chee Keong</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Kota Orkid will operate alongside three sister "O" class vessels previously put into service by PIL — <strong>Kota Oasis, Kota Ocean, and Kota Odyssey</strong> — on the <strong>South West Africa Service (SWS)</strong> route. This service <strong>strengthens maritime trade connectivity between Asia and West Africa</strong> and <strong>enhances PIL's cargo capacity and service flexibility</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A significant step in fleet renewal strategy</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Kota Orkid marks PIL's <strong>eighth LNG-fueled vessel</strong> delivered to date. Since 2024, the company has added four 14,000 TEU LNG dual-fuel vessels and four 8,200 TEU "O" class vessels to its fleet.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">PIL also has <strong>12 new LNG dual-fuel vessels on order</strong>:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Five vessels of 13,000 TEU</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Seven vessels of 9,000 TEU</strong>,<br>
to be delivered in the coming years.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Environmentally friendly technologies</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Kota Orkid and her sister vessels are equipped with <strong>next-generation low-methane-emission engines</strong> and <strong>biomethane-compatible systems</strong>. These engines <strong>improve fuel efficiency while minimizing environmental impact</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, the vessels feature:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Hydrodynamic hull designs</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Advanced anti-fouling coatings</strong>,<br>
which deliver <strong>significant fuel consumption savings and speed optimization</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">These features form the technical foundation of PIL's <strong>vision for "transition to green shipping"</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Statements from PIL management</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">PIL Executive Chairman <strong>S.S. Teo</strong>, speaking at the ceremony, assessed the company's <strong>fleet renewal strategy</strong> as follows:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Adding our eighth new vessel to the fleet in the past year is a significant step in our journey of growth and environmental transformation. As we expand our LNG-fueled fleet, we are investing not only in capacity and technology, but also in a more sustainable future."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">PIL CEO <strong>Lars Kastrup</strong> commented:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Kota Orkid demonstrates that our fleet is becoming cleaner, smarter, and more efficient. Through advanced technologies, digital systems, and environmentally friendly fuels, we ensure that PIL remains sustainable, flexible, and innovative."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>PIL's growth in African and Asian operations</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">PIL continues to expand, particularly on <strong>West African and Indian Ocean</strong> routes. The company announced in September that it had established a new agency called <strong>PIL (KENYA) LIMITED</strong> for <strong>Mariana Express Lines (MELL) in Kenya</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">These steps are part of PIL's <strong>strategy to deepen its presence in the African market and strengthen regional connectivity</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Green fleet transformation accelerating</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">PIL's LNG investments have established the company as <strong>one of Asia's fastest-growing environmentally conscious maritime shipping operators</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
With the completion of its vessel orders, PIL's fleet share of <strong>LNG and low-carbon fuel vessels will approach 30%</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">With Kota Orkid entering service, PIL not only achieves <strong>fuel savings and emission reductions</strong>, but also marks significant progress in its transition to a fleet that complies with <strong>international maritime emission standards (IMO 2030/2050 targets)</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Kota Orkid</strong> is PIL's <strong>fourth "O" class LNG dual-fuel vessel</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The vessel will operate on the <strong>Asia–West Africa route (SWS Service)</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">PIL currently has <strong>8 LNG vessels</strong> in its fleet; <strong>12 additional vessels are on order</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The vessels are equipped with <strong>engines that reduce methane emissions</strong> and <strong>biomethane-compatible systems</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Hydrodynamic design</strong> and <strong>anti-fouling coatings</strong> enhance fuel efficiency.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>S.S. Teo:</strong> "Our LNG investments form the foundation of sustainable and reliable shipping."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>PIL Kenya Limited</strong> represents the latest milestone in the company's <strong>African expansion strategy</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
