<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Port of Gothenburg</strong> has awarded a contract to <strong>Boskalis Sweden AB</strong> for blasting and dredging works under the <strong>Skandia Gateway project</strong>, a major infrastructure programme aimed at expanding capacity for larger vessels. The contract is valued at approximately <strong>SEK 202 million ($22 million)</strong>. The works will deepen the fairway into the port from the current 13.5 metres to a maximum of 17.5 metres, enabling larger and deeper-draught vessels to call at <strong>Scandinavia's largest port</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Boskalis Sweden AB was selected following a competitive tender involving five bidders. <strong>Jan Andersson</strong>, Project Manager for Skandia Gateway at the Port of Gothenburg, said: "This is a highly competent contractor with solid experience in projects of a similar nature." Since spring 2024, the port has already completed quay reinforcement works at its container terminal, forming a prerequisite for the upcoming dredging and blasting phase.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Skandia Gateway project targets the capacity of the Port of Gothenburg, which <strong>handles half of Sweden's economy</strong>, to host <strong>Ultra Large Container Vessels (ULCV)</strong> calling directly from Asia to Northern Europe. Boskalis' experience from previous large-scale dredging projects in Northern European waters proved decisive in the technically challenging tender, particularly given the need for drill-and-blast operations on the seabed rock layer.</p>
