<p>India and Iran have signed a 10-year agreement to develop and manage Chabahar Port. By bypassing Pakistan's Karachi and Gwadar ports, India is building Chabahar Port on Iran's southeastern coast along the Gulf of Oman, from which it ships goods to Iran, Afghanistan, and Central Asian nations.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>However, according to Reuters, development of the port has slowed in recent years due to U.S. sanctions imposed on Iran. The slowdown has continued even after India took over port operations at the end of 2018.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>According to BBC News, during a press briefing on May 14, U.S. State Department Deputy Spokesperson Vedant Patel stated that U.S. sanctions against Iran remain in force and will be enforced. Patel said, "Any institution or individual considering doing business with Iran should be aware of the potential risks and sanctions exposure they could face."</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>The agreement was signed on May 13 between India Ports Global Limited (IPGL) and Iran's Ports and Maritime Organization. According to Reuters, IPGL will invest approximately $120 million under the agreement and provide an additional $250 million in financing, bringing the total value of the contract to $370 million.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5>Key Points</h5>\n<ul>\n  <li><p>A 10-year agreement was signed between India and Iran.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The agreement involves developing and managing Chabahar Port.</p></li>\n  <li><p>U.S. sanctions have slowed port development.</p></li>\n  <li><p>IPGL will make a total investment of $370 million.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The agreement was reached despite U.S. sanctions imposed on Iran.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><br></p>\n
