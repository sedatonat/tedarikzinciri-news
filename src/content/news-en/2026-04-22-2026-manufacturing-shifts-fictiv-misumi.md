<p style="text-align: justify">Date: April 22, 2026; Time: 1:00 p.m. ET; Live Webinar: 1 hour. This webinar presents a high-level review of the <strong>2026 State of Manufacturing &amp; Supply Chain</strong> report, distilling insights from 300+ industry leaders into clear, actionable themes. The session examines how <strong>AI</strong> is becoming foundational to manufacturing operations, how digital platforms are shifting from optional tools to essential infrastructure, and how leading organizations are redesigning their supply chains to address sourcing complexity, quality demands, and external volatility. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Fictiv</strong>, as a <strong>cloud manufacturing</strong> platform, offers <strong>CNC machining</strong>, <strong>3D printing</strong>, <strong>injection molding</strong>, <strong>urethane casting</strong>, and <strong>sheet metal</strong> manufacturing services through a global network of suppliers. <strong>MISUMI</strong>, a Japanese-origin provider of <strong>industrial components</strong> and <strong>automation parts</strong>, acquired <strong>Fictiv</strong> for $350 million in 2025.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Participants gain perspective on the major forces shaping 2026 — including regional flexibility strategies, tariff and geopolitical pressures, sustainability expectations, and emerging compliance standards — and learn what these trends mean for engineers, procurement teams, and supply chain leaders. The session connects data with real-world implications, helping organizations benchmark their strategies and determine where to focus next to remain competitive. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>State of Manufacturing</strong> reports are also published by <strong>Deloitte</strong>, <strong>NAM</strong> (<strong>National Association of Manufacturers</strong>), <strong>ASME</strong>, <strong>IndustryWeek</strong>, and <strong>SME</strong> (<strong>Society of Manufacturing Engineers</strong>). 2026 trends concentrate on <strong>AI/ML adoption</strong>, <strong>digital thread</strong>, <strong>connected factory</strong>, <strong>cybersecurity</strong>, <strong>workforce reskilling</strong>, and <strong>scope 3 emissions</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Speakers: <strong>Dave Evans</strong>, President and <strong>CEO</strong> | <strong>CEO</strong> and Founding Partner, <strong>MISUMI Americas</strong> | <strong>Fictiv</strong>; <strong>Nate Evans</strong>, <strong>AI</strong> President | Founding Partner and <strong>CXO</strong>, <strong>MISUMI</strong> | <strong>Fictiv</strong>; <strong>Sankara Narayanan</strong>, Industry Director, <strong>Frost &amp; Sullivan</strong>; and moderator <strong>Nick Bowman</strong>, Senior Editor, <strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong>. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Frost &amp; Sullivan</strong> is positioned alongside <strong>Gartner</strong>, <strong>IDC</strong>, <strong>Forrester</strong>, <strong>ABI Research</strong>, and <strong>IoT Analytics</strong> as a major player in technology research and consulting. <strong>Industrial IoT</strong>, <strong>predictive maintenance</strong>, <strong>computer vision</strong>, <strong>robotic process automation</strong> (<strong>RPA</strong>), and <strong>generative design</strong> represent the dominant technology investments in modern manufacturing.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>distributed manufacturing</strong> models enhance flexibility through regional supplier networks rather than a single global supplier. <strong>On-demand manufacturing</strong> is a platform market shaped by <strong>Fictiv</strong>, <strong>Xometry</strong>, <strong>Hubs</strong> (formerly <strong>3D Hubs</strong>), <strong>Protolabs</strong>, and <strong>Fast Radius</strong> (now closed). <strong>Quality assurance</strong>, <strong>dimensional inspection</strong>, <strong>CMM</strong> (<strong>Coordinate Measuring Machine</strong>), <strong>X-ray inspection</strong>, <strong>vision system</strong>, and <strong>SPC</strong> (<strong>Statistical Process Control</strong>) applications are fundamental tools of manufacturing quality. <strong>Tariff engineering</strong>, <strong>USMCA preferential treatment</strong>, <strong>FTZ</strong> (<strong>Foreign Trade Zone</strong>), and <strong>duty drawback</strong> are critical elements of the modern manufacturing economy. In conclusion, the <strong>Fictiv</strong>/<strong>MISUMI</strong> joint webinar promises to clearly summarize the multidimensional challenges of the 2026 manufacturing landscape.</p>
