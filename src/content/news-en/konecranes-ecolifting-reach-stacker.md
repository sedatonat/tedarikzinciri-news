<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Konecranes</strong> has launched the company's first <strong>electric reach stacker</strong> model at the <strong>TOC Asia</strong> event in Singapore, marking a significant transformation in its port and industrial equipment portfolio. The launch comes as global port operators accelerate their demand for electric equipment, while the company also announced that its <strong>Remote Services</strong> platform is now accessible worldwide. These two developments extend Konecranes' <strong>Ecolifting</strong> initiative to a broader range of solutions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new <strong>electric reach stacker</strong> will initially be offered in the <strong>APAC</strong>, <strong>Middle East &amp; Africa</strong>, and <strong>South America</strong> markets. The equipment operates with high-performance <strong>electric drivetrain</strong> and advanced <strong>Li-Ion battery</strong> technology. With up to <strong>16 hours</strong> of operating time per charge, the machine is now suitable not only for short shifts but also for intensive terminal operations. The company notes that the electric powertrain does not compromise container handling-and-transport capacity; instead, it delivers improved operational efficiency through reduced noise and lower maintenance costs.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Andreas Falk</strong>, SVP of Konecranes Lift Trucks, underscores the company's commitment to electrification, emphasizing that it has achieved a comprehensive electric vehicle portfolio capable of meeting all operational needs across diverse sectors—from ports to steel mills. The electric reach stacker complements the company's Ecolifting approach alongside previously introduced solutions such as the <strong>electric empty container handler</strong>, <strong>E-Hybrid RTG</strong>, and <strong>Noell Hydrogen Fuel Cell Straddle Carrier</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Konecranes' <strong>Remote Services</strong> platform, now accessible globally, is a technical support system designed to accelerate problem resolution and reduce equipment downtime for operators. The platform delivers instant <strong>troubleshooting</strong> assistance at first contact, then guides on-site technical teams with precision-targeted diagnostics to streamline the resolution process. The company reports that Remote Services has already delivered operational efficiency gains at terminals in Southeast Asia and Europe, enabling customers to employ a hybrid support model that addresses failures without waiting for on-site technicians.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This technological expansion parallels innovations introduced throughout 2025. During the year, Konecranes has brought to market equipment including <strong>hydrogen fuel cell</strong>-based straddle carriers, <strong>E-Hybrid RTG</strong> systems, and electric empty container handlers. These products provide alternatives for port and industrial customers seeking to build low-emission or zero tailpipe emission fleets. <strong>Ecolifting</strong> encompasses not only fully electric solutions but also <strong>eco-optimized diesel drives</strong>, more efficient power management systems, and maintenance optimization—all aimed at reducing carbon footprint.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Konecranes operates as a global material handling company headquartered in Finland and listed on <strong>Nasdaq Helsinki (KCR)</strong>. The company serves a broad customer base ranging from ports to heavy industrial facilities through its design, manufacturing, maintenance, and modernization capabilities. In 2024, group sales reached <strong>EUR 4.2 billion</strong>. Its global workforce and regional service centers form the foundational infrastructure supporting the global expansion of Remote Services.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Overall, the launch of the electric reach stacker and the globalization of Remote Services reinforce Konecranes' leadership in sustainable material handling technologies while expanding its low-emission product range under the Ecolifting umbrella. These steps align with accelerating global trends toward port equipment electrification and are expected to strengthen the company's competitive position.</p>
<p><br></p>
