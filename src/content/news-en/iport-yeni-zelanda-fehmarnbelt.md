<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>iPort Zealand</strong>, developed within the boundaries of Ringsted Municipality in Denmark, will stand at the centre of a massive supply chain transformation that Northern Europe will experience after 2029. Spearheaded by <strong>Verdion</strong> and envisioning a total investment of <strong>€1 billion</strong>, the project aims to radically reshape Europe's northern logistics map upon the opening of the Fehmarnbelt Fixed Link.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Built across a <strong>250-hectare</strong> vast area, the project will create <strong>570,000 m²</strong> of logistics, manufacturing, and advanced industrial space. One of the most critical elements of iPort Zealand is the <strong>intermodal rail terminal</strong> that will be housed within the facility. This terminal will have the capacity to handle <strong>750-metre-long freight trains</strong>. According to the developer's calculations, when operating at full capacity, the terminal will remove <strong>approximately 720 long-distance trucks daily</strong> from motorways. This presents a modal shift strategy fully aligned with Denmark's <strong>green transition</strong> objectives.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic Location: Ringsted Logistics Intersection</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Ringsted sits at the exact intersection of Denmark's <strong>north–south</strong> and <strong>east–west</strong> rail corridors. Its proximity to the <strong>E20 motorway</strong> (the main highway connecting Western Europe to Sweden) offers the project unprecedented accessibility from both road and rail perspectives. Verdion CEO <strong>Michael Hughes</strong> emphasises the importance of the location as follows:</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>"iPort Zealand is set to become a major new cluster for Danish business in an unparalleled strategic location."</strong></p>
<p><br>
According to Hughes, when the Fehmarnbelt Tunnel opens, new logistics flows will emerge between all Nordic countries and Germany, and the project's commercial potential will grow exponentially.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Fehmarnbelt Tunnel: A New Northern Europe in 2029</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">When completed, the Fehmarnbelt Fixed Link will be the world's largest immersed tunnel at <strong>18 km long</strong>. Its opening is expected in <strong>2029</strong>. Thanks to the tunnel:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Hamburg–Copenhagen rail journeys will be <strong>2 hours shorter</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Freight trains will be able to <strong>bypass</strong> the circuitous route over the Great Belt (Storebælt),</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Trade flows between Denmark and Germany will become <strong>significantly faster and more cost-efficient</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This new corridor will strengthen iPort Zealand's function as an <strong>intermodal hub</strong>, transforming Ringsted into a new logistics "pivot" point for Northern Europe.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Project Components</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>570,000 m²</strong> of logistics, manufacturing, and advanced manufacturing space</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Intermodal rail terminal</strong> with capacity for 12+ trains per day</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Dedicated facilities for high-tech sectors and pharmaceutical industries</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Potential to create thousands of new jobs</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Operational launch planned in parallel with the Fehmarnbelt opening</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This planning approach reflects Denmark's objective to have infrastructure <strong>ready before the tunnel opens</strong>. This way, the increased traffic that will emerge after 2029 can be managed without creating "new capacity bottlenecks".</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Potential Impact for Northern Europe</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">When completed, iPort Zealand is expected to be a mega-hub capable of generating impacts in the following areas:</p>
<ol>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Rail-first modal shift:</strong> Diverting trucks to rail, reducing CO₂ emissions, and alleviating regional motorway congestion.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Germany–Nordic integration:</strong> Combined with the Fehmarnbelt link, the Hamburg, Lübeck, Malmö, Copenhagen triangle will transform into a stronger commercial axis.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Supply chain resilience:</strong> Creating a hinterland logistics system less dependent on weather conditions and maritime bottlenecks, with higher predictability.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>High-tech &amp; pharma clustering:</strong> Denmark's industrial clusters focused on biotechnology and pharmaceutical sectors can grow further.</p></li>
</ol>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, iPort Zealand represents not just a logistics centre for Denmark alone, but a next-generation logistics hub for the <strong>Nordic–Baltic–Germany</strong> corridor. With the opening of Fehmarnbelt, a redesign of Europe's northern distribution networks is expected, and iPort Zealand is poised to be the epicentre of this transformation.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">iPort Zealand investment size: <strong>€1 billion</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Location: <strong>Ringsted</strong>, adjacent to E20, at the intersection of two major rail corridors.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Rail terminal at full capacity: can replace <strong>approximately 720 trucks daily</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Fehmarnbelt Tunnel opening: <strong>2029</strong>, Hamburg–Copenhagen rail time –2 hours.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">570,000 m² of logistics &amp; manufacturing space, 12+ trains per day capacity.</p></li>
</ul>
