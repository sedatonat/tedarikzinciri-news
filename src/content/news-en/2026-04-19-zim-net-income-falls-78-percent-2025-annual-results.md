<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>ZIM Integrated Shipping Services Ltd.</strong> reported <strong>$481 million</strong> in net income for 2025, a sharp decline from $2.15 billion the year prior. Annual revenue fell 18 per cent to <strong>$6.9 billion</strong>, while operating income (EBIT) dropped from $2.53 billion to <strong>$1.02 billion</strong>. Adjusted <strong>EBITDA</strong> reached $2.17 billion, contracting 41 per cent year-on-year as freight rates and volumes both weakened.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The carrier handled <strong>3.66 million TEUs</strong> over the year, representing a 2 per cent decline. <strong>Average freight rates per TEU</strong> fell 18 per cent to <strong>$1,551</strong>. In Q4, net income was $38 million on revenues of $1.48 billion, with carried volume at 898,000 TEUs and an average rate of $1,333 per TEU. ZIM ended the year with <strong>net debt of $2.92 billion</strong> and a leverage ratio of 1.3x.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">President and CEO <strong>Peter Glickman</strong> emphasised that amid ongoing hostilities with Iran, the company's top priority remains the safety of seafarers and fleet. During 2025, ZIM concluded <strong>22 newbuild charter agreements</strong> to maintain a modern and competitive fleet. Despite pressure from softer freight rates and shrinking volumes, the company is positioning itself for the new period through capital discipline and operational flexibility.</p>

<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. ZIM's 2025 net income fell 78 per cent to $481 million.<br>
2. Annual revenue declined 18 per cent to $6.9 billion.<br>
3. The company carried 3.66 million TEUs at an average freight rate of $1,551 per TEU.<br>
4. Year-end net debt stood at $2.92 billion with a 1.3x leverage ratio.<br>
5. ZIM signed 22 newbuild charter agreements to maintain a modern fleet.</p>
