<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Amazon</strong> has launched a new partnership with the <strong>U.S. Postal Service (USPS)</strong> to provide customers with a more convenient return experience. Through this collaboration, users will be able to use the <strong>doorstep pickup</strong> option to return eligible products from their homes.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to a company spokesperson, the <strong>system, which was piloted during the summer months</strong>, is currently active for <strong>select customers</strong>. While Amazon has not disclosed details about the service's full scope, the company stated that the system aims to <strong>increase customer satisfaction and operational efficiency</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The USPS pickup service requires <strong>customers to box and label the package they wish to return</strong>. Subsequently, <strong>a postal worker picks up the package directly from the customer's address</strong>, eliminating the need for the customer to visit a store or shipping location.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Third Person</strong> CEO <strong>Matthew Hertz</strong> announced in a <strong>LinkedIn</strong> post that this option is active for his own Amazon order in the <strong>Nashville, Tennessee</strong> area. Hertz noted that the return process was completed "<strong>free of charge, boxed, and labeled</strong>."</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A New Revenue Stream for USPS</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>U.S. Postal Service</strong>, seeking to overcome its longstanding financial challenges, is turning toward the <strong>reverse logistics</strong> market. According to a report published in 2024 by the <strong>Office of Inspector General</strong>, USPS processed <strong>132 million return packages in fiscal year 2023</strong> and generated <strong>$665 million in revenue</strong> from these operations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The report states, "Given the growth in the reverse logistics market, USPS has opportunities to expand its market share and increase revenue in this area." This demonstrates that <strong>e-commerce returns have become a strategic growth area for postal services</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Through this partnership with Amazon, USPS aims to <strong>directly generate revenue from rising return volumes</strong> and <strong>make more effective use of its logistics network</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Amazon's Return Ecosystem Expands</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Amazon already offers customers various return methods:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Label-free and box-free returns</strong> at <strong>Staples</strong>, <strong>Whole Foods Market</strong>, and <strong>The UPS Store</strong> locations,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Easy dropoff through more than 8,000 <strong>drop-off points</strong> within a 5-mile access distance,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Now, <strong>doorstep return pickup</strong> service via USPS.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">An Amazon spokesperson stated, "Four out of every five customers in the U.S. can access at least one return point within 5 miles. Now we're adding the doorstep pickup option to that," the company said.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This new model carries strategic importance, particularly as <strong>preparation for the busy post-holiday return period</strong>. According to a report prepared by the <strong>National Retail Federation (NRF)</strong> and <strong>Happy Returns</strong>, <strong>17% of sales made during the 2025 holiday season are expected to be returned</strong>. This rate exceeds the annual average <strong>return rate of 15.8%</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Consequently, the USPS partnership is part of Amazon's comprehensive strategy to <strong>alleviate peak-season return volume pressure</strong>, <strong>prevent bottlenecks in its logistics network</strong>, and <strong>offer customers more flexible options</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A Customer Experience-Focused Transformation</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Amazon's new return strategy carries not only operational convenience but also the aim of <strong>strengthening customer loyalty</strong>. The return process is a critical factor that directly impacts customer experience, particularly in e-commerce.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>USPS doorstep return model</strong> reduces customer time loss while also contributing to <strong>a lower carbon footprint</strong>; in this model, <strong>route-based pickup planning</strong> is implemented rather than individual parcel transportation.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, the Amazon and USPS partnership is viewed as a win-win step for both parties—strengthening <strong>the postal service's revenue diversification</strong> and reinforcing <strong>Amazon's customer experience strategy</strong>.</p>
