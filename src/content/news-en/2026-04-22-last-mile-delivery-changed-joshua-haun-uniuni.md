<p style="text-align: justify">As consumer expectations rise and volatility persists, retail and <strong>e-commerce</strong> brands must rethink how they select last-mile partners, balancing speed, cost, data and reliability. Reliability has replaced speed as the primary metric. As <strong>e-commerce</strong>'s once-frenetic pandemic growth normalizes to pre-pandemic levels, the defining characteristics of good delivery have shifted. Recent <strong>McKinsey</strong> research shows that by 2024, speed has dropped to 5th place among shopper priorities—down from first place in 2022—falling behind cost, flexibility and reliability. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>UniUni</strong> operates a last-mile delivery network focused on <strong>Asian e-commerce</strong> (<strong>Shein</strong>, <strong>Temu</strong>, <strong>TikTok Shop</strong>) in the <strong>U.S.</strong> and <strong>Canada</strong> markets, competing against <strong>FedEx</strong>, <strong>UPS</strong>, <strong>USPS</strong>, <strong>OnTrac</strong> and <strong>LaserShip</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Joshua Haun</strong>, vice president of business development at <strong>UniUni</strong>, says, "Reliability has quietly become the new speed." "Most leaders prefer to offer a clear, honest 2-4 day delivery window rather than promise 1-2 days and miss half the time. Shoppers forgive slightly longer transit times; they don't forgive inconsistency." For brands, no matter how aggressive the original shipping promise, missed and unreliable deliveries result in significant decline. Missed <strong>SLA</strong>s and downstream exceptions not only disappoint shoppers but disrupt forecasting, inflate costs and quietly erode brand reliability. From a supply chain perspective, the last-mile space is <strong>e-commerce</strong>'s core operational challenge, with <strong>parcel volume</strong> now exceeding 23 billion packages annually in the <strong>U.S.</strong> over the past few years. The <strong>Pitney Bowes Parcel Shipping Index</strong> tracks year-over-year shifts in total market share among <strong>USPS</strong>, <strong>UPS</strong> and <strong>FedEx</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Rate transparency matters more than the lowest price. <strong>E-commerce</strong> brands often operate on thin margins easily eroded by unexpected shipping cost increases. Unpredictable surcharges—from peak and fuel fees to residential or oversized package charges—create risk for brands. As surcharges change each season or with fuel prices, they further squeeze already slim margins. From a supply chain perspective, the transparent presentation of <strong>parcel pricing</strong> components including <strong>dimensional weight</strong> (<strong>DIM</strong>) calculations, <strong>fuel surcharge</strong>, <strong>residential delivery surcharge</strong>, <strong>peak season surcharge</strong>, <strong>oversize fee</strong>, <strong>address correction fee</strong> and <strong>declared value</strong> is the problem that <strong>parcel auditing</strong> firms (<strong>71lbs</strong>, <strong>ShipMatrix</strong>, <strong>Reveel</strong>, <strong>Refund Retriever</strong>) are solving. <strong>GRI</strong> (<strong>General Rate Increase</strong>) is implemented 6-8 times annually by major carriers and typically ranges from 5-7 percent.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>UniUni</strong>, founded by <strong>Peter Lu</strong> in <strong>Vancouver</strong> in 2019 and entering the <strong>U.S.</strong> market in 2021, competes with <strong>Veho</strong>, <strong>Roadie</strong>, <strong>DoorDash Drive</strong> and <strong>Uber Connect</strong> with its gig economy-based last-mile driver network model. A <strong>multi-carrier strategy</strong> has become essential for modern <strong>e-commerce</strong> brands: it requires routing intelligence across <strong>regional carrier</strong>s (<strong>UniUni</strong>, <strong>OnTrac</strong>, <strong>Pitney Bowes</strong>, <strong>X Delivery</strong>), <strong>USPS Priority Mail</strong>, <strong>UPS SurePost</strong>, <strong>FedEx SmartPost</strong> and gig delivery providers. <strong>EDI 214</strong>, <strong>EDI 990</strong>, <strong>API</strong>, <strong>tracking event</strong>, <strong>POD</strong> (<strong>Proof of Delivery</strong>), <strong>signature capture</strong>, <strong>address validation</strong> and <strong>geocoding</strong> are the operational foundations of data integrity. In sum, <strong>UniUni</strong>'s last-mile checklist redefines modern carrier selection criteria for <strong>e-commerce</strong> brands.</p>
