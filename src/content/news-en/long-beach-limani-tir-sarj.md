<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Port of Long Beach</strong>, one of the United States' busiest port complexes, has opened what is North America's largest <strong>Class 8 zero-emission truck charging facility</strong> to date. This milestone is viewed as a pivotal turning point both for strengthening sustainable logistics infrastructure in the San Pedro Bay port region and for meeting California's <strong>2035 zero-emission drayage truck mandate</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new Long Beach charging hub is operated by <strong>4 Gen Logistics</strong> and features <strong>30 ultra-fast charging units</strong> that form the facility's backbone, providing uninterrupted power to the company's fleet of 79 zero-emission trucks. The facility will be expanded shortly to serve other battery-electric heavy-duty vehicles operating within the San Pedro Bay port complex.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A new threshold in sustainable transportation</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Long Beach Harbor Commission President <strong>Bonnie Lowenthal</strong> delivered the following message at the opening ceremony:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"This port and our industry partners are ensuring our economic and environmental sustainability for future generations."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">The project is part of a broader sustainability initiative aimed at creating <strong>a carbon-neutral supply chain</strong> for goods movement from the San Pedro Bay ports to the Inland Empire region. In addition to the Long Beach facility, 4 Gen Logistics brought 14 charging units online at its <strong>Rialto charging hub</strong>, which opened in August. Both facilities are planned to expand with greater capacity in the next phase.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Public-private partnership strengthens</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Port of Long Beach CEO <strong>Mario Cordero</strong> emphasized the acceleration of transformation in the sector, stating:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"We are proud to work with companies like 4 Gen, WattEV, and Forum Mobility. We are paving the way for greener commerce and transportation models both locally and on a global scale."<br></blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">These investments aim not only to reduce the Port of Long Beach's carbon footprint but also to support state policies targeting the scaling of zero-emission truck operations across California.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Vehicle fleet and technical infrastructure</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">4 Gen Logistics' zero-emission fleet is based on three core technologies:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Volvo VNR Electric</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Kenworth T680E battery-electric trucks</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Nikola hydrogen fuel cell trucks</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Battery-electric vehicles utilize the Long Beach charging hub, while hydrogen vehicles have access to a separate fueling station. This diversity makes the company's fleet more operationally flexible and future-ready.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Toward California's 2035 mandate</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The state plans to require that all drayage trucks operating in port yards be zero-emission by 2035. The massive charging facility opened at Long Beach represents concrete progress toward this goal.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">4 Gen Logistics CEO <strong>David Duncan</strong> summarized it this way:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Zero-emission transportation is no longer a future vision; it is a reality today."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">This facility offers both cost advantages for regional logistics operators and robust infrastructure for statewide carbon reduction policies.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Port of Long Beach opened <strong>North America's largest Class 8 zero-emission truck charging facility</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">30 ultra-fast charging units → in operation for 79 zero-emission trucks.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The facility will soon be extended to serve other electric trucks.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The project supports California's <strong>2035 zero-emission drayage truck</strong> target.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The Long Beach and Rialto facilities will expand with increased capacity.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The fleet includes Volvo VNR, Kenworth T680E, and Nikola hydrogen vehicles.</p></li>
</ul>
