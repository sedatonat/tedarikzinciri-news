<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Singapore</strong> and <strong>South Korea</strong>, two of Asia's major maritime hubs, have joined forces to advance the <strong>decarbonization and digital transformation of maritime shipping</strong>. The relevant ministries of both countries signed a <strong>Memorandum of Understanding (MoU)</strong> to establish the <strong>Singapore–ROK Green and Digital Shipping Corridor (GDSC)</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Signing ceremony and parties</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The agreement was signed by <strong>Singapore's Acting Transport Minister Jeffrey Siow</strong> and <strong>South Korea's Minister of Oceans and Fisheries Chun Jae Soo</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The ceremony took place in Singapore on <strong>November 2, 2025</strong>, with the document exchange conducted between <strong>Murali Pillai, Senior Minister of State at Singapore's Ministry of Transport</strong>, and Minister Chun. The event was witnessed by <strong>Singapore Prime Minister Lawrence Wong</strong> and <strong>South Korean President Lee Jae Myung</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Joint vision for transitioning to zero-carbon fuels</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under the agreement, <strong>Singapore's Maritime and Port Authority (MPA)</strong> and <strong>South Korea's Ministry of Oceans and Fisheries</strong> will undertake joint initiatives to promote the use of <strong>zero or near-zero greenhouse gas emission fuels</strong> (such as ammonia, methanol, LNG, and hydrogen).</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This collaboration encompasses:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Development of green fuel bunkering infrastructure</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Harmonization of technical standards and certification protocols</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Technology transfer and knowledge sharing</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Joint research and development and industry testing</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Training and competency programs for maritime professionals.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Digitalization and information sharing take center stage</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The GDSC initiative is not limited to eco-friendly fuels; it also aims to <strong>accelerate digitalization in the maritime sector</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">To this end, the parties will develop projects to facilitate <strong>data sharing between port systems</strong>, <strong>information security in maritime operations</strong>, and <strong>standardization of digital processes</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Jeffrey Siow</strong> emphasized that this initiative represents <strong>a strategic step that will shape the future of Asia's maritime sector</strong>:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"This partnership demonstrates our shared commitment to building a sustainable and future-ready maritime sector. By advancing clean energy, digital innovation, and talent development, we support both our national priorities and global green maritime goals."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>South Korea's emphasis on decarbonization</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Chun Jae Soo</strong>, South Korea's Minister of Oceans and Fisheries, noted that this collaboration with Singapore will <strong>lead Asia's green maritime transformation</strong>:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"The Republic of Korea and Singapore are two major maritime partners in Asia. Together, we will take the lead in decarbonizing international maritime shipping. Through this collaboration, by promoting the adoption of green fuels and digital solutions, we will contribute to sustainable growth in the global maritime sector."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic significance: A green bridge between Asia's maritime hubs</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Singapore and South Korea, as two nations with <strong>some of the world's busiest port networks</strong>, occupy leading positions in <strong>fuel bunkering, ship repair, and logistics innovation in international transport</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This new corridor will serve as a model for <strong>harmonizing green fuel infrastructures across the Asian continent</strong>. The ports of both countries will also function in the future as <strong>fueling points for alternative fuels such as ammonia and methanol</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, the GDSC will form <strong>the core of Asia's green maritime network</strong> in line with <strong>the IMO's 2050 net-zero emission targets</strong> and <strong>the Paris Agreement's climate commitments</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Convergence of digital transformation and green energy</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The GDSC will also integrate the <strong>digital port infrastructures of Singapore and South Korea</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
This will enable vessel traffic, fuel bunkering, and customs processes to be optimized through <strong>real-time data sharing</strong>; applications such as <strong>digital certification</strong> and <strong>e-document systems</strong> will be widely deployed.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, this integration will both <strong>increase efficiency</strong> and <strong>contribute to reducing the carbon footprint of maritime shipping</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Singapore</strong> and <strong>South Korea</strong> have signed a <strong>Memorandum of Understanding (MoU)</strong> to establish the <strong>Green and Digital Maritime Corridor (GDSC)</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The collaboration covers <strong>zero or low-carbon fuels</strong>, <strong>bunkering infrastructure</strong>, <strong>digitalization</strong>, and <strong>maritime education</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Parties involved: <strong>MPA (Singapore)</strong> and <strong>ROK Ministry of Oceans and Fisheries</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The GDSC aligns with <strong>IMO's 2050 net-zero target</strong> and the <strong>Paris Climate Agreement</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Jeffrey Siow:</strong> "This partnership is an investment in a sustainable and digital maritime future."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Chun Jae Soo:</strong> "Asia will lead in green maritime shipping."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The GDSC is viewed as a strategic initiative that will integrate <strong>Asia's green port network and digital data infrastructure</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
