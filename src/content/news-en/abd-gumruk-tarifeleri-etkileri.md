<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>DHL</strong> and <strong>New York University (NYU) Stern School of Business</strong> released a report showing that while <strong>new U.S. customs tariffs</strong> are disrupting global trade, they are unable to halt it.</p>
<p><br>
The research reveals that <strong>global trade in the first half of 2025 showed its fastest growth since 2010</strong>, and <strong>world trade volume gained strength despite the U.S.'s protectionist policies.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>U.S. Now Has Limited Impact on Global Trade</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The report clearly demonstrates that <strong>the U.S. economy is not large enough to determine the direction of global trade.</strong></p>
<p><br>
As of 2024, <strong>only 13% of global merchandise imports go to the U.S.</strong>, while <strong>9% of exports originate from the U.S.</strong></p>
<p><br>
Therefore, while Washington's tariffs create <strong>localized turbulence</strong> in global trade, they are <strong>not on a scale to alter the overall balance.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the DHL report, many countries <strong>are not following the U.S.'s broad-based tariff increases</strong>, which allows trade to be redirected through different regions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Global Trade Continues to Grow</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the special update of the <strong>DHL Global Connectedness Tracker 2025</strong>, global trade volume is expected to grow at an <strong>average annual rate of 2.5%</strong> <strong>during the 2025–2029 period.</strong> This rate is nearly the same as the pace of the previous decade.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, <strong>before the new wave of tariffs in January 2025,</strong> this rate was forecasted at <strong>3.1%.</strong><br>
Due to the impact of tariffs, the growth forecast was revised down to <strong>2.5%.</strong></p>
<p><br>
On a regional basis, the steepest decline occurred in <strong>North America:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Growth expectations of 2.7% in January 2025 declined to 1.5% as of September.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Declines in other regions remained more limited.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Nevertheless, according to DHL's analysis, <strong>global trade is structurally resilient</strong> and growth is expected to continue <strong>steadily over the next four years.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>China Strengthens Trade with ASEAN and Africa</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>China,</strong> the country most affected by the tariffs implemented by the U.S., is offsetting the impact by redirecting its exports to different markets.</p>
<p><br>
China compensated for the <strong>decline in shipments to the U.S.</strong> with increased trade with <strong>ASEAN (Association of Southeast Asian Nations),</strong> <strong>Africa,</strong> <strong>the European Union,</strong> and other regions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">During the same period, <strong>U.S. importers</strong> also accelerated their imports through <strong>frontloading</strong> before the tariff increases took effect.</p>
<p><br>
This situation temporarily raised <strong>U.S. import volume in the first quarter of 2025,</strong> but over the long term, <strong>weakening in North American trade flows is expected.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>DHL: Trade Barriers Cannot Stop Global Creativity</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>DHL Express CEO John Pearson</strong> made the following assessment following the report's release:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Trade barriers do not serve the world's interests. However, we must never underestimate the creativity of buyers and sellers around the world when it comes to doing business."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Pearson emphasized <strong>the resilience of the global economy,</strong> noting that trade will remain strong thanks to <strong>innovative business models, digitalization, and regional cooperation.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>U.S. Tariffs: Slowing Down But Not Stopping Trade</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the report, <strong>U.S. tariffs are slowing the pace of global trade growth,</strong> but they <strong>do not have the effect of stopping trade.</strong></p>
<p><br>
<strong>Post-tariff growth rate (2.5%)</strong> still tracks <strong>close to levels expected at the beginning of the 2020s.</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, <strong>the diversification of global trade flows</strong> reduces dependence on a single economy, thereby creating <strong>a more balanced trade network.</strong></p>
<p><br>
This is particularly strengthening <strong>supply chains between Asia, Africa, and Europe.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: Global Trade Is Evolving Toward a Multipolar Structure</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The DHL report clearly shows that <strong>global trade continues to grow despite the U.S.'s protectionist policies.</strong><br>
In the new era, the center of trade is shifting from <strong>a single country to regional networks.</strong><br>
The rising role of China, ASEAN, the EU, and Africa signals that the future of world trade is shaping a <strong>multipolar</strong> structure.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Despite the obstacles ahead for global trade, <strong>innovation, diversity, and resilience</strong> principles support <strong>the sustainable growth of the world economy.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">According to the <strong>DHL and NYU Stern</strong> report, global trade in the first half of 2025 recorded <strong>its strongest growth since 2010.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The U.S.</strong> accounts for only <strong>13% of global imports</strong> and <strong>9% of exports.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Global trade's annual growth forecast was reduced from 3.1% to 2.5%.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>North America</strong> experienced the steepest decline, while other regions saw <strong>limited revisions.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>China</strong> offset U.S. losses with <strong>ASEAN, Africa, and EU markets.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>DHL CEO John Pearson</strong> emphasized <strong>global cooperation,</strong> stating "Trade barriers cannot stop the global desire to do business."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Conclusion: <strong>Global trade is entering a new era that is multipolar, resilient, and digitalization-focused.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
