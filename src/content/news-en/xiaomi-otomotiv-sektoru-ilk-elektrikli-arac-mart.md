<p>Xiaomi has announced it will begin deliveries of its first electric vehicle (EV) in March, marking the technology giant's initial foray into the competitive automotive sector. According to BBC News, this will be the company's first venture into the automotive industry, with the vehicle's price to be revealed on March 28. China's fifth-largest smartphone manufacturer said it is ready to accept orders through 59 showrooms across 29 cities. The announcement comes as EV manufacturers face intensifying price wars in China, the world's largest automobile market.&nbsp;</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Competitors like <a href="https://www.byd.com/eu"><u>BYD</u></a> and Tesla have slashed their prices. Xiaomi has disclosed plans to invest 10 billion dollars in its vehicle operations over the next decade.
