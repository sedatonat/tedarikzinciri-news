<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The United States and South Korea</strong> are poised to <strong>complete a new trade agreement</strong> following lengthy negotiations. <strong>U.S. President Donald Trump</strong> stated during remarks on <strong>Wednesday, October 29, 2025</strong> in South Korea's <strong>Gyeongju city</strong> that the two countries had <strong>"substantially concluded their trade agreement."</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Trump spoke with <strong>South Korean President Lee Jae Myung</strong> at a dinner held during the <strong>Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC)</strong> summit. "We've made the deal, we've substantially completed the trade agreement. We also discussed national security and some other matters," Trump said, emphasizing that the talks had both economic and strategic dimensions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">While official documents have not yet been released, multiple sources reported that the <strong>October 29 meeting clarified details of a framework agreement whose foundations were laid in July.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key provisions of the framework agreement</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the draft framework announced in July, the <strong>United States</strong> agreed to <strong>reduce tariffs on goods imported from South Korea from 25 percent to 15 percent.</strong> In return, <strong>South Korea is expected to commit to 350 billion dollars in investment in the United States.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new agreement is expected to formalize these provisions. Additionally, <strong>cooperation protocols in strategic sectors</strong> covering energy, semiconductors, and defense industry investments are expected to be included in the agreement text.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Trump administration aims to use such bilateral agreements to <strong>reconfigure its trade and security network in Asia.</strong> The agreement is viewed particularly as <strong>Washington's move to deepen relations with Seoul</strong> during a period of intensifying <strong>U.S.-China trade competition.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Diplomatic intensity at the APEC summit</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Trump and Lee's meeting took place during the <strong>APEC Summit held in Seoul this week.</strong> On the summit's agenda were the U.S. regional economic integration policies, energy security, and supply chain resilience.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Trump is known to have conducted a series of meetings spanning <strong>October 29–30</strong> across his Asia tour, covering <strong>Malaysia, Japan, and South Korea.</strong> During talks held with <strong>Chinese Vice Premier He Lifeng in Malaysia over the weekend,</strong> agreement was reached on a <strong>"preliminary agreement" regarding the U.S.-China trade framework.</strong> However, this draft will be formalized through <strong>talks between Trump and Chinese President Xi Jinping on October 30 in South Korea.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>The national security dimension</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Trump hinted that the agreement with South Korea covers not only trade but also <strong>defense and security cooperation.</strong> This statement indicates that <strong>North Korean missile threats and regional defense coordination,</strong> both recently elevated agenda items between the two countries, will be integrated into the agreement.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">U.S. Treasury Secretary <strong>Scott Bessent</strong> also stated last week that "<strong>the U.S. is integrating its commercial relationships in Asia with security partnerships</strong>" and noted that the talks in Malaysia were part of this process.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>The significance of the trade agreement</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, this new U.S.-South Korea agreement could establish an <strong>updated model of economic cooperation</strong> that may replace the <strong>KORUS FTA (Korea–U.S. Free Trade Agreement).</strong> Signed in 2007 and implemented in 2012, KORUS increased bilateral trade volume but has created friction in recent years due to <strong>tariff disputes.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new agreement is expected to both <strong>boost industrial investment</strong> and <strong>strengthen U.S. economic influence in Asia.</strong> Joint R&D projects in <strong>semiconductors, batteries, and green energy sectors</strong> may particularly come into focus.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In Trump's words, "<strong>This is not just about trade; it will be an agreement that secures the future of both nations.</strong>"</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>U.S. President Trump</strong> announced there is an <strong>"almost completed" trade agreement</strong> with South Korea.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The agreement includes <strong>tariff reductions from 25 percent to 15 percent</strong> and <strong>South Korea's commitment to 350 billion dollars in U.S. investment.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The talks took place <strong>at the APEC Summit</strong> in <strong>Gyeongju.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The agreement is viewed as an <strong>updated version of KORUS FTA.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>National security and technology cooperation</strong> provisions are expected to be included in the agreement.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Trump plans to meet with <strong>Xi Jinping on October 30</strong> to also finalize the <strong>U.S.-China trade framework.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
