<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Maersk</strong> has announced that it has completed its first transit through the <strong>Red Sea</strong> and <strong>Bab-el-Mandeb Strait</strong> since December 2023. The company is re-establishing maritime shipping operations in the region following two years of intensified attacks by <strong>Houthi</strong> militants against vessels. According to <strong>The Maritime Executive</strong>, which publishes coverage of the maritime industry, the <strong>Maersk Sebarok</strong> container vessel transited the <strong>Red Sea</strong> successfully on December 18-19, 2025, on its <strong>U.S. East Coast</strong> - <strong>India</strong> route. In its official statement, <strong>Maersk</strong> emphasized that the highest possible safety measures, described as <strong>"the highest possible safety measures,"</strong> were implemented during the transit. The statement also noted that customers with cargo on the vessel were informed in advance directly.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The company characterizes this transit as a significant milestone; however, it has clearly stated that this move does not signify readiness for a comprehensive network realignment for the <strong>trans-Suez corridor</strong>. Looking ahead, <strong>Maersk</strong> is adopting a <strong>"stepwise approach"</strong> to gradually resume traffic through the <strong>Suez Canal</strong> and <strong>Red Sea</strong>. The company is planning a limited number of additional <strong>trans-Suez</strong> sailings in the coming months; however, it is not stating that the entire network will be comprehensively diverted back to the Suez route. This phased approach is grounded in a risk framework that carefully manages both security intelligence and the timing of customer notification.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Towards the end of 2025, there has been a notable decline in attacks by <strong>Houthi</strong> groups against commercial vessels. This situation is creating conditions for other major container carriers to present their own roadmaps for returning to the region. <strong>CMA CGM</strong> is planning to fully resume its <strong>India-Mediterranean Express</strong> service through the <strong>Suez Canal</strong> in January 2026. <strong>Ocean Network Express (ONE)</strong> is aiming to launch a new <strong>Red Sea-China</strong> service on January 15. These moves indicate that the cost and transit time burden of the lengthy alternative routes taken via the <strong>Cape of Good Hope</strong> since the end of 2023 is diminishing.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The gradual return to the Red Sea route is significant for several critical indicators of the global container supply chain. First, increased transits through the <strong>Suez Canal</strong> will reduce transit times between Asia and Europe by approximately 10-14 days and reduce the use of emission-intensive secondary routes. Second, it creates downward pressure on <strong>war risk insurance</strong> premiums and <strong>bunker</strong> consumption, which becomes a factor balancing ship operators' unit transport costs. Third, the moderation of volatility in <strong>spot rates</strong> and long-term contract prices offers shippers a more predictable outlook for their 2026 budgets. Conversely, <strong>Maersk</strong>'s clear position that a "full network return is not being undertaken" preserves flexibility to revert to alternative routes should the security situation deteriorate. In sum, the gradual return to the region sends early signals of both geopolitical normalization and the repricing of maritime operational risk.</p>
<p><br></p>

1. <strong>Maersk Sebarok</strong> is making the first <strong>Maersk</strong> transit through the <strong>Red Sea</strong> since December 2023.<br>
2. The route on the <strong>U.S. East Coast</strong> - <strong>India</strong> line occurred on December 18-19, 2025.<br>
3. <strong>Maersk</strong> is planning a limited number of additional <strong>trans-Suez</strong> sailings with a <strong>"stepwise approach."</strong><br>
