<p style="text-align: justify">In 2024, railway border crossings between <strong>Kazakhstan</strong> and <strong>China</strong> experienced significant growth. The <strong>Dostyk</strong> border gate, in particular, saw considerable congestion during the autumn months. Annual statistics show that a decade-long record was broken at Dostyk in 2024. Cargo volumes passing through this border gate increased by 13.1 percent compared to 2023, reaching 32 million tonnes. This increase underscored the importance of railway transportation along the <strong>Middle Corridor</strong> once again.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Record Growth at Dostyk Border Gate</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In 2024, cargo passing through the <strong>Dostyk</strong> border gate totaled 18 million tonnes. This figure surpassed the previous record of 17.8 million tonnes set in 2013. This achievement highlights Dostyk's importance in international logistics and serves as an inspiring example for future success.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Increases at Alashankou and Khorgos Crossings</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In 2024, a total of 7,684 freight trains heading toward Europe passed through the <strong>Alashankou</strong> border gate on the Chinese side. This represents a 15.8 percent increase compared to 2023. Similarly, train traffic through the <strong>Khorgos</strong> border gate also rose significantly. In 2024, 8,730 trains passed through this border gate, representing a 12.5 percent increase from 2023. These trains carried a combined 12 million tonnes of cargo, marking an 11 percent increase compared to the previous year.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Growth in Container Transportation</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Container transportation through <strong>Kazakhstan</strong> also showed growth in 2024. A total of 1.4 million TEU containers were transported, representing an 8.8 percent increase from the previous year. This growth reflects the increasing importance of railway transportation along the <strong>Middle Corridor</strong> and the development of logistical infrastructure in the region.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Challenges and Solutions at Border Crossings</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Throughout 2024, the <strong>Kazakhstan</strong>-<strong>China</strong> border crossings experienced occasional difficulties. The <strong>Dostyk</strong> border gate, in particular, faced serious congestion during the autumn season. This situation led to delays at border crossings and disruptions in logistical processes. However, both countries took various measures to address these issues. For example, processing times at the <strong>Khorgos</strong> border gate were reduced from 12 hours to 6-8 hours. Additionally, <strong>Kazakhstan</strong> and <strong>China</strong> are working on new projects to increase border crossing capacity.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Future Plans</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Kazakhstan</strong> and <strong>China</strong> are planning various projects to sustain this growth in railway transportation. For example, the establishment of a transport and logistics hub in the <strong>Almaty</strong> region is being planned. Additionally, further development of the <strong>Dostyk</strong>-<strong>Alashankou</strong> border crossing is targeted. These projects will contribute to strengthening logistical infrastructure in the region and facilitating international trade.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
