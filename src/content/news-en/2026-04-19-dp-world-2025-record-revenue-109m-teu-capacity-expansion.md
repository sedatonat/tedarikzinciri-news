<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>DP World</strong> posted record results for 2025. Revenue rose 22 per cent to <strong>$24.4 billion</strong>, while adjusted <strong>EBITDA</strong> grew 18 per cent to <strong>$6.4 billion</strong> at a 26.3 per cent margin. <strong>Ports & Terminals</strong> and <strong>Logistics</strong> segments led the growth. Total group throughput climbed 5.8 per cent to <strong>93.4 million TEUs</strong>, while profit jumped 32.2 per cent to <strong>$1.96 billion</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Chairman <strong>H.E. Essa Kazim</strong> said the diversified portfolio, disciplined capital allocation, and focus on high-yield cargo enabled resilient earnings amid heightened uncertainty and changing trade dynamics. Group CEO <strong>Yuvraj Narayan</strong> noted that Ports & Terminals performed strongly, supported by healthy volumes, improved yield, and disciplined cost management; like-for-like revenue per TEU increased 8.5 per cent.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">For 2025, <strong>operating cash flow</strong> reached <strong>$6.3 billion</strong>, up 14 per cent. DP World expanded its <strong>port capacity to 109 million TEUs</strong>, reinforcing its position as a global capacity leader in the sector. The company emphasised that its integrated platform and adaptability are reflected in the results as supply chains continue to reconfigure.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. DP World's 2025 revenue rose 22 per cent to $24.4 billion.<br>
2. Adjusted EBITDA grew 18 per cent to $6.4 billion.<br>
3. Total group throughput reached 93.4 million TEUs (+5.8 per cent).<br>
4. Net profit climbed 32.2 per cent to $1.96 billion.<br>
5. Port capacity expanded to 109 million TEUs.</p>
