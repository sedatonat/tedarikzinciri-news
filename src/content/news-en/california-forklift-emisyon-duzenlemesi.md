<p><a href="https://ww2.arb.ca.gov/"><u>California Air Resources Board (CARB)</u></a> has approved a new regulation requiring forklifts used in California to transition to zero-emission models between 2028 and 2038. The regulation stipulates different implementation timelines for fleets of certain sizes and various forklift classes. The mandate to shift to zero-emission forklifts will initially apply only to 50 percent of designated fleet sizes. Additionally, exemptions will be provided for forklifts used in low-utilization, emergency, and certain specialized applications.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5>Key Points:</h5>\n<ul>\n  <li><p>The transition to zero-emission forklifts will take place between 2028 and 2038.</p></li>\n  <li><p>By the initial compliance date, 50 percent of large fleets must transition to zero-emission forklifts.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Exemptions exist for certain forklifts, such as low-utilization and emergency-use units.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The industry has raised concerns over implementation costs and infrastructure challenges.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>News Link:</strong> <a href="https://www.thescxchange.com/articles/10518-california-air-resources-board-approves-final-rule-requiring-zero-emission-forklifts"><u>https://www.thescxchange.com/articles/10518-california-air-resources-board-approves-final-rule-requiring-zero-emission-forklifts</u></a></p>
