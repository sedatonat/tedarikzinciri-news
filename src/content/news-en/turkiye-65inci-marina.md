<p style="text-align: justify">Turkey has added another chapter to its maritime infrastructure expansion by <strong>increasing the number of marinas to 65 with the opening of Gazipaşa Marina</strong>. Transport and Infrastructure Minister <strong>Abdulkadir Uraloğlu</strong> stated at the opening ceremony that the new marina will bring <strong>year-round yacht tourism</strong> to the region, not just during summer months. The aim is to extend economic activity based on marine tourism in eastern Antalya throughout the winter season as well.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>"Part of Our Maritime Culture and Strength"</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Uraloğlu emphasized that Turkey's location surrounded by seas on three sides is not merely a geographic advantage but also a cultural one. He noted that the contributions of maritime activities to the economy, tourism, regional development, and employment are among the Ministry's priority areas.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Turkey's Ports Rising in Global Rankings</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Minister Uraloğlu announced that <strong>Aliağa Port has entered the world's busiest 100 container ports for the first time</strong>, ranking 91st. Moreover, Turkey now has <strong>five ports</strong> within the top 100: Ambarlı, Kocaeli, Tekirdağ, Mersin, and Aliağa. This development reflects Turkey's increasing weight in global maritime logistics.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Commercial Fleet Expanded Six-Fold</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Uraloğlu, Turkey's merchant fleet has achieved remarkable growth over the past 20 years.</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Fleet capacity in 2002 was <strong>8.9 million DWT</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">In the first half of 2025, it reached <strong>53.1 million DWT</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This expansion has positioned Turkey among the <strong>world's top 10 merchant fleet nations</strong>. The fleet's number of vessels increased to <strong>2,203</strong> and represents approximately <strong>six-fold growth</strong> in tonnage.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Top-Tier in Shipbuilding and Recycling</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Turkey ranks among major players in the global shipbuilding market:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>9th globally in ship orders</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>11th in total tonnage</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>85 active shipyards</strong> and</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>More than 94,000 employees</strong> supporting a robust production ecosystem.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">In 2024, ship and yacht exports reached <strong>2 billion dollars</strong>. Additionally, with <strong>23 ship recycling facilities</strong>, Turkey ranks fourth globally and first in Europe.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Marina Capacity Tripled in 20 Years</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Turkey's marina infrastructure offered only <strong>8,500 mooring capacity</strong> in 2002, while today that figure has grown to nearly <strong>26,000</strong>. This expansion further strengthens the country's important position in Mediterranean yacht tourism.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Ministry continues to accelerate new marina projects.<br>
Ongoing projects include:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Datça Marina</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tekirdağ Marina</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Haliç Marina</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Mersin Aydıncık Marina</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Infrastructure work on Mersin Aydıncık Marina is planned to be completed by year-end.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Record Traffic in Cruise Tourism</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">In the first 10 months of 2025, Turkey's ports received:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>1,278 cruise ships</strong> (+14.9%),</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>More than 2.02 million passengers</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">These results demonstrate Turkey's rapidly expanding cruise potential and rising competitive strength in the Eastern Mediterranean market.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Highlights:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Turkey's marina count has reached <strong>65</strong> (Gazipaşa Marina opening).</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Aliağa entered the global top 100 port rankings for the first time (91st place).</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Turkey's merchant fleet of <strong>53.1 million DWT</strong> ranks in the world's top 10.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">85 shipyards, 94,000 employees; Europe's leading ship recycler.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Marina capacity increased from 8,500 to 26,000 moorings.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">2025 cruise passenger count exceeds <strong>2 million</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
