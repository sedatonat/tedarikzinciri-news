<p style="text-align: justify">According to <strong>Russian Railways</strong>, <strong>export rail freight traffic</strong> carried via <strong>Russia-Kazakhstan border crossing checkpoints</strong> grew <strong>20.7 per cent</strong> year-on-year during January-February 2026. The overall transportation volume amounted to <strong>5.7 mn tons</strong> of various commodities including <strong>58.1 thousand TEU of containerised cargo, up 8.5 per cent year-on-year</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The most important export commodities are: <strong>oil and products making 1.6 mn tons (up 3.5 per cent year-on-year)</strong>, <strong>ferrous metals (1.1 mn tons, up 26.5 per cent)</strong>, and <strong>grain, meal and flour (623 thousand tons, up 2.2-fold)</strong>. The data clearly reflects Russia's pivot toward Central Asia — and Kazakhstan in particular — in the post-sanctions era.</p><p style="text-align: justify">Freight trains cross the Russia-Kazakhstan border via key checkpoints including <strong>Kanisay, Kigash, Ozinki, Kartaly, Orsk, Petropavlovsk, and Zauralye</strong>. These checkpoints are being modernised to speed up transit flows and their customs processes digitised. Kazakhstan serves both as a final destination and as a transit hub providing connections to China, Europe, and Gulf markets via the Middle Corridor. Forward-looking projections indicate Russia-Kazakhstan rail trade will continue to grow through 2026.</p>

<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. Russia-Kazakhstan export rail traffic grew 20.7% in January-February 2026.<br>
2. Total volume reached 5.7 mn tons; containerised cargo reached 58.1K TEU (+8.5%).<br>
3. Oil and products: 1.6 mn tons (+3.5%); ferrous metals: 1.1 mn tons (+26.5%).<br>
4. Grain, meal, and flour exports rose 2.2-fold to 623 thousand tons.<br>
5. Border checkpoints (Kanisay, Kigash, Ozinki, Kartaly, Orsk, Petropavlovsk, Zauralye) are being modernised.</p>
