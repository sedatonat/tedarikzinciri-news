<p>MRO (Maintenance, Repair and Operations) specialists are emphasizing that businesses need to focus more on risk management and inventory optimization. According to research conducted by Verusen, 71% of participants believe that MRO processes should be treated as a strategic initiative. However, only 58% state that these processes are being managed strategically. The data reveals that inadequate planning of critical spare parts leads to operational risks, and companies are struggling with inefficient inventory management and inconsistent data formats. Verusen CEO Scott Matthews notes that better management of MRO will provide cost advantages for businesses.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5>Key Takeaways:</h5>\n<ul>\n  <li><p>MRO processes should be evaluated as a strategic initiative.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Spare parts criticality is important for businesses' risk management.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Data inconsistencies lead to inefficient inventory management.</p></li>\n  <li><p>New technologies such as artificial intelligence (AI) have the potential to improve MRO processes.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n
