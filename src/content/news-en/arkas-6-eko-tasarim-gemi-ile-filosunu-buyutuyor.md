<p>Arkas announced plans to expand its fleet with six new-generation eco-design container vessels valued at $360 million. The move will bring the company's total vessel count to 55 and boost its TEU capacity to 112,230. Arkas secured long-term financing through an agreement with CIMC Financial Leasing, enabling the fleet expansion and modernization.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Arkas, along with representatives from China Shipbuilding Trading Co, CSSC Huangpu Wenchong Shipyard, and CIMC Financial Leasing, signed a partnership agreement covering vessels to be delivered in 2028. The Turkish maritime company has initiated procurement of the six new vessels. Each vessel will have a capacity of 4,300 TEU, making them the largest-capacity vessels in Arkas's fleet, and will be sourced from China's CSSC Huangpu Wenchong Shipyard.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Lucien Arkas, Chairman of the Board at Arkas Holding, commented: "Now, for the first time in Turkey, we are ordering eco-design vessels from China. These environmentally friendly vessels will enable us to transport more cargo with less fuel, bringing us closer to our sustainability targets. This is a step toward both modernizing and expanding our fleet."</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li><p>Arkas is expanding its fleet with a $360 million investment.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Total vessel count will reach 55, with TEU capacity increasing to 112,230.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Six new vessels will be sourced from CSSC Huangpu Wenchong Shipyard.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Each vessel will have a capacity of 4,300 TEU.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Vessels will be delivered in 2028.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Long-term financing agreement concluded with CIMC Financial Leasing.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Lucien Arkas described the investment as an important step toward sustainability goals.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>News Link: </strong><a href="https://www.porttechnology.org/news/arkas-splashes-360-million-in-six-eco-design-vessels/"><u>https://www.porttechnology.org/news/arkas-splashes-360-million-in-six-eco-design-vessels/</u></a></p>\n<p>-------------------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
