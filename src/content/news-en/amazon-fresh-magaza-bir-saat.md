<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Amazon</strong> is developing a new <strong>one-hour in-store pickup</strong> model to accelerate fulfillment speeds and make more efficient use of its physical store network. According to <strong>Business Insider</strong>, the company is working on what it internally calls "<strong>rush pickup</strong>"—a service that would allow customers to collect their orders from <strong>Amazon-owned stores within one hour</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The planned system offers a more advanced integration than Amazon's current click-and-collect offerings. Customers will be able to combine products from both Amazon's online marketplace and items in stock at physical stores (<strong>Whole Foods</strong>, <strong>Amazon Fresh</strong>, <strong>Amazon Go</strong>) in a single <strong>unified order</strong>. Once the order is prepared, the customer can pick up the items from their selected store within approximately <strong>60 minutes</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">According to the report, Amazon plans to launch this model as a pilot in at least one major metropolitan area in <strong>Q1 2026</strong>. The pilot aims to test both customer demand and measure how effectively online and physical inventory can be <strong>operationally synchronized</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Amazon's Speed Race: From 30 Minutes to One Hour</strong></h3>\n<p style="text-align: justify">This initiative is part of Amazon's aggressive expansion of speed-focused delivery solutions. Just a week earlier, the company launched <strong>Amazon Now</strong>, a <strong>30-minute delivery</strong> service in select areas of Seattle and Philadelphia. Additionally, Amazon is testing rapid delivery models in markets such as the <strong>United Kingdom, India, and Mexico</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Currently, Amazon offers <strong>next-day in-store pickup</strong> for certain orders in the U.S., and grocery customers can pick up specific items within <strong>30 minutes</strong>. However, the new "rush pickup" model goes beyond these offerings by enabling <strong>combined online and store inventory in a single order</strong> and systematically reducing pickup time to one hour.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Competition Intensifies in Click-and-Collect Market</strong></h3>\n<p style="text-align: justify">This move reflects Amazon's efforts to strengthen its competitive position in the rapidly growing <strong>click-and-collect</strong> market. According to <strong>eMarketer</strong> data:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Click-and-collect sales in the U.S. are expected to reach <strong>112.96 billion USD</strong> in 2025.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">This represents a <strong>17% increase</strong> compared to 2023.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>153 million Americans</strong> are expected to use click-and-collect services at least once in 2025.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">While Amazon is a strong e-commerce leader in this space, <strong>Walmart</strong> is a formidable competitor in terms of speed and pickup advantages. Walmart, with <strong>more than 4,600 U.S. stores</strong>, can reach approximately <strong>95% of households within three hours</strong> and is expected to generate <strong>38.5 billion USD</strong> in click-and-collect revenue in 2025.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Amazon's new model is viewed as a strategic step to offset Walmart's physical store advantage. While Amazon lags behind Walmart in store count, it aims to differentiate through <strong>high inventory diversity</strong>, <strong>advanced software infrastructure</strong>, and <strong>micro-fulfillment</strong> capabilities.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Fulfillment and Store Network Integration</strong></h3>\n<p style="text-align: justify">According to Amazon's internal documents, the "rush pickup" model aims to address two key challenges:</p>\n<ol>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Customer speed expectations</strong>,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">More efficient utilization of Amazon's physical store network.</p></li>\n</ol>\n<p style="text-align: justify">With this pilot, the company is testing a model in which stores function not just as points of sale but also as <strong>hyper-local fulfillment nodes</strong>. This approach aims to reduce last-mile delivery costs while achieving tighter integration of in-store operations with e-commerce.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic Assessment</strong></h3>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Amazon's one-hour in-store pickup model carries critical importance on three fronts:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Speed differentiation:</strong> The gap between online shopping and physical pickup virtually disappears.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Inventory efficiency:</strong> Combined use of online and store inventory accelerates inventory turnover.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Omnichannel deepening:</strong> Amazon's digital DNA is forming stronger bonds with physical stores.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">If the pilot succeeds, Amazon is expected to expand this model to broader geographies during 2026–2027. This could lead to the rapid normalization of <strong>"one-hour fulfillment"</strong> as a standard in the retail sector.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h3>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Amazon is preparing to test a <strong>one-hour in-store pickup</strong> model.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The model combines online and store inventory in a <strong>single order</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Pilot launch is scheduled for <strong>Q1 2026</strong> in a major city.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Whole Foods, Amazon Fresh, and Amazon Go stores are included in the scope.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The click-and-collect market will reach <strong>112.96 billion USD</strong> in 2025.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The move is a strategic response to Walmart's pickup and speed advantages.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>----------</p>\n\n<p>--------------------</p>\n<p><strong>Author: </strong><a href="http://sedatonat.com/"><u>SedatOnat.com</u></a></p>\n<p>--------------------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
