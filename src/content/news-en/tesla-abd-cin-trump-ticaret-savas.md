<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tesla</strong> has expressed concern that retaliatory actions by other countries in response to broad import tariffs imposed by US President <strong>Donald Trump</strong> could increase production costs and diminish the competitiveness of its vehicles in international markets. In a letter dated March 11, the company told US Trade Representative <strong>Jamieson Greer</strong> that US exporters could face disproportionate impacts from such trade actions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tesla</strong> emphasized that despite manufacturing in the US, its supply chain depends on foreign sources for several critical components. Specifically, the company noted that additional tariffs on imports of minerals such as <strong>lithium</strong> and <strong>cobalt</strong>, which are available in limited quantities in the US, could increase electric vehicle production costs. Tesla stressed that while it is making efforts to localize its supply chain, some parts and components cannot be sourced domestically or would be extremely difficult to do so.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tesla</strong> stated that if other countries retaliate against US trade practices, tariffs on electric vehicles produced in the US could increase, which would negatively impact the company's competitive position in international markets. It is known that countries such as the <strong>European Union</strong> and <strong>Canada</strong> are planning to take broad retaliatory measures in response to US tariffs on steel and aluminum imports.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tesla</strong>'s CEO <strong>Elon Musk</strong> contributed over 250 million dollars to support <strong>Donald Trump</strong>'s reelection and was subsequently appointed to head the newly created unit known as the <strong>Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE)</strong>. However, despite this close relationship, <strong>Tesla</strong> did not shy away from expressing concerns about the negative effects of trade policies on the company.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In its letter to the US Trade Representative, <strong>Tesla</strong> emphasized that careful consideration is needed to ensure that trade actions do not negatively impact US manufacturers and exporters. The company stated that trade actions should not undermine the goal of supporting domestic production. Additionally, <strong>Tesla</strong> proposed adopting a gradual approach to implementing trade policies and providing US companies sufficient time to prepare their supply chains and compliance measures.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tesla</strong> noted that US trade policies could also affect its operations in <strong>China</strong>. The company has made significant inroads into the Chinese market through its factory in <strong>Shanghai</strong>, which represents its second-largest market. However, rising trade tensions between the US and China could negatively affect <strong>Tesla</strong>'s operations and market share in China.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tesla</strong> warned that US trade policies could increase the company's production costs and reduce its competitive position in international markets.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tesla</strong> stated that it depends on foreign sources for certain critical supply chain components, and tariffs on these components could increase production costs.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tesla</strong> proposed adopting a gradual approach to implementing trade policies and allowing US companies sufficient time to comply.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tesla</strong> stated that trade policies could negatively affect its operations and market share in China.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">In its letter to the US Trade Representative, <strong>Tesla</strong> emphasized that careful consideration is needed to ensure that trade actions do not negatively impact US manufacturers and exporters.</p></li>
</ul>
