<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The United States</strong> vetoed <strong>Canada</strong>'s proposal to establish a task force against oil tankers referred to as <strong>Russia</strong>'s "shadow fleet." This move signaled a shift in <strong>the U.S.</strong>'s approach to international maritime security.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Canada</strong> called for stricter measures against <strong>Russia</strong>'s "shadow fleet," which the country uses to continue its oil exports. This fleet consists of older vessels that typically fail to meet safety standards and are deployed to circumvent sanctions. <strong>Canada</strong>'s proposal included establishing a task force to monitor and prevent such activities.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, <strong>the United States</strong> vetoed this proposal and adopted a different approach. <strong>The Trump administration</strong> argued that existing policies are sufficient and that an additional task force is unnecessary. This situation signaled changes in <strong>the U.S.</strong>'s stance on international maritime security.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This development revealed differing approaches among the international community regarding maritime security and the enforcement of sanctions. In particular, disagreements emerged over what measures should be taken against the methods <strong>Russia</strong> uses to sustain its oil exports. <strong>Canada</strong>'s proposed task force aimed to increase international cooperation in monitoring and preventing such activities.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Nevertheless, <strong>the U.S.</strong>'s veto sparked broader discussions on international maritime policies. In particular, the effectiveness of sanctions and how such "shadow fleets" should be monitored became focal points for future diplomatic negotiations.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, <strong>the U.S.</strong>'s veto brought new discussions on international maritime security and sanctions enforcement. This situation demonstrated that divergent policies and approaches exist among <strong>G7</strong> nations. In the coming period, these issues will need to be discussed further on international platforms and common solutions will need to be found.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The United States</strong> vetoed <strong>Canada</strong>'s proposal to establish a task force against <strong>Russia</strong>'s "shadow fleet."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Trump administration</strong> stated that existing policies are sufficient and that an additional task force is not needed.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Canada</strong> demanded action against older vessels and ships that do not comply with safety standards, which are used to evade sanctions.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The U.S.</strong>'s veto revealed disagreements among <strong>G7</strong> nations regarding international maritime policies.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The effectiveness of sanctions and the monitoring of "shadow fleets" became an important issue in future diplomatic negotiations.</p></li>
</ul>
