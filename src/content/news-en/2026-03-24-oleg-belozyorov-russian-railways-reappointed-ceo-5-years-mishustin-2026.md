<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Oleg Belozyorov</strong> has been re-appointed <strong>CEO and Chairman of the Board of Russian Railways (RZD)</strong> for the next five years. According to the RF Government press office, the decision was formalised by an order signed by <strong>Premier Mikhail Mishustin</strong>. Belozyorov secured the opportunity to continue leading one of Russia's largest state-owned companies.</p><p style="text-align: justify">Belozyorov was <strong>appointed President of Russian Railways in 2015</strong> and, after this position was renamed in 2017, became <strong>CEO of the company</strong>. He was <strong>re-appointed in 2021 for a 5-year period</strong>. This latest appointment extends his total 10 years of uninterrupted leadership to 15. He is credited with leading the restructuring of Russia's rail sector during the war and sanctions era.</p><p style="text-align: justify">Before this appointment, Belozyorov's career included serving as <strong>head of the RF Federal Road Agency during 2004-2009</strong>. He then held the position of <strong>Deputy Minister of Transport</strong>. This experience makes him an expert on both road and rail infrastructure issues. RZD is grappling with challenges in 2025 including an aging wagon fleet and declining loading volumes; Belozyorov's reappointment highlights the continuity Moscow affords the sector.</p>

<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. Oleg Belozyorov will serve as Russian Railways CEO for another 5 years.<br>

4. He previously headed the RF Federal Road Agency during 2004-2009.<br>
5. RZD faces challenges including an aging wagon fleet and declining loading volumes.</p>
