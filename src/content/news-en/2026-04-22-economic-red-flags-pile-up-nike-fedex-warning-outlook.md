<strong>Herzogenaurach, Germany</strong>), <strong>Puma</strong>, <strong>New Balance</strong> (private; CEO <strong>Joe Preston</strong>), <strong>Asics</strong>, <strong>Under Armour</strong> (<strong>Kevin Plank</strong>), <strong>Lululemon</strong> (CEO <strong>Calvin McDonald</strong>), <strong>On</strong> (<strong>Olivier Bernhard</strong>; partnership with <strong>Roger Federer</strong>), <strong>Hoka</strong> (owned by <strong>Deckers Brands</strong>), and <strong>Skechers</strong> (<strong>Robert Greenberg</strong>), serving as key ecosystem players.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>FedEx Corporation</strong> is based in <strong>Memphis, Tennessee, U.S.</strong>; <strong>Atlanta</strong>), <strong>DHL Express</strong>, <strong>Amazon Logistics</strong>, and <strong>USPS</strong> (Postmaster General <strong>Louis DeJoy</strong>) serve as primary <strong>FedEx</strong> competitors.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the primary U.S. macroeconomic indicators—for assessing tariff impacts—are (1) <strong>BLS CPI</strong> (<strong>Consumer Price Index</strong>), (2) <strong>BLS PPI</strong> (<strong>Producer Price Index</strong>), (3) <strong>BEA GDP</strong>, (4) <strong>BEA Personal Consumption Expenditures</strong> (<strong>PCE</strong>), (5) <strong>U.S. Census Bureau</strong> consumer spending, (6) <strong>Federal Reserve Beige Book</strong>, (7) <strong>ISM Manufacturing PMI</strong>, (8) <strong>ISM Services PMI</strong>, (9) <strong>Conference Board Consumer Confidence Index</strong>, and (10) <strong>U Mich Consumer Sentiment Index</strong>, serving as primary tracking indicators.
