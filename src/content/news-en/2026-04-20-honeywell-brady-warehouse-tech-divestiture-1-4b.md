<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Honeywell</strong> announced the sale of its <strong>Productivity Solutions and Services (PSS)</strong> business — the backbone of warehouse and distribution operations — to <strong>Brady Corporation</strong> for <strong>$1.4 billion</strong>. The unit generated about $1.1 billion in revenue in 2025.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The PSS portfolio includes critical daily warehouse equipment such as <strong>barcode scanners</strong>, <strong>mobile industrial computers</strong> and <strong>label printers</strong>. Wisconsin-based Brady, known for industrial labels and safety signs, aims to add mobile scanning hardware through this acquisition to build an integrated offering for warehouse and logistics customers.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The deal is viewed as a final step in Honeywell's plan to split its <strong>Aerospace</strong> and <strong>Automation</strong> businesses into two independent public companies in 2026. CEO Vimal Kapur also said the company is reviewing options for its <strong>Warehouse and Workflow Solutions</strong> unit, which includes <strong>Intelligrated</strong> and <strong>Transnorm</strong> brands.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. The transaction is valued at $1.4 billion.<br>
2. The PSS unit generated about $1.1 billion in revenue in 2025.<br>
3. Barcode scanners, mobile computers and label printers are included.<br>
4. Brady aims to build an integrated warehouse and logistics offering.<br>
5. Options are also being reviewed for the WWS unit (Intelligrated, Transnorm).</p>
