<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Port of Oakland</strong> achieved two critical milestones in 2024 in pursuit of its goal to become "the cleanest and greenest port in the United States." The port's <strong>Power Source Disclosure Report</strong>, verified by the California Energy Commission, revealed that <strong>86% of the electricity supplied to the port's tenants and facilities in 2024 came from renewable or zero-carbon sources</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the report's details, the energy portfolio comprised:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>62% from renewable sources</strong> (geothermal, solar, biomass),</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>24% from large-scale hydroelectric sources</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This level is equivalent to <strong>nearly twice</strong> California's <strong>44% Renewable Portfolio Standard</strong> target and positions Oakland among the leaders in sustainable energy use among U.S. ports.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>"This is a turning point"</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Andre Basler, Director of Public Utilities at Port of Oakland, emphasized that energy transformation is a strategic priority for the port:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"This achievement reflects our commitment to building a resilient, cost-effective and sustainable energy portfolio for the port and its tenants. Despite market challenges, we continue to focus on energy supply that reduces emissions and supports California's energy goals."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Basler's statement highlights Oakland's effort to not only consume renewable energy but also secure <strong>energy security and price stability</strong> over the long term.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Major step forward with energy storage agreement</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The port signed a <strong>20-year Energy Storage Service Agreement (ESSA)</strong> with <strong>Trolley Pass Project LLC</strong> to support renewable energy use. Through this agreement, Oakland secured <strong>4 MW of capacity in a 400 MW battery storage system in San Bernardino County</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The project, with a total cost not exceeding <strong>$13.3 million</strong>, will:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Balance the fluctuating output of renewable energy generation,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Reduce dependence on grid electricity,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Support California's goal of transitioning to 100% clean electricity by 2045 under <strong>Senate Bill 100</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Help the port meet its Resource Adequacy (RA) requirements.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This agreement is regarded as one of the most important steps in securing the port's long-term energy supply.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Regional collaborations strengthening</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Port of Oakland, as a publicly owned energy provider, is a member of the <strong>Northern California Power Agency (NCPA)</strong>. Through NCPA, the port collaborates with other regional public agencies on:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">energy sharing,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">sustainable pricing models,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">equipment modernization,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">scaling clean energy projects<br>
and similar initiatives.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This collaboration enables tenants to access more resilient and affordable energy while helping the port rapidly approach its carbon-neutral vision.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Port of Oakland sourced <strong>86% of its electricity from renewable/zero-carbon</strong> sources in 2024.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Portfolio composition: 62% renewable, 24% hydroelectric.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Achievement reached <strong>nearly twice</strong> California's RPS target.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Port entered into a <strong>20-year energy storage agreement</strong> with 4 MW capacity.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">ESSA will balance fluctuating renewable energy output and enhance energy security.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Oakland is expanding regional energy collaborations through its NCPA membership.</p></li>
</ul>
