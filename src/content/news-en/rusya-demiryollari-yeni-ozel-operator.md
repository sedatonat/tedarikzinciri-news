<p style="text-align: justify">Russia's rail freight transport sector is undergoing significant changes. The merger of <strong>Freight One (PGK)</strong>, <strong>Transfin-M</strong>, and <strong>Atlant</strong> is planned to establish a new private rail operator with a fleet of 200,000 wagons.&nbsp;</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Freight One (PGK)</strong> is known as Russia's largest private logistics company. <strong>Transfin-M</strong> operates as a leading leasing company, while <strong>Atlant</strong> functions as a rail operator. The merger of these three companies signals a major transformation in Russia's rail transport sector.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In recent years, Russia has made substantial investments in rail transport. Due to Western unilateral sanctions and instability in the Middle East, Russia is focusing on new transport corridors. In particular, it is expanding its targets in rail freight transport to facilitate trade with China. Russian Railways (<strong>RZD</strong>) plans to increase rail freight transport capacity to ports by 200 million tonnes annually over the coming years.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, construction has begun on Russia's first high-speed rail line. This route between Moscow and St. Petersburg is scheduled to enter service in the second quarter of 2028 and will have an annual passenger transport capacity of 23 million passengers. This project is viewed as a major turning point in the country's transport infrastructure.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">These developments in Russia's rail freight sector play a significant role in the country's economic and logistics strategies. The establishment of the new private operator could increase competition in the sector, improve service quality, and reduce transport costs. Furthermore, this merger could strengthen Russia's position on trade routes between Asia and Europe.​</p>
