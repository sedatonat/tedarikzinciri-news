<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Russia</strong> is entering a new phase in its strategy to evade Western sanctions. The country has for the first time conducted a <strong>ship-to-ship transfer (STS) of liquefied natural gas (LNG) via a "shadow fleet" in cooperation with a Chinese LNG carrier</strong>. Satellite imagery and <strong>Automatic Identification System (AIS)</strong> data with partially disabled signals indicate this operation took place <strong>off the coast of Malaysia on October 18, 2025</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The operation was conducted between the <strong>Perle</strong>, a vessel with a capacity of 170,471 m³, and the <strong>CCH Gas</strong>, with a capacity of 145,000 m³. The <strong>Perle</strong> was carrying its cargo from <strong>Gazprom's Portovaya LNG facility</strong>—a facility that <strong>was sanctioned by the United States in January 2025</strong> and has since struggled to find foreign buyers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This move is viewed as part of Russia's effort to <strong>remove its energy exports from Western oversight</strong>. The "dark transfer" method of transporting LNG on the high seas aims to obscure the cargo's origin, preventing buyer countries from facing sanctions consequences.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>First "Dark" LNG Operation: A New Milestone</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">This operation marks the <strong>first time LNG carriers</strong> are being used for the "dark transfer" tactics that Russia has frequently applied in crude oil shipping.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>TankerTrackers</strong> co-founder <strong>Samir Madani</strong> commented on the situation:<br>
"Yes, this is the first dark STS transfer of Russian LNG in this region. CCH Gas did a rather poor job of concealing its AIS signal."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Although Russia has previously conducted <strong>hundreds of STS operations in Norwegian and Russian waters</strong>, this incident is recorded as the <strong>first "dark" LNG transfer off Malaysia's coast</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>CCH Gas: An Aged Vessel Transferred from the West to China</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CCH Gas</strong>, the vessel involved in the operation, is an older <strong>LNG steamship built in 2006</strong>. During 2024–2025, this vessel was among those sold by Western operators to <strong>unnamed Chinese buyers</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The vessel's former name was <strong>Condor LNG</strong>, and it was divested in early 2025 by <strong>Greece-based TMS Cardiff</strong>. Currently registered under <strong>Hong Kong-based CCH-1 Shipping Co. Ltd.</strong>, it is managed by <strong>Shanghai-based Primepath Shipping Ltd.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This demonstrates that China is now using the elderly LNG fleet it has acquired from the West to help Russia circumvent sanctions. In effect, China has become a functional part of <strong>Russia's "shadow logistics network."</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sanctions Impact and Alternative Routes</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Gazprom</strong> has faced serious difficulties in LNG sales following sanctions on the Portovaya facility. Consequently, Russia is shifting towards covert transfer methods by <strong>diverting its energy exports outside official trade channels</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
Two months ago, Russia launched direct shipments from <strong>Novatek's Arctic LNG 2 project</strong> to China's <strong>Beihai Terminal</strong>. Since then, <strong>ten deliveries have been successfully completed.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Although the <strong>United Kingdom</strong> added Beihai Terminal to its sanctions list last week, China had anticipated this move and <strong>rerouted all LNG shipments to alternative terminals</strong>. Therefore, sanctions are not expected to be effective in the short term.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A Shadow Era in Global Energy Trade</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">This development reveals that <strong>Western sanctions are driving Russia towards shadow trading networks, with China playing an active role in these networks</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
The shutdown of AIS systems even for <strong>a commodity as traceable as LNG</strong> demonstrates the <strong>limitations of oversight mechanisms</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Furthermore, Russia's <strong>"dark fleet" strategy</strong> is expanding beyond oil shipping to also take root in <strong>the natural gas market</strong>. This raises serious questions regarding <strong>energy security, environmental oversight, and global trade transparency</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Ultimately, China's involvement in this dark LNG operation strengthens the energy alliance between the two countries while establishing <strong>a new model of economic cooperation that reduces the impact of U.S. and European sanctions</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">On <strong>October 18, 2025, off Malaysia's coast</strong>, the first "dark" <strong>LNG ship-to-ship transfer (STS)</strong> was conducted between the <strong>Perle</strong> and <strong>CCH Gas</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The operation involved sanctioned gas originating from <strong>Gazprom's Portovaya LNG facility</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CCH Gas</strong>, an older <strong>LNG steamship</strong> acquired from the West, is currently registered under <strong>Hong Kong-based CCH-1 Shipping Co. Ltd.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">According to <strong>TankerTrackers</strong> data, this operation with disabled AIS signals is recorded as the <strong>first dark STS example involving Russian LNG</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Russia is continuing <strong>LNG exports through its "shadow fleet" following Western sanctions.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Shipments from <strong>Novatek's Arctic LNG 2 project</strong> to China are proceeding in a similar manner.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The UK's sanctions on Beihai Terminal</strong> are proving ineffective as China has rerouted its shipments.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The development points to the <strong>growing role of shadow operations in global energy trade</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
