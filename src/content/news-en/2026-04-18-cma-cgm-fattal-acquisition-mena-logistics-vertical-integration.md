<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CMA CGM Group</strong> has signed an agreement to acquire 100 per cent of <strong>Fattal Group</strong>. The transaction, agreed with Fattal Group shareholders, remains subject to regulatory approvals and is expected to complete in <strong>Q3 2026</strong>. The deal aims to strengthen CMA CGM's position beyond maritime transport, extending its reach deeper into the <strong>logistics value chain</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">By integrating Fattal Group's established distribution platform, the company is expected to enhance cargo flows between <strong>ports, terminals, and final delivery points</strong>, particularly across fast-growing <strong>Middle East and North Africa (MENA)</strong> markets. For ports and terminal operators, the acquisition signals continued <strong>vertical integration</strong> by a major ocean carrier, with potential implications for cargo routing, hinterland connectivity, and supply chain visibility.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The deal aligns with broader industry trends where shipping lines deepen their involvement in <strong>landside operations</strong> to enhance end-to-end logistics capabilities. CMA CGM's move will allow the carrier to extend influence beyond mainline operations into <strong>inland transport</strong>, <strong>warehousing</strong>, and <strong>distribution</strong>. Once finalised, the transaction targets more integrated solutions for customers across the MENA market.</p>
