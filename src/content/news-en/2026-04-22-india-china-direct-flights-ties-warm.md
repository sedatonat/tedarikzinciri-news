<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>India</strong> and <strong>China</strong> are resuming direct flights after more than five years — with two of the world's largest economies seeking to rebuild political relations at a time of rising global trade uncertainties. The <strong>Indian Ministry of External Affairs</strong> said in an October 2 statement that air service with <strong>China</strong> could begin by month-end, pending commercial decisions by the designated carriers of both countries and compliance with other operational criteria. The move follows bilateral discussions earlier this year aimed at restoring air connectivity. <strong>Bloomberg News</strong> reported in August, citing people familiar with the matter, that direct flights were expected to resume shortly. Passenger flights between <strong>India</strong> and <strong>China</strong> were suspended following the <strong>COVID-19</strong> pandemic. Diplomatic relations between the two countries hit their lowest point after border clashes in 2020 — a connection that never resumed. Passengers between the two neighboring countries currently transit through hubs such as <strong>Hong Kong</strong> or <strong>Singapore</strong>. From a supply chain perspective, the <strong>Galwan Valley</strong> border clash (<strong>Aksai Chin</strong>; <strong>Ladakh</strong>) in June 2020 resulted in the deaths of at least 20 <strong>Indian</strong> soldiers and an unknown number of <strong>Chinese</strong> troops — causing relations between the two nuclear powers to sink to their lowest level in decades. <strong>S. Jaishankar</strong> is <strong>Indian External Affairs Minister</strong>. <strong>Wang Yi</strong> is <strong>Chinese Foreign Minister</strong> — meeting in <strong>New Delhi</strong> in August.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, diplomatic relations between the long-time rivals are rising at a time when <strong>U.S.</strong> tariffs are adding to economic uncertainty in the world's two most populous countries. In August, <strong>China</strong> and <strong>India</strong> agreed to facilitate bilateral trade and investment following foreign affairs minister talks in <strong>New Delhi</strong>. <strong>India</strong>'s largest carrier, <strong>IndiGo</strong>, said it will resume flights to <strong>China</strong> starting October 26 — launching daily service. <strong>InterGlobe Aviation</strong> (<strong>IndiGo</strong>) was founded in 2006 by <strong>Rahul Bhatia</strong> and <strong>Rakesh Gangwal</strong>, based in <strong>Gurugram India</strong> — becoming <strong>India</strong>'s dominant domestic and international airline — commanding over 60% of <strong>India</strong>'s domestic market share. <strong>Pieter Elbers</strong> is <strong>IndiGo</strong>'s CEO. <strong>Air India</strong> (<strong>Tata Group</strong> owned); <strong>Vistara</strong> (merging with <strong>Air India</strong>); <strong>SpiceJet</strong>; <strong>Akasa Air</strong> (founded by <strong>Rakesh Jhunjhunwala</strong>); <strong>Alliance Air</strong>; <strong>Air India Express</strong> are major <strong>Indian</strong> airlines. <strong>Air China</strong>; <strong>China Eastern Airlines</strong>; <strong>China Southern Airlines</strong>; <strong>Hainan Airlines</strong>; <strong>Xiamen Airlines</strong>; <strong>Sichuan Airlines</strong>; <strong>Shenzhen Airlines</strong>; <strong>Shandong Airlines</strong>; <strong>Spring Airlines</strong>; <strong>Juneyao Airlines</strong> are major <strong>Chinese</strong> airlines.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>India-China</strong> economic relations are navigating complex balances within major multilateral frameworks: the world's 2nd and 5th largest economies by nominal <strong>GDP</strong> (<strong>China</strong> and <strong>India</strong>); the largest two populations (1.4 billion+ each); and <strong>BRICS</strong> (<strong>Brazil</strong>, <strong>Russia</strong>, <strong>India</strong>, <strong>China</strong>, <strong>South Africa</strong>, and expanding <strong>BRICS+</strong> with <strong>Egypt</strong>, <strong>Ethiopia</strong>, <strong>Iran</strong>, <strong>UAE</strong>); the <strong>SCO</strong> (<strong>Shanghai Cooperation Organisation</strong>); <strong>RCEP</strong> (<strong>Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership</strong>; excluding <strong>India</strong>); and the <strong>QUAD</strong> (<strong>U.S.</strong>, <strong>Japan</strong>, <strong>India</strong>, <strong>Australia</strong>). <strong>India</strong>'s imports from <strong>China</strong> exceed $100 billion in 2024 — with main product categories being electronics, machinery, chemicals, pharmaceutical <strong>APIs</strong> (<strong>Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients</strong>), and mobile phone components. <strong>India</strong>'s exports to <strong>China</strong> are only $15-20 billion — with iron ore, cotton, granite, and seafood as main export categories. This large imbalance has been a long-standing concern of <strong>India</strong>'s <strong>Modi</strong> government.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>India</strong>'s rising role in global supply chains is driven by <strong>Make in India</strong> (launched 2014), <strong>PLI</strong> (<strong>Production Linked Incentive</strong>), <strong>Atmanirbhar Bharat</strong> (self-reliant India), the <strong>India Semiconductor Mission</strong>, <strong>Vande Bharat</strong> (domestic trains), <strong>Bharat Forge</strong>, <strong>Mahindra &amp; Mahindra</strong>, <strong>Tata Motors</strong>, <strong>Tata Steel</strong>, <strong>Reliance Industries</strong>, <strong>Adani Group</strong> (<strong>Adani Ports</strong>, <strong>Adani Logistics</strong>, <strong>APSEZ</strong>, <strong>Mundra Port</strong>), <strong>JSW Group</strong>, <strong>Jindal Steel</strong>, <strong>Wipro</strong>, <strong>Infosys</strong>, <strong>TCS</strong>, <strong>HCL Technologies</strong>, <strong>Tech Mahindra</strong>, <strong>Larsen &amp; Toubro</strong>, <strong>Bharat Heavy Electricals</strong>, <strong>Hindustan Aeronautics</strong>, <strong>Bharat Electronics</strong>, <strong>Hindustan Unilever</strong>, <strong>ITC</strong>, <strong>Asian Paints</strong> among leading industrial giants. <strong>Foxconn</strong>, <strong>Pegatron</strong>, <strong>Wistron</strong> (<strong>Tata Electronics</strong>), <strong>Apple</strong> suppliers are manufacturing iPhones in <strong>Tamil Nadu</strong>, <strong>Karnataka</strong>, <strong>Telangana</strong> and <strong>Maharashtra</strong>. Major <strong>Indian</strong> ports include <strong>Mundra</strong>, <strong>JNPT</strong> (<strong>Jawaharlal Nehru Port Trust</strong>; <strong>Nhava Sheva</strong>), <strong>Chennai</strong>, <strong>Visakhapatnam</strong>, <strong>Cochin</strong>, <strong>Kandla</strong>, <strong>Paradip</strong>, <strong>Tuticorin</strong>. The <strong>India-Middle East-Europe Economic Corridor</strong> (<strong>IMEC</strong>) was announced at the 2023 G20 Delhi — positioned as an alternative to <strong>China</strong>'s <strong>BRI</strong>. Ultimately, the resumption of <strong>India-China</strong> direct flights represents a notable concrete indicator of the rebalancing of global geoeconomics and <strong>India-China</strong> normalization.</p>
