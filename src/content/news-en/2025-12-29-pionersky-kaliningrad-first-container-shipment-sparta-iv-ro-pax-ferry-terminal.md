<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>new cruise and ro-pax ferry terminal of Pionersky in Kaliningrad region</strong> has handled its first test shipment of containers. According to the <strong>RF Federal Agency for Maritime and River Transport</strong>, the containers were delivered on board the <strong>"Sparta IV" dry cargo ship</strong> and unloaded from the vessel <strong>directly onto trucks</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">Using the terminal of Pionersky for container handling will <strong>improve the efficiency and reliability</strong> of transport services to the region. Kaliningrad is Russia's economic exclave surrounded by EU territory, with overland traffic periodically disrupted by sanctions and security inspections. Direct sea connections to the region are therefore a strategic priority for supply chain continuity.</p><p style="text-align: justify">During 2025, the Pionersky terminal handled <strong>18 ro-pax ferries</strong> loading and unloading a total of <strong>56,000 tons</strong> including more than <strong>2,500 ro-ro units</strong>. Ro-ro cargo includes trucks, tractors, trailers, and caravan vehicles. Rosmorrechflot's investments are gradually increasing the terminal's capacity in both freight and passenger transport. The new container handling capability has raised the terminal's overall logistical flexibility for the region.</p>
