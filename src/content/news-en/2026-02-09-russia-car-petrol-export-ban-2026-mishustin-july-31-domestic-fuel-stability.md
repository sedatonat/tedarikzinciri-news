<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Government of the Russian Federation</strong> has introduced a new ban on the export of car petrol, diesel fuel, and other fuel types. According to the Government press office, the ban was formalised through a Governmental Order signed by <strong>Premier Mikhail Mishustin</strong>. The ban on petrol export will remain in force <strong>until July 31, 2026</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The ban <strong>does not apply to car petrol manufacturers</strong>. Refineries and integrated oil companies can continue exporting the petrol they produce themselves. This exemption allows major players such as Rosneft, Gazprom Neft, and Lukoil to sustain their export activities. Independent traders and spot-trade operators are covered by the ban.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The decision aims at <strong>maintaining a stable situation in the home fuel market</strong>. Russian petrol prices were under upward pressure throughout 2025 driven by both military and civilian domestic demand. The export ban aims to stabilise pump prices by increasing supply in the domestic market. Similar petrol export bans were previously applied in 2023 and 2024, typically for 3-6 month periods. Russia's restriction on fuel exports creates price pressure in international markets, with Türkiye and Central Asian republics among the main affected markets.</p>

<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. Russia introduced a new export ban on petrol, diesel, and other fuel types.<br>
2. The ban is in force until July 31, 2026.<br>
3. The order was signed by Premier Mikhail Mishustin.<br>
4. Car petrol manufacturers (refineries) are excluded from the ban.<br>
5. The goal is to maintain price stability in the domestic fuel market.</p>
