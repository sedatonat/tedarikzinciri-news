<p style="text-align: justify">Saudi Arabia's Minister of Transport <strong>Al-Jasser</strong>, together with the <strong>Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority (ZATCA)</strong> and the <strong>Saudi Ports Authority (Mawani)</strong>, launched the <strong>Logistics Corridors Initiative</strong> to handle containers and cargo redirected from the Kingdom's Eastern Region ports to <strong>Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC)</strong> countries.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Within the scope of the initiative, the western coast's main gateway, <strong>Jeddah Islamic Port</strong>, plays a key role in receiving redirected shipments from the Eastern Region ports. Through <strong>bonded warehouse</strong> zones, importers and exporters gain operational flexibility by deferring customs and tax obligations.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">ZATCA's <strong>Eng. Abanmi</strong> emphasized that the corridors are an important step in integrating customs and logistics procedures. Mawani President <strong>Al-Mazroua</strong> said the infrastructure is fully capable of managing redirected cargo flows.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. The Logistics Corridors Initiative has been launched.<br>
2. Jeddah Islamic Port plays a key role for redirected cargo.<br>
3. Bonded warehouse zones provide operational flexibility.<br>
4. ZATCA is integrating customs and logistics procedures.<br>
5. Mawani infrastructure is fully ready for the redirected flows.</p>
