<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>India</strong> has secured <strong>over $7 billion in fresh investment commitments</strong> from <strong>DP World</strong> and <strong>A.P. Moller–Maersk Group's subsidiary APM Terminals</strong>, announced during the <strong>2025 India Maritime Week</strong>. This development provides significant momentum to the nation's goal of becoming <strong>a global maritime hub</strong> in line with its <strong>Maritime Amrit Kaal Vision 2047</strong> strategy.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>DP World's $5 billion additional investment</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Dubai-based DP World</strong> has announced a <strong>$5 billion new investment plan</strong> to strengthen its <strong>integrated logistics and supply chain network</strong> in India. This new funding increases the company's <strong>total investment commitment in India over the past 30 years to $8 billion</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">DP World stated that the investments will focus on <strong>strengthening multimodal connectivity, reducing logistics costs, and enhancing export competitiveness</strong>. The company also signed <strong>five new Memoranda of Understanding (MoU)</strong> with Indian maritime authorities.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">These MoUs cover:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Green coastal shipping</strong> – DP World's feeder operator <strong>Unifeeder</strong> will support sustainable coastal shipping projects with <strong>Sagarmala Finance Corporation</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ship repair infrastructure</strong> – <strong>Drydocks World</strong> and <strong>Cochin Shipyard Limited</strong> will expand <strong>ship maintenance facilities in Kochi</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Automated low-emission rail system</strong> – DP World will launch a pilot project with <strong>Deendayal Port Authority</strong> and <strong>Nevomo (MagRail)</strong> to develop India's <strong>first low-emission magnetic rail port track system</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Skills development and maritime mobility</strong> in new collaborative initiatives.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sultan Ahmed bin Sulayem</strong>, Group Chairman and CEO of DP World, commented on the investment:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"This investment reflects our shared vision of building a connected, secure, and self-reliant India."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Maersk's $2 billion Pipavav port expansion</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Meanwhile, <strong>APM Terminals Pipavav</strong> signed a <strong>$2 billion port expansion agreement</strong> with the <strong>Gujarat Maritime Board</strong>. This investment will be executed as part of <strong>A.P. Moller – Maersk's broader $5 billion infrastructure plan for India</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The project will significantly increase <strong>Pipavav Port's container, liquid cargo, and ro-ro (vehicle shipping) capacity</strong>. It will also strengthen the port's integration with the <strong>Dedicated Freight Corridor (DFC)</strong> rail line to support <strong>multimodal transportation</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Jon Goldner</strong>, CEO of APM Terminals Asia & Middle East, commented on the investment:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"This expansion will open new trade opportunities for Gujarat and India. Pipavav will become a model that meets global standards in efficiency and green growth."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Employment and regional development impact</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Pipavav Port project alone is expected to create <strong>approximately 25,000 new jobs</strong>. Combined with DP World's investments, this figure could <strong>exceed 40,000</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new port and logistics investments will contribute to India's ability to:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Expand its export capacity</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Transition to green transportation models that will reduce carbon emissions</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strengthen global competitiveness by reducing supply chain costs</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Global context</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, these investments form the cornerstones of India's strategy to become a <strong>"global maritime hub."</strong> The Indian government's <strong>Maritime Amrit Kaal Vision 2047</strong> plan aims for the country to become <strong>one of the world's five largest maritime trading economies by 2047</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new investment wave also aligns with India's plan to <strong>add 430 new ships (valued at $26 billion)</strong>. These developments indicate that the country is undergoing a major transformation centered on <strong>ports, shipbuilding, logistics, and green energy</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Highlights:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>DP World</strong> is investing an additional <strong>$5 billion in India</strong>, raising its total commitment to <strong>$8 billion</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>APM Terminals (Maersk)</strong> will invest <strong>$2 billion in Pipavav Port</strong> to expand capacity.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Total investment amount: Over $7 billion.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The investments include <strong>green coastal shipping, ship repair, automated rail systems, and multimodal connectivity</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The projects will create <strong>approximately 25,000–40,000 new jobs</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">These initiatives represent <strong>India's largest leap forward in achieving its Maritime Amrit Kaal Vision 2047 goals</strong> toward becoming <strong>a global maritime powerhouse</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
