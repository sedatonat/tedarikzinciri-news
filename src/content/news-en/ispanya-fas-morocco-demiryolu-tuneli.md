<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The project to link Spain and Morocco with a rail tunnel across the Strait of Gibraltar</strong>, while technically feasible, has been assessed as <strong>"extremely complex" due to engineering, geological, and economic challenges.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This conclusion emerged from the first findings of <strong>a feasibility study conducted by the Spanish government</strong>, as reported by local media. According to the report, the project <strong>can be executed with advanced engineering solutions</strong>, but entails <strong>an unprecedented level of difficulty</strong> in terms of cost, timeline, and infrastructure risks.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tunnel to be decided in 2027</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Authorities have announced that <strong>a final decision will be made in 2027</strong> following the completion of feasibility studies.</p>
<p><br>
Throughout this process, both <strong>Spain's Ministry of Transport, Mobility and Urban Agenda (MITMA)</strong> and <strong>Morocco's Ministry of Equipment and Water</strong> are jointly conducting technical and economic assessments.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The project aims to strengthen Europe-Africa connections and <strong>create a new land transport corridor across the Mediterranean.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Geological and engineering challenges</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the feasibility report, the primary challenge is <strong>the geological structure and depth of the Strait of Gibraltar.</strong></p>
<p><br>
At its narrowest point, the strait stretches <strong>28 kilometres</strong>, with sea depths reaching <strong>approximately 300 metres.</strong> These conditions require specialized engineering solutions due to <strong>high water pressure</strong>, <strong>seismic activity risk</strong>, and <strong>complex geotechnical conditions.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The report highlights <strong>the route between Spain's Tarifa region</strong> and <strong>Morocco's Tangier (Tanca) city</strong> as a potential tunnel corridor.</p>
<p><br>
Under this route, the tunnel is planned as <strong>a double-track railway system.</strong> Additionally, <strong>ventilation, emergency exit galleries, and maintenance tunnels</strong> would be integrated into the infrastructure.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A strategic project linking Europe and Africa</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">If realized, the Spain–Morocco tunnel would <strong>directly connect the European and African continents by rail for the first time.</strong></p>
<p><br>
The project would link to European TEN-T networks <strong>via the Malaga–Algeciras rail line</strong> and <strong>provide direct access to North African logistics hubs.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, once completed, the tunnel would spark <strong>a revolution not just between the two nations, but in trade and transport corridors across Europe, the Mediterranean, and Africa.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Economic and environmental dimensions</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Beyond technical feasibility, <strong>economic viability</strong> remains a subject of debate.</p>
<p><br>
Initial estimates suggest the project would cost <strong>between €20 and €25 billion.</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br>
Experts note that an investment of this magnitude would need to be financed through <strong>the European Union, the African Development Bank,</strong> and <strong>public-private partnerships.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From an environmental standpoint, the tunnel is expected to <strong>provide a transport alternative that reduces carbon emissions.</strong> The <strong>rail connection is projected to significantly lower emissions</strong> compared to road and air transport.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Political dimension and regional implications</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Relations between Spanish and Moroccan governments have been reinvigorated through cooperation agreements signed in 2022. The tunnel project is now viewed as <strong>a flagship initiative symbolizing this new era of bilateral relations.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, some European analysts approach the project with caution regarding <strong>political risks</strong> and <strong>regional instability.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Notably, <strong>infrastructure security in North Africa</strong> and <strong>financing sustainability</strong> are among the factors that could complicate the project.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: Technically feasible, but a challenging path</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The feasibility report's conclusion: <strong>"The Strait of Gibraltar rail tunnel can be technically constructed, but presents unprecedented engineering and financial challenges."</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The decision to be made in 2027 will either establish this project as <strong>a symbol of Europe–Africa integration</strong> or <strong>record it in history as one of logistics' most ambitious concepts.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
