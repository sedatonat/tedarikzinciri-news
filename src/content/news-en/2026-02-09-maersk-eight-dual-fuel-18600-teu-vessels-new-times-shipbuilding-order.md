<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>A.P. Moller – Maersk (Maersk)</strong> has signed an order for eight large vessels with China-based <strong>New Times Shipbuilding Co. Ltd.</strong> All eight ships will have the same characteristics and make up a new series of <strong>18,600 TEU</strong> vessels, with delivery in 2029 and 2030. At <strong>366 metres in length and 58.6 metres in breadth</strong>, these vessels are more compact than the current maximum container vessel length of 400 metres.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The ships will be equipped with <strong>dual-fuel engines</strong> able to operate on conventional bunker fuel or liquefied gas. Following this new order, Maersk now has <strong>33 vessels on order</strong>, with four scheduled for delivery in the remainder of 2026. Anda Cristescu, Head of Chartering & Newbuilding at Maersk, said the agreement is part of the carrier's "ongoing fleet renewal" programme.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The order embodies Maersk's <strong>fleet transformation strategy toward the 2030s</strong>; dual-fuel options offer both operational flexibility within the existing bunker fuel chain and a transition path to future low-carbon fuels. The more compact size preserves operational flexibility by maintaining port compatibility while increasing capacity. The deal can also be read as Maersk's strategic response to the <strong>Hapag-Lloyd–ZIM merger</strong> in the global container capacity race.</p>
