<p>Following the Dieselgate scandal, automakers are facing legal proceedings in the UK within the scope of class-action lawsuits.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>The lawsuits are based on allegations that emission values were artificially lowered through software manipulation. From a supply chain perspective, strengthening compliance by design, supplier software validation, and regulatory reporting processes is critical. For manufacturers, the risks extend beyond financial penalties; they impact brand reputation, used vehicle values, and post-sale customer confidence. Experts note that the widespread adoption of Over-The-Air (OTA) updates and independent testing standards will enhance transparency.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Points:</strong><br>
1. Class-action lawsuits focus on emission manipulation.<br>
2. Compliance processes must include software validation.<br>
3. Risks are both financial and reputational.<br>
4. OTA updates provide transparency.<br>
5. Independent testing standards are strengthening.</p>
