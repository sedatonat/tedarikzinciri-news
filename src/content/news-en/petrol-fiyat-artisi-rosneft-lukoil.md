<p><strong>NEW YORK – Reuters (Scott DiSavino)</strong><br>
<strong>The United States' imposition of new sanctions on Russian energy giants Rosneft and Lukoil</strong> has triggered sharp volatility across global oil markets.</p>
<p><br>
Oil prices on Thursday <strong>jumped 5%, reaching their highest level in two weeks.</strong></p>
<p>The sanctions were <strong>imposed due to Russia's war in Ukraine.</strong></p>
<p><br>
This step has revived concerns about global supply tightening — as <strong>data from the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA)</strong> shows <strong>Russia was the world's second-largest crude oil producer in 2024.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>Global Supply Concerns Drive Prices Higher</strong></h5>
<p>Experts warn that U.S. sanctions could create <strong>a sudden contraction on the global supply side.</strong></p>
<p><br>
Russia is estimated to have produced <strong>over 10 million barrels per day in 2024.</strong><br>
The withdrawal of a portion of this volume from the market due to sanctions <strong>quickly pushed prices upward.</strong></p>
<p>Analysts reported that <strong>Brent crude oil rose by nearly $5 per barrel to around the $90 mark,</strong> while <strong>WTI (West Texas Intermediate)</strong> prices <strong>climbed to approximately the $85 level.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>Rosneft and Lukoil: The Two Energy Giants in the Crosshairs</strong></h5>
<p>At the center of these sanctions are <strong>Rosneft</strong> and <strong>Lukoil,</strong> which form the backbone of Russia's energy sector.</p>
<ul>
  <li><p><strong>Rosneft</strong> is Russia's largest state-controlled oil producer.</p></li>
  <li><p><strong>Lukoil</strong> is the country's largest private energy company and ranks second in export volume.</p></li>
</ul>
<p>These two companies represent <strong>more than half of Russia's total oil exports.</strong></p>
<p><br>
Thus, the sanctions have the potential to directly affect not only the Russian economy but also <strong>global energy flows.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>Markets Search for New Balance</strong></h5>
<p>Immediately following the sanctions, <strong>energy traders in Asia and Europe</strong> began exploring alternative supply channels to counter potential supply disruptions.</p>
<p><br>
It is reported that the U.S. and its allies have accelerated talks with <strong>Middle Eastern, African, and Latin American producers</strong> to replace Russian oil.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>This oil price increase has brought <strong>geopolitical risk premiums</strong> back into play, while <strong>cost pressures are expected to rise,</strong> particularly in energy-intensive sectors.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>Economic Effects: Inflation and Energy Security</strong></h5>
<p>The 5% increase in global oil prices could <strong>intensify inflationary pressures in oil-importing countries.</strong><br>
Although European nations have shifted toward alternative sources over the past two years to reduce their Russian energy dependence, <strong>supply tightening could trigger short-term price shocks.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p>On the U.S. side, energy analysts note that the effects should remain limited thanks to sustained high levels of domestic production, though <strong>temporary increases in gasoline and diesel prices</strong> may occur.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>Conclusion: Sanctions Reshape the Energy Balance</strong></h5>
<p>The U.S. sanctions imposed on Rosneft and Lukoil aim not only at <strong>limiting Russia's energy revenues</strong> but also at <strong>restructuring global energy supply chains.</strong></p>
<p><br>
However, in the short term, this move has <strong>increased supply concerns in the global oil market,</strong> pushing prices higher.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>According to experts, the market is expected to find <strong>a new equilibrium price</strong> in the coming weeks; however, <strong>geopolitical uncertainty</strong> and <strong>supply disruption risks</strong> could keep prices elevated.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li><p><strong>The U.S.</strong> has imposed <strong>sanctions</strong> on Russia's energy giants <strong>Rosneft</strong> and <strong>Lukoil</strong> due to the Ukraine war.</p></li>
  <li><p>Following the decision, oil prices <strong>rose 5%, reaching two-week highs.</strong></p></li>
  <li><p><strong>Russia,</strong> as of 2024, <strong>ranks as the world's second-largest crude oil producer.</strong></p></li>
  <li><p><strong>Brent oil</strong> climbed to $90, while <strong>WTI</strong> reached the $85 level.</p></li>
  <li><p>The sanctions are tightening <strong>global supply and driving energy prices higher.</strong></p></li>
  <li><p>Experts anticipate increased pressure on <strong>energy security and inflation.</strong></p></li>
  <li><p>Over the medium term, <strong>a search for new equilibrium in global energy supply chains</strong> will begin.</p></li>
</ul>
