<p>Panama President Laurentino Cortizo has made a forceful statement defending his country's sovereignty in response to a potential "takeover" threat regarding the Panama Canal raised by former U.S. President Donald Trump. He emphasizes that the Panama Canal is a crucial part of national pride and that any attempt by an external power to seize this strategic waterway will not be tolerated. In particular, Trump's comments regarding possible Chinese influence and references to the historical ties of the United States to the Panama Canal have been met with disapproval by Panama's administration.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Cortizo notes that the Panama Canal has long been operated by his country's state institutions and that every effort is being made to preserve its independence and integrity. He states that work carried out within the Panama Canal Authority (ACP) is focused on ensuring the security of international trade flows, and that domestic mechanisms are being kept strong against external interference or unilateral threats. He also acknowledges respect for the U.S. role in the past, but recalls that with the complete handover of the Panama Canal to Panama in 1999, a new era began in the country.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>From Cortizo's perspective, the management of the Panama Canal involves not only economic dimensions but also political and sovereignty aspects. Therefore, any hint of "recapture" or "takeover" from the United States is creating concern not only in Panama's domestic politics but also in regional diplomatic circles. Meanwhile, concerns arising from economic relations established with China, as part of global projects like the Belt and Road Initiative, are also being addressed. However, Cortizo rejects any interpretation of these relations as transferring control of the canal, and he repeatedly insists that no country has unilateral authority over the channel.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>On Trump's side, concerns are being raised about China's strategic moves globally. Due to the geographical and commercial importance of the Panama Canal, it is argued that indirect or direct control of this waterway by China would harm U.S. interests. In response, Cortizo asserts that both national and international law will be upheld, that the Panama Canal will continue to be operated in accordance with universal trade rules, and that his country has the strength to protect its own strategic assets.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>This tension sheds light on the current state of relations between Panama and the U.S., which have deep historical roots. While the U.S. held broad rights over the Panama Canal from 1904 to 1999, the canal's management passed entirely to Panama with the handover. This is considered an important symbol of national pride for the country. Therefore, Donald Trump's comments about reclaiming the Panama Canal or protecting it against Chinese threats contradict Panama's sovereignty claims. Cortizo emphasizes that such rhetoric has no legal or factual basis.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>The prevailing view in international circles is that the Panama Canal must maintain its neutrality as a global trade point beyond political disputes. Therefore, Cortizo's statements are seen as reassuring both the Panamanian people and global trade stakeholders. There is commentary suggesting that Trump's claims may be evaluated as a message directed at his own base within U.S. domestic politics. Nevertheless, the commercial operations of the Panama Canal and Panama's own management rights continue to be clearly protected despite all these political statements.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li><p>Donald Trump is concerned about Chinese influence over the Panama Canal.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Laurentino Cortizo emphasizes that sovereignty rights over the canal belong to Panama.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The Panama Canal Authority is focused on ensuring the security of international trade.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Since 1999, control of the canal has been entirely under Panama's administration.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Trump's "takeover" language has been met with disapproval in Panama and across the region.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>---</p>\n<p><strong>News Link: </strong><a href="https://splash247.com/panama-president-hits-back-at-trump-over-canal-takeover-threat/"><u>https://splash247.com/panama-president-hits-back-at-trump-over-canal-takeover-threat/</u></a></p>\n<p>-------------------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
