<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> reports that, according to analyst insight, nearly every logistics or supply chain facility can benefit from modern automation. But how do decision-makers go about selecting the right vendor and right systems? Technology, in both software and hardware forms, plays an indispensable role in boosting the performance of logistics facilities today. Yet with so many options available, it is easy to become paralyzed in trying to determine what is best for you. This episode offers a guide to getting the most out of a technology acquisition &mdash; from initial assessment through implementation and follow-up &mdash; to maximize the return on investment from systems that can be quite expensive. The <strong>SupplyChainBrain Podcast</strong> series features weekly conversations with industry professionals and serves as a leading industry discussion forum.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the main <strong>warehouse automation</strong> technology categories are: (1) <strong>AS/RS</strong> (<strong>Automated Storage and Retrieval Systems</strong>; <strong>Daifuku</strong>, <strong>Dematic</strong>, <strong>SSI Schaefer</strong>, <strong>Mecalux</strong>, <strong>Murata Machinery</strong>, <strong>Vanderlande</strong>); (2) <strong>Goods-to-Person</strong> (<strong>AutoStore</strong>, <strong>Exotec Skypod</strong>, <strong>Symbotic</strong>, <strong>Geek+</strong>); (3) <strong>Robotic Picking</strong> (<strong>Berkshire Grey</strong>, <strong>Covariant</strong>, <strong>Righthand Robotics</strong>, <strong>Plus One Robotics</strong>); (4) <strong>AMRs</strong> (<strong>Autonomous Mobile Robots</strong>; <strong>Locus Robotics</strong>, <strong>Fetch Robotics</strong> (<strong>Zebra</strong>), <strong>6 River Systems</strong> (<strong>Ocado</strong>), <strong>Geek+</strong>, <strong>Quicktron</strong>); (5) <strong>AGVs</strong> (<strong>Automated Guided Vehicles</strong>); (6) <strong>Conveyors and sortation</strong> (<strong>Honeywell Intelligrated</strong>, <strong>Bastian Solutions</strong>, <strong>Interroll</strong>); (7) <strong>Voice picking</strong> (<strong>Honeywell Vocollect</strong>, <strong>Lucas Systems</strong>, <strong>Voiteq</strong>); (8) <strong>RFID</strong>/<strong>barcode scanning</strong> (<strong>Zebra Technologies</strong>, <strong>Honeywell Productivity Solutions</strong>, <strong>Cognex</strong>); (9) <strong>WCS</strong> (<strong>Warehouse Control Systems</strong>); and (10) <strong>WES</strong> (<strong>Warehouse Execution Systems</strong>).</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the main <strong>warehouse automation</strong> integrators and consulting firms include <strong>Bastian Solutions</strong> (owned by <strong>Toyota Industries</strong>), <strong>Dematic</strong> (owned by <strong>KION Group</strong>; CEO <strong>Mike Larsson</strong>), <strong>Honeywell Intelligrated</strong> (a <strong>Honeywell</strong> division), <strong>Daifuku</strong> (President <strong>Yoshitaka Kume</strong>; <strong>Osaka, Japan</strong>), <strong>Vanderlande</strong> (owned by <strong>Toyota Industries</strong>; <strong>Netherlands</strong>), <strong>SSI Schaefer</strong> (<strong>Germany</strong>), <strong>Murata Machinery</strong>, <strong>Knapp</strong> (<strong>Austria</strong>), <strong>TGW Logistics</strong>, <strong>Witron</strong>, <strong>Swisslog</strong> (owned by <strong>KUKA</strong>), <strong>Mecalux</strong> (<strong>Spain</strong>), and <strong>System Logistics</strong>. Among next-generation automation players are <strong>Symbotic</strong> (Founder <strong>Rick Cohen</strong>; strategic partner with <strong>Walmart</strong>), <strong>Berkshire Grey</strong> (owned by <strong>SoftBank</strong>), <strong>AutoStore</strong> (CEO <strong>Mats Hovland Vikse</strong>; <strong>Norway</strong>), <strong>Exotec</strong> (CEO <strong>Romain Moulin</strong>; <strong>Lille, France</strong>), <strong>Locus Robotics</strong> (CEO <strong>Rick Faulk</strong>), <strong>GreyOrange</strong> (<strong>Samay Kohli</strong>), <strong>RightHand Robotics</strong>, and <strong>Geek+</strong>. Key industry ranking references include the <strong>Gartner Magic Quadrant for Warehouse Management Systems</strong> and the <strong>Gartner Magic Quadrant for Real-Time Transportation Visibility Platforms</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the main <strong>technology selection methodology</strong> phases are: (1) business requirements and process mapping; (2) <strong>RFI</strong>/<strong>RFP</strong> (<strong>Request for Information</strong>/<strong>Request for Proposal</strong>) preparation; (3) vendor short-list; (4) <strong>POC</strong> (<strong>Proof of Concept</strong>); (5) <strong>TCO</strong> (<strong>Total Cost of Ownership</strong>) analysis; (6) <strong>ROI</strong>/<strong>IRR</strong>/<strong>NPV</strong> financial analysis; (7) integration architecture (<strong>ERP</strong>, <strong>WMS</strong>, <strong>TMS</strong>, <strong>OMS</strong>); (8) agile implementation; (9) change management and training; and (10) continuous improvement and <strong>KPI</strong> monitoring. The <strong>Material Handling Industry of America</strong> (<strong>MHI</strong>; CEO <strong>John Paxton</strong>) is the leading U.S. material handling trade organization, with <strong>MODEX</strong> and <strong>ProMat</strong> being the two major biennial trade shows. Additional key U.S. warehouse industry organizations include the <strong>Council of Supply Chain Management Professionals</strong> (<strong>CSCMP</strong>), the <strong>Warehouse Education and Research Council</strong> (<strong>WERC</strong>; President <strong>Susan Rider</strong>), and the <strong>International Warehouse Logistics Association</strong> (<strong>IWLA</strong>). The <strong>WERC DC Measures</strong> is an annual distribution center performance benchmark survey. In summary, <strong>SCB</strong>'s technology selection podcast underscores that warehouse automation investment decisions globally are becoming fundamentally disciplined &mdash; <strong>POC</strong>/<strong>TCO</strong> analysis and <strong>WMS</strong>+<strong>WCS</strong>+robotics integration appear to be the primary strategic priorities for supply chain managers.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. <strong>SCB Podcast</strong> is a technology and automation selection guide.<br>
2. <strong>AS/RS</strong>, <strong>Goods-to-Person</strong>, and <strong>AMRs</strong> are leading automation categories.<br>
3. <strong>Dematic</strong>, <strong>Daifuku</strong>, <strong>Vanderlande</strong>, and <strong>SSI Schaefer</strong> are major integrators.<br>
4. <strong>Symbotic</strong>, <strong>AutoStore</strong>, and <strong>Locus</strong> are leading next-generation players.<br>
5. <strong>RFP</strong>, <strong>POC</strong>, <strong>TCO</strong>, and <strong>ROI</strong> are key selection methodology phases.</p>
