<p style="text-align: justify">French maritime giant <a href="https://www.cma-cgm.com/"><u><strong>CMA CGM</strong></u></a> plans to invest $20 billion in the United States over the next four years. This investment aims to expand the company's presence in the U.S. and revitalize the country's maritime sector. <strong>CMA CGM</strong> CEO <strong>Rodolphe Saadé</strong> announced the plan at the White House alongside U.S. President <strong>Donald Trump</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Investment Details</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CMA CGM</strong>'s $20 billion investment is allocated as follows:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>$8 billion</strong>: Container vessels</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>$7 billion</strong>: Logistics</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>$4 billion</strong>: Ports</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>$1 billion</strong>: Air cargo</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">The investment is expected to create 10,000 new jobs in the United States.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Shipbuilding and Expansion of the U.S.-Flagged Fleet</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CMA CGM</strong> plans to increase the number of U.S.-flagged vessels from 10 to 30. The company is in discussions with U.S. shipyards for the construction of mid-size container vessels with 6,000 TEU capacity. This move aligns with efforts to revitalize the U.S. shipbuilding sector.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Air Cargo and Logistics Expansion</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The company plans to establish an air cargo hub in Chicago, which will be supported by five new <strong>Boeing 777</strong> cargo aircraft. Additionally, <strong>CMA CGM</strong> aims to modernize approximately 200 of its warehouses in the U.S. and open a research and development center in the Boston area.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Trump Administration's Approach to the Maritime Sector</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">President <strong>Trump</strong> continues efforts to revitalize the U.S. maritime industry and weaken China's dominance in the sector. The administration is preparing an executive order that would impose fees on China-linked vessels at U.S. ports. <strong>Trump</strong> also indicated that he would soon announce a new program containing incentives for shipbuilding in the United States.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CMA CGM</strong> will invest $20 billion in the United States over the next four years.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Investment breakdown: $8 billion for container vessels, $7 billion for logistics, $4 billion for ports, and $1 billion for air cargo.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">10,000 new jobs are expected to be created in the United States.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The company aims to increase the number of U.S.-flagged vessels from 10 to 30.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">An air cargo hub will be established in Chicago and an R&amp;D center will be opened in Boston.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">President <strong>Trump</strong> is planning new incentive programs to revitalize the U.S. maritime industry.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This investment will significantly increase <strong>CMA CGM</strong>'s presence in the United States and strengthen the country's maritime sector.</p>
