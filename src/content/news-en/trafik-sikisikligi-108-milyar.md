<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>American Transportation Research Institute (ATRI)</strong> research shows that traffic congestion cost the U.S. trucking sector approximately $108 billion in 2021. This situation negatively affects the industry's productivity and profitability.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The research examined in detail the impact of traffic congestion on trucking operations. Particularly in major cities and heavily congested areas, the time trucks spend in traffic has increased significantly. This leads to longer delivery times, increased fuel consumption, and rising operational costs.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to <strong>ATRI</strong> data, trucks experienced a total delay of 1.2 billion hours due to traffic congestion in 2021. This is equivalent to the annual work hours of approximately 425,000 full-time drivers. These delays reduce workforce productivity in the sector and create additional costs.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Major metropolitan areas experiencing the heaviest congestion include <strong>Los Angeles</strong>, <strong>New York</strong>, <strong>Chicago</strong>, and <strong>Atlanta</strong>. The main highways and intersections in these cities are identified as the most problematic points for trucking operations.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The research also highlights the environmental impact of traffic congestion. As trucks spend longer periods in traffic, fuel consumption increases and consequently carbon emissions rise. This situation negatively contributes to environmental pollution and climate change.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>ATRI</strong>'s report emphasizes that in addition to the economic effects of traffic congestion, there are also negative impacts on driver job satisfaction and safety. Extended work hours and increased stress can lead to driver dissatisfaction and even departures from the industry.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">To address these challenges, increased infrastructure investments, improved traffic management systems, and promotion of alternative transportation methods are recommended. Additionally, through the use of technological innovations and digital solutions, the goal is to make traffic flow more efficient.​</p>
<p><br></p>

<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Traffic congestion cost the U.S. trucking sector approximately $108 billion in 2021.​</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Trucks experienced a total delay of 1.2 billion hours due to traffic congestion.​</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">These delays are equivalent to the annual work hours of approximately 425,000 full-time drivers.​</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Los Angeles, New York, Chicago, and Atlanta are among the areas experiencing the heaviest traffic congestion.​</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Traffic congestion increases fuel consumption and carbon emissions, leading to environmental problems.​</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Driver job satisfaction and safety are negatively affected by traffic congestion.​</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">To address these issues, infrastructure investments, improved traffic management systems, and use of technological innovations are recommended.</p></li>
</ul>
