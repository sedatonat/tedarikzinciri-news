<p><strong>American Bureau of Shipping (ABS)</strong> President and CEO <strong>Christopher J. Wiernicki</strong> highlighted the critical role of maritime shipping in the U.S. sustainable transport future at a Sustainable Freight Transportation Workshop held at the White House.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Wiernicki emphasized that sustainability programs in the transport sector typically focus on specific modes of transport, but what truly matters is a holistic systems approach that encompasses all transportation modes from the point of origin to the point of destination.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Addressing the advantages of maritime shipping, Wiernicki noted that this mode of transport produces lower emissions per ton-mile compared to others, is cost-effective, and has the capacity to carry large-scale cargoes.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>He also stated that maritime shipping demonstrates less vulnerability to infrastructure-damaging events such as natural disasters.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>However, he drew attention to the necessity of close cooperation with other transport modes, given that maritime shipping is slower and has limited delivery points.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>ABS</strong> addresses the sustainability of cargo transportation from a broad perspective, aiming to reduce the carbon footprint of cargoes throughout maritime shipping and all intermodal systems.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>In this context, the <strong>U.S. Maritime Administration's Office of Environment and Innovation</strong> has tasked <strong>ABS</strong> with establishing and operating the <strong>U.S. Maritime Innovation Center</strong> under a five-year cooperation agreement.</p>\n<p>Authorized by Congress, this center will conduct comprehensive research, development, and training programs to support the use of clean energy in U.S. vessels.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Wiernicki stated that the center will play a central role in developing sustainable U.S. transportation systems, serving as an important resource in industry leadership, the formation of cargo-focused collaborative working groups, and the implementation of freight mode plans for maritime shipping.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>The Sustainable Freight Transportation Workshop was held with the participation of institutions such as the <strong>U.S. Department of Energy</strong>, the <strong>Environmental Protection Agency</strong>, the <strong>Department of Transportation</strong>, and the <strong>Joint Office of Energy and Transportation</strong>, as well as representatives from industry, environmental organizations, and government.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>The workshop brought together representatives from the maritime, rail, and trucking transportation sectors, state and federal government officials, infrastructure providers, ports, and environmental and labor organizations.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li><p><strong>ABS</strong> President and CEO <strong>Christopher J. Wiernicki</strong> highlighted the critical role of maritime shipping in the U.S. sustainable transport future.</p></li>\n  <li><p>Maritime shipping produces lower emissions per ton-mile compared to other transport modes and has the capacity to carry large-scale cargoes.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>ABS</strong> adopts a comprehensive approach to enhance the sustainability of cargo transportation.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The <strong>U.S. Maritime Administration</strong> has tasked <strong>ABS</strong> with establishing and operating the <strong>U.S. Maritime Innovation Center</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The Sustainable Freight Transportation Workshop brought together representatives from various sectors and government levels.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>---</p>\n\n<p>-------------------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
