<p style="text-align: justify">The Russian Federation Government has begun imposing customs duties on the export of certain leguminous products.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This new regulation marks a significant shift in Russia's agricultural sector. Leguminous crops occupy an important place in Russia's agricultural production and exports. Products such as peas, chickpeas, and lentils are consumed in the domestic market and also sold abroad.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">One of the underlying reasons for introducing export duties is to increase supply in the domestic market and ensure price stability. In recent years, increased exports of leguminous products have created supply shortages in the domestic market, leading to higher prices. The government aims to use the export duty to redirect producers toward the domestic market, thereby ensuring consumers access to these products at more affordable prices.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Furthermore, this measure is considered part of Russia's agricultural policy framework to support domestic production and enhance food security. Abundant supplies of leguminous products in the domestic market are important for meeting the needs of both consumers and the food processing sector.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The implementation of export duties may also have implications for international trade relations. Countries importing leguminous products from Russia may face difficulties in procurement or encounter higher costs. This situation could lead to price fluctuations in the global legume market and changes in supply chains.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Meanwhile, Russia's decision sends signals that similar policies could be applied to other agricultural products. In fact, export duties have previously been imposed on products such as sunflower and soybeans, and these measures have been extended through 2026.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Such measures are viewed as part of Russia's efforts to increase self-sufficiency in its agricultural sector and reduce dependence on imports of strategic agricultural products. However, the long-term effects of these policies and their implications for international trade are important issues that warrant continued monitoring.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
