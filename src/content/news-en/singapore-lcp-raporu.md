<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>DNV</strong> and <strong>Menon Economics</strong> have released the inaugural <strong>Leading Container Ports of the World (LCP)</strong> report, positioning <strong>Singapore</strong> as the world's leading container port. The new index evaluates modern container ports across five core pillars by integrating multidimensional criteria including the scale of global maritime trade flows, port capacity, digitalization, environmental performance, and infrastructure quality.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In the report's overall rankings, <strong>Shanghai</strong> ranks second and <strong>Ningbo-Zhoushan</strong> third. <strong>Rotterdam</strong> and <strong>Busan</strong> round out the top five. According to the study, maritime shipping carries approximately <strong>90%</strong> of global trade volume, while container ports process more than <strong>80%</strong> of non-bulk trade, underscoring their critical role.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The report examines port transformation across three main dynamics: rising trade volumes, technological advances, and sustainability pressures aligned with climate objectives. These factors are reshaping the long-term competitiveness of container ports.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The analysis evaluated <strong>160 ports</strong> and examined performance across <strong>35 indicators</strong>. These indicators are grouped into five pillars covering <strong>infrastructure</strong>, <strong>operations</strong>, <strong>connectivity</strong>, <strong>governance</strong>, and <strong>sustainability</strong> stages. Indicators include metrics such as <strong>throughput volumes</strong>, <strong>berth productivity</strong>, <strong>emissions per TEU</strong>, and <strong>alternative fuel availability</strong>. The evaluation also incorporates expert insights from industry stakeholders. The report is noted as complementary to the long-running <strong>Leading Maritime Cities of the World</strong> index.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">DNV Maritime CEO <strong>Knut Ørbeck-Nilssen</strong> stated that container ports handle more than <strong>930 million TEU</strong> annually and must make strategic investments to scale to this magnitude. The report presents four core recommendations for port operators: <strong>scalable infrastructure</strong>, <strong>accelerated digitalization</strong>, <strong>sustainability measures</strong>, and <strong>strong customer relationships</strong>. Port authorities are noted as playing a central role in the transformation, with many ports directing new investments toward closing performance gaps.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Menon Economics Partner <strong>Dr. Erik Jakobsen</strong> observed that top-ranking ports differentiate themselves through capacity expansion, automation, digital tools, integrated transport connectivity, and clean energy solutions.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Singapore's leadership in the report is attributed to its strong performance across all five pillars. The city's position reflects <strong>infrastructure</strong>, <strong>governance</strong>, <strong>connectivity</strong>, <strong>automation</strong>, and <strong>early alternative-fuel investments</strong> as critical factors. Shanghai's strong ranking is explained by its 2024 throughput of <strong>51.5 million TEU</strong>, advanced network connectivity, and sustainability initiatives. Ningbo-Zhoushan stands out as a rapidly expanding logistics hub under China's <strong>Belt and Road Initiative</strong>; the port handled <strong>39.3 million TEU</strong> in 2024 and demonstrated <strong>26%</strong> growth over three years.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Regional leaders include <strong>New York & New Jersey</strong>, <strong>Hamburg</strong>, <strong>Tanger Med</strong>, <strong>Jebel Ali</strong>, and <strong>Sydney</strong>. These ports demonstrate strong performance in connectivity, modernization, and multimodal transport integration within their respective geographies.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>DNV</strong> is an independent institution providing <strong>assurance</strong>, <strong>risk management</strong>, <strong>certification</strong>, and technical advisory services across various sectors, including maritime and energy. <strong>Menon Economics</strong> is recognized as a research organization conducting international projects in economic analysis, sector modeling, and strategy development.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The newly released LCP report redefines competitive dynamics among global container ports and provides essential data sets to guide port investment decisions moving forward.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Highlights:</strong></h3>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Singapore was named the world's leading container port in the LCP report.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Shanghai ranks second, Ningbo-Zhoushan third; the top five is completed by Rotterdam and Busan.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The report evaluates 160 ports across 35 indicators.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Singapore achieved high scores across all five pillars.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Shanghai handled 51.5 million TEU, while Ningbo-Zhoushan processed 39.3 million TEU.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Regional leaders include New York–New Jersey, Hamburg, Tanger Med, Jebel Ali, and Sydney.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>----------</p>\n<p><strong>News Link: </strong><a href="https://en.portnews.ru/news/385091/"><u>https://en.portnews.ru/news/385091/</u></a></p>\n<p>--------------------</p>\n<p><strong>Author: </strong><a href="http://sedatonat.com/"><u>SedatOnat.com</u></a></p>\n<p>--------------------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
