<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The world's three largest container shipping giants — MSC, Maersk and CMA CGM — are preparing to reflag their vessels under the Indian flag to capitalize on the country's cabotage laws and rapidly expanding coastal trade.</strong></p>
<p><br>
This strategic move aligns directly with <strong>India's "Maritime India Vision 2030" plan, which aims to strengthen the country's maritime sector on the global stage.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>CMA CGM Led the Way, MSC and Maersk Follow</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">In early 2025, <strong>French carrier CMA CGM</strong> became the first global liner company to take this step by registering four vessels with <strong>the Indian Register.</strong></p>
<p><br>
This decision came following <strong>Prime Minister Narendra Modi's official visit to CMA CGM's headquarters in Marseille.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">CMA CGM has since taken a step further, announcing plans to <strong>build container vessels in India</strong> — a development viewed as potentially positioning India's shipbuilding industry in the international league.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Maersk: First Vessels Registered</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to <strong>maritime database Equasis</strong>, <strong>Maersk Vilnius</strong> is now operating under the <strong>Indian flag.</strong></p>
<p><br>
Local sources report that a second vessel, <strong>Maersk Vigo</strong>, is also undergoing the <strong>reflagging process.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This move will give <strong>Copenhagen-based Maersk</strong> direct access to cabotage operations in Indian waters.</p>
<p><br>
Under India's cabotage regulations, <strong>only Indian-flagged vessels have priority rights in domestic cargo transportation.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>MSC's Move Expected During India Maritime Week</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>MSC (Mediterranean Shipping Company), the world's largest container line,</strong> is expected to make a similar announcement during <strong>India Maritime Week, scheduled for next week.</strong><br>
The company is anticipated to register some of its vessels with <strong>the Indian Register,</strong> both to <strong>strengthen its presence in the local trade network</strong> and to <strong>contribute to the country's tonnage capacity.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Reflagging: Economic and Strategic Impact</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Dr. Asha Pillai,</strong> Strategy Director at <strong>Docker Vision,</strong> a port automation firm, evaluated this development on <strong>LinkedIn</strong> as follows:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"This is not merely a symbolic move.<br>
Each re-registered vessel increases India's national tonnage, keeps freight revenue within the country, and creates new opportunities for Indian seafarers, ports, and ship management companies."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Pillai emphasized that if this momentum continues, India's position in global maritime transportation will be reshaped, with policy objectives translating into concrete tonnage growth and strategic maritime presence.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Maritime India Vision 2030: Strategic Background</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The Indian government's "Maritime India Vision 2030"</strong> plan aims to comprehensively transform the country's maritime sector.<br>
The plan's main pillars include:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Port modernization and digitalization,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Expansion of coastal and inland waterways,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Investment in shipbuilding and maintenance industries,</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Increasing the global appeal of the Indian Register.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This strategy aims to position India as <strong>a regional maritime hub</strong> and as <strong>a strong logistics player in intra-Asian trade networks.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: India Charting Course as New Maritime Center</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The reflagging of vessels by global majors such as <strong>MSC, Maersk and CMA CGM</strong> is profoundly transforming <strong>India's cabotage market and maritime ecosystem.</strong></p>
<p><br>
These moves have the potential to invigorate a broad economic chain spanning <strong>local employment, port infrastructure, shipbuilding, and maritime services.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In line with <strong>its 2030 vision,</strong> India is now advancing towards becoming one of the centers not only <strong>for cargo traffic but also for maritime tonnage and ship management.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CMA CGM</strong> initiated the process by registering four vessels with <strong>the Indian Register.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Maersk</strong> reflagged <strong>Maersk Vilnius</strong> to the Indian flag; <strong>Maersk Vigo</strong> is in the process.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>MSC</strong> is expected to announce a similar move at <strong>India Maritime Week 2025.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Reflagging will increase <strong>India's national tonnage and local maritime revenue.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Dr. Asha Pillai:</strong> "Each Indian-flagged vessel represents employment growth and increased strategic capacity."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Maritime India Vision 2030</strong> focuses on port modernization, shipbuilding, and cabotage development.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">This trend has the potential to establish <strong>India as a new logistics center in Asian maritime trade.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
