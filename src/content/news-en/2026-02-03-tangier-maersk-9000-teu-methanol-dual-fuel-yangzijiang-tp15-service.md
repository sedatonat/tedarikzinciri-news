<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>A.P. Moller – Maersk</strong> has taken delivery of <strong>Tangier Maersk</strong>, the first of six mid-size 9,000 TEU vessels in a new series, at Yangzijiang Shipbuilding Group's yard in Jingjiang, China. Tangier Maersk features a dual-fuel engine capable of operating on <strong>methanol</strong> and will begin its maiden voyage calling Shanghai before entering Maersk's <strong>TP15 service</strong>, linking East Asia with the US Gulf Coast via the Panama Canal.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Yangzijiang delivered the vessel <strong>three months ahead of schedule</strong>. Maersk expects to take delivery of four more ships later this year, with the final vessel scheduled for early 2027. Anda Cristescu, Head of Chartering & Newbuilding at Maersk, said: "We are pleased to take delivery of Tangier Maersk, marking the first of six vessels in this new series," adding it will contribute to fleet renewal and the carrier's long-term decarbonisation goals.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Tangier Maersk's methanol dual-fuel capability is a concrete deployment of Maersk's <strong>low-emission shipping</strong> strategy; it provides both operational flexibility within volatile bunker fuel chains and a transition path to low-carbon fuels such as green methanol and <strong>bio-methanol</strong>. By bringing down the average fleet age, the vessels also progressively reduce Maersk's carbon intensity.</p>
