<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>PayCargo</strong> and <strong>Gnosis Freight</strong> are partnering with <strong>Hapag-Lloyd</strong>, one of the world's leading container shipping companies, to launch the new <strong>Container Payment Portal</strong> (CPP). This innovative solution delivers real-time container status updates to customers, streamlines invoicing and payment processes, and enables faster container release. As a result, cargo operation efficiency is improved and container dwell times are reduced.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Container Payment Portal</strong> serves as the primary interface for <strong>Hapag-Lloyd</strong> customers to manage compliance with the <strong>Ocean Shipping Reform Act of 2022 (OSRA)</strong> and to facilitate payment of import line demurrage fees for shipments to the United States. Powered by <strong>PayCargo</strong>'s industry-leading payment network and <strong>Gnosis Freight</strong>'s <strong>Container Lifecycle Management® Platform</strong>, the portal offers the following features:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Enhanced shipment visibility</strong> with real-time container status updates</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Automated processes</strong> with real-time OSRA-compliant invoice generation</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Faster container release</strong> through seamless electronic payments and fee hold removal</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Efficient issue management</strong> with integrated dispute resolution tools</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Enhanced tracking and planning</strong> with user-friendly container tracking list interface</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">By combining advanced payment processing with comprehensive container tracking, the portal represents a significant milestone in digitalization for the shipping industry. The <strong>CPP</strong> investment underscores <strong>Hapag-Lloyd</strong>'s commitment to customer-centric digital solutions and improves cargo flow at U.S. ports.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Jason Drouyor</strong>, Director of Regulatory Affairs at <strong>Hapag-Lloyd</strong>, noted that revisions to demurrage and detention rules made by the <strong>U.S. Federal Maritime Commission (FMC)</strong> in <strong>May 2024</strong> introduced new requirements for invoicing, timelines, and dispute processes for unreasonable charges. The <strong>Container Payment Portal</strong> has been designed to simplify compliance with these regulations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>CPP</strong> will be rolled out progressively throughout <strong>2025</strong> across all major U.S. ports, including <strong>Houston</strong>, <strong>Virginia</strong>, <strong>Charleston</strong>, <strong>Savannah</strong>, <strong>Wilmington</strong>, <strong>New York/New Jersey</strong>, <strong>Baltimore</strong>, <strong>Philadelphia</strong>, <strong>Miami</strong>, <strong>Mobile</strong>, <strong>New Orleans</strong>, <strong>Tampa</strong>, <strong>Jacksonville</strong>, <strong>Port Everglades</strong>, <strong>Seattle</strong>, <strong>Tacoma</strong>, <strong>Oakland</strong>, <strong>Long Beach</strong>, and <strong>Los Angeles</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Eduardo Del Riego</strong>, President and CEO of <strong>PayCargo</strong>, expressed excitement about partnering with <strong>Gnosis Freight</strong> to support <strong>Hapag-Lloyd</strong>. He emphasized that their aim is to simplify payment and data flow processes by delivering a seamless experience for all users. With this portal, <strong>Hapag-Lloyd</strong> customers will benefit from faster and more efficient cargo management and gain added value to their operations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Austin McCombs</strong>, CEO and co-founder of <strong>Gnosis Freight</strong>, noted that combining <strong>PayCargo</strong>'s payment solution with <strong>Gnosis Freight</strong>'s advanced <strong>Container Lifecycle Management® Platform</strong> represents a critical step in modernizing and streamlining import processes in the container shipping industry. He expressed enthusiasm about being part of this collaboration.</p>
<p><br></p>
