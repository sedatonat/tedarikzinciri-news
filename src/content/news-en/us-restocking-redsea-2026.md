<p style="text-align: justify">As the global container shipping sector approaches 2026, industry experts are focusing on two major unknowns: <strong>a potential restocking wave in the United States</strong> and <strong>the timing of a return to the Red Sea–Suez Canal route</strong>. These two dynamics will be decisive across the entire global network, from capacity management to freight rates.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Hapag-Lloyd CEO <strong>Rolf Habben Jansen</strong> stated in an online panel discussion that US retail and industrial inventories are <strong>at historically low levels</strong>, meaning a possible "restocking sprint" could push 2026 demand into <strong>turbo mode</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>1. US Restocking Wave: Can Suddenly Spike Demand</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Prolonged weak inventory levels in the US signal that a widespread replenishment process may be at the door.<br>
According to experts:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">If restocking begins, <strong>Trans-Pacific demand could suddenly surge</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Freight rates would rise rapidly,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Pressure on vessels and container equipment would increase,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">West Coast–East Coast cargo balances would shift.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Habben Jansen, this impact could be "<strong>very large and sudden</strong>," since throughout 2025 many US companies delayed inventory purchases due to high tariffs.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>2. Return to Red Sea–Suez Route: Could Upend Global Traffic Flow</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The second major uncertainty highlighted in the panel is <strong>when carriers will return to the Red Sea and Suez Canal</strong>.<br>
While the ceasefire with the Houthi group has brought temporary relief, major lines remain cautious.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Habben Jansen:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Hapag-Lloyd has a ready "return plan" in place,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">But is not yet planning to begin transits,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The route will not open until security, insurance costs, and stability become permanent.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">If lines collectively return to Suez:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Serious congestion</strong> could develop at European and Mediterranean ports,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Transhipment pressures increase</strong> at Middle East and South Asian ports,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Lines serving the US East Coast (EC) could experience <strong>schedule disruptions</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Ship routes operating via the Cape of Good Hope would be redistributed and <strong>global schedule stability</strong> would decline.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Experts note that the entire fleet shifting to Suez at once could create congestion "<strong>approaching 2021–Ever Given levels</strong>".</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>3. If Both Uncertainties Converge: 2026 Could Be Highly Volatile</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">If US restocking and Suez return occur together:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Cargo volumes could rise to <strong>15–25% higher</strong> than normal levels,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">European hinterland capacity would quickly become locked up,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Queues would extend at Asian loading ports,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Trans-Pacific costs would surge sharply,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Global freight rates would again become <strong>highly volatile</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Furthermore, since the global container ship fleet will continue to grow in 2026, the supply–demand balance could be <strong>highly variable</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>4. Hapag-Lloyd: "Our Plan Is Ready, but We're in No Rush"</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Habben Jansen said Hapag-Lloyd has a ready operational manual "sitting on the shelf" for a Suez return.<br>
However, the company will not make the transition until conditions mature.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">He also noted that 2026 priorities will be:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Efficiency in the Gemini Cooperation network,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Schedule reliability,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Fuel optimization<br>
</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Two major unknowns for 2026: <strong>US restocking</strong> and <strong>return to Suez</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">If restocking occurs, Trans-Pacific demand and freight rates could suddenly spike.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">A Suez return could create serious congestion at European, Asian, and East Coast ports.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Hapag-Lloyd says it has a ready plan for the return but will not move until security becomes clear.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">If both uncertainties materialize, 2026 could be <strong>extremely volatile</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
