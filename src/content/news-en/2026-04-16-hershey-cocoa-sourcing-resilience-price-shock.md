<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Hershey</strong> is making its cocoa supply more resilient by diversifying sourcing and building stronger supplier relationships, according to Chief Supply Chain Officer (<strong>CSCO</strong>) <strong>Jason Reiman</strong>. That effort is being supported by growing cocoa production beyond market leaders the <strong>Ivory Coast</strong> and <strong>Ghana</strong> to other countries of origin, such as <strong>Ecuador</strong> and <strong>Brazil</strong>, giving <strong>Hershey</strong> more ways to keep its supply chain robust. "Once we have a resilient supply network, we can turn it to how we think about an agile network," <strong>Reiman</strong> said during <strong>Hershey</strong>'s <strong>Investor Day</strong> last month, noting the company is also evaluating cocoa alternatives.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">To keep cocoa costs in check, <strong>Hershey</strong> has locked in prices through <strong>hedging</strong> contracts while relying on a disciplined <strong>commodities governance</strong> framework to avoid outsized risk. "We have a sophisticated tool set for hedging," <strong>Reiman</strong> said, noting <strong>Hershey</strong> combines derivatives, market intelligence and formal oversight of its commodity positions to reduce volatility and maintain prices that are competitive versus its competitors. Beyond these financial tools, <strong>Hershey</strong> utilizes social initiatives to help secure long-term, affordable cocoa supplies; for example, the company's <strong>Income Accelerator Program</strong>, used with farmers in the <strong>Ivory Coast</strong>, promotes good agricultural practices, higher productivity, and improved family income.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Additional levers for securing supply include <strong>R&D</strong> on cacao tree resilience to disease and drought. <strong>Hershey</strong> is also investing in "<strong>chocolate resiliency</strong>" by modernizing production; the company opened its first "fully digitally integrated facility" last year. The 250,000-square-foot chocolate processing facility in <strong>Hershey, Pennsylvania</strong>, is part of a multi-year, $1 billion investment in the company's North American facilities. The investment includes adding 13 production lines and upgrading 11 existing lines.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. Hershey expands cocoa sourcing diversity beyond Ivory Coast and Ghana to Ecuador and Brazil.<br>
2. Hedging contracts and commodities governance framework reduce price volatility.<br>
3. Income Accelerator Program boosts farmer income and productivity for long-term supply.<br>
4. R&D focused on disease- and drought-resistant cacao trees.<br>
5. First fully digital integrated plant in Pennsylvania part of $1B North American investment.</p>
