<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>A new era is beginning in the decarbonization process of global maritime shipping:</strong> <strong>LNG (liquefied natural gas)</strong> has become the <strong>most preferred alternative fuel</strong> in the container shipping sector.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to analysis by <strong>Peter Tirschwell</strong>, the stalling of the decarbonization agenda at <strong>the International Maritime Organization (IMO)</strong>'s <strong>policy meeting in October 2025</strong> has directed many carriers toward <strong>LNG rather than more complex alternatives such as methanol, ammonia, or hydrogen.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Record growth in LNG vessel orders</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to data from <strong>S&amp;P Global Market Intelligence</strong>, <strong>LNG-powered container ship orders</strong> have risen from <strong>194 units at the end of 2023 to 362 by mid-2025</strong> — representing an <strong>86 percent increase</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This trend shows that carriers are beginning to adopt LNG as a <strong>"transition fuel" to meet their carbon-neutral targets.</strong> In the short term, LNG is seen as <strong>compatible with existing port infrastructure</strong>, while in the medium term it <strong>facilitates a transition to carbon-neutral variants such as biomethane and synthetic LNG.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Methanol falling behind</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Despite high expectations for methanol-powered vessels in recent years, <strong>infrastructure gaps</strong>, <strong>fuel costs</strong>, and <strong>global supply chain shortcomings</strong> have slowed its widespread adoption.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Tirschwell:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"For carriers, methanol is still the technology of tomorrow; LNG is today's solution. Decision-makers are adopting a phased approach to reach zero-carbon targets."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Major line operators such as <strong>CMA CGM, Hapag-Lloyd, MSC, ONE</strong>, and <strong>Evergreen</strong> are expanding their <strong>LNG fleet investments.</strong> For example, <strong>CMA CGM's LNG fleet has reached 45 vessels</strong> and the company has more than 60 new LNG ship orders on the books.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>"Pragmatic" approach to decarbonization</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Although the IMO's target of <strong>net-zero carbon emissions by 2050</strong> remains unchanged, interim targets such as <strong>20–30 percent emissions reductions by 2030</strong> have not been clearly defined. This uncertainty has prompted shipowners to <strong>seek solutions that reduce investment risk and are immediately implementable.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Advantages of LNG:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Can be integrated into existing port infrastructure.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Not as toxic as methanol or ammonia.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Fuel bunkering operations are well established in many regions (for example, Singapore, Rotterdam, Marseille).</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Delivers <strong>20–25 percent CO₂ emissions reductions.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Limitations:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Methane slip remains a significant environmental challenge.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Not a fully carbon-neutral solution in the long term.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, the industry aims to reduce methane slip issues <strong>through new-generation engine technologies (for example, MAN Energy Solutions ME-GA series).</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Industry consensus: LNG as transition fuel</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Many industry experts define <strong>LNG not as a final solution but as a transitional fuel.</strong> <strong>CMA CGM CEO Rodolphe Saadé</strong> stated last month that the company is <strong>investing in biomethane and e-LNG technologies</strong>, saying:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"LNG is today's most concrete step in emissions reduction. But our long-term goal is to combine LNG infrastructure with carbon-neutral fuels."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Similarly, <strong>Hapag-Lloyd</strong> has signed an agreement with <strong>Hamburg Port</strong> and <strong>Shell Energy</strong> to establish <strong>green LNG production and bunkering facilities.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>IMO agenda stalling accelerates LNG adoption</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The IMO's failure to make progress on <strong>carbon capture (CCS)</strong> and <strong>ammonia safety standards</strong> at its October 2025 meeting has accelerated carriers' preference for LNG. This situation has prioritized <strong>"implementability" in the short-term decarbonization process.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, <strong>the readiness of LNG's global supply infrastructure</strong> has made it the <strong>"lowest-risk investment option"</strong> for now.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: LNG as today's pragmatic solution</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>path to carbon neutrality</strong> for the container shipping sector is still long, but LNG is accepted as the first stop on that journey.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In Tirschwell's words:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Political stalling at the IMO has altered the industry's direction. Carriers are now rapidly implementing implementable solutions rather than 'waiting.' LNG is today's answer and the foundation of tomorrow's infrastructure."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>LNG-powered vessel orders</strong> have risen from <strong>194 in 2023 to 362 in 2025.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>LNG</strong> has surpassed alternatives such as <strong>methanol and ammonia</strong> to assume the position of <strong>"transition fuel."</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Delivers <strong>20–25 percent CO₂ emissions reductions;</strong> infrastructure already in place.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Major operators such as <strong>CMA CGM, MSC, and Hapag-Lloyd</strong> are increasing LNG investments.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Uncertainties at the IMO</strong> have driven the industry to <strong>shift toward implementable solutions in the short term.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Experts say: "LNG is the bridge to a carbon-neutral future — not the final goal, but the starting point."</p></li>
</ul>
