<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Europe's rail freight sector</strong> has drawn attention in recent weeks with mounting crisis signals. Sector representatives are raising alarms, saying they are <strong>not receiving adequate support from EU institutions</strong> and that <strong>member states are placing their national interests ahead of Europe's rail objectives.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The "pleas for help" from within the sector have intensified due to <strong>rising costs, declining market share, slow infrastructure investments, and regulatory uncertainty.</strong> Conversely, the <strong>lack of political will and coordination at the EU level</strong> is deepening the crisis.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Weakening sector, intensifying competition</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Rail freight in Europe has long been losing ground against <strong>the dominance of road transport.</strong> Road transport's flexible structure, cost-effective fuel alternatives, and liberal policies are undermining rail's competitive strength.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to 2024 data, <strong>rail's share of intra-European freight transport stands at just 17%.</strong> This figure <strong>falls short of even half of the European Commission's 2030 target of a 30% market share.</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Many countries, particularly <strong>Germany, France, and Poland,</strong> have postponed investments in rail infrastructure projects due to economic pressures. This has <strong>increased network congestion, delays, and capacity constraints.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>National priorities override European objectives</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Sector sources argue that EU countries are prioritizing their own national transport policies, thereby <strong>preventing the implementation of a comprehensive European rail strategy.</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">For example:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Germany</strong> promised discounts on rail access charges to reduce costs, but this initiative was delayed due to <strong>federal budget crises.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>France</strong> continues to subsidize road transport despite carbon emission targets.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Spain and Italy</strong> are slowing infrastructure modernization projects while <strong>coordination among national transport agencies remains lacking.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This picture is damaging <strong>Europe's integration objectives in cross-border transport.</strong> <strong>Different signaling systems, technical standards, and bureaucratic procedures</strong> remain major obstacles.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sector warnings: "We've reached the breaking point"</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Rail freight representatives are saying the current situation is unsustainable. Officials at the European Rail Freight Association (ERFA) state that <strong>consecutive financial losses and declining freight volumes</strong> have exposed many operators to bankruptcy risk.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">One sector representative summed up the situation as follows:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"European rail transport is at a critical juncture right now. If policymakers do not act, achieving our carbon targets will remain a dream."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">These concerns suggest that the <strong>European Green Deal</strong> objective of transitioning to <strong>carbon-neutral transport by 2050</strong> is also at risk.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Calls for reform intensifying</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, Europe needs <strong>three fundamental reforms</strong> to save rail freight:</p>
<ol>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Increased investment and infrastructure modernization:</strong> Faster and more equitable distribution of EU funds.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Technical and administrative harmonization:</strong> A <strong>single signaling system (ERTMS)</strong> and <strong>common operating standards</strong> should be made mandatory across Europe.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Leveling the playing field in competition:</strong> <strong>Tax incentives and energy subsidies</strong> should be provided to enable equal competition with road transport.</p></li>
</ol>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, implementing these measures requires <strong>strong political will and coordination in Brussels.</strong> Otherwise, <strong>rail freight will remain "the weakest link" in Europe's Green Transition.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: Is Europe derailing?</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The European Union's <strong>vision of carbon-free transport</strong> depends on a strong rail freight sector. However, in the current situation, the sector is <strong>caught between bureaucracy, investment shortfalls, and conflicting national interests.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Experts warn that without significant restructuring beyond 2026, <strong>rail's share of intra-European freight flows will continue to decline.</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Europe's rail freight sector is at a breaking point due to <strong>cost pressures, capacity bottlenecks, and political neglect.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Rail's share of European freight transport has fallen to <strong>17%;</strong> the 2030 target is 30%.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Lack of EU-level investment and coordination</strong> is stalling the reform process.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>National interests</strong> are overriding cross-border integration efforts.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Sector representatives warn that "carbon-neutral transport targets are at risk."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Solutions: <strong>increased investment, technical harmonization, and political commitment.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Otherwise, Europe could <strong>derail on Green Deal objectives.</strong></p></li>
</ul>
