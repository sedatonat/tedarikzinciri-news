<p>Following the Russia-Ukraine war, the diversion of oil transfers from pipelines to sea routes has driven the tanker market upward. This trend has accelerated further with the crisis in the Red Sea. Attacks in the Red Sea have forced route changes, leading to increased fuel consumption and rising oil inventories, with the freight rate index climbing 37 percent in just two weeks. Turkish shipowners have capitalized on this opportunity by securing competitive charter agreements in the tanker market.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5>Key Points</h5>
<ul>
  <li><p>The Red Sea crisis has accelerated the tanker market's upswing.</p></li>
  <li><p>The freight rate index rose 37 percent in two weeks.</p></li>
  <li><p>Turkish shipowner Yasa chartered its vessel to ExxonMobil at a record rate.</p></li>
  <li><p>The International Energy Agency raised its 2024 oil demand forecast.</p></li>
  <li><p>Newbuilding tanker prices have reached their highest level since the financial crisis.</p></li>
  <li><p>Domestic shipowners have become active market participants with lucrative charter rates.</p></li>
</ul>
