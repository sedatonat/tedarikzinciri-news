<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Logistics</strong> giant <strong>DP World</strong> has secured a contract to operate a new terminal near <strong>Montreal</strong> &mdash; a project intended to increase the port region's container-handling capacity by more than 50%. The <strong>Montreal Port Authority</strong> (<strong>MPA</strong>) and <strong>DP World</strong>'s <strong>Canada</strong> unit have signed a development agreement for the new <strong>Contrecoeur</strong> terminal &mdash; according to an announcement on September 8 &mdash; confirming earlier reporting by <strong>Bloomberg News</strong>. <strong>DP World</strong> is one of the world's largest container port operators and already operates five other port facilities in the country. <strong>DP World Canada</strong> is 45% owned by <strong>Caisse de Depot et Placement du Quebec</strong>. The new terminal is located on the <strong>St. Lawrence</strong> River &mdash; in the northeast of <strong>Montreal</strong>, Canada's second-largest urban area. In-water works could begin as early as this month, pending final approval from <strong>Canada</strong>'s fisheries department. The project is expected to cost approximately 1.6 billion <strong>Canadian Dollars</strong> (1.2 billion <strong>U.S. Dollars</strong>).</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>DP World Limited</strong>, headquartered in <strong>Dubai, United Arab Emirates</strong>, is one of the world's largest container port operators &mdash; with <strong>Sultan Ahmed Bin Sulayem</strong> as <strong>Group Chairman &amp; CEO</strong>. <strong>DP World</strong> operates more than 100 ports and terminals globally across 75 countries, handling over 80 million <strong>TEU</strong> containers annually. <strong>Jebel Ali Port</strong> (<strong>Dubai, UAE</strong>) is <strong>DP World</strong>'s flagship facility and the largest port in the <strong>Middle East</strong>. Key <strong>DP World Canada</strong> facilities include <strong>Vancouver Centerm</strong>, <strong>Vancouver Fraser Surrey</strong>, <strong>Prince Rupert Fairview</strong>, <strong>Saint John, New Brunswick</strong>, and <strong>Nanaimo Duke Point</strong>. <strong>Caisse de Depot et Placement du Quebec</strong> (<strong>CDPQ</strong>) is a <strong>Quebec</strong> pension fund and one of the world's largest pension funds, managing over 450 billion <strong>CAD</strong> in assets, with <strong>Charles Emond</strong> serving as <strong>President &amp; CEO</strong>. The <strong>Port of Montreal</strong> is Canada's largest port on the <strong>Atlantic</strong> coast, providing access to the <strong>Great Lakes</strong> via the <strong>St. Lawrence Seaway</strong>, and handles approximately 1.7 million <strong>TEU</strong> annually. <strong>Julie Gascon</strong> is <strong>CEO</strong> of the <strong>Montreal Port Authority</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, major global container port operators include <strong>PSA International</strong> (<strong>Singapore</strong> — 95M <strong>TEU</strong>); <strong>Hutchison Ports</strong> (<strong>Hong Kong</strong> — 84M <strong>TEU</strong>); <strong>COSCO Shipping Ports</strong> (<strong>Hong Kong</strong> — 130M <strong>TEU</strong>); <strong>APM Terminals</strong> (<strong>Maersk</strong>; <strong>The Hague</strong> — 88M <strong>TEU</strong>); <strong>DP World</strong> (<strong>Dubai</strong> — 80M <strong>TEU</strong>); <strong>China Merchants Ports</strong> (<strong>Hong Kong</strong>); <strong>Eurogate</strong> (<strong>Bremen, Germany</strong>); <strong>HHLA</strong> (<strong>Hamburg, Germany</strong>); <strong>ICTSI</strong> (<strong>Manila, Philippines</strong>); <strong>SSA Marine</strong> (<strong>Seattle, USA</strong>); <strong>Yilport</strong> (<strong>Istanbul, Turkey</strong>); <strong>Port of Singapore Authority</strong>; <strong>Hyundai Merchant Marine</strong>; <strong>Evergreen Marine</strong>; <strong>Yang Ming</strong>; and <strong>Wan Hai</strong>. Major competing <strong>Canada</strong> ports include <strong>Halifax</strong>, <strong>Vancouver</strong>, <strong>Prince Rupert</strong>, <strong>Saint John</strong>, and <strong>Quebec City</strong>. On the <strong>U.S.</strong> side, major competing <strong>Atlantic</strong> ports include the <strong>Port of New York-New Jersey</strong>, <strong>Port of Boston</strong>, <strong>Port of Baltimore</strong>, and <strong>Port of Norfolk</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the <strong>Contrecoeur</strong> project adds new capacity to the <strong>Port of Montreal</strong>'s existing <strong>Cast</strong>, <strong>Racine</strong>, <strong>Bickerdike</strong>, <strong>Maisonneuve</strong>, and <strong>Tarte</strong> terminals &mdash; critical infrastructure for growing global container traffic on <strong>Asia-Europe</strong>, <strong>North Atlantic</strong>, <strong>St. Lawrence Seaway</strong>, and <strong>Great Lakes</strong> routes. The <strong>St. Lawrence Seaway</strong> is jointly operated by the <strong>St. Lawrence Seaway Management Corporation</strong> (<strong>SLSMC</strong>; <strong>Canada</strong>) and the <strong>Saint Lawrence Seaway Development Corporation</strong> (<strong>SLSDC</strong>; <strong>U.S.</strong>), with key lock structures including the <strong>Welland Canal</strong>, <strong>Iroquois Lock</strong>, <strong>Eisenhower Lock</strong>, <strong>Snell Lock</strong>, <strong>Beauharnois Lock</strong>, <strong>St. Lambert Lock</strong>, and <strong>Cote Sainte-Catherine Lock</strong>. Major <strong>Port of Montreal</strong> line operators include <strong>Maersk</strong>, <strong>MSC</strong>, <strong>CMA CGM</strong>, <strong>Hapag-Lloyd</strong>, <strong>ONE</strong>, <strong>Yang Ming</strong>, <strong>Evergreen</strong>, <strong>HMM</strong>, <strong>ZIM</strong>, <strong>OOCL</strong>, and <strong>COSCO</strong>. Key port operating technologies include <strong>OEC</strong> (Optimization Encore Capacity), <strong>Stevedoring</strong>, <strong>STS</strong> (Ship-to-Shore) cranes, <strong>RTG</strong> (Rubber Tyred Gantry), <strong>RMG</strong> (Rail Mounted Gantry), and terminal operating system (<strong>TOS</strong>). In conclusion, the <strong>DP World Contrecoeur</strong> contract represents a strategic investment that significantly expands <strong>Canada</strong>'s <strong>Atlantic</strong> port capacity in response to growing global container traffic.</p>
