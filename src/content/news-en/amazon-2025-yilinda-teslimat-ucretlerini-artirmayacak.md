<p>Amazon has announced that it will not increase delivery fees for third-party sellers in 2025. The e-commerce giant said it will focus on "stability and simplicity" following new fee structures it implemented at the start of the year.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Amazon continues to reduce customer service costs by transitioning to a regional delivery model. This strategy allows the company to maintain Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA) fees without increases despite inflation and employee investments. Additionally, under its "minimal shipment splits" plan for large and bulky items, incoming placement service fees will be reduced in certain circumstances. Starting January 15, these fees will decrease by an average of $0.58 per unit.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Earlier this year, Amazon introduced new fees for third-party sellers related to incoming shipments and low inventory levels. These fees helped offset the company's costs in increasing delivery speed and positioning products closer to customers. <a href="https://www.supplychaindive.com/news/amazon-fba-2025-fulfillment-fee-changes/733418/"><u>https://www.supplychaindive.com/news/amazon-fba-2025-fulfillment-fee-changes/733418/</u></a>However, Amazon acknowledged that these new fees added complexity and required some sellers to adjust their business models. For this reason, the company wants to focus on "simplicity and stability" next year to minimize sellers' operational burdens and expenses.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>This change could be welcomed by businesses attempting to maximize Amazon's logistics capabilities without straining their budgets. Eva Hart, Senior Manager of Demand Creation at Jungle Scout, said in a LinkedIn post, "We've seen consistent fee increases over the years, and this announcement is a welcome turning point to ease seller costs and support growth on the platform."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Meanwhile, Tyler Wallis, Founder and CEO of TripleLine, noted in a LinkedIn post, "If you're a seller, use this brief respite wisely. Focus on profitability, optimize your listings, and diversify your incoming supply options." Wallis emphasized that this turning point does not recover the increased fees sellers have borne over the years.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Additionally, Amazon sellers faced challenges this year including capacity constraints at West Coast fulfillment centers and earlier inventory delivery deadlines for the holiday season. These difficulties required sellers to reassess their inventory management and delivery strategies.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Although Amazon's 2025 announcement has elicited mixed reactions among sellers, it is generally viewed as a positive step for businesses seeking stability and cost control. This strategy could enable sellers to grow more sustainably on the Amazon platform.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li><p>Amazon will not raise FBA fees for third-party sellers in 2025.</p></li>
  <li><p>Costs are being reduced through the regional delivery model.</p></li>
  <li><p>Incoming placement service fees for large and bulky items will decrease by $0.58 per unit.</p></li>
  <li><p>The new fees implemented this year aimed to improve delivery speed and product positioning.</p></li>
  <li><p>Amazon is targeting to minimize sellers' operational burdens and expenses.</p></li>
  <li><p>Sellers should focus on profitability during this period and diversify their supply options.</p></li>
</ul>
