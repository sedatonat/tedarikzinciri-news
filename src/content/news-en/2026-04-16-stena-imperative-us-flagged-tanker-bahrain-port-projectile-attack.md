<p style="text-align: justify">A <strong>US-flagged tanker, the Stena Imperative</strong>, caught fire at the <strong>Port of Bahrain</strong> on <strong>2 March</strong> after being struck by at least two <strong>projectiles</strong>. The incident occurred amid the ongoing conflict between the US, Israel, and Iran. According to <strong>Argus Media</strong>, crew members were unharmed; however, owner <strong>Stena Bulk</strong> and vessel manager <strong>Crowley</strong> reported that one shipyard worker was killed and two others injured. The fire was successfully extinguished.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The incident marks the <strong>first known attack</strong> on a <strong>US-flagged vessel</strong> in the region since the conflict began. It follows strikes on three other vessels in the Gulf: the 11,000 dwt <strong>Skylight</strong>, the 74,000 dwt <strong>Mkd Vyom</strong>, and the 47,000 dwt <strong>Sea La Donna</strong>. Part of the <strong>US Tanker Security Program</strong>, the Stena Imperative is one of 10 US-registered product tankers supplying fuel to armed forces.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The attack comes amid a wider <strong>escalation</strong> across the Middle East maritime region. Fresh <strong>US–Israeli strikes</strong> on Iran and Iran's retaliatory actions have undermined hopes of meaningful regional stability. The incident underscores the rising risks faced by commercial shipping in the Gulf and the potential need to reassess <strong>tanker security</strong> protections.</p>

4. Three earlier vessels — Skylight, Mkd Vyom, and Sea La Donna — were also targeted in the Gulf.<br>
