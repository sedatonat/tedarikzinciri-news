<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>AD Ports Group</strong> has signed a <strong>30-year concession agreement</strong> with the <strong>Aqaba Development Corporation (ADC)</strong> to manage and operate the Aqaba Multipurpose Port. The port is reportedly <strong>Jordan's only and exclusive general cargo and multipurpose seaport</strong>. The agreement establishes a Joint Venture to manage and operate the port, with AD Ports holding 70 per cent ownership and ADC holding the remaining 30 per cent.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under the terms of the agreement, AD Ports Group will invest a total of <strong>AED 141 million ($38.4 million)</strong> in the Joint Venture, in addition to bringing extensive port development and operational expertise to the transaction. The Group anticipates formally assuming operations of the port in <strong>August of this year</strong>. The signing ceremony was held at the headquarters of the Aqaba Special Economic Zone Authority (ASEZA) in Aqaba.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The deal accelerates AD Ports' strategic expansion across the <strong>Red Sea corridor</strong> and marks a milestone for the modernisation of Jordan's port infrastructure. The investment is expected to deliver tangible capacity and efficiency gains at the terminal in <strong>equipment upgrades, digitalisation and hinterland connectivity</strong>. The agreement also reflects the UAE's regional port diplomacy.</p>

<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>

3. AD Ports will hold 70% and ADC 30% in the Joint Venture.<br>
4. AD Ports' investment commitment stands at AED 141 million ($38.4 million).<br>
5. Formal operations transfer is planned for August, marking strategic expansion in the Red Sea corridor.</p>
