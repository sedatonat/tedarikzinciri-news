<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Hamburg Port Authority (HPA)</strong> has partnered with bbe Moldaenke GmbH, BUKEA and the <strong>University of Lübeck</strong> to launch <strong>SIMON – Smarter Water Protection Inspired by 4-Dimensional Underwater Monitoring</strong>. Funded under the Federal Ministry of Research, Technology and Space's (BMFTR) Digital GreenTech Robotics Call, the 24-month project is backed by around <strong>€1 million ($1.19 million)</strong>, with approximately €232,000 ($276,389) allocated to HPA.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">SIMON advances the use of <strong>autonomous underwater vehicles (AUVs)</strong> for continuous, high-precision environmental monitoring in the tidal Elbe within the Port of Hamburg. Equipped with advanced sensor technology, the AUVs will measure suspended solids, oxygen levels, algal composition and <strong>oil residues</strong>, including potential sources of contamination. The collected data will be integrated directly into operational platforms.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">On the port management's sustainability agenda, <strong>four-dimensional underwater monitoring</strong> both strengthens ecosystem protection and informs dredging planning and sediment management. The approach can offer a scalable model for <strong>smart environmental protection</strong> at European ports; the data generated will also contribute to long-term analysis of the Elbe ecosystem under climate change.</p>
