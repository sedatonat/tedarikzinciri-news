<p style="text-align: justify">Recent progress on the Middle Corridor and the expectation of a brisker pace in 2026 were outlined at <strong>"The Caspian at a Crossroads — What to Watch in 2026"</strong> Webinar on <strong>22 January</strong>, organised by the <strong>Caspian Policy Center</strong>. The session assessed Central Asia's rising importance as multiple initiatives advance. <strong>Samuel Doveri Vesterbye, Managing Director, European Neighbourhood Council (ENC)</strong>, highlighted that Central Asia is becoming much more important, especially given its formidable energy resources and the determination of regional governments to link into the world economy.</p><p style="text-align: justify">Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, Turkmenistan, Tajikistan, and Kyrgyzstan are critical suppliers to global markets with reserves of oil, natural gas, uranium, gold, copper, and rare-earth elements. Regional governments in recent years have been attracting investment through the <strong>Middle Corridor</strong>, the <strong>Trans-Afghan Corridor</strong>, and the <strong>International North-South Transport Corridor (INSTC)</strong>. The webinar showed that multiple parallel initiatives — the EU's Global Gateway, China's Belt and Road Initiative, and the US-led C5+1 — are turning the region into a competitive arena.</p><p style="text-align: justify">Speakers emphasised that Central Asia's goal of <strong>opening doors to the world</strong> will be realised through accelerated infrastructure investment, digitalised customs processes, and harmonised cross-border logistics standards. Kazakhstan's Port of Aktau, Uzbekistan's Termez dry-port facilities, and Azerbaijan's Alat Port capacity expansion were cited as examples. The ENC and the Caspian Policy Center foresee 2026 as a critical year for Central Asia through corridor investments, tender wins, and the launch of new block train services.</p>

<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. The Caspian Policy Center's 22 January webinar addressed recent Middle Corridor developments.<br>
2. ENC's Samuel Doveri Vesterbye stressed Central Asia's rising importance due to its energy resources.<br>
3. Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan and other regional nations are critical suppliers to global markets.<br>
4. EU Global Gateway, China's Belt and Road, and US C5+1 initiatives compete in Central Asia.<br>
5. 2026 will be a critical year with corridor investments and new block train services.</p>
