<p style="text-align: justify">The US has announced it is <strong>removing tariffs on hundreds of agricultural and food products imported from Brazil</strong>. The decision took effect through a new executive order signed by President Donald Trump and was detailed in a notice published by the US Customs and Border Protection Agency (<strong>CBP</strong>).</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The exemptions are designed to align with adjustments made to <strong>global reciprocal tariffs</strong> last week. In total, <strong>238 product classifications and 11 agricultural/food categories</strong> received exemptions under this framework.</p>
<p><br>
Exempted products include:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Coffee</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Beef</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Spices</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Tropical fruits and fruit juices</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new regulation will be effective <strong>retroactively from November 13</strong>, and overpaid duties will be refunded by CBP through standard procedures.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A decision that eases the burden on Brazil's critical export products</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">These exemptions are relieving the significant tariff burden that Brazil's exports have faced in recent months. <strong>Coffee</strong> in particular is a critical product for Brazil both economically and in terms of global trade.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to US Department of Agriculture data, Brazil:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Produces <strong>37% of global coffee output</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">When tariffs combined with declining yields due to climate change, they created <strong>additional costs and price pressure</strong> in the global coffee market.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Extra 40% tariff on Brazil is also being eased</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">In addition to global reciprocal tariffs, products imported from Brazil:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Faced an <strong>additional 40% levy from August 6</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Trump administration had imposed this tax on the grounds that "US national security, foreign policy, and economic interests were under threat."</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Key factors cited in this justification:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Judicial proceedings against Jair Bolsonaro,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Penalties imposed by Brazil's Supreme Court on US companies such as <strong>X (Twitter), Meta, and Google</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new exemption decision was made due to <strong>"first progress" in trade negotiations</strong> between the two countries.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Positive signals in US-Brazil trade discussions</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to the executive order, Trump initiated negotiations on <strong>October 6</strong> in a meeting with Brazilian President <strong>Lula da Silva</strong>.<br>
After weeks of discussions, the US announced the exemptions in question, citing "signs of progress."</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The Trump administration is also conducting a <strong>Section 301 investigation</strong>. This investigation:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Digital trade,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Anti-corruption and anti-bribery measures,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Ethanol market access<br>
is examining Brazilian policies on these matters.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Section 301 is a powerful trade instrument that allows the US to impose <strong>unilateral sanctions</strong> against unfair trade practices.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Impact on the US domestic market and consumers</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">The tariff exemptions are expected to <strong>ease consumer prices particularly for coffee and tropical fruits</strong>.<br>
Tariffs introduced after August had pushed retail prices higher for many agricultural products in the US.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Notably:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Coffee chains,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Food manufacturers,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Beverage companies</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">will benefit significantly from the new exemptions.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>5. Key Takeaways:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The US has granted tariff exemptions on <strong>coffee, meat, spices, and fruit products from Brazil</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The exemption is <strong>retroactively effective from November 13</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The measure is a result of <strong>progress in trade negotiations between the two countries</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Brazil is a critical supplier with 37% of global coffee production.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The previously applied <strong>additional 40% tariff</strong> has effectively been eliminated on these products.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The Section 301 investigation continues; tariff policies have not been fully resolved despite this decision.</p></li>
</ul>
