<p style="text-align: justify">Japanese maritime and logistics conglomerate <strong>NYK (Nippon Yusen Kabushiki Kaisha)</strong> is taking a significant step forward in digitalization and autonomy in maritime transport. The company announced it has placed an order with <strong>Shinkai Toyohashi Shipbuilding</strong> for a next-generation <strong>pure car carrier</strong> equipped with <strong>AI-enabled autonomous navigation</strong> and comprehensive <strong>Marine DX (Digital Transformation)</strong> systems. The vessel, approximately <strong>200 meters long</strong> and <strong>38 meters wide</strong>, is scheduled for delivery on <strong>31 March 2026</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new vessel will serve not merely as a commercial car carrier but also as <strong>a test platform for advanced navigation and safety technologies</strong>. NYK aims to test the vessel under real operational conditions during regular commercial voyages.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>MEGURI2040 and Autonomous Navigation</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The vessel's most notable feature is its use of an autonomous navigation system developed under the <strong>MEGURI2040 fully autonomous ship programme</strong>, led by the <strong>Nippon Foundation</strong>. The system operates under <strong>crew supervision</strong>; that is, the captain and navigating officers remain aboard and can manually disable the system if necessary.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The autonomous navigation package employs both <strong>AI-based image recognition</strong> and <strong>automatic radar target analysis</strong> technologies. These systems enable the vessel to:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">detect surrounding maritime traffic,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">assess <strong>collision</strong> and <strong>grounding risks</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">plan appropriate evasive maneuvers,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">and execute these maneuvers through automatic steering.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">NYK emphasizes that the fundamental objective of this approach is to <strong>reduce the workload of navigating personnel</strong> and enhance safety given the reality that approximately <strong>70% of maritime accidents stem from human error</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Japan Marine Science and Intelligent Route Planning</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Japan Marine Science (JMS)</strong>, operating within the NYK Group, will supply the vessel with <strong>Advanced Routing Simulation and Planning</strong> software. This software simulates not only the vessel's own movements but also the probable behavior of surrounding vessels.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The system continuously recalculates <strong>the vessel's optimal route and speed</strong>. The software operates in real time and can be <strong>overridden at any moment by navigating officers through manual control</strong>. This architecture provides a <strong>hybrid operational model</strong> between autonomy and human control.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Furuno Electric and Collision Avoidance</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Japanese navigation equipment manufacturer <strong>Furuno Electric</strong> will supply the <strong>collision-avoidance support package</strong> to be integrated into the vessel. Furuno's system processes data from radar and other sensors to calculate collision risk and, working in conjunction with <strong>JMS software</strong>, generates the optimal evasion route.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Furuno will also supply the vessel with <strong>radar</strong>, <strong>ECDIS</strong>, and other essential navigation equipment, providing a broader <strong>Marine DX package</strong>. This package serves <strong>operational efficiency</strong> objectives as well as safety.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Cargo Security and Motion Mitigation</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The next-generation car carrier will be equipped with a <strong>large-motion mitigation system</strong> specifically designed to protect <strong>vehicle cargo in adverse weather conditions</strong>. This system uses <strong>real-time wave radar data</strong> and vessel status information to simulate potential rolling and motions; it provides <strong>course and speed adjustment recommendations</strong> to the captain and autonomous system. In this way, both cargo damage and operational risks are reduced.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Digital Infrastructure and Connectivity</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">NYK plans to install a <strong>shipwide Wi-Fi network</strong> on the vessel. This network will be designed to cover areas such as the <strong>engine room</strong>, <strong>decks</strong>, and <strong>cargo holds</strong> where signal is traditionally weak. This infrastructure will enable:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">access to digital manuals,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">real-time video technical support,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">remote medical consultation,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">mitigation of <strong>signal shadow zones</strong> created by the steel hull</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">to be provided.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic Context</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The order directly aligns with <strong>MEGURI2040</strong>, Japan's initiative aimed at addressing safety, digitalization, and <strong>crew shortage</strong> challenges in maritime operations. NYK aims to test these technologies not in a laboratory environment but on <strong>a commercial car carrier</strong>, bringing autonomous navigation closer to routine operations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Overall Assessment</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">NYK's next-generation car carrier with AI-enabled autonomous navigation system provides an important example of how <strong>semi-autonomous operations</strong> can be implemented in practice at sea. This model, in which human oversight and artificial intelligence work in tandem, serves as an industry reference in terms of safety, efficiency, and sustainability.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">NYK ordered a car carrier with an <strong>AI-enabled autonomous navigation</strong> system.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The vessel will utilize technologies developed under the <strong>MEGURI2040</strong> program.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Delivery date: <strong>31 March 2026</strong>.</p></li>

  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The system operates <strong>semi-autonomously</strong> under human supervision.</p></li>
</ul>
