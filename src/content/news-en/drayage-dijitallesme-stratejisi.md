<p><strong>The drayage sector</strong> faces greater pressure than ever in 2025.</p>
<ul>
  <li><p>Customers demand <strong>real-time visibility</strong>.</p></li>
  <li><p>Drivers want <strong>simpler workflows</strong>.</p></li>
  <li><p>Operators work with <strong>near-zero margins</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p>Manual processes — quoting, dispatch, tracking, invoicing — are both slow and error-prone. The result: more disputes, sluggish cash flow, lower productivity, and lost competitive advantage.</p>
<p><strong>CargoWise Landside</strong> product portfolio leader <strong>Tom Burke</strong> sums up the situation this way:</p>
<blockquote>"If you don't have current technology, shippers won't choose you anymore."</blockquote>
<p>The solution driving the sector's future is clear: <strong>Digitalization and end-to-end automation</strong>.</p>
<p>Below are <strong>five essential best practices</strong> transforming drayage operations:</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>1. Digitalize Rates, Quotes, Orders, and Dispatch</strong></h5>
<p>Manual quoting, multiple spreadsheets, distance and toll calculations… All of these waste time.</p>
<p>Leading operators:</p>
<ul>
  <li><p>Consolidate all rates (fuel, reefer, accessorials, tolls) into <strong>a single digital system</strong>.</p></li>
  <li><p><strong>Automate</strong> the quote → order → dispatch workflow.</p></li>
  <li><p>Establish a standard of "quotes in minutes."</p></li>
</ul>
<p>A mid-sized firm was generating <strong>60–70 quotes daily</strong> before digitalization; with CargoWise, it has scaled to <strong>200 quotes per day</strong>.</p>
<p>Results:</p>
<ul>
  <li><p>More consistent pricing</p></li>
  <li><p>Higher conversion rates</p></li>
  <li><p>Stronger customer confidence</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>2. Optimize Driver Experience with Mobile Tools</strong></h5>
<p>Every minute a driver spends on the road generates revenue. Office visits, phone traffic, and paper PODs are both costly and draining.</p>
<p>By providing mobile applications:</p>
<ul>
  <li><p>Tasks are delivered directly to the driver's phone,</p></li>
  <li><p>POD is recorded instantly,</p></li>
  <li><p>Status updates flow automatically into the system,</p></li>
  <li><p>Dispatch teams manage only exceptions.</p></li>
</ul>
<p>Research shows:</p>
<ul>
  <li><p><strong>Over 70% of drivers</strong> say mobile tools improve their work.</p></li>
  <li><p>Some companies have improved their <strong>driver-to-dispatcher ratio by 3x</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
<p>In Burke's words:</p>
<blockquote>"Every minute a driver doesn't spend behind the wheel is money lost."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5><strong>3. Automate Wait Times and Tracking</strong></h5>
<p>Wait time is drayage's biggest hidden cost.</p>
<ul>
  <li><p>Port congestion</p></li>
  <li><p>Terminal queues</p></li>
  <li><p>Warehouse delays</p></li>
</ul>
<p>When these times go unrecorded, billing disputes become inevitable.</p>
<p>Best practices include:</p>
<ul>
  <li><p><strong>Geofencing</strong> to automatically detect arrival and departure</p></li>
  <li><p>Wait times digitally measured and logged</p></li>
  <li><p>Event updates automatically sent to the customer</p></li>
  <li><p>EDI/API ensures seamless data sync between systems</p></li>
</ul>
<p>Results:</p>
<ul>
  <li><p>Disputes drop significantly</p></li>
  <li><p>Dispatch calls decrease</p></li>
  <li><p>Revenue per employee <strong>doubles</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>4. Digitalize Invoicing and Reconciliation</strong></h5>
<p>Drayage's sore spot: <strong>slow and error-prone invoicing</strong>.</p>
<p>With automation:</p>
<ul>
  <li><p>Loads are automatically classified,</p></li>
  <li><p>Invoices are issued the same day,</p></li>
  <li><p>Documents from the mobile app are instantly attached to the invoice,</p></li>
  <li><p>Cash flow accelerates.</p></li>
</ul>
<p>What once took <strong>10 days</strong> to complete the order-to-invoice cycle can now happen in <strong>1–2 days</strong>.<br>
"Slow invoicing means money left on the table," Burke says.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>5. Make Digital Transformation an Organizational Strategy</strong></h5>
<p>Technology alone is not the solution.<br>
Successful firms share common traits:</p>
<ul>
  <li><p>Executive sponsorship,</p></li>
  <li><p>Pilot users,</p></li>
  <li><p>Phased rollout with expanding scope,</p></li>
  <li><p>Integration of feedback from the field.</p></li>
</ul>
<p>Without this approach, technology investments don't yield results.</p>
<blockquote>"Technology cannot win without executive leadership driving it," Burke says.</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
