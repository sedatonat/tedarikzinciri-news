<p style="text-align: justify">Hong Kong-based <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CK_Hutchison_Holdings"><u><strong>CK Hutchison Holdings</strong></u></a> has decided to sell its majority stake in <a href="https://www.ppc.com.pa/"><u><strong>Panama Ports Company</strong></u></a>, which operates the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balboa,_Panama"><u><strong>Balboa</strong></u></a> and <a href="https://logistics.gatech.pa/en/logistics-platform/logistics-assets/seaports/main-seaports-in-panama/cristobal/"><u><strong>Cristóbal</strong></u></a> ports located at the entrance and exit points of the Panama Canal, to a U.S. consortium led by <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BlackRock"><u><strong>BlackRock</strong></u><u> </u></a>. The deal, valued at $22.8 billion, has drawn sharp criticism from the Chinese government.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">​</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In an article published in <strong>Ta Kung Pao</strong>, a Beijing-backed newspaper, the sale was described as "power politics" and contrary to national interests. The article emphasized that <strong>CK Hutchison</strong> should "think twice." Following these criticisms, the company's shares lost more than 6% of their value on the Hong Kong stock exchange.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">​</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The deal covers <strong>CK Hutchison</strong>'s 90% stake in <strong>Panama Ports Company</strong> as well as an 80% stake in <strong>Hutchison Ports</strong>, which operates 43 ports across 23 countries. These ports include facilities in the <strong>United Kingdom</strong>, <strong>Germany</strong>, <strong>Southeast Asia</strong>, the <strong>Middle East</strong>, <strong>Mexico</strong>, and <strong>Australia</strong>. The company plans to generate over $19 billion in cash from this sale. ​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">U.S. President <strong>Donald Trump</strong> has repeatedly expressed concerns about China's influence over the Panama Canal and indicated his intention to reclaim control of the channel. In this context, <strong>BlackRock</strong> CEO <strong>Larry Fink</strong> held talks with the Trump administration and the U.S. Congress to support this acquisition. ​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Following the sale, the Chinese government expressed concerns that other business operations of <strong>CK Hutchison</strong> in China and Hong Kong could be negatively affected. The company's 2023 revenues derived approximately 14% from China and Hong Kong. ​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CK Hutchison Holdings</strong> has decided to sell its Panama ports to a U.S. consortium led by <strong>BlackRock</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The Chinese government has criticized the sale harshly, characterizing it as "power politics."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The deal covers 43 ports in 23 countries with a total value of $22.8 billion.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">U.S. President <strong>Donald Trump</strong> has previously expressed concerns about China's influence over the Panama Canal.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>CK Hutchison</strong>'s other business operations in China and Hong Kong may be negatively affected by this sale.</p></li>
</ul>
