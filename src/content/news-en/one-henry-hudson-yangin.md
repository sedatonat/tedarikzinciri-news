<p style="text-align: justify">The Port of Los Angeles faced a major emergency on Friday evening when a fire erupted aboard the <strong>8,212 TEU capacity container vessel ONE Henry Hudson</strong> moored at the <strong>Yusen Container Terminal</strong>. The Los Angeles Fire Department (LAFD) disclosed that the fire started at <strong>6:38 p.m. in the below-deck compartments</strong>, with the probable cause being an <strong>electrical fault</strong>. The rapid spread of flames to multiple sub-level areas heightened the severity of the incident.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">A <strong>mid-deck explosion</strong> that occurred during the fire disabled the vessel's electrical systems, causing <strong>complete loss of lighting</strong>, shutdown of crane operations, and complicating emergency evacuation procedures. The LAFD responded to the fire from both land and sea; harbor fire boats conducted intensive cooling with water cannons, while <strong>HazMat units</strong> continuously monitored air quality due to hazardous materials detected in various containers.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">All <strong>23 crew members aboard were safely evacuated</strong> with no injuries reported. However, due to the hazardous cargo and fire spreading through the vessel's lower decks, a <strong>"shelter-in-place"</strong> advisory was issued to neighborhoods surrounding the port. This advisory was lifted on Saturday morning after the vessel was safely towed to an open anchorage area away from the port.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">The impact of the fire on port operations was significant. The Port of Los Angeles suspended operations at <strong>four of its seven container terminals</strong> when the fire broke out. The vessel's relocation to an open anchorage area near <strong>Angel's Gate Lighthouse</strong> reduced risks for the port and provided emergency teams with a wider and safer work space for firefighting efforts. With micro-level reconfiguration of terminal and port traffic, operations at the four terminals resumed normal activities on Saturday morning.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Approximately <strong>200 firefighters and emergency personnel</strong> were deployed to combat the fire. This number represents one of the largest firefighting operations seen at the Port of Los Angeles in recent years. While the fire is believed to have begun as a result of an electrical fault, authorities assess that potential reactions from hazardous material-carrying containers accelerated the fire's spread.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Authorities note that the incident has brought to the fore weaknesses in hazardous cargo management aboard vessels, electrical infrastructure, and emergency protocols. Such an incident, particularly at the Port of Los Angeles—the United States' busiest import gateway—creates critical supply chain continuity risks.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">While the fire requires investigation and cooling processes that could take days, authorities announced that the surrounding community is no longer at risk and port operations have largely returned to normal.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h4>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The fire appears to have originated from a <strong>below-deck electrical fault</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>The 8,212 TEU ONE Henry Hudson</strong> experienced an explosion while at Yusen Terminal.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">23 crew members were safely evacuated; no injuries.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Approximately <strong>200 emergency personnel</strong> responded.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">HazMat teams continuously monitored air quality due to hazardous materials.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The vessel was relocated to open waters near Angel's Gate; the port reopened four terminals.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The "shelter-in-place" advisory was lifted on Saturday morning.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>----------</p>\n<p><strong>News Link: </strong><a href="https://splash247.com/one-henry-hudson-fire-spurs-port-emergency-in-los-angeles/"><u>https://splash247.com/one-henry-hudson-fire-spurs-port-emergency-in-los-angeles/</u></a></p>\n<p>--------------------</p>\n<p><strong>Author: </strong><a href="http://sedatonat.com/"><u>SedatOnat.com</u></a></p>\n<p>--------------------</p>\n<p><strong>!!!
