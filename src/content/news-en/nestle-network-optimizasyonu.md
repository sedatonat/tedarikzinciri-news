<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Nestlé USA</strong> uses a <strong>distribution network optimization modeling</strong> approach that it refines annually as part of its supply chain strategy, enabling the company to reduce <strong>total delivery costs</strong> while simultaneously addressing <strong>risk, sustainability, and customer satisfaction</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Karan Gill, Head of Artificial Intelligence for Supply Chain Optimization</strong> at Nestlé, presented at the <strong>CSCMP EDGE Conference</strong> on how the company shapes its distribution network decisions using a data-driven approach.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A Comprehensive and Dynamic Distribution Network</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Nestlé USA operates approximately <strong>25 distribution centers</strong> across the country.<br>
Some of these centers are owned by Nestlé, while others are operated by <strong>third-party logistics (3PL)</strong> providers.<br>
A majority of the centers are located in the <strong>Midwest</strong> region.<br>
Through this network, Nestlé delivers to <strong>approximately 5,000 customer distribution centers (DCs)</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Gill noted that the system maintains a careful balance between <strong>customer focus, cost optimization, resilience, and sustainability</strong>:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"When determining our distribution points, we evaluate not just cost, but also risks, capacity, carbon footprint, and service levels within the same model."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Three Networks – Three Strategies: Ambient, Chilled, Frozen</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Nestlé USA manages <strong>three distinct distribution networks</strong> to account for its diverse product portfolio:</p>
<ol>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ambient (room-temperature products)</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Chilled (refrigerated products)</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Frozen (frozen products)</strong></p></li>
</ol>
<p style="text-align: justify">Gill and his team publish separate <strong>roadmaps</strong> annually for each of these three networks.<br>
These roadmaps determine which customers will be served from which distribution centers and how to optimize the <strong>flow design</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The objective is to minimize <strong>total delivered cost</strong> while accounting for physical and operational constraints.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Model Components: A Multi-Layered Optimization Approach</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Nestlé's network model evaluates <strong>multiple cost and constraint parameters</strong> simultaneously:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Transportation costs</strong> (distance &amp; mode costs)</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Handling and storage costs</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Warehouse capacity limits</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Flow balance and inventory strategy</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Delivery time windows and customer contract terms</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">The model also determines which products should be shipped <strong>directly from factory to customer</strong> and which should be distributed through warehouses.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Gill emphasized that this system not only reduces costs but also improves <strong>inventory efficiency and service levels</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Continuous Re-optimization: Adapting to Changing Conditions</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Gill stressed that optimization is <strong>not a one-time project but a continuous process</strong>:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Both internal and external conditions constantly change: our production sources shift, transportation costs fluctuate, and customer demand moves.<br>
That's why we review our network design every year."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Nestlé's annual re-modeling process is closely tied to the company's <strong>mergers and acquisitions (M&amp;A)</strong> activities.</p>
<p><br>
Gill noted that <strong>past acquisitions and divestitures have frequently required the network structure to be rebalanced</strong>, and optimization has provided "critical decision support" throughout these processes.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Works in Both Growth and Crisis</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Gill, network optimization serves as the company's "compass" in both growth and crisis periods:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Network optimization shows us where to build new facilities when times are good.<br>
But when times are tough, it tells us just as clearly which points to consolidate or close."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">This strategic flexibility strengthens Nestlé's <strong>supply chain resilience</strong> while maintaining an optimal balance between <strong>cost control and service continuity</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Data-Driven Sustainability and Customer Focus</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Nestlé incorporates <strong>carbon emissions and energy efficiency</strong> criteria into its distribution network model.<br>
This allows the company to maintain cost advantages while achieving <strong>sustainable logistics goals</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Gill noted that this approach is not merely a "green" strategy but also delivers <strong>long-term operational efficiency</strong>.</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Sustainability is no longer a line item; it's part of strategic resilience."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion: A Dynamic, Intelligent, and Responsive Distribution Ecosystem</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Nestlé's optimization model is built on principles of <strong>data-driven decision-making, continuous learning, and flexible network planning</strong>.</p>
<p><br>
This approach enables the company to both <strong>enhance customer experience</strong> and <strong>reduce logistics costs</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The presentation at CSCMP EDGE demonstrated that Nestlé has developed a best-practice example of <strong>AI-driven network design</strong> not only in the <strong>food and beverage sector</strong> but also in <strong>supply chain management</strong> more broadly.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Nestlé USA</strong> operates 25 distribution centers and ships to <strong>5,000 customer DCs</strong> annually.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The company designs separate optimization networks for <strong>ambient, chilled, and frozen</strong> products.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The model simultaneously optimizes <strong>transportation, storage, capacity, delivery times, and inventory strategies</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Annual re-modeling enables rapid adaptation to <strong>changing demand, costs, and production structures</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Optimization</strong> provides strategic decision support in both growth (new facility investment) and crisis (center consolidation) scenarios.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sustainability</strong> criteria are now an integral part of cost optimization.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Karan Gill:</strong> "Network optimization sits at the heart of Nestlé's supply chain flexibility."</p></li>
</ul>
