<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Pakistan</strong> has proposed that <strong>Bangladesh use the Karachi Port Trust (KPT)</strong> to <strong>facilitate trade with China and other countries</strong>. The Karachi Port, one of South Asia's busiest and most strategically important deep-water harbors, stands out as a key node in Pakistan's regional logistics network.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The proposal was presented by Pakistan's Petroleum Minister <strong>Ali Pervaiz Malik</strong> at the <strong>9th Bangladesh–Pakistan Joint Economic Commission (JEC) meeting held in Dhaka on October 27, 2025</strong>. Bangladesh was represented by <strong>Dr. Salehuddin Ahmed</strong>, Finance Adviser to the interim government.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A strategic move in regional trade</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Pakistan's proposal aims to <strong>diversify regional trade routes in South Asia</strong> and <strong>strengthen logistics flows connected with China</strong>. The geographic location of Karachi Port serves as a bridge between <strong>China's western regions and sea routes to the Middle East and Africa</strong>, particularly within the framework of the <strong>China–Pakistan Economic Corridor (CPEC)</strong> and the <strong>Belt and Road Initiative (BRI)</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The proposal offers Bangladesh a new option to support its growing trade volume with China and create a more competitive export route across the Indian Ocean.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Opportunities for Bangladesh</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Much of Bangladesh's industrial exports currently flow through <strong>Chittagong Port</strong>. However, this port occasionally suffers from <strong>congestion due to infrastructure and capacity constraints</strong>. The entry of Karachi Port could <strong>increase Bangladesh's supply chain flexibility</strong> and <strong>accelerate its access to Chinese, Central Asian, and Middle Eastern markets</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, such cooperation carries the potential to <strong>revive economic relations that have been limited between the two countries for some time</strong>. Bangladesh currently imports significant quantities of <strong>industrial machinery, textile equipment, and chemicals from China</strong>; Pakistan, meanwhile, seeks to boost <strong>logistics revenues</strong> by channeling this flow through Karachi.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Geopolitical implications</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Pakistan's proposal coincides with a period when <strong>trade corridors in South Asia are being reshaped</strong>. Many countries in the region are <strong>seeking alternative routes to sea lanes dominated by India</strong>. Karachi's opening to Bangladesh could <strong>make it easier to conduct regional trade through non-Indian channels</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">However, observers note that this initiative will require <strong>careful diplomatic balance in India–Bangladesh relations</strong>. India is both one of Bangladesh's largest trading partners and a leading player in South Asian infrastructure projects.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Economic vision and regional integration</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Petroleum Minister <strong>Ali Pervaiz Malik</strong> stated after the meeting:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Karachi Port is a strategic center not only for Pakistan but for all of South Asia. Bangladesh's utilization of this port will accelerate regional trade integration," he said.</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">Bangladesh indicated that it would examine the proposal from a <strong>"technical and economic feasibility" perspective</strong>. According to observers, if the project moves forward, it could represent <strong>Pakistan's soft power gains in regional logistics cooperation</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Pakistan</strong> has proposed that Bangladesh use <strong>Karachi Port Trust</strong> for trade.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Objective: <strong>Facilitate Bangladesh–China trade</strong> and strengthen regional logistics flows.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The proposal was presented at the <strong>9th Bangladesh–Pakistan JEC meeting</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Bangladesh</strong> is in the process of evaluating the proposal economically.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Karachi Port</strong> is one of the <strong>key maritime links of CPEC and BRI</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The initiative carries strategic importance in developing <strong>alternative trade routes to India</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, the project could strengthen <strong>regional trade integration</strong> and initiate <strong>a new period of economic cooperation between the two countries</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
