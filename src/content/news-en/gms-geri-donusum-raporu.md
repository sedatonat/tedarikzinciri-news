<p style="text-align: justify">Global ship recycling markets are navigating yet another challenging week as 2025 draws to a close, according to the <strong>GMS Weekly Podcast – Forums and Frictions (Week 47)</strong> update. Deteriorating macroeconomic indicators combined with regional imbalances are exerting marked pressure on recycling hubs in South Asia and Turkey. <strong>GMS (Global Marketing Systems)</strong>, one of the world's largest ship recycling brokers, reports that weakening steel prices, currency fluctuations, sanctions-related risks, and constrained tonnage supply are creating a squeeze on the market.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">As the final week of November approaches, <strong>oil futures</strong> have retreated to approximately <strong>USD 57.7</strong>, signaling declines both over the past month and year-to-date. The <strong>freight market</strong> remains active in certain segments but continues to trade well below last year's levels. Notably, <strong>steel plate prices</strong> are declining in Bangladesh and Pakistan, putting pressure on recycling facility margins.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Currency volatility in <strong>India</strong> and <strong>Bangladesh</strong> is eroding purchasing power among recycling buyers. Meanwhile, additional sanctions on <strong>Russia</strong> and <strong>Iran</strong> assets imposed by the <strong>US</strong> and <strong>EU</strong> are intensifying scrutiny of the <strong>dark fleet</strong>. This development threatens not only short-term trading but also has the potential to affect long-term recycling flows.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The report highlights signals that global indicators will not soon turn in favor of recycling markets. The <strong>Baltic Dry Index</strong> shows weekly gains in certain segments but remains approximately <strong>9%</strong> below its 30-day average and roughly <strong>50%</strong> lower compared to November 2024. The <strong>20th sanctions package</strong> prepared by the EU contains measures that could allow member state naval forces to intercept suspicious <strong>shadow-fleet</strong> vessels. The US has imposed additional restrictions on approximately <strong>170 Iran-linked</strong> vessels. These pressures are narrowing the commercial scope for older tonnage and beginning to create conditions that could accelerate recycling intentions.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Bangladesh</strong> remains the region's strongest market by pricing: <strong>USD 410 / LDT</strong> (bulk), <strong>USD 430</strong> (tanker), <strong>USD 440</strong> (container). However, transaction volumes are limited; 2025 has been described as "<strong>anorexic</strong>." Inflation hovers in the <strong>8–9%</strong> band, while the <strong>Taka</strong> has weakened to <strong>BDT 122.5 per USD</strong>. Local <strong>steel plate</strong> prices have fallen to <strong>USD 525.9</strong>, intensifying pressure on recycling facilities. Pre-election political tensions also create operational risk. On a positive note, another facility obtained <strong>Hong Kong Convention (HKC)</strong> certification, bringing the total to <strong>20</strong> facilities.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>India – Alang</strong> presents a more challenging picture. The market is described in the report as "<strong>losing bigly</strong>"; buyers are backing away from firm offers. The <strong>Rupee</strong> has weakened <strong>1%</strong> in a week to <strong>Rs 89.6 / USD</strong>, reducing forward pricing confidence. While steel prices have risen to <strong>USD 398</strong>, they remain below the critical <strong>USD 400</strong> band. Small and lower-quality tonnage, despite being offered at <strong>USD 400 / LDT</strong> indications, is finding buyers at lower levels. The market is being dragged into a two-tiered price structure due to <strong>discounted dark-fleet tonnage</strong> and cheap imported steel. On HKC compliance, the presence of only one vessel in port and no new deals pose risks.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Pakistan – Gadani</strong> demonstrated the region's most critical structural development during the week. The country's first <strong>HKC-compliant yard</strong> is reportedly nearing final approval. Other facilities are expected to gain approval within <strong>3–6 months</strong>. In the near term, however, fundamentals remain pressured: steel plate prices have fallen to <strong>USD 586</strong>, the <strong>PKR</strong> has experienced mild depreciation at <strong>282.6 /USD</strong>, and tonnage arrivals have been minimal for a third consecutive week.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Turkey – Aliaga</strong> market remains steady. Prices are holding firm: <strong>USD 260 / LDT</strong> (bulk), <strong>USD 270</strong> (tanker), <strong>USD 280</strong> (container). The <strong>Lira</strong>, having weakened to <strong>42.4 / USD</strong>, combined with sluggish steel demand, is pushing facilities to operate at reduced capacity.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The overall picture for South Asia and Turkey is characterized by weak tonnage flows, flat or declining steel prices, weakening local currencies, and intensifying sanctions scrutiny. Conversely, HKC progress in <strong>Bangladesh</strong> and <strong>Pakistan</strong> holds potential for establishing a more sustainable recycling cycle beyond 2026. However, the sector's trajectory will depend both on how dark fleet pressures unfold and on how much freight markets recover in 2026.</p>
<h2 style="text-align: justify"><br></h2>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The global recycling market is under pressure from weak steel prices and currency volatility.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">EU and US sanctions are tightening scrutiny of the <strong>shadow fleet</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Bangladesh maintains its position as the price leader but transaction volumes remain low.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">In India–Alang, currency weakness and two-tiered pricing create risks.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Pakistan is entering a structural transformation phase with approval of its first HKC-compliant facility.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Turkey–Aliaga shows flat prices and demand.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The 2026 outlook hinges on how tonnage flows are affected by sanctions and freight market recovery.</p></li>
</ul>
