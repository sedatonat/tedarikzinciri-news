<p style="text-align: justify">According to ShippingWatch, container carriers are tightening capacity management against freight rate pressure on Asia-Europe and Transpacific lanes as the Iran war drags on. Analysts expect blank sailings, service cancellations and rerouting to continue into the second quarter.</p>

<p style="text-align: justify">Security risks in the Strait of Hormuz and periodic transit restrictions have pushed some Asia-Europe services to shift to transpacific routes via Panama. That shift has underpinned record Panama Canal auction prices and sharpened concerns about bunker fuel shortages.</p>

<p style="text-align: justify">In Q1 reports, the major carriers flagged pressure on margins from security risk premiums and rising bunker fuel prices. If demand weakens over the medium term, capacity growth could turn into sustained rate pressure. Industry executives therefore continue to use blank sailings as an active tool.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. Container carriers are tightening capacity management amid the Iran war.<br>
2. Blank sailings and service cancellations are expected to continue into Q2.<br>
3. Some Asia-Europe services have shifted to the Panama-transpacific route.<br>
4. Panama Canal auction prices sit at record levels.<br>
5. Rising bunker fuel prices are pressuring carrier margins.</p>
