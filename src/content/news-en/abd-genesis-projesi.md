<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Genesis Mission Executive Order</strong>, issued by the U.S. President on November 24, 2025, is regarded as one of the most comprehensive federal initiatives aimed at transforming the nation's scientific and technological capacity through artificial intelligence. The executive order underscores the United States' commitment to achieving global leadership in the artificial intelligence race and emphasizes that this competition has become a matter of national security and strategic advantage, extending far beyond economic competition alone.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The document defines Genesis Mission as a national-scale program with the objective of <strong>"AI-accelerated innovation and discovery."</strong> This initiative aims to leverage the high-performance computing infrastructure of the <strong>DOE – Department of Energy</strong> and national laboratories to develop large-scale scientific foundation models and to automate research processes through <strong>AI agents</strong>. The invocation of the <strong>Manhattan Project</strong> as an analogy for the mission's inspiration underscores both its scope and strategic significance.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Under the executive order, Genesis Mission integrates the world's largest collection of scientific data, drawn from federal databases, into a single <strong>American Science and Security Platform</strong>. The platform is fundamentally composed of six components:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>HPC infrastructure</strong> including DOE's supercomputers</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Secure cloud-based AI computing environments</strong> for large-scale model training</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Automation with <strong>AI modeling and analysis frameworks</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Domain-specific scientific foundation models</strong> spanning multiple fields</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">A <strong>dataset ecosystem</strong> enabling secure access to federal, public, and synthetic data</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">AI-controlled <strong>autonomous experimentation &amp; manufacturing</strong> infrastructure</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The active executive order grants DOE broad authority to build and operate the platform. Within the first 90 days, an inventory of federal computing resources must be completed; within 120 days, a standardization plan for datasets; within 240 days, identification of AI laboratory capabilities; and within 270 days, delivery of an "initial operating capability."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Another critical dimension of Genesis Mission is the compilation of a list of <strong>at least 20 major challenges</strong> to be addressed across national science and technology domains. DOE is required to prepare this list within 60 days and expand it in coordination with the <strong>APST – Assistant to the President for Science and Technology</strong> and the NSTC. These priorities include areas of high economic and geopolitical importance such as <strong>advanced manufacturing</strong>, <strong>biotechnology</strong>, <strong>critical materials</strong>, <strong>fusion &amp; fission energy</strong>, <strong>quantum information science</strong>, and <strong>semiconductors &amp; microelectronics</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The program's second phase focuses on <strong>interagency coordination</strong> across federal agencies. The NSTC, Chief Data Officer Council, and Chief AI Officer Council coordinate the alignment of federal databases, infrastructure, and research and development activities with this mission. The executive order also establishes a <strong>workforce development</strong> component that includes student researchers, AI fellowship programs, and internship and apprenticeship models at national laboratories.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Genesis Mission establishes a broad framework for public-private partnerships. DOE is tasked with establishing standards in areas such as <strong>cooperative research agreements</strong>, user facility programs, data usage protocols, and intellectual property management, while collaborating with external partners at the highest levels of cybersecurity.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Finally, the President requests that DOE provide a comprehensive <strong>annual report</strong> detailing the platform's progress, outputs from AI models, inter-laboratory integration, public-private projects, and commercial transformation results.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Genesis Mission is regarded as a landmark federal investment designed to accelerate U.S. scientific research processes, develop next-generation AI-based scientific models, and strengthen the nation's strategic technological advantage.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Genesis Mission is established as a national-scale program for AI-powered scientific breakthroughs.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">DOE is the primary executor; national laboratories will provide HPC and data infrastructure.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The program carries strategic importance comparable to the Manhattan Project.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Federal scientific data repositories are consolidated under a single <strong>secure AI platform</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">AI agents will automate research processes; foundation models will be developed.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Target domains for challenge solutions include quantum, semiconductors, fusion, biotech, and advanced manufacturing.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Annual progress reports will be prepared and platform capabilities will be expanded.</p></li>
</ul>
