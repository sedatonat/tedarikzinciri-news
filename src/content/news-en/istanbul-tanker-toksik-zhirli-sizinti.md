<p style="text-align: justify">Early this week, a <strong>toxic substance leak</strong> occurred on a newly built Panama-flagged tanker transiting in international waters off Istanbul, resulting in the death of a crew member. The incident took place as the tanker was beginning commercial operations, prompting swift action from Turkish authorities and the vessel's classification society and operator.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">While the tanker was on routine transit in international waters near Istanbul, a <strong>sudden gas leak</strong> erupted from one of the tanks containing the chemical cargo aboard the vessel. Initial reports indicated that a crew member working in the upper compartments of the tank inhaled the toxic gas in a short period of time, leading to his death at the scene.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Upon detection of the leak, the ship's captain immediately activated emergency procedures. Crew members were directed to evacuation points wearing breathing apparatus, and the <strong>emergency response team</strong> aboard the vessel isolated the affected area and shut down ventilation systems. The ship also made contact with the <strong>Turkish Coast Guard Directorate General</strong> in Istanbul to request medical and technical assistance.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Turkish authorities respond swiftly</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Coast Guard teams dispatched vessels to the area immediately upon notification of the chemical leak. Personnel reaching the scene ensured that conditions aboard the vessel were stabilized, gas measurements were conducted, and the leak was brought under control. The deceased crew member's body was removed from the vessel, and the remaining personnel underwent health checks. Authorities reported that two individuals showing signs of poisoning were placed under observation.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The tanker was subsequently directed to a <strong>controlled anchorage in Turkish territorial waters</strong> for safety purposes. Technical examination and evidence collection procedures were initiated at the location. While the precise cause remains unclear, the probable source of the leak is believed to be an <strong>unexpected pressure surge in the gaseous phase of the chemical product in the tank</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>International safety procedures activated</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">As the Panama-flagged vessel is newly built, all safety certificates are known to be current. However, the incident has brought renewed focus to <strong>confined space work protocols</strong> used particularly on chemical tankers and the effectiveness of gas detection systems. Experts emphasize that even on newly built vessels, gas accumulation inside tanks can have fatal consequences.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The classification society and the operator announced they have initiated an investigation to determine the details of the incident. In accordance with the <strong>IGC Code</strong> and <strong>STCW safety regulations</strong> established by the International Maritime Organization (IMO), the manner in which operations were conducted will be examined.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Regional safety and environmental risk limited</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to authorities, the incident remained contained within the vessel and there was no chemical spillage to the environment. As a result, <strong>no marine pollution risk materialized</strong>. However, the failure to disclose the type of toxic gas leak has raised questions about the nature of the incident.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Such incidents, given the intensive tanker traffic in the Istanbul Strait and its vicinity, represent an important warning with respect to both maritime safety and the emergency response capacity of port authorities.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">A newly built Panama-flagged tanker experienced a <strong>toxic leak</strong> near Istanbul.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">One crew member died in the incident.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Turkish teams responded quickly and brought the leak under control.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The vessel was directed to a controlled anchorage and technical investigation is ongoing.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">No environmental pollution risk materialized; the incident points to an operational human-caused accident.</p></li>
</ul>
