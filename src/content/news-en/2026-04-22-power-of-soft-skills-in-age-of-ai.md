<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> reports that artificial intelligence is rewriting the rulebook for procurement, according to <strong>SCB Think Tank</strong> analyst insight. Professionals face a challenging new workforce hurdle—balancing technological advancement with the irreplaceable human touch. Undoubtedly, <strong>AI</strong> delivers greater efficiency, increased automation, cost savings, and smarter decision-making. Yet it also creates a skills gap that is hard to ignore. Procurement leaders must juggle technology integration while making the most of human qualities that keep teams competitive. Consider supplier relationships. <strong>AI</strong> can analyze performance data, predict risks, and suggest cost-saving opportunities. Yet it is the procurement professional who builds trust, negotiates complex contracts, and resolves supplier issues requiring human relationship skills.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the primary <strong>procurement soft skills</strong> categories are: (1) <strong>negotiation</strong>; (2) <strong>communication</strong>; (3) <strong>relationship building</strong>; (4) <strong>strategic thinking</strong>; (5) <strong>change management</strong>; (6) <strong>cross-functional collaboration</strong>; (7) <strong>data interpretation</strong>; (8) <strong>cultural intelligence</strong>; (9) <strong>leadership</strong>; and (10) <strong>ethics and integrity</strong>. The <strong>World Economic Forum Future of Jobs Report</strong> 2025 identifies analytic thinking, resilience, <strong>AI</strong> and big data, leadership, and social impact as the core skills in demand for 2025-2030. The <strong>LinkedIn Workplace Learning Report</strong>, <strong>Coursera Global Skills Report</strong>, and <strong>Gartner CSCO</strong> skills trend reports are among the leading industry talent studies. The <strong>Institute for Supply Management</strong> (<strong>ISM</strong>, CEO <strong>Tom Derry</strong>) is the primary U.S. procurement professional organization, with the <strong>CPSM</strong> (<strong>Certified Professional in Supply Management</strong>) credential representing the U.S. industry standard.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the primary <strong>AI</strong> and <strong>generative AI</strong> applications for procurement include: (1) <strong>spend analytics</strong> (<strong>SAP Ariba</strong>, <strong>Coupa</strong>, <strong>GEP</strong>, <strong>Ivalua</strong>, <strong>Jaggaer</strong>); (2) <strong>contract analytics</strong> (<strong>Icertis</strong>, <strong>Conga</strong>, <strong>SirionLabs</strong>); (3) <strong>supplier discovery</strong> (<strong>Sourcing Network</strong>, <strong>Tealbook</strong>); (4) <strong>autonomous sourcing</strong> (<strong>Globality</strong>, <strong>Keelvar</strong>, <strong>Fairmarkit</strong>); (5) <strong>AI co-pilot for procurement</strong> (<strong>Microsoft Copilot for SAP</strong>, <strong>Coupa Navi</strong>); (6) <strong>tail spend management</strong>; (7) <strong>category management</strong>; (8) <strong>risk monitoring</strong> (<strong>Resilinc</strong>, <strong>Everstream</strong>); (9) <strong>e-invoicing automation</strong> (<strong>Tipalti</strong>, <strong>Bill.com</strong>); and (10) <strong>chatbot user experience</strong>. <strong>Anthropic Claude</strong>, <strong>OpenAI GPT-4o</strong>, <strong>Google Gemini</strong>, <strong>Meta Llama</strong>, <strong>Microsoft Azure OpenAI</strong>, <strong>AWS Bedrock</strong>, and <strong>NVIDIA NIM</strong> are among the primary enterprise <strong>LLM</strong>/<strong>generative AI</strong> platforms. <strong>McKinsey State of AI in Procurement</strong>, <strong>Deloitte Global CPO Survey</strong>, and <strong>Bain Procurement Excellence Study</strong> are leading consulting firm research reports in this space.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the primary procurement talent development and training providers include: <strong>Institute for Supply Management</strong> (<strong>ISM</strong>); <strong>Chartered Institute of Procurement &amp; Supply</strong> (<strong>CIPS</strong>, CEO <strong>Ben Farrell</strong>, <strong>UK</strong>); <strong>Association for Supply Chain Management</strong> (<strong>ASCM</strong>, formerly <strong>APICS</strong>, CEO <strong>Abe Eshkenazi</strong>); <strong>Council of Supply Chain Management Professionals</strong> (<strong>CSCMP</strong>); <strong>Procurement Leaders</strong> (owned by <strong>Wax Digital</strong>); <strong>NextLevel Purchasing Association</strong> (<strong>NLPA</strong>); <strong>Source One Management Services</strong>; and <strong>The Hackett Group</strong> (CEO <strong>Ted Fernandez</strong>). Leading U.S. academic supply chain and procurement programs include <strong>MIT Sloan</strong>, <strong>Harvard Business School</strong>, <strong>Wharton</strong>, <strong>Stanford GSB</strong>, <strong>Michigan State Eli Broad</strong>, <strong>Penn State Smeal</strong>, <strong>Arizona State W.P. Carey</strong>, and <strong>Tennessee Haslam</strong>. <strong>Gartner</strong>, <strong>Forrester Wave</strong>, <strong>Spend Matters</strong>, and <strong>Procurement Tactics</strong> are among the leading procurement technology research firms. In sum, the <strong>SCB Think Tank</strong>'s advocacy for soft skills underscores that the global procurement talent profile is being fundamentally redesigned in the post-<strong>AI</strong> era—with <strong>AI</strong> co-pilot tools and human value-add modules emerging as key strategic priorities for supply chain leaders.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>\n1. <strong>SCB Think Tank</strong> emphasizes the <strong>AI</strong> plus human balance—highlighting soft skills.<br>\n2. <strong>Negotiation</strong>, <strong>relationship building</strong>, and <strong>cultural intelligence</strong> are core soft skills.<br>\n3. <strong>SAP Ariba</strong>, <strong>Coupa</strong>, and <strong>GEP</strong> are leading <strong>AI</strong>-enabled procurement platforms.<br>\n4.
