<p style="text-align: justify">Amazon is preparing to break Prime delivery speed records for the <strong>third consecutive year in 2025</strong>. During its earnings call, Amazon's SVP &amp; CFO <strong>Brian Olsavsky</strong> emphasized that the primary driver of Amazon's acceleration is <strong>positioning inventory in the right locations</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Over the past two years, Amazon has transformed its fulfillment network into a new 8-region architecture, focusing on storing products in locations closer to consumers. This strategy has accelerated delivery speeds even further throughout 2025.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>U.S. Inbound Lead Time Cut by 4 Days</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Amazon has redesigned its inbound processes, reducing domestic inbound lead time in the U.S. by <strong>approximately 4 days compared to the prior year</strong>.<br>\nThis improvement has delivered:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">more efficient inventory purchasing,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">lower carrying stock,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">stronger working capital management<br>\n.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Olsavsky stated, "Thanks to this improvement, our inventory purchasing efficiency has increased, which provides an advantage to our cash management."</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>The "Stock Closer to Consumer" Effect on Delivery Speed</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Amazon's regional network strategy reduces the distance products must travel to reach customers, accelerating both fulfillment and last-mile operations. As a result:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">fewer transfer points,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">fewer handling points,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">faster (and cheaper) delivery<br>\nare achieved.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">This strategy enables Amazon to deliver <strong>record-level Prime speeds</strong>. If current pace is maintained, 2025 will be the <strong>third consecutive record year</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>3-Hour Delivery and Rural Area Acceleration</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">CEO <strong>Andy Jassy</strong> announced that Amazon has begun rolling out a <strong>3-hour delivery</strong> model in select U.S. cities.<br>\nAdditionally:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Access to same-day and next-day delivery in rural areas has increased by <strong>60% over the past 4 months</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">This demonstrates that Amazon's speed investments extend beyond major cities to low-density markets.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">According to Jassy, these speed gains are redefining consumer expectations from Amazon.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Major Expansion in Grocery Delivery</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Improvements in inventory placement have also strengthened Amazon's <strong>grocery delivery capabilities (Amazon Fresh + Whole Foods)</strong>.</p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Amazon currently:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">offers free same-day delivery for perishables in <strong>more than 1,000 cities and towns</strong>,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">plans to expand this reach to <strong>2,300 locations by the end of 2025</strong>.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Jassy remarked, "Now customers can order milk alongside electronics in the same cart and have everything delivered to their door in a few hours. This is a game-changer."</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Costs Remain Controlled as Speed Increases</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">In Q3 2025, Amazon's fulfillment expenses increased <strong>12% year-over-year</strong>. However:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The main drivers of the increase are sales volume and network investments,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">due to operational efficiencies, the ratio of fulfillment costs to net sales slightly declined from <strong>15.5% to 15.4%</strong>.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">This demonstrates that acceleration has not entirely inflated costs, and Amazon has achieved a <strong>balance between scale and efficiency</strong>.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Industry Takeaway: "Speed is Now Table Stakes, Not Competitive Advantage"</strong></h5>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Amazon's aggressive inventory positioning model:</p>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">more regional fulfillment center placement,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">inbound optimization,</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">SKU distribution managed through machine learning<br>\nhas set a new benchmark in the logistics sector.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Competing retailers, market chains, and quick-commerce players are expected to accelerate similar investments during 2025–2026.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Amazon is on track for the <strong>third consecutive delivery speed record in 2025</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">U.S. inbound lead time has been <strong>cut by 4 days</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">3-hour delivery has launched in select U.S. cities.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Fast delivery access in rural areas has increased by <strong>60%</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Same-day grocery delivery is expanding from <strong>1,000 to 2,300</strong> locations.</p></li>\n  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Fulfillment cost ratio declined slightly: <strong>15.5%
