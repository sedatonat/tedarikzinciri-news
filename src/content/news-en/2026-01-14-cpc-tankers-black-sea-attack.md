<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Caspian Pipeline Consortium (CPC)</strong>'s <strong>Black Sea</strong> loading terminal has come under attack with reports that two oil tankers have been struck; the incident marks the latest significant disruption complicating <strong>Kazakhstan</strong>'s crude oil exports. According to sources close to the matter, the <strong>Delta Harmony</strong> and <strong>Matilda</strong> vessels were queued at the <strong>CPC</strong>'s offshore buoy station awaiting loading of barrels originating from <strong>Kazakhstan</strong>. Operators of both vessels have confirmed the incidents and stated that their ships have moved away from the area following the attacks. Before the assault, the vessels had departed the <strong>CPC</strong> facility while waiting their turn for loading; the extent of damage to both vessels remained unclear immediately.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The attacks heighten the risk of further disruptions to loading operations at <strong>CPC</strong>. Planned shipments have already experienced significant declines due to harsh winter conditions and buoy damage sustained from a drone strike in November. These interruptions are moderating a notable surplus in the global oil market this quarter and supporting important futures contract prices. The <strong>CPC</strong>, which exported crude oil at rates as high as 1.7 million barrels per day during certain periods last year, is currently loading between 800,000 and 900,000 barrels daily in January. <strong>Kazakhstan</strong>'s energy ministry, in a statement via <strong>Telegram</strong>, confirmed the incidents; noting that <strong>Matilda</strong> experienced an explosion without fire and sustained no critical hull damage, while <strong>Delta Harmony</strong> experienced a fire that was rapidly extinguished with no crew injuries.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Caspian Pipeline Consortium</strong> press office declined to comment on the matter. <strong>Matilda</strong>'s operator <strong>Thenamaris</strong> stated that its vessel was struck by an unspecified number of drones outside the <strong>CPC</strong> terminal, sustained limited deck damage, and maintains full seaworthiness. <strong>Delta Harmony</strong>'s operator <strong>Delta Tankers</strong> disclosed that the vessel was hit by a projectile and experienced a brief fire that was extinguished by crew, with the ship departing the high-risk area. <strong>Chevron</strong>, which chartered one of the tankers, reported that the vessel remained stable and was being directed to a safe port, with no impact observed on its <strong>Kazakhstan</strong> operations or exports.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Ukraine</strong>'s military authorities did not comment on the matter; they have previously confirmed involvement in strikes against Russia-linked tankers. Cargo operations at the <strong>CPC</strong> were halted over the weekend of January 10-11 due to adverse weather conditions; <strong>Kazakhstan</strong>'s energy ministry stated that operations were proceeding normally as of January 13. According to port agency data, one vessel arrived at the terminal on January 12 and was scheduled to depart the following day. From a supply chain perspective, these incidents directly impact insurance premiums on crude oil transported across the <strong>Black Sea</strong>, freight rates, and route preferences, while reigniting strategic discussions about <strong>CPC</strong>'s alternative export pathways.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. <strong>Delta Harmony</strong> and <strong>Matilda</strong> tankers have been attacked near the <strong>CPC</strong> terminal.<br>
2. <strong>CPC</strong> is loading between 800,000 and 900,000 barrels daily in January.<br>
3. <strong>Thenamaris</strong> stated that <strong>Matilda</strong> remains seaworthy.<br>
4. <strong>Chevron</strong> reports no impact on <strong>Kazakhstan</strong> operations.<br>
5. The incidents are moderating surplus in the global oil market and supporting prices.</p>
