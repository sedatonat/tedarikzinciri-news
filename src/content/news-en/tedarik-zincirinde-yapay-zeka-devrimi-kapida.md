<p>Remko van Hoek, professor of Supply Chain Management at the University of Arkansas, emphasized the need for a fundamental rethinking of procurement processes at the Digital Procurement World (DPW) 2024 conference in Amsterdam, stating that "procurement will look very different within a year or a year and a half."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>The conference highlighted that supply chains will undergo radical automation transformation through the adoption of large language model artificial intelligence and generative AI technologies. However, it was noted that most companies are lagging in this transformation, with progress in supply process automation ranging between 15% and 50%.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Shashi Mandapathy, Chief Procurement Officer at Johnson &amp; Johnson, noted that procurement is a rare business function that impacts all stakeholders—customers, employees, suppliers, and communities. Mandapathy expressed that making supply chains more transparent and efficient could make the world a better place.</p>
<p>Paul Polman, former CEO of Unilever and author of the book "Net Positive," stated that 80% of sustainability-enhancing technologies now pay for themselves. Polman illustrated with examples how human-centered approaches increase efficiency and how the cost of taking action is lower than waiting too long.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Kris Timmermans from Accenture Strategy noted that supply chains that do not rapidly adopt artificial intelligence-enabled automation will face serious challenges. He stated that rising trade restrictions, climate change, and cybersecurity risks make supply chains more complex, and this complexity cannot be managed with human resources alone.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p>Jag Lamba, CEO of Certa, presented a demonstration showing what artificial intelligence can do in procurement processes. Lamba noted that generative AI is ideal for procurement and that this technology will lead to a productivity revolution.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li><p>Remko van Hoek stated that procurement processes will undergo radical transformation within the next year and a half.</p></li>
  <li><p>Companies are lagging in adopting large language model artificial intelligence and generative AI technologies.</p></li>
  <li><p>Shashi Mandapathy emphasized that procurement is a rare business function affecting all stakeholders.</p></li>
  <li><p>Paul Polman expressed that sustainable technologies pay for themselves and stressed the importance of taking action.</p></li>
  <li><p>Kris Timmermans stated that supply chains cannot be managed without artificial intelligence.</p></li>
  <li><p>Jag Lamba presented a demonstration showing that artificial intelligence will revolutionize procurement processes.</p></li>
</ul>
