<p>DHL Supply Chain deployed two artificial intelligence applications last month in collaboration with Boston Consulting Group (BCG). The logistics service provider aims to enhance its data management and analytics capabilities through these Generative AI (GenAI) applications. The company is using this technology to improve customer insights, better evaluate proposals, and deliver more "personalized" solutions.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Markus Voss, DHL Supply Chain Global Chief Development Officer, stated, "Generative AI is transforming the way we manage data and develop proposals." Voss noted that engineers who design logistics concepts can work far more efficiently after GenAI cleans and evaluates data from potential customers.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>CIO Mike Kreider told Supply Chain Dive that DHL Supply Chain manages its GenAI use cases through a "product funnel approach" that includes a pilot phase. The two use cases, referred to as the first and second GenAI applications, currently target specific DHL Supply Chain user groups and adopt an intentional product rollout strategy.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>The first GenAI use case is designed for business development, enabling faster analysis of customer requirements. Kreider noted that thanks to GenAI's data management, DHL Supply Chain's business development team can create more personalized proposals. The second GenAI use case helps DHL Supply Chain's solutions design team serve customers better by organizing existing data to provide improved solutions.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>The press release also stated that AI tools can summarize customer queries and process legal documents. Kreider said, "DHL is transforming our core business processes using GenAI tools and enhancing our analytics capabilities. These two GenAI solutions are being prepared for full implementation and will be fully deployed shortly."</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Beyond adding AI to its operations, DHL Supply Chain continues to use automation and technology for workforce retention and warehouse management. The company appointed a new CEO for North America earlier this year and continues its technology investments.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>These innovations aim to further strengthen DHL Supply Chain's customer-focused services and gain competitive advantage in the logistics sector. The data analytics and automation capabilities provided by GenAI increase the company's operational efficiency while enabling it to deliver more customized solutions to customers.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5>Key Highlights:</h5>\n<ul>\n  <li><p><strong>Partnership:</strong> DHL Supply Chain deployed two GenAI applications in collaboration with Boston Consulting Group (BCG).</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Objectives:</strong> Data management, analytics capabilities, customer insights, proposal evaluation, and delivery of customized solutions.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Executive Statements:</strong> Markus Voss and Mike Kreider discuss GenAI's contributions to efficiency and process transformation.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Use Cases:</strong> Customer requirement analysis for business development and data organization for solutions design.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Additional Functions:</strong> Summarizing customer queries and processing legal documents.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Rollout Approach:</strong> Product funnel approach and pilot phase.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Additional Technologies:</strong> Use of automation and technology for warehouse management.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>Management Change:</strong> Appointment of a new CEO for North America.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>---</p>\n<p><br></p>\n\n<p><br></p>\n<p>-------------------</p>\n\n<p>We would be delighted to receive your feedback.</p>\n<p>We wish you happy reading in advance.</p>
