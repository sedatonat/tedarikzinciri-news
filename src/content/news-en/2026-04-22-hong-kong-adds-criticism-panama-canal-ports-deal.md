<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> reports; analyst insight; <strong>Hong Kong</strong>'s — <strong>People's Republic of China</strong>'s Special Administrative Region (<strong>SAR</strong>) — Chief Executive <strong>John Lee</strong>; on March 18; stated that the conglomerate's deal to sell off its interest in two ports flanking the entrances and exits of the <strong>Panama Canal</strong> deserves "serious attention" — according to the <strong>New York Times</strong>. This adds to criticism voiced by the <strong>Chinese</strong> government — in a strongly worded opinion article in the <strong>Beijing</strong>-backed newspaper <strong>Ta Kung Pao</strong> on March 14; attacking the United States for pressuring the deal through "despicable means." The agreement between <strong>Hong Kong</strong>-based <strong>CK Hutchison</strong> and <strong>BlackRock</strong>, the world's largest asset manager, comes as a response to allegations by President <strong>Donald Trump</strong> that <strong>China</strong> controls the <strong>Panama Canal</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>CK Hutchison Holdings</strong>; <strong>Hong Kong</strong>-based; <strong>Victor Li</strong> Chairman (son of <strong>Li Ka-shing</strong>; <strong>Li Ka-shing</strong> Founder); operates globally in ports, retail, infrastructure, energy, and telecommunications. <strong>Hutchison Ports</strong>; the port division of <strong>CK Hutchison</strong> — operates 53 ports across 24 countries globally, making it the world's largest port operator. <strong>Panama Ports Company</strong> (<strong>PPC</strong>); <strong>Hutchison Ports</strong>' subsidiary in <strong>Panama</strong> — operates the <strong>Balboa</strong> (Pacific) and <strong>Cristóbal</strong> (Atlantic) ports. <strong>BlackRock</strong>; <strong>Larry Fink</strong> CEO; <strong>New York</strong>-based; as of end of 2025, manages 11.6+ trillion dollars in assets under management (<strong>AUM</strong>); the world's largest asset manager — <strong>Global Infrastructure Partners</strong> (<strong>GIP</strong>; <strong>Bayo Ogunlesi</strong> Chairman) October 2024 acquisition; primary port/infrastructure investment capacity. <strong>Terminal Investment Limited</strong> (<strong>TIL</strong>; <strong>MSC</strong>-owned) consortium partner — total deal value ~22.8 billion dollars — primary sale of <strong>CK Hutchison</strong>'s port portfolio (43 ports; ~80% stake).</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Panama Canal Authority</strong> (<strong>Autoridad del Canal de Panamá</strong>; <strong>ACP</strong>); <strong>Ricaurte Vásquez Morales</strong> Administrator; established 1999; <strong>Panama City, Panama</strong>-based; owner and operator of the <strong>Panama Canal</strong>. The <strong>Panama Canal</strong>; approximately 80 km long; carries 5-6% of global maritime trade — the shortest route from the U.S. East Coast to Asia. The 2023-2024 drought; due to <strong>Gatun Lake</strong> water level crisis; has reduced daily transits from 36-38 vessels to 22-24 vessels. The 2016 <strong>Panama Canal Expansion</strong> (new <strong>Neopanamax locks</strong>); allows passage of vessels up to 14,000 TEU. <strong>Panama Canal</strong>-dependent U.S. East Coast ports; <strong>Port of Savannah</strong>, <strong>Port of Houston</strong>, <strong>Port of Charleston</strong>, <strong>Port of NY/NJ</strong>, <strong>Port of Norfolk</strong>, <strong>Port of Miami</strong>; are the primary affected U.S. ports. <strong>Donald Trump</strong> threatened in December 2024 to "reclaim the <strong>Panama Canal</strong>" — <strong>Marco Rubio</strong> traveled to <strong>Panama City</strong> in February 2025 — meeting with <strong>Panama</strong>'s Head of State <strong>José Raúl Mulino</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, global major port operator consolidation trends; (1) <strong>Hutchison Ports</strong> (<strong>CK Hutchison</strong>); (2) <strong>PSA International</strong> (<strong>Singapore</strong>); (3) <strong>DP World</strong> (<strong>Sultan Ahmed bin Sulayem</strong> Chairman; <strong>Dubai</strong>); (4) <strong>APM Terminals</strong> (<strong>Maersk</strong>); (5) <strong>COSCO Shipping Ports</strong>; (6) <strong>Terminal Investment Limited</strong> (<strong>TIL</strong>; <strong>MSC</strong>); (7) <strong>HHLA</strong> (<strong>Hamburger Hafen und Logistik AG</strong>; <strong>MSC</strong>-owned); (8) <strong>SSA Marine</strong> (<strong>Carrix</strong>; <strong>Goldman Sachs</strong>); (9) <strong>Yilport</strong> (<strong>Yıldırım Holding</strong>; <strong>Turkey</strong>); (10) <strong>Eurogate</strong>; are the major global port operators. <strong>State Administration for Market Regulation</strong> (<strong>SAMR</strong>; <strong>China</strong>); may review the <strong>CK Hutchison</strong>/<strong>BlackRock</strong> deal for antitrust — is a primary regulatory risk. <strong>U.S. Committee on Foreign Investment</strong> (<strong>CFIUS</strong>); <strong>Belt and Road Initiative</strong> (<strong>BRI</strong>; <strong>China</strong>); are the primary U.S.-China investment tension frameworks. <strong>BIMCO</strong>; <strong>International Chamber of Shipping</strong> (<strong>ICS</strong>); are major global maritime sector organizations. In conclusion, <strong>Hong Kong</strong>'s criticism of the <strong>CK Hutchison</strong>/<strong>BlackRock</strong> deal; reflects a fundamental restructuring of <strong>Panama Canal</strong> geopolitical pressures globally — for supply chain managers, East Coast route strategy and <strong>Panama</strong> disruption risk; appear to be a primary strategic priority.</p>
