<p>Volvo Group and Westport Fuel Systems Inc. have launched a joint venture aimed at accelerating the commercialization of renewable-fuel internal combustion engine (ICE) technology for long-haul and off-road applications. The initiative targets cost-effective solutions for demanding sectors, particularly heavy-duty transportation and off-road applications. Utilizing Westport's high-pressure direct injection (HPDI) fuel system, the venture will replace greenhouse-gas-emitting fuels like diesel with carbon-neutral or zero-carbon fuels such as biogas or hydrogen. This approach enables users to continue operating internal combustion engines without compromising torque, power, or efficiency while achieving near-zero greenhouse gas emissions.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>This new effort is supported by billions of dollars in funding provided by the Biden Administration, as new regulations come into force in regions such as California that will mandate the use of battery electric vehicles only. However, this federal effort has focused on supporting hydrogen fuel cells that generate electricity rather than hydrogen combustion engines.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>According to Volvo and Westport, using internal combustion engines powered by renewable fuels is the best way to reduce transportation carbon emissions in certain sectors. The joint venture will operate as an independent unit, with Westport contributing fixed assets, intellectual property, and business related to HPDI. Volvo Group is participating in the joint venture with a 45% stake for approximately $28 million.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5><strong>Key Takeaways</strong></h5>\n<ol>\n  <li><p>The joint venture will focus on renewable-fuel ICE technology for heavy-duty transportation and off-road applications.</p></li>\n  <li><p>HPDI technology enables internal combustion engines to operate on carbon-neutral fuels, achieving near-zero emissions.</p></li>\n  <li><p>New regulations will soon take effect in California mandating the use of battery electric vehicles only.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The Biden Administration is providing billions of dollars in funding to support the green hydrogen market.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The joint venture will be temporarily managed by Westport CEO Dan Sceli.</p></li>\n</ol>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>News Link:</strong> <a href="https://www.thescxchange.com/articles/10361-volvo-and-partner-launch-joint-venture-to-provide-renewable-fueled-ice-vehicles"><u>https://www.thescxchange.com/articles/10361-volvo-and-partner-launch-joint-venture-to-provide-renewable-fueled-ice-vehicles</u></a></p>
