<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>FedEx CEO Raj Subramaniam</strong> said that fluctuations in global supply chains are not temporary but rather <strong>permanent and structural changes</strong>. Speaking in Singapore at the <strong>Bloomberg New Economy Forum</strong>, Subramaniam indicated that supply chains are progressing toward a <strong>"new equilibrium point"</strong>, particularly due to <strong>technological transformation, geopolitical tensions, and trade wars</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>"Regional supply chain model becoming permanent"</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Subramaniam, the global industrial economy is still in a state of transformation:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"A new supply chain order is taking shape, and this order is more <strong>regional</strong> in nature. The transformation of the industrial economy will take time, but once the transformation is complete, reversing it will be difficult."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">FedEx noted that because it operates one of the world's most extensive air transportation networks, it sees <strong>"signals from the ground"</strong> very quickly and adjusts capacity accordingly.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>U.S. tariffs hit parcel trade hard</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">U.S. President Donald Trump's:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Broad-based tariffs</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Elimination of de minimis exemptions</strong> (ending customs exemptions for low-value goods),</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">such measures have <strong>severely disrupted global parcel trade</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">FedEx had announced that it expects <strong>$1 billion in revenue loss</strong> in September 2025 due to trade uncertainty.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br>A large portion of this loss stems from <strong>a sharp decline in shipments from China to the U.S.</strong> This route has become the trade corridor where the trade war is most acutely felt.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Subramaniam said this trend would <strong>continue throughout 2026</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Capacity shifted from China–U.S. route to other regions</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">FedEx is reconfiguring the distribution of its <strong>aircraft fleet in response to changes in package volumes</strong>:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Sharp decline in volume from China to the U.S.,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">In contrast, increases are being observed in <strong>China–Europe</strong>, <strong>China–Latin America</strong>, and <strong>intra-Asia</strong> shipments.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">For this reason, FedEx is redirecting aircraft and network capacity <strong>to regions where demand is stronger.</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Subramaniam:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"We can move our capacity much faster than manufacturing supply chains can shift."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>"Companies are only now realizing the cost of holding inventory"</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Speaking on the same panel, <strong>ABB Board Chairman Peter Voser</strong> said that supply chain disruptions are no longer a phenomenon tied to electoral cycles but rather <strong>stem from structural market changes</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Voser, companies have begun to more clearly see, due to disruptions in recent years:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The risks of over-optimization,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The cost of single-source dependency,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The drawbacks of maintaining minimum stock levels<br></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Voser:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"Companies around the world have come to understand that the cost of disruption can be far higher than the cost of holding inventory."</blockquote>
<p style="text-align: justify">This suggests that the just-in-time approach may give way to more resilient <strong>"just-in-case"</strong> inventory strategies.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>New global order: regionalization + resilience</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to experts, the assessments by FedEx and ABB summarize the global trade dynamics of the post-2020 period:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">U.S.–China trade wars,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Geopolitical tensions (Ukraine, Middle East),</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Companies diversifying their supply sources,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Nearshoring and friend-shoring trends,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Digitalization and automation playing a larger role in supply chains…</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">These factors signal <strong>a permanent restructuring of global trade</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">FedEx CEO summarized this new order with these words:</p>
<blockquote style="text-align: justify">"A new market order is taking shape. When something changes, going back is very difficult."</blockquote>
<blockquote><br></blockquote>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">FedEx CEO: Global supply chains are shifting toward a <strong>"new regional balance."</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">U.S. tariffs and elimination of de minimis exemptions are <strong>pressuring global parcel trade.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">FedEx is redirecting China–U.S. capacity to China–Europe and China–Latin America routes.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">ABB: Companies are only now recognizing that <strong>disruption costs exceed inventory costs.</strong></p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Resilience, regionalization, and source diversification</strong> are becoming permanent features of supply chains.</p></li>
</ul>
