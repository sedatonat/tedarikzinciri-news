<p>The alternative fuels agenda in transportation is accelerating, with options spanning from diesel to renewable diesel (RD), HVO, CNG/LNG, hydrogen fuel cells, and battery-electric solutions. The first reality is that total cost of ownership (TCO) depends on more than a single variable; fuel prices, incentives, infrastructure access, and duty profile must be evaluated together. The second reality is that infrastructure readiness is decisive; the coverage of hydrogen and high-power charging networks constrains the speed of transition in long-haul operations. The third reality is carbon accounting; the well-to-wheel approach measures carbon not only at the exhaust but across the entire supply chain, which encourages choices aligned with Scope 3 targets. From an operational standpoint, route profiling and payload sensitivity analyses match the right technology to energy density requirements. In terms of maintenance and safety, high-voltage systems and high-pressure storage require specialized competencies; training &amp; certification plans must be implemented.&nbsp;</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>In conclusion, there is no single winning technology; fleets balance risks with a mixed energy portfolio.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>Key Points:</strong><br>\n1.
