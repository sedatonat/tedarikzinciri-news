<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>SupplyChainBrain</strong> reports that in today's fast-paced delivery environment, efficiency is not merely a goal—it is essential. <strong>GLS</strong> is addressing this challenge with its new application—<strong>GLS Go!</strong>—transforming traditional delivery methods into streamlined, eco-conscious operations that benefit businesses, drivers, and recipients. At the heart of <strong>GLS Go!</strong> lies advanced real-time route optimization technology. Using <strong>GLS</strong>'s geocoding technology, the application continuously analyzes delivery priorities to generate the most efficient routes possible. With live routing, drivers spend less time navigating and more time delivering—significantly improving delivery windows while reducing idle time and fuel consumption. The application's precision does not end with routing. Smart sequencing, one of the key features of <strong>GLS Go!</strong>, is the intelligent management of pickup and drop-off stops. The application automatically organizes stops using smart sequencing that takes time-sensitive packages and location details into account. Drivers cover more ground with less driving—flowing naturally from one stop to the next. Alongside route optimization, this application comes with built-in sustainability. By reducing wasted miles, <strong>GLS Go!</strong> directly cuts fuel consumption and carbon emissions. It represents a win for both operational efficiency and environmental responsibility.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>General Logistics Systems</strong> (<strong>GLS</strong>), based in <strong>Amsterdam, Netherlands</strong>, with CEO <strong>Karl Pfaff</strong>, separated from <strong>Royal Mail Group</strong> (<strong>UK</strong>) under <strong>International Distributions Services Plc</strong> after 2024 and was acquired by <strong>EP Group</strong> (led by <strong>Daniel Křetínský</strong>, <strong>Czechia</strong>). <strong>GLS</strong> is one of Europe's four largest parcel delivery companies—operating in 41 countries across key markets including the <strong>UK</strong>, <strong>Germany</strong>, <strong>France</strong>, <strong>Italy</strong>, <strong>Spain</strong>, <strong>Belgium</strong>, <strong>Netherlands</strong>, <strong>U.S.</strong>, <strong>Canada</strong>, <strong>Mexico</strong>, <strong>Hungary</strong>, <strong>Czechia</strong>, <strong>Slovakia</strong>, <strong>Poland</strong>, <strong>Romania</strong>, and <strong>Croatia</strong>, <strong>Serbia</strong>, <strong>Slovenia</strong>—delivering 1.0 billion parcels annually in 2024. <strong>GLS US</strong>, based in <strong>Lake Forest, Illinois, U.S.</strong>, was established in 2006 through the acquisition of the former <strong>GSO</strong> (<strong>Golden State Overnight</strong>) and offers 1-day and 2-day delivery services in the <strong>U.S.</strong> Other major last-mile delivery companies include <strong>UPS</strong> (CEO <strong>Carol Tomé</strong>), <strong>FedEx</strong> (CEO <strong>Raj Subramaniam</strong>), <strong>USPS</strong> (<strong>U.S. Postal Service</strong>), <strong>Amazon Logistics</strong>, <strong>DHL Express</strong>, <strong>OnTrac</strong>, and <strong>LaserShip</strong>, among the key ecosystem players.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, last-mile route optimization providers include <strong>Routific</strong>, <strong>OptimoRoute</strong>, <strong>Onfleet</strong>, <strong>Bringg</strong>, <strong>FarEye</strong> (CEO <strong>Kushal Nahata</strong>, <strong>India</strong>), <strong>DispatchTrack</strong> (owned by <strong>Beans.ai</strong>), <strong>Locus</strong> (CEO <strong>Nishith Rastogi</strong>, <strong>India</strong>), <strong>Project44</strong>, <strong>Descartes Systems</strong> (CEO <strong>Edward J. Ryan</strong>), <strong>Trimble Maps</strong>, <strong>HERE Technologies</strong> (CEO <strong>Edzard Overbeek</strong>, <strong>Netherlands</strong>), <strong>TomTom</strong>, <strong>Mapbox</strong>, <strong>Google Maps Platform</strong>, <strong>Esri ArcGIS</strong>, and <strong>OptimoRoute</strong>, among key ecosystem players. <strong>GLS Go!</strong>'s features include (1) real-time route optimization, (2) smart sequencing, (3) delivery window management, (4) customer SMS and email alerts, (5) driver performance tracking, (6) fuel consumption analysis, (7) electronic signature capture, (8) location verification, (9) attached package management, and (10) environmental reporting as core functions. <strong>GLS</strong>'s electric vehicle fleet aligns with major carbon reporting frameworks including the <strong>EU Emissions Trading System</strong> (<strong>EU ETS</strong>) and the <strong>Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive</strong> (<strong>CSRD</strong>)—with the <strong>GHG Protocol</strong> serving as the primary standard for <strong>Scope 1</strong>, <strong>Scope 2</strong>, and <strong>Scope 3</strong> emissions calculations.</p>

<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, last-mile delivery trends include (1) parcel lockers (<strong>Amazon Locker</strong>, <strong>Pudo</strong>, <strong>InPost</strong>, <strong>Parcel Pending</strong>, <strong>HIVED</strong>), (2) crowdsource delivery (<strong>Uber Eats</strong>, <strong>DoorDash</strong>, <strong>Roadie</strong>, <strong>Veho</strong>), (3) autonomous delivery robots (<strong>Starship Technologies</strong>, <strong>Nuro</strong>, <strong>Serve Robotics</strong>, <strong>Coco Robotics</strong>), (4) drone delivery (<strong>Amazon Prime Air</strong>, <strong>Wing</strong>, <strong>Zipline</strong>, <strong>Manna</strong>), (5) autonomous trucks (<strong>Aurora</strong>, <strong>Kodiak Robotics</strong>, <strong>Plus</strong>), (6) electric delivery vans (<strong>Rivian Amazon Delivery Van</strong>, <strong>Ford E-Transit</strong>, <strong>Mercedes eSprinter</strong>), (7) cargo bikes and micro-fulfillment, (8) same-day and 2-hour delivery, (9) green pickup points, and (10) predictive ETA and customer experience among the key innovation axes. <strong>FarEye</strong> and <strong>Bringg</strong> are recognized globally as leaders in the Last Mile Delivery Software category by <strong>Gartner</strong> and <strong>Forrester</strong>. In conclusion, <strong>GLS</strong>'s <strong>GLS Go!</strong> application represents a fundamental redesign of last-mile delivery operations and sustainability requirements globally, powered by artificial intelligence and real-time data—mobile-based driver applications and green route optimization appear to be key strategic priorities for supply chain managers.</p>
