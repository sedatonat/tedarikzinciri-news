<p style="text-align: justify">The fashion industry faces constantly evolving customs tariffs and trade policies due to the dynamic nature of global commerce. This situation directly impacts fashion brands' supply chains and cost structures. In recent years, particularly, trade tensions between the United States and China have had significant repercussions across the fashion sector. Many brands are reassessing their strategies to cope with these challenges, as <strong>Computer Generated Solutions</strong> (CGS) President <strong>Paul Magel</strong> has noted.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Paul Magel</strong> emphasizes that fashion brands need to be proactive in facing such trade barriers. He points out that brands must mitigate risks by diversifying their supply chains and seeking alternative production centers. In particular, other countries in Asia are being evaluated as potential alternatives for manufacturing. This strategy reduces brands' dependency on a single country, making them more flexible in adapting to sudden changes in trade policies.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, <strong>Magel</strong> draws attention to the importance of technology in this process. Advanced supply chain management software and analytical tools help brands make faster and more informed decisions. Through these technologies, companies can identify potential disruptions in their supply chains in advance and take necessary precautions.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Furthermore, brands are encouraged to adopt <strong>nearshoring</strong> strategies to respond more quickly to customer demand. Shifting production to regions closer to consumer markets reduces costs and shortens delivery times. This approach helps brands maintain their competitive advantage.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Magel</strong> points out that sustainability must not be overlooked in this process. Environmentally friendly production methods and the use of sustainable materials help brands comply with regulatory requirements and meet consumer expectations. This, in turn, enhances brands' reputation and customer loyalty in the long term.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, fashion brands must develop flexible and proactive strategies to adapt to changing trade policies and customs tariffs. Supply chain diversification, effective use of technology, <strong>nearshoring</strong>, and sustainability-focused approaches help brands succeed during this challenging period.​</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Supply chain diversification provides flexibility against changes in trade policies.​</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Effective use of technology helps make faster and more informed decisions in supply chain management.​</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Nearshoring</strong> strategies reduce costs and shorten delivery times.​</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Sustainable production methods ensure both regulatory compliance and meet consumer expectations.​</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Proactive and flexible strategies help brands maintain their competitive advantage.</p></li>
</ul>
