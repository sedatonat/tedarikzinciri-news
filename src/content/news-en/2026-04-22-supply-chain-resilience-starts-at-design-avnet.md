<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Robert Cowan</strong>, director of supply chain solution development at <strong>Avnet Velocity</strong>, offers analyst insight that today's products are born from hyper-connected supply chain networks. When security and resilience are not embedded at the design stage, organizations face risk from long-term vulnerabilities that ripple across the globe. The high-tech sector in particular is witnessing the butterfly effect of distant disruptions. Yet today, supply chain professionals are leveraging cutting-edge technology to adapt faster. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Avnet</strong>, based in <strong>Phoenix, Arizona</strong>, is a global electronic components distributor within the <strong>Fortune 500</strong>. <strong>Arrow Electronics</strong>, <strong>Future Electronics</strong>, <strong>WPG Holdings</strong>, <strong>TTI Inc.</strong> (<strong>Berkshire Hathaway</strong>), <strong>Mouser Electronics</strong>, <strong>Digi-Key</strong>, and <strong>Element14</strong> (<strong>Newark</strong>) are <strong>Avnet</strong>'s major competitors.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Despite facing a landscape shaped by geopolitical shifts and environmental challenges, the world's interconnected supply chains are demonstrating remarkable adaptability and growth potential. Each disruption serves not merely to expose vulnerabilities but as a catalyst for innovation and positive transformation across industries. Organizations are learning to anticipate volatility and investing in smarter, more flexible strategies that not only protect profit margins but also open new pathways for collaboration and efficiency. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>design for supply chain</strong> (<strong>DfSC</strong>), <strong>design for manufacturing</strong> (<strong>DfM</strong>), <strong>design for assembly</strong> (<strong>DfA</strong>), <strong>design for excellence</strong> (<strong>DfX</strong>), <strong>design for sustainability</strong>, and <strong>design for compliance</strong> are core disciplines of modern <strong>NPI</strong> (<strong>New Product Introduction</strong>) processes.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">Nearly all supply chains have experienced disruptions in the past two years, with half of them classified as high-impact. These disruptions increase costs across supply chain networks and reduce revenue. By embracing change and leveraging advanced technologies, businesses are turning obstacles into opportunities. They are emerging better prepared and positioned to thrive in a continuously evolving global marketplace. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>BOM</strong> (<strong>Bill of Materials</strong>) design decisions—<strong>sole-source</strong>, <strong>dual-source</strong>, <strong>second-source qualification</strong>, <strong>part standardization</strong>, <strong>obsolescence management</strong>, and <strong>component lifecycle visibility</strong>—are core disciplines of the <strong>EMS</strong> (<strong>Electronics Manufacturing Services</strong>) industry. <strong>SiliconExpert</strong>, <strong>IHS Markit</strong> (<strong>S&P</strong>), <strong>Z2Data</strong>, <strong>Octopart</strong> (<strong>Altium</strong>), <strong>Findchips</strong>, and <strong>Ciiva</strong> are component data and risk intelligence providers.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, <strong>resilience by design</strong> strategy encompasses <strong>multi-source qualification</strong>, <strong>geographic diversification</strong>, <strong>strategic stockpile</strong>, <strong>buffer inventory</strong>, <strong>flexible manufacturing</strong>, and <strong>postponement</strong> tactics. The <strong>chip shortage</strong> (2020-2023), <strong>capacitor crisis</strong>, <strong>MOSFET shortage</strong>, <strong>FPGA scarcity</strong>, and <strong>automotive grade chip</strong> supply disruptions have been among the high-tech sector's greatest tests in recent years. The <strong>CHIPS and Science Act</strong> (<strong>U.S.</strong>), <strong>European Chips Act</strong>, <strong>Taiwan</strong>'s <strong>TSMC</strong> dominance, <strong>Korea</strong>'s <strong>Samsung</strong>, <strong>SK Hynix</strong>, <strong>Japan</strong>'s <strong>Renesas</strong>, <strong>Sony</strong>, <strong>Toshiba</strong>, and <strong>China</strong>'s <strong>SMIC</strong>, <strong>YMTC</strong> are central players in semiconductor geopolitics. <strong>Avnet Velocity</strong>, as <strong>Avnet</strong>'s supply chain solutions and consulting division, provides sourcing, logistics, and compliance services to <strong>OEM</strong> customers. In conclusion, <strong>Cowan</strong>'s insight clearly demonstrates that the design phase is the true starting point for supply chain resilience.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>\n1.
