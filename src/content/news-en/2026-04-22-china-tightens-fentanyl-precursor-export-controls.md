<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>China</strong> is tightening export controls on fentanyl precursor chemicals being shipped to <strong>North America</strong> &mdash; as <strong>China</strong> and the <strong>U.S.</strong> continue to make progress toward ending months of trade turbulence between the two nations. According to <strong>The New York Times</strong>, <strong>China</strong>&rsquo;s Ministry of Commerce is requiring manufacturers to obtain a license to export 13 separate chemicals to the <strong>U.S.</strong>, <strong>Mexico</strong>, and <strong>Canada</strong>. Among these chemicals are several precursors used to synthesize fentanyl, the opioid that has triggered a sharp rise in overdose deaths across the <strong>U.S.</strong> for years. The <strong>Centers for Disease Control and Prevention</strong> (<strong>CDC</strong>) estimates fentanyl killed more than 48,000 people in 2024 &mdash; compared to more than 76,000 in the prior year. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>fentanyl</strong>, as a synthetic opioid, is 50-100 times more potent than morphine and 50 times more potent than heroin &mdash; making it the primary driver of the deadliest drug crisis in the past decade globally.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Trump</strong> administration has long criticized <strong>China</strong> for failing to adequately address illicit shipments of fentanyl precursors to the <strong>U.S.</strong> President <strong>Donald Trump</strong> has also identified the fentanyl crisis as one of the primary motivations behind the heavy tariffs being imposed on <strong>Chinese</strong> imports. From a supply chain perspective, <strong>Trump 2.0</strong> imposed a 20 percent &ldquo;<strong>fentanyl tariff</strong>&rdquo; on <strong>China</strong> under IEEPA in February 2025 &mdash; and a 25 percent fentanyl tariff on <strong>Mexico</strong> and <strong>Canada</strong>. The <strong>U.S.</strong> accounts for more than 75 percent of global fentanyl deaths &mdash; with primary entry routes being the <strong>U.S.-Mexico</strong> border (cartel production in <strong>Sinaloa</strong> and <strong>Jalisco New Generation</strong>), the <strong>U.S.-Canada</strong> border, and direct mail shipments from <strong>China</strong>. The <strong>U.S. DEA</strong> (<strong>Drug Enforcement Administration</strong>), <strong>CBP</strong> (<strong>Customs and Border Protection</strong>), <strong>HSI</strong> (<strong>Homeland Security Investigations</strong>), <strong>ICE</strong> (<strong>Immigration and Customs Enforcement</strong>), <strong>FBI</strong>, and <strong>USPS</strong> (<strong>U.S. Postal Service</strong>) are the primary agencies combating fentanyl.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the fentanyl supply chain comprises <strong>China</strong> precursor chemicals (<strong>NPP</strong>, <strong>4-ANPP</strong>, <strong>4-piperidone</strong>, <strong>norfentanyl</strong>, <strong>1-boc-4-anilinopiperidine</strong>), <strong>Mexico</strong> cartel synthesis (<strong>Sinaloa Cartel</strong>, <strong>Jalisco New Generation Cartel</strong> / <strong>CJNG</strong>), <strong>U.S.</strong> distribution (street gang networks), and fatal use (<strong>fentanyl-laced</strong> heroin, <strong>counterfeit pills</strong>, <strong>xylazine combo</strong> &ldquo;<strong>tranq</strong>&rdquo;) as the main pipeline. The <strong>UNODC</strong> (<strong>United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime</strong>) and <strong>INCB</strong> (<strong>International Narcotics Control Board</strong>) are the regulatory bodies for global narcotics control. The <strong>CCC</strong> (<strong>Counter Narcotics Working Group</strong>) is the bilateral cooperation framework between the <strong>U.S.</strong> and <strong>China</strong> &mdash; launched at the <strong>Biden-Xi APEC San Francisco</strong> summit in November 2023. The <strong>Trump-Xi APEC South Korea</strong> summit in October 2025 marks a new phase of trade-fentanyl negotiations. <strong>Scheduling</strong>, <strong>controlled substance</strong>, <strong>precursor chemical</strong>, and <strong>analog</strong> are the key terms in drug control legislation.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the global pharmaceutical and chemical supply chain comprises <strong>API</strong> (<strong>Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient</strong>), <strong>intermediate</strong>, <strong>excipient</strong>, and <strong>finished dosage form</strong> as the main production stages. <strong>China</strong> produces more than 40 percent of global <strong>APIs</strong> and pharmaceutical intermediates. <strong>Sinopharm</strong>, <strong>WuXi AppTec</strong>, <strong>WuXi Biologics</strong>, <strong>Hisun Pharmaceutical</strong>, <strong>Hengrui Medicine</strong>, <strong>Yangtze River Pharmaceutical</strong>, and the <strong>Beijing-Tianjin-Hebei</strong>, <strong>Yangtze River Delta</strong>, and <strong>Pearl River Delta</strong> regions represent <strong>China</strong>&rsquo;s major pharmaceutical clusters and players. <strong>Pfizer</strong>, <strong>Johnson &amp; Johnson</strong>, <strong>Roche</strong>, <strong>Novartis</strong>, <strong>Merck</strong>, <strong>AbbVie</strong>, <strong>AstraZeneca</strong>, <strong>GSK</strong>, <strong>Sanofi</strong>, <strong>Eli Lilly</strong>, and <strong>Bristol Myers Squibb</strong> are the global <strong>Big Pharma</strong> players. The <strong>U.S. BIOSECURE Act</strong> is legislation that restricts the <strong>U.S.</strong> from doing business with <strong>Chinese</strong> biotechnology companies (<strong>WuXi</strong>, <strong>BGI</strong>). In conclusion, <strong>China</strong>&rsquo;s move to place 13 fentanyl precursors under a licensing regime represents a significant win in the <strong>U.S.-China</strong> &ldquo;trade-narcotics&rdquo; negotiations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Points:</strong><br>
1. <strong>China</strong> is mandating export licenses for 13 fentanyl precursor chemicals.<br>
2. The <strong>U.S.</strong>, <strong>Mexico</strong>, and <strong>Canada</strong> are the licensing requirement destinations.<br>
3. The <strong>CDC</strong> reports 2024 fentanyl deaths at 48,000+, and 2023 at 76,000+.<br>
4. <strong>Trump</strong> is identifying fentanyl as a primary motivation for <strong>China</strong> tariffs.<br>
5. <strong>U.S.-China</strong> trade turbulence is signaling progress toward resolution.</p>
