<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Gemini Cooperation</strong>, formed by Maersk and Hapag-Lloyd, has attracted market attention through its strong on-time delivery performance in 2025. According to Sea-Intelligence data, Gemini achieved <strong>89.1% on-time reliability across all arrivals in September</strong>, positioning itself as one of the sector's most consistent networks.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This success has prompted the two carriers to <strong>consider charging a premium for reliability</strong>. However, this proposal has triggered a clear divide among shippers.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Gemini: "You're removing 1–2 weeks of inventory from the supply chain"</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">Gemini executives emphasize at conferences that reliability is now a genuine "cost-saving line item."<br>
The argument:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">More predictable sailings,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Less safety stock requirements,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Lower warehouse costs,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Less excess inventory,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Shorter cash conversion cycle.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Some major shippers agree with this view:<br>
 <strong>"If I'm holding two weeks less inventory, that translates to more savings numerically than the ocean freight itself."</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>But not all shippers see it that way</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">BCOs at the ShiftX conference in Hamburg <strong>split into two camps</strong>:</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>1) Those opposed to premium charges</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">This group raises the following objections:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">"Ships still route around Cape of Good Hope; transit times are already long."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">"Reliability depends more on schedule adherence than ship routing."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">"If they want premium pricing, the network must operate at full capacity with Suez open."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">"Ensuring reliability is already the carrier's responsibility — additional charges cannot be justified."</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Additionally, some shippers point out that they still experience <strong>feeder connection issues</strong> and <strong>transhipment delays</strong> even when ships arrive on time.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>2) Those warming to the premium</strong></h5>
<p style="text-align: justify">Shippers in this camp argue:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">inventory costs are rising,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">they want less variability in supply chains,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">schedule reliability directly impacts product availability.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">One BCO's comment stands out:<br>
 <strong>"89% reliability is worth its weight in gold in this market. If they guarantee it, a premium makes sense."</strong></p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Gemini's argument: "Reliability reduces costs"</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Maersk and Hapag-Lloyd, the Gemini partnership:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">streamlined connections through a centralized hub-and-spoke model,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">reduced the risk of domino effects (downstream delays) from late arrivals,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">delivered higher utilization, fewer delays, and more balanced service.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">For these reasons, they argue that "high reliability" should command premium pricing.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>The real issue: Shippers demand guarantees</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The main objection from those skeptical of premium charges:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">"No guarantee, no premium payment."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">"If there's an SLA like 89% → 94%, then we can talk."</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">"If you're charging a premium, refund mechanisms must apply if delays occur."</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">At a time when shipping traffic remains volatile and the Suez is closed, a "reliability surcharge" is viewed by many BCOs as <strong>premature and risky</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Market impact: Will the premium model spread?</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">If Gemini implements this pricing:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">other major carriers may adopt the "pay for reliability" model,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">supply chains may begin purchasing high SLA levels as a "premium product,"</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">but divisions among BCOs could deepen.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Given the perception of "collapsed reliability" seen in container shipping over the past 15 years, premium charges carry both <strong>opportunity and risk</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Gemini achieved <strong>89.1% on-time arrivals in September</strong>, placing it among industry leaders.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Maersk &amp; Hapag-Lloyd are considering premium pricing for high reliability.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">BCOs are split: "We'll pay" vs. "It's already their job, why should we?".</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Shippers are reluctant to pay premiums without guarantee mechanisms.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">If the premium model takes hold, similar practices may emerge across the market.</p></li>
</ul>
