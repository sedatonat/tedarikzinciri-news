<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Government of the Russian Federation</strong> has set a new quota for the export of <strong>ferrous metal scrap and waste</strong>. According to the Government press office, per the <strong>Governmental order signed on December 27, 2025</strong>, the quota amounts to <strong>2.2 million tons effective January 1, 2026</strong>. This is <strong>400 tons more</strong> than the quota previously set in August 2025.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The new quota is <strong>in force until December 31, 2026</strong>. The additional quota is to be distributed between exporters by the <strong>RF Ministry of Industry and Trade</strong>. The duty for ferrous metal scrap and waste export within the quota is <strong>5 per cent by no less than 15 euro per ton</strong>; for export exceeding the quota it is <strong>5 per cent no less than 290 euro per ton</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The core objective of scrap export restrictions is to preserve <strong>the Russian domestic steel industry</strong>'s access to affordable raw materials. Russian steel producers (Severstal, NLMK, MMK) need growing scrap supply to meet domestic demand during periods of constrained export markets. Türkiye is traditionally the largest buyer of Russian scrap; the quota restrictions are expected to create supply chain pressure for Turkish steel producers.</p>
