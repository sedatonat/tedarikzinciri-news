<p style="text-align: justify">Ukraine has taken a significant step toward establishing a new international fund for <strong>strengthening transport infrastructure</strong>, a critical component of post-war reconstruction efforts. The Kyiv administration, together with <strong>Canada</strong>, <strong>Lithuania</strong>, and <strong>Sweden</strong>, has signed a joint declaration for the <strong>Ukraine Transport Support Fund (UTSF)</strong>. The UTSF will be an international financing mechanism designed to address both urgent and long-term infrastructure needs.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Serhiy Derkach</strong>, First Deputy Minister of Ukraine responsible for crisis management, community development, and regional development, emphasized the fund's strategic importance, stating that the UTSF is not merely a <strong>political commitment</strong> but a <strong>"practical tool delivering real, tangible projects"</strong>. According to Derkach, the fund will play a central role in the processes of <strong>restoration &amp; modernization</strong> of roads, bridges, border checkpoints, logistics corridors, and public transport systems.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The UTSF declaration anticipates sending invitations to like-minded countries to join the fund in the early part of next year. This structure aims to transform the fund into a multilateral framework and manage flows of <strong>targeted donations</strong> according to Ukraine's needs.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Fund's Priority Objectives</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Ukraine, the fund's scope of work encompasses a broad range:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Urgent repairs:</strong> Rapid restoration of roads, railways, and bridges damaged by the war.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Strategic upgrades:</strong> Long-term investments supporting EU integration.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Border efficiency:</strong> Modernization of border crossing points, reducing time losses in logistics corridors.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Resilient logistics:</strong> Establishment of alternative logistics corridors and strengthening of critical infrastructure.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Public transport modernization:</strong> Renewal of urban transport systems.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Derkach emphasizes that the fund is particularly significant in the field of <strong>Europe connectivity</strong>. Ukraine's alignment with European Union infrastructure is viewed as critical for both the recovery of the national economy and the securitization of foreign trade flows.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>EU Standards Alignment: Standard-Gauge Railways</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The text particularly highlights efforts to transition to <strong>standard-gauge (1435 mm)</strong> railways. Shifting from broad gauge (1520 mm) to standard gauge for full integration into the EU railway network represents a long-term strategic goal for Ukraine in terms of <strong>interoperability</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Chop–Uzhhorod railway</strong> corridor is presented as one of the pilot projects for this transformation. This corridor will play a critical role in Ukraine's access to Central Europe and will shorten logistics timelines. Moreover, this integration is viewed as an important milestone in Ukraine's alignment with the <strong>TEN-T (Trans-European Transport Network)</strong> process.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Geopolitical Context and Fund's Significance</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Russia's post-2022 attacks caused severe damage to Ukraine's transport infrastructure. With major logistics corridors destroyed and security and capacity issues emerging in border regions, the UTSF aims to address this vulnerability and accelerate Ukraine's integration with the <strong>European logistics ecosystem</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Furthermore, the fund's support from NATO and EU-aligned countries such as Canada, Lithuania, and Sweden is also indicative of Ukraine's Western-oriented infrastructure diplomacy.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Conclusion</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The UTSF represents a critical step for Ukraine both in its post-war recovery process and in its path toward sustainable economic integration with Europe. Its multilateral donor structure and focus on "delivering concrete projects" establish the fund not merely as a financing resource but also as an instrument for <strong>transport-sector transformation</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Ukraine signed a joint declaration for the <strong>UTSF</strong> with Canada, Lithuania, and Sweden.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The fund targets modernization of roads, bridges, logistics corridors, border checkpoints, and public transport.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The UTSF will send invitations to other countries to participate in early 2026.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Standard-gauge</strong> projects (such as <strong>Chop–Uzhhorod</strong>) play a key role in EU integration.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The fund is viewed as a strategic instrument in Ukraine's post-war reconstruction.</p></li>
</ul>
