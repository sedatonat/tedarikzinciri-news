<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Port of Klaipėda</strong> has started the year with solid cargo growth, handling nearly <strong>11 million tonnes</strong> in the first quarter, up 9 per cent compared with the same period last year. The port was the only <strong>Baltic gateway</strong> to record positive growth, strengthening its regional position. Container handling was the standout performer, rising 38 per cent year-on-year (YoY) to more than 4 million tonnes.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Over <strong>374,000 TEUs</strong> were processed in the first three months of the year, with March marking the strongest monthly performance in both tonnage and container volumes in the port's history. <strong>Algis Latakas</strong>, CEO of the Klaipėda State Seaport Authority, said the results reflect sustained investment and adaptability across operations. Latakas commented: "Three months of stable and sustainable cargo growth – that's how we can describe the first quarter of this year. Consistent investments in infrastructure and terminal capacity have allowed Klaipėda Port to diversify cargo flows."</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">As Lithuania's largest maritime gateway, the Port of Klaipėda is positioned as a strategic transit hub for <strong>East-West supply chain flows</strong>. Following the significant decline in traditional Eastern European cargo flows transiting through Belarus, the port is pursuing a diversification strategy emphasising routes to Asia, Scandinavia and Western Europe. The strong growth in the container segment is seen as a tangible payoff from terminal modernisation and port logistics infrastructure investments highlighted by CEO Latakas.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. Port of Klaipėda handled 11 million tonnes in Q1 2026, up 9 per cent year-on-year.<br>
2. Container handling rose 38 per cent YoY to over 4 million tonnes.<br>
3. Over 374,000 TEUs were processed in Q1, with March the port's strongest month ever.<br>
4. Klaipėda was the only Baltic gateway to report positive cargo growth.<br>
5. CEO Algis Latakas attributed growth to consistent infrastructure and terminal capacity investment.</p>
