<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Turkey</strong>, <strong>Syria</strong> and <strong>Jordan</strong> are working to modernize their railway systems. The agreement aims ultimately to create a seamless corridor between southern <strong>Europe</strong> and the <strong>Persian Gulf</strong>. This information is based on a statement by <strong>Turkey</strong>'s Transport Minister <strong>Abdulkadir Uraloğlu</strong>. <strong>Uraloğlu</strong> told <strong>Bloomberg</strong> on April 15 that an agreement was reached during a meeting held last week with his <strong>Syrian</strong> and <strong>Jordanian</strong> counterparts. From a supply chain perspective, the <strong>Europe-Gulf</strong> overland corridor stands out as a strategic alternative to mitigate the geopolitical vulnerabilities of the <strong>Suez Canal</strong> route and the effects of the <strong>Iran</strong> conflict. Distinct from the main <strong>Asia-Europe</strong> flow, intra-Middle East overland traffic provides more predictable transit times compared to <strong>RoRo</strong> in the perishable, automotive parts and retail categories.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Uraloğlu</strong> says the network construction is expected to take four to five years, after which the system is planned to be connected to <strong>Saudi Arabia</strong>'s railway system. The minister did not provide details on the project's financing, only adding that discussions on the matter are ongoing. From a supply chain perspective, the 4-5 year construction timeline requires <strong>Turkey</strong> to reactivate and modernize the <strong>Aleppo</strong>-<strong>Damascus</strong> line and remnants of the <strong>Hejaz Railway</strong>. Clarification on the use of <strong>1,435 mm standard gauge</strong> is critical from an interoperability standpoint. Otherwise, the existing <strong>Hejaz Railway</strong> remnants' <strong>1,050 mm narrow gauge</strong> may require bogie change or transshipment at border crossings.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The project also includes improvements to roads leading into <strong>Turkey</strong>, though the scope of this work is not yet clear. From a supply chain perspective, capacity and modernization investments at border gates such as <strong>Cilvegozu</strong>, <strong>Öncüpınar</strong>, <strong>Habur</strong> and <strong>Nusaybin</strong> directly determine the success of <strong>TIR Carnet</strong>-based international road transport and intermodal transformation. <strong>Turkey</strong>'s <strong>Mersin</strong> and <strong>Iskenderun</strong> port complexes stand out as Mediterranean entry points for this corridor. On the European side, the <strong>Halkalı</strong>-<strong>Kapıkule</strong> main artery is integrated with <strong>Pan-European Corridor IV</strong> and <strong>Corridor X</strong> connections, reaching the <strong>Belgrade</strong>-<strong>Munich</strong>-<strong>Rotterdam</strong> axis.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">From a supply chain perspective, the connection to <strong>Saudi Arabia</strong> could provide a single railway chain from <strong>Europe</strong> to the <strong>Persian Gulf</strong> once the <strong>GCC Railway</strong> project extending from the <strong>Riyadh</strong>-<strong>Dammam</strong> axis through the <strong>Bahrain Causeway</strong> to the <strong>UAE</strong> is completed. This structure competes with the <strong>India-Middle East-Europe Economic Corridor (IMEC)</strong> and the <strong>Belt and Road Initiative (BRI)</strong>. <strong>Turkey</strong>, by virtue of its geographical position, is positioned at the intersection of both corridors. The <strong>Iskenderun</strong>-<strong>Mersin</strong> port axis and the <strong>Halkalı</strong> railway hub stand out as natural transshipment points for future container and <strong>RoRo</strong> flows. In conclusion, <strong>Uraloğlu</strong>'s statement represents the official articulation of <strong>Turkey</strong>'s medium-term strategic vision to integrate the <strong>Levant</strong>-<strong>Gulf</strong> line with <strong>European</strong> flows.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Points:</strong><br>
1. <strong>Turkey</strong>, <strong>Syria</strong> and <strong>Jordan</strong> reach agreement on railway modernization.<br>
2. <strong>Uraloğlu</strong> states the network will be built in 4-5 years.<br>
3. Connection to <strong>Saudi Arabia</strong>'s railway system is planned.<br>
4. Project financing is not yet clear; discussions are ongoing.<br>
5. Road improvements leading into <strong>Turkey</strong> are included in the plan.</p>
