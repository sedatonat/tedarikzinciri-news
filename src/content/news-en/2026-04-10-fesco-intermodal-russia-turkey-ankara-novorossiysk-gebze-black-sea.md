<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>FESCO Transport Group</strong> has launched a new intermodal container service between <strong>Ankara</strong> and <strong>Novorossiysk</strong> via <strong>Gebze</strong>. According to the Group, cargo is delivered by <strong>block trains from Ankara to Gebze</strong> and shipped by the <strong>FESCO Turkey Black Sea service</strong> to Novorossiysk. The transit time by rail is one to two days and by sea about three days on average.</p><p style="text-align: justify">From Novorossiysk, containers can be delivered to Moscow via the <strong>FESCO Novorossiysk Moscow Shuttle</strong> rail service and from there to other Russian regions. This structure offers Turkish-based manufacturers and traders uninterrupted access to Russia's domestic market. In export mode, FESCO transports from Russia to central Türkiye and provides a <strong>road delivery option to customers' warehouses in central Anatolia</strong>.</p><p style="text-align: justify">The service was launched in line with the developing bilateral trade volume between Türkiye and Russia. Russia, under Western sanctions following the Ukraine war, has turned to alternative trade partners; Türkiye has taken on a strategic role. FESCO's integrated service combines rail, sea, and road transport under a single booking, providing <strong>shippers a single point of contact</strong>. This is particularly preferred for automotive parts, textiles, food, and machinery shipments.</p>

<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong><br>
1. FESCO launched an intermodal service between Ankara and Novorossiysk via Gebze.<br>
2. Ankara-Gebze rail transit time is 1-2 days; Gebze-Novorossiysk sea time about 3 days.<br>
3. From Novorossiysk, containers can be delivered to Moscow via the FESCO Novorossiysk Moscow Shuttle.<br>
4. In export mode, road delivery to central Anatolia is offered from Russia.<br>
5. The service was launched alongside developing Türkiye-Russia bilateral trade volume.</p>
