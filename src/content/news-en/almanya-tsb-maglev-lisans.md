<p style="text-align: justify">Europe's long-standing regulatory barriers to <strong>maglev (magnetic levitation)</strong> technology have been overcome through a historic decision from Germany. The <strong>Federal Railway Authority (EBA)</strong> announced that it has granted a full operating license for passenger transport to <strong>Transport System Bögl (TSB)</strong>. This license confirms that TSB technology meets Germany's stringent safety and technical standards, and marks a turning point for maglev research in Europe, which has stalled since the cancellation of <strong>Transrapid</strong> projects.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">With the EBA's approval, TSB Betriebs GmbH can now legally operate as a <strong>railway infrastructure company</strong>. This distinction, mandated by German railway law, fulfills the requirement that infrastructure managers and operating units be independent from one another. TSB has thus evolved from a mere technology provider into a <strong>market-ready transport system</strong> compliant with infrastructure and operations models.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>A New Generation of Maglev Beyond Transrapid</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">TSB's most significant distinction is its focus not on speeds exceeding 400 km/h like the previous-generation <strong>high-speed Transrapid</strong>, but on the <strong>short-to-medium distance urban mobility</strong> segment. The system, with an operating speed of <strong>150 km/h</strong>, is optimized for <strong>commuter corridors</strong> such as airport connections, university shuttle routes, suburban-to-urban links, or fairground connections.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Max Bögl Group CEO <strong>Stefan Bögl</strong> described this license as "a first in Europe":<br>
 <strong>"This confirms that our technology is not just a vision, but a market-ready solution for the mobility of the future."</strong><br>
This statement demonstrates that the company's decade-long research and development efforts on modular maglev lines have gained official recognition.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Technical Characteristics of TSB Architecture</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The TSB system differs distinctly from conventional rail systems and earlier maglev designs:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Speed:</strong> 150 km/h, operating within the low-to-medium speed efficiency band.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Driverless Operation (GoA4):</strong> Provides fully automatic driving capability with metro-level service reliability.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Propulsion:</strong> Linear motor technology is embedded in the guideway; the vehicle houses all propulsion equipment and magnetic levitation mechanisms within its body "enveloping" the guideway.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Infrastructure:</strong> Prefabricated concrete tracks enable rapid deployment, operating more quietly and requiring less maintenance compared to steel rail systems.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Noise & Routing:</strong> Low noise emissions and tighter turning radius enable adaptation to urban environments.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">This approach makes TSB a more <strong>scalable</strong>, <strong>modular</strong>, and city-focused solution, independent of the massive costs encountered by high-speed maglev projects.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Next Step: Nuremberg Pilot Project</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Following the license, attention has turned to where Germany's first commercial maglev line will be built. The most advanced discussions are being conducted in the city of <strong>Nuremberg</strong>. Feasibility studies for a <strong>TSB urban line</strong> connecting the city's university campus, trade fair center (<strong>Messe</strong>), and <strong>Klinikum Süd</strong>—locations generating high passenger demand—are ongoing. The license grant may accelerate decision-making processes by the city council and federal authorities.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Experts describe the absence of a commercial maglev line in Germany, a nation positioned as a technology leader in this field, as a "Transrapid trauma." The TSB license is being interpreted as the most concrete evidence that Germany is prepared to overcome this <strong>innovation gap</strong> in the sector.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Significance for European Transport Policy</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">The EU's <strong>Fit for 55</strong> and <strong>Green Mobility</strong> programs encourage the addition of low-noise, low-emission next-generation solutions to existing urban transport infrastructure. The TSB system:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">reduces carbon footprint through electric and automatic operations,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">fills a complementary role on medium-distance routes where metro and LRT capacity is insufficient,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">creates advantages for urban expansion projects due to its rapid constructability in new urban areas.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">With the TSB license, Europe is expected to shift toward "maglev 2.0"—the new generation of low-to-medium-speed magnetic systems.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h3>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The EBA granted TSB maglev system an <strong>operating license for passenger transport</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">TSB became Europe's <strong>first commercial maglev</strong> system to receive a license.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">150 km/h speed, GoA4 full automation, linear motor, and prefabricated concrete tracks are among the core features.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The license paves the way for a potential pilot line in Nuremberg.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The decision is viewed as a critical threshold in the rebirth of European maglev projects since Transrapid.</p></li>
</ul>
