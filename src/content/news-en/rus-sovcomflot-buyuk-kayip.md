<p style="text-align: justify">Russia's state-backed tanker company <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovcomflot"><u><strong>Sovcomflot</strong></u></a> experienced a 55% decline in net profit during 2024, reflecting the severe impact of sanctions on Russian oil on the company's operations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Following the invasion of Ukraine in 2022, international sanctions against <strong>Sovcomflot</strong> and other Russian energy companies intensified, adversely affecting the company's operations and profitability.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Due to sanctions, <strong>Sovcomflot</strong> lost access to Western financial institutions, complicating its cash flow. Although the company attempted to secure financing through alternative payment systems and different banks, the process proved more complex than anticipated.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The company redirected a significant portion of its fleet to Asia and Middle Eastern markets. However, as competition increased in these regions and shipping revenues declined due to price caps imposed on Russian oil, <strong>Sovcomflot</strong> began working with alternative insurance providers. By partnering with Russian and Chinese insurance companies instead of Western firms, the company managed to continue operations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Despite these efforts, the company's operational costs rose substantially. <strong>Sovcomflot</strong> attempted to generate liquidity by divesting parts of its fleet, but faced severe restrictions on new ship investments.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Some industry experts suggest that <strong>Sovcomflot</strong> has limited prospects of establishing a supply chain entirely independent from Western sanctions in the long term. While demand from countries such as China and India has partially offset losses, the loss of European and U.S. markets continues to strain the company.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">To mitigate the impact of sanctions, <strong>Sovcomflot</strong> attempted to continue operations by transferring some vessels to third-party companies. However, this strategy complicated operational control.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, despite implementing various countermeasures against sanctions, <strong>Sovcomflot</strong> experienced a substantial decline in profitability in 2024. Despite efforts to develop alternative markets and financing models, returning to historical profit margins in the near term appears unlikely for the company.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Sovcomflot</strong> experienced a 55% decline in net profit in 2024.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">International sanctions on Russian oil negatively affected the company's financial performance.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Access to Western financial and insurance systems was lost, with alternative solutions proving insufficient.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Redirecting the fleet to Asia and Middle Eastern markets failed to fully compensate for losses.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Operational costs increased, with some vessels divested to generate liquidity.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Completely eliminating the effects of sanctions long-term appears difficult.</p></li>
</ul>
