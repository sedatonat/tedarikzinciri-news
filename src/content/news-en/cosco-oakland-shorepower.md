<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>COSCO SHIPPING North America</strong> has reached one of the most significant milestones in emissions reduction across U.S. port operations. According to the company's <strong>2024 Sustainability Report</strong>, <strong>100% shore power</strong> connectivity has been achieved for all eligible vessel calls at <strong>Port of Oakland</strong>. Meanwhile, shore power connection rates at <strong>Ports of Los Angeles and Long Beach</strong> have risen to <strong>96.5%</strong>, representing substantial progress compared to the prior year.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Shore power</strong> (cold ironing) enables vessels to draw electricity from onshore facilities while docked instead of operating diesel auxiliary engines. This practice significantly reduces <strong>NOx</strong>, <strong>SOx</strong>, and particulate matter emissions, particularly benefiting residential areas surrounding ports. COSCO's full compliance at Oakland is regarded as a benchmark implementation for environmental regulatory adherence at U.S. West Coast ports.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Los Angeles–Long Beach Performance</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">COSCO has achieved high compliance levels at the <strong>Los Angeles–Long Beach port complex</strong>, the nation's busiest container gateway. Shore power connection rates have increased from just over 90% in 2023 to <strong>96.5%</strong> in 2024. This improvement represents significant progress within California's increasingly stringent <strong>port emissions rules</strong> framework.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Terminal-level shore power adoption is also accelerating rapidly. At <strong>Pier J (Long Beach)</strong> specifically, shore power consumption reached approximately <strong>7.9 million kilowatt-hours</strong> throughout 2024. This figure demonstrates that shore power is transitioning from an exceptional practice to a <strong>standard operational practice</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Electric Drayage Trucks Coming Online</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">COSCO's decarbonization strategy extends beyond vessel operations. The company is progressively expanding its use of <strong>electric drayage trucks</strong>. As of 2024, electric drayage trucks handle <strong>5% of COSCO's total container movements</strong> in the United States.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This progress has been enabled through partnerships with <strong>CARB-certified truck providers</strong> and participation in the <strong>Ports of Los Angeles &amp; Long Beach Clean Truck Program</strong>. COSCO aims to <strong>double</strong> its electric drayage usage rate during 2025. This target is critical for reducing land-based emissions around port facilities.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Speed Limits and Fuel Compliance</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Vessel speed reduction (VSR)</strong> initiatives form another pillar of the company's emissions reduction program. COSCO maintains <strong>100% compliance</strong> within the <strong>20 nautical mile zone</strong> around Long Beach port and <strong>over 99% compliance</strong> in the 40 mile zone. Reduced transit speeds lower both <strong>fuel consumption</strong> and provide positive impacts for <strong>marine life protection</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Regarding fuel, COSCO reported <strong>100% compliance</strong> with <strong>low-sulfur marine fuel</strong> regulations. The <strong>U.S. Coast Guard</strong> issued no violation notices against the company throughout 2024. This result demonstrates that COSCO has operationally internalized fuel regulations across its North American port operations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Energy Efficiency in Warehouses and Facilities</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">COSCO's sustainability initiatives extend beyond port operations. Comprehensive improvements are underway in <strong>warehouse and facility operations</strong> throughout the United States. The company is deploying:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>LED lighting</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>energy-efficient HVAC systems</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>electric forklifts</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>solar panels</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>EV charging stations</strong></p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">As a result of these investments, energy intensity in office buildings has been reduced by more than <strong>12%</strong>, and water usage has declined by more than <strong>34%</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>2026 Priorities</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">Looking ahead to 2026, COSCO SHIPPING North America plans to further expand its sustainability agenda. Company priorities include:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Increasing <strong>electric drayage</strong> usage,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Expanding deployment of <strong>real-time supply chain visibility tools</strong>,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Strengthening cross-border logistics on the <strong>U.S.–Mexico–Canada</strong> corridor</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">These initiatives demonstrate COSCO's integrated approach to environmental compliance and operational efficiency.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Overall Assessment</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify">COSCO's achievement of 100% shore power compliance at Oakland clearly demonstrates the stage the port decarbonisation process has reached in U.S. ports. A holistic approach spanning vessel, truck, terminal, and warehouse operations shows that the company is making sustainability an <strong>operational reality</strong>, not merely a reporting exercise.</p>
