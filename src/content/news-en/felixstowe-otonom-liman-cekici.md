<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Port of Felixstowe</strong>, one of Europe's busiest container terminals, has entered a new phase in autonomous land transport, taking delivery of the first two units of a new generation of <strong>34 autonomous trucks (AT)</strong> manufactured by <strong>Shanghai Westwell Technology</strong>. These vehicles will double the fleet operating at the port's Trinity Terminal, which already has 34 ATs in service, and will significantly expand autonomous operating capacity.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Felixstowe was the first port in Europe to integrate autonomous trucks into real operations in a <strong>mixed traffic environment</strong>. The new vehicles play a critical role in the port's operational transformation vision and are seen as a key lever in the port's plan to achieve <strong>net-zero Scope 1 & 2 emissions by 2035</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new ATs are equipped with a <strong>360° industrial-grade stereo camera system</strong>, advanced <strong>128-channel LiDAR</strong>, and an advanced sensor fusion architecture. The vehicles also use <strong>illuminated signal bars</strong>, directional expressions, and audio alerts to facilitate communication between in-vehicle and external operators. These systems enable autonomous driving to maintain more transparent interaction with the environment and significantly reduce the risk of potential collisions with manned vehicles.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">All autonomous trucks are <strong>fully electric</strong> and zero-emission. Since April 2023, Port of Felixstowe has used only <strong>renewable energy-certified electricity</strong>, which minimizes the carbon footprint of the AT fleet.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">To enhance operational continuity, a new <strong>automatic battery swap station</strong> installed at the port enables trucks to exchange depleted batteries for fully charged ones in just <strong>5–6 minutes</strong>. This means that charging times—the biggest bottleneck for electric autonomous vehicles—have virtually no impact on operational efficiency.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Clemence Cheng</strong>, CEO of Hutchison Ports and Port of Felixstowe, said at the delivery ceremony that the new vehicles represent a "significant leap" in sensor technology and safety features, and that the existing AT fleet will also be upgraded to the same level. Cheng emphasized that autonomous technology works in harmony with the port's experienced workforce and that the efficiency gains are tangibly felt.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Kenny Tan</strong>, founder of Westwell, stated that they have enhanced Felixstowe's 150-year port heritage with artificial intelligence-powered autonomous technology called "Ainergy". Tan noted that the global partnership with Hutchison Ports exemplifies sustainable port transformation.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The new AT fleet will deliver at the port:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Higher operational speeds,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Lower energy costs,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Better traffic safety,</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">And significant reductions in carbon emissions<br>
as multifaceted improvements.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">Port of Felixstowe's investment is viewed as a step that strengthens technological leadership in both the UK and Europe in autonomous and electric port operations.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Felixstowe received the first two of 34 ATs on order.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Europe's first port to operate autonomous trucks in mixed traffic environments.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">ATs feature advanced sensor fusion with 128-channel LiDAR and 360° cameras.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">All vehicles are fully electric and use only renewable energy.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The battery swap station enables automatic exchange in 5–6 minutes.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The port has taken another critical step toward its 2035 net-zero goal.</p></li>
</ul>
