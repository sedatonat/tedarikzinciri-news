<p><strong>Climeon</strong> has signed an agreement with <strong>NovaAlgoma Cement Carriers (NACC)</strong> to install its <strong>HeatPower 300</strong> system on a newly built 38,000 DWT methanol dual-fuel cement carrier. The system will generate up to 300 kW of sustainable electricity by harnessing low-grade waste heat from the vessel's engine cooling water and exhaust gases. This will reduce fuel consumption and emissions, contributing to NACC's sustainability objectives.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>The vessel, which is being built at <strong>Zhejiang Xingle Shipbuilding</strong> and will be classed by <strong>RINA</strong>, sets a new standard for sustainable transportation in the cement industry. <strong>Climeon's HeatPower 300</strong> system is an <strong>Organic Rankine Cycle (ORC)</strong> technology that generates electricity from low-temperature waste heat. This technology provides flexibility by offering both environmental and economic benefits across different vessel types.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>Francesco Costagliola, Technical Director at NACC, stated that the integration of Climeon's waste heat recovery technology provides a practical pathway to enhance operational efficiency while supporting sustainability goals and complying with increasingly stringent environmental regulations.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>This collaboration supports the maritime industry's transition to cleaner and more efficient technologies. The Climeon <strong>HeatPower 300</strong> system captures engine waste heat regardless of fuel type, providing both economic and environmental benefits for NACC's dual-fuel vessel. This partnership underscores Climeon and NACC's shared commitment to advancing sustainability in the cement shipping sector.</p>\n<p><br></p>\n<h5><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>\n<ul>\n  <li><p><strong>Climeon</strong> has signed an agreement with <strong>NovaAlgoma Cement Carriers (NACC)</strong> to install the <strong>HeatPower 300</strong> system on a new cement carrier.</p></li>\n  <li><p><strong>HeatPower 300</strong> is an <strong>Organic Rankine Cycle (ORC)</strong> technology that generates up to 300 kW of electricity from low-temperature waste heat.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The system will reduce fuel consumption and emissions by harnessing heat from the vessel's engine cooling water and exhaust gases.</p></li>\n  <li><p>The vessel is being built at <strong>Zhejiang Xingle Shipbuilding</strong> and will be classed by <strong>RINA</strong>.</p></li>\n  <li><p>This collaboration contributes to sustainability and energy efficiency goals in the maritime industry.</p></li>\n</ul>\n<p><br></p>\n<p>---</p>\n\n<p>-------------------</p>\n
