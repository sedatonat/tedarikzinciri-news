<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>TSMC (Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company)</strong> is accelerating <strong>capacity investments</strong> in response to rapid growth in global semiconductor demand. The company is implementing <strong>a multi-dimensional expansion plan</strong> to maintain its leadership in advanced manufacturing technologies while strengthening supply security.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">At the core of the strategy lies <strong>advanced nodes</strong> production — particularly <strong>N3 (3nm)</strong> and <strong>N2 (2nm)</strong> processes. Alongside this, <strong>CoWoS (Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate)</strong> advanced packaging technology and <strong>HBM (High Bandwidth Memory)</strong> supply chains address the growing needs of the artificial intelligence and high-performance computing (HPC) segments. However, <strong>foundry capacity planning</strong> and <strong>equipment setup lead times</strong> emerge as the most critical constraints in bringing these production lines online.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Geographic diversification</strong> forms a fundamental pillar of TSMC's resilience strategy. The company is expanding fab investments across regions such as the <strong>United States (Arizona)</strong>, <strong>Japan (Kumamoto)</strong>, and <strong>Europe (Germany)</strong>, thereby providing <strong>supply resilience</strong>. These moves also grow <strong>regional talent pools</strong> and the <strong>supplier ecosystem</strong>, reducing global operational risks.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">With respect to production continuity and sustainability, <strong>energy and water management</strong> play a decisive role in fab performance. TSMC is increasing renewable energy usage through <strong>PPA (Power Purchase Agreement)</strong> arrangements, while <strong>water recycling programs</strong> reduce the environmental footprint of manufacturing processes. These initiatives support operational efficiency as part of sustainable production goals.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">On the customer side, <strong>capacity reservation</strong> and <strong>long-term supply agreements</strong> are becoming increasingly common. These contracts secure access to critical production capacity for major customers—such as Apple, NVIDIA, and AMD. Simultaneously, through <strong>design–manufacturing co-optimization (DMCO)</strong> processes, the design and manufacturing phases of products are integrated to optimize <strong>performance</strong> and <strong>yield</strong> simultaneously.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, TSMC's expansion strategy presents a model that balances <strong>the pace of innovation in advanced manufacturing technologies</strong>, <strong>supply security</strong>, and <strong>sustainable operational infrastructure</strong>. Through these investments, the company is not only increasing capacity but also shaping the direction of the global semiconductor ecosystem.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li><p>N3/N2 and CoWoS/HBM capacity are focal points.</p></li>
  <li><p>Geographic diversification provides resilience.</p></li>
  <li><p>Energy and water management are critical for production.</p></li>
  <li><p>Long-term agreements secure access to capacity.</p></li>
  <li><p>DMCO improves yield and performance.</p></li>
</ul>
