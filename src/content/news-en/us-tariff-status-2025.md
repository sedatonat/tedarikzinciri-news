<p style="text-align: justify">2025 has been a year of <strong>intense and rapid shifts in U.S. trade policy</strong>. Since the President's return to office, the Trump administration has both expanded trade initiatives inherited from the previous period and implemented new comprehensive tariff programs. The administration is <strong>pursuing tariffs and trade investigations against different countries simultaneously</strong>, creating significant uncertainty for both U.S. companies and global commerce.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Starting in January, Trump imposed high tariffs on <strong>Canada, Mexico, and China</strong> through executive orders signed on February 1st. However, the implementation of these tariffs <strong>progressed differently for each country</strong>; some saw rapid deployment while others experienced phased or limited rollouts.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>comprehensive "reciprocal tariff" policy</strong> announced on April 2nd further complicated the global trade landscape. This policy calls for levying equal tariff rates on countries that run trade deficits with the U.S. On August 7th, the U.S. put country-specific tariff rates into effect.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">During the same period, the U.S. negotiated tariff reductions and framework agreements with <strong>the EU, Japan, and South Korea</strong>. These agreements resulted in partial tariff reductions between the parties. For example, negotiations with South Korea saw tariffs reduced from 25 to 15 percent; the EU was exempted from additional tariffs under Section 232 on pharmaceutical products.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The most complex relationship unfolded between the U.S. and <strong>China</strong>. The two countries engaged in a trade war throughout the year marked by <strong>continuous tensions and interim compromises</strong>. However, in October, following discussions between the two leaders in South Korea, a <strong>mutual de-escalation agreement</strong> was reached — under which certain tariffs were reduced and retaliatory levies were suspended.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Legal and political resistance</strong> to these policy moves also intensified. The U.S. International Trade Court (CIT) issued an <strong>injunction</strong> that would invalidate tariffs applied under IEEPA. The ruling is under appeal, and <strong>the Supreme Court is expected to issue a final decision in November</strong>. This decision will directly shape the future of the U.S. tariff regime.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Due to the rapid tariff changes, U.S. companies and supply chain managers are operating in a <strong>constantly updating tariff environment</strong>.</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Some countries are implementing retaliatory tariffs.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Certain sectors (e.g., automotive, agriculture, pharmaceuticals, semiconductors) face intense pressure.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Some agreements are providing temporary relief.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify">To track this complex landscape, the Supply Chain Dive team has compiled a list containing the <strong>real-time status</strong> of all tariff threats and applied taxes.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h5 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Points:</strong></h5>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The Trump administration is operating a <strong>multi-layered tariff regime</strong> in 2025.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Tariffs on Canada, Mexico, and China began on February 1st.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>reciprocal tariff</strong> system went into effect on April 2nd.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">As of August 7th, <strong>country-specific tariff rates</strong> are in effect.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">In the trade war with China, a <strong>temporary settlement</strong> was reached in October.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">IEEPA tariffs are <strong>under Supreme Court review</strong>; the outcome will determine the future of tariffs.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Landscape for U.S. companies: <strong>high uncertainty plus rapidly shifting policy direction</strong>.</p></li>
</ul>
