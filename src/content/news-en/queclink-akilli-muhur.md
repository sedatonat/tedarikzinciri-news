<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Container security</strong>, one of the most critical challenges facing global supply chain companies, is poised for a significant transformation through a new intelligent security solution developed by IoT hardware manufacturer <strong>Queclink Wireless Solutions</strong>. The company is introducing the <strong>smart bolt-seal GES100CG</strong> device, which complies with the <strong>ISO 17712</strong> high-security standard, aiming to enhance the security of high-value cargo across maritime, road, and rail transport.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Queclink UK Sales Director <strong>Vernon Bonser</strong> notes that the new product addresses a critical vulnerability in intermodal transportation. Traditional bolt-seal seals can be manipulated by being <strong>cloned</strong>, <strong>swapped</strong>, or <strong>re-installed</strong>. This situation often results in breaches going undetected until the container reaches its destination, creating a serious security gap that threatens container integrity. In contrast, the GES100CG, thanks to its <strong>tamper detection</strong> capability, sends immediate alerts when the seal is broken, tampered with, or subjected to unauthorized access. Since <strong>reassembly</strong> is impossible when the device is damaged, the risk of fraudulent sealing is eliminated.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Among the advanced features of the GES100CG is its <strong>LTE Cat-1 bis (4G)</strong> connectivity architecture. The device features a <strong>buffering</strong> capability that records security notifications even in areas without coverage. This ensures no data loss even when containers are in transit at sea for extended periods. Once coverage is restored, all recorded data is transmitted to the system. Additionally, the device integrates <strong>GSM (2G) fallback</strong>, <strong>GNSS</strong>, and <strong>network-based LBS positioning</strong> technologies to provide global traceability.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The security sensor infrastructure operates with an embedded <strong>tamper sensor</strong> in the internal mechanism, with all movement, breakage, or tampering reported in real time. Integrated with real-time location tracking, this architecture enhances operational visibility for carriers, freight forwarders, and port operators alike. The device's internal <strong>power management</strong> system delivers approximately <strong>90 days</strong> of operational time during typical biweekly reporting usage. Additionally, a <strong>low battery alarm</strong> alerts users early to power depletion.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The GES100CG is not merely an electronic security solution; its physical design also delivers high-security protection. The device is engineered to be <strong>tamper-resistant</strong>, with an <strong>IP67-rated casing</strong> that withstands environmental conditions. This design provides additional durability against saltwater exposure, high humidity, and mechanical impact risks inherent in maritime transport, enabling the seal to deliver advantages in both security and operational sustainability.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">According to Queclink, the intelligent seal solution will be a critical tool for mitigating risks such as <strong>theft</strong>, <strong>contamination</strong>, <strong>damage</strong>, and <strong>contraband insertion</strong> throughout the supply chain. Particularly in long-distance maritime transport, passive methods prove insufficient for ensuring container integrity. The GES100CG represents a transition from passive sealing to active, connected security management. This transformation strengthens the security chain for shipping lines, freight forwarders, warehouses, port terminal operators, and customs agencies.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">In conclusion, Queclink's GES100CG solution reinforces the <strong>connected integrity assurance</strong> approach in the context of rising security threats and insurance costs in global supply chains. By providing real-time visibility in container operations, it both reduces risk and supports the secure transportation of high-value shipments.</p>
<p><br></p>
