<p style="text-align: justify">The U.S. exported <strong>10.1 million metric tonnes (mmt)</strong> of LNG in October 2025, becoming the first country in world history to achieve this level of LNG exports in a single month. According to preliminary data shared by financial institution <strong>LSEG</strong>, this volume surpassed the previous month's revised shipment of <strong>9.1 mmt</strong>, setting a new record. This momentum represents the continuation of four consecutive record-breaking months for the U.S. in 2025.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">The U.S. was already the world's largest LNG exporter; however, 2025 has further strengthened America's position in the global market, particularly with the commissioning of two major facilities. The key drivers behind this surge include the ramp-up of <strong>Venture Global's Plaquemines LNG facility</strong> and <strong>Cheniere Energy's Corpus Christi Stage 3 expansion project</strong>.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Facility-Based Performance: Plaquemines and Corpus Christi Lead the Way</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">The <strong>Plaquemines LNG facility</strong> in Louisiana achieved <strong>2.2 mmt</strong> in sales in October, surpassing its September level of 1.6 mmt and setting its own record. This facility stands out as the fastest-growing contributor to U.S. export growth.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Cheniere's <strong>Corpus Christi facility</strong> similarly achieved its highest monthly exports on record with <strong>1.6 mmt</strong>. The company's other major facility, <strong>Sabine Pass</strong>, exported <strong>2.6 mmt</strong> of LNG in October. Combined, Cheniere's two facilities accounted for <strong>4.2 mmt</strong>, representing <strong>42%</strong> of U.S. LNG exports.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Cheniere CEO <strong>Jack Fusco</strong> stated during a recent investor call that once Corpus Christi Stage 3 is completed, the company will reach annual LNG export capacity of <strong>over 50 mmt</strong> and will benefit from greater supply strength from 2026 onward.</p>
<p><br></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Combined, Venture Global and Cheniere accounted for <strong>72%</strong> of the U.S. LNG exports in October.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Primary Markets: Europe Leads</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">In October 2025, Europe remained the most important destination for U.S. LNG.</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>6.9 mmt</strong> of LNG went to Europe (representing 69% of total exports).</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">This volume exceeded the 6.22 mmt shipped in September.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">European countries continued to rapidly fill storage in preparation for the approaching winter season.</p></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify"><strong>LNG sent to Asia</strong> increased from the September level of 1.63 mmt to <strong>1.96 mmt</strong>.<br>
<strong>Exports to Latin America</strong> declined from 0.63 mmt to <strong>0.57 mmt</strong> due to the onset of the summer season.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">Regional distribution also included:</p>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Egypt</strong>: 0.43 mmt (September: 0.5 mmt)</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Senegal</strong>: 0.1 mmt</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify"><strong>Spot cargoes seeking buyers</strong>: 0.1 mmt</p></li>
</ul>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Prices and Market Dynamics</strong></h4>
<p style="text-align: justify">In October, Europe's TTF price stood at <strong>$10.88/MMBtu</strong>, while Asia's JKM price reached <strong>$11.11/MMBtu</strong>.<br>
With the price differential being extremely narrow, <strong>there was no strong incentive for U.S. exporters to redirect volumes away from Europe toward Asia</strong>. This preserved Europe's dominant share of U.S. LNG shipments.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify">This record month signals that the U.S. is becoming an increasingly critical player in global LNG supply security, and that supply stability will strengthen further from 2026 onward.</p>
<p><br></p>
<h4 style="text-align: justify"><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong></h4>
<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">The U.S. became the <strong>first country to export 10.1 mmt of LNG in a single month</strong>.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Primary growth drivers: <strong>Plaquemines LNG</strong> and <strong>Corpus Christi Stage 3</strong> ramp-up process.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Cheniere alone accounted for <strong>42%</strong> of U.S. exports.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Europe retained its position as the largest customer, purchasing <strong>69%</strong> of U.S. LNG.</p></li>
  <li style="text-align: justify"><p style="text-align: justify">Tight TTF-JKM price spreads → Limited redirection to Asia.</p></li>
</ul>
